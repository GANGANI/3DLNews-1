Mom says child 'tossed' by Tumwater school bus driver

by Joel Moreno

Thu, March 2nd 2017 at 11:10 PM
Updated Thu, March 2nd 2017 at 8:58 AM
5
VIEW ALL PHOTOS
Monica Cornell believes a school bus driver mistreated her special-needs son after daycare workers told her the driver aggressively tossed the boy from the vehicle in Tumwater. (Photo: Monica Cornell)

TUMWATER, Wash. – A mom believes a school bus driver mistreated her special-needs son after daycare workers told her the driver aggressively tossed the boy from the vehicle.

“I'm angry and i want answers,” said Monica Cornell, the child’s mother.

The issue began on Friday when her 5-year-old son, Ryen, was getting off his special needs school bus to go to day care. As the child made his way down the stairs, staff at the facility said the driver suddenly intervened.

“The bus driver gets out of his seat, aggressively grabs Ryan, and tosses him to the daycare worker,” she said.

Ryen Cornell is mildly autistic but sweet and good natured, according to his mother. However, his behavior has taken a sharp turn since the incident last week.

“He's trying to leave the classrooms, and when he's told he can't go out the door, he becomes upset,” Cornell said.

The school district is investigating. A spokesperson said the safety and well-being of students is of utmost importance.

However, according to Laurie Wiedenmeyer, the district does acknowledge that there was "possible inapproprite behavior by the driver” and that "because of this, the driver has been placed on administrative leave.”

Cornell said the district denied her request to see the surveillance footage from the cameras aboard the bus. A district official told KOMO News that nothing is viewable on that video.
