Police seek third suspect in Yakima teen shooting death

by Trisha McCauley, Marie Schurk

Tue, October 24th 2017 at 10:51 AM
Updated Thu, October 26th 2017 at 6:02 PM
4
VIEW ALL PHOTOS
Yakima teen shot, killed

YAKIMA, Wash. - Police say a teen is dead from multiple gunshot wounds.

His death marks the 12th homicide for the city of Yakima this year.

Officers from the Yakima Police Department (YPD) arrived at 310 S. 7th Street for reported gunshots in the area at 9:43 a.m. Tuesday.

Responders found the victim lying on the sidewalk, suffering from multiple gunshot wounds.

Medics transported him to Astria Regional Medical Center where he was pronounced dead. YPD officers said the victim is 17-year-old Napolean Prado from Yakima.

Police found the vehicle driven in the shooting and arrested two male suspects Tuesday afternoon at their Yakima homes: a 17 and 18-year-old.

Police are also seeking a third suspect, a 15-year-old Luis Enrique Medina-Beltran, who is wanted for 1st degree murder. Medina-Beltran's picture, which is noted to be from at least two years ago, is attached to this story.

Action News does not usually use the name of minors in crimes. However, because YPD officials said the suspect is considered armed and dangerous, his name is being released.

Detectives believe the shooting is gang-related as all of the suspects involved have gang ties.

Police Spokesperson Mike Bastinelli said since the shooting happened earlier in the day, more people were out to witness it.

"This happened in the morning in daylight hours so we do have people that saw certain things and obviously, we're trying to talk to those people," he said.

Eli Angelo Salazar was down the street visiting a friend when he heard the gunshots.

He says he heard four shots and then went down the street to see what happened.

"It’s getting pretty ridiculous here in Yakima, but you have to remember we have officers here that are fast and they will catch up with them, they will," he said.

A family who did not want to be identified said they were driving down the street with the three young children in the car when the shooting happened.

They said as they drove by they saw a man pull out a gun.

As they pulled up to a stop sign at the end of the street, they heard shots and turned around their car to see what kind of car the gunman was driving.

Anyone with additional information in this homicide is asked to contact Yakima County Crime Stoppers at 1-800-248-9980.

This is a developing story. Information will be provided as available.
