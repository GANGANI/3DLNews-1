WEATHERWATCH
Stawicki bought blueberry bush for former teacher during manhunt
by KOMO Staff
Sunday, Jun 3rd 2012
SEATTLE -- While police were looking for him in the wake of two fatal shootings,
ADVERTISEMENT
Ian Stawicki
went to a nursery where he purchased a blueberry bush, according to police.


Police believe it was Stawicki who opened fire at Cafe Racer around 11 a.m. last Wednesday, wounding five people - four of them fatally. The same man is believed to have shot and killed a woman in a parking lot in the First Hill neighborhood before taking off in her Mercedes SUV.


Police soon found that car abandoned in West Seattle. But Stawicki himself wasn't spotted until approximately 4 p.m. When approached by police, he turned the gun on himself. He died several hours later from his injuries.


So what did he do during the hours between the shootings and his surrender? It appears he went shopping at West Seattle Nursery and Garden Center.


Police cannot confirm his whereabouts or his actions; however, nursery manager Marcia Bruno said she received a phone call from detectives who said they found a receipt that shows Stawicki had purchased the blueberry bush and two packets of seeds from the store around 1:40 p.m. Wednesday.


"I was actually speechless," Bruno said.


The employee who sold Stawicki the plant told Bruno she remembered him, but said he was "creepy" and she did not chat with him at length during the transaction.


Another employee said she helped Stawicki select one of the last blueberry bushes of a desired type.


"I showed him that we had two left," said employee Corinne Kennedy. "And I said, 'Well, this one is a lot better than the other one.' And then I said, 'This is your lucky day."'


The nursery also received a phone call from a West Seattle woman who said someone had left the plant and seeds with a "creepy note" on her doorstep that day.


"We got the phone call the next morning of the recipient of the blueberry," Bruno said. "She was freaked out."


Patricia Guenther, a former teacher of Stawicki's, issued a statement on Monday stating the gift came as a complete surprise:


"When I arrived at my home from work on Wednesday evening, May 30, I discovered a bag containing a blueberry plant and seed packets from the West Seattle Nursery, along with a pre-printed commercial thank you card (not personalized or signed) in a bag on my porch. I live in the North Admiral area of West Seattle.


There was no identifying information on the items and I did not know who left them there. I later learned that these items were purchased by Ian Stawicki, the shooter in the recent North Seattle and First Hill shootings, who was a former student of mine.


I have not had contact with Ian Stawicki since he was in my Summit K-12 School classroom almost 25 years ago, and I was surprised to learn that he was the source of the bag that was left on my porch.


I was in communication with the Seattle Police Department and the items were turned over to them.


No person, no neighborhood, no community is immune to being touched by tragedy. Please be compassionate with each other as we each process this recent heartbreaking event in our city in our own way.


I have no additional information and I ask that the media please respect my privacy."


Bruno said news of Stawicki's visit to the store has left her alarmed.


"I definitely lost sleep two nights thinking about what could have happened," she said.


The incident has hit especially close to home for Jenn Patrick, who works at the nursery and frequents Cafe Racer where she often saw victims Drew Keriakedes and Joe "Vito" Albanese.


"You know, they were our Cliff and Norm, like from "Cheers" on the bar stools, like always there, being them, she said.


Despite the harrowing tragedy that left four cafe patrons dead, Patrick said she will not stop visiting the cafe.
