Dick's Drive-In: 5 claims alleging health, COVID-19 violations were without merit

by KOMO News Staff

Tue, March 16th 2021 at 5:40 AM
Photo: Sunny Martini, Seattle Refined

    TOPICS:

    AnneJasmine DonvanSeattleSeattle,Washington,United StatesTWITTER, INC.TwitterWashingtonWashington,United States

SEATTLE -- Dick's Drive-In is responding to complaints that it violated some COVID-19 and health requirements at their Queen Anne and Capitol Hill locations.

Working Washington said the state Department of Labor and Industries had received complaints from five workers amid the two locations alleging mask violations, pressure to work while awaiting COVID-19 test results, poor sanitization among other issues.

Monday, Dick's Drive-In president Jasmine Donvan posted a letter on Twitter saying an investigation found most of the claims are without merit and the other allegations could only reference isolated incidents because they're blatant violations of training and procedures.

Dick's says unannounced inspections found everything in compliance at both locations.

Donovan says no other employees corroborated the issues in five claims filed with the state last week.
