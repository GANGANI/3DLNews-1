Crane collapse may be due to human error, AP reports
by KOMO News Staff & Associated Press
Monday, Apr 29th 2019
3
View All Photos
KOMO News image{p}{/p}{p}{/p}

Parts of the construction crane that crashed to the ground on Saturday are now sitting in lot at Mercer Street and Terry Avenue, according to construction workers on the project.

ADVERTISEMENT

Investigators from the state Department of Labor and Industries are examining the pieces for clues about why the crane collapsed killing four people, injuring four more and crushing six cars.

L & I confirms the tower crane that collapsed was certified last June by a third-party certifier after it was built at that construction site. They found no issues.

An expert out of South Carolina said it appears human error may have caused the crane collapse, according to the Associated Press.

Based on videos of the collapse, the expert said it appears workers who were disassembling the construction crane had removed pins securing the sections of the crane's mast to each other, and that could explain why the crane toppled in relatively minor wind gusts.

Crane accident investigator Tom Barth said the only safe way to disassemble a tower crane is to hook up the section being removed to another crane, and then remove the pins from that section only. In this case, he said, it appears the workers removed pins from all of the sections in advance.

The same cause was blamed for a crane collapse that killed two workers in Dallas in 2012.

Dashcam%20video%20shows%20crane%20toppling%20onto%20Seattle%20street%20(Courtesy%20Natalie%20Williams/Scott%20Gaines)

Tim Church of L & I said Sunday two Washington state investigators are assigned to the case which he expects will take about six months to finish.

Church says investigators finished their on-site work Monday.

"We're really at the beginning of an investigation that will likely take months," said Church. "We take situations like this very seriously. No one should ever have to go to work and not come home from their job.

Five companies involved in the project are part of the investigation. They include the general contractor, GLY, Northwest Tower Cranes Service, Omega Morgan Inc., Morrow Equipment Company and Seaburg Construction.

On Saturday, two roads were closed around the construction site, but not Mercer Street. SDOT says the contractors did not ask the City to close it in their permit request. Ultimately SDOT says they leave it up to those companies to decide how much space they need to safely operate.

Aerials%20of%20the%20crane%20collapse%20in%20Seattle's%20South%20Lake%20Union%20neighborhood%20(KOMO%20Air%204%20Video)

Fifty-nine cranes still line Seattle’s skyline, the most of any city in the U.S.

“This tragedy has been a wake-up call for us to make sure that a thorough review of safety procedures and guidelines and standards is conducted,” Councilmember Kshama Sawant said.

The council has a hearing scheduled Wednesday morning in which they will discuss crane safety.

Under state law, Church said tower cranes like the one that collapsed Saturday must be certified every time they are erected at a construction site. That's done by private third-party company accredited to do certifications by the state.

Impromptu%20memorial%20to%20crane%20collapse%20victims%20(KOMO%20Photo)

The collapsed happened just three days after Worker Memorial Day in Washington state to commemorate workers who died on the job or in work-related accidents. In 2018, that number was 91.

"It incredibly saddens us to see three days after Worker Memorial Day to have two other workers and, of course, members of the public added to this year's list. Our goal is to never have that happen and unfortunately, it happens all too often," said Church.

Meanwhile, an impromptu memorial appeared along Mercer street not far from the scene. Flowers, a cross, an American flag and a Marine Corp flag sit in the median in the middle of the thoroughfare to memorialize the four people who died on Sunday.

One of the Victims, Travis Corbet, was a five-year veteran of the Corp.
