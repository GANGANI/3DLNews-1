4 Ingraham High School students arrested for attempted armed robbery

by KOMO News Staff

Sat, February 10th 2024 at 5:38 PM
Updated Sat, February 10th 2024 at 9:22 PM
5
VIEW ALL PHOTOS
Seattle police responding to an attempted armed robbery that occurred at Ingraham High School on the morning of Feb. 9, 2024. (KOMO News)

SEATTLE — Seattle police arrested four juvenile boys following an attempted armed robbery that occurred at Ingraham High School Friday morning.

Police said that shortly after 10 a.m., officers were called to Densmore Avenue North at Ingraham High School for an attempted robbery that involved a firearm.

RELATED: 2 Garfield High School students robbed at gunpoint, suspects remain at large

Officers said a male student was walking to school when a red Honda with four juvenile boys inside drove up. After that, the four boys exited the car and pointed a gun toward the victim. The four juveniles then demanded the victim's shoes and AirPod earphones, according to officials.

The victim was able to run away from the suspects to a nearby home and call 911, police said.

RELATED: Franklin High student robbed at gunpoint after school in south Seattle

Ingraham High School was briefly locked down while police searched for the four juveniles. Officers found the red Honda suspected in the incident parked outside the school with all four juveniles inside. All four boys were positively identified and taken into custody without incident, police said.

All four juveniles were identified as students of Ingraham High School, police said.

RELATED: 14-year-old robbed, assaulted by 3 teens Sunday in Seattle

The red Honda was impounded and taken to the Seattle Police Department's (SPD) evidence garage to be processed for forensic evidence.

The four boys were arrested for robbery and taken to the Judge Patricia H. Clark Children and Family Justice Center.
