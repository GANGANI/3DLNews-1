1 at-large, 2 arrested after home burglary in Edmonds

by Alton Worley II, KOMO News Staff

Sun, February 11th 2024 at 1:15 PM
Updated Tue, February 13th 2024 at 4:16 PM
4
VIEW ALL PHOTOS
1 at-large, 2 in custody after alleged car theft, Edmonds home invasion (KOMO)

    TOPICS:

    EdmondsBurglaryPoliceArrestedCustodySuspectsVehicleHome

EDMONDS, Wash. — Two people are in custody for allegedly stealing a car and then breaking into a home in Edmonds Saturday evening. Police are still looking for a third person involved.

Edmonds police initially posted about the incident on X at 9:02 p.m. with reports of two people in custody after they allegedly fled from a stolen vehicle and into a person’s home in the 8700 block of Main Street. Police said the homeowner was able to get out of the house safely, leaving the suspects inside.

After a few hours of calling out to the suspects inside the home, both surrendered and were taken into custody.

Edmonds police said Sunday they arrested a 45-year-old Lakewood woman and a 28-year-old Spanaway man for residential burglary and resisting arrest.

Edmonds police said the third and final suspect has been identified as a 29-year-old Lakewood woman who will also face residential burglary charges once arrested. Authorities said she's the primary suspect in another vehicle theft that happened overnight after police finished their initial search.

Suspects could face additional charges for possession of a stolen vehicle, according to the Edmonds Police Department.

There were no injuries to anyone involved in the incidents, officials said.
