Migrants refuse to return to Tukwila refugee camp citing inhumane conditions

by Paul Rivera, KOMO News Reporter

Wed, January 17th 2024 at 6:49 PM
Updated Wed, January 17th 2024 at 6:49 PM
6

TUKWILA, Wash. — On Tuesday, 50 African migrants who have been staying at a hotel said they do not wish to return to a refugee camp that is located on the grounds of Riverton Park United Methodist Church.

Jan Bolerjack, the pastor, said the church paid to house the asylum seekers at the Homewood Suites in Tukwila to get them out of the freezing temperatures.

They have been staying at the hotel since late last week.

“I have no funding to continue their stay there. It was just for cold weather,” Bolerjack said.

As last week’s cold weather was on its way, Bolerjack said she was working to coordinate with the King County Regional Homelessness Authority (KCRHA) to get more of the migrants living in the refugee camp on church grounds out of the elements. She said emergency housing was not available, but the church was able to pay for rooms at the Homewood Suites.

On Tuesday morning, a group staying at the hotel organized a press conference in the parking lot to speak out about the conditions at the camp, which they’ve said are inhumane.

The refugees spoke French, Portuguese, and Spanish. Interpreters shared their message in English.

One asylum seeker from Angola told KOMO News she was in America looking for a better life. A college student, she told us through the interpreter that it has been extremely difficult to live at the camp on the church grounds. She’d like to see the government arrange a better housing option or have them stay at the hotel.

Jessica Rojas with the International Migrants Alliance said, “There are rats, it’s dirty, it’s unsanitary,” she added about conditions at the camp. “This is the first time they’ve lived indoors (in the hotel).”

Wednesday morning is when the refugees will need to check out of the hotel. It's unclear where they will go after that or if another opportunity to stay indoors will present itself.

“King County should’ve provided somewhere for all refugees, all houseless people to stay,” Rojas said. “I demand more of my government. They’ve been to the camp. They’ve seen the conditions.”

RELATED: $3 million for hotel rooms could take pressure off Tukwila church's refugee camp

“Throwing in temporary hotel vouchers and random donations, turning a blind eye, and allowing a church and a few random nonprofits to try to do the work isn’t a solution,” Rojas said.

Last month, we told you that $3 million had been set aside by King County to help secure 100 rooms at another hotel in town, the Double Tree. That is where many of the families, children, and most vulnerable are now, according to the pastor.

Currently, Bolerjack said 30-40 people are living at the camp, but new people still show up every day. She does not deny that conditions there are difficult.

“They deserve better than having to sleep in a tent,” she said.

KOMO News asked Bolerjack how long the situation at the camp would last, “until someone else steps up and has a place for these people to live. These folks are asylum seekers, and they can’t work. They’re waiting for work permits. So, for right now, there’s nothing they can do. Someone has to step up and help them with housing.”

In a statement to KOMO News, King County officials said, “As of last week, our program has housed 304 people in around 100 hotel rooms. The Department of Community and Human Services is contracting with Thrive International to move people from the church to a hotel. King County DCHS began moving people in mid-November and is prioritizing pregnant people and families with young children.”

A spokesperson with Gov. Jay Inslee also responded to questions about the camp and any path towards a resolution, claiming they are providing support and guidance when possible.

“Our office is one of many partners engaged on this issue, but for our part, we can only work with the resources we have in the budget and help connect people with other resources. Notably, there isn’t a federal financial program that specifically supports asylum seekers.”

They said Inslee is asking for additional funding that would send millions of dollars to help immigrant, refugee communities, and asylum seekers, but the funding would have to be approved and wouldn’t be available until the summer.

A City of Tukwila spokesperson said, “We agree with concerns about the safety and health of the families living at the encampment – they deserve better. This is a complex issue with no easy solution. Since the spring of 2023, Tukwila has worked directly with church staff to help improve encampment conditions and help the asylee residents.”

They said that work continues on the camp and pointed to the progress made over the last couple of months to get people inside.

“But more needs to be urgently done, especially at the state and federal level.”
