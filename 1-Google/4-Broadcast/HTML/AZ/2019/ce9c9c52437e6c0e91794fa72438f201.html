What can be done about youth suicide? Student journalists at Arizona State University’s Walter Cronkite School of Journalism and Mass Communication have set about answering that question, seeking to uncover workable solutions to what’s become an alarming public health crisis.

Drawing on field work and interviews with scores of their peers, parents, teachers, lawmakers, clinicians and counselors from across the state and country, students have identified a multiplicity of factors linked to youth suicide, rooting out insights into prevention efforts that have worked. It is an attempt to go beyond the simple answers and provide a more complete account of a phenomenon that health experts say is expected to worsen amid the COVID-19 crisis.

A recent Centers for Disease Control and Prevention survey found that, shortly after the pandemic swept the country, about 1 in 4 young adults, ages 18-24, had “seriously considered suicide.” The project will culminate in a 26-minute documentary to be simulcast Tuesday, Jan. 12, 2021, at 6:30 p.m. on more than 25 Arizona TV stations, including Arizona PBS, and at 5 p.m. on Spanish channels.

Stories

At Teen Lifeline, teens help in ways only they can

When distressed teens call Teen Lifeline, a peer-to-peer crisis call center in the Phoenix area, it's not an an adult health professional picking up the line but a teen their own age, a model experts consider the gold standard in youth crisis communications.

The other pandemic: Loneliness widespread with the loss of social connections

Behavioral scientists say people suffer when their desire for belongingness is not met, and they feel they have become a burden to those around them. Both of these feelings have been amplified during the COVID-19 pandemic, especially for youth.

The fight at home: Suicide rate is highest among younger military veterans

Younger veterans ages 18 to 34 commit suicide more often than all other age brackets, and most of them had not experienced combat, recent reports say. Veterans Affairs is trying to address the issue.

COVID-19 highlights Arizona's lack of mental health care providers

Arizona has a shortage of mental health care providers, and that’s prompting some groups to help people through problems related to COVID, including anxiety and depression.

Group brings moms, daughters together to boost mental health

Among teens, Latinas face high rates of attempted suicide. One Texas group is helping them build resilience by strengthening the bond between mothers and daughters.

Researcher: Genetic screenings may increase suicide prevention

For decades, researchers have looked to human genetics for linkages to mental illness. Patterns of inheritance are murky, but one research said it's clear that “stuff runs in families.”

Hotline offers young people hope, chance to talk with peers

In 2003, Teen Lifeline became the first teen-run hotline nationwide accredited by the nonprofit American Association of Suicidology. Teen Lifeline has since expanded its own services.

Breaking the silence: Native Americans battle against suicide

Native Americans have the highest suicide rates in the U.S., and youth are especially affected. Efforts are underway to build resilience and break the taboo of talking about mental health.

Experts: $20 million for suicide prevention welcome, more needed

Education and government officials called a $20 million grant for suicide prevention programs in Arizona schools an important first step, even as they said much more needs to be done.

Suicide may run in the family. A closer look at genetic risk.

Genetics may account for up to 50% of suicide risk, says Dr. Douglas Gray, a researcher at the University of Utah School of Medicine. “We need to tell people who’ve had a suicide that their family’s at risk,” he says.

LGBTQ suicide rates remain high. But there is hope.

Over the past year, an estimated 40% of LGBTQ youth "seriously considered" suicide, according to a 2020 National Survey on LGBTQ Youth Mental Health, recently produced by The Trevor Project, a suicide prevention organization.
