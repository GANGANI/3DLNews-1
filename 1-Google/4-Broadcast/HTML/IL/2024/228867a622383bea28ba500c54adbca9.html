7 migrants allegedly involved in NY attack on officers indicted; several still at large

by JESSICA A. BOTELHO | The National Desk

Thu, February 8th 2024 at 1:37 PM
Updated Thu, February 8th 2024 at 3:40 PM
15
VIEW ALL PHOTOS
Photos of suspects allegedly involved in New York attack on officers released (Photo: Manhattan District Attorney's Office)

    TOPICS:

    MigrantsAttackOfficersNYIndictedManhattanNYPDAssault

NEW YORK (TND) — Manhattan District Attorney Alvin Bragg said seven suspects who were accused of recently assaulting two New York police officers have been indicted.

Bragg described the incident, which was caught on camera, as disturbing and horrifying.

This assault, as it did to many of you, sickened me and outraged me," he said Thursday during a news conference alongside Mayor Eric Adams, who is a former police captain, as well as New York Police Department's Chief of Detectives Joseph Kenny. "We all rely on our members of the NYPD, as I said, to keep us safe and keep this the safest big city in America. As a lifelong New Yorker, I do not tolerate attacks on our police officers, and certainly I do not as Manhattan district attorney. So, let me be clear, plain and simple. The behavior depicted many of you have seen is despicable."

Images of suspects were made public earlier on Thursday. The photos, as well as security footage, show the suspects kicking and punching the officers.

This is a despicable act," Adams said.

Files were unsealed for five of the suspects, including the following:

Yoheny Brito

Darwin Andres Gomez-Izquiel

Yorman Reveron

Kelvin Servita Arocha

Wilson Juarez

Brito, Gomez-Izquiel, Reveron and Servita Arocha were each charged with second-degree assault while Juarez was charged with evidence tampering.

Other suspects remain on the run.

Adams noted a majority of the suspects are believed to be migrants.

There is a small minority that is participating in illegal behavior," he said during the event, adding that most migrants are following the law and just want to live quiet lives.
The overwhelming number of migrants and asylum seekers are law abiding and they're pursuing the American dream," the mayor said. "This district attorney's office and the NYPD will partner with each other to pursue those who break the laws in our city. This was a despicable act. It was not only an attack on the individuals who wore the police uniform, but it was an attack on our symbol of justice."

The attack broke out Jan. 27 around 8:30 p.m. when police attempted to break up an unruly crowd outside a shelter for migrants near Times Square.

Rep. Nicole Malliotakis, a Republican who represents New York, has said the incident shows the issues caused amid the migrant crisis.

"If @NYCMayor wants these individuals deported, he can immediately overturn de Blasio-era laws & allow #NYPD to cooperate with @ICEgov," she wrote on X, which is the social media platform formerly known as Twitter. "#NYC has the power to end this crisis TODAY, but instead criminals are being protected & cop-beaters are walking free!"

Authorities urged anyone who has information about the suspects or their whereabout to contact police at 213-335-9040.
