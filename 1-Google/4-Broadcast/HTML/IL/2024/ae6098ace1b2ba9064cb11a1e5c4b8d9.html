Local TV stations in hundreds of cities launch on Zeam streaming app

Zeam aggregates nearly 300 TV stations’ local programming
By Gray News staff
Published: Feb. 8, 2024 at 8:10 AM CST

(Gray News) - There is now a new way to watch local news and information, including this station, on a streaming service available for free on all major streaming platforms.

Zeam aims to make a splash with an advertising campaign featuring actor and musician John Stamos that will air on about 100 local television stations, including during the Super Bowl.

In several cities, the ads will be customized for that city.

The Zeam app features local programming from nearly every city in the U.S., allowing users to check in on communities they care about from anywhere.

Zeam’s aim is “to connect every broadcaster to every viewer across the country,” said Jack Perry, Zeam’s founder. “It gives them the ability to not only reach out to the community they’re in, but also reach out to people in other markets.”

Nearly 300 local television stations from 30 different companies, including Gray Television, are included in the new app.

Perry describes Zeam as “a streaming home for in-between binge sessions to get informed an entertained through news, sports and culture.”

In addition to Gray Television, Zeam features local broadcast stations owned by CBS, Hearst, News Press & Gazette and Morgan-Murphy, among others. Together, the stations included in Zeam cover nearly 80% of the U.S.

For Stamos, becoming a spokesperson for Zeam is an important endeavor.

“We want to make sure that local news doesn’t wither away,” said Stamos. “It’s important to stay connected to where you’re from and who you are.”

The app allows local television stations to expand their coverage and connection to their communities.

“We call it ‘going beyond broadcast,’ and going beyond broadcast is getting out and covering more things,” Perry said.
