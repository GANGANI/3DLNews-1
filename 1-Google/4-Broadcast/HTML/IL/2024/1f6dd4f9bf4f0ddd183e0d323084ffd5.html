Fact Check Team: Biden trails behind Trump in latest NBC polling

by KONNER MCINTIRE & COURTNEY RAU | The National Desk

Wed, February 7th 2024 at 3:23 PM
Updated Thu, February 8th 2024 at 10:02 PM
4

WASHINGTON (TND) — New polling data released by NBC reveals a concerning trend for President Biden as he trails behind former President Trump on several critical issues.

According to the latest figures, Trump holds a 5-point lead over Biden, with 47% of respondents favoring Trump compared to 42% for Biden. This marks a significant increase from November's numbers, where Trump held a 2-point lead at 46% to 44%. Remarkably, this is the largest lead Trump has garnered across 16 polls conducted by NBC in comparison to Biden.

There are a few other key takeaways. In terms of being “competent and effective,” Biden is trailing 16 points behind Trump, which is a major reversal from 2020 when he was ahead by nine points.

On economic matters, Biden trails Trump by a substantial 22-point margin, with 55% favoring Trump compared to 33% for Biden. This represents a notable shift from 2020, where Biden's deficit stood at just seven points. Furthermore, President Biden faces deficits in other significant areas such as immigration policies (35-point deficit) and addressing crime and violence (21-point deficit).

Despite holding advantages on issues like abortion (12-point lead) and immigrant rights protection (17-point lead). Despite that, Biden's overall approval rating, 37%, is the lowest recorded by NBC since George W. Bush's second term, standing at a mere 29%.

Polling is not a perfect indicator of who could win the presidency, as a lot could happen between now and Nov. 5. However, according to a historical analysis by Gallup, incumbents with an approval rating of 50% or higher a year before the presidential race typically secure reelection.

Conversely, first-term presidents who fail to achieve that 50% threshold at any point within a year of the election, tend to face electoral challenges. Since World War II, only four presidents - Harry S. Truman, Bill Clinton, George W. Bush, and Barack Obama - have successfully won reelection after dipping below the 50% mark at least once with a year or less to go before the election.
