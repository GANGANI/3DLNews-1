Voters have more confidence in Trump over Biden when it comes to 'competency,' polls show

by KAYLA GASKINS | The National Desk

Wed, February 7th 2024 at 2:33 PM
Updated Wed, February 7th 2024 at 2:37 PM

WASHINGTON (TND) — With Donald Trump in his late 70s and Joe Biden in his early 80s, a major issue for the 2024 presidential election is whether either candidate is mentally and physically up to the job.

This week, for example, President Biden appeared to forget the name of Hamas during a press conference. Days before, he appeared to mix up France's current president with one that served in the 80s and 90s.

On Tuesday, reporters pressed White House Press Secretary Karine Jean-Pierre asked about these concerns.

I’m not even going to go down that rabbit hole with you sir," she responded.

Trump appears to have a significant upper hand on the issue.

In a new NBC News poll 48% of respondents said they see Trump as more "competent" and "effective" than Biden. Only 32% reported feeling Biden was more competent and effective than Trump. It's 16 point difference.

In a separate question, 42% of those polled said Trump was the better of the two when it comes to having the necessary mental and physical health to be president. Only 23% chose Biden.

Twenty-eight percent said neither one was mentally or physically fit to serve.

Referring to Biden, Democratic challenger Dean Phillips wrote on X, “Shame on all of you pretending everything is ok. You are leading us - and him - into a disaster, and you damn well know it."

Critics are also pilling on Trump for repeatedly mixing up Nikki Haley with Nancy Pelosi. Haley participated in a skit on this past weekend's Saturday Night Live that poked fun at the apparent confusion. Haley also launched an attack ad slamming Trump's age.

Trump has challenged Biden to physical and mental competency tests saying he already took a cognitive test and aced it.
