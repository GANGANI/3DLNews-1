Presidential candidate Tulsi Gabbard visits SE Iowa with hopes to end regime change wars

by Lynsey Whitaker

Thu, April 18th 2019 at 10:31 PM
3
VIEW ALL PHOTOS
If there is one thing the presidential candidate says she has on the entire democratic presidential lineup, it's service.

FORT MADISON, IA — Democratic presidential hopeful Tulsi Gabbard says it's time to end the regime change wars.

The Hawaii congresswoman is the fourth democratic candidate to visit southeast Iowa in just five weeks.

"Today we are at a greater risk at a nuclear catastrophe now than ever before in history," Gabbard said to a room full of people at Sub Arena Sandwich Shop in Fort Madison.

If there is one thing the presidential candidate says she has on the entire democratic presidential lineup, it's service.

During Gabbard's visit Wednesday, she spoke a lot about her service in the U.S. Army National Guard.

She served 16 years in the military and believes her service makes her the best candidate for the job.

Gabbard called President Trump's past veto on a War Powers Resolution to end U.S. support on wars disheartening. It's something she said she'll change if elected.

"To end U.S. support for this genocidal war in Yemen that is being waged right now without any congressional approval or authority." Gabbard said.

She says the war on terror is costing taxpayers trillions of dollars.

"Now there is an issue that is essential for us to address, that is central to our ability to make these kinds of investments we need to make, to serve the needs of our community and that issue is the cost of war," Gabbard said.

Investments such as healthcare, a key issue for voters.

"It is wrong that in this country that we the American people, we spend far more for healthcare than any other country,” explained Gabbard. “Yet we still have worse outcomes, and we still have far too many people that cannot get the care that they need."

Gabbard is calling for a Medicare-for-all program.

"To make sure everyone is able to get access to that basic quality level of care, regardless of how much or how little money they make,” Gabbard said.

The latest estimates from the CDC show 12.8% of Americans do not have health insurance.

U.S. Representative Tim Ryan is also expected to make a stop at the Sub Arena sandwich shop in Fort Madison on Friday.
