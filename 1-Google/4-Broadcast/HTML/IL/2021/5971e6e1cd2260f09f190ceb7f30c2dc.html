Memories of Ta'Naja Barnes live on 2 years after her death

by Jordan Elder

Thu, February 11th 2021 at 5:08 PM
Updated Fri, February 12th 2021 at 10:37 PM
5
VIEW ALL PHOTOS
Two-year-old Ta'Naja Barnes died in February 2019 due to extreme cold from environmental neglect. (Obtained from the family by WICS)

DECATUR, Ill. (WICS/WRSP) — It's been two years since the death ofTa'Naja Barnes.

The toddler suffered abuse and neglect despite the involvement of Illinois' Department of Children and Family Services (DCFS).

Her death was a catalyst in launching our Project Illinois investigation into the department.

In the days leading up to her death, Ta'Naja Barnes was cold, dehydrated, and hungry.

Those close to her say she was confident, bubbly, and radiant, and that’s how they choose to remember her two years later.

"You try to remember the good times, but when this day comes around, it's like it's just brings back everything from the day it actually happened," said Iiesha Dean, Ta'Naha's foster mother.

Dean described this day as a cloud blocking the sun, saying she can feel it weigh on her every year.

A spaghetti-loving, trampoline-jumping, happy toddler is how Dean describes her one-time foster daughter, saying that's how she chooses to remember the child.

Dean said Ta'Naja would drag a baby doll all over the house, ride around outside on her tricycle, and eat everything in sight.

"She had a smile that would light up the room wherever she was at," Dean said.

That smile is memorialized in photos and celebrated in balloon releases every few months.

The child's mother, T'wanka Davis, is serving 20 years in prison for her murder.

In July 2020, Davis' boyfriend, Anthony Myers, was found guilty of first-degree murder in Ta'Naja's death. He was sentenced to 30 years last October.

Myers is appealing that ruling.

The trials painted a picture of a brutal life for Ta'Naja.

Police found 2-year-old Ta’Naja Barnes unresponsive and wrapped in a urine-soaked blanket inside her home on Feb. 11, 2019.

Medical staff said her core temperature was so low that it wouldn't register on their device.

She died at a Decatur hospital.

The autopsy revealed she died from dehydration, malnourishment, neglect, and the cold after living a short life.

Dean said this year, she wants the narrative to be about Ta'Naja's life instead of her death.

"She'll never be forgotten," Dean said. "She will always be remembered, and we'll remember the happy times we had together."

Ta'Naja would have been 5 years old in March.

We've been following Ta'Naja's story since the day she died.
