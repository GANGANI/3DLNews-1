by KHQA

Fri, March 10th 2017 at 6:57 PM
Updated Fri, March 10th 2017 at 11:06 PM
Lovelace Retrial: Day 7 - Curtis Lovelace takes the stand

Lovelace's defense team called on another forensic pathologist today to help them convince a jury that Cory Lovelace died a natural death.

The defense's 5th witness and second forensic pathologist, Dr. William Oliver took to the stand today for about 3 hours.

He was firm in his belief that Cory Lovelace died from fatty liver disease brought on by complications of alcohol withdrawal. Oliver testified in his 30 years, he's never seen a woman as physically able as Cory be smothered.

Oliver testified if Cory had been suffocated like the defense argues, he would expect to see more injuries to her face, neck and arms as well as on the attacker.

Read Jenny's tweets from Oliver's testimony below or click here if you're on the mobile app.

It was a total moment of shock many people even gasped when the defense called Curtis Lovelace himself to the stand.

Click here to see his testimony from Thursday.

KHQA knew Lovelace was listed as a potential witness for this trial. But this was the first time we have heard him talk at any length about what happened Valentine's Day 2006.

During his nearly 3 hour and at times emotional testimony Lovelace admitted that both he and Cory were alcoholics and that he wished there was more he could have done to help her. Lovelace also clarified that he and Marty jointly decided to cremate Cory's body.

Lovelace at one point even apologized on the stand for the way he told Marty her daughter was dead. He also told both sides point blank he did not kill Cory Lovelace.

Logan Lovelace also took to the stand Thursday and testified that he specifically remembers what happened Valentine's Day 2006.

Lincoln Lovelace also took to the stand Thursday saying he also remembered seeing Cory alive.
