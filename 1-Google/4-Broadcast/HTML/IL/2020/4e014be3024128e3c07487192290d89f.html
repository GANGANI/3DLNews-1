'Red Mirage' election scenario: Trump wins by a landslide, then loses election

by KRISTINE FRAZAO, Sinclair Broadcast Group

Wed, September 2nd 2020 at 3:19 PM
President Donald Trump speaks from the South Lawn of the White House on the fourth day of the Republican National Convention, Thursday, Aug. 27, 2020, in Washington. (AP Photo/Evan Vucci)

WASHINGTON (SBG) - Two months out, before the 2020 Presidential election, and questions about voting are piling up: Will it be safe? Will it be accurate?

And is it possible that what we know on November 3 will not reflect the final outcome of the election?

These questions come after some new reporting, predicting President Trump could hold a massive lead, even double the number of electoral votes on election night, even if he eventually loses. Analysts are calling it "The Red Mirage."

"The mirage is referring to the fact that something that seems to be true right in front of your eyes is not really there," said Stef Kight, a political reporter at Axios which spoke to the Democratic data firm Hawkfish, which is funded by Michael Bloomberg.

It found this initial landslide is increasingly likely given the partisan divide over *who* plans to vote by mail. 51% of Democrats and just 20% of Republicans according to a recent poll by FiveThirtyEight.

"In one scenario that Hawkfish looked at, It could be that Trump could be winning electoral votes 408 to 130 only to see as the absentee ballots come in by the mail for Biden to win by a significant margin," Kight said.

This follows after a brand new investigationfrom the Postal Service Inspector General, which lists multiple concerns, including "out of date voter addresses" and that "ballots mailed too close to the election could result in insufficient time for the postal service to process and deliver the mailpieces."

In an unscientific study conducted by Sinclair Broadcast Group, more than a dozen stations each sent out 100 mock ballots and in many counties, all 100 were returned though in a few just 97 or 98 percent did. If that happens in the presidential election that could mean the difference between a state that’s won and one that’s lost.

The House Oversight Committee now plans to subpoena Postmaster General Louis Dejoy after he failed to produce documents due a week ago, following his heated testimony on capitol hill.

Lawmakers are issuing warnings but also trying to stay optimistic

“There are 650,000 postal workers who are committed to america who are committed to democracy who want to make sure that ballots are treated with priority and get to the local election boards in a timely fashion so they are counted," said Rep. Gerry Connolly, D, Virginia, in an interview following last week's hearing.
