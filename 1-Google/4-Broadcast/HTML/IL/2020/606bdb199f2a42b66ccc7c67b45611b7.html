Federal judge dismisses Trump campaign lawsuit over Nevada mail-in voting bill

by Matthew Seeman

Mon, September 21st 2020 at 11:37 AM
Updated Tue, September 22nd 2020 at 1:15 PM
This file image shows a lockbox that holds election ballots. A federal judge dismissed a lawsuit from President Donald Trump's campaign over Nevada's new mail-in voting policy. (KSNV)

LAS VEGAS (KSNV) — A federal judge has dismissed the lawsuit filed by President Donald Trump's re-election campaign over Nevada's mail-in voting measure.

The order comes as a blow to the Trump campaign, which had filed a lawsuit over Assembly Bill 4, passed into law by Nevada state lawmakers during a recent special session.

Under the law, all active voters in the state would receive a mail-in ballot for an election if there's a state of emergency, regardless of whether they request one.

In-person voting would still be available, both for early voting and election day.

The president has railed against the law, claiming without evidence that it could lead to increased voter fraud. His campaign ultimately filed suit over it last month, arguing the measure could disproportionately affect GOP voters by confusing them and creating a disincentive to vote.

In a motion to dismiss the lawsuit, Cegavske and the Nevada Attorney General's office responded that the campaign was pushing a political issue into U.S. courts and had no standing to bring the case.

District Court Judge James C. Mahan granted Nevada Secretary of State Barbara Cegavske's motion to dismiss in an order dated Sept. 18 and made public Monday.

In his order, Mahan agreed with Cegavske that the campaign lacked standing. He added that the plaintiffs' arguments were "too speculative" and did not establish a "substantial risk" of any alleged harm.

"Ultimately, as plaintiffs concede, they hold 'policy disagreements' with proponents of AB 4," he writes. "Although they purport to allege constitutional harms that go beyond these policy disagreements, at this juncture, plaintiffs' allegations remain just that."

Nevada Gov. Steve Sisolak, who signed AB4 into law, applauded the dismissal, tweeting Monday the measure would "expand options for Nevadans and provide for safe, fair & accessible elections during the pandemic."
