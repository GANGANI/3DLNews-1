Designers lay Easter eggs in new Jeep Renegade

by Jill Ciminillo, Auto Matters Editor

Mon, January 26th 2015 at 11:24 AM
Updated Fri, August 9th 2019 at 9:49 AM
16
VIEW ALL PHOTOS
2015 Jeep Renegade / Photo by Jill Ciminillo

If there were one thing you should know about me it's this: I'm doggedly persistent.

When I was a kid and wanted to learn something, I single-mindedly practiced until I got it right. When I was in high school and couldn't understand physics, I memorized the book. As an adult, when I have a goal, I chip away until I achieve it.

So, I should probably apologize to the folks at Jeep. When I heard that the designers had left a series of "Easter eggs" - or inside design jokes - buried throughout the 2015 Jeep Renegade, I obsessively searched every square inch of the compact utility vehicle until I found them all.

However, I discovered after the driving portion of the launch event that I missed one of the Easter eggs because I didn't drive the Renegade trim that it was in. And then I badgered three Jeep execs until I knew what it was.

At the end of the hunt, I found 14 of the fun - and sometimes funny - inside jokes. There are two recurring design themes for many of the Easter eggs - a military-inspired X and the iconic 7-slot grille.

This is the evidence of my journey, ranking from the obvious to least obvious - and coincidentally my favorite.

14. All the Renegade's doors have an image of the 7-slot grille embossed on the metal ring surrounding the speakers.

13. The military-inspired X is stamped in the plastic liner of the front cup holders.

12. The liner of the cubbyhole in front of the gearshift has a topographical map of Moab imprinted on it.

11. Circling the ignition is the phrase "To New Adventures."

10. The handle of the church key that removes the roof panels is in the shape of the 7-slot grille.

9. In the center of the X on the taillight is an image of the 7-slot grille.

8. On models equipped with Forward Collision Warning Plus, the 7-slot grille image appears on the side of the radar unit behind rearview mirror.

7. When you lift up the rear hatch, be sure to look up, and you'll find the 7-slot grille emblem.

6. The metal in the center of the headlight is in the shape of the 7-slot grille.

5. Taller drivers will be able to see the X motif on the roof, but I had to step on the doorsill to get a good look.

4. It never would have occurred to me to scroll through the menus on the behind-the-wheel gauges to find this Easter egg, so thank you to Art Anderson, chief engineer for Renegade, for pointing out that the 7-slot grille appears next to menu selections instead of a check mark.

3. If you follow the black flecks surrounding the glass on the windshield, you'll notice the little Jeep Wrangler on the passenger's side.

2. This one inside the fuel door area made me bark with laughter, not only because the spider was so unexpected but also because of the "Ciao Baby!" cartoon bubble. However, rumor has it regular consumers may miss out on this Easter egg, as Jeep execs say it was in the pre-production models only.

1. This last Easter egg is my favorite. Similar to the Jeep on the windshield, there is a Yeti climbing a mountain hidden in the black flecks on the rear window above the wiper.

I won't spill the beans on the one Easter egg I couldn't find, but I will tell you it's on the Trailhawk model with cloth seats. Happy hunting!
