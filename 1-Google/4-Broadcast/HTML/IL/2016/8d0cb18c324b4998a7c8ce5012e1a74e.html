UPDATE: Man dies while in custody of Winnebago County Sheriff deputies

By WIFR Newsroom
Published: Jul. 27, 2016 at 9:45 AM CDT

UPDATE: WINNEBAGO COUNTY, Ill. (WIFR) -- The Winnebago County Coroner's Office has identified the man who died while in the custody of the Winnebago County Sheriff's Office.

The Coroner's Office says an autopsy is inconclusive and more tests are needed to determine how 58-year-old John Heffran died.

Investigators with the Winnebago Boone County Integrity Task Force say deputies were called to a home in the 6700 block of Old River Road for a welfare check around 8:00 Tuesday night. The deputies then tried to take Heffran into custody when there was a struggle. We're told Heffran became unresponsive and he was taken by ambulance to a local hospital where he was pronounced dead.

The Coroner's Office says the results of toxicology tests are expected to be back in the next few weeks. The death is under investigation by the Winnebago Boone County Integrity Task Force.

-----

Posted: July 27, 2016

WINNEBAGO COUNTY, Ill. (WIFR) -- The Winnebago Boone County Integrity Task Force is investigating a death in rural Rockford.

According to the commander of the task force, Illinois State Police Sgt. Sam Thomas, the incident happened just after 8:00 p.m. Tuesday in the 6700 block of Old River Road.

Sergeant Thomas says deputies were called to a home for a welfare check. That's where they found a 58-year-old man. Deputies were attempting to take the man into custody when there was a struggle. He fell unconscious and was taken to Rockford Memorial Hospital where he died.

The man's identity isn't being released at this time. An autopsy is expected later Wednesday.
