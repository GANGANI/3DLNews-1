New leads in search for Lost Boys of Hannibal

by KHQA Staff

Fri, February 11th 2022 at 12:12 PM
Updated Sat, February 12th 2022 at 3:58 PM
16
VIEW ALL PHOTOS
Screenshot of video showing the three boys from Hannibal, Missouri who went missing in 1967. (Screenshot of KHQA video)

HANNIBAL, Mo. (KHQA) — The Missouri Geological Survey is in Hannibal Friday and Saturday to examine a newly discovered entry to the Lost Boys Cave near Lover's Leap.

Billy and Joey Hoag and Craig Dowell, went missing in 1967.

Authorities, friends and loved ones think they might've gotten lost in a cave while exploring one.

Lost Boys of Hannibal podcast co-host Chris Koetters has been examining and reporting on the mystery for three years.

A property owner near Lover's Leap recently discovered a new entry and reached out to Koetters.

It just so happens to be located 40 yards away from where the boys were last seen in 1967.

The Missouri Department of Transportaion then started working with the Missouri Geological Survey to search and investigate the newly discovered entryway.

No matter what geologists find or discover, Koetters is committed to uncovering every stone and clue to help find closure to the 55-year-old mystery.
