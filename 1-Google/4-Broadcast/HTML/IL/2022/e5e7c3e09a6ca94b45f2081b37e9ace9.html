Daniel Winn

Anchor

Daniel Winn is an evening anchor at WGEM News.

He grew up in the Central Illinois town of Casey. Moving to Quincy was more than a career move for Daniel, it puts him closer to family, and he's thrilled to be back in the Land of Lincoln.

Throughout his career, he has spent more than 14 years in TV and radio. It has taken him to Kansas, Oklahoma, Missouri, and most recently, Iowa.

As a reporter, his stories have been honored by the Kansas City Press Club, and his breaking news coverage of a massive warehouse fire in St. Louis garnered an Emmy nomination.

Daniel spent six years in Columbia, Missouri, and his last role at KMIZ was co-anchor of the station's 5p.m newscast. During his time in Mid-Missouri he field-anchored and reported from the riots in Ferguson, Missouri, following the shooting of Michael Brown. He also covered the release of Ryan Ferguson who had his murder conviction overturned, after spending ten years in prison. The story made national headlines and Daniel reported live from the Missouri State Penitentiary and Boone County Jail where he was eventually released.

Daniel's next stop took him to Tulsa, Oklahoma, where he co-anchored the morning and midday news at KJRH, Channel 2. It was in Tulsa where he became more acquainted with wall-to-wall severe weather coverage. During his nearly two years in Tulsa, there were numerous severe weather events, but the most memorable was a tornado that hit Mid-Town Tulsa, about a half a mile from his station. He was one of the first reporters on scene to see windows blown out of office buildings, and trees toppled on to cars. While in Tulsa, he also did a story about squatters’ rights and exposed loopholes in state laws that were allowing squatters to live in luxury homes for free.

Throughout his career he has covered five governors and presidential visits from former Presidents Donald Trump and Barack Obama, as well as former Vice President Mike Pence.

Most recently, Daniel spent the last four years as an anchor at KWWL, the NBC affiliate in Cedar Rapids, Iowa. He routinely interviewed Senators Chuck Grassley and Joni Ernst and Congresswoman Ashley Hinson. In 2020, a derecho, also known as an inland hurricane hit Cedar Rapids. It caused billions of dollars worth of damage, which meant it was a prime time for crooked contractors and tree trimmers. Daniel caught a Texas group posing as an Iowa company, using their good name to drum-up business. He worked with one homeowner who had been overcharged thousands of dollars for a single tree removal. After confronting the Texas group and getting the State Attorney General's office involved, the homeowner got nearly half of her money back.

When Daniel isn't at work, he's probably spending time with his family. His daughter, Caroline keeps him busy with soccer and softball games. His family has always had Golden Retrievers, but the newest K-9 member of their family is an English Cream Labrador named Maxine. If you see them walking around town, be sure to say, hello!
