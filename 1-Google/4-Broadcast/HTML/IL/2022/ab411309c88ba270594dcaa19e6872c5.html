Despite rumors, there aren't litter boxes in QPS bathrooms

by KHQA Staff

Fri, September 9th 2022 at 4:33 PM
Updated Sat, September 10th 2022 at 8:33 PM
3
VIEW ALL PHOTOS
FILE Quincy Public School sign near the entrance of the building on Aug. 11, 2021. (KHQA/Kendall Hyde)

QUINCY, Ill. (KHQA) — There are no litter boxes in Quincy Public Schools' bathrooms

That's the message from Quincy Public Schools, QPS, Superintendent Dr. Todd A. Pettit, who sent a statement to parents to address rumors that QPS had installed litter boxes in restrooms.

"This is simply not true," Pettit wrote.

Dr. Pettit said rumors about litter boxes in school bathrooms are part of a national trend he called a hoax.

RELATED: Despite rumors, students aren't dressing up as cats, requesting litter boxes

"Almost every rumor includes the same core proponents: children identifying as animals, usually cats or dogs, and schools allowing those students to use litter boxes," Dr. Pettit said in the note.

Dr. Pettit shared his message on Friday:

QPS Families,

In the last few weeks concerns have been raised through rumors that QPS has installed litter boxes in restrooms. This is simply not true. Let me share the history behind this hoax.

School districts across the country have responded to false social media posts that administrators have installed litter boxes in restrooms for students who identify as animals. This hoax began circulating nationally online during the 2021 - 2022 school year. School superintendents in multiple states have issued statements dispelling the hoax allegations. Almost every rumor includes the same core proponents: children identifying as animals, usually cats or dogs, and schools allowing those students to use litter boxes.

QPS administration responded to similar rumors last spring when this hoax began circulating on social media. As we have received a few inquiries from parents in the last few weeks, I wanted to set minds at ease that we do not have litter boxes in any of our school restrooms. Thank you for sharing this with your family and friends as we strive to dispel these false claims. Your support allows our educators to focus on supporting your children to achieve their personal excellence.

Yours in education,

Dr. Todd A. Pettit, Superintendent
