2023 top stories across central Illinois

SPRINGFIELD, Ill. (WICS) — This year we had many stories from shootings to deadly car crashes.

We take a look at a few of the biggest stories of 2023.

EMS workers facing first-degree murder charges

On Dec. 18, 2022, EMS responded to the 1100 Block of North 11th. Officials say Earl Moore Jr. was hallucinating due to alcohol withdrawals. EMS workers from LifeStar Ambulance Service, Peggy Finley and Peter Cadigan, arrived at the home.

Moore strapped face down and put in the ambulance. Moore was pronounced dead at HSHS St. John's Hospital at 3:14 a.m. Officials said Moore died of compression and positional asphyxia.

Both Finley and Cadigan are facing charges of first-degree murder for their role in the death of Moore.

While this happened in 2022, Finley and Cadigan were charged in 2023.

22 staff members at Graham Correctional Center hospitalized

On Jan. 18, 2023, 22 staff members, and three inmates at Graham Correctional Center in Hillsboro were hospitalized due to an unknown substance.

The incident started when the initial responding officers found two inmates running into the walls and being combative. While correctional officers were cuffing the two inmates, a third inmate began having a seizure.

ISP said the substances tested returned results for over-the-counter items:

  • Nasal Spray was Acetaminophen/Paracetamol
  • Powder was baby powder – Aluminum Phosphate, Ethylpyrrole, and Benzene

Everyone involved in this incident has been discharged from the hospital.

8 dead, 37 injured, in 72-vehicle crash on I-55

On May 1, 2023, eight people died, and 37 people were injured in a 72-vehicle crash on Interstate 55.

The crash was caused by excessive winds, which blew dirt from farms across the highway leading to zero visibility on the road.

The crash happened on both I-55 South and I-55 North from milepost 72 -80 near Divernon.

The eight victims of the crash were Ruth Rau, 81, Otto Medina-Salazar, 58, of Carthage, M, Amy and Michael Zinchuck, of Champaign, and Shirley Harper, 88, of Franklin, Wis., Joseph Bates, 73, of Crystal Lake, Ill., Donna Bates, 71, also of Crystal Lake, and Earl LeGrand, 64, of Florissant, Mo.

Derecho that hit central Illinois left 40,000 without power

On June 29, several rounds of severe storms passed through Illinois producing heavy rainfall, flash flooding, hail, tornadoes, and straight-line winds.

Central Illinois experienced a derecho that brought damaging straight-line winds resulting in five confirmed tornadoes, along with wind gusts in excess of 100 miles per hour across multiple counties.

The tornados and straight-line winds resulted in large debris fields, affecting homeowners, businesses, utilities, and local governments. The storms also caused disruptions to transportation due to flash flooding and debris on the roadways.

City, Water, Lights, and Power says at their peak 40,000 customers in Springfield were without power.

On Nov. 29, Governor Pritzker issued a disaster declaration for Christian, Hancock, McDonough, Sangamon, and Washington counties.

Emma Shafer murdered, suspect still on the loose

On July, 11, 2023, Sangamon County Dispatch said they received a call from a woman in Bethalto stating that her brother possibly injured his ex-girlfriend, Emma Shafer, who lived in Springfield.

Springfield Police responded to the 1000 block of S. 8th St. to check on the welfare of Shafer, where police found her deceased with multiple stab wounds.

Former Sangamon County State's Attorney Dan Wright said an arrest warrant was issued for Gabriel P. Calixto, 25, in connection with the death of Shafer.

The warrant charges Calixto with three counts of first-degree murder and aggravated domestic battery with a bond of $3 million.

Springfield Police said they are actively searching for Calixto with assistance from the U.S. Marshals Service and additional agencies.

Carlinville Funeral Home under investigation following multiple identification issues, treatment of remains

On Sept, 29, 2023, The Sangamon County Coroner along with the Sangamon County Sheriff opened an investigation into the practices of Heinz Funeral Home/Family Care Cremations in Carlinville, Ill.

Jim Allmon, the coroner for Sangamon County, said the Heinz Funeral Home is under investigation following multiple incidents.

Allmon said that on September 25th, a local hospital called his office and notified them that a deceased woman had been at a hospital for around a month. Allmon notified the family.

Allmon said investigations have led to uncovering more instances where Heinz Funeral Home provided families ashes of a loved one even though a cremation never occurred. They’ve discovered that medallions that help identify loved ones weren’t accurate.

Allmon says he’s been in contact with families who've been affected by Heinz Funeral Home.

“We've probably contacted at least 70 or so that have had the wrong cremains," Allmon said.

Truck crash kills 5, seriously injures 5, and ammonia leak forces hundreds to evacuate

On Sept. 29, 2023, a truck tractor hauling a cargo tank semitrailer with over 7,000 gallons of anhydrous ammonia tried to avoid a crash with two other vehicles on the road at the time and drove into a ditch in Teutopolis.

During the crash the tank struck a trailer parked on the side of the road which punctured the tank, releasing the chemical into the air.

Hundreds of residents had to be evacuated due to the leak, and five people died from exposure to the gas including a father and his two children.

Seven others were severely injured and treated at local hospitals.

On Sept. 30, 2023, an armed Camren Darder, 17, died after being shot by a Springfield police officer at the Sangamon County Juvenile Center.

We're told the Darder was an inmate of the Sangamon County Juvenile Center, had a firearm, and tried to leave the facility.

Darder fired four shots inside the building and took a female inmate hostage.

Two Springfield officers made a plan to enter the building. One officer was a new recruit, who was being trained. The other was a veteran officer who is a member of the emergency response team who was familiar with active shooter training, said Scarlette.

The veteran officer went to the trunk of the squad car and grabbed a department-issued AR-15.

ISP trooper who was shot identified, suspect arrested

On Oct. 24, 2023, Springfield police and other departments responded to the 1600 block of Toronto Road to reports of an officer shot.

Cristobal Santana is accused of shooting Trooper Dakotah Chapman-Green during a traffic stop in Springfield.

Santana fired at least 10 gunshots hitting Trooper Dakotah "Kody" Chapman-Green several times, and struck him repeatedly causing facial fractures, a skull fracture, and a brain bleed.

Responding officers gave medical aid on the scene until first responders transported the officer to a local hospital. Santana fled after the shooting but was taken into custody at 1:58 a.m.

Trooper Chapman-Green is now out of the hospital and is recovering from his injuries.

Santa is facing two counts of Attempted First Degree Murder, one count of Aggravated Battery with a Firearm to a Police Officer, one count of Aggravated Battery to a Police Officer, and one count of Aggravated Unlawful Use of a Weapon.

Santana will be back in court on Feb. 20.

Santana is also a suspect in a Chicago homicide. He is suspected of shooting and killing his ex-girlfriend on Oct. 22.

Mother, two daughters killed in Ashland shooting

On Dec. 14, 2023, two teenagers and an unborn child were among the victims of a pair of shooting incidents in Cass County.

Multiple law enforcement agencies were dispatched to the 300 Block of Philadelphia Road (Rural) Ashland, Illinois for a male who had been shot.

Upon arrival, Cass County Sheriff’s Deputies and Ashland Police Department located a male with a gunshot wound and a deceased female at the residence.

A preliminary investigation identified a suspect and a suspect vehicle.

A short time later, the Jacksonville Police Department located the suspect, Ronald Cobren, vehicle near North Main and Oak Street in Jacksonville and conducted a traffic stop on Cobren's vehicle.

Cobren was located inside the vehicle with an apparent self-inflicted gunshot wound. The male suspect was pronounced deceased on-scene by the Morgan County Coroner’s Office.

The surviving male victim was transported to an area hospital with life-threatening injuries. The investigation is still active and ongoing.
