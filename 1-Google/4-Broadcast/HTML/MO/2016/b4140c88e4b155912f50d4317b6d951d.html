Boone County Sheriff's Department warning of deputy impersonation scam

by Dan Ebner

Mon, April 18th 2016 at 12:48 PM
The Boone County Sheriff's Department is warning citizens about a scam where people are posing as sheriff deputies. File photo.

BOONE COUNTY, Mo. — The Boone County Sheriff's Department is warning citizens about a scam where people are pretending to be sheriff deputies and coning people out of money.

Detective Tom O'Sullivan said the department has received several calls saying someone is calling claiming to be a sheriff's deputy and that there's a warrant out for that person's arrest. The scammer goes on to say the arrest can be taken care of by making a payment over the telephone.

The department says citizens are being told over the phone they have warrants for failure to appear for jury duty and they need to obtain a Green Dot card or a similar product with a certain amount of money.

The scammer then directs the citizen to call back to a specific number and provide the information from the money card.

Detective O'Sullivan said several people have been fooled into turning over hundreds of dollars.
