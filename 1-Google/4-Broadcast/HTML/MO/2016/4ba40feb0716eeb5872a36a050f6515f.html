The News-Leader’s Answer Man: Building Near I-44/Hwy 65

by: Staff

Posted: Jan 22, 2016 / 06:48 AM CST

Updated: Jan 22, 2016 / 07:22 AM CST

by: Staff

Posted: Jan 22, 2016 / 06:48 AM CST

Updated: Jan 22, 2016 / 07:22 AM CST

SHARE

Here’s a question sent my way twice – by Steve Barrow of Strafford in May and by Ralph Satterfield of Buffalo this month. Barrow already knew who owned the property.
What’s the large building under construction northwest of the intersection of Interstate 44 and Highway 65?  You can see it best when southbound on 65. 

Gentlemen, it’s a home being built by Lawana and Robert Low.
Robert is the founder and president of Prime Inc. trucking company. 

“We own a little more than 300 acres where  we raise cows and horses,” he said, via email.   “We call it ‘Primatara,’ the stable name we use for our thoroughbred racing and breeding operation.  As you know, we’re just outside the city limits and very convenient to Prime’s terminal.   Makes for a nice short commute for me in the mornings!”

The entrance gate to Primatara is off of Farm Road 94.
“Completion of the house is 2-3 years  away.  The architecture is a collaborative effort by Slone Architects, Springfield, and Richard Davis, Dallas, Texas.  Killian Construction is the general contractor and several local sub-contractors are involved.  Killian and most of the subs are folks that we’ve worked with on Prime’s projects over the years. 

“We hope to create a wonderful, environmentally friendly, private residence that my wife and I and our family can enjoy for many years.  We truly want the farm and residence to be a beneficial and attractive addition to the community. … That’s about all I can share with you for now.”

More information on the house is available from building permits on file with Greene County.
According to those plans, the house has an estimated value of $20 million and will have 26,500 square feet on two stories, which does not include a 16,540 basement and two garages – one 3,897-square-feet and the other 3,653.

Some of the indoor amenities include a pool, spa, gym, bowling alley and shooting range.
Already constructed is a wine cave with ceiling heights ranging from 9 feet to 14 feet. Within the cave, according to the plans, are a wine-storage room, a tasting grotto, a wine-tasting display area and a dining room.

According to the Prime Inc. website, Low founded the company in 1970 in Urbana, Missouri as a 19-year-old engineering student at the University of Missouri. He moved the business to Springfield in 1980.
The Lows are also known for their philanthropic work in the community. 

In 2013, Robert stepped in and bought the historic Gillioz Theatre a day before it was slated for auction.  He purchased the theater and entered into a lease agreement with the nonprofit Springfield Landmark Preservation Trust, the previous owner.

In 2006, the Lows donated $1 million toward the construction of the John Q. Hammons Arena at Missouri State University.

The Springfield Business Journal published a story about Primatara  back in 2001.  Plans had been in the making since 1995 for a horse farm, according to the story.

In 2001, structures already built included a huge hay and equipment barn; a covered bridge; a well house; a man-made lake; an exercise ring for horses; and a stable to house 25 Kentucky thoroughbreds. 

The story stated the Lows, at the time, were building a 4,000-square-foot home. That house exists today.
