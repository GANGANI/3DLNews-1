    14-year-old dead 1 week after DUI crash involving 7 teens in Fairfax County, Va.

    by JeanneTyler Moodee Lockman & Caroline Patrickis

    Mon, November 27th 2023 at 4:01 PM
    Updated Tue, November 28th 2023 at 7:04 PM
    5

    14-year-old Kevin Landaverde Guardado died after being involved in a DUI crash in Fairfax County, Va. Nov. 20, 2023 (Credit: Ana Landaverde)

      TOPICS:

      DUI crashFairfax CountyTeensVehicleKevin LandaverdeDriverArrestedInvestigation

    FAIRFAX COUNTY, Va. (7News) — A 14-year-old is dead a week after a single-vehicle DUI crash in Merrifield, Virginia on Monday, Nov. 20, a source told 7News.

    The crash occurred in the 2600 block of Pioneer Lane when a young driver lost control of an SUV while going up a hill at a high speed, according to Fairfax County Police officials.

    The car hit a tree before going into a tailspin, officials said.

    Officials determined that five teens were thrown from the vehicle during the crash.

    Kevin Landaverde died after being involved in the crash, his mother Ana Landaverde told 7News Monday.

    His mother said he was an eighth grader at Longfellow Middle School.

    Another teen who was seriously injured had to be pulled from the vehicle, officials said.

    The other teens involved were between the ages of 14 and 17 years old.

    Four of the teens remain hospitalized, and one was released, officials said Monday.

    The driver – who was also underage – was not injured, and was arrested for driving under the influence, according to officials.

    Charges against the driver are pending as the investigation continues.

    A community member started a GoFundMe page for the family.
