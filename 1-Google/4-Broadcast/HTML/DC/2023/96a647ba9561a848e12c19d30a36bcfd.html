    Gov. Youngkin issues letter, urging full Federal return to office to reduce WMATA deficit

    by Nick Minock

    Fri, December 8th 2023 at 2:26 PM
    Updated Fri, December 8th 2023 at 6:46 PM

    VIRGINIA (7News) — Virginia Governor Glenn Youngkin is raising concerns about the Washington Metropolitan Area Transit Authority (WMATA) budget deficit.

    7News obtained a letter from Virginia Gov. Glenn Youngkin to President Joe Biden’s Office of Personnel Management. Read that letter below:

    Youngkin is urging the administration to mandate and enforce a full return to office policy for Federal employees and provide guidance to WMATA to right-size its service levels and costs to paying riders.

    RELATED | As Metro faces budget deficit, some ask if they're making the right cuts

    Youngkin told the OPM Director that federal government employees must return to office work to infuse needed energy into the regional economy and provide WMATA with a sustaining ridership level.

    Youngkin mentioned ridership is down 40 percent compared to 2019 levels and he says outsized operating expenses are significant contributors to WMATA’s $650 million operating deficit.

    Youngkin writes without a significant uptick of federal commuters and dramatic operational restructuring, he fears that WMATA cannot recover and a key regional asset will be lost.

    Going forward, Youngkin says WMATA must also address the issue of fare skipping.
