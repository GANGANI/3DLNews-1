    Flash floods wash out roads and bridges, damage homes, force more than 100 water rescues

    by Brad Bell, Tim Barber and StormWatch7/WJLA

    Mon, July 8th 2019 at 8:40 AM
    Updated Tue, July 9th 2019 at 1:05 AM
    8
    VIEW ALL PHOTOS
    Several active water rescues underway in Montgomery Co. after heavy volume of calls. Monday, July 8, 2019. (Pete Piringer/Montgomery County Fire and Rescue/Twiiter)

    ARLINGTON, Va. (ABC7) — More than 100 water rescues were conducted throughout the D.C. area amid Monday morning's flash flooding.

    D.C., Arlington, Fairfax and Montgomery counties all reported water numerous water rescues.

    DC Fire and EMS confirmed rescuing 15 people from their cars in high water. Six were rescued on Canal Road, NW; three at Malcolm X Avenue and South Capitol Street, SE; and three at 15th Street and Constitution Avenue, NW.

    StormWatch7: It's official. Today's rain was a record-breaking amount.

    Arlington County Fire ran 38 water rescue incidents, with 25 in Arlington County and the remainder in neighboring jurisdictions. In those incidents, at least two people were assisted in being removed from their vehicles — one in Arlington and one in McLean. Most of those people, officials said, were able to self-extricate themselves or were assisted by bystanders to safety prior to the arrival or rescue units. No significant injuries were reported in relation to the flooding.

    WATCH: ABC7’s Tim Barber show us the devastating damage in Virginia:

    Fairfax County Fire and Rescue Department responded to 55 swift water rescue calls, including multiple evacuations of people in mobile homes in the 3400 block of Moon Street in the Hybla Valley area.

    Montgomery County Fire and Rescue said it conducted 15 to 20 water rescues, with around 25 people overall rescued. Most of those cases involved people needing to be rescued from their vehicles. Overall, they responded to around 40 to 50 calls for possible water rescues, though at least some of those people managed to self-rescue or didn't assistance.

    WATCH: ABC7’s Horace Holmes with what you need to know about flooded cars:

    Specifically in Montgomery County, first responders performed water rescues and safely removed people from at least five rescues from vehicles stranded in high and moving water on Clara Barton Parkway near the Beltway.

    The driver of a vehicle was removed during a water rescue at Barnesville Road and W. Old Baltimore Road. Fire officials say the driver was not injured.

    Two other water rescues were reported on 10300 Logan Drive nears Falls Bridge Lane and Brickyard Road and Coach Street, both in Potomac.

    A water rescue also took place for a person on the roof of a car stranded in high water on 10300 Logan Drive.

    Montgomery County firefighters also responded to a vehicle in high water at Brickyard Road and Coach Street.

    Crews responded to a water rescue after a vehicle was stranded at Quince Orchard Road and Wonderview Way.

    Montgomery County Fire officials say another vehicle was in high water and a rescue is taking place at Turkey Foot Road and Falling Leaf Drive.

    According to Montgomery County Fire, it received around 250 calls for service between 7:30-10:30 a.m., when it usually receives around 350 in a 24-hour period.
