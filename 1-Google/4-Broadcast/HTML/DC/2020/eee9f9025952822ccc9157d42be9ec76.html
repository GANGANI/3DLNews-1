    Virginia rescue group saves dozens of dogs from shelters hit hard by impacts of COVID-19

    by JAY KORFF, WJLA

    Thu, April 2nd 2020 at 11:21 PM
    Updated Thu, April 2nd 2020 at 6:09 PM
    6

    Dogs saved from shelters in South Carolina impacted by COVID-19 make it to Lost Dog and Cat Rescue in Falls Church. (Photo: WJLA)

    FALLS CHURCH, Va. (WJLA) – The unfettered joy at Lost Dog and Cat Rescue in Falls Church is boundless if you ask Riley and Ollie: two dogs plucked from certain death just days ago from a shelter in Georgia.

    Executive Director Barbara Hutcherson says shelters across the country with few resources are euthanizing animals at alarming rates due to wide-ranging impacts of COVID-19. And her rescue foundation is stepping in to help.

    Hutcherson says, “No more room. They don’t have the resources. They don’t have the staff and so they had no choice but to euthanize over space."

    To thrive in these challenging times, Lost Dog and Cat Rescue reworked many of their policies to protect staff, volunteers and prospective pet owners from COVID-19. The first thing they did was put out an emergency foster push to free up kennel space. The community responded by taking in most of their animals. They had a record month in March of adoptions too.

    “Absolutely amazing in the middle of this crazy pandemic. If there’s something to smile about that’s it,” says Hutcherson.

    Jessica and Josh Greenberg of Arlington got George on Saturday. He was also saved from a shelter in Georgia.

    Jessica Greenberg says, “He’s really helping us get through this time.”

    But temporary became permanent when the family adopted George.

    “Once we met him it was like instantly I thought to myself I can’t say goodbye to this guy,” says Greenberg.

    With plenty of room in their kennels Lost Dog and Cat reached out to Save Them All Pet Transport. They found great dogs at multiple shelters in South Carolina this week and drove through the night to Northern Virginia.

    Wednesday night, the transport team pulled into Lost Dog and Cat Rescue's lot in Fairfax County with some two dozen delighted dogs.

    Hutcherson says, “Us getting, taking these 25 means they get a second chance and we hope they are going to love Virginia.”
