    Trump one-on-one: Masks, monuments and promises for a second term

    by Leandra Bernstein

    Wed, July 1st 2020 at 7:48 PM
    President Donald Trump joined "America This Week w/ Eric Bolling," Wednesday, July 1, 2020. (Sinclair Broadcast Group)

    WASHINGTON (Sinclair Broadcast Group) — President Donald Trump joined "America This Week" host Eric Bolling for an exclusive one-on-one interview covering his response to coronavirus mask mandates, social unrest and his bid for a second term in office.

    As more states implement mask mandates to combat the growing number of coronavirus cases, President Trump has offered conflicting advice. He continued to deliver the mixed message Wednesday.

    "Do what you're supposed to do and also do what makes you feel good," Trump said.

    Masks have been proven effective in helping to reduce the spread of COVID-19 but they remain politically controversial, mostly among conservatives. President Trump has rarely been seen in public wearing a mask and has often criticized his political opponents for covering their faces.

    "I have no objection to masks whatsoever," Trump said. He explained that he rarely wears on because as president, "I have a different kind of life." Everyone around him gets tested and he said he typically keeps his distance from people.

    "But if I needed a mask, if I was in a crowd...I have no problem with a mask at all," Trump continued.

    The president ridiculed his Democratic rival, former Vice President Joe Biden for wearing a mask at unnecessary times, like before giving speeches. "When there's nobody around, I don't see any reason to be wearing it," Trump said.

    Biden has called Trump a "fool" for not wearing a mask in public and recently said he would make face masks mandatory if he were president.

    The Centers for Disease Control and Prevention recommends wearing a mask anytime social distancing is not possible.

    Amid a nationwide push to remove Confederate and other monuments from public spaces, Trump has taken an aggressive stance against the destruction or removal of monuments. Last week, he signed an executive order relying on a statute that would result in a ten-year prison sentence for anyone who intentionally destroyed a federal monument.

    "If I weren't here, all of Washington would have been knocked down. That's what would have happened," Trump said.

    Since the order took effect, there have been fewer instances of federal monuments being defaced. "It's been very powerful because people don't want to go to prison for ten years for knocking down a statue," the president continued.

    Trump has tried to position himself as a "law and order" candidate while making inaccurate claims about his Democratic rival. On Wednesday, Trump argued that Biden wanted to "abolish police."

    "He wants to defund and abolish police because that's what he's being told to do," Trump said of Biden.

    Biden publicly distanced himself from calls to defund the police in early June, saying he supported policing reforms but not cutting off funding.

    Trump argued that "the radical left" is making political decisions for Biden. "They're telling him what to do," the president claimed.

    Throughout the interview, Trump swiped at the Democratic candidate, including taking the opportunity to question his mental fitness.

    "He's mixed up," Trump said of Biden, who was recently asked if he has been tested for cognitive decline. Biden has said he looks forward to testing cognitive abilities against Trump.

    National polls have shown the president trailing his Democratic rival by a wide margin. Media reports have suggested the president is slipping because of his response to widespread protests and calls for racial justice as well as his response to the coronavirus pandemic.

    Comparing recent polls to those in 2016 that showed him losing the election, Trump argued, "They're suppression polls. They're meant to dampen the enthusiasm of your voters of your people because we have tremendous enthusiasm."

    Trump also attacked the media as "fake" and "corrupt," saying his only recourse is to appeal to his supporters online. "All I can do is fight it on social media," Trump said.

    Trump is seeking a second term amid massive economic losses and historic levels of unemployment. Asked what his second term would look like, Trump told Bolling, "It's very simple. We're going to make America great again."

    The president said his second term would look similar to his first. He emphasized economic development, jobs and trade deals, like the U.S.-Mexico-Canada Agreement that went into effect Wednesday.

    "Our country will be so strong at the end of our first term," Trump said. "At the end of the second term it's going to be at a level that nobody will have ever seen the country."

    Before the coronavirus hit, the strong economy had been a key selling point for the Trump campaign. The White House has claimed the economy will rebound quickly, though many experts project it will take years to recover.
