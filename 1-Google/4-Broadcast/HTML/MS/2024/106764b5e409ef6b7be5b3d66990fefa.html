News 11 speaks with Congressman Guest about problems at the southern border

By Ross McLeod
Published: Feb. 7, 2024 at 9:24 PM CST

MERIDIAN, Miss. (WTOK) - The United States Senate failed to pass a border security and foreign aid bill on Wednesday, meaning still no resolution for problems at the southern border. Congressman Michael Guest says the people who live on the border are in need of help.

“And I’ll tell you that everybody we’ve had the chance to meet with has told me, particularly those who are lifelong residents of the border. They told me two things. One is they feel like they’ve been abandoned by the federal government, and they’ve also said that the situation on the border is worse than they’ve ever seen it in their lifetime,” said Representative for Mississippi’s 3rd Congressional District Michael Guest.

The federal government is working to create legislation.  The House of Representatives passed a border protection bill in the spring of 2023, but it has not been voted on by the Senate.  The Senate attempted to pass legislation but failed to get the proposed bill out of its own chamber.

“The total cost of the bill was $118 billion, of which only a fraction of that, a small fraction of that, actually went to securing the board. So, I’m glad to see that the bill is not going anywhere in the Senate. What I would like to see if the Senate takes up HR to take up the bill that Republicans sent to them. Almost a year ago, if they don’t like the legislation, amend the legislation, debate the legislation, send it back to the House, and let’s see if we can work out any agreements that we may have, but for the leader of the Senate just to say this, the bill is never going to see the light of day over here. We’re not even going to discuss it. And by the way, we’re going to now blame Republicans for the crisis. I think that’s hypocrisy,” said Guest.

Guest says that it’s not the legislative bodies that are to blame but rather President Biden and Secretary Mayorkas. Tuesday, the House even went as far as attempting to impeach Mayorkas but was one vote shy.

“Leadership is trying to determine when Leader Scalise will be back in DC, but we are led to believe by Speaker Johnson and his team that there will be another vote on this vote, at which times Leader Scalise will be present and his vote last night would have made the difference,” said Guest.
