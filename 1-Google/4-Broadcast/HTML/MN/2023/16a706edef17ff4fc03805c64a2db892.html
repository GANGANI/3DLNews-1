November 30 Recipe: Bacon Egg Bake by Diane Gohl

12 eggs                                                                      Beat eggs with milk, salt & pepper, then lightly scramble (Grease baking dish)

1/2 cup milk                                                            Add scrambled eggs to dish. Spread sour cream over eggs as evenly as possible.

salt & pepper to taste                                             Sprinkle on cheese and crumbled bacon. Cover and refrigerate overnight.

8 oz. sour cream                                                      Next morning bake at 300 degrees F for 20 - 25 minutes.

12 slices crisp crumbled bacon

1 cup shredded cheese

December 4 Recipe: Cherry Cookies by Karen Walker

1 Cup Butter                                   Cream butter & brown sugar, Beat in egg and vanilla

1 Cup Brown Sugar                        Stir in flour, baking soda, baking powder and salt

1 Egg                                                  Add coconut and mix well.

1/2 tsp. Vanilla                               Stir in nuts, cherries and cherry juice.

2 Cups Flour                                    Drop by teaspoon about 2 inches apart onto ungreased cookie sheets.

1 tsp. Baking Soda                          (Could use parchment paper)

1/2 tsp. Salt

1 Cup Coconut

December 5 Recipe: Salted Caramel Pretzel Bark by Wendy Young

12 oz. bag of Semi-sweet Chocolate Chips              Line a large cookie sheet with parchment paper and set aside

8 oz. Pretzels                                                                 In a microwave safe bowl, melt the chocolate chips in the microwave for 30 second increment and stir in between each time.

11 oz. bag of Kraft Caramels                                      Spread about 2/3 of the melted chocolate on top of the parchment paper. Top the chocolate with pretzels. Gently press pretzels onto chocolate.

2 Tbsp. Heavy Cream                                                 Melt the caramels with the heavy cream in a microwave safe bowl for 2 minutes. Stir until smooth.

Sea Salt for sprinkling                                                Drizzle the caramel evenly over the pretzels. Drizzle the remaining melted chocolate over the caramel.

Sprinkle with the flaky sea salt. Refrigerate until set, cut into pieces and serve.

Mix: 1/2 cup melted Butter

2 cans Cream of Chicken Soup

16 oz. Shredded Cheddar Cheese

12 oz. Sour Cream

Chopped Onions & Peppers to taste

Salt & Pepper to taste

Spread over Hash Browns

Bake @ 350 degrees for 1 hour

December 7 Recipe: Pot Luck Fruit Salad by Dorothy Heinz

8 oz. Cream Cheese (softened)                                          Mix 1st 4 salad ingredients and chill several hours or overnight

12 oz. Whipped Topping                                                     Bake butter and other dry ingredients at 350 oven for 7 to 12 minutes.

1/2 cup Powdered Sugar                                                     Watch closely; cool and cover.

20 oz. Pineapple (crushed) slightly drained                  Combine gently the two mixtures just before serving, add extra fruit.

Add Red Grapes and Blueberries as desired                 Creamy mixture keeps well in refrigerator for 5 days. Butter mixture also keeps well.

1/2 cup Butter                                                                      The salad can be made ahead.

1/2 cup Powdered Sugar

1/2 cup Chopped Pecans

1 cup Pretzels (chopped, not too fine)
