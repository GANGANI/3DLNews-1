Rampant crime threatening Baltimore County businesses: 'Elected officials need to do something'

by GARY COLLINS | FOX45 News

Tue, October 10th 2023 at 5:32 PM
Updated Wed, October 11th 2023 at 11:29 PM
6
VIEW ALL PHOTOS
Lil Cakes and Creamery's manager Paige DeAntonio told FOX45 News she is concerned about rises in juvenile crime in the area. (WBFF)

WHITE MARSH, Md. (WBFF) — With juvenile crime continuing to surge across the region, the Baltimore County business community is worried about its future.

As part of an ongoing survey, FOX45 News has interviewed several businesses in Owings Mills, Towson and White Marsh about their top concerns.

Regardless of the product or service provided, crime and safety fears were outlined by business owners and employees alike.

Emily Winters, the Executive Director for the Northwest Chamber of Commerce (NCC), told FOX45 News she has been fielding constant complaints about juvenile crime from businesses in Reisterstown, Owings Mills, Glyndon and Pikesville.

Many businesses are complaining about looting, homeless people sleeping in front of [their] store [or] vestibules [and a] lack of response for crime calls,” Executive Director Emily Winters told FOX45 News in a survey response.

According to a study recently published by Newmark Research, commercial leasing activity in the Baltimore region has slowed to historic lows.

Leasing activity in the [Baltimore] market remained slow, with the first half of 2023’s leasing activity being the lowest on record since 2007,” according to a recent Newmark Research publication on commercial leasing trends.

While Newmark Research suggested uncertain economic outlooks and higher interest rates are driving this trend, NCC’s Executive Director Emily Winters pointed to crime.

Several anchor stores are so fed up, they are looking to relocate,” Emily Winters explained to FOX45 News. “[T]heir employees’ safety and constant lack of sales [and] security [is] pushing them away.”

Despite the Baltimore region following national trends of decreased crime in some categories, juvenile delinquency and vehicle thefts are seeing triple-digit surges.

In January 2023, Towson Town Center experienced a large disturbance of unruly teenagers, prompting Baltimore County Police to deploy K-9 and aviation units to disperse the crowd.

Weeks later, a social media post called for an organized meetup at White Marsh Mall. The invitation called on attendees to “run from the police” and “don’t get locked up."

As a preventative measure, a heavy police presence was successfully deployed to White Marsh Mall to thwart the potential juvenile unrest.

Across the street at The Avenues at White Marsh, Lil Cakes and Creamery manager Paige DeAntonio told FOX45 News she has personally experienced safety concerns.

Having personally relocated from western Maryland, the 23-year-old retail worker said she does not feel safe locally.

It’s a lot of the areas, it’s not just here. People have been bringing guns,” Paige DeAntonio shared with FOX45 News.

While she expressed gratitude for her shopping center’s security team, DeAntonio explained she is frequently worried.

Coming here, coming to Baltimore, it’s like a completely different world, I feel like," DeAntonio explained to FOX45 News. “You always hear something, I’m always seeing stuff.”

DeAntonio said that despite noticing an uptick in shopping center visitors this summer, the increased foot traffic has not translated into increased sales.

Instead, DeAntonio said she and her co-workers have had to stop juveniles from stealing from the small business. As a preventative measure, she said the store has placed their baked goods behind glass.

People are crazy. The crime is really bad,” Paige DeAntonio shared with FOX45 News.

Nancy Hafford, the Executive Director for the Towson Chamber of Commerce, points to thefts and robberies as a problem all consumers should be worried about.

As consumers, everything is getting so experience,” Nancy Hafford explained to FOX45 News. “The cost of theft and robberies are increasing the cost of goods.”

As a veteran business leader in Towson, Hafford blames recent revisions to the state's juvenile crime laws as the primary threat to the business community.

They [juveniles] cannot be arrested. The worst consequence that can happen to them is you can call their parents," Nancy Hafford described to FOX45 News.

Hafford's belief is the only way to avoid what she described as a business crisis is a holistic approach that immediately brings together community leaders to revise the state's laws.

They [state legislators] need to bring the police department, school resource officers, the social workers, and the state’s attorneys to the table and holistically figure out how to come up with a solution,” Towson Chamber of Commerce’s Nancy Hafford said to FOX45 News. “They all have to be there.”

Lil Cakes and Creamery’s Paige DeAntonio shared Hafford's vision, joining a growing call on elected officials to solve the area’s juvenile crime dilemma.

The elected officials need to do something about crime,” Paige DeAntonio demanded to FOX45 News. “They talk a lot, but what do they actually do about it?”

If you are a Baltimore area business owner, FOX45 News invites you to take the Baltimore Business Crime Survey.
