Camilla woman voices concern as more Autry inmates test positive for COVID-19

by Simone Jameson

Wed, May 6th 2020 at 11:13 PM
Updated Wed, May 6th 2020 at 8:00 AM
Photo by: Simone Jameson

    TOPICS:

    CamillaSandra LaraI (Almost) Got Away With ItPrivate Prisons In The United States

"I don't want my brother to get this virus. I know that a lot of inmates have already tested positive, and that worries me a lot," said Sandra Lara.

As COVID-19 continues to spread, she worries about the safety of her brother, who's serving time at Autry State Prison.

"I remember in about December, he got real sick, to the point where I thought he either had pneumonia, or bronchitis," added Lara. "He was sick for about three weeks."

At least 68 inmates at Autry State Prison have tested positive for COVID-19, as well as four staff members, according to the Georgia Department of Corrections. At least 33 inmates have recovered, but Lara is troubled when she thinks of her brother.

"I've just gotten so many stories from him...from my brother, about what goes on in there, and it's horrible." she added. "He doesn't deserve to be in there. He's not a violent person."

FOX 31 News reached out to the Department of Corrections for an interview, but the request was denied. The DOC sent an email that no one would be made available for interviews at this time, but said that staff is focused on the safety and welfare of the inmate population.

Changes include enhanced sanitation at all entry points, and two sneeze and cough masks given to all staff and offenders. Visitations are suspended until May 13th. These are among a list of measures on the Georgia Department of Corrections' website.

Lara anxiously awaits the day she and loved ones will see her brother again.

"He's loved by so many. His kids, my mom, my dad...I love him. I'm like his mom. He's such a lovable person everywhere he goes...everywhere."

They pray that neither the virus nor prison time will take that opportunity away.

"He'll take the shirt off his back to give to anybody. He does it in there. He cooks in there, they all come looking for him," Lara went on to say. "Before this happened, I could visit every other week. It's just hard. It's hard in there."
