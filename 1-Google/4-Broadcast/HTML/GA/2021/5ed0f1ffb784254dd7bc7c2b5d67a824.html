Former WFXL, WALB Meteorologist Chris Nisinger dies at 49

by FOX 31 News

Tue, August 24th 2021 at 3:51 PM
Updated Thu, August 26th 2021 at 5:24 PM
10
VIEW ALL PHOTOS
Photo: WFXL

Chris Nisinger, a former meteorologist for nearly 20 years in southwest Georgia, died Tuesday morning at the age of 49.

Chris joined the FOX 31 Weather Team in October of 2009 and forecasted the weather at the station until November 2017.

In 1998, Chris joined the WALB weather team before making his way down the street a few blocks to FOX 31.

His delivery and on-air presence helped many people get out of danger in some of the worst storms to hit Southwest Georgia.

Current and former WFXL employees have taken to social media to share their love for Chris.

WFXL Station Manager Deborah Gay:

Chris Nisinger was a dedicated and fun part of our work family for 8 years. He was always willing, or maybe not :) , to participate in some silly morning shenanigans for the sake of the show. He loved his job as a meteorologist and did it very well. We all have a heavy heart today, but know that he is in a better place. Prayers for his family.

Former Good Day Morning Show Reporter and Anchor Doug Reardon:

Chris Nisinger was a massive part of our FOX31 family, and a mentor to so many young journalists. Away from our homes and friends and loved ones, Chris filled the void in family that many of us were lacking in our first television jobs and never failed to make you feel welcome and warm. Chris never let you forget about your best accomplishments and was always the first to tell you to shake off a bad day or a bad show. He knew life was far too short to dwell on things that didn’t make you happy. On birthdays and times of celebration, Chris was typically the first person to knock on your door or offer up a call or text that always made you feel loved and seen. Since leaving Albany I’ve kept in touch with Chris - wishing now that I had reached out more. But the resounding images I’ll keep of Chris in my memories will be the sleepy early morning laughs we all shared in the dark studio before the morning shows, the wry half-smile that never left his face, his willingness to go to any lengths to cheer you up or make a 1 a.m. shift a good time, and our mutual love for the place and the industry that brought us together. I’ll miss my friend dearly and I send my sincerest condolences to his son and his family. I hope you know that we consider Chris very much a part of our families as well and we grieve with you.

Former Good Day Morning Show Anchor Kelly D'Ambrosio:

My heart is broken to hear about the passing of my friend and former coworker Chris Nisinger.

Our team was a little family with inside jokes and feelings and hardships only the other one could understand.

We came in at 1:30AM every day and put on a 4 hour live morning show. You can't do that without being a family.

Chris was genuine. He was always up for a good time. He brought a lot of laughter to us especially in times where we really needed some laughter. He was a great sport when we would make him do things for the show he wasn't quite thrilled about (like demonstrating the Gangham style dance for everyone.)

I'm so thankful for the years we had together and all I learned from him.

Former Good Day Morning Show Reporter Sasha Lenninger

My heart hurts. Chris was not only my first newsroom dad, but he was also a good friend. We were a little family, cranking out a 4 hour morning show in Albany, starting our shift around 1:30. From the stories we shared, the crying laughter and to several of our bad haircuts, we were always happy. One happy family.

My best memory of him, screaming and running in the opposite direction when the zoo brought in an owl. Little did we know, Chris didn’t like owls LOL!

I’m so thankful for the laughs we shared and the years we had together. He taught me so much. Chris, you are missed dearly.

Former Good Day Producer Clay Poulnott

You will be missed Chris! It’s a very sad day for all of us that knew and got to share the studio with you. We created some great memories that I and the rest of us will never forget. RIP my man. I’ll be cheering on the other Bulldogs for you down here.

WFXL Digital Content Manager Kyle Proctor

My best memories of Chris will always be walking into the studio Monday morning after college football aired over the weekend to see what his reaction would be and what funny remarks he had for the die hard Alabama and LSU fans at the station, especially if they lost!

If it wasn't football, Chris had an appreciation for classic rock and not a week could go by that there wouldn't be a conversation about Rush with him. Those are the times with Chris that I will always cherish.
