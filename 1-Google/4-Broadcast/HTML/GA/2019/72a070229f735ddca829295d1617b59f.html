UPDATE: Albany firefighters say cause of fire is unknown

by FOX 31 News

Sat, November 23rd 2019 at 8:31 PM
Updated Mon, November 25th 2019 at 3:27 PM
An investigation is underway to determine the cause of a fire at an Albany comic book store. / Kerri Copello

UPDATE: According to Battalion Chief James Brown, the Albany Fire Department has determined that the fire that occurred at Phoenix Comics on Friday evening was unknown and cannot be determined what started it. Brown says this is no longer an active investigation.

ORIGINAL:

An investigation is underway to determine the cause of a fire at an Albany comic book store.

Around 7:15 p.m. Friday, Albany firefighters and Dougherty County EMS responded to a fire at Phoenix Comics, located in the shopping plaza in the 1100 block of North Westover Boulevard.

Upon arrival, flames and smoke were visible; the fire quickly extinguished.

According to Battalion Chief James Brown there were no customers inside at the time of the fire, the store was closing.

Brown adds that the damage contained to the back area of the store.

The Albany Fire Department is investigating the cause.

This is a developing story; stay connected to FOX 31 News on-air and online as more information becomes available.
