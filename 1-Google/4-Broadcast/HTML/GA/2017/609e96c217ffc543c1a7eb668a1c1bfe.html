GBI warns of dangerous counterfeit street pills

by Jazmyne Hankerson

Sat, May 27th 2017 at 10:19 PM
4
VIEW ALL PHOTOS
An alert from the Georgia Bureau of Investigations is warning people about harmful street drugs./ Courtesy: GBI

An alert from the Georgia Bureau of Investigations is warning people about harmful street drugs.

The GBI said since January 2015, its crime lab has gotten 454 counterfeit pills.

These pills are marked to look like Xanax, oxycodone, or other depressants and opiates, but actually have more dangerous drugs in them such as fentanyl.

In 2017 so far, there were eight fentanyl, six furanyl fentanyl, and 15 U-47700, also known as pink pills, that officials found disguised as non-transdermal drugs.

"We've never seen before these types of concoctions with the counterfeit pills you would see other types of drugs in there that would essentially not be consistent with what’s on the imprint but these days there putting all kinds of dangerous drugs in them," said GBI Director of Public Affairs Nelly Miles.

The GBI reports that most cases of these case have been found in the in Metro Atlanta but there have been a few finds in Dougherty, Lee and Crisp counties.

Miles said if you know anyone who may be selling these or have them in their possession to contact authorities immediately.
