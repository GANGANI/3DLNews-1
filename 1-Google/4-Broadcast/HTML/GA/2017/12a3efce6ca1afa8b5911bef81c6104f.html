ASU student speaks out publicly about his battle with cancer

by Kailey McCarthy

Tue, October 24th 2017 at 12:56 PM
Faculty and students stood around to hear breast cancer survivors tell their stories of survival.

Photo: Kailey McCarthy

Albany State University students, faculty and staff were invited to come out and partake in a Breast Cancer Awareness Ceremony on Monday.

Breast cancer survivors were recognized for their bravery and also shared their stories of survival.

ASU student Charles LaPrince had the room in tears when he shared his emotional story about facing cancer for the first time publicly.

LaPrince is still battling his cancer. He has already had eight rounds of chemotherapy and is heading towards remission.

According to LaPrince, it's all about staying positive and having the right mind set.

"I think that one thing that Leslie Charles who we worked together here at Albany State University she always says, I chose life. And when I woke up every morning I had to keep in my mindset that I choose life. My parents taught me at a young age to dress nice every day and never forget your bible scripture."

Participants who came to the Breast Cancer Awareness Ceremony formed a human breast cancer ribbon to honor survivors and to promote the significance of finding a cure.
