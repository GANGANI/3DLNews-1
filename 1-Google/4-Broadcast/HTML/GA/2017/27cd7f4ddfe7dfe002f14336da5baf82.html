Martinez neighbors unite in support of crash victims

By Ben Billmyer
Published: Jun. 6, 2017 at 12:03 AM EDT

News 12 NBC 26 News At 11 | Monday, June 5, 2017

MARTINEZ, Ga. (WRDW/WAGT) -- Folks from across the Martinez community come together on a dark and dreary Monday night to shine a light and pray for healing and a full recovery after six children are injured in a drunk driving crash.

The accident happened Friday afternoon on North Belair Road near the intersection at Columbia Road. Six children were in the car not wearing seatbelts when it happened; two of them remain in critical condition .

The driver of the car, 46-year-old Tara McConnell is facing multiple charges including DUI, failure to yield turning left, reckless driving, six counts of child restraint requirements, six counts of DUI child endangerment, six counts of serious injury by vehicle. Investigators say she had a blood-alcohol level of .461, nearly six times the legal driving limit.

Neighbors braved rainy skies and fears for six young children. Those dark clouds and fears were not enough to dim the light coming from the front of Martinez Elementary Monday night.

The harmonies of "Amazing Grace" and "It Is Well With My Soul" filled the doorway to the school while candlelight filled the area. Jaime Nairn, a 5th-grade teacher at the school, spoke about their hopes for two children laying in hospital beds instead of at home.

"The doctors may say that Violet will not walk again, but God has the ultimate decision on that," Nairn says to the crowd. "Arwen may not be conscious right now, but she will eventually wake up because we believe she will."

Friends and family gathered in support of each victim from last week's car crash; 11-year-old Preston McConnell, 11-year-old Jeremiah Elliot, 11-year-old Madeline Elliot and 10-year-old Matt Bargeron.

The crash left 11-year-old Arwen Bargeron and her 6-year-old sister Violet fighting for their lives. Keisha Lavergne-Lewis drives buses for Columbia County... and says Arwen is close friends with her son Dominic.

"It's heartbreaking, it's very heartbreaking," Lavergne-Lewis says. "I've cried for them. I feel for the parents. And the only thing I can do is think about what if that was mine, what if it was mine in there?"

The vigil comes just a few hours after the driver, Tara McConnell, was locked up for DUI among other charges. Parents are left wondering why she picked up the keys and put six children, including her own son, at risk.

"What could possibly go through your head to make you put those children in your car and drive off with those children," Lavergne-Lewis says, "especially while you were drinking? That's the only thing that goes through my mind is why?"

As the families continue to grieve, Lavergne-Lewis has one message for the driver.

"If she's seeing this, you can see the support that everybody has for these children, see the support that we have for your child," Lavergne-Lewis says. "Just think and be remorseful, just be remorseful for what you've done."

The group behind the vigil raised $554, which is going back to the families to help them get through this time. They're also raising money to help buy violet a wheelchair as they pray for her and Armen to come home from the hospital.
