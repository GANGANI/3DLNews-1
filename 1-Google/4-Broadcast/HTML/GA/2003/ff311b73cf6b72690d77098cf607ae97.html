WTVM Celebrates 50 Years on the Air

Published: Oct. 1, 2003 at 2:18 PM EDT|Updated: Oct. 7, 2003 at 2:01 PM EDT
Did you know...
On October 6, 1953, WTVM made history. It was the first station on the air in Columbus, Georgia. It was UHF back then. In fact, it was Channel 28. Back then, everything was live, even commercials. And newscasts were only 15 minutes. In 1956, the Martins took over full ownership of the TV station. It became WTVM, which stands for Television Martin.

Remember the Milton Berle Show, Dragnet, and Lawrence Welk...all national programs carried on WTVM, in the beginning. There were also a lot of live, local programs, including the "Popeye" show with broadcaster Tom Hipps dressed as a sailor. Audiences enjoyed musicals on WTVM and shows with clowns or a children's theme. The most popular was "Miss Patsy's Playhouse," which aired throughout the 1960's.

Join us for a week long look back at 50 years of television history, a look at the present and the opportunities of the future during News Leader 9 at 6. Then, October 6th, join us at 7 eastern for an hour long special called "First Then First Now."
