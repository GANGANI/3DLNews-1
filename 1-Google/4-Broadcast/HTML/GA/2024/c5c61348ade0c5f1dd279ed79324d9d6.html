2 children die in Meriwether County house fire, mother hospitalized, officials say

By Atlanta News First staff and Asia Wilson
Published: Feb. 4, 2024 at 7:57 AM EST|Updated: Feb. 4, 2024 at 11:42 PM EST

ATLANTA, Ga. (Atlanta News First) - Two children died, and their mother was hospitalized with severe burns after a house fire broke out early Sunday morning in Meriwether County, officials said.

The Meriwether County Emergency Management Agency (EMA) said they received a call around 3 a.m. for a house fire on Ira Parks Way in Manchester, with victims possibly trapped inside.

A girl died at the scene, according to the agency.

Officials said two victims, a mother and son, were rescued and taken to the hospital.

The boy was pronounced dead on arrival, said Meriwether County EMA Director Brin Jones.

The mother was taken to the hospital in critical condition to be treated for severe burns, Jones said.

The children’s family identified them as Brionna Childs and Breon “BJ” Childs, both 10 years old.

“The house caught on fire right in the room where she was, and the little boy, which is Breon, he was on the bed with his mother,” said Stanley Childs, the children’s grandfather.

Stanley said he rushed to the home and saw it in flames.

“I’ll remember that for the rest of my life, the way that house was burning, knowing my grandbaby, my granddaughter was still in there,” Stanley said.

“They are some very intelligent children. I mean, they honor-roll A plus students. They weren’t identical twins, but paternal twins, boy and girl… very mannerable in everything,” Stanley said.

The children’s grandmother said the twins would have turned 11 on Feb. 15.

“We just need prayer. Like I say, they can’t be replaced. My babies are gone. They gone,” said Trudie Childs. “I’m just asking the community, just pray. Give us strength, Lord. Pray for my son, pray for their mother. She down there fighting too, Jesus.”

Jones said the children’s father was not home at the time. He rushed to the house after a neighbor called him about the fire, and he arrived around the same time law enforcement did, Jones said.

“My son, when he got there, he snatched the air condition out of the window and his girlfriend’s arm was hanging out the window, and he just pulled her arm out and then the little boy, he got him out, but there was no movement or nothing from neither one of those,” Stanley said. “But the little girl, they couldn’t get to her.”

Stanley said his son cut his hands and feet trying to rescue everyone.

“He broke windows trying to get them. You know, he tried everything in his power to try to get them,” Stanley said.

“They lost everything. It’s not a good thing left. Everything just burned. Everything. Even burned her car up that was in the driveway,” Stanley said.

Atlanta News First also spoke to a woman who sent us videos of the fire. She said the children would always come over to play with her grandkids.

“My grandkids play with them after school sometimes, you know, we see them in the yard playing. They come over here playing. Those were just like my little kids. It’s just a tragedy,” Victoria Favors said. “It’s so sad to me right now. I feel like I have lost a family member.”

Favors said someone in her home called the kids’ father to tell him his home was on fire.

“Just to see them go together. They came together and they left together. It’s so sad. It breaks my heart. Like I said, I don’t know if this street will ever be the same because when I walk out my door, I see them. I see them playing in the summertime. I see them coming over here playing with my kids. They were wonderful kids. Had a great mom and a great dad,” Favors said.

The cause of the fire is under investigation, but officials said they haven’t found anything suspicious at this time.

The State Fire Marshal’s Office has been called in to investigate because of the fatalities, Jones said.
