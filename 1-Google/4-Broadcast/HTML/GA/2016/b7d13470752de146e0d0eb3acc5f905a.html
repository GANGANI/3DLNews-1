Southwest Georgia News Roundup: ‘Drive-Thru’ drug operation busted in Albany

January 12, 2016 Staff Reports Around our Area, News 0

ALBANY, GA (WALB) –
Police say they have arrested one man, pulled in a large haul of illegal drugs, and are looking for another man, after a raid at the Super 8 Motel Friday night.

Albany-Dougherty Drug Unit agents say they busted a drug dealing operation that was working out of the window of a Northwest Albany motel.

Agents arrested 19-year-old Franklin Lawson Friday night, and seized from inside the room more than a pound of marijuana, ecstasy, and oxycodone pills, worth about $13,000 on the street.

“There was a hole in the window, where potential patrons or customers could approach this window from the bottom floor and purchase any type of drug that you needed,” said ADDU Commander Major Prurince Dice.

Daytime car thefts plague downtown Thomasville

THOMASVILLE — Two people who worked at the same business went to lunch in separate vehicles and returned to work about the same time.

When the employees left work that afternoon, they discovered their unlocked vehicles had been entered and contents stolen.

A purse valued at $80 was taken from one vehicle.

The console was broken in the other vehicle. Apple headphones valued at $50 and $3 in cash were taken from the console.
