Suspected human smuggler involved in crash that killed 8 identified as 17-year-old boy from Honduras

by WOAI Staff

Mon, November 13th 2023 at 8:17 AM
Updated Mon, November 13th 2023 at 3:02 PM

Eight people, including four migrants are dead after a major accident on Nov. 8 off U.S. Highway 57 in Zavala County. (PHOTO: Lt. Chris Olivarez/Texas Department of Public Safety)

ZAVALA COUNTY, Texas (WOAI) — The latest on last week's high-speed human smuggling chase that turned deadly in Zavala County.

Texas Department of Public Safety officials confirmed that the suspect smuggler was 17-year-old Luiz Alfredo Mencias-Escobar from Honduras, who was living in Houston.

The deadly crash happened around 6:30 a.m. on Nov. 8 off Highway 57 between La Pryor and Batesville.

RELATED: Eight dead, including four migrants after high-speed chase ends in horrific head-on crash in Zavala County

Eight people died in the crash, including four migrants and the suspected smuggler.

Authorities said that Zavala County Deputies were chasing a suspected smuggler. The smuggler had tried passing an 18-wheeler in a no-passing zone and slammed head-on into an SUV killing Jose and Isabel Lerman from Georgia.

A GoFundMe page was set up to help the family pay for the unexpected funeral costs.
