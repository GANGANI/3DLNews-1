Son Surprises Mom After Boot Camp

By Catherine Van
Published: Dec. 20, 2015 at 11:26 PM PST

SPARKS, NV - A local mother who thought her son was in boot camp for Christmas gets the surprise of her life and it was all caught on camera.

From the moment Theresa Cox's son, Private Noah Vigue, left for boot camp in South Carolina, all she could do was wait and imagine all the dangers he was up against.

"'My son is going to be playing with grenades!'" she said.

With rare 10-minute phone conversations and snail mail, there was barely any interaction with her son.

"They get their internet taken away, they get their phones taken away from them, we used to talk all the time and now, pfft, no more."

"She's been so upset that he's been gone. Really just broke her heart," said Gabbi Vigue, Noah's sister.

Because of work, Cox had to miss his graduation in November. It was then that her children began planning the perfect homecoming.

"They didn't tell me he was coming home. All I knew is that he got time off work to get his wisdom teeth removed and he was going to stay in the barracks," said Cox. "I'm getting text messages from him, 'how are you son' 'I'm in pain mom this hurts.'"

It was just a little white lie, conjured to give their mother the best Christmas surprise.

"I ran into the house and said 'mom someone just rear-ended my car. It was a hit and run. She was like 'did you call the cops, did you get a description?'" said Gabbi.

When Cox rushed outside to check the damage, out pops her son.

"I was not expecting you at all. It was a big boo you're the boogey man in a green outfit that came from behind the car," Cox said.

A mother reunites with a her son, without the puffy cheeks, just in time for the holidays.

"It's the best Christmas ever!"
