WEATHERWATCH
Clubhouse destroyed in Sparks fire at D'Andrea Golf Club
by
Wednesday, Oct 28th 2015
SPARKS, Nev. (MyNews4.com & KRNV) -- A community that used to surround a golf course has now lost the only reminder that the green ever existed. People living near D'Andrea in Sparks woke up to smoke and flames coming from the clubhouse at about 2:30 Wednesday morning.
ADVERTISEMENT


Firefighters responded to neighbors' 9-1-1 calls, but within minutes of arriving, the flames were too intense to fight the fire from inside the building. "Within about 10 minutes, 15 minutes or so, we had to get out because there was pretty heavy fire in there," Sparks Fire Department's Public Information Officer, Bob King, said. "It started venting through the roof and it was too dangerous for us to be in there."


Jeff Hanson lives in the area. He watched before dawn as the fire developed from smoke to a near explosion of flames. "There was no saving it then. In the first 20 minutes of the fire you could tell it looked like it wasn't going to make it," he said.


Crews eventually used a tractor to demolish the backside of the clubhouse, allowing firefighters to fight hot spots still smoldering inside. Much of the building is scorched and collapsed, but King said what's left standing will probably have to be demolished because it's structurally unsafe.


Desert landscape replaced the golf green, after the owner stopped maintaining the course in 2012. The clubhouse used to be the center of the D'Andrea community, but the Sparks Fire Department said it is now a total loss.


"Green grass. Incredible view. Beautiful restaurant up here. It was one of the nicest places around," community board member John Rhoads said.


With their golf course gone and their clubhouse now charred, many neighbors are worried about the future of the backyard. "It's sickening. It's a beautiful building," Jeff Hanson said. "We were able to see it a little bit inside and it's a gorgeous building. I would hope that something could come back to replace it at some time, but my fear is that it's not."


The vacant clubhouse has been a frequent site of vandalism, prompting some neighbors to believe the fire was arson. "We did hear that there has been activity around this area," Bob King of the Sparks Fire Department said, "kids around the area and stuff."


One homeowner captured surveillance video of people heading down to the clubhouse right before the fire started. The video also shows six people running back up the hill. The Sparks Fire Department is investigating the video. They are also looking for a white four-door Ford pick-up truck and a brown sedan that were in the area at the time the fire started.


Anyone with information about the fire is encouraged to contact Secret Witness at (775) 322-4900.





SPARKS, Nev. (MyNews4.com & KRNV) -- The project managers for the D'Andrea winery and activities center project believe that the fire at the former D'Andrea clubhouse this morning was most likely arson-related.


A statement from project managers Randi Thompson and Steve Trollope calls this a huge setback in the process of revitalizing the clubhouse.


READ the full statement below:


"We are devastated and frustrated over the destruction of the clubhouse at D'Andrea that occurred earlier today and according to media reports is most likely arson-related. We, and the current property owners, have spent considerable efforts and monies over the past several months to clean up the property and repair the considerable damage done by vandals, in anticipation of re-opening the clubhouse in the coming year.


"These numerous repairs have included:


August: replacement of a number wood panels that had been ripped off existing doors and windows, to prevent vandals from entering the clubhouse

Early September: repaired a large hole in the exterior clubhouse walls that vandals created to allow them entry into the clubhouse (photos attached)

Mid-October: the entire grounds were cleaned up and repainted the back of the clubhouse to remove extensive graffiti (photos attached)

Mid-October: repaired an exterior metal sliding door into the cart storage room that vandals had damaged to gain access to the clubhouse

After these repairs were made, the clubhouse property was secure and clean and we were in the process of ordering video equipment to monitor and capture the people who have continually been vandalizing the building


"This fire is a tremendous set back to our efforts to revitalize the D'Andrea clubhouse and grounds for the entire D'Andrea community. The revitalization project encompassed establishing a winery with vineyards, the creation of an events center, as well as creating "community focused" programs that would support the local D'Andrea community (community gardens, hiking trails, biking trails and an area for a community recreation/pool).


"We talked to a winery owner last week that is interested in relocating to Reno and was anxious to tour the D'Andrea clubhouse. We have met with Urban Roots, a local community organization that educates children on gardening, to establish a satellite operation that would serve the citizens of Sparks. Additionally, a catering company was lined up to take over the food and beverage operations for the events center. All these efforts are now on hold as we determine the best way to move forward on the project.


"We hope the individuals responsible for this terrible incident are found quickly and prosecuted. Their actions have created a tremendous loss to the residents of D'Andrea and Sparks community, and we are deeply saddened by what has happened."

SPARKS, Nev.- The Sparks Fire Department is investigating the cause of a fire Wednesday morning that burned down a clubhouse at the D'Andrea Golf Club.

Officials responded to a call around 2:45 A.M. from a nearby resident saying there was smoke coming from the clubhouse.

Sparks Fire Department's Public Information Officer, Bob King, told News 4 they have obtained video from a nearby residence that shows two vehicles, a white Ford 4-Door pickup and a brown Sedan, leaving the scene of the fire after 6 people were seen near the clubhouse.

The clubhouse has collapsed and officials consider it a total loss. The building was abandoned and the golf course has been closed since 2012.

There are no reported injuries.

King also says this is the largest structure fire the city has ever seen.

All local fire stations are stepping in to help cover for Spark Fire as they are still in active fire fighting mode.

Investigators are looking into the cause of the fire.
