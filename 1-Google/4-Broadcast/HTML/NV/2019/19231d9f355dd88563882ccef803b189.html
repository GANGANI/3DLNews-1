It takes a village: The secret to what could be the safest school in Reno

by Kim Burrows

Thu, April 25th 2019 at 2:46 PM
Updated Fri, April 26th 2019 at 7:46 PM
BrookfieId School

    TOPICS:

    ArticlesBrookfield, ConnecticutComputer Network SecurityComputer SecurityCrime preventionCryptographyInformation GovernanceLaw enforcement

Jason Patterson wanted to make his daughter's school safer. It already had some security measures in place, but he knew it needed more. So he spend $30,000 of his own money to increase the security.

"This is the life we live in now, and if we're not taking these kinds of steps, then we're already behind," Patterson said.

Brookfield School is on S. McCarran near Lakeside in southwest Reno. The private school has been at that location for 40 years.

"Safety is a real issue for all our parents," Katie Osgood-Johnson said, the Director of Brookfield School. "With some of the past school violence, I'd say over 15 years ago, we started with the simple fencing around the property."

Over the years, the school has increased its security little by little. Today, it's the tightest it's ever been. And it's probably one of the safest schools in town.

This is the life we live in now and if we're not taking these kinds of steps then we're already behind --Jason Patterson

A fence surrounds the property. When visitors approach the front gate, a camera will greet them. It has a motion detector to alert staff inside. It can also warn if a car sits in the parking lot for an extended period of time. Visitors have a call button to the front office to be let onto the campus.

Once inside, anchors secure the doors if there was an active shooter or a suspicious person on campus. The metal brackets attach to the doors and secure into the ground to stop the door from opening and to keep the intruder out.

Special film covers the classroom windows.

"What this prevents is someone from punching or popping out the glass and reaching in from any point and opening the door from the inside," Patterson said.

There's also gunshot microphones on campus. The devices can detect the sound of a gunshot and alert either the school staff, the security company or the police.

"It is layering security, layering measures so no one solution is one you rely upon," Patterson said.

The PTA and the school paid for the security upgrades at Brookfield. Patterson was able to help with $30,000 worth of equipment and instillation because he's the owner and president of Martin Ross and Associates, a security company.

Brookfield's Director said they've found a balance between feeling safe and feeling welcome.

"I think there is an over-zealousness that can cause students and teachers stress and that's not the point of coming to school," Osgood-Johnson said.
