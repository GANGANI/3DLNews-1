Police Report: Man who crashed into Pitt Grill told police someone else was driving

By Brooke Buford
Published: Apr. 25, 2018 at 11:54 AM CDT

We're learning new details about an accident on April 17 when a car crashed into the Pitt Grill restaurant on North MacArthur Drive.

News Channel 5 has obtained the accident report filed by the responding officer from the Alexandria Police Department that evening. According to witness reports, the man's vehicle went airborne, hit another vehicle in the parking lot, and then hit the restaurant. The driver of the car has been identified as Jason Kirkland.

Witnesses told police that Kirkland had fled the car after the accident and tried to get away before a person eating inside caught him and brought him back to the scene.

Kirkland told police while he was being treated at Rapides Regional Medical Center that he was the passenger in the vehicle and that "Tori was driving."

Kirkland told police that the two were driving on North MacArthur Drive "when he realized that he had somewhere to go." Kirkland said "he then climbed into the driver's seat with Tori and when he got into the driver's seat his foot hit the accelerator causing him to lose control of the vehicle."

When the officer asked Kirkland where Tori was, he said that he didn't remember. Kirkland told the officer that "he wasn't sure what happened he just hoped no one was hurt."

Kirkland was released on a citation. Witnesses told police that they never saw a second person inside the car and that only Kirkland climbed out of the vehicle.
