How Easy Is It For Kids to Buy "Mature" Video Games?

Published: Feb. 14, 2006 at 2:45 AM CST|Updated: Feb. 15, 2006 at 12:26 AM CST

Video games bring in billions of dollars of revenue each year, and sex and violence help them sell.  So how easy is it for your kids to get their hands on those games meant for adults only?  You may be surprised.  9 News put some Baton Rouge video game stores to the test in an undercover investigation.

The video game world has taken on an edge.  Profanity, obscenity, violence and sex have all seemed to find a place in some of the more popular games, like "Grand Theft Auto: San Andreas."  In that game, players stab prostitutes, hijack cars and gun down police officers.  Stores are also selling games that play off of sex, like "The Playboy Mansion" or "Leisure Suit Larry."

The Entertainment Software Rating Board rates almost all games, and those ratings are clearly labeled on the front of game covers, with a "mature" rating for players 17 years or older.  But how easy is it for a kid under 17 to get a mature game?  9 News put some local video games stores to the test.

We sent a 15-year-old boy to see if he could buy a mature game.  He visited several stores, where clerks repeatedly asked him for his I.D., and ultimately denied to sell him the game.  That's when we sent in two nine-year-old girls to the same stores to see how they could do.  Some stores still refused to sell them the games, but when we sent them into a Blockbuster video store on College Drive, but the girls were able to buy a mature video game -- no questions asked.

As the girls came out, our 9 News crew went in to ask the clerk a few questions.

Reporter:  "Are you aware you just sold this game to a nine-and-a-half-year-old girl?  (Pause) Anything you have to say?  (Pause) Do you normally do this?"

The clerk refused to answer any of our questions and eventually kicked the 9 News crew out of the store. The game the girls bought was clearly marked "M" for mature.  Blockbuster issued a statement saying it strictly enforces its ban against renting mature games to minors, and says it's investigating our report the the policy was violated.

Reporter:  Matt Clough
