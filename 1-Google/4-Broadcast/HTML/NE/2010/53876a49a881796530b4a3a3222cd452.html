Big Picture: Dome Home in Clay Center

Tue, May 11th 2010 at 9:28 PM
12460599_BG1.jpg

This week in our Big Picture report, NTV News is taking a look at some rather odd homes throughout central Nebraska.

Back in March, on our Facebook page, we asked for your suggestions on which homes to visit. We wrote a list and narrowed it down to three.

Our first stop was Clay Center.

We head out to the junction of Highways 14 and 41 in Clay Center to a home that's the talk of the town.

Chris Eads, the clerk of Clay Center, said, "They call it the snow cone houses. The igloo houses. The snowball houses."

Eldon Shetler was happy to show off the dome home he's spent the past five years building along side his bride, Grace.

There are three bedrooms, three baths, a library and what will be the great room complete with a winding staircase and balcony. It all adds up to 2,500 square feet not including Eldon's shop.

He said it was after he retired that he decided to build a new home.

"But, I didn't want to build with stick. So, we were looking around and my wife was very adamant. She was agreeable except she did not want to live in a cave," Eldon said.

Grace said, "I had seen a home similar to this on HGTV and so we started looking into it."

The home on TV belonged to Lloyd Turner, an architect. The Shetlers went to northern California to see it and figure out how to build it.

"When we came home, why we had in mind what we wanted to design and this is basically what we came up with," said Eldon.

There are seven domes. The one in the back will serve as a hangar for the airplane Eldon also recently built.

Grace said, "The runway is going to go on the west property line, so, our neighbors think that's okay."

In their 70s, the husband and wife team did the math and most of the labor themselves to create each elliptical made out of concrete and polyurethane foam for insulation.

"We didn't build it this way just to be different," said Eldon. "We built it because I liked the challenge."

While some might say they're living in a bubble, the Shetlers say they wouldn't have it any other way.

"Yes, we definitely know it's a little different," said Grace. "But, I like it that way."

Reporter's Notes by Jessica Phinney:

The Shetlers hope to finish construction and move in within the next few months.

NTV News asked how much this is going to cost them. Eldon replied he hasn't added up all the
receipts yet.
