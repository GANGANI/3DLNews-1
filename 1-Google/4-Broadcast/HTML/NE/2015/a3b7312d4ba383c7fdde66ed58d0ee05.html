Study Calls Out Sunscreen Brands With "Inferior Sun Protection"

By 10/11's Natalie Micale
Published: Jun. 2, 2015 at 8:03 AM CDT

With the summer almost here and pools now open, many of us are thinking about sunscreen. New research shows some of the sunscreens we use may not be getting the job done.

Wearing sunscreen can prevent painful sunburn, reduce your risk of skin cancer and prevent or delay wrinkles and other signs of aging.

As an important part of your skin regimen, when your sunscreen doesn't work, you're skin is left vulnerable.

Recently the Environmental Working Group a non-profit research organization based out of Washington D.C came out with their sunscreen guide for 2015.

In the study the EWG notes "persistent problems with the ingredients and the marketing of sunscreens" on the American market. They say 80% of the 1,700 sunscreen products they examined this year offer inferior sun protection or contain worrisome ingredients like Oxybenzone and Vitamin A.

They also call out major sunscreen brands, including Banana Boat, Coppertone and CVS, who score poorly in the study.

The EWG estimates that more than half of the sunscreens on the American market would not make it to store shelves in Europe.

Catherine Verplank a nurse at CHI Health, St. Elizabeth explains that putting on sunscreen half an hour before you go outside is vital for skincare. Also, it matters how much you put on, as a 1-2 tablespoons need to be put on every body part. Remember, if you sweat or swim, you'll also need to reapply sooner.

"The SPF needs to be at least 15 and SPF 30 doesn't mean it's twice as good it just means it lasts a little longer. Again, SPF of at least 15 and then reapply, reapply, reapply," said Verplank.

While some studies find some sunscreen products may differ in how effective they are, most experts agree wearing some sunscreen is better than none at all.
