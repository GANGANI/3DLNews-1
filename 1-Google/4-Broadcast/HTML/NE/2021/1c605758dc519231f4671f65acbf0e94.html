Concerts and cows: Adams County Fair brings folks together

by Steve White

Fri, July 16th 2021 at 4:57 PM
Updated Fri, July 16th 2021 at 5:38 PM
3
VIEW ALL PHOTOS
Harper and her bucket calf at the Adams County Fair 2021 (NTV News)

HASTINGS, Neb. — The Adams County Fair is known for big concerts and big entertainment and also big beef shows.

"Favorite week out of the whole year is fair time," said Mikeayla Samuelson.

They say absence makes the heart grow fonder and that's true for Mikeayla. Last year it was just her, her cattle, and the judge. Not even her fellow competitors like Lydia Schukei could share the ring because of COVID protocols.

Schukei said, "Last year I came it for 30 minutes and was gone and that doesn't county as a fair honestly."

This time folks crowd together for a place along the rails.

"It feels like fair time, it's awesome I love it," Samuelson said.

Nate Allen of the Adams County Agricultural Society said fairgrounds provide the venue but it's 4-H and Nebraska Extension that deserves credit for bringing generations together.

"It's people that are retired, many have family in it, people that at one point in their life they connected to fair. It's nice to see people back and next generation coming up," he said.

Like Harper, and 8-year-old showing a bucket calf for the second time up to seasoned exhibitors like Mikeayla and Lydia.

These 4-H members remind folks the fair is much more than concerts and carnivals.

Samuelson said, "City people from town they talk about rides and fair food but they don't take into consideration 4-Hers that put in hard work that's what it's all about for us."

"Everyone works very hard and puts time and work into this the whole year," said Claire Scheideler who is showing pigs and cattle.

The livestock arena is the focus during the day and concerts bring in crowds at night.

"Sun goes down it's really different," Schukei said.

Fair board members say those coming for concerts owe it to themselves to walk the barns, to see the animals and see the smiles.

Nate Allen said, "Agriculture's the heart and soul of Adams County and so to have an opportunity to celebrate the good work in Adams County and bring that next generation along what an opportunity."
