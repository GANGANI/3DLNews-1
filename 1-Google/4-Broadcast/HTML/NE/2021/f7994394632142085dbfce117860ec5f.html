Oh yeaaaaaaaaaaaah! Kool-Aid Days pays tribute to its inventor!

by McKenzy Parsons

Sun, August 22nd 2021 at 3:24 PM
Kool-Aid Days (PHOTO: Courtesy of NTV News)

OMAHA, Neb. (KPTM) — Every August Hastings, Nebraska hosts a festival called Kool-Aid Days to pay homage to the inventor of the drink, according to nationaldaycalendar.com.

The inventor, Edwin Perkins, invented the drink in Hastings in 1927, said the website.

He started making it as a soft drink with six flavors: cherry, grape, orange, raspberry, lemon, and root beer, said the website.

He was inspired by Jell-O and Coca-Cola, said the website.

There were problems with shipping the drinks which made the manufacturer ship it as the powder we still use today, said the website.

Nebraska's official state soft drink is Kool-Aid, said the website.


Y'all hear that "The Kool-Aid Man" started a baseball team?
He's the pitcher.

Here's how to celebrate this holiday:

  • Drink Kool-Aid
  • Try fried Kool-Aid which is pancakes made with Kool-Aid powder mixed in
  • Marinate dill pickles in Kool-Aid
  • Visit Hastings for the festivities
  • Make Play-Doh with Kool-Aid
  • Use #KoolAidDays on social media to share how you celebrated


The holiday was founded by Kraft Foods, said the website.

This year's festivities in Hastings, Nebraska are taking place from August 20 through August 22, according to Kool-Aid Days' website.

For information on next year's celebration, visit kool-aiddays.com.
