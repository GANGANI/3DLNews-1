UPDATE: Bellevue Public Schools releases statement after 9-year-old dies in crash
by Taleisha Newbill, KPTM
Sunday, Sep 15th 2019
KPTM{p}{/p}
OMAHA, Neb. (FOX 42 KPTM) —

UPDATE:

ADVERTISEMENT

Tuesday afternoon, Bellevue Public Schools released a statement about Caleb Roman, who died in a Sunday crash:

This is a tremendous loss for Caleb’s family and our school community. Our thoughts are with his family, friends and loved ones.

The principal of Avery Elementary school, John Campbell also sent a note to the families of Avery Elementary where Roman was a student.

In his letter to families, Campbell said its Response Team of professionals will be available to help students surrounding this loss.

PREVIOUS COVERAGE 3:00 p.m.:

Bellevue Police have released the name of the boy who died in the Sunday crash.

Police say 9-year-old, Caleb Roman died shortly after arriving at Nebraska Medicine.

According to police, when they arrived at the scene of the crash, officers found that Roman had been hit by an SUV.

Police say the SUV was headed south on North 4th Street.

PREVIOUS COVERAGE: 9:40 p.m.: The Bellevue Police Department says the 9-year-old boy who was hit by a car Sunday afternoon has died.

The BPD says the boy was taken to the hospital after the crash and was pronounced dead shortly after arriving.

Officials have not released the name of the boy and say no arrests have been made.

PREVIOUS COVERAGE:

Neighbors are standing outside in silence near fourth & Pleasantview, hoping for good news.

One person who called police tells FOX 42 they heard someone screaming outside around 5:00 p.m.

The man says a child was riding around and was then hit by a car.

Police have not confirmed any details of the crash as of 6:45 p.m.

The neighbor talks about the children of the neighborhood who are always running and riding bikes in the streets with no helmet or any protection.

"It was bound to happen, it's sad that it did."
