Buffett Magnet investigating teacher after reports of 'inappropriate remarks'

By 6 News
Published: Mar. 5, 2019 at 12:49 PM CST

HR personnel at OPS and Alice Buffett Magnet Middle School are investigating an incident involving a teacher who allegedly "introduced an inappropriate classroom discussion" and "made an inappropriate remark during class."

Details are limited "due to privacy," according to Buffett Magnet and Omaha Public Schools officials, who each sent a note out to parents about the incident.

The principal's note stated that human resources immediately contacted about the incident and "is conducting a full investigation." The OPS statement referenced diversity respecting "all students' ethnicity, backgrounds and culture."

Tuesday, OPS sent 6 News the following statement about the incident:

Late last week, we were made aware that a teacher reportedly engaged in an inappropriate discussion with students during class. We are conducting a full investigation of the incident.
The comments reported are in no way reflective of the values of Omaha Public Schools. As the most diverse school district in the state, we pride ourselves on being respectful of all students' ethnicity, backgrounds and culture.

Here is the letter sent home to school families Monday:

Good Evening Buffett Magnet Middle School Staff and Families:
We want you to know that we were recently made aware of concerns regarding a teacher who reportedly introduced an inappropriate classroom discussion/lesson, as well as made an inappropriate remark during class.
We take reports like this very seriously. Our Human Resources department was immediately contacted regarding the incident and is conducting a full investigation.
At this time, we cannot share additional details.
If you have any questions, please contact the school at (531) 299-2320.
Thank you for your continued support of Buffett Magnet Middle School and Omaha Public Schools. Please know that due to privacy, I cannot share additional details but will answer any questions that I can.

Sincerely,

Anthony Clark-Kaczmarek, Principal
Buffett Magnet Middle School
