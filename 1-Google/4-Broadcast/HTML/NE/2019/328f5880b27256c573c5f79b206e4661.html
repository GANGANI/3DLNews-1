Why D.C. sex workers are flocking to Logan Circle: 7 On Your Side investigates

by WJLA

Mon, November 4th 2019 at 9:18 AM
Updated Tue, November 5th 2019 at 12:11 PM
Why are sex workers flocking to D.C.'s Logan Circle? 7 On Your Side investigates. (WJLA)

WASHINGTON (WJLA) — WASHINGTON — It’s no secret the Logan Circle neighborhood in D.C. has been a hot bed for prostitution for years, but now neighbors say the problem is endemic.

Residents say they have coexisted with the sex workers since the 50’s, but say the situation has deteriorated so much that many fear going to work in the morning or taking their kids to school.

“I was walking home, and I got a notification on my phone that someone was at my door and it was someone’s backside hitting my ring doorbell while engaging in intercourse,” one neighbor told us on camera.

Other neighbors provided surveillance video showing sex on top of cars, in English basements, and sex workers beaten by men in their driveways.

ABC 7 spent several mornings watching the activity near 12th and N Streets Northwest. Each day between 3 A.M. and 8 A.M., woman walk the neighborhood looking for clients; traffic begins to build by 5 A.M.

“It’s an open-air prostitution market,” one man tells us. “They are walking down the street and there’s traffic jams every morning,” another woman describes to our cameras.

ABC 7 asked two sex worker advocates why there seems to be an uptick in prostitutes on the streets. Current prostitutes and advocates Tamika Spellman and Emmelia Talarico say when the federal government shutdown sites like Craigslist Personals and Backpage.com, woman had no choice but go back to the streets.

“The federal government implemented FOSTA and CESTA which were two bills that wiped out sex workers ability to earn money online,” Talarico explains. “When those online venues closed. It became impossible for sex workers to work indoors,” Spellman adds.

Spellman says the women come to Logan Circle for safety and money. Spellman estimates on a good morning, a woman earns about $1000.

“They were the better dates [Logan Circle]. They pay better. It’s a lot less violence on this end compared to the other sex worker strips,” Spellman says.

D.C. Police say they routinely run operations in this area, but it can be a game of cat and mouse.

“We will make arrests of guys or gals who are out there doing it. They will get stay away orders and what will happen is they will disappear, and a new girl will be brought in,” Third District Police Commander Stuart Emerman said. “I hear from community members which is awful, hey I got family coming to town and they pull up to my house at 8 in the morning and this is what they see. I feel for you. It’s an awful thing to see.”

DC Police say in 2018 they arrested 117 people for prostitution in Logan Circle and more than 80 this year.

“We’ll do some plane clothes operations where we will try to solicit men to engage with the undercover or do vice versa with men going out and trying to approach the girls. Also, we try to make ourselves visible,” Emerman added.

Spellman, who has been a sex worker in D.C. for more than 36 years, and Talirico agree that locals here should not have to wake up to this behavior, adding that they plan to work with the women to reduce the friction.

“I will have conversations with the sex workers to put a stop to the early morning activity. I will see if I can get them to move away from the area to do their business,” Spellman said.

D.C Council is considering legislation that would decriminalize sex work. At least four council members have voiced support. A public comment period closed on November 1st.
