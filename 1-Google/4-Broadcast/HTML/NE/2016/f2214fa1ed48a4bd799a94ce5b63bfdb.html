Girl critically hurt in crash won't survive

By WOWT 6 News
Published: Nov. 11, 2016 at 5:00 PM CST

Family tells WOWT 6 News one of the girls injured in an accident last month won't survive.

The hit-and-run crash at North 30th Street and Sorensen Parkway on October 24th sent three children to the hospital.

The driver who fled the scene had run a red light and police followed the trail of debris to Miller Park where the car was found. That driver, 36-year-old Benjamin Thompson, was dumping beer cans and a liquor bottle into the trash and trying to get away. Thompson's three daughters were still in the car. One-year-old Jade Hetrick was treated for minor injuries. Six-year-old Aly Thompson has since been released from the hospital and is undergoing rehab.

Their mother says 8-year-old Kazlynn Thompson won't survive her injuries and will be taken off life support next week. The family has set up a

GoFundMe

account to help with an expected $10,000 in funeral expenses.

Thompson has four prior DUI convictions.
