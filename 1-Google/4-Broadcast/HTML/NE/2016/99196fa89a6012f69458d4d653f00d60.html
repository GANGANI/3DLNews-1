Months later, child’s stolen wheelchair is replaced

by Lisa Nico

Wed, September 21st 2016 at 11:56 PM
Updated Wed, September 21st 2016 at 8:13 AM
3
VIEW ALL PHOTOS
Months later, child’s stolen wheelchair is replaced (Photo: KUTV)

SALT LAKE CITY (KUTV) - Keaton Johnson rolled out of Shriner's Children's Hospital with an extra large smile on his face Tuesday.

The generally jovial 5-year-old had a reason to be extra thankful. After 10 weeks with limited mobility, Keaton had a brand new, custom made, portable wheelchair all his own.

"I can roll it on my own!" Keaton said under an ear to ear grin.

Keaton suffers from cerebral palsy, a degenerative disease which causes musculoskeletal issues including difficulty walking and talking.

However, it wasn't the disease that created the temporary mobility issue. Earlier this summer, Keaton's mobility was taken from him when his family's car was stolen from their Tooele apartment with his portable wheelchair inside.

With help from news and social media, community members and businesses donated money, goods, and services, including a new car from Bargain Buggies and a custom made wheelchair from Alpine Home Medical.

“He’ll be able to ride the bus to school. He’ll be able to get around at school. Because he’s currently in a stroller,” said Keaton's father, Austin Johnson.

Scott Jerome, Keaton's physical therapist at Shriner's, said Keaton has the attitude and spunk of any other child his age.

“Keaton is outstanding," said Jerome. “What we want to see with Keaton is that he’s doing what every other 5-year-old is doing.”

His new wheelchair will allow him to play, explore, and interact with other kids his age as he enters preschool.

Keaton's customizable wheelchair will grow along with him over the next five years. He will continue to get adjustments at Shriner's.

Keaton's family plans to donate any extra donations back to Shriner's Childrens Hospital.
