Can I get vaccinated for COVID-19?

Starting April 5, any Nebraskan old enough to receive a COVID-19 will be allowed to, according to Gov. Pete Ricketts. He said some health departments may still limit who can get vaccinated.

To see what criteria your health department is following, click here.

Health care workers with direct contact with patients, EMT's and long term care facility residents and staff were first in line to receive the vaccine in Nebraska.

The first doses of the Pfizer vaccine arrived in Nebraska on December 14. On Dec. 15, CHI St. Francis NICU Nurse Aria Diehl was the first person in central Nebraska to be vaccinated.

To register to get the vaccine through the state system, click here. For more information, click here.

Some pharmacies participating in the Federal Pharmacy Program are also administering vaccines. To receive a shot through this program contact a participating pharmacy.

Governor Pete Ricketts announced on May 24 that Nebraska would discontinue all Directed Health Measures effective at midnight.

The Centers For Disease Control and Prevention said it is thought that the virus is spread mainly from person-to-person through respiratory droplets. They outline what you should know:

What are some of the symptoms of COVID-19?

  • Fever
  • Cough
  • Shortness of breath
  • Loss of smell or taste

When do these symptoms require immediate medical attention?

  • Difficulty breathing or shortness of breath
  • Persistent pain or pressure in the chest
  • New confusion or inability to arouse
  • Bluish lips or face

What should I do if I’m sick?

  • Stay home
  • Stay in touch with your doctor
  • Call ahead before getting medical care
  • Avoid public transportation
  • Stay away from others
  • Limit contact with pets and animals
  • Wear a facemask
  • Cover coughs and sneezes with a tissue and dispose of it
  • Wash you hands frequently for at least 20 seconds. If soap and water is not available, use an alcohol-based hand sanitizer that contains at least 60% alcohol.

How do I protect myself and help stop the spread?

  • Get vaccinated if you can.
  • Wash you hands with soap frequently for at least 20 seconds
  • If soap and water is not available, use an alcohol-based hand sanitizer that contains at least 60% alcohol
  • Avoid touching your eyes, noise and mouth with unwashed hands
  • Stay six feet away from other people
  • Do not gather in groups of more than 10 people

How do I get tested for coronavirus?

Contact your doctor, or sign up through TestNebraska.

Governor Ricketts said on March 24, that people who are experiencing symptoms, have tested negative for other respiratory illnesses like influenza and who have traveled to areas like Europe, Asia or other regions with a high number of COVID-19 cases will be tested. Soon after the state expanded testing to first responders, health care workers and nursing home workers.

On April 20, Ricketts announced that health providers would be given more flexibility to decide who is tested.

On April 21, the governor announced a plan to dramatically increase the number of people tested. People who have symptoms, have been exposed to the coronavirus or have traveled to hot spots would soon be eligible for free drive-thru tests. The partnership is with a coalition of private companies who have started similar programs in Utah and Iowa. Ricketts urged people to go to testnebraska.com to fill out a free assessment. The program was later expanded to all Nebraskans.

What should I do if I've traveled or plan to travel?

"Returning international travelers are no longer required to self-quarantine and self-monitor for 14 days upon return/arrival, according to DHHS. "However, several countries are seeing increasing cases of COVID-19 and we continue to recommend that travelers practice strict social distancing and self-monitor for symptoms." Learn more here.

How did the outbreak start?

The virus was first detected in Wuhan City, Hubei Province, China, according to the CDC. The agency said it is believed to have been transferred from an animal to a human before spreading person-to-person.

What is "community spread?"

The CDC said "community spread" means people have been infected with the virus in an area, including some who are not sure how or where they became infected. The term is often used in cases that are not linked to travel outside the community.

How long does the virus stay on surfaces?

According to Harvard Medical School, a recent study found COVID-19 can survive up to four hours on copper, up to 24 hours on cardboard and up to two to three days on plastic and stainless steel. They said researchers also found that the virus can hang as droplets in the air for up to three hours before falling, but most will fall more quickly.

Can I get unemployment if I'm laid off or my hours are reduced?

In update on May 24, it was announced that Nebraska was ending its participation in the federal pandemic unemployment programs

The CARES Act creates new benefits available to Nebraskans. Learn more here.

What executive orders from the pandemic are still in effect?

Gov. Pete Ricketts is rescinding several executive orders that he had issued during the coronavirus pandemic. They will be rescinded on June 1, 2021 and include EO 20-06, EO 20-09, EO 20-13, EO 20-14, EO 20-19, EO 20-21 and EO 20-22.

How long is the pandemic predicted to last?

Gov. Pete Ricketts said on March 30, 2020 that model showed the virus could peak in Nebraska in mid to late April. State leaders have said the pandemic won't be "over" until a vaccine is widely available. As of the beginning of October, the test positivity rate was highest in April. However, record hospitalizations, positive cases and total tests were reported by DHHS in October.
