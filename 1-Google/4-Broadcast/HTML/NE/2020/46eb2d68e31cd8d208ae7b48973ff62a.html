Two teens found dead in vehicle submerged in Holdrege lake

by KHGI

Fri, November 6th 2020 at 10:16 AM
Updated Fri, November 6th 2020 at 3:26 PM
3

HOLDREGE, Neb. — Two deaths were caused by a vehicle going under the water at the North Park Lake in Holdrege.

The Holdrege Police Department (HPD) said two teenagers were found in the vehicle and both were deceased at the scene.

On Nov. 5, a one vehicle accident was investigated by the HPD, Phelps County Sheriff’s office and Holdrege Fire Department.

The HPD received a 911 call at 11:10 p.m. regarding a vehicle going under the water at the North Park Lake in Holdrege.

The HPD said the caller advised there was one or two occupants in the vehicle.

When an officer arrived, the vehicle was fully submerged in the lake.

The officer dove into the water but was unable to locate the vehicle.

The Buffalo County Dive team was called to assist with the search, however, the vehicle was removed from the water before they arrived.

A member of the Holdrege Fire Department with dive experience, located the vehicle.

The Holdrege County Attorney did decline to comment, but NTV News was able to confirm that one student went to Holdrege Public School and the other went to Axtell Public School.

The vehicle was removed from the lake by Kearney Towing.

The family has been notified of the deaths.

The incident is under further investigation.
