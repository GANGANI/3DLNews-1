Abbott says more National Guard troopers, razor wire coming to portion of border

by MICHAEL ADKISON | KEYE Staff

Thu, February 8th 2024 at 5:41 PM
Texas Gov. Greg Abbott and several of America's Governors attend a briefing about the border at the Texas Department of Public Safety in Eagle Pass, Texas on Sunday, Feb. 4, 2024. (Jay Janner/Austin American-Statesman via AP)

    TOPICS:

    TexasBorderAbbottTroopersRazor wireNational GuardImmigrationLegislation

EAGLE PASS, Texas (KEYE) — After hinting at a "border security announcement" regarding Texas' border security initiatives, Texas Gov. Greg Abbott said Thursday in a news conference that the state was looking to implement more Texas National Guard troopers and razor wire at a portion of the border in Eagle Pass the state has effectively taken over, something other officials had previously noted or hinted at.

We are working on expansion plans to expand the successful operations that we’ve seen in certain locations that deter illegal immigration," Abbott said at the conference. "Some of those will be announced after they occur, and some will be announced beforehand."

The governor specifically named more troopers and more razor wire as increased initiatives, but he did not give specific details as to how those will be implemented, or any solid numbers behind them.

Those remarks come just a day after Congressional Republicans voted down legislation that would have drastically expanded the executive branch's oversight of the border, notedly the ability to shut down the border if it grows overwhelmed, something President Joe Biden said he would do the day he signs that into law, if it was passed.

Republicans, including from Texas, have argued that the federal government already has the authority to do that with executive action.

That mirrors comments from former President Donald Trump earlier this week on Truth Social: "ALL A PRESIDENT HAS TO DO IS SAY, 'CLOSE THE BORDER,' AND THE BORDER WILL BE CLOSED. A COSTLY NEW BILL IS NOT NECESSARY!"

"No executive action, no matter how aggressive, can deliver the significant policy reforms and additional resources Congress can provide, that Republicans have rejected," Karine Jean Pierre, White House Press Secretary, said in a briefing on Thursday.

That legislation was the result of months of negotiations with bipartisan lawmakers and the Biden administration. One of its key supporters was an endorsement from the Border Patrol union.

"There are aspects of this bill that would have added additional hundreds of Border Patrol agents to our rank and file, that would have given us more technology, that would have given us more equipment and infrastructure," Jason Owens, Chief of the Border Patrol, said in an interview on Fox News. "Of course I'm going to be supportive of that."
