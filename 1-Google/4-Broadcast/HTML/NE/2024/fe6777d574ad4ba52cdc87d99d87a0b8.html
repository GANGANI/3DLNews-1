Biden sharply critical of Israel, calls response in Gaza 'over the top'

by KAYLA GASKINS | The National Desk

Fri, February 9th 2024 at 4:33 PM
7
VIEW ALL PHOTOS
Palestinians look at the destruction after an Israeli airstrike in Rafah, Gaza Strip, Friday, Feb. 9, 2024. (AP Photo/Fatima Shbair)

    TOPICS:

    BidenIsraelGazaMichiganCriticismHamasArab-AmericanElection

WASHINGTON (TND) — President Biden delivered the sharpest criticism to date of Israel during Thursday evening’s press conference calling Israel's response in Gaza, "over the top."

After Hamas’ Oct. 7 massacre, Biden staunchly defended Israel saying on Oct. 10, "Like every nation in the world, Israel has a right to respond and indeed has a duty to respond."

But amid unrelenting protests from some of his key supporters, the president now appears to shift his stance. This comes as warning signs for his re-election campaign begin to flash.

Support for Biden is noticeably plummeting among Michigan’s large Arab-American community. A new poll has him trailing Donald Trump by five points.

The key battleground state helped deliver Biden the White House in 2020.

Michigan Congresswoman Rashida Tlaib has been one of Biden's harshest critics when it comes to Israel.

"The level of support for Netanyahu’s war crimes by the Biden Administration and the majority of this body is beyond belief," Tlaib said on the House floor on Jan. 31.

To mend ties, Biden sent top aides to Michigan where they met with Arab Americans Thursday including Muslim activist Osama Siblani, a public supporter of the terror groups Hamas and Hezbollah. He's also the publisher of Arab American News and a leader in the community. Siblani said he received a personal invitation from the White House.

The private sit-down unleashed immediate backlash from the Jewish community.

Also this week, Biden published a memorandum placing new conditions on U.S. military aid to Israel something progressives have pushed for. In the past, U.S. aid to Israel didn't come with conditions.
