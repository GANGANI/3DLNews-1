Lighthouse unveiled to honor boy killed at Disney World

By WOWT
Published: Aug. 7, 2017 at 4:49 PM CDT

Walt Disney World has unveiled a sculpture in honor of an Omaha boy who was killed by an alligator at one of its resorts near Orlando. The sculpture of a lighthouse was built in memory of Lane Thomas Graves.

An alligator grabbed the 2-year-old who was playing along the Seven Seas Lagoon beach outside Disney's Grand Floridian Resort and Spa in 2016. The child's father, Matt Graves, jumped into the water to try to free his son. The child's body was found 16 hours later and his death was ruled an accident.

In the year since Lane's death, Disney has made numerous changes to increase safety for its guests. It added signs warning guests of reptiles in its waterways. It also constructed a boulder wall along the edge of the lagoon.

In recognition of National Lighthouse Day, please see the lighthouse created to honor our loving boy Lane. 💙

Posted by Lane Thomas Foundation on Monday, August 7, 2017
