Court sides with Nebraska State Fair in suit over Aksarben Stock show

by NTV News

Thu, May 26th 2022 at 10:48 AM
Updated Thu, May 26th 2022 at 2:44 PM
4
VIEW ALL PHOTOS
SEPTEMBER 2020: The Aksarben Stock Show is held at Fonner Park, produced by the Nebraska State Fair (Photo Credit: NTV News)

LINCOLN, Neb. — The Nebraska State Fair has won the first round in a legal battle over the use of the Aksarben Stock Show name, as a judge rules the Fair's suit is valid and can proceed.

The State Fair board had filed suit against former employee Greg Harder and his associates Trent and Kelli Loos. Trent Loos was recently running for lieutenant governor. Together they had formed "Nebraskans 4 Youth Livestock" and filed with the Nebraska Secretary of State’s office seeking rights to the “Aksarben Livestock Show” name.

Harder was fired by the fair days after the 2020 Aksarben Stock Show.

AKSARBEN STOCK SHOW PLANS TO CONTINUE AFTER CUTTING TIES WITH DIRECTOR

The Nebraska State Fair then sued Harder for trademark infringement and other claims. The attorney for Nebraskans 4 Youth Livestock, Inc. (N4YLI) filed a motion to dismiss the case and also transfer it from Lancaster County.

Now a judge has rejected the motion to dismiss and overruled the motion to transfer the case to another court. The court also finds Greg Harder, Trent Loos, and Kelli Loos "can be held personally liable."

The court said there is "likelihood of confusion" if Nebraskans 4 Youth Livestock were to produce a show using the Aksarben name.

The judge's order says "Harder, while still employed by the plaintiff, formed a competing business, N4YLI, and participation in N4YLI's registration of the 'Aksarben Stock Show' trade name being used by the plaintiff; and then, after he was fired, he not only expressed his intent to conduct a stock show under the 'Aksarben Stock Show' name, but also, participated in N4YLI's registration of the service make being used by the plaintiff. The Court finds that these facts, taken as true, are sufficient to state a plausible claim to relief for breach of the duty of loyalty."

This does not conclude the lawsuit but the court said the facts back up the State Fair's position and the judge dismissed many of the arguments made by Nebraskans 4 Youth Livestock.

Attorney Mike Degan represents Nebraskans 4 Youth Livestock. He said this is a ruling on a preliminary motion.

"My clients believe strongly in livestock exhibition and the importance of providing opportunities for kids to participate in order to continue a great Nebraska tradition. They look forward to defending the remaining claims in Court," he wrote in an email.

Attorney Mark Fahleson represents the fair. He previously served on the State Fair Board.

Harder came to Nebraska from Minnesota and has a background in running livestock competitions. Trent Loos is a rancher who also produces social media content on agricultural and political issues and was recently running for lieutenant governor as running mate to Theresa Thibodeau. He has also served as an announced for cattle shows at the Nebraska State Fair.
