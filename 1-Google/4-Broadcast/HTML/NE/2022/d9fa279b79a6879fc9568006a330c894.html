Pacha Soap continues to expand and increase its production capacity

by Risell Ventura

Tue, November 29th 2022 at 4:49 PM
Updated Wed, November 30th 2022 at 6:29 AM

HASTINGS, Neb. — What used to be a dream is now a business that continues to grow in Hastings and in the United States. Andrew and Abi Vrbas founded the Pacha Soap Company in 2013, and now they are looking to expand the business.

Pacha Soap focuses on creating all-natural, environmentally-safe crafted soaps and bath products. They currently ship the items to people and stores throughout the country.

“I think what has accelerated that is that people are caring more about what they eat but also what they put on their skin, so they are wanting a better, higher quality, more natural product, so that has really accelerated our growth," said Pacha Soap CEO Andrew Vrbas.

Pacha Soap is working on expanding its facility by purchasing new equipment to increase its production capacity at their new Hastings headquarters (located at 317 S Elm Ave. Hastings). The expansion will be partly funded by a $217,000 Community Development Block Grant (CDBG) the city of Hastings requested.

The total project cost is around $427,000. Matching funds of approximately $210,000 for the expansion will come from the business. Hastings will use $7,000 for general grant administration.

“We’ll be adding more equipment to make our operations scalable in the next few years as we grow our business," Vrbas said. "Really consolidating all of our manufacturing under one roof will allow us to better meet the needs of our growing market."

The requested CDBG funds will allow $210,000 to be conditionally granted by the City of Hastings to the Hastings Economic Development Corporation, acting as the non-profit development organization, conducting activities on behalf of the city. The Hastings Economic Development Corporation will then utilize such conditionally granted CDBG funds to make a performance-based loan of $210,000 to the business for the expansion project. One-half of the $210,000 (an amount of $105,000) performance-based loan will be forgiven after Pacha Soap satisfies a national objective and completes all of the lending requirements.

Pacha Soap currently has 71 employees. According to the company, around seven more jobs will be created as a result of the expansion.

“It’s exciting, I love the fact that we can build a national brand here in Hastings," Vrbas said. "It's always a pleasure of mine when I get to share where we are from and talk a little bit about Nebraska. Most people don’t know anything about Nebraska, so the fact that we come from here and have a really good support here in Hastings is something to be proud of."

According to Pacha Soap, the expansion project already started, and they are looking to construct new manufacturing lines within their Hastings headquarters.

As they plan to move all their manufacturing to their Hastings plant, Vrbas said he is not sure what the future will hold for the current Pacha Soap store in downtown Hastings.
