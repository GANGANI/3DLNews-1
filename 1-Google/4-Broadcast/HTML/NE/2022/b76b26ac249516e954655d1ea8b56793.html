Food For Thought: Omaha's growing brewery industry

by Geoffrey Roth

Sat, December 31st 2022 at 10:15 PM
Updated Sat, December 31st 2022 at 10:41 PM
Pouring a Pilsner at Brickway Brewery and Distillery (Photo: Geoff Roth/Fox 42 News)

More and more people in the Metro are not just hitting regular bars, but are heading to breweries that produce their own beers.

A city’s ‘culinary coolness’ is often measured by its breweries.

“The brewery scene in Nebraska is incredible. We’ve gone from 10 breweries to over 60 breweries in the last ten years.”

That’s Zac Triemert, the President and Master Distiller at Brickway Brewery and Distillery in the Old Market.

“In fact, the beer being brewed in Nebraska is every bit as good as everywhere else, not only in the U.S., but in the world," he said.

And, he should know. Many of his beers have won awards from competitions around the country – including what Zac says is the favorite beer of Americans.

“A favorite beer in Nebraska and around the country are the American light lagers, and I can tell you as a brewer, when we go to visit other breweries we drink their Pilsner, because that’s how you can judge the quality of a brewer, and we’re very proud of the Pilsner we have at Brickway.”

What makes Brickway stand out is that it isn’t just beer they make at their Old Market facility.

Zac spent time in Scotland and studied to become a Master brewer and distiller.

“Here at Brickway, we make a variety of spirits, that include vodka, gin, 5 different whiskies, and then some fun things like cinnamon whiskey and rum.”

They are Omaha’s first combination brewery and distillery since Prohibition ended in 1933.

The spirits are distilled in a unique distillery on display in the taproom, consisting of two copper pot stills, one made in Canada and one made in Scotland, that Zac says are designed to produce lighter and smoother spirits. The whiskeys are then aged in a production area up the street from the taproom for four years.

But – for the spirits and the beer - it all begins in the brewery behind the tap room.

“We mill the grain and add hot water to the milled grain, which starts an enzymatic reaction that breaks down the starch into fermentable sugars. Then we strain away the liquid. For making beer it’s called ‘wort’, if we’re making whiskey it’s called ‘wash’ – literally the exact same stuff. Then we strain off the liquid and send it off to the brew kettle. The brew kettle is where we boil the wort or wash, for making beer that’s where we add the hops, if we’re making whiskey, clearly we don’t add hops.”

The beer is then fermented in giant one-thousand gallon tanks.

While Zac says his passion is brewing and distilling, what he most enjoys is what his creations and his tap room bring to his customers.

“It’s these 3 words behind me – celebrate, commiserate, commemorate. That’s why we do it. It doesn’t matter what’s going on in your life. We’re going to be there for you.”

Brickway Brewery and Distillery is located at 1116 Jackson Street in the Old Market.

They’re open Monday through Wednesday from 1 pm to 11 pm. Thursday from 1 pm to midnight, Friday and Saturday from 11 am to midnight, and Sunday from 11 am to 11 p.m.

They also book tours of the brewery and distillery held on Saturdays.

Brickway is also part of a special Fox 42 promotion called 'The Brew Crew Card'. It gets you discounts on beer flights and growlers at five area breweries.
