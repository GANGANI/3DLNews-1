Alma girl starts free little library in town

by Lauryn Bentley

Tue, November 22nd 2022 at 10:11 PM
Updated Tue, November 22nd 2022 at 8:31 AM
4

ALMA, Neb. — Free little libraries have sprouted up towns across the state, giving people access to free books and letting kids have something of their own for no cost.

After learning about these free little libraries, Brynlee Koch decided her hometown of Alma needed one. So after doing research and presenting to city council, she made the library out of an old newspaper stand.

Her mom, Shelly, said she’s proud of the steps Brynlee has taken to make this happen.

“It took a long time to get it approved by the city council, the library council and then to construct it and then get some books donated and here we are. Having an opportunity to get some kids to read books, maybe those kids who didn’t have that opportunity. I know there’s a library in town, but they’re not always open and kids like the opportunity to have something they can call their own,” said Shelly.

The little library is located right outside of the library in town. It’s encouraged to leave a book and take some home with you.
