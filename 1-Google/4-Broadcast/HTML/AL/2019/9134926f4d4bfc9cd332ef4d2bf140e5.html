‘This is a tough moment for this city’: B’ham mayor reacts to discovery of body believed to Kamille McKinney

By WBRC Staff
Updated: Oct. 23, 2019 at 8:53 AM CDT

BIRMINGHAM, Ala. (WBRC) - Birmingham Police Chief Patrick Smith opened Tuesday night’s news conference with honesty.

“I wish I had all of you gathered here with good news. It’s not that kind of press conference,” he said.

Chief Smith, along with Birmingham Mayor Randall Woodfin, delievered the heartbreaking update than the body of a 3-year-old, believed to be Kamille McKinney, has been found in a dumpster in a landfill in the Birmingham area.

It is a tragic ending to the search for Kamille that began on October 12.

Chief Smith said during the news conference that warrants for capital murder and kidnapping will be issued for Patrick Stallworth, 39, and Derick Brown, 29. Both are already in custody.

As Birmingham Mayor Randall Woodfin said Tuesday night, “This is tough. This is a tough moment for this city. This is a tough moment for this family.”

This is the question and answer part of Tuesday night’s news conference with Chief Patrick Smith.
