LeBron James surprises 'I Promise' students with news of $1M grant for new gym

by Chris White

Fri, May 10th 2019 at 6:24 AM
Updated Fri, May 10th 2019 at 10:35 AM
8
VIEW ALL PHOTOS
NBA superstar Lebron James announcing a new $1 million dollar grant from the Dick's Sporting Goods Foundation for a new gym at the "I Promise" school on May 9, 2019. (Courtesy: Dick's Sporting Good's Foundation)

    TOPICS:

    Akron Public SchoolsAkron, OhioBasketballDickI PromiseI Promise SchoolLeBron JamesOhio

AKRON, Ohio (WSYX/WTTE) — NBA great LeBron James announced Thursday that students at his "I Promise" school are getting a new gym thanks to a grant.

The Dick's Sporting Goods Foundation awarded the school a $1 million grant to build a new gym.

“To my kids, this is more than a gym. The DICK’S Sports Matter program is helping us provide even more opportunities. An opportunity to play and learn in a safe place that many don’t have access to. I can’t imagine where my friends and I would be if it weren’t for the coaches and teachers who cared about us and the opportunities we had," said James.

The "I Promise" school was founded in partnership with Akron Public Schools and the LeBron James Family Foundation. The school serves 240 of Akron Public School’s most at-risk 3rd and 4th grade students.

“I believe the sky is the limit for these kids and the results we’re seeing are just the beginning. DICK’S Sporting Goods Foundation believes in the opportunities and values sports provide, and I’ve experienced that at every level. I saw it growing up with my teammates and I see it with my kids now. The benefits of playing sports can extend to every aspect of your life, and I’m excited we’re able to provide a space for that growth at the I PROMISE School," added James.
