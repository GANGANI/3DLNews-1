Fox News' Geraldo Rivera slams NRA, calls 2nd Amendment 'stupid'

by Lindsey Leake, Sinclair Broadcast Group

Thu, December 3rd 2015 at 2:45 PM
Updated Fri, December 4th 2015 at 6:02 AM
Geraldo Rivera. (MGN)

WASHINGTON (Sinclair Broadcast Group) — While the New York Daily News reprimanded Republican presidential candidates for offering prayers after Wednesday's deadly mass shooting in San Bernardino, California but no solutions pertaining to gun control, a Fox News fixture didn't hesitate to call the Second Amendment "stupid."

It's happened again. Another day, another deadly shooting in America. Just five days after a gunman opened fire at a Planned Parenthood facility in Colorado Springs, Colorado, a man and woman opened fire at a center for the developmentally disabled in San Bernardino on Wednesday, killing 14 and injuring 21 others.

Similar incidents are occurring with such frightening regularity that a BBC reporter described the attacks on air as "just another day in the United States of Americaanother day of gunfire, panic and fear."

Police say 28-year-old Syed Farook and 27-year-old Tashfeen Malik--whom Hussam Ayloush, the executive director of the Greater Los Angeles Chapter of the Council on American-Islamic Relations, says were married with a 6-month-old daughter--were responsible for this latest slaughter. As of Thursday afternoon their motives remained unclear. Was it an ISIS-inspired or -affiliated terrorist attack, or an overblown workplace dispute? A twisted combination of both? Some other sinister reason that hasn't yet been discovered?

No matter the shooting suspects' reason(s) for wreaking havoc on the southern California city, their crime has become political--as it frequently seems to do in the wake of a shooting--with politicians and lobbyists on either side of the aisle hashing out their views on gun control.

But this time a prominent Fox News journalist jumped into the conversation, saying "The 2d (sic) Amendment is Stupid!!! Don't rationalize."

He was also quick to shame the National Rifle Association, simply tweeting: "The NRA is full of sh**."

Though Rivera tends to be critical of President Barack Obama (on Tuesday he tweeted "#POTUS just concluded lame presser") he's on the same page with the commander in chief when it comes to gun control.

"POTUS wrong re so much-like loyalty toward double dealing Turkey-but he's so right re gun violence-More than 1 gun for every American? Why?" Rivera tweeted Wednesday.

He added, "14 Dead!! Terror in America. This is our future if we don't act."

However, as the New York Daily News was quick to point out, GOP candidates' tweets largely focused on sending their thoughts and prayers to the city of San Bernardino and its victims:

ATF officials say the guns Farook and Malik used in the attacks were purchased legally in the United States. According to the FBI, the bureau's record for the number of firearms background checks was broken just last week on Black Friday, with 185, 345 total background checks--that's roughly two per second. The agency said it received approximately 5 percent fewer check requests the previous Black Friday.

San Bernardino Mayor Carey Davis told CNN's Chris Cuomo on "New Day" Thursday morning, "It's not the gun that kills, it's the shooter that kills."

"Communities throughout the United States are dealing with violence and crime," he added. "It's an issue that will not go away overnight."

Andy Parker, whose daughter Alison was murdered at the hands of a gunman in Virginia this summer, compared tighter gun control laws to seat belts on "New Day."

"What do you say to the people who say the madmen will always be able to get their hands on a gun?" CNN's Alisyn Camerota asked him.

"It's like wearing a seat belt. A seat belt is not going to save your life in an horrific accident, but it's going to give you a better chance," he said. "So, doing nothing ... we're gonna continue to see this, regardless of what state has the tougher gun laws."
