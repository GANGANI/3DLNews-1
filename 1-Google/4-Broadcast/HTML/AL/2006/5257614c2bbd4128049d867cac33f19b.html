Double murder, suicide under investigation

Published: Aug. 22, 2006 at 4:21 PM CDT|Updated: Sep. 26, 2006 at 5:25 PM CDT

Authorities in Etowah County are investigating a murder-suicide that left three people dead.

A married couple and the shooter were discovered this morning north of Attalla in Etowah County.

"It scared me. It's shocking, very shocking," one neighbor told WAFF 48 News.

Etowah County Sheriff's Department officials identified the shooter as Johnny Ray Holderfield, a convicted sex offender who was registered in Marshall County.

Authorities said Holderfield shot and killed his sister, 45-year-old Jane Stokes, at a trailer on Buster Willett Road Monday afternoon.

Authorities said three teenagers, a 15-year-old and two 14-year-olds that includes Stokes' child, were bound, taped and held at another location after they got off the school bus, but were not harmed.

When Stokes' husband Brian arrived home from work, Holderfield shot him.

"They were nice people, quiet, just a normal family," a neighbor said.

One of the teens freed himself early this morning and notified authorities.

When deputies arrived at the trailer where Holderfield was hiding, he ran into woods behind the home. That's where he killed himself.

The community is shocked.

"Oh my gosh, couldn't believe it," said a neighbor. "It's hard to believe, like, just right down the road from where I live."

WAFF 48 News uncovered information about Holderfield's background on the Alabama Department of Public Safety's website.   Holderfield's picture and past were posted on-line because he is a convicted sex offender.

Holderfield used to live on Highway 168 in Boaz in Marshall County. That's not where he committed his past crime, though.

Holderfield was convicted of first degree rape and sexual assault with a child in the state of Texas. The victim in that case was an 8-year-old girl.

Holderfield registered as a sex offender in Marshall County in September of 2005.
