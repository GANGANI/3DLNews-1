Investigators believe killer may be in news footage of 1995 murdered girl

by Dan Rascon

Wed, September 28th 2016 at 6:15 PM
Updated Thu, September 29th 2016 at 7:31 AM
7
VIEW ALL PHOTOS
Investigators believe killer may be in news footage of 1995 murdered girl (Photo: KUTV 2News archive footage)

Salt Lake City — (KUTV) A cold case murder investigation involving a 6-year-old Salt Lake girl has a new lead that involves old news footage from the week of the murder.

According to detectives, the killer may be visible in some of the Aug. 1995 news reports covering the abduction, sexual assault and murder of young Rosa Tapia -- or “Rosie” as she’s known by family.

“A lot of times, people that commit these crimes end up watching what’s going on, watching the news reports, or actually being at the scene,” Detective Greg Wilking said.

Wilking said, according to witness statements from the time of the murder, a friend of Tapia wrote that she was watching a news report and recognized a man who had sat and watched her and Rosie playing on the playground of the apartment prior to the abduction and murder.

Police are also looking for a man who was taking out his trash at the apartment complex early that morning who may have witnessed something.

“It feels like yesterday,” said an emotional Lewine Tapia, Rosie’s mother. "She was starting her life. Starting school and they just came and took her in a flash in one night."

The day was Aug. 13, 1995 when Tapia says she woke up to find her daughter missing from her bedroom. Rosie’s body was found hours later in nearby canal.

Tapia hopes the old news footage can help.

“Hopefully these leads will lead to something and they will find the person. It's more than 20 years that we haven't had any formation on the case."

Tapia said it’s time for the killer be found and locked up. "If he can do this to my Rosie, has he done it before? Or is he still doing something like this with someone else?"

Wilking says it is a needle-in-a-haystack search, but detectives hope to review the news footage with the friend and see if any of the images spark her memory.
