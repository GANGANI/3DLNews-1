Chickasaw police chief suspended for 3 days over TikTok videos

by Darwin Singleton

Thu, December 7th 2023 at 8:35 AM
Updated Fri, December 8th 2023 at 6:21 PM
TikTok posts made by police chief trigger investigation by city of Chickasaw (WPMI)

CHICKASAW, Ala. (WPMI) — UPDATE:

The City of Chickasaw offered this statement to our further inquiries into the investigation of the Chickasaw police chief:

Today, Mayor Barry Broadhead and I met with Interim Chief of Police Tommie McDuffie regarding his recent social media posts and their content. Mayor Broadhead suspended Interim Chief McDuffie for 3 days for violating the City’s Social Media Activity Policy. Chief McDuffie has accepted his punishment and provided the Mayor and the City Council with a written public apology. I have attached that written apology for you to publish if you see fit.
On behalf of Mayor Broadhead, the City of Chickasaw takes this matter very seriously and will continue to require professionalism from its employees. Interim Chief McDuffie has reassured the Mayor that he will treat this as a learning experience and focus his priorities on serving and protecting all of the citizens of Chickasaw.

- Nash Campbell

Police Chief Duffie submitted this letter.

December 7, 2023

To Mayor Barry Broadhead and The Chickasaw City Council

I deeply apologize for my recent behavior on social media. I regret any embarrassment that I have caused the City of Chickasaw or you the Mayor and Council. I hope that anyone that was offended by my actions can forgive me. It was truly not my intent; however, I understand that my behavior is not becoming of a Chickasaw Police Officer or a leader in your city. It will never happen again.

Sincerely,

Tommy McDuffie

Chickasaw Police Department



It was a joke told on TikTok, then reposted on the Facebook page "Voices from Chickasaw," and it starts like this:

"Three women were sitting at a bar, discussing..."

We're not going to tell you what they were discussing because it would not be appropriate to share that here.

But we can tell you the person telling that joke, then posting it online is Tommie McDuffie- known in the city of Chickasaw at Police Chief Tommie McDuffie.

And there are other posts... such as this one, a lip sync to a sermon about, well...

"Do you believe that the Lord tonight has set you free?"

"Yes, sir."

"Turn around and tell those people! Tell 'em!"

"I'm not gay no more! I am delivered! I don't like mens no more! I said I like women!"

(organ playing in background)

"Women, women, women, women!"

These and several other posts, some featuring the chief in his office, wearing his badge, have now been taken down form the site.

But not before being recorded and shared by others, creating quite an online buzz.

And while the posts were obviously meant to make people laugh, someone who is not laughing is the Chickasaw mayor and his city council.

NBC 15 reached out to Mayor Barry Broadhead, who had not returned our call at news time.

But he definitely is aware of the posts and has talked with city attorney Nash Campbell about it, as I did earlier this evening.

"The city of Chickasaw does not condone or support the statements that have been put out on interim Chief McDuffie's TikTok account recently,” says Nash, “and they are currently looking into the issue, and I'm also looking into any if any potential policies may or may not have been violated. We hope to be able to give a more thorough statement in the very near future."

We stopped by the Chickasaw Police department in hopes of talking with the chief or someone with the department. And while we were able to enter the lobby, no one appeared when the call button was pressed.

The postings come at a time when the department has seen instability at the top.

In 2022, Police Chief Brian Fillingim was terminated following his court testimony involving a city council member.

No official reason for his dismissal was given.

He was replaced by former Mobile County Deputy Keith Miller, who was dismissed from that position a year and a half later.

No official reason for his dismissal was given.

Miller was then replaced by McDuffie who is now serving as interim chief.

Chief Miller remains on the mobile phone version of the city's web site, and a parking space at the police station is still reserved for the former chief.

Now, these shared TikTok posts by McDuffie have put his future in question at a department still looking for a permanent leader.
