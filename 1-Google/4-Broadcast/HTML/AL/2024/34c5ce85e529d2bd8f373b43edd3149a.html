Chris Christie suggests he'd consider resuming presidential run with 'No Labels'

by RAY LEWIS | The National Desk

Tue, February 6th 2024 at 11:23 AM
FILE - Former New Jersey Gov. Chris Christie speaks during a gathering, June 6, 2023, in Manchester, N.H. (AP Photo/Charles Krupa, File)

WASHINGTON (TND) — Chris Christie indicated Tuesday he may consider resuming his presidential campaign as a third-party candidate.

The former New Jersey governor disclosed to ABC News he would need to discuss the prospect with his wife.

“It'd be a long conversation between me and Mary Pat, I can guarantee you that,” Christie said.

Christie, who suspended his Republican presidential campaign last month, argued prospective "No Labels" candidates should see a clear path to winning the 2024 presidential election. He noted third-party contenders have favorable chances of creating meaningful change.

“If there was ever a time in our lifetime when a third-party candidate could make a difference, I think it’s now,” Christie claimed. “The question, though, is, ‘What kind of difference?”

The former New Jersey governor revealed the biggest frustration during his campaign for the Republican nomination was other candidates’ unwillingness to oust former President Donald Trump from the party.

“We have so many people in our party who complain about Donald Trump, but none of them are willing to do the hard work that needs to be done to rid our party and our country of him,” he said.

Christie vowed he would not vote for Trump in the general election, as well as abstain from penciling in a GOP candidate in New Jersey’s June primary.

“The one thing I can tell you for sure is I don’t know what I’m going to do in November, but I’m not voting for Donald Trump under any circumstances," he declared.

Christie also predicted Trump will accept the Republican nomination as a convicted felon in July.

“If the Senate had done its job in January of 2021 and convicted him, which, I believe he’ll be convicted by a jury of his peers for that conduct ... my guess is that he will be – more likely than not – a convicted felon when he gets up on the stage at the nominating convention,” Christie stated.

He concluded Trump secured the GOP nomination after the first Republican debate in August, when three-fourths of the candidates affirmed their support for the former president regardless of a felony conviction.

“What it says to the largest debate audience we were ever going to have during the primary is his conduct is normal,” Christie explained. “Six of these eight candidates who were running against him say it’s okay, so how do you expect voters to think it’s not okay?”

Trump’s final Republican challenger, Nikki Haley, was one of his declared supporters at the time. Christie insisted he will not endorse the former South Carolina governor due to her allegiance.

“Imagine the position I’d be in if I endorse someone who then turned around and endorsed Donald Trump,” Christie offered. “I’m not going to be put in that position again.”

Christie endorsed Trump in 2016, something he refers to as “purely” as a political strategy.

“It was the biggest mistake I’ve made in my political career, and I’m just not going to repeat that mistake for anybody,” he asserted.
