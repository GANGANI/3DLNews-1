7-year-old fighting disease since birth passes away

By Pat Thomas and Patsy Montesinos
Published: Jan. 5, 2024 at 7:56 AM CST

ROANOKE, Va. (WDBJ) - A 7-year-old boy fighting an illness his entire life has passed away, according to his family.

Mikah Furrow had Congenital CMV, a common cause of birth defects in the U.S. The CDC reports that one of 200 babies is born with the disease, and one of five babies with CMV will have long-term health problems. Due to his illness, Mikah’s life in Vinton was marked by cerebral palsy, epilepsy, and developmental delays.

His family placed him in hospice care a year ago, and in recent days, Mikah’s health took a turn for the worse, according to Carilion Hospice RN Case Manager Krysta Smoot.

His family says he has gained his angel wings in a place where he can run, laugh and talk.

“The essence of Mikah’s legacy resides in the collective memories, the wisdom gleaned from his remarkable journey, and the love he effortlessly instilled in those fortunate enough to have crossed his path.”

“When he was born, we knew that he had CMV,” said his grandmother, Donna Furrow, and great aunt, Carlene Robinson. “But we had no idea the magnitude of what that meant.”

“We made a decision together, that every day that he lived was going to be the best day of his life,” said Furrow and Robinson. “And that’s what it’s been.”

Mikah’s family encourages every pregnant woman to ask her doctor for a CMV screening or seek information about it if considering having a baby. They believe this can reduce the severity and potentially save a child’s life.

A memorial service and funeral are being planned.
