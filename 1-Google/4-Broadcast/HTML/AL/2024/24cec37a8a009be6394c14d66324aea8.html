‘It was reckless’: Family distraught after dog shot by delivery driver at the wrong house

By Bria Chatman
Published: Feb. 5, 2024 at 11:03 PM CST|Updated: Feb. 6, 2024 at 10:34 AM CST

BIRMINGHAM, Ala. (WBRC) - Rose Kirk says Sunday night started normal for her and her children until they saw an Amazon truck at their house. The family’s two dogs, Solar and Lunar, were lying in chairs on the porch before things took a turn for the worse.

“The next thing I know, I just heard a gunshot right by my front door, and I heard my dog make that little sound,” says Kirk.

Hueytown Police say the driver shot the dog in self-defense, but Kirk says that’s not the case. She says the driver noticed the dogs and still continued to her front door.

“If you notice dogs, why are you steadily walking toward the dog if they’re a threat to you?,” says Kirk. “If I don’t know a dog, I’m not going to go near it.”

The family was in disbelief when they heard a gunshot and realized the driver had shot Solar while his sister Lunar watched. Kirk says she doesn’t even know why they were at her home.

“When I saw all the blood dripping from my dog, and I was like, my dog was on a leash. He was on a chain,” says Kirk. “Why would you come up on my porch? Both of them were sitting in their chairs. He noticed them, but instead of backing off, he did not back off. Suppose he had a package to deliver, which I never ordered a package. He had no reason to be on my property.”

Kirk says the bullet entered through Solar’s shoulder and exited his chest. Now, he has internal bleeding, and his operations will cost the family thousands.

Law enforcement says no charges will be filed against the driver.

“That was reckless,” says Kirk. “You can see where the bullet ricocheted, and it was right by our door.”

The family rescued both dogs in 2017 from the Birmingham Humane Society.

“The lady at the shelter says the rest of them were separated, so I took them in, and they’ve been our love,” says Kirk. “They did nothing wrong. They were at their home.”

Now, Lunar is concerned for her brother.

“She’s been crying every time she hears a dog cry outside; she thinks it’s Solar,” says Kirk. “I have to tell her that it’s not him and he’s not here, but she doesn’t understand.”

A spokesperson with Amazon says it’s against their policy for drivers to carry weapons. He shared this statement:

“We are in touch with the customer, and we’ve reached out to Hueytown Police Department as they investigate. The driver involved is no longer delivering packages for Amazon.”

The family says they’re grateful to have solar still here, but it will be a long road to recovery. They created a GoFundMe. To donate, click here.
