High school students wear 'Osama bin Laden" and 'ISIS' costumes

by Venton Blandin

Fri, September 26th 2014 at 9:38 PM
Updated Tue, September 1st 2015 at 5:36 PM

TALLADEGA - AL — An international threat has inspired two high school students to wear costumes. One wore an Osama bin Laden costume. Another dressed up to look like a member of the terror group Isis.A 17 year old shared the picture with ABC 33/40. The person who shared the picture says it was taken inside a classroom at Talladega High School. The person who shared it is not in the photo and does not go to the school.Kaitlyn Shaddix alerted ABC 33/40 to the picture she saw on Facebook."What did the picture make you think about," asked ABC 33/40's Venton Blandin."It's offends me on so many levels that they would dress up as Isis and Osama bin Laden," said Shaddix.The picture shows two people dressed as a member of the terror group Isis and bin Laden."I don't even know how to put into words how it makes me feel or how it has to make other people feel," added Shaddix.Shaddix first saw the picture Wednesday in her social media news feed. She learned from friends the two people in the photos are students at Talladega High."You see on the news, all the people who are forced out of their homes because of Isis and all the terrible things they do. One is of James Foley getting beheaded. It's not something you dress up as and make a laughing matter. It is real and it is happening right now," added Shaddix.The former Talladega High School student understands both dressed up for Villain Day' to celebrate homecoming week."I commented on the Facebook post and told them it offended me. They said they did not understand why it made me so upset," added Shaddix.ABC 33/40 reached out to the people whose names were attached to the photo on Facebook. Messages were sent. None were returned."It appalls me that they were not sent home or suspended. They were allowed to dress up like that and the teachers did not care. The principal did not care. What does that say about the school system," added Shaddix.ABC 33/40 made several calls to school leaders to ask if they knew the students were going to wear the costumes and what happened when classmates saw the costumes. The principal told ABC 33/40 he was not at liberty to discuss anything. Superintendent Donna King has not returned ABC 33/40's calls and e-mails.The student who posted the picture contacted ABC 33/40 to share her side of the story: Her comments are posted below, anonymously, with her mother's permission."The boy who dressed up as Osama bin Laden is a very patriotic person. He's an active member in JROTC and one of the nicest boys I know. The same for the boy who portrayed ISIS. Their goal here was not a silly joke," said the student who posted the photo.

The student went on to say, "That's what you've neglected to publish. These boys would go fight at a moments notice to defend her rights and her opinions. I am rather offended that you'd show them so badly. You could've easily contacted the boys or me."

ABC 33/40 sent messages to the boys wearing the costumes and girl who posted the photos. The messages were never returned. Those messages arent acknowledged at the time of this posting.

The student told us, "They were trying to show real villains, not some stupid cartoon. They were not being funny, they were not making fun of anyone eaffected by the events." I would appreciate that being said. Because you do not have the full story.
