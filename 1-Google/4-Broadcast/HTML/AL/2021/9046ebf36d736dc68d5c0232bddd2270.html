COVID longhauler now walking again, says recovery was 'nothing short of a miracle'

by CYNTHIA GOULD | WBMA Staff

Mon, March 22nd 2021 at 5:47 PM
Updated Wed, March 24th 2021 at 10:38 PM
3
VIEW ALL PHOTOS
Alabama woman works to overcome lingering effects from COVID-19. (Family photo)

BIRMINGHAM, Ala. (WBMA) — A significant issue in the fight against COVID-19 has been treating the so called longhaulers. Surveys find from 50-80% of patients continue to have lingering symptoms three months later. That can range from a loss of taste and smell to serious neurological issues.

When we first talked with Afsheen Ali in mid-January, the Homewood businesswoman and mother struggled to walk and drive. "I was very limited back then with no mobility and brain fog. I was completely dependent on my husband," explains Ali.

Desperate for help a friend recommended the Georgia Chiropractic Neurology Center outside Atlanta. "Don't think you're crazy, you're alone, the symptoms are real," says Ali.

  • SEE ALSO: Alabama COVID-19 vaccine eligibility expands to Phase 1C on March 22

"It's very terrifying for people. We have people coming in everyday with similar symptoms," remarks Dr. Marc Ellis.

The first step for Dr. Ellis and his staff is to pinpoint the area of the brain causing the issues with a full battery of exams.

Ali could not sense where her feet were and how to use them. "People become disoriented. In Afsheen's case she had spatial disorientation and lost her ability to move," explains Dr. Ellis.

With targeted neurological rehabilitation they work to retrain the brain and restore the nervous system to optimal function.

Dr. Ellis says they have to look at each patient individually and how their nervous system is responding. He urges patients to not give up.

"The number one determination to getting better is to keep trying. There is hope out there," remarks Dr. Ellis.

Ali continues to improve with therapy. "I'm going to be 100% percent. I even have moments like I'm back .... it was nothing short of a miracle for me."

In Birmingham, the UAB Post-COVID clinic has had 1,300 referrals since November. The demand we're told has significantly outpaced resources. Appointments are full until mid-fall.
