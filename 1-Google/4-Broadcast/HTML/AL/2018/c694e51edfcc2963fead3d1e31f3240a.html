School may have violated law by not calling law enforcement after threat

By Ken Curtis
Published: May. 4, 2018 at 7:32 AM CDT

The mother of an Ashford Middle School student said, had she not notified law enforcement officers, parents may never have known about a hit list written by a student that threatened her daughter and other students.

Alexandrea Wright told WTVY she learned of the list when her fifth-grader came home this week and told her about it. “She was upset,”, Wright said.

She then did what the school wouldn’t—notify the Houston County Sheriff’s Office.

Sheriff Donald Valenza confirms school officials failed to call officers who investigated after receiving Wright’s complaint.

Houston County Superintendent David Sewell said he didn’t learn of the threat until about 8:30 Wednesday night, eight hours after Wright said the hit list was discovered. “The administration was working very diligently (to investigate) and they let me know as soon as they could.” He said by 9 o’clock parents were notified via emails and text messages.

The school may have not only failed to notify law enforcement but may have also violated state law. Alabama’s Safe Schools Act requires, “The principal shall notify appropriate law enforcement officials when any person violates local board of education policies concerning drugs, alcohol, weapons, physical harm to a person, or threatened physical harm to a person.”

Sewell, though, believes the school did exactly what it should have done by investigating the complaint internally. “Our number one priority to making sure our students are safe,” he said. He also points out that extra security officers were placed on campus to reassure parents.

Preliminary indications, per Sewell, are the threats were not credible and there are “other things” that he can’t discuss publicly due to privacy regulations. Wright claims the girl with the hit list has a history of emotional issues.

Wright said, going forward, she will do whatever is necessary to make sure parents are notified of threats in the timely manner.

“I think there are a lot of parents that are scared and don’t know how to get something done and I think we all need to step up.”
