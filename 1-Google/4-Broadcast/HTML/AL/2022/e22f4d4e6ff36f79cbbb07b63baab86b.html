Saudi man offers Amber Heard marriage proposal, says he's better than Johnny Depp

by The National Desk

Wed, June 8th 2022 at 12:14 PM
Updated Wed, June 8th 2022 at 12:15 PM
16
VIEW ALL PHOTOS
FILE - Actress Amber Heard arrives at the High Court for a hearing in actor Johnny Depp's libel case, in London, Monday, July 13, 2020. (Aaron Chown/PA via AP)

WASHINGTON (TND) — A Saudi man has offered to marry Amber Heard after Johnny Depp's bombshell defamation trial ended in the actor's favor.

The bizarre proposal comes after a six-week-long trial in Fairfax County, Virginia. The pair were married for just 15 months before splitting in 2017. Depp sued Heard for $50 million because of an opinion piece she wrote for The Washington Post in which she called herself “a public figure representing domestic abuse.”

The "Pirates of the Caribbean" actor was not specifically named in the op-ed, titled "Amber Heard: I spoke up against sexual violence — and faced our culture’s wrath. That has to change.," and it was written more than a year after she first began making public accusations against him.

The verdict handed down in the drawn-out trial found Depp had been defamed by three statements in the 2018 op-ed piece. Jurors also concluded Heard was defamed by a lawyer for Depp and awarded her $2 million. That lawyer accused Heard of creating a hoax surrounding the abuse allegations.

Despite the jury ruling Heard owed Depp more than $10 million in damages, she has caught the eye of one Saudi man for different reasons. In an Instagram post, a screenshot shows the unidentified man sent a private voice recording to Heard's official account.

That private message has since been made public. Speaking in Arabic, the man said Heard should marry him and called Depp an "old man."

Amber, since all doors are closing on you, you have no one except me to take care of you. I’ve noticed that some people hate and bully you, therefore, I decided to marry you," according to a rough translation from the voice note sent to the "Aquaman" actress. "May Allah bless us both. You are a blessing, but people don’t appreciate that. I am better than that old man."

Heard has not officially acknowledged the proposal.

Heard has been inactive on her Instagram since the jury's defamation ruling was delivered.

"The disappointment I feel today is beyond words. I’m heartbroken that the mountain of evidence still was not enough to stand up to the disproportionate power, influence, and sway of my ex-husband,” her latest Instagram post reads. "I’m even more disappointed with what this verdict means for other women. It is a setback. It sets back the clock to a time when a woman who spoke up and spoke out could be publicly shamed and humiliated. It sets back the idea that violence against women is to be taken seriously.”

"I believe Johnny’s attorneys succeeded in getting the jury to overlook the key issue of freedom of speech and ignore evidence that was so conclusive that we won in the UK,” Heard concluded. "I'm sad I lost this case. But I am sadder still that I seem to have lost a right I thought I had as an American – to speak freely and openly."

Depp wrote his own social media post the day the trial ended.

"Six years ago, my life, the life of my children, the lives of those closest to me, and also, the lives of the people, who for many, many years have supported and believed in me were forever changed," Depp posted on Instagram just moments after the verdict. "All in the blink of an eye. False, very serious and criminal allegations were levied at me via the media, which triggered an endless barrage of hateful content, although no charges were ever brought against me. It had already traveled around the world twice within a nanosecond and it had a seismic impact on my life and my career."

"And six years later, the jury gave me my life back. I am truly humbled. My decision to pursue this case, knowing very well the height of the legal hurdles that I would be facing and the inevitable, worldwide spectacle into my life, was only made after considerable thought," Depp continued. "From the very beginning, the goal of bringing this case was to reveal the truth, regardless of the outcome. Speaking the truth was something that I owed to my children and to all those who have remained steadfast in their support of me. I feel at peace knowing I have finally accomplished that."
