Two students test positive for coronavirus at Edmond North HS, ACT officials report
by Kevin Severin & Erika Stanish
Thursday, Jul 23rd 2020
According to officials, neither of the students were experiencing symptoms or had traveled outside of Edmond. (KOKH/FILE)
EDMOND, Okla. (KOKH) —

The Edmond North High School ACT testing staff was notified that two students who took the exam on July 18 have tested positive for the coronavirus.

ADVERTISEMENT

All students and monitors were required to complete coronavirus screening questions.

"According to room assignment, it's likely that you or your child were within the area of one or both of these students for up to 15 minutes," said ACT officials in a letter to students and parents.

According to officials, neither of the students were experiencing symptoms or had traveled outside of Edmond.

Current CDC guidelines recommend self-quarantining for 14 days.

Edmond Public Schools said nearly 200 students were at Edmond North High School that day.

They did not have a comment Thursday but did say it was "apprehensive" to hold the ACT testing at one of its buildings.

President of the Oklahoma Education Association Alicia Priest said state officials must mandate statewide safety protocols in order to keep Oklahoma students and staff safe come fall.

"This is exactly what we’re going to see when we start school back, if we start it back face-to-face without statewide safety protocols put in place," she said.

Priest said the OEA put out a request to have personal protective equipment readily available at all schools, among other things.

"Our governor needs to step up and release some of that stockpile. Not making districts pay for it but as a precaution to keep our kids safe. Let’s get that safety equipment into our schools to keep all of our community safe," she said.

She said the organization also recommended having hand sanitizer and soap available.

"This is sad [but we need] running water in all of the bathrooms," Priest said. "We have schools that we know that sinks are broken. There may be only one sink in a bathroom that has running water. Kids don’t wait in line to do things like that. We’ve got to fix this issue."

She said on top of wearing masks, schools also need good airflow.

"Many of our windows now are not opening in our school buildings. So, we’re going to put 30 kids in a classroom, maybe more, with an adult teaching them without good airflow and not have mandated safety protocols in place? That is a disaster waiting to happen," Priest said.

She called the Oklahoma State Board of Education's decision Thursday to not mandate statewide safety protocols, "disappointing" and "disheartening."

"The ACT did not have a mask mandate. They said it’s recommended and encouraged but it wasn’t mandatory. This is exactly what our State Board of Education has done," Priest said. "It’s suggested it’s recommended but not mandatory. Now we have an entire group of students who have been exposed and we have adults that were monitoring that test who have been exposed."

ACT released the following statement Thursday night:

ADVERTISEMENT
As part of ACT’s test center social distancing guidelines, students and monitors were asked to complete a series of COVID-19 symptom and travel screening questions, instructed to practice social distancing guidelines while on campus, and it was recommended that masks be worn by all. Test centers also used multiple rooms and floors within their buildings to provide additional physical distance between examinees. Test center staff can remove students from the test center if they are reporting symptoms or if they’re non-compliant with these policies. At the time of the test, neither of the students indicated they were experiencing symptoms or had traveled outside of Edmond. Within our communication to students who tested at Edmond North High School, we referred them to the current CDC guidelines which recommend self-quarantining for 14 days after any type of exposure to the virus. We continue to require test centers to abide by the COVID-19-related policies for the health and safety of all test-takers and staff, and expect these requirements to carry on into the fall test dates. If CDC recommendations change, we will adapt our policies to comply with those guidelines.
