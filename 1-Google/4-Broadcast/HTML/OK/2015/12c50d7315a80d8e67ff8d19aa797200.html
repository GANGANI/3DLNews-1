Tulsa man charged after police say he stole ATV's and scooters on Christmas Day

TULSA, Okla. — A man is facing multiple charges after police say he stole multiple ATV's and scooters on Christmas Day.

According to Tulsa Police, officers were out patrolling near 11th and 129th late Christmas night when they saw a truck and trailer speeding.

Officers tried to pull the truck over, but it took off.

After a short chase, the truck crashed into a parked car near 121st.

Police say after the crash, several people got out and took off, but one of the suspects, Rashad Phipps, was quickly taken into custody.

After investigating, officers learned the pickup truck and trailer were both stolen, along with the three ATV's and two motorized scooters. The ATV's and scooters were returned to a nearby business they were stolen from.

Phipps faces multiple charges, including:

  • Possession of a Stolen Vehicle AFCF (After Former Conviction of a Felony)
  • Hit and Run AFCF
  • Resisting Arrest
  • 2nd Degree Burglary AFCF
  • Knowingly Concealing Stolen Property AFCF
  • Four counts of Possession of a Stolen Vehicle

According to the jail, his bond was set at $15,000 and he's bonded out.

The investigation is ongoing and officers are still looking for the other suspects who fled the scene.

Anyone with information is asked to call Tulsa Crime Stoppers at 918-596-COPS.
