Suspects seen fleeing scene of Myrtle Beach T-shirt shop fire caught

by WPDE

Wed, May 18th 2016 at 5:41 AM
Updated Wed, May 18th 2016 at 1:03 PM
Crews on scene of a fire near Ocean Blvd in Myrtle Beach on 5.18.2016

Myrtle Beach, S.C. (WPDE) — Crews responded to a fire inside a building with multiple businesses early Wednesday morning in Myrtle Beach.

Officials on scene said the fire started just after 1 a.m. inside the Wacky T's t-shirt shop near Ocean Boulevard.

Officials said they had to enter the Nightmare Haunted House next door to the t-shirt shop in order to get to the fire.

Two suspects spotted in surveillance footage fleeing the scene of an early morning fire Wednesday were identified during a traffic stop a short time later, according to our media partner, The Sun News.

Myrtle Beach police reviewed camera footage in the area and spotted two men running southbound towards the intersection of Mr. Joe White Avenue and Withers Alley around the time of the fire.

Surveillance footage showed the men getting into a silver sedan and leaving the area traveling northbound on Ocean Boulevard, according to an incident report. An officer spotted the vehicle a short time later and conducted a traffic stop. The two men were identified, but no charges have been filed so far.

Preliminary finding suggest that a t-shirt press may have been left on inside the store, according to Battalion Chief Bruce Arnel.

Lt. Christian Sliker said one firefighter was hurt while fighting the fire. He sustained minor injuries and is at home recovering.

Myrtle Beach Fire Rescue and Myrtle Beach police responded to the scene.

No word on the cause or extent of the damage.

The fire is under investigation.
