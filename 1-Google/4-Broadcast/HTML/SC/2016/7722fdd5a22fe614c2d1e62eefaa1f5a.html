Florence family says they're living in fear with loved one's killer not yet caught

by Tonya Brown

Thu, May 5th 2016 at 6:25 PM
Updated Fri, May 6th 2016 at 10:14 PM
3
VIEW ALL PHOTOS
The family of Ivory Henry III, who say they live in fear because his killer has not yet been caught. (Tonya Brown / WPDE)

Florence, S.C. (WPDE) — The family of Ivory Henry, III says they're constantly looking over their shoulders.

Henry was shot and killed on May 9, 2015 at the Zaxby's on Highway 52 in Florence where he worked as a manager.

Henry's family said they are living in fear because the person who killed their loved one hasn't been caught.

"And the police did share with us to be careful. Because it could be somebody that was at the house during the death. You know, we don't know," said Ivory Henry, Jr.

The family said they can't understand why anyone would want Henry dead. They said he bothered no one and kept to himself.

"I don't have no clue whatsoever. He don't hang with nobody. He always quiet," said Tony Felder, Henry's brother.

"It's been hard. You know everyday you open up your eyes. You're thinking about it. It'll never be the same no more," said Kimbery Felder, Henry's sister.

Henry's wife said the past year has been like a nightmare.

She said she never imagined she would be a widow at 26 years old and left to raise her four small children as a single parent.

"We grew up together. Living and he's not here, it's not right. It hurts a lot. I just really want justice. And that's the only thing I can ask for," Aneglica Henry said.

Henry said she feels her road to justice is being blocked by the Florence County Sheriff's Office.

She added investigators are not returning phone calls, emails or text messages.

"I feel like my husband is just forgotten about, I do," said Henry.

Florence County Sheriff's Office Major Michael Nunn said deputies have been diligently working the case from day one and they've been following up on every lead in the case.

"The case is continuing to be investigated. As we receive tips we follow up on them. If there's something that we need to touch base with the family on for clarification or additional information. We certainly do that. But not every lead will result in contact with the family. But they can rest assured that the investigation is ongoing," explained Nunn.

The Henry family said they're praying the case is solved and soon.

"I don't know what to think. I'm still looking for some closure. Hopefully this will refresh somebody's memories and hopefully somebody will come forward with something," said Henry.

Anyone with information is asked to call the Florence County Sheriff's Office at 843-665-2121.
