'I'm scared': Patients face uncertainty as local dialysis center prepares to shut down
by Sydaija Smalls
Wednesday, Dec 27th 2023
4

'I'm scared': Patients face uncertainty as local dialysis center prepares to shut down (Provided)
MONCKS CORNER, S.C. (WCIV) —

Every minute counts.

ADVERTISEMENT

Meliah Freeman has Lupus, and she is now waiting for an organ transplant after her kidneys failed two years ago.

Mom and daughter are clinging to each other and their faith after receiving news they said felt like a setback.

"A man informed me that they would be closing in 30 days," Yvonne Richardson said. "I was very distraught because this dialysis center -- the staffing is awesome."

Richardson is the mother of a patient who gets dialysis three times a week at Satellite Healthcare in Moncks Corner. Treatments there can last more than three hours.

"She stays a little longer because sometimes she had some issue bleeding longer than usual," Richardson said. "After that, she's drained. Sometimes, she's cramping and not feeling well, so she sleeps all day. Sometimes, she's really nauseated and painful. It's tough for her."

The center closes in three days, and Richardson is frantically searching to find a new facility.

"Nobody's telling us why they're closing down," Richardson said. "I could be a little bit more understanding, but they weren't informative about why they were closing down."

Read more: "'She put time on my clock': Cousins become forever connected through a kidney transplant."

Staff notified patients that assistance would be provided for smooth transitions, but Richardson said she hasn't gotten much help.

"I was informed that in the three centers that we have, there isn't any room," Richardson said. "There isn't any room for any patients right now."

Time is of the essence, and Richardson only wants her daughter's health to improve.

"She has to have her care," Richardson said. "For a dialysis patient, they cannot go six to seven days without treatment, or they could die. I'm scared. I don't know what to do."

News 4 called the Satellite Healthcare Office in Moncks Corner and was redirected to their headquarters.
