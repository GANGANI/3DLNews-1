S.C. removes Disney from State's investment list for joining 'far-left' activist
by Ian Kayanja
Tuesday, Dec 5th 2023
FILE - S.C. removes Disney from State's investment list for joining 'far-left' activist (AP Photo/Richard Drew, File).
CHARLESTON COUNTY, S.C. (WCIV) —

The Walt Disney Company was removed from South Carolina's investment portfolio, State Treasurer Curtis Loftis announced Tuesday.

ADVERTISEMENT

The State Treasurer's Office portfolio contains $105 million of Disney debt instruments that will mature and will not be replaced, the office said.

"Disney has abandoned its fiduciary responsibility to its investors and customers by joining far-left activists in boycotting legal, taxpaying, employment-creating corporations to further Disney's political agenda," Loftis said of the move in a statement posted to the office's website.

Read more: "Disney fights DeSantis' effort to have judge removed from free speech lawsuit."

"Multi-billion-dollar corporations should not engage in boycotts designed to silence legitimate debate," he continued.

"Since America’s founding, freedom of speech has been one of its core principals, and Disney should not engage in nefarious practices aimed at silencing those with less power and money.”

In recent weeks, Disney, among 200 other companies, including Airbnb, Amazon, Coca-Cola, and Microsoft, paused or stopped advertising on X due to Elon Musk’s controversial comments he made on the social media platform.

Due to the advertisement boycott, it is reported that X could lose over $75 million in revenue.

Read more: "Elon Musk blasts advertisers who pulled money from X: 'Go f--- yourself'."

Disney has also been in a long-standing feud with Florida Governor and Republican presidential hopeful Ron DeSantis, dating back to early 2023.

It was in the heart of that political battle that former South Carolina Governor Nikki Haley, who since late April announced she is running for president in the Republican primary, stated South Carolina would openly accept Disney and its "70,000+ jobs" in a post on X.

Read more: "Florida Gov. DeSantis says Disney lawsuit is political."

"Hey @Disney, my home state will happily accept your 70,000+ jobs if you want to leave Florida," she wrote in the post. "We’ve got great weather, great people, and it’s always a great day in South Carolina! SC’s not woke, but we’re not sanctimonious about it either."

Haley went on to say: “South Carolina was a very anti-woke state. It still is. If Disney would like to move their hundreds of thousands of jobs to South Carolina and bring the billions of dollars with them, I’ll let them know I’ll be happy to meet them in South Carolina and introduce them to the governor and legislature that would welcome it.”

Following Lofits' announcement, News 4 reached out to Haley's campaign and is awaiting a response.
