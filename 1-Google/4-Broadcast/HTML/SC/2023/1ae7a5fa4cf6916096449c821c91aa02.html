Only on WACH FOX News: Owners make peace with families over missing headstones

COLUMBIA, S.C. (WACH) — Last November, several Midlands families were upset after they say the headstones of their loved ones were missing at a cemetery located at 8909 Wilson Blvd.

The families say they were driving by when they noticed the land had been cleared.

Since then law enforcement, historic preservationists, planning and zoning and the Coroner’s Office have been looking into what took place.

“It appears that it has been cleaned up a bit and we did notice there were missing gravestones after the flood was damaged," said Deputy Coroner William Stevens.

Now, the Coroner's Office is looking to identify the more than 50 gravesites and the bodies that call Wilson Blvd Cemetery home.

During the investigation the current property owner Carmen Romero, who lives with her son Richard Romero, says they were given the land through a deed and were not told about the removal of any headstones when they had the land cleared.

During the investigation the cemetery was not listed in the Richland County property records.

Since finding out about the missing headstones, the Romero's have worked to bring peace to the families with loved ones buried at the site by having an easement drawn up that separates the land so that families can visit loved ones at any time.

I think it's a pretty good idea, but I still have a lot of mixed emotions about it," said Emily Gantt, who's family is buried at the cemetery.

According to the previous owner, the property was a church called St. Peters Overcoming Church and says the cemetery files were destroyed in the 2015 flood leaving dozens of bodies unclaimed.

Right now, there are more than thirty unidentified gravesites at 8909 Wilson Blvd.

Deputy Coroner Stevens is calling on anyone with family, friends or members of the previous church to reach out to the Richland County Coroners Office to help identify the bodies.
