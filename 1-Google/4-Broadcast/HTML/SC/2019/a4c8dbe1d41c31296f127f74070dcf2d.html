Teacher calls bullying a problem in Colleton County after fight sends student to hospital

by Brodie Hart

Tue, March 26th 2019 at 6:31 PM
Updated Wed, March 27th 2019 at 12:33 PM
Forest Hills Elementary School, Hiers Corner Road, Walterboro, SC (Google Earth)

WALTERBORO, S.C. — A Colleton County teacher says bullying is a problem in the district, just one day after a fight at Forest Hills Elementary sent a 5th grade girl to the hospital.

"I feel that our children aren’t safe," said the teacher, who wished to remain anonymous. She said she's witnessed safety issues that have impacted her classroom and her own home.

"My child had personally went through it on two different occasions," she said. "At one point she had to switch schools and it was not handled properly."

The Colleton County School District did not confirm the identity of the students involved in the fight.

ABC News 4 confirmed a Go Fund Me started by the girl's family, however, listed her as unresponsive as of Tuesday afternoon.

Officials at the MUSC Children's Hospital also confirmed Tuesday afternoon the Walterboro 5th grader was listed in critical condition.

"It’s making me want to leave, making me want to pack up my children and just move on," the Colleton County teacher said.

She says she's reported several incidents of bullying in her class room this year.

"I immediately stop it when I see it. If I notice it as a continuous thing, I report it."

An official with the Colleton County School District confirmed 11 people were part of a crisis team at Forest Hills Elementary Tuesday, counseling students in the wake of Monday's fight.

Officials could not confirm if the fight would be classified as an instance of bullying. The other student involved is suspended from school until the investigation is complete.

The Colleton County Sheriff's Office confirms the fight is still under investigation.

Officials confirm there is not a dedicated school resource officer at Forest Hills Elementary.

Two School Resource Officers are shared among the five elementary schools in Colleton County.
