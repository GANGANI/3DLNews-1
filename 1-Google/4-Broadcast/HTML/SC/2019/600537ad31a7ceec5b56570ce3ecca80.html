Lara Trump disputes 2020 Dems' attacks, defends progress under President Trump

by Stephen Loiaconi

Fri, September 13th 2019 at 2:23 PM
Updated Fri, September 13th 2019 at 7:46 PM
Lara Trump, the president's daughter-in-law and campaign adviser, speaks to WPEC about statements made during the third Democratic primary debate. (WPEC)

    TOPICS:

    American People Of German DescentBusinesspeopleClimate Change Skepticism And DenialDonald TrumpDonald Trump 2016 Presidential CampaignForeign Policy Of The Donald Trump AdministrationMike PencePolitics of the United States

WASHINGTON (Sinclair Broadcast Group) — President Donald Trump’s reelection campaign defended his record on the economy and foreign policy Friday, rebutting the latest round of attacks from Democrats competing for the chance to run against him in 2020.

The top 10 Democratic presidential candidates spent much of a three-hour debate in Houston Thursday night assailing Trump’s handling of trade, diplomacy, and pretty much everything else.

“I think this president has done so much in his first two-and-a-half years in office,” Lara Trump, the president’s daughter-in-law and a senior adviser to his campaign, told Sinclair Friday.

Democrats argued during the debate that most of what Trump has done has been bad for the country and for average Americans, and he has not acted in the areas where government intervention is most needed. They also accused him of exacerbating cultural divisions, with some calling him racist and blaming him for a mass shooting fueled by anti-immigrant hatred in Texas last month.

“A racism and violence that had long been a part of America was welcomed out into the open and directed to my hometown of El Paso, Texas, where 22 people were killed, dozens more grievously injured by a man carrying a weapon he should never have been able to buy in the first place, inspired to kill by our president,” former Texas Rep. Beto O’Rourke said.

“It’s rather disgusting that this continues to be brought up and thrown at this president,” Lara Trump said of the racism accusations.

She praised the president’s response to mass shootings that have occurred on his watch, stressing that problems with gun violence in America predate his administration and insisting he is committed to finding ways to prevent these tragedies in the future.

“Fortunately, we have a president who now wants to address that,” she said.

The president has so far been vague about how he intends to do that, but his adviser made clear his solution will not include a mandatory buyback of assault-style rifles that O’Rourke has proposed.

“Hell, yes, we’re going to take your AR-15, your AK-47,” O’Rourke said Thursday night. “We’re not going to allow them to be used against fellow Americans anymore.”

Lara Trump accused Democrats of promoting socialism and “government-run everything,” including costly and complicated health care reform plans. She warned their policies could hinder the growth seen in the economy and the job market under President Trump.

“I think most Americans want to see four more years of a great economy,” she said, noting that candidates did not say much about the current state of the economy Thursday.

Several Democrats alleged that Trump has no coherent strategy for trade negotiations with China or dismantling North Korea’s nuclear weapons program.

“He has put us in the middle of this trade war and he is treating our farmers and our workers like poker chips in one of his bankrupt casinos. And if we are not careful, he is going to bankrupt this country,” said Sen. Amy Klobuchar, D-Minn.

Lara Trump maintained progress is being made on resolving both conflicts, though she acknowledged shutting down Kim Jong Un’s nuclear ambitions would probably not be accomplished in President Trump’s first term.

“We are also, it feels like, very close to seeing some really, really big step there in denuclearizing the Korean peninsula,” she said, suggesting Trump can complete this deal and others if voters give him four more years in office.

President Trump is trailing the top Democratic candidates in head-to-head matchups in most recent polls, but Lara Trump dismissed those numbers, claiming the campaign’s internal polling is much better.

“Now, more than ever, people are afraid to voice their support for Donald Trump,” she said.

Between socialized medicine, higher taxes, and taking away law-abiding citizens’ guns, she predicted the Democratic agenda will scare Americans who are satisfied with the economy and prefer to stay on the course Trump has set.

“They want to know that the future for their families looks good,” Trump said.
