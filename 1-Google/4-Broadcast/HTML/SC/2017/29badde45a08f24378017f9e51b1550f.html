SeekingArrangement website offers to "foot the bill" for CCU cheerleaders' case

by Mikayla Mercer

Fri, April 7th 2017 at 4:34 PM

Conway, S.C. (WPDE) — Representatives from SeekingArrangement.com, the website and app that some Coastal Carolina University cheerleaders have been accused of using to run an escort service, has now offered to cover the legal fees for the accused cheerleaders.

RELATED: Attorney for 5 CCU cheerleaders responds to incident report mentioning escort service

ABC15 News reached out to SeekingArrangement.com. ReflexMedia Inc., the marketing company that represents SeekingArrangement.com, responded by email Friday afternoon.

ReflexMedia Inc. also represents a number of other companies, including MissTravel, WhatsYourPrice, SeekingMillionaire, Let's Talk Sugar, and PairMeUp.

Maura Welp, Public Relations Manager for ReflexMedia Inc. said, "We are a little disappointed in the claims that the school is making against these young women. SeekingArrangement.com is a dating site, it is not a site for escorts."

Welp's email also included the following statement from founder and CEO Brandon Wade:

"To portray these women as “prostitutes” just because you do not understand or agree with their dating choice is bullying. We will not stand for slut shaming, and find it wildly inappropriate for a public university to comment and pass judgment on the dating habits of their students.Sugar Babies are not escorts they are ambitious, goal oriented individuals who is looking for a specific kind of relationship.”

Welp said SeekingArrangement.com "does not allow and specifically prohibits any kind of pay-per-meet or transactional type relationship."

She also said the company will be reaching out to the cheerleaders' legal counsel to offer to "foot the bill" for the cheerleaders' case.

RELATED: Anonymous letter sent to CCU officials alleges cheerleaders involved in escort service

Last week, Coastal Carolina University suspended the cheerleading team indefinitely pending a conduct investigation.

The university refused to say any more about the case and said, at the time, that there were no incident reports to release.

At the time, the team responded, calling the accusations false and said that they have "led to harassment on campus as well as through social media."

Early Thursday, Lawrence issued a statement describing the suspension of the team as "unprecedented" and "disheartening."

Thursday evening, an incident report regarding what led to the suspension was released in response to a Freedom of Information Act request.

According to the newly obtained report, several university officials received a letter last month alleging that CCU cheerleaders were operating an escort service and were working in strip clubs and prostituting themselves.
