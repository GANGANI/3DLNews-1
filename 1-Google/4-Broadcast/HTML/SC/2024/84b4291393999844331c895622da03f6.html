Former UNCP student dead after shooting near campus in Robeson County

by Tonya Brown, Brianna Allison

Wed, February 7th 2024 at 5:41 PM
Updated Thu, February 8th 2024 at 3:14 PM
3
VIEW ALL PHOTOS
Campus police responded to an active shooter situation at UNC Pembroke on Wednesday, Feb. 7, 2024. This situation is now all clear. (Credit: Jordie Brotherton/WPDE)

ROBESON COUNTY, N.C. (WPDE) — UPDATE: Parents devastated after son killed in UNC Pembroke shooting: 'Just a living nightmare'

According to UNC Pembroke officials, a former UNCP student died following a shooting near campus on Wednesday.

They said that campus police responded to reports of shots fired around 4:45 p.m. at the Courtyard Apartments. When they arrived at the scene, they did not find any shooters but located shell casings on the ground.

Shortly after, the Robeson County Sheriff's Office responded to a second incident off campus, university officials stated.

Sources involved in the investigation told ABC15 that a gunshot victim was dropped off at a church across the street from the school.

The official with Pembroke Rescue Squad said that they responded and called for the gunshot victim to be airlifted to the hospital, however, the victim passed away before that could happen.

The original alert message from the university said:

Alert: UNC Pembroke emergency notification. Active shooter. University Courtyard apartments.

At the time of the incident, people on campus were being told not to leave their facility.

The campus police then said the situation was all clear. UNCP's social post said the area was "considered safe and secure."

Following the shooting incident, the university announced classes that begin before 11 a.m. on Thursday are canceled.

They also made students aware that counseling and psychological services are available on the second floor of the Brave Health Center.

Additionally, the school said students who live at the Courtyard Apartments and are not able to access their room should go to the second floor of the Chavis Center and get assistance from the Housing and Residence Life Office.

The investigation is ongoing. We are working to confirm additional details with law enforcement.
