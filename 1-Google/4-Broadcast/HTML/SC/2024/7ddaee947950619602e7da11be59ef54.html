Students protest CCU, call for transparency & safety amid Donald Trump's rally on campus

by Brandi Martin

Sat, February 10th 2024 at 2:43 PM
Updated Sun, February 11th 2024 at 9:03 AM
5
VIEW ALL PHOTOS
Protestors march the CCU campus ahead of former president Donald Trump's campaign stop. (Credit: Seth Herlinger/WPDE)

CONWAY, S.C. (WPDE) — A group of Coastal Carolina University students are calling for better communication from their school amid former president Donald Trump's rally on campus Saturday afternoon.

Students gathered on campus holding signs with slogans such as "There Won't be a Fuss if You Just CC Us," "Why is Info Being Kept Private at our Public Institution," and "How Did the News Know Before Us? No CCU Alert or Nothing."

Student protesters said that they are concerned about student safety due to the lack of communication with students ahead of the rally.

They claim that the university did not give students sufficient notice that the campaign event was taking place even though it is being held near the residence halls.

Due to the lack of notice, the protesters claim that some students are locked in their rooms because they feel unsafe leaving and were unable to leave campus in time.

They added that because most of the university's population is out-of-state students, many students did not have the opportunity to stay elsewhere during the rally.

The university released the following statement:

Free speech is a key part of the learning experience at CCU. As an institution dedicated to fostering an environment of free expression, we respect our students’ right to peacefully voice their concerns.
Administrators were present and answered questions from students during the protest. Administrators will also meet with students at a later date to engage in further dialogue, creating an additional venue for concerns from students to be both heard and considered.

Officials with the university added in a statement given to ABC15:

The University worked diligently to provide the campus community with event details as they were confirmed. CCU’s Department of Public Safety and various departments on campus worked closely with local, state, and federal officials as well as law enforcement regarding the logistics of the event which came together according to the following timeline:

The university then provided the following timeline:

Monday, Feb. 5

  • The signed contract for the event was received at approximately 8 p.m.

Tuesday, Feb. 6

  • CCU worked with the campaign staff and relevant agencies to acquire and confirm details surrounding the logistics and requirements of the event.

Wednesday, Feb. 7

  • Approx. 8:50 a.m. -- An email was sent to campus acknowledging the fact that there would be a campaign event for Donald Trump on campus and explaining parking implications, as that was the extent of the information that was confirmed at the time. Donald Trump’s name was used in the subject line of the email as well as in the headline of the body of the email.
  • Approx. 9:45 p.m. -- An email was sent providing further details, including the increased security presence due to increased traffic on campus.
