Donald Trump makes campaign stop in Conway ahead of SC Republican presidential primary

by BRANDI MARTIN | WPDE Staff

Sat, February 10th 2024 at 8:38 AM
Updated Sat, February 10th 2024 at 4:28 PM
22
VIEW ALL PHOTOS
Donald Trump speaks during his campaign event at Coastal Carolina University. (Credit: Jenna Herazo/WPDE)

CONWAY, S.C. (WPDE) — Former President Donald Trump was in Conway Saturday afternoon campaigning to voters.

The 2024 GOP presidential candidate spoke at a Get Out to Vote Rally at Coastal Carolina University.

The rally was held at Coastal Carolina University's HTC Center located on Founders Drive. Doors opened at 11 a.m. and the event began at 1 p.m., with Trump taking the stage around 2:30 p.m.

Trump discussed the key issues of the 2024 election, including immigration, international relations, crime rates and LGBTQ+ issues.

Trump's speech was briefly interrupted by protesters who were ushered out of the building by security. The crowd responded with the chant "F*** Joe Biden."

The former president ended his event by declaring "We will fight for America like no one has before. 2024 is our final battle."

He then requested that his supporters "Get out and vote."

The South Carolina Republican Party's Presidential Primary will be held on Saturday, February 24, and the early voting period is from Monday, February 12 through Thursday, February 22.

Anyone participating in the Republican Primary needs to have been registered by Thursday, January 25.
