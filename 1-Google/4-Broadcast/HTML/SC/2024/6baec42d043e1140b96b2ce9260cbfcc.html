Officials continue search for suspect in shooting of 2 Tennessee sheriff's deputies

by WZTV Staff

Sat, February 10th 2024 at 4:14 PM
4
VIEW ALL PHOTOS
Kenneth DeHart (TBI)

BLOUNT COUNTY, Tenn. (WZTV) — Police in Tennessee arrested the brother and girlfriend of the man wanted for allegedly shooting two deputies Thursday night.

Police say Kenneth DeHart, 42, is still at large after shooting two Blount County Sheriff's deputies. Deputy Greg McGowan was killed and Deputy Shelby Eggers was shot in the leg. On Friday, the sheriff's office arrested DeHart's brother and girlfriend.

Deputies arrested 41-year-old Marcus DeHart, Kenneth's brother, for accessory after the fact. The sheriff's office says Marcus helped his brother after the shooting.

He is being held in the Blount County Jail on $1 million bond with a court appearance scheduled for Monday.

According to reports, deputies also arrested the suspect's girlfriend, Carrie Mathews, 32, for accessory after the fact. She is currently being held in the Sevier County Jail.

BCSO and others are now offering more than $80,000 as a reward to find Kenneth DeHart.
