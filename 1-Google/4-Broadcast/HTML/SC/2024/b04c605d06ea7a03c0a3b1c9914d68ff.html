Trump Campaign rallies voters in candidate's first SC stop ahead of Primary

by Andrew James

Sat, February 10th 2024 at 6:52 PM
Updated Sun, February 11th 2024 at 8:34 AM
4
VIEW ALL PHOTOS
Trump Campaign rallies voters in candidate's 1st SC stop ahead of Primary (wpde)

CONWAY, S.C. (WPDE) — Former President Donald Trump brought his fiery campaign for re-election to Conway on Saturday.

The GOP frontrunner for the 2024 nomination spoke for nearly two hours to a full HTC Center.

“Fourteen days from now each and every one of you is going get out and vote and deliver a gigantic win right here in South Carolina," Trump said. “We want to send a signal for November.”

RELATED: Donald Trump makes campaign stop in Conway ahead of SC Republican presidential primary

He spoke on a variety of issues and subjects including security at the Southern Border, the current President's age and mental state, and the ongoing international conflicts in Israel and Ukraine.

Trump is in SC to push voters to go to the polls as he faces a continued challenge for the party nomination by former South Carolina Gov. Nikki Haley.

"Haley is the candidate of globalists and warmongers who want to spend trillions. Trillions on endless wars," Trump said.

Trump, however, spent a majority of his speech focused on Biden and talking about how the issues with higher consumer prices and wars in Europe and the Middle East would not have existed if he had been re-elected in 2020.

"We wouldn't have had any of those problems," Trump said. "We wouldn't have had Israel attacked. We wouldn't have had Ukraine and Russia fighting and killing everybody and blowing up their cities. And we wouldn't have inflation."

Trump also continued his false claims that the 2020 election was stolen and defended his actions with classified documents. The former President faces felony charges for allegedly mishandling top secret records.

“The real problem facing our country is not Joe Biden’s decline, the real problem is that Joe Biden is causing America's decline," Trump said.

RELATED: Students protest CCU, call for transparency & safety amid Donald Trump's rally on campus

When Trump referred to Haley, he continued a message that his campaign has carried for weeks calling her a friend to foreign advisories and criticizing her stance on raising the age to receive Social Security benefits.

Haley's campaign has launched a series of new ads, similar to the tone that won her the Governor's office in South Carolina. In them, she heavily criticizes Trump and Biden for their age and once again talks about how a woman needs to take on the "male-heavy" establishment in Washington akin to her message concerning Columbia when she ran for governor.

Trump, however, seemed unbothered by Haley and at one point said her challenge to his race for the GOP nomination was 'irrelevant.'

"We have to win, not against her, that's irrelevant," Trump said. "That's why you have to vote, we have to set a signal out to the whole world that come November it's going to be different."

Trump's visit brought in voters from both South and North Carolina with Trump's influence heavy in North Carolina since he has family in Wilmington. Trump visited Horry County which also carries the third largest registered voter population in the state and aside from Greenville County, the largest Republican-centered voting population.

Haley has secured key endorsements in the area from the likes of Georgetown Mayor Carol Jayroe and Myrtle Beach Mayor Brenda Bethune.

Ahead of his speech, Trump had several state dignitaries speak including 7th District Congressman Russell Fry, Lt. Governor Pam Evette, and SC Governor Henry McMaster.

Early voting for the GOP Primary in South Carolina begins on Monday at select locations dictated by county election offices. The GOP Presidential Preference Primary is Saturday, Feb. 24.
