Brother, girlfriend charged with aiding Blount County shooting suspect; manhunt ongoing

by WZTV

Sat, February 10th 2024 at 9:37 PM
Updated Sat, February 10th 2024 at 10:19 PM
5
VIEW ALL PHOTOS
Kenneth DeHart, photo from Febuary 8th, 2024.

BLOUNT COUNTY, Tenn. (WZTV) — The Blount County Sheriff's Office has arrested the brother and girlfriend of the man wanted for shooting two deputies Thursday night.

Kenneth DeHart, 42, is still at large after shooting two Blount County Sheriff's deputies, killing one of them. On Friday, the sheriff's office arrested DeHart's brother and girlfriend.

Deputies arrested Marcus DeHart, 41, the brother of the shooter, for accessory after the fact. The sheriff's office says Marcus helped his brother after the shooting.

He is being held in the Blount County Jail on $1 million bond with a court appearance scheduled for Monday.

According to reports, deputies also arrested the suspect's girlfriend, Carrie Mathews, 32, for accessory after the fact. She is currently being held in the Sevier County Jail.

BCSO and others are now offering more than $80,000 as a reward to find Kenneth DeHart
