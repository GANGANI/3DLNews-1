Blount County Sheriff’s Office increases reward in manhunt to $100K

DeHart is accused of shooting and killing a Blount County Sheriff’s Office deputy and injuring another.
By Josh Jarnagin
Published: Feb. 10, 2024 at 6:03 PM CST

BLOUNT COUNTY, Tenn. (WVLT) - The Blount County Sheriff’s Office has increased the reward for information leading to Kenneth DeHart’s arrest to $100,000.

BCSO Chief Deputy Jeff French provided the following update on the investigation on the department’s Facebook page:

DeHart is accused of shooting and killing a Blount County Sheriff’s Office deputy and injuring another.

“Today, nearly 150 dedicated law enforcement officers and federal agents from departments throughout East Tennessee, have been tirelessly combing through dense forests, rugged terrain, and executing search warrants in our relentless pursuit of DeHart. Despite our concerted efforts, he remains at large,” French said. “I want to stress the gravity of this situation. Kenneth DeHart is considered armed and extremely dangerous. We cannot allow him to roam freely, posing a threat to our community.”

The BCSO announced late Thursday that two deputies, Greg McCowan and Shelby Eggers, stopped DeHart as he was driving. The situation escalated, BCSO said, and DeHart shot both deputies, killing McCowan and injuring Eggers.

This reward joins several other contributions Sheriff James Lee Berrong announced on Friday, including a $10,000 reward from the U.S. Marshals Service, another $10,000 from Blount County, $2,000 from East Tennessee Valley Crime Stoppers, $2,500 from the Tennessee Bureau of Investigation, $10,000 from Knox County and $40,000 from Smith & Wesson CEO Mark Smith.
