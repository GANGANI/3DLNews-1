Catastrophic tornado hits Middle Tennessee: Six lives claimed, dozens hurt, buildings damaged

by WTVC

Sun, December 10th 2023 at 10:57 AM
9
VIEW ALL PHOTOS
WZTV: Child among 3 killed after tornado slams Clarksville (Photo: Brittany Black)

CLARKSVILLE & NASHVILLE, Tenn. — At least six people died Saturday when a catastrophic tornado outbreak slammed Middle Tennessee.

The Tennessee Emergency Management Agency (TEMA) has activated to a Level 3-State of Emergency.

Authorities say three people, two adults and a child, were killed in Clarksville. Twenty three others are being treated at an area hospital with injuries, according to the Montgomery County Sheriff's Office.

In Nashville, WZTV reports three people died on Nesbitt Lane in Madison, Tennessee, two adults and one child. Metro Nashville Police have identified them as Joseph Dalton, 37, Floridema Gabriel Perez, 31, and her son, Anthony Elmer Mendez, age 2.

The image below is from the Nashville Office of Emergency Management. It shows part of the damage on Nesbitt Lane. You can see the debris piled up, cinderblocks, parts of roofs, and even a red car beat up in the storm.

Currently, the Madison area seems to be hit the hardest, according to the Nashville Office of Emergency Management.

Chilling video from a Tennessee Department of Transportation camera was captured just before 6 p.m. in northern Nashville.

You can see the cloud traveling in the distance, and then a bright flash. We are told that's the tornado knocking out power stations.

"It was loud!" said one resident, interviewed by WTVZ in Nashville. "It sounded like a hundred freight trains."

One Hendersonville business, Big Play Entertainment, told WZTV that the whole back of their building is gone.

"As the weather started to get bad we took everyone away from the windows and into the center of the building." says Kaleb Moon, an employee at Big Play Entertainment. "And as we did that, that's when the wall caved in and the roof started falling through."

Employees say there were about 200 people inside for holiday and birthday parties when the tornado hit. Workers say it happened quickly and lasted less than thirty seconds.

"You could hear all the kids screaming and crying," says Karina Agner, an employee at Big Play Entertainment. "It was really devastating. The debris and the smoke that happened immediately after was like.. there's not words for it. I was like ' wow, there's no way this just happened.'"

Thankfully, no one there suffered any major injuries, just small scratches and bruises, according to MNPD.

The City of Nashville says other buildings have heavy damage as well, but first responders are still assessing the extent. They are asking folks to stay off the roads so emergency crews can get through to clean up and respond to emergencies.

A lot of people couldn't get to their safe place as the severe weather moved through, according to Tennessee Highway Patrol.

The image below is in Springfield. You can see a car overturned laying on it's side.

Power poles bent in half also created a gridlock situation on I-65. There were several reported accidents and problem spots in both directions on I-65, says THP. The interstate was so jammed up in some spots, officers say some people were driving backwards to try to get out of harms way.

Another video was taken from a road in Clarksville as one family tried to get home. You can see the debris swirling and powerlines sparking. They also captured a car upside down in the middle of the road right after. We are told everyone made it out of that car safely.

Clarksville Mayor Joe Pitts declared a State of Emergency and public curfew to be enforced for the city of Clarksville Saturday night.

Many in the midstate are still without power. WZTV says power crews are out right now trying to make a dent in these massive numbers. About 36-thousand Nashville Electric Service customers were the dark just in the metro area. Nashville Electric Service says extra crews have been called in.

Other areas including southern Kentucky were also battered by tornadoes throughout Saturday evening. A long track tornado was spotted in Bowling Green, Kentucky. This is the same area where 17 people died after a different tornado almost two years ago.

At least 56 million Americans are on alert for severe flooding, according to That storm now taking aim at the northeast.
