After latest deadly crash, CPD reveals car accidents are increasing on Shallowford Road

by WTVC, Isaiah Kim-Martinez

Wed, November 6th 2019 at 6:14 AM
Updated Fri, November 8th 2019 at 7:46 AM
6
VIEW ALL PHOTOS
The accident happened at about 2 a.m. (Image: WTVC)

CHATTANOOGA, Tenn. — A big guy with a big heart.

That’s how Bubba Cagle describes his friend Eric Pitts through a Facebook message sent to us.

Pitts, 35, was identified today as the man who was killed after driving into a traffic light pole at a high rate of speed, splitting the car in half on Shallowford Road.

Cagle also called Pitts a “hard working dad who loved his children."

For the community, this accident has shed light on just how often crashes happen on intersections on the 7000 block of Shallowford Road.

“It’s a fairly dangerous road," says Latoya Rollins, who says she works at the nearby Hometown Suites.

Rollins says this is far from the first accident she's heard about in the area.

She says she’s had some close calls with incoming vehicles herself.

"Those are two very dangerous intersections -- cars rarely stop there," she said. "Even if it’s a red light, they’ll still keep going."

We reached out to Chattanooga Police to get an exact number of the crashes and deaths on this block in recent years.

Their data shows total crashes increasing in each of the last three years, totaling 445 crashes during that span.

Even though there's only been one previous death recently -- back in 2018 -- there's been 79 total people hurt.

And comparing those numbers to another busy intersection like East Brainerd Road and Gunbarrel Road -- Shallowford Road has topped it each of the last three years.

UPDATE (Thursday morning):

Chattanooga Police have identified the man killed in the wreck as 35-year-old Eric Lewis Pitts.

UPDATE (Wednesday evening)

We now know more information about the deadly crash that closed down parts of Shallowford Road this morning.

Earlier, crews towed away a car that was split in half.

Chattanooga Police say the driver topped a hill at a high rate of speed and hit a traffic light pole.

The crash sent debris all over the road, and the road was closed for more than six hours this morning.

The driver was ejected from the car and died at the scene.

CPD says the victim was a 35-year-old man.

We spoke with a man who watched it all unfold from a nearby gas station:

"You could hear however fast he was going and by the time I walked through the door of the gas station, you just heard loud boom then you kept hearing 'boom, boom, boom,'" Lemarcus Francois told us.

If you know any information about this crash, please call police immediately.

See our previous live coverage from the scene below.

UPDATE (Wednesday 10:45 a.m.):

Shallowford Road remains closed.

Chattanooga Police have said in a news release that the still-unidentified victim was a 35-year-old man.

Police say the Infinity was traveling east at a high rate of speed, topped the hill and began to lose control. It slid to the left, crossed over into oncoming traffic lanes, and struck the curb in front of the Wendy's restaurant.

The car slid sideways into a traffic light pole, and separated into two pieces.

The accident ejected the driver from his car, and he was pronounced dead on the scene.

Chattanooga Police ask anyone with information regarding this incident to call 423-698-2525 or submit a tip via the CPD Mobile App. You can remain anonymous.

Depend on us to keep you posted.

PREVIOUSLY:

Chattanooga Police confirm that one person is dead after a serious accident early Wednesday morning, down the road from Hamilton Place Mall.

Mariah Rock reported live from the scene:

The accident happened a little before 4 a.m. at in the 7000 block of Shallowford Road. Our crew on the scene says debris is all over the roadway, with the vehicle apparently split in half.

Chattanooga Police say the investigation into this crash will close the road to traffic in both directions "for several hours." Drivers are advised to take an alternate route.
