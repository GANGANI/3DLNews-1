Man airlifted after crash between garbage truck & VW on Lee Hwy in Ooltewah Wednesday

by WTVC

Wed, November 27th 2019 at 3:10 PM
Updated Thu, November 28th 2019 at 11:15 PM
11
VIEW ALL PHOTOS
Collegedale's police chief says several people are lucky they weren't seriously injured or killed after a bad crash on Lee Highway Wednesday afternoon. (Images: WTVC, Tri-Community Volunteer FD Staff Photographer Reed)

OOLTEWAH, Tenn. — Collegedale's police chief says several people are lucky they weren't seriously injured or killed after a bad crash on Lee Highway Wednesday afternoon.

Chief Brian Hickman says just before 2 p.m., they responded to a report of an explosion on Lee Highway near Edgmon Road in Ooltewah, and found a crash involving a Volkswagen Beetle and a garbage truck.

Chief Hickman says prior to this point, they'd been told to be on the lookout for a VW driving all over the roadway - and it was this vehicle was believed to be the one in this crash.

Hickman says per their initial investigation, the VW crossed the center line and crashed into the garbage truck, causing the truck to tip over near the railroad tracks. The VW driver was trapped in the car, and Hickman says the garbage truck caught on fire.

The Chattanooga Fire Department says they assisted the Tri-Community Volunteer Fire Department in getting the man out. He was taken by LifeForce to Erlanger.

Chief Hickman says the driver of the garbage truck and a passenger in the truck suffered minor injuries, and were treated on scene.

Hickman was not sure of the extent of the injuries of those involved, but this could have been much worse.

“We have a very lucky situation where nobody has died from this crash,” said Chief Hickman.

Hickman says many different agencies responded to the scene, including Collegedale Police, the Hamilton County Sheriff's Office, Norfolk Southern officials, the Tri-Community Volunteer Fire Department and Chattanooga Fire Department, Hamilton County EMS, hazmat crews, and TDEC.

NewsChannel 9's Kelly Curtin was on the scene, and saw the LifeForce helicopter take off.

Collegedale Police spokesperson Bridgett Raper told us earlier that the road between Edgmon Road and Cambridge Square was closed. Tri-Community VFD later confirmed traffic reopened in the area.

On the Collegedale Police Department's Facebook post about the crash, a person voiced concerns about the intersection of Lee Highway & Edgmon Road, saying there have been "too many accidents" there, and that the city has known of the problem for years but hasn't put up a traffic light.

Collegedale PD responded to the person, saying, "The City is well aware of the problem and our city leaders have tirelessly petitioned the State for several years now to bring the needed improvements to that intersection, as that is a state route and not a city road. The good news is that the City’s hard work has paid off and the State has already begun preparations to construct the new intersection."

We reached out to TDOT for more information on the history of the intersection. Spokesperson Jennifer Flynn gave us the following statement:

"We do have a safety project in development to improve that intersection of Lee Hwy. and Edgmon Road by adding a turn lane and traffic signals. We have been ready to go with this project for a while, but we cannot proceed until we have an executed agreement with the nearby railroad. As soon as that is done, we can go forward with the project. Currently we are hoping to have the project let to contract (bids opened) sometime in the 1st quarter of 2020."

This is a developing story and will be updated as we learn more.
