Woman brutally bodyslammed at Tennessee Popeyes in video speaks for the first time

by Dennis Ferrier

Tue, November 26th 2019 at 6:20 PM
Updated Wed, November 27th 2019 at 12:52 PM
Woman brutally bodyslammed at Tennessee Popeyes in video speaks for the first time (FOX 17 News.Steven Watkins)

In an interview you’ll only see on FOX 17 News, the woman who was body slammed in a Popeyes parking lot is speaking out.

Video showing Deborah Staggs being brutally body slammed in a Popeyes parking lot in Columbia has been viewed thousands of times.

But until now, most have never heard from the woman herself. In the exclusive interview, Staggs talks about being double-victimized and the pain of her personal life. She explains why she's not a racist and why people think they heard a racial slur in the video leading up to her being body slammed.


Staggs doesn’t care if it helps or hurts her legal situation, she wants to tell her story. She wants people to know her heart.

It’s the tale of two videos. One shows a 56-year-old woman bodyslammed in the Popeyes parking lot by a man. In the other, she appears to be arguing with employees. With the confrontation inside the Popeyes, everyone has made judgements based on the videos.

Now, Staggs is recovering in an undisclosed recovery hospital due to death threats. Her arm is broken in 16 places. She has nine broken ribs, a shattered kneecap and a shattered life.

“I’ve missed my grandson’s three ballgames. I can’t get that back. My grandchildren are my life,” Staggs said.

She has watched news reports where several witnesses claim she used the n-word in a confrontation with Popeyes employees. She says that’s not true. She claims one employee told everyone she used the word and that escalated an argument over a $13.11 overcharge.

“It just feels like I’m in a nightmare. I don’t understand why I even feel sorry for the individual who did this because I felt like he was told a lie and provoked. My heart breaks for him because it destroyed his life and mine,” Staggs said.

Again no matter how many people say she used the word, she swears she did not.

“It horrifies me to hear somebody use a racial slur towards anybody because that’s offensive and I didn’t do that. That was hurtful I would ever be accused of that,” Staggs said.

A lot of people have wondered why she just didn’t leave when things got so heated.

“What bothered me so much, I’m not arguing. I’m a military daughter. I’m very direct. If you accuse me of something I didn’t do, I am going to correct it,” Staggs said.

Her goddaughter Deanna O’Brien says Staggs volunteers every year to help clothe and feed children in Maury County housing projects. They have 23 kids on their list this year. She says she feels people need to know that about Deborah.

“I’m just trying to give the missing pieces of the puzzle, the whole truth of who Deborah is and not that one moment in time,” O’Brien said. “A very tough person, but a heart of gold.”

Meanwhile, her assailant, 29-year-old Deriance Hughes, will be in court on the aggravated assault charges after Thanksgiving.
