US Postal Service says it's not too late to mail packages for Christmas

by Brittany Nicholson

Fri, December 11th 2015 at 10:46 PM
Updated Fri, December 11th 2015 at 11:32 PM
package.PNG

    TOPICS:

    ChristmasHolidaysdelivery

Chattanooga (WTVC) — It's the busiest time of year for many especially for postal workers.

The postal service says you still have time to send a Christmas card or package and if you're expecting a package they have a few tips to make sure you get it.

"We start planning for the holiday season the day after the holidays end so we've been working on this all year long," David Walton, a spokesperson for the United States Postal Service, said.

He says until Christmas day workers and carriers will be working around the clock to make sure your gift or card gets there.

"Just for this season alone the postal service has hired an additional 30,000 employees," Walton said.

Fred Peterson is the acting plant manager in Chattanooga. He gave NewsChannel 9 a tour of the processing center here.

"They go through the conveyor and the little arms are trying to pick off just little pieces at a time," Peterson said. "Because we're going from a hamper of mail to eventually a single piece of mail."

He showed us the massive amount of cards moving every second.

"These machines are capable of processing about 40,000 pieces of mail per hour or 11 per second," he explained.

But even if you sent that present or Christmas Card on time there are still a few things Walton says to remember to make sure it arrives without any problems.

"Don't put a wrong zip code on a package. No zip is better than a wrong zip," Walton said. "A lot of people tend to use boxes over and over and they kind of lose their rigidness so we have boxes for free in the postal service won't cost you a dime. We'll also deliver them to your house."

He says they also hear a lot about what they call porch poachers, or people who steal packages that have already been delivered.

He says asking a neighbor to keep an eye out or having it delivered to work are two good options.

"Something else you can do is.. if you go to our website and you input the tracking number, you can actually leave specific instructions as to where you want your carrier to leave your box, maybe at a back door, under a bush," Walton explained.

Walton says there is still time to send a holiday card or package and have it arrive on time for Christmas.

Last days to guarantee package will make it:

Dec 10: Military mail

Dec 19: First Class

Dec 21: Priority

Dec 23: Express

The postal service says carriers will be delivering on Christmas Day this year.
