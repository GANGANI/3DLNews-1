Tucker Trivette - A Teenager Inspired to Help the Homeless

by Sarah Jennings

Tue, April 28th 2015 at 10:00 PM
Updated Mon, June 20th 2016 at 3:13 PM
Tucker Trivette.png

Chickamauga — With cold weather well behind us - a winter coat may be the last thing on your mind.

But, in North Georgia a young man is on a mission to make sure the less fortunate have what they need once the mercury falls.

This week we meet Tucker Trivette, a star student at Gordon Lee Middle School in Chickamauga with a great, big heart.

Chad Cochran is a church minister in Trenton where Tucker attends and he can hardly wait to recognize his student who helped launch a community campaign last month to warm the homeless.

Chad tells me, "As soon as this happened he (Tucker) said, 'I'm gonna go to my school. I'm gonna try the best I can to get something going.' And, he went to his teachers and went to his principal and said, 'Hey, is there anything we can do to help these people?'"

They responded and weeks later Tucker had already collected 75 coats to hand out this Fall.

"Not just in the school, but in the church and in his own life personally he puts others first, always willing to help. He is always trying to find out a way - 'How can I help somebody today?'"

It's time to recognize the unsuspecting teenager who's just down the hallway.

So, we count out our $500 in Pay it Forward cash.

The principal knocks on the door and calls Tucker out of class.

Chad appears and explains why we're here.

Speaking to Tucker he says, "On behalf of McMahan Law Firm and NewsChannel 9 I'm here to Pay it Forward to you, Tucker, for all you've done. Hold your hand out - $100, $200, $300, $400, $500, buddy."

A surprised Tucker shyly asks, "Now what did I do?"

"You're being recognized, Tucker, for all you've done, for this coat drive mainly and for just taking the initiative and helping people without receiving recognition."

We gather inside the principal's office and I ask Tucker how it all got started.

Tucker says, "I was really inspired by a friend at church and he came up to me and he was talking about the coat drive and I was really excited about it. I said, 'Here, I'll go talk to my school and see if they'd like to do it and I went to them and they were like, yeah, we'll do it.'"

He credits his faith for his good deeds, telling me that he prays each morning that he can be a light to others.

"You're awesome!" I exclaim.

"You know that? Do people tell you that you're an awesome kid?" I ask.

He laughs and with a modest shrug he says, "Yah, sometimes."

Tucker and young people like him are proving that you're never too young to Pay it Forward.
