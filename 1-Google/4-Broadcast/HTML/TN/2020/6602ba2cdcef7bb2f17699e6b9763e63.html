Trump 2024? President Trump could run again if bid fails
by ADRIAN MOJICA | WZTV Staff
Friday, Nov 6th 2020
{p}If President Donald J. Trump loses the 2020 election for a second term to Joe Biden, President Trump could run again in 2024 and become the first president since 1888 to run again after serving only one term. PHOTOS: Martin Van Buren Library of Congress photo, Donald Trump official White House photo{/p}

If President Donald J. Trump loses the 2020 election for a second term to Joe Biden, President Trump could run again in 2024.

ADVERTISEMENT

While the results of the 2020 election have yet to be announced, the hypothetical idea of Trump running again should he lose has been circulating on social media. FOX 17 News spoke with Linda Schacht, a veteran political analyst with Lipscomb University, on the possibility and the historical background should a potential Trump 2024 campaign become reality.

In short, Schacht says President Trump could run in four years should he ultimately lose the current election. "There's nothing that keeps President Trump from running for a second term," Schacht says. "He could come back and run again."

In 1840, then President Martin Van Buren lost his re-election campaign as the 8th President of the United states and chose to run again in 1848 as a member of the Free Soil Party. President Grover Cleveland was both the 22nd and 24th president of the United States. He served from 1885 to 1889 and again from 1893 to 1897. Theodore Roosevelt became President in 1901 following the assassination of William McKinley and was elected to a full term in 1904. After leaving politics, he attempted a failed return in 1912 running as a Progressive. A century after Van Buren's bid, Franklin D. Roosevelt won a 4th term as president in 1944 which brought about the ratification of the 22nd Amendment in 1951.

The 22nd Amendment states "No person shall be elected to the office of the President more than twice, and no person who has held the office of President, or acted as President, for more than two years of a term to which some other person was elected President shall be elected to the office of the President more than once."

Schacht says while it is still too early to consider the possibility given the current election has not been called, if Trump were to lose and decide to run again, he would have to again go through the primary process and there would be multiple factors for the Republican Party to consider.

"A lot could depend on the midterm elections and if there are more in favor of another candidate," Schacht says. "But it could be at the party's own peril given how strong President Trump's base is."
