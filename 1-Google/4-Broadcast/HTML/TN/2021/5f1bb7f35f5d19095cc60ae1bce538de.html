Catastrophic flooding reported in some parts of Middle Tennessee with more rain expected

by Adrian Mojica

Sat, August 21st 2021 at 1:19 PM
Updated Sat, August 21st 2021 at 5:43 PM
4
VIEW ALL PHOTOS
Catastrophic flooding reported in some parts of Middle Tennessee with more rain expected. PHOTO/VIDEO: FOX 17 News

NASHVILLE, Tenn.-- Catastrophic flooding has taken place in some counties in Middle Tennessee with more expected rainfall expected on Saturday.

Houston, Humphreys, Dickson, and Hickman Counties have each seen heavy rainfall leading to reports of water rescues in these areas. The Tennessee Emergency Management Agency (TEMA) is assisting in the effort.

The National Weather Service (NWS) also reports the situation in Humphreys County at the moment is a "very dire situation" with 911 down and people trapped in their homes. Those in the areas McEwen and Waverly should move to higher ground or avoid the area completely if possible.

Meriwether Lewis Electric Cooperative issued the following statement on power outages in the area: "Crews are responding to several outages across our territory such as washed out poles and downed equipment. As some areas are unreachable due to flooding, expect outages to be lengthy as we wait for water to recede before we can access equipment for repairs. We will continue working areas we can reach and serving as quickly and safely as possible. Roads are dangerous so be careful out there or stay in. We’ll weather this together."

FOX 17 CODE RED Chief Meteorologist reports 8-10" of rain has fallen so far with some areas seeing closer to a foot of rainfall. A Flash Flood Emergency has been extended until 12:45pm.

More showers and storms are expected this afternoon and evening which could add to issues in these areas.
