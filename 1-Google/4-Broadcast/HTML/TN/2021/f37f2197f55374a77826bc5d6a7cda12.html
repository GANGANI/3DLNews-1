Gov. Lee's decision to end federal unemployment pay in Tennessee garners mixed reaction

by Erika Glover

Thu, May 13th 2021 at 11:13 PM
Updated Thu, May 13th 2021 at 10:10 AM
Tennessee Gov. Bill Lee (FOX 17 News)

    TOPICS:

    Bill LeeTennessee,United StatesMarsha BlackburnKari KueflerKevin RiggsLee FederallylaborBiden

NASHVILLE, Tenn. (WZTV) — Starting July 3, Tennessee Gov. Bill Lee says those receiving unemployment will no longer get the additional $300 per week supplement. The extra cash was federally funded assistance added due to the ongoing COVID-19 pandemic.

Wednesday pastors from across the mid-state joined in virtual prayer to send a message to the governor.

RELATED: Gov. Lee: Federally-funded pandemic unemployment benefits in Tennessee will expire July 3

“We are calling on Governor Lee to change course,” exclaimed Franklin Community Church Pastor, Rev. Dr. Kevin Riggs.

Williamson county mother and wife Kari Kuefler participated in the virtual meeting. Kuefler explained how both she and her husband had careers in the music industry. Sadly she was furloughed from her job of more than 15 years back in May of 2020. Her husband kept working, but took a 50% pay cut.

“It’s just not right,” said the Williamson county mom. “It’s a disappointment. We are paying into these systems as workers and we should get the support of our government.”

It's a message she wants Governor Lee and Senator Marsha Blackburn to hear loud and clear.

ALSO READ: Blackburn pushes against Biden's unemployment bonus 'paying people to stay at home'

“Myself, a lot of my peers who are gig workers, we want to work. Let that be heard! We want to work! It’s not that we’re just sitting at home collecting money from the government.”

Sen. Blackburn also sounding off:

“We have about 250,000 jobs that are available and 49,000 people are still on unemployment benefits,” said Senator Blackburn. “The legislation that I’m supporting at the federal level would begin to wind down these benefits. They would be down to $150 benefit by the end of May and then would phase out by the end of June.”

Tennessee already ranks among the lowest when it comes to state unemployment benefits, paying recipients $275 a week. That's more than $100 below the national average.
