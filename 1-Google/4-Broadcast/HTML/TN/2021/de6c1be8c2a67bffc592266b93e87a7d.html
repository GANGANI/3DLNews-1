Florida man banned from United flights, LinkedIn for wearing underwear mask on plane
by Tanner Stewart
Monday, Dec 27th 2021
Courtesy Adam Jenne III{p}{/p}

CAPE CORAL, Fla. (WEAR) -- A passenger aboard a United Airlines flight was asked to leave before takeoff because of the mask policy. It wasn't that he refused to wear one, it was the type of mask he wore.

ADVERTISEMENT

Now, he feels he's being unfairly punished, and his work is taking a hit.

WEAR

Adam Jenne says he's worn his version of a COVID mask, a piece of red underwear, on at least 20 flights in the U.S. since July.

"The first time I did it, I had no idea how it would be received," Jenne said. "People just loved it. And not just 60 percent, 99.9."

Jenne claims TSA, generally speaking, love it as well.

But earlier this month, the jig was up. The United Airlines flight, where Jenne was scheduled to fly, asked him to exit the plane for wearing his underwear mask.

Moments later, others could be seen leaving -- outraged that Jenne would be kicked off his flight for wearing the undergarment as a face covering.

Jenne says he knows the attention the red garment attracts, but it's intended to prove a point: Jenne and others want the mass transit mask mandate to end.

"I think that really signals or at least sends a signal that generally speaking, we as a people, are done with this," Jenne said.

After exiting the plane, Jenne was escorted to the main gate. He says airport officials told him his mask fit all criteria for usage.

"That's when the TSA agent told me that it's a customer service issue there's nothing legal that we're going to pursue, you're fine," Jenne said. "It's a complete and total charade, you've got the FAA, the TSA and United pointing fingers at each other like their the three stooges."

While Jenne says he's flown on half a dozen other airlines wearing the mask, he received a letter from United shortly after the incident -- banning him from any future flights, pending a review by the board.

Since the ordeal, Jenne is facing backlash he says impacts his well being.

He claims his LinkedIn account, which he utilizes for his sales job, has been cancelled -- only after his story made its rounds on the platform.

"It isn't about being graphic or grotesque, inappropriate, or anything like that.," Jenne said. "I'm trying to illustrate the absurdity of wearing a mask on a plane by being absurd."

Channel 3 reached out to LinkedIn for a response, we're waiting to hear back.

So far in 2021, there were about 5,800 complaints of unruly behavior by airline passengers, most of which were related to mask rules.

A spokesman with Pensacola International Airport told Channel 3 they've seen creative masks this year but no one's been removed from a flight.

According to officials, the only guideline the airport has is wearing a mask that covers your face and nose.
