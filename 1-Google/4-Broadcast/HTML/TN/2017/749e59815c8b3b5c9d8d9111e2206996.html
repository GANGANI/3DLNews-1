Fake News sites peddling weight-loss, muscle, wrinkle products settle $179 million suit

by Adrian Mojica

Wed, November 15th 2017 at 4:24 PM
Updated Wed, November 15th 2017 at 4:38 PM
The Federal Trade Commission (FTC) has announced a court order settling a suit against online marketers believed to have taken $179 million from consumers through fake news sites. PHOTO: FTC

WASHINGTON, D.C.--The Federal Trade Commission (FTC) has announced a court order settling a suit against online marketers believed to have taken $179 million from consumers through fake news and media sites.

The suit surrounds deceptive practices used by three people behind a network on fake sites and online marketers. The defendants, according to the FTC, sold over 40 weight-loss, muscle-building, and wrinkle-reduction products using unsubstantiated health claims and using fake celebrity endorsements.

The sites used the likenesses of celebrities and used fake mastheads designed to look like legitimate news organizations. The marketers used the likeness of well-known brands of Ment's Health, Good Housekeeping, Women's Health, and photos of celebs like Jennifer Aniston to sell their products.

The marketers then lured consumers in by allegedly promising "risk free" trials, only to have the customers enrolled in "negative option" programs, programs which could charge the consumer in full if they didn't cancel within a short period of time. Consumers would be billed $87 every month thereafter. The suit claims consumers were never made aware of the need to cancel.

The marketers are permanently banned from using negative option features to sell their products and prohibited from misrepresenting terms and conditions as a result of the settlement.
