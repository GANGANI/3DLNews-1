Hundreds Pay Respects to Fallen Colorado State Trooper

Published: Nov. 24, 2015 at 8:11 AM MST

Colorado's law enforcement paused to remember one of their own. Trooper Jaimie Jursevics' funeral was held, Monday.

Her procession to the church in Denver included a huge number of police officer and firefighters. Troopers stood next to her casket inside the church. A flag was presented to her husband.

Jaimie leaves behind a 8-month-old daughter. The Fremont County Sheriff's Office shared a powerful picture of the baby surrounded by CSP gear. They're using it as a message to not drink and drive because the little girl will now grow up without a mother.

"Jaimie was a leader in every sense of the word. She loved her family and was so proud of Morgan and DJ. She carried that same sense of pride to her patrol family."

Investigators say Jaimie Jursevics was hit and killed by a drunk driver on I-25, near Castle Rock nearly a week ago.

Deputies say the driver is retired Fort Carson Colonel Eric Henderson from Peyton. He was tailgating at a Bronco's game, and then got behind the wheel.
