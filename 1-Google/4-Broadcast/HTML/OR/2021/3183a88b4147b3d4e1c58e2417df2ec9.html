Family given grim news as Medford teen continues to fight for her life

by Ambar Rodriguez

Wed, February 17th 2021 at 6:43 PM
6

CENTRAL POINT, Ore. — The family of the 16-year-old Medford teen fighting for her life after being hit by a car last month hopes she can beat the odds and take a turn for the better.

Hailee Patnesky was hit by a car in Central Point on Jan. 9 while walking near the corner of Gibbon Road and Azalea Drive with her younger sister and a friend. Since then, the teen has been at Asante Rogue Regional Medical Center.

“Basically, at this point, they think the damage is severe enough that they don’t believe she’s going to wake up,” Errin Johnson, Patnesky’s older brother said. “It hit us pretty hard hearing that.”

Johnson explained Patnesky’s mother, Michelle has been by her bedside since the incident occurred. He said she is taking the latest news extremely hard.

Johnson noted although the family was given the discouraging news just last Thursday, he said Patnesky still has a chance to be moved to a rehabilitation hospital if she improves.

“The plan is to give her more time after that I believe there is one hospital up in Portland that Hailee could go to if she starts to have more directive movements and there is another one down in Southern California that she could go to with the stipulations of being able to coherently hear commands and move her hands,” Johnson said.

There has been no arrest in connection to the incident.

On Tuesday News 10 requested an update on the investigation into the incident from the Jackson County Sheriff’s Office, who is the lead agency in the case.

In an email response, Mike Moran the spokesperson for the department wrote, “the case is still under investigation, primarily waiting for the accident reconstruction work to be completed and assessed.”

“You hear these kinds of stories all the time, it's sad and you feel bad for the people that go through it but when it happens to you, you don’t even believe it's real,” he said.

The family has set up a GoFundMe account to help pay for her ongoing medical expenses.
