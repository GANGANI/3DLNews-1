KVAL-TV went on the air April 15, 1954. The region's first TV station grew with Western Oregon and responded to calls for quality programming, public service local journalism, and a platform for advertisers to share their message with potential customers.

DON'T MISS | 1954: Gas cost 21 cents, homes cost $8,600 - and TV arrived in Eugene, Oregon

KVAL News has made the community a cornerstone of newsgathering efforts, calling on neighbors to "Chime In" with news and views from where they live - and then sharing those images and ideas on local TV.

The team in Eugene, Coos Bay and Roseburg live and play in their communities with you - and we look forward to sharing the triumphs and challenges of our communities #LiveOnKVAL CBS 13.

Who remembers Cap'n Shipwreck?

Ask any kid who grew up in this area during the 1960s, and they will tell you there was only one king of the kids shows: Cap'n Shipwreck.

From 1961 to 1968, Cap'n Shipwreck entertained children on screen - and in the studio.

But to hear the man behind the captain's hat tell the story, his voyage into Oregon TV history almost never set sail.

DON'T MISS | 'Absolutely phenomenal. It was a benefit that I had no concept would be coming my way'

America in 1954 was another world.

The average price of a U.S. home? $8,600.

Gasoline went for 21 cents a gallon.

You could buy an RCA black-and-white television set for $189.

And on April 15, a TV station went on the air for the first time in Eugene: KVAL-TV.

Jim Bradley and Wink Guthrie are synonymous with those early days of TV in Western Oregon.

The father of TV news in Eugene

Many people regard John Doyle as the father of television news in Eugene.

Doyle anchored KVAL News and served as news director. He was on the air in Western Oregon for 40 years.

And tonight #LiveOnKVAL, you can watch the latest local news, weather and more from the John Doyle Studios atops Blanton Heights in the South Hills of Eugene.
