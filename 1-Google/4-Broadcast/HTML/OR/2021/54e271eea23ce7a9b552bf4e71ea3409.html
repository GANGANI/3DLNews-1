From Extreme to High Risk: here's what the move means

by KTVL

Tue, February 23rd 2021 at 12:26 PM
Updated Wed, February 24th 2021 at 6:55 PM
3

Covid-19 safety measure guidelines list in front of the Jackson County Health and Human Services building. The county is currently at extreme risk but its positive cases are trending down and it will be moving to High risk starting Friday. (Ambar Rodriguez/News 10)

JACKSON Co., Ore. — The Jackson County Public Health Department announced that the county has been moved from the Extreme to High-risk level of the state's COVID-19 risk assessments based on case counts per number of residents.

“This is hopeful news that Jackson County has a decrease in cases and able to move to a lower risk category,” Dr. Jim Shames, Jackson County Health Officer wrote in a press release. “Even though we are seeing a decrease in cases and moving to a lower risk category, it remains critical that we continue to wear a mask, wash our hands, watch our distance, stay home when we are sick, and get vaccinated against COVID-19 when it's our turn."

This follows a warning week, or the week proceeding risk level calculations, that ended Feb 20 during which the county had a case rate of 179 per 100,0000 residents. Counties must be below 200 per 100,000 residents in order to move from Extreme to High-risk level.

The new risk level will allow restaurants in the county to open for in-door dining at 25% capacity maximum occupancy or 50 people, whichever is smaller. Outdoor dining will now be allowed for 75 people maximum. Restaurants will be allowed to stay open till 11:00 p.m. Indoor entertainment will also be allowed in a limited capacity.

Here are the changes that will become effective in Jackson County:

  • Faith-Based Institutions allow expanded capacity: Indoor Capacity: Maximum 25% occupancy or 150 people total, whichever is smaller. Outdoor Capacity: 200 people maximum.
  • Indoor Entertainment capacity changes: Capacity: Maximum 25% occupancy or 50 people total, whichever is smaller. 11:00 p.m. closing time.
  • Indoor Recreation and fitness capacity changes: Capacity: Maximum 25% occupancy or 50 people total whichever is smaller. Indoor full-contact sports prohibited.
  • Long term care facilities can now add indoor visitation
  • Offices, remote work changed from required to recommended

These changes will be effective from Feb 26 till at least March 11 with the next announcement scheduled for March 9. The week prior to March 9 will be a warning week during which each county's level's will be assessed.

“For the second time in a row, we are seeing great progress in stopping the spread of COVID-19 across Oregon and saving lives," said Governor Brown in a press release. "Oregonians continue to step up and make smart choices. While these county movements are welcome news, we must continue to take seriously health and safety measures."

Another 15 counties in the state were also lowered in risk level and three were upgraded.

These Counties were downgraded:

  • Clatsop: moved from High to Low
  • Lincoln: moved from High to Low
  • Wasco: moved from Extreme to Low
  • Clackamas: moved from High to Moderate
  • Linn: moved from High to Moderate
  • Malheur: moved from Extreme to Moderate
  • Hood River moved from High to Moderate
  • Union: moved from Extreme to Moderate
  • Washington: moved from High to Moderate
  • Crook: moved from Extreme to High
  • Lane: moved from Extreme to High
  • Marion: moved from Extreme to High
  • Polk: moved from Extreme to High
  • Umatilla: moved from Extreme to High
  • Yamhill: moved from Extreme to High

These counties were upgraded :

  • Douglas: moved from High to Extreme
  • Curry: moved from Lower to Moderate
  • Harney: moved from Lower to Moderate
