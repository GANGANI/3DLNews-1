Oregon sees 41 new coronavirus deaths as U.S. deaths hit daily high
by KATU Staff
Wednesday, Jan 13th 2021
Holding test tube with COVID-19
PORTLAND, Ore —

Oregon saw 1,346 new confirmed and presumptive coronavirus cases in the state as the case total reached 129,109 since the pandemic first began.

ADVERTISEMENT

The state also reported another 41 new COVID-19-related deaths, raising Oregon's death toll to 1,708. The news comes as the U.S. coronavirus deaths hit another daily high at more than 4,300.

TUESDAY'S REPORT: Oregon sees record-tying high COVID-19 deaths, attributed to year-end surge in cases

Oregon health officials are attributing the spike in the COVID-19-related deaths to the year-end surge in coronavirus cases from November and December.

The Oregon Health Authority is still compiling all the data related to the newly-reported deaths and will release that information later.

RELATED: US coronavirus deaths hit another daily high

Of the new coronavirus cases in Oregon, the counties that saw the biggest spikes are Multnomah County with 307 cases and Clackamas County with 105 cases and Washington with 110 cases.

Hospitalizations in the state have risen slightly, as there are 434 COVID-19 patients in the hospital currently, which is 31 more than reported on Tuesday. There are also 103 COVID-19 patients in the ICU, which is ten more than reported on Tuesday, as well.

To date, more than 129,782 first and second doses of the COVID-19 vaccine have been administered. The governor announced on Tuesday that Oregonians over the age of 65 and school educators and staff will be next in line to receive the vaccine, starting on January 23.
