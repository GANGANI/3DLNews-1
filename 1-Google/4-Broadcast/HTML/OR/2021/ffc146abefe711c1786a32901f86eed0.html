Newberg school board fires superintendent without cause in 4-3 vote

By FOX 12 Staff
Updated: Nov. 10, 2021 at 12:03 PM PST

NEWBERG, Ore. (KPTV) – The Newberg School District is out of a superintendent after the school board voted 4-3 Tuesday night to fire Superintendent Dr. Joe Morelock without cause.

The four conservative board members who voted to fire Morelock are the same four who passed a controversial rule banning political symbols in schools, including Pride and Black Lives Matter signs. It’s the latest in a string of controversies that has put Newberg in the spotlight.

Morelock’s termination is effective Nov. 19. Because the board fired him without cause, they will have to continue paying him for 12 months.

“So everyone following along at home is clear ... we’ll have to find an interim superintendent, which we will also need to be paying ... The financial and legal liabilities for the district are endless,” said Board Director Brandy Penner after the vote.

Penner did not vote to fire Morelock.

In a release, Newberg district administrators said they were “shocked and dismayed that the Board would take this disruptive action in the middle of the school year.”

“Under his leadership, new contracts with our associations brought better pay to teachers and staff, more educational school days for our students, a stabilized financial situation with a safety net of over $9 million in the ending fund balance, and the passage of the largest Construction Bond in the history of Newberg Public Schools,” the Newberg School District said.

The board discussed the matter behind closed doors and did not say why they voted to fire the superintendent. The only documentation made public was a complaint about a rainbow heart on display at Dundee Elementary. Morelock concluded that the sign, which came from nearby George Fox University, did not violate the district’s new rule on political signage.

Board Director Rebecca Piros offered this when discussing her dismay at the firing.

“Because he didn’t rush to do something he felt was illegal,” Piros said. “He was protecting the school district from our rashness.”

FOX 12 attempted to reach all board members for comment. Piros was the only board member who returned FOX 12′s call, saying she was saddened by the loss of the superintendent.

FOX 12 visited the homes of three of the four board members who voted to fire Morelock.

A person at one board member’s home chose not to answer the door but was visible through a window.

Another person who answered the door at Renee Powell’s home told Fox 12 she likely wouldn’t want to speak with KPTV.

Board Chair Dave Brown’s neighbor has put up Black Lives Matter signs and a pride flag facing Brown’s property.

“I want him to look every day at these two signs and realize that these are people. That’s all we care about, these are people, and it shouldn’t matter what your identity is,” said Bill Parker.

Morelock became interim superintendent for the 2018-2019 school year before being hired for the permanent position in 2019.

“Dr. Morelock has worked tirelessly on behalf of our students and staff. He has been key in the transformation of a struggling district,” the Newberg Education Association posted on its Facebook page. “This move is yet another example of a board majority bringing instability to our schools.”

The teacher’s union has filed a lawsuit against the school district and the four board members over their ban on political flags.
