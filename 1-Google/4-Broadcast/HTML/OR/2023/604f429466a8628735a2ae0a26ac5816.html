EO Media pays homage to the Mail Tribune with the name 'The Tribune'

by Malik Patterson

Wed, January 25th 2023 at 5:47 PM
Updated Wed, January 25th 2023 at 6:20 PM
5

EO Media will be having their first online paper in Medford within the next two weeks. The new paper will be called The Tribune. (Malik Patterson/ KTVL)

MEDFORD, Ore. — The Tribune is a new newspaper coming to Medford within the new two weeks.

EO Media is the parent company of The Tribune and other newspapers throughout Oregon. Bend Bulletin will be the oversight company over the new 14-person newspaper in Medford.

Bend Bulletin says it decided to choose the name "The Tribune" to pay homage to the long-standing Mail Tribune.

“We think that readers would actually gravitate towards something that is similar but different in its presentation and so The Tribune is the perfect name for that,” said Gerry O’Brien, Editor at the Bend Bulletin.

The Tribune will run seven days a week as an online subscription-based paper. The company said it will also send out physical newspapers three days a week and will partner with The Ashland Tidings once a week for local coverage.

‘’The actual print version of the newspaper has been falling off for many years, but people still like their print and we like to give that to them. At the same time, the digital subscription is what’s growing our revenue across all of EO Media,” O’Brien said.

The Tribune will have an office in Medford where it will operate and send copies to the community.

When asked if The Tribune would hire former Mail Tribune employees, O'Brien editor at the Bend Bulletin said they hope to have familiar names as well as new names.

“They have the institutional knowledge of that community, with some of them being there many years, and they’re well read by the audience there and we hope they come on board,” O’Brien said. “I think it’ll be a mixture of new faces and old faces.”

EO Media Group knew there would be competition for the new market of Medford and welcomed the new rivalry.

“You know the competition is a healthy thing and how often do you get to have competition among newspapers?” O’Brien said. “We look forward to getting to have that competitive edge in Medford.”

The Daily Courier also announced their new coverage in Jackson County on January 11. So when The Tribune was announced on January 20, Publisher Travis Moore at the Daily Courier had this to say:

When we began planning our expansion into Jackson County, we knew there would be competition for such a great market.

Ultimately, the people of the Rogue Valley will be the ones who decide. And we think the differences between the Daily Courier and EO Media's Tribune tip the scales in our favor.

The Daily Courier is delivered by carrier to doorsteps five days a week; the Tribune will be sent out by mail three days a week. The Daily Courier has been owned by the same Rogue Valley family for the last 125 years; the Tribune will be one of more than a dozen newspapers owned by a Salem-based chain.

The Daily Courier has the financial stability to continue growing on the path to becoming the region's daily. We are encouraged by the reception we’ve had so far in the Medford area. With roughly 1,000 new subscribers in Jackson County, we are moving forward with our expansion plans.
