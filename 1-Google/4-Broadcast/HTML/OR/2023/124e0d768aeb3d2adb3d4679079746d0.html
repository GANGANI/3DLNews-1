Jack Smith asks Supreme Court to rule quickly on whether Donald Trump can be prosecuted

Monday, Dec 11th 2023

WASHINGTON (TND) —

The special counsel in former President Donald Trump's 2020 election case asked the Supreme Court to decide if Trump is immune from prosecution.

ADVERTISEMENT

Jack Smith filed the request on Monday. He urged the high court to proceed as soon as possible so the case can continue "promptly."

This case presents a fundamental question at the heart of our democracy: whether a former President is absolutely immune from federal prosecution for crimes committed while in office or is constitutionally protected from federal prosecution when he has been impeached but not convicted before the criminal proceedings begin," Smith wrote in an 81-page document." The district court rejected respondent's claims, correctly recognizing that former Presidents are not above the law and are accountable for their violations of federal criminal law while in office.

The document also noted "it is of imperative public importance that respondent’s claims of immunity be resolved by this Court and that respondent’s trial proceed as promptly as possible if his claim of immunity is rejected."

The former president’s trial is set to begin on March 4.

Donald%20Trump%20sits%20at%20the%20defense%20table%20at%20New%20York%20Supreme%20Court,%20Thursday,%20Dec.%207,%202023,%20in%20New%20York.%20(AP%20Photo/Eduardo%20Munoz%20Alvarez,%20Pool)

Smith is trying to bypass the appeals court. The request directly reflects Smith’s desire to keep the trial on track and to prevent any delays that could push back the case until after next year’s presidential election.

A federal judge ruled the case could go forward, but Trump signaled he would ask the federal appeals court in Washington to reverse that outcome.

Shortly after news broke that Smith made the request, Trump shared several posts on his social media platform Truth Social. None of the posts mentioned the case or Smith, as many focused on his current campaign for the White House.

Trump was previously charged with plotting to overturn the election results, which now-President Joe Biden won.
