Man dies in police transport post hospital discharge in Milwaukie, investigation begins

by KATU News

Wed, December 13th 2023 at 10:42 AM
Updated Thu, December 14th 2023 at 6:24 PM
FILE - KATU image - police lights.

    TOPICS:

    PoliceMilwaukieManHospitalInvestigationDeathTransportMedical

MILWAUKIE, Ore. — Portland Police investigators are working to figure out how a man died in Milwaukie Police custody.

Police say the man was discharged from Providence Milwaukie Hospital Tuesday night, but he refused to leave.

Milwaukie Police officers arrived and learned the man had several warrants out for his arrest, so they planned to take him to jail.

At some point, they determined he was not in a condition to be in jail, so they took him to the Unity Center for Behavioral Health.

Around 11:00 p.m. North Precinct officers were called to help Milwaukie Police after they discovered the man was unresponsive.

Officers performed CPR and called for EMS who performed life-saving measures, but he was pronounced dead.

A spokesperson for Legacy Health says a law enforcement transport to Unity is a community transport, not a medical transport, and therefore, there is typically no medical personnel present.

A spokesperson for Portland Police says if this had happened in their jurisdiction, officers might have called an ambulance transport. But he also acknowledged this situation is unique because the person was evaluated by doctors, treated, and released.

“Police officers have a huge amount of training over a wide area of subjects but they’re not able to be good observers of medical conditions,” said Jason Renaud, with the Mental Health Association of Portland.

A medical examiner hasn’t revealed how the man died while in transit from the hospital to the mental health center, but Renaud said it's best practice to call for medical help when dealing with a person in any medical crisis - whether it's physical or mental.

For now, he says there are more questions than answers for what went wrong, and who is responsible.

“I want to know more about the hospital that released this individual or did not treat them,” said Renaud.

“Hospitals have a requirement to treat people who are in a medical emergency. I want to know why the Milwaukie Police decided to take them to a separate hospital, and not back to the hospital of origin. I want to know what the medical condition of this individual was, and what the cause of death was.”

The Portland Police Bureau Major Crimes Division is investigating the case.

Officials say they will release the man's name when his family is notified and the cause and manner of death will be determined by the medical examiner.

A spokesperson for PPB said the Milwaukie police officers were wearing body cameras at the time of the transport.

KATU News has requested that video to learn more.

Anyone who has information about the case is asked to contact Detective Travis Law at Travis.Law@police.portlandoregon.gov 503-823-0395 or Detective Ryan Foote at Ryan.Foote@police.portlandoregon.gov 503-823-0781. Please refer to Portland Police case number 23-320906 or Milwaukie Police case number 23-8554.
