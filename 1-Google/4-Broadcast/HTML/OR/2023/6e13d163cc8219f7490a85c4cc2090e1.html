Portland police take down large crime ring that allegedly included a mother and son

by Tanvi Varma, KATU Staff

Wed, December 27th 2023 at 5:36 PM
Updated Wed, December 27th 2023 at 10:03 AM
4
VIEW ALL PHOTOS
Police seized this vehicle and gun as part of their investigation. (Portland Police Bureau)

PORTLAND, Ore. — An unusual combination -- a mother and son -- accused of committing crimes in an intricate crime web together.

“That’s actually the first time I've run across that, is a mother and son that are working together to distribute drugs in the neighborhood,” said Officer William Shelton with the Portland Police Bureau.

An anonymous neighbor from the Northeast Portland neighborhood next to the Glendoveer Golf Course told us, “You can always tell when a house has numerous vehicles coming back and forth to it that don’t belong to your neighborhood. And in those vehicles, occasionally you see people who look like they’re under the influence.”

A yearlong investigation led to Portland police seizing over 30 illegally possessed firearms, ammunition, many drugs, stolen vehicles, and more than $10,000 in cash.

One bust happened at the mom's home off Northeast 141st Avenue and Halsey Street, where the mother, Tracie Harbison, 53, and three others were arrested.

“You don’t know what it’s going to introduce with crime, with these drug busts in our own neighborhood they seized multiple automatic weapons," said the anonymous neighbor.

Then another bust in the 1800 block of Northeast 66th Avenue and a small house in the area off Southeast Steele Street.

“We recovered 12 firearms, methamphetamine, stolen vehicles,” said Shelton.

The investigation into the mom's house is what led them to the other locations, which wasn't solely done by Portland police. They teamed up with the FBI and the Multnomah County Sheriff's Office, specifically the MCSO dangerous drug team.

Police are testing the weapons to see if they are connected with any crimes.

Police also arrested Harbison's son, 26-year-old Chase.

In total, police arrested eight people.
