'I knew I had to do something,' says woman who saved baby after 3 killed by live wire

by Emily Girsch and KATU Staff

Fri, January 19th 2024 at 6:36 PM
Updated Wed, January 24th 2024 at 10:32 PM
4

Majiah Washington rescued a 9-month-old baby after three people were killed, including the baby's father, when a power line fell on their car in Northeast Portland. (KATU)

    BabyPower lineWashingtonRescueKilledSafetyPortlandHospital

PORTLAND, Ore. — An 18-year-old woman on Thursday told of the moments she saved a 9-month-old baby from a live power line the day before.

The baby’s mother, father and uncle were killed.

Majiah Washington watched the horrifying event from her bedroom window and called 911. She asked the operator if it was safe to approach the baby. The operator told her it was dangerous, but she did it anyway.

Washington said once she saw him move his head, she knew she had done the right thing. She said she works at a day care and didn’t think twice about springing into action.

A power line crashed on top of a car Wednesday morning on Siskiyou Street in Northeast Portland, killing three people who got out of the car and were electrocuted. The baby was taken to the hospital.

Firefighters previously said it was a toddler, but KATU has confirmed it was an infant.

Portland Fire & Rescue said the car started sparking, and the two adults and a teenager got out of the car. The baby’s father was carrying him in his arms.

"I knew I had to do something, because the baby started moving. He was the only one still moving. He was moving his head, and that's how I know he was still there, basically," Washington said.

She said she carefully approached the bodies, and the baby was still tucked in his father’s arms. Somehow, she was able to get close enough to grab him.

Neighbors said the two adults and the teenager ran toward a house, before slipping on the ice and falling on the live wire.

Daniel Buck lives next door. He said he heard a loud bang and saw the three people trying to get to safety.

He said he’s thankful that someone was able to rescue the baby.

“Our hearts are broken for the family, for the little one especially. But we’re praising God that he’s alive and doing OK,” Buck said.

Eileen Lundell, a neighbor, said she saw blue lights, heard pops, and then the grandmother of the baby screaming.

“I think the thing that got me the most was the grandmother screaming, ‘My babies! My babies!’ That was awful,” she said.

Ta’Ron Briggs, 15, a 10th grader at Milwaukie High School and his 21-year-old sister, Tajaliyah Briggs, the mother of the baby, died, as did the baby's father.

The baby's name is Nahmir. The name of his father has not been publicly released.

The father of Ta’Ron and Tajaliyah told KATU over the phone he lost his daughter and son but fortunately was able to be reunited with his grandson.

He said the family was not ready to speak publicly yet.

Power Line Safety

Power companies are urging all of us to use caution around downed lines.

If you come across one, stay away.

Downed lines can be live, even if they don't appear to be.

They can become energized at any moment.

Stay in your vehicle. Even if a power line is not directly touching your car, the area near your car could still be energized.

If you have to exit the vehicle for any reason, wrap your arms around yourself and jump clear of the car.

Do not touch the ground and the car at the same time. Then, shuffle away slowly, keeping both feet on the ground at all times.
