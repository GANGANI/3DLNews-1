15-year-old migrant accused of shooting woman, firing at NY officer in custody

by JESSICA A. BOTELHO | The National Desk

Fri, February 9th 2024 at 8:16 AM
Updated Fri, February 9th 2024 at 3:06 PM
3

NEW YORK (TND) — New York police said a suspected shoplifter who allegedly shot a woman in a store and fired his gun at a police officer along a busy street was taken into custody Friday.

Authorities said the suspect is 15-year-old Jesus Alejandro Rivas Figueroa, a migrant from Venezuela who entered the U.S. in September.

The U.S. Marshals Fugitive Recovery Task Force captured him in Yonkers after a manhant that lasted nearly 24 hours.

If you think you can attack a member of this department, if you think you can threaten the lives of the very people who keep us safe, if you think you can put others at deadly risk and get away with it, then think again," Commissioner Edward A. Caban said during a news conference following the arrest. "We will never stop pursuing you. We will find you and we will arrest you and then we will do everything in our power to bring you to justice.

In a news conference earlier on Friday, Chief of Patrol John Chell said the suspect lives in a temporary shelter at a hotel in the area.

We don't care who you are (or) what your status is -- we're not going to broad brush a whole migrant community as being bad people, just like we wouldn't broad brush a whole community of gang members from another borough for a whole borough," Chell said. "But we're not going to put up with this."

Chell added that he is also a suspect of an unrelated armed robbery in the Bronx, as well as and a separate shooting in Times Square.

Chell said the suspect pulled out a gun at a JD Sports in Times Square Thursday around 7 p.m. and fired when a security officer approached him. The bullet hit a 37-year-old woman in the leg.

Surveillance video showed the shooter and another teenager running from the store before they were spotted by two police officers, according to Chell.

Chell said officers caught the second suspect, as well as a third, with one of the officers chasing the shooter. The shooter then fired at the officer.

Chell said the officer didn't return fire because the street was too populated.

Our officer draws his weapon, but he cannot fire,” Chell said. "Too many people around -- too many people ducking."

Chell said the shooter then fired a second shot at the officer before he ran into nearby subway tracks. He was later taken into custody.

The woman who was shot, said police, was visiting the U.S. from Brazil. She was treated at an area hospital and later released, Assistant Chief Jason Savino said.
