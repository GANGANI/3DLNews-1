Newsom sued by former Air National Guard general over alleged antisemitism, report says

by JACKSON WALKER | The National Desk

Fri, February 9th 2024 at 1:21 PM
3
VIEW ALL PHOTOS
FILE - California Gov. Gavin Newsom speaks during an event in San Francisco on Thursday, Nov. 9, 2023. (AP Photo/Jeff Chi, File)

SACRAMENTO, Calif. (TND) — A former Air National Guard general is alleging he was discriminated against in his termination last year in a legal complaint against California Gov. Gavin Newsom, Fox News reported.

Retired Brig. Gen. Jeffrey Magram, who is Jewish, filed the lawsuit on January 24 in a Los Angeles court. His former boss, Maj. Gen. Matthew Beevers, who now serves as the California Military Department’s adjutant general, allegedly created a hostile workplace through his use of antisemitic behavior. Newsom reportedly ignored several complaints on the matter.

Magram was fired in January 2023 after being accused of using troops to run personal errands and nearly sending a fighter jet to disburse civilian protests. He denies these circumstances played a role in his firing, and instead points to a campaign against him by Beevers after reporting the behavior to superiors.

Beevers allegedly began "resurrecting and misrepresenting minor settled issues" from as far back as ten years. When Magram still refused to quit, Beevers allegedly sought Newsom’s approval to cut his disability benefits.

I want to make sure this never happens again. MG Beevers actions were disturbingly aggressive and unexplainable, and he needs to be held accountable,” Magram told the outlet. “It’s shocking that Governor Newsom disregarded the many attempts made to correct this situation and appointed MG Beevers permanently as the Adjutant General.”

The California Military Department slammed the lawsuit as "retaliatory and baseless" in a statement to the outlet. The department went on to provide the outlet documents proving Magram was fired after it lost "faith, trust, and confidence" in his leadership.

"All actions were taken based on independent investigations by the U.S. Air Force and the Military Department Inspector General,” it said. “We look forward to defending our actions to hold a senior leader accountable for his misconduct.”
