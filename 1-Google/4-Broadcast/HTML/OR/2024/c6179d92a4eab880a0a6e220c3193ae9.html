K9 helps deputies track and arrest man accused of stealing from campers in Coos County

by News Staff

Fri, February 9th 2024 at 9:01 AM
K9 helps deputies track and arrest man accused of stealing from campers in Coos County. Photo courtesy of Coos County Sheriff's Office.

    TOPICS:

    Coos CountyK9TheftCampersArrestedTrackingTentBurglary

COOS COUNTY, Ore. — The Coos County Sheriff's Office (CCSO) tells us they arrested a man, with the help of a K9 unit, after he allegedly stole numerous items out of a camper's tent.

According to CCSO, on February 8th at approximately 9:31 p.m., dispatch took a 9-1-1 call from two campers who returned to their campsite, to find a man inside their tent stealing numerous items. The campers confronted the man who fled on foot.

Deputies S. Starr and H. Francis responded with Sgt. Slater & K9 Cena to attempt to track the suspect.

After a tracking the suspect for two miles, including over: sand dunes, Transpacific Highway, a wood processing plant, and several pools of water, K9 Cena located John P. Corby, 48, hiding in the sand dunes.

Corby was arrested on:

  • Burglary I
  • Theft II
  • Identify Theft
  • Criminal Trespass II

John Corby was transported to the Coos County Jail, where he was booked and remains in custody.

The victims of the theft had most of their items returned, CCSO tells us.
