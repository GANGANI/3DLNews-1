Trump PAC launches new ad hitting Democrats on border: 'Joe Biden does nothing'

by JULIAN BARON | The National Desk

Fri, February 9th 2024 at 1:30 PM
Updated Sat, February 10th 2024 at 6:30 PM
3
VIEW ALL PHOTOS
FILE - Republican presidential candidate former President Donald Trump addresses members of the press during a campaign stop in Londonderry, N.H., Tuesday, Jan. 23, 2024. (AP Photo/Matt Rourke)

ARLINGTON, Va. (TND) — A PAC backing former President Donald Trump released a new advertisement Friday targeting Democrats for promoting an "open border policy."

The ad, which was produced by Make America Great Again Inc., pins the ongoing southern border crisis on President Joe Biden while also taking aim at Vice President Kamala Harris and Sen. Chuck Schumer, D-N.Y.

While Democrats champion an open border policy, law enforcement is buckling. Our cities, crumbling. Our country, disappearing," the ad says. "And Joe Biden does nothing to stop it."

The release comes on the heels of the failed bipartisan border security package pushed by President Biden. The bill, which had limited Republican support, was defeated in the Senate after opposition from several GOP figures, including Trump.

The ad focuses firmly on the immigration crisis and makes no mention of Republican presidential candidate Nikki Haley or the fresh concerns over President Biden's mental acuity.

Featured in the ad are references to "felons" and "criminals" who have entered the country illegally via the U.S.-Mexico border, including an image of Rafael Govea Romero. The 23-year-old illegal immigrant was charged with murder late last year in the stabbing death of 16-year-old Lizbeth Medina, a high school cheerleader from Edna, Texas.

Migrants charged with crimes have been a key focus for GOP lawmakers who say the southern border crisis is jeopardizing public safety nationwide. New York Republicans recently urged Gov. Kathy Hochul to send troops from the state's National Guard to assist state and federal law enforcement on the northern and southern borders, citing the impact lax immigration enforcement has on New Yorkers.

"The migrant influx has compromised the ability of localities to provide services and restricted access to programs for New Yorkers seeking essential assistance," Sen. Robert Ortt, R-Sweden, and Rep. Will Barclay, R-Fulton, wrote to Gov. Hochul.

"Thousands of illegal immigrants, including terrorists, rapists, and murderers, will be streaming across the border while Americans are enjoying the game. America is less safe because Joe Biden reversed President Trump's policies," Alex Pfeiffer, a spokesman for Make America Great Again Inc., told The National Desk.

Earlier this week, the Biden campaign tried to flip responsibility for the southern border crisis onto Trump. A Biden campaign spokesperson accused Trump of "standing in the way" of border security by not supporting Biden's legislative package.

"Donald Trump’s role is clear as day: He is personally standing in the way of giving Border Patrol the resources it needs to stop the flow of fentanyl and secure the border,” Biden campaign spokesperson Ammar Moussa told The National Desk.
