Today: Thursday will start off a bit chilly, but we'll see plenty of sunshine for Southern Oregon and Northern California. Expect warmer afternoon temperatures with breezy westside winds and gusty coastal winds. Those gusty offshore winds along the coast will lead to some warmer temperatures for the Brookings area. Highs could push 80 degrees around Brookings. Meanwhile, inland highs in our westside valleys will climb to around 80 degrees with lower 70s expected for the Klamath Basin.

Tonight: Skies will stay clear tonight, and winds will calm down inland. However, coastal winds could still gust up to 25 MPH. Otherwise, overnight lows will turn a bit milder compared to Wednesday night. It should be in the mid-50s along the coast, with mid to upper-40s for the Rogue Valley. Expect low to mid-40s for the Shasta Valley and mountains, with mid to upper-30s for the eastside.

Extended:  Inland temperatures will keep climbing on Friday with highs getting into the middle to upper 80s in our westside valleys and highs around 80 degrees in the Klamath Basin.

As we head into this weekend, we'll see some increased clouds and winds picking up for the region. This will drop temperatures just a bit, but highs will still be running a little bit above average for early June. A storm system late Sunday into Monday will bring the chance for showers to our region. It'll also cool temperatures down to start next week. We'll keep an eye on this one, but it looks like wet weather could be a bit more widespread for us than with these last few storm systems.

Tuesday will be dry and brighter with temperatures starting to warm up again. Long-range forecast models are hinting at some hot weather building into the region by later next week.

Stay up to date on the forecast by tuning into our future newscasts or right here on KDRV.com
