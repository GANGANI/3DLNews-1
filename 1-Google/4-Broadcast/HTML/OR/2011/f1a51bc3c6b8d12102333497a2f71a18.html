SCAM ALERT: 'WELLS FARGO NOTICE: Your card has been deactivated'

by Katie Boer KVAL News

Sun, October 23rd 2011 at 5:00 PM
kval1759-1319343648.mar.mp4

SPRINGFIELD, OR--"Simply don't share confidential information."

That's the bottom line--according Andres Speck who manages the Wells Fargo in Springfield.

The scam that has been going on since the summer and now picking up speed once again.

The text or phone message saying your 'Wells Fargo card has been de-activated' is one of the largest phishing scams the Oregon Attorney General's office says they've seen in some time.

"We're definitely not going to call you and ask you to share confidential information," said bank manager Speck. "We have the confidential information that we require already in our system.

Jody Stafford doesn't even bank at Wells Fargo, but she was still a target.

"I just got this text message. It said 'Wells Fargo' and 'Your account has been deactivated'", described Stafford. "l thought about it and I was like 'But I don't have a Wells Fargo account' so how can they deactivate it?

Stafford isn't the only one, Wells Fargo, the Oregon Attorney General's office and our KVAL newsroom have received dozens of Facebook messages, emails and calls from people who received the text.

The Attorney General's office says it's similar to a scam that started in back in August.

The scammer sends a text from a '541' area code, claiming to be Wells Fargo saying your card has been deactivated.

Tony Green, a spokeman for the Attorney General says most of these numbers aren't even legitimate! Instead--part of what the department of justice office calls international "spoofing", or scammers who change their overseas numbers to look like the real thing.

So KVAL News took the numbers--and tried to call the scammers directly.

No surprise--not one number connected us to Wells Fargo.

Instead...

"We're sorry your call cannot be completed as dialed."
"The number you have dialed is not a working number, please check the number and dial again," or...
"If you would like to make or receive calls anywhere in the united states absolutely free simply visit MagicTalk.com."

If that message sounds familiar--don't be fooled!

You can protect yourself by not responding to the message or deleting it and reporting the scam to Wells Fargo or the Attorney General's office directly.
