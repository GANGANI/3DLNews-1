18-year-old drowns after distress on Santiam River

by News Staff

Sat, May 30th 2015 at 12:00 AM
Updated Sat, May 30th 2015 at 5:00 PM
DetroitL-1433123751.j4z.mp4

DETROIT, Ore. -18-year-old Cody Ellingsworth died as a result of drowning after he attempted to swim across the Santiam River Saturday afternoon with a group of friends.

While swimming, Ellingsworth became distressed. When his friends looked back for him, he was out of sight.

The friends found him and pulled him out of the water. They performed CPR immediately and he was later transported to an area hospital via Life Flight.

Marion County Sheriff's Deputies and fire personnel responded to the report of the distressed swimmer near Blowout Road. Ellingsworth was swimming near where the Santiam River enters Detroit Lake.

Ellingsworth was a senior at Sprague High School and was expected to graduate in a couple weeks.

With the warming weather, the sheriff's office is asking swimmers to know their swimming abilities and to always use a life preserver when swimming in open waters.

This year, because of a lack of rain, water levels are about 40 feet below normal at Detroit Lake. Unchartered shorelines bring out a new set of safety concerns.

"Today we're standing where 20 feet of water would probably be. We still have the stumps and the rocks. The new hazards are going to be the closer you get to the shoreline," said marine deputy David Zahn.

Zahn said that because the water levels are so much lower, the river current is running a lot closer to the surface and that also means the water is going to be colder.
