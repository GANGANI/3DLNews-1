Debate over new Erickson Air-Crane training site

by Ashley Carrasco

Tue, December 8th 2015 at 6:40 PM
Erickson Air-Crane (KTVL / Ashley Carrasco)

    TOPICS:

    Erickson

GRANTS PASS - Monday, the manager of Josephine County airports addressed concerns over a possible training site for Erickson, an aviation company which specializes in government services, manufacturing and MRO, and commercial services such as firefighting, energy construction, timber harvesting, HVAC & specialty, and oil and gas. Currently the company owns a training site in White City, Oregon. Pacific Power has built towers on the land next to the training site, "...making it more dangerous of pilots and crews to train," said Andy Mills, Vice President of Commercial Aviation.

The corporation needs to find a new training site. It is considering several locations including Illinois Valley Airport in Josephine County.

Managers at Erickson approached airports manager Larry Graves in August about leasing out a portion of the airport. The company would build its own helipad and pay for any adjustments that need to be made on the land in order to conduct safe training.

There have been multiple public meetings to collect feedback from the community. In early December, about 40 people protested outside one of the meetings.
"Illinois Valley Airport is the ideal location because we want to stay local. It's closer to our manufacturing site and we will use local employees, but we don't want to upset the community because that's not what we're about," Mills said.

The airport manager said it's a small minority of the residents who oppose the training site. On a map, he pointed out the training would occur over property belonging to The Bureau of Land Management.

"It would not create anymore noise than what people already hear from current helicopters that fly in and out of the area," Graves said.

Training would consist of 150 hours for the entire season. Erickson is hoping to get pilots and crews out there for one to two days at a time.

"You would have one day where there training all day, no training the next week, and then training maybe on Tuesday and Thursday the following week. So there wouldn't be constant activity," Graves said.

Training usually lasts through May.

"We'd only be training during the off season because June through October is fire season. We're off fighting fires," Mills said.

There has also been concern about how the helicopters will impact people's health. Mills ensured the helicopters and air-cranes are just as loud, possibly quieter than a regular helicopter. "Unless you're standing right under it and not taking proper safety precautions, it will not impact residents hearing ability," he said.

The Airport Advisory Board and Erickson are working together to address residents concerns. Both companies also want to hear from people who are in support of the change. Mills said they are also considering doing a ground helicopter tour to answer any questions people might have.
