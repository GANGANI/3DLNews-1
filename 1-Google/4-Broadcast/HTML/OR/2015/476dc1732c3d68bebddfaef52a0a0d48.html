Witnessing the Attack on Pearl Harbor

by David De Rurange

Tue, December 8th 2015 at 6:42 PM
Jim Runels shows a copy of the Honolulu Star's front page about the attack on Pearl Harbor on Dec. 7, 1941. Dec. 7, 2015. (KTVL/Dave De Rurange)

CENTRAL POINT, Ore. The United States of America was drawn into World War II after a devastating attack on Pearl Harbor 74-years-ago. Central Point resident Jim Runels said he witnessed the Japanese planes pummel the U.S. Naval base two weeks before his ninth birthday on December 7, 1941.

"I got up on Sunday morning. I had my breakfast and I suddenly heard planes flying over and I was curious. I remember my mother hearing over the radio that we were under attack." Runels said.

Japanese war planes bombed the base where Jim's father was stationed. But at the time, Navy Ensign Donald S. Runels was out at sea. Meanwhile, Jim's family was told to stay inside.

"But me, I mean, well our house was situated where we had a breezeway where a garage was, and we had two garage doors that swung vertical. So I climbed up on the garage door, up onto the roof of the house. And I was up on the roof of the house, up on the front and was watching the planes fly over. And I was looking at the pilots. And I didn't quite know what was going on. And then my mother was hysterical, when she found out where I was."

More than 2,400 Americans were killed in the attack on Pearl Harbor. The Japanese casualties counted fewer than 100.

"We lived in the highlands, right above Waikiki Beach. And I could look right straight down into Pearl Harbor, I could see the whole thing going on." Runels said.

Runels said his mother climbed up on the garage and took him down into safety. He said he didn't understand what was happening.

"The Arizona was the first one they hit. And it sank, almost immediately."

Runels' father returned the next day and they walked to the port to see the damage. He showed News10 pictures of what he and his father witnessed.

"Now that's one of the dry docks, that was where we went and that's where we found the camera. Somebody just left it and forgot about it." Runels said as he pointed to a picture with a severely damaged submarine in the background.

Almost a year after the attack on Pearl Harbor, Runels' father, Donald, was killed in action while aboard the Northhamption during the battle of Tassafaronga Strait.
