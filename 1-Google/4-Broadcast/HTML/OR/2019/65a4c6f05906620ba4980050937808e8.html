20 years ago, fear of Y2K dominated the New Year's Eve news

by News Staff

EUGENE, Ore. - Twenty years ago as the world prepared to party BECAUSE it was 1999, the specter of Y2K loomed.

Would computers stop working after Dec. 31, 1999?

Would the so-called Millennium Bug cause systems to crash, knocking out power ... disrupting financial systems ... causing prison doors to unlock ... unleashing unimaginable havoc on the world ... or other chaos and calamity.

Well, hindsight, like the New Year now upon us, is 20/20.

Public safety officials said New Year's Eve 1999/2000 was quieter in Lane County, Oregon, than a typical weeknight.

So we took a dive in our archives and assembled a montage of stories from Y2K that show just how seriously people took the possibility of widespread mayhem.
