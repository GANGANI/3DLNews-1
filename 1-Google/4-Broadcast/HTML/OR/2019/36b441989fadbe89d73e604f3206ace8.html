Large fight erupts between two groups in NE Portland, woman appears knocked unconscious

by Kellee Azar, KATU News

Thu, May 2nd 2019 at 10:55 PM
Updated Fri, May 3rd 2019 at 7:13 PM
In this still image from video, a woman who appeared to be knocked unconscious during a fight in Northeast Portland Wednesday, May 1, 2019 is picked up and carried away. (Contributed image) (WARNING: Video contains profanity and violence)

PORTLAND, Ore. — Police responded to a large fight in Northeast Portland Wednesday night between two groups.

That fight appeared to leave a woman unconscious.

Neighbors say they were shocked when they watched the whole brawl unfold.

"This guy pushed a girl to the ground, then she got up and rushed at him then she got maced and a guy swung and hit her in the head with a baton and knocked her cold out," one neighbor told KATU News.

Police did not identify the two groups, but video obtained by KATU News showed Patriot Prayer leader Joey Gibson in attendance.

The most surprising part to neighbors in the area of Northeast Couch and 8th was that all day things had stayed peaceful.

"I heard it was pretty peaceful for most of the day, then it just sort of popped off right here and wasn’t peaceful," the neighbor who didn't want to be identified said.

Neighbors tell KATU the fight went on for some time but police didn't arrive for at least 30 minutes.

"It probably took cops about 30-40 minutes to get here. And they showed up it was 6 cops they really didn’t do much." The neighbor said.

(WARNING: Video contains profanity and violence)

Police said they were called to near Northeast 8th Avenue and Couch Street around 7:30 p.m. They said they were told someone was unconscious but officers couldn’t find the person.

Police said when they arrived at the scene the two groups had separated. They said there were about 60 people involved in the fight.

KATU News also reached out to the Mayor's office about the response time:

"To respond safely for all involved -- PPB went to emergency calls only across the city and officers responded to the fight with adequate police to safely manage the large confrontation. Understandably this took time to coordinate a safe police response. As Police Commissioner – the Mayor trusts PPB officers on their training and expertise involving tactics."- Mayor Ted Wheeler's Office

Video showed the groups shouting at each other. The confrontation devolved into punches and a woman was knocked to the ground. She appeared to be knocked unconscious and was carried away.

Police said they are reviewing video and photographs of the fight and are working to determine whether any arrests will be made.

Police said no one self-identified as a victim and no one wanted to make a police report.

The fight came on the heels of a peaceful day of May Day marches and rallies.
