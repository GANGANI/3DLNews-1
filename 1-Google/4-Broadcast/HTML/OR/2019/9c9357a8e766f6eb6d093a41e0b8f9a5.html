WEATHERWATCH
Beloved KOMO weathercaster Steve Pool to retire
by KOMO News Staff
Wednesday, Nov 20th 2019
48
View All Photos
Steve Pool (KOMO News){&nbsp;}

After more than 40 years in the business, KOMO TV's beloved weathercaster Steve Pool is retiring from broadcasting.

ADVERTISEMENT

A University of Washington graduate, Steve spent nearly his entire career at KOMO, starting as an intern in the late 1970s and becoming one of the most well known broadcasters in the history of the Northwest.

“Steve Pool is in a class by himself as one of the great local TV personalities ever," said KOMO General Manager Janene Drafs. "I can’t think of a single project we produced at KOMO with Steve that he didn’t make better. A huge presence on and off camera. KOMO and Seattle were lucky to have him."

Steve%20Pool%20file%20photo%7B %7D%7Bp%7D%7B/p%7D

Steve built a career unmatched in community service while winning several Emmy awards as a weathercaster for KOMO and host of the award-winning nationally syndicated show Front Runners.

Recognized as one of the first African-American weathercasters in the nation, Steve often stepped in as a weather anchor for ABC's Good Morning America.

"I'd say 99.9% of the things I was able to do here was so far...so hugely more than I ever dreamed in my wildest dreams...really to be able to do this business and to be able to be successful with it," Steve said about his career.

When asked about anchoring weather, Steve said he enjoyed how fun it was.

"It's fun. It's just fun. What I liked about it is that I could say things the way I just wanted to say them," Steve said. "There was no script, and for some people that might be threatening...but for me, it was just, 'Hey I'm with my friends let me tell you what's going on over here and what's going on over there.'"

Steve%20Pool%20file%20photo%7B %7D

When asked about his retirement, Steve said he will miss being in the broadcast business.

"I'll tell you this much...I'm sure that I will...I will miss it," he said. "And in some ways, I wish I could just snap my finger and be right back down there trying to get the forecast right."

Steve%20Pool%20(KOMO%20News)%7B %7D%7Bp%7D%7B/p%7D

KOMO anchor Eric Johnson described Steve as "television magic."

“He just has that special ‘thing’ that we all aspire to in this business: he can be himself in front of a camera. He can cut through the invisible barrier of the lens and communicate freely. When he was up there doing the weather at the top of his game, he was like great jazz. Ad-libbing, free-flowing, natural, graceful. He made something very difficult look very easy. He was the master. And he cared about his job and worked tirelessly show after show, year after year, for a long, long time," Eric said. "And he is as graceful as a friend as he has been as a broadcaster. He cares. He is decent. He is generous. He loves his friends. He cares about the viewers. He loves being loved."

KOMO anchor Molly Shen said this is the end of an era for KOMO.

"Steve isn’t someone who simply worked here. He has been one of the most recognizable faces on our air for decades. And he’s a friend. I’ll miss hearing his laugh around the newsroom every day and his knack for making a complex job seem easy. But I’m so happy for Steve and his family. He’s earned this retirement, and I hope he loves every minute of it," Molly said.

Eric%20Johnson,%20Molly%20Shen%20and%20Steve%20Pool%7B %7D%7Bp%7D%7B/p%7D

In late September of last year, Steve shared that he was diagnosed with prostate cancer and was taking time off to get treatment.

As of now, we are happy to say Steve is in complete remission and will return to KOMO for a farewell forecast next Tuesday at 6 p.m.

“A true professional and the consummate communicator, Steve Pool set the standard for telling a weather story, rather than giving a weather report," said KOMO News Director Bill Dallman. "He has a national reputation for all the right reasons. Steve will be sorely missed in the newsroom."

KOMO's Mary Nam also shared her thoughts.

“Steve has been a part of KOMO for so long that my brain has a hard time accepting the news," Mary said. "The 4 o’clock newscast with Steve is special to me. But when I think about his family and how many decades Steve has spent working nights, I’m thrilled that he can now enjoy his well-deserved retirement and savor that time with Michelle and their girls. He better tune into the 4 every now and then and submit some good guesses for the KOMO QUESTION!”

Tune in at 11 p.m. Monday as KOMO's Molly Shen talks with Steve about his cancer treatment and health.

And at 6 p.m. Tuesday, Steve will return to KOMO for a farewell forecast.

Our friends at Seattle Refined will also have a retirement special at 3 p.m. Tuesday, and KOMO's Eric Johnson will have a special Eric's Heroes on Steve at 6 p.m. the same day.
