'Not a call to arms,' Curry County Sheriff explains Facebook post on rumored "Antifa bus"

by Christina Giardinelli

Thu, June 4th 2020 at 7:59 PM
Updated Thu, June 4th 2020 at 10:12 AM
9

Coquille residents with open carry weapons wait for rumored Antifa protest. The same rumor that brought counter-protesters to Coquille spread to Curry County thanks in part to a public post form the Curry County Sheriff's Department (courtesy Michael Giardinelli)

    TOPICS:

    FACEBOOK, INC.FacebookJohn WardPortlandTWITTER, INC.Jonathan P. WardCoos BayJohn Huttl

Curry County, Ore. — Note: Since this article was published the formerly public Curry County Justice Facebook page that Sheriff John Ward confirmed he manages is no longer visible to the public either because the page was deleted or made private. This article has been updated with screenshots of the post taken before it was removed.

The unsubstantiated claim that busloads full of Antifa activists were heading to small rural areas bringing with them outside instigators "looking for trouble," has made its way to Southern Oregon but it was not just residents on social media that were concerned.

On Tuesday Curry County Sheriff, John Ward, posted the rumor to a public Facebook page that he uses to provide information to residents of the county. The entire Facebook was either deleted or made private shortly after this article was published.

"I don’t know if the rumors are true or not just yet but I got information about 3 (busloads) of ANTIFA protestors (who) are making their way from Douglas County headed for Coquille then to Coos Bay," the post read.
During a phone interview on Wednesday, Ward confirmed that he manages the page and had made the post.


The rumor is similar to the ones that have been widely circulated on social media nationwide. Twitter and Facebook have since busted some of the instigators behind the unsubstantiated rumor and exposed them as members of the white supremacist group, Identity Evropa.

Ward said his information came from a message that was forwarded to him by the County Council, John Huttl.

"Our county attorney forwarded me a post from somebody and it was sent to him by another attorney that is kind of a private attorney," Ward said.

He said the message read "there are three busses full of Antifa coming, they left less than 45 minutes ago." He said the message went on to say "law enforcement is geared up in Coquille, I'm in Coquille now, if they blow through they are headed to Coos Bay and we will need help, they stated they are ready for a fight."

A Coos County-based news outlet reported today that 100's of armed residents showed up in Coquille to protect the small town based on what appears to have been the same rumor.

Huttl was not immediately able to provide more information but his office has told News 10 he would respond to questions as soon as possible.

Ward's post went on to say that "local police and the Oregon State Police," were prepared to respond if the threat proved to be real. He also wrote "without asking I am sure we have a lot of local boys too with guns that will protect our citizens and their property. "


While the majority of the over 200 comments responding to his post were supportive of its content, some comments expressed concern that the post could incite violence. Many of those that responded vowed they would show up armed and ready to provide support.

Ward, however, told News 10, the post was in no way calling on citizens to "take the law into their own hands." Though the post and subsequent comments he made to the post, said it was nice to know that armed citizens had the department's back, he said this was a matter of fact and not a call to arms.

"A lot of people in Curry County are already armed prior to this whole thing," he said. "It's an open carry state, I see people walking around in stores all the time with guns on their side."

He said the fact that residents in the county would show up to the protest armed was a given.

"I wouldn't have to ask for them to come, if they hear about it they are going to protect their citizens and their homes," Ward said.

In a comment, Ward had called Antifa protesters "thugs" and warned residents that "they have stated they are seeking to kill law enforcement." When asked where this information was coming from Ward said it was based on what he had heard from violent riots in other parts of the state and country.

When asked if he felt the armed citizens would have been a help to law enforcement if Antifa activists did show up, Ward said he did fear the department would have been overwhelmed had Antifa showed up.

"In Portland, I heard through the Oregon State Sheriff's association that when they were confronting the people up there rioting they were close to running out of ammunition and bodies," he said. "I would expect if we were confronted by 3 busloads of Antifas and they started throwing rocks and bottles and pushing towards us and we're getting our asses kicked or handed to us that they (armed citizens) are going to step in and help us out."

Ward reinforced that the post was not meant as a call for armed citizens to "take the law into their own hands. However, he said he felt their presence could be a deterrent for any potential violent behavior from protesters.

"It's just a show of force, you know people are going to show up and they see maybe 20 law enforcement officers and you got 300 rioters we are going to be outmanned," he said. "But if you have a lot of citizens that are just standing there saying 'hey we support our law enforcement' it makes a big difference."

Both the Medford Police Department and the Grants Pass Public Safety department told News 10 they had looked into the rumors of Antifa coming and found them to be unsubstantiated. The rumor made its way through many local neighborhood watch groups on social media.

Protests in both Medford and Grants Pass saw armed residents show up to "protect" the city from what one Grants Pass counter-protester defined on Tuesday as "thugs."

Both protests remained peaceful through the Medford Police Department arrested a man on Monday for pointing a gun at protesters in two separate incidents. The Mail Tribune reported that the man had previously been head chanting "all lives matter," at protesters.
