Tesla crashes at 100 mph in Corvallis, sends batteries shooting through apartment windows

by News Staff

Thu, November 19th 2020 at 8:55 AM
Updated Fri, November 20th 2020 at 9:14 AM

CORVALLIS, Ore. - A driver high on marijuana crashed a Tesla into an apartment building late Tuesday night, sending hundreds of small batteries flying, police said.

Two of the batteries shot through apartment windows.

One landed in a resident's lap.

The other came to rest on a bed - and set the sheets on fire.

The force of the crash also sent a tire hurtling into the second story wall of an apartment, breaking a water pipe in the wall. That in turn destroyed the bathroom in the unit - and flooded the downstairs.

The batteries prompted a warning from the Corvallis police and fire departments to be aware of "potentially hazardous batteries in the area of the NW Walnut and NW Fairlawn."

"A concerted effort was made to collect as many of the batteries as possible at the time of the crash, but we received reports of the batteries actually going through windows of nearby residences because of the crash," police said.

The agencies cautioned that the batteries:

  • Can reportedly stay hot to the touch for up to 24 hours and could cause burns if handled.
  • Can release toxic fumes that may harm people and animals if inhaled.
  • Can leak substances that may harm people and animals if they are exposed.

Anyone who finds one is asked to call the Corvallis Regional Communication Center at 541-766-6914. "Do not touch the battery," authorities said. "Corvallis Fire Department personnel will respond to collect the battery for proper disposal."

Police responded to the crash after 10 p.m. Tuesday.

The investigation found the suspect was going over 100 mph in a 2019 Tesla S when he lost control of the electric car.

The Tesla left the road and hit a utility pole, breaking the pole and knocking over two trees and a telephone junction box, police said.

"The damage from the collision caused the batteries from the Tesla to enter two different residences by breaking through the windows, one landing on a person’s lap and the second landing on a bed, catching the bedding on fire," police said. "A tire was ripped from the car during the collision and struck the second story siding of a nearby apartment complex with such force that it ruptured the water pipes within the wall, destroying the bathroom to the apartment and flooding the downstairs portion of the apartment as well."

Police said the suspect fled on foot. He was found 3 blocks away and taken to the hospital.

Police cited the driver - Dylan Milota of Corvallis - on suspicion of DUII - Drugs (Marijuana), Hit and Run, Criminal Mischief 2, Reckless Driving and Reckless Endangering.
