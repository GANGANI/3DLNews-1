Police explain their "hands-off" approach to Medford protest

by Shelby Reilly

Tue, June 2nd 2020 at 4:20 PM
Updated Wed, June 3rd 2020 at 8:33 PM
4
VIEW ALL PHOTOS
Protestors at Monday's protest, holding up signs and chanting in the street (Shelby Reilly/News 10)

MEDFORD, Ore. — Police said they took a “hands-off” approach to the protests that happened throughout the day on Monday, and are happy with the outcome.

“There’s always a fine line if protests get out of hand and there’s an injury to citizens or damage to property if that’s a line that gets crossed we are going to step in,” Scott Clauson, Chief of the Medford Police Department, said.

Clauson said the Medford Police Department decided to watch from afar, and only intervene if things started to get out of hand.

“Overall, I was pretty happy with how the protest went. We certainly respect people's right to gather and express their first amendment right,” Clauson said.

He explained the department took this approach because they wanted to give people on either side the opportunity to protest in a safe manner.

"It really doesn't matter what the issue is or what the topic is, we just want to create a safe environment," Clauson said.

Clauson said leading up to the protest, the department did not feel they would need to block off streets or surround protestors.


"We didn't really think we needed to block any streets off. The organizer really kind of self-managed and self-policed the crowd," Clauson said.

He said the only concern leading up to the protests were rumors of ANTIFA groups showing up, but that did not end up being the case.

“I do think that there were folks from out of town. There were rumors that we were going to get a very violent, ANTIFA type group down from Portland and Eugene. That never materialized, there wasn’t any damage at all,” Clauson said.

Nearly 1,000 people showed up in front of City Hall on Monday to protest the death of 46-year-old George Floyd who died at the hands of Minneapolis police officers.

“That was really our philosophy, to allow them to express their protest rights. We again, wanted to provide protection and to prevents any assaults and any issues such as vandalism to downtown businesses,” Clauson said.

Clauson said thought things remained peaceful there were a few incidents where police were forced to get involved.

“We did respond to a man waving a gun around. We knew that was quickly going to get out of hand. This situation resulted in him getting arrested for menacing and he was taken to jail,” Clauson said.

He said there were also other incidents reported to the department.


“We are currently investigating a case in which a motorist drove a little hastily I’ll say at this point, into a group of protestors,” Clauson said. “There were no injuries but the car was surrounded."

Clauson said the department intervened and interviewed the driver who could potentially be charged with reckless driving.

He said when police were called to the scene, officers attempted to handle it as quickly as possible and then leave the scene to let participants continue protesting.

“When we were called to the scene a couple of times, we also elected to, once we deescalated the situation, to leave and let them do their thing,” Clauson said.

Clauson explained he wants to remind the public on behalf of the department that he is devasted by the death of George Floyd, and supports the prosecution of those involved.

“I really truly was saddened by what happened in Minneapolis. We as a law enforcement community are mourning along with the family, we’re mourning along with the community,” Clauson said.

He explained the Medford Police Department cares about the community and wants to reassure people that would not happen from them

“What we saw in that situation were tactics that were not taught, tactics that I’m confident we would never see from our officers,” Clauson said.
