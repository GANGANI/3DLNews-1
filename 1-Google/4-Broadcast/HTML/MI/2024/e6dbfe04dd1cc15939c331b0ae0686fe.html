Michigan man sentenced for threatening to blow up News Channel 3

by Katie Sergent | News Channel 3

Thu, February 8th 2024 at 3:25 AM
Updated Thu, February 8th 2024 at 11:43 AM
Brandon Bazzi, 37, will spend years behind bars for threatening to blow up News Channel 3 in April 2023. Bazzi had been sentenced to 55 months to 20 years on one count of false report or threat of terrorism on Jan. 16, 2024. (Michigan Department of Corrections/WWMT)

    TOPICS:

    MichiganNews Channel 3BazziBombThreatTerrorismSentencedPolice

KALAMAZOO, Mich. — A 37-year-old Michigan man will spend years behind bars for threatening to blow up News Channel 3.

Brandon Bazzi was sentenced to serve 55 months to 20 years in prison on one count of false report or threat of terrorism Jan. 16, Kalamazoo County Chief Assistant Prosecuting Attorney Jeff Williams confirmed Wednesday.

He previously pled guilty to the charge on Dec. 15, 2023.

  • More details: Michigan man who threatened to blow up News Channel 3 pleads guilty to terrorism charge

Bazzi, a father of two, pushed through the front doors of News Channel 3's Kalamazoo building around 2:15 p.m. April 13, 2023, and asked to talk to a reporter.

Meeting with News Channel 3 Chief Engineer George Markle, the 37-year-old was told there wasn't anyone he could speak to at the time.

Bazzi then told Markle he had a bomb and "wasn't afraid to die," prompting Markle to lock the door, trap Bazzi in the lobby and evacuate the building.

For two hours, police negotiated with Bazzi over the phone as he smoked cigarettes inside the lobby.

He had repeatedly told officers that he had a bomb and he wanted them to shoot him, but surrendered to officers around 4:30 p.m.

  • Criminal past:Man who threatened to blow up News Channel 3 has long criminal past

"I did not think that that was real," Bazzi said. "I didn't. Honest to God. I didn't think that was real."

Bazzi's backpack that contained the alleged explosive was found to have only various electronics and wires designed to look like a bomb, according to police.

It remains unknown why he targeted News Channel 3.
