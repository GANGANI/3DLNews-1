Karine Jean-Pierre dodges question about Biden's health: 'I am not a medical doctor'

by JACKSON WALKER | The National Desk

Thu, February 8th 2024 at 3:54 PM
3
VIEW ALL PHOTOS
White House press secretary Karine Jean-Pierre speaks during the daily briefing at the White House in Washington, Monday, Feb. 6, 2023. (AP Photo/Susan Walsh)

    TOPICS:

    BidenHealthJean-PierrePresidentMedicalDoctorAgeElection

WASHINGTON (TND) — White House press secretary Karine Jean-Pierre appeared hesitant Thursday to answer a question about President Joe Biden’s health, instead referring reporters to his doctor.

“You see him for yourself just like I see him for myself,” Jean-Pierre said when asked to characterize Biden's overall health. “I am not a medical doctor, I cannot comment on his overall health. Obviously, you’ve heard directly from his physician. He’s put out a detailed, detailed memo and document on the president’s health.”

She then noted the president has been very open about his health and continues to see his physician for regular check-ups.

“He’s going to have another annual physical which we are going to be transparent, just like we have the last two years and share that with all of you. I think its just not for me to comment on the president’s medical health,” Jean-Pierre said.

Earlier in the press conference, Jean-Pierre defended Biden after he appeared to confuse world leaders with their predecessors multiple times in the past week. The gaffes were due to the president’s depth of “experience” on the world stage, she explained.

It is not uncommon,” Jean-Pierre said of other leaders making similar mistakes. “This is a president that has had a very successful three years."

“His leadership, his experience, I believe speaks for itself. I think that’s what is important and I hope the American people see that," Jean-Pierre added.

Biden, 81, continues to face concerns about his age as the 2024 election approaches. A recent poll found 76% of respondents agreed Biden may lack the required mental and physical health to serve a second term.

Age has become an issue for both leading presidential candidates, as former President Donald Trump, the presumed GOP nominee, has begun to draw similar remarks from his Republican primary challenger.

“He said multiple times that he ran against President Obama," GOP candidate Nikki Haley said at an event in New Hampshire in January. "These things happen because, guess what? When you’re 80, that’s what happens."
