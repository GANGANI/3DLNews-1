‘He was always a hero’: DNA testing identifies remains of missing WWII soldier

By Robert Castillo and Gray News staff
Published: Feb. 9, 2024 at 4:57 AM EST|Updated: Feb. 9, 2024 at 5:01 AM EST

PRINCETON, W.Va. (WVVA/Gray News) - The remains of an Army soldier who was declared missing in action have been identified, according to his family.

Private First-Class Mose Vance entered the U.S. Army during World War II. He was from McDowell County, West Virginia.

He joined the Army in October 1943, but he was killed in action less than two years later.

Vance was killed in Wildenguth, France on Jan. 11, 1945. Vance’s remains weren’t immediately recovered, and he received a missing in action status and was presumed dead.

His remains were eventually recovered by U.S. Forces but weren’t identified until this year thanks to two curious cousins.

“My grandmother had plaques on the wall with his military picture and a picture of him being in the military band. Some news clippings on him. He was always a hero I thought about growing up,” said Vance’s nephew, Ferrell Vance.

Vance along with his cousin Chester Ball made it their goal to find out what exactly happened to their lost uncle. Vance managed to find an organization in France dedicated to connecting families to lost soldiers.

The group was able to recover Mose Vance’s personalized bayonet from his death site. The U.S. military used this information to cross-examine DNA from Ferrell Vance and his uncle Mose’s unidentified remains. It was a match.

“I teared up, I couldn’t hardly talk to him. It’s hard to believe it’s real. I wanted to do it for my grandmother because she never would accept that he had gotten killed. I just couldn’t get it off my mind. I had to try and find him,” said Vance.

Private First Class Vance’s remains are currently being held at an Air Force base in Nebraska awaiting final arrangements.

Vance and Ball say their uncle’s remains will be returned home to McDowell County to be buried in the family’s graveyard. They’re hopeful it will be sometime this year.
