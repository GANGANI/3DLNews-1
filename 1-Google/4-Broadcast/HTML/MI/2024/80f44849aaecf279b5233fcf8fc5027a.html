Amber Alert canceled as 4 missing children found safe in UP, Green Bay

By TV6 News Team
Published: Feb. 9, 2024 at 5:50 AM EST|Updated: Feb. 9, 2024 at 6:08 PM EST

GREEN BAY, Wis. (WLUC) - UPDATE: An Amber Alert issued Friday morning in Wisconsin has been canceled as all four children have been located safely, according to the Wisconsin Crime Alert Network.

The two suspects in the alert, Diana Halfaday and Deanna Ward, are members of the Hannahville Indian Community, according to Hannahville Tribal Police. Ward says she had no idea anyone was looking for her children until the alert was issued.

Three children were found in the U.P. with their grandmother, Halfaday. The Hannahville Tribal Police said they were taken to the Forsyth Township Police Department, where their identifications were confirmed and social services will now work to have them transported back to Wisconsin. One child was found in Green Bay with their mother, Ward.

This story will be updated if further information is released.

Editor’s note: This story was updated to include that the children found in the U.P. were transported to the Forsyth Township Police Department.
