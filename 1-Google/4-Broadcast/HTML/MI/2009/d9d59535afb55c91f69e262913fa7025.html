Chuck E. Cheese Liquor License

Tue, June 16th 2009 at 5:44 PM

Tue, 16 Jun 2009 21:44:08 GMT — Chuck E. Cheese has always been known to have a liquor license. After a gang fight broke out in the Flint Chuck E Cheese, January, 2008 the location voluntarily stopped selling alcohol. At a Flint Township meeting, board members voted and approved the request to change the Flint Chuck E. Cheese liquor license. The company's corporate spokesperson Brenda Holloway said, "Chuck E Cheese has applied for and was granted a change from a Full Service C Liquor license to a Tavern License in all of their Michigan Chuck E. Cheese locations. At this time, however, we have no plans to resume selling beer and wine at our Flint township location." The change of licenses was a cost saving move that allows the company to keep selling beer and wine in other Michigan locations.
