Medal of Honor recipient responds to President Trump's re-tweet of altered image

by Andrew Minegar and Mike Krafcik | Newschannel 3

Thu, October 31st 2019 at 9:48 PM
Updated Thu, October 31st 2019 at 10:34 PM
President Donald Trump bestows the nation's highest military honor, the Medal of Honor, to retired Army medic James McCloughan during a ceremony in the East Room of the White House Monday, July 31, 2017, in Washington. McCloughan is credited with saving members of his platoon nearly 50 years ago in the Battle of Nui Yon Hill in Vietnam. (AP Photo/Alex Brandon)

SOUTH HAVEN, Mich. — President Donald Trump re-tweeted an altered image Wednesday of himself giving the Medal of Honor to the dog that was wounded in the raid that killed Abu Bakr al-Baghdadi.

The original image appears to be the Associated Press photograph of Trump giving the Medal of Honor to Vietnam veteran James McCloughan, a South Haven resident, in 2017.

McCloughan said he wasn’t bothered by the picture at all.

“I’m not offended by another hero,” he said.

McCloughan, a self-described dog lover, said he looks up to the dog for the bravery he displayed during the raid.

The Belgian Malinois joined the raid on the Islamic State leader’s compound near the Syrian-Turkey border. Trump announced Al-Bahdadi’s death on Sunday from the White House.

The image was originally shared by The Daily Wire, a conservative news and opinion website created by Ben Shapiro.
