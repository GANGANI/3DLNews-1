MUST WATCH: Man lucky to be alive, rescued seconds before truck sinks in Lake Huron

by NBC25/FOX66 Newsroom

Sun, February 17th 2019 at 8:42 PM
Updated Tue, February 19th 2019 at 7:40 PM
6
VIEW ALL PHOTOS
Police say 62-year-old man from Kimball was driving the truck that went into the Lexington State Harbor. (Picture from Sanilac County Sheriff)

SANILAC COUNTY, Mich. -- The Sanilac County Sheriff's Department released new details into the case of a man who is accused of driving onto the ice at the Lexington Harbor.

The man's vehicle went through the ice, but not before the man had to be rescued after going back to the truck.

The video of the entire incident went viral over the weekend.

The Sheriff's Department says it was a 62-year-old Kimball man who was driving the truck.

Investigators arrested the man on suspicion of drunk driving.

Crews pulled the truck from the harbor on Sunday.


The Sanilac County Sheriff doesn't know why the man went back to the truck as it was sinking.

You can hear people yelling at the man in the video to get away from the truck.

The man was checked out by paramedics and was then arrested.

The man's name has not yet been released.
