Genesee County Sheriff blames fentanyl for increase in overdose deaths

by Dave Bondy

Wed, July 21st 2021 at 8:56 PM
Updated Wed, July 21st 2021 at 11:11 PM
3
VIEW ALL PHOTOS
Photos of fentanyl crystals and pills. (Photo courtesy the Drug Enforcement Agency)

FLINT, Mich - Genesee County Sheriff Chris Swanson said so far this year 106 people have died from drug overdoses in Genesee County.

In the same time period in 2020 -- 81 people died.

Sheriff Swanson said one of the big reasons for the increase in overdoses is Fentanyl.

RELATED LINK: US overdose deaths hit record 93,000 in pandemic last year

Fentanyl is a powerful synthetic opioid that is similar to morphine but is 50 to 100 times more potent.

"It only takes one gram to kill somebody, that's how potent it is," said Swanson.

RELATED LINK: US opioid lawsuits on verge of settlements with 4 companies

According to the Drug Enforcement Agency fentanyl is primarily manufactured in foreign labs and then smuggled into the United States through the U.S - Mexico border.

Swanson is urging everyone to carry Narcan, which is the brand name of naloxone which can immediately reverse an overdose.


"We want to put Narcan out more than ever before because those numbers are going up, everybody should have access to Narcan," said Swanson.
