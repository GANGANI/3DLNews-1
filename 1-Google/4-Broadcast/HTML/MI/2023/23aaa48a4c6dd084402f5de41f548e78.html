R-S-V, COVID-19, and influenza concerns heading into winter season
by Leo Goldman, WSBT 22 Reporter
Monday, Dec 4th 2023
5
View All Photos
The South Bend Medical Foundation said the rising number of COVID and flu cases are now affecting their blood donation efforts with many people sick and forced to cancel. (WSBT Photo)

Three different illnesses are on the rise as winter looms.

ADVERTISEMENT

R-S-V, COVID-19, and influenza are all increasing in caseloads across our region.

The South Bend Medical Foundation said the rising number of COVID and flu cases are now affecting their blood donation efforts with many people sick and forced to cancel.

WSBT(Video)


The Berrien County health department medical director saying it's important for people to remain vigilant during signs of illness.

“After going through the pandemic, everyone's just really tired, and they don't want to do all the careful checking of do I have covid, if I’ve got a runny nose or symptoms, am i testing myself before i go visit grandma and grandpa, because they're also at risk of severe R-S-V, in the latter years, 65 years and older, says,” Rex Cabaltica, BCHD medical director.

But Dr. Cabaltica says it's not time to hit that panic button.

Thanks to vaccines for all three increasing illnesses, he says the resources we have now put us in a better position, a sentiment shared by a member of Beacon Medical Group.

“There's a new booster available, and if you haven't recently had Covid, or recently had a booster, you probably should go get it, there's a lot of people getting sick right now, and this might prevent you from getting ill and it might protect others around you, who are more vulnerable. Particularly, if you're over age 50, or have other medical illnesses, you may want to consider getting a booster soon,” says Dr. Dale Patterson, Beacon Medical Group President.

In addition, while numbers are up across the board, they're still not as high as this time last year.

However, Dr. Cabaltica says he thinks it's likely that a spike will happen again around Christmas time.

” I'm not a fan of masking when I’m with my family, obviously i don't think anyone is a fan of masking with their family either so i think there's going to be some spike, but we're just not in the same position we were at the beginning of the pandemic, i don't think we'll ever, knock on wood, i don't think we'll ever be overwhelmed by covid 19 again as long as we don't see a major mutation that gets around all of our vaccines as well as our treatments,” says Dr. Cabaltica.

ADVERTISEMENT

Dr. Cabaltica says a slight bit of good news is that compared to last year, hospitalizations from influenza are down in our area.
