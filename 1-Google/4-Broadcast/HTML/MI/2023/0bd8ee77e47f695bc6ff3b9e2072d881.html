WK Kellogg receives approval for renaissance zone, keeping jobs in Battle Creek

by Katie Sergent | News Channel 3

Tue, December 12th 2023 at 11:44 AM
Updated Wed, December 13th 2023 at 8:30 AM
The Kellogg Company's Porter Street plant in Battle Creek is pictured in this photo. WK Kellogg is setting its sights on re-investing in the Cereal City. (Mike Krafcik/WWMT)

BATTLE CREEK, Mich. — Hundreds of jobs at WK Kellogg Co are staying in Battle Creek.

Tuesday, a 15-year designated renaissance zone for WK Kellogg Co was approved by the Michigan Economic Development Corporation, or MEDC.

This comes two years after the former Kellogg Company announced in September 2021 that they would be moving some of its cereal volume from Battle Creek to its plant in Belleville, Ontario, by the end of 2023.

Despite saving money through lower manufacturing costs, the move would have idled two of Battle Creek's three operational lines and eliminated 170 jobs, officials said.

Three years later, leadership at the now-WK Kellogg Co reconsidered the move.

By investing in Battle Creek instead of Belleville, the company could retain 170 jobs, create at least 43 others that pay an average of $2,200 per week plus benefits, invest between $43-143 million in infrastructure and invest up to $60 million in employee training, according to a project memorandum by WK Kellogg and the MEDC.

In addition, the move would save WK Kellogg Co about $20 million that would have been lost if volume was moved to Ontario, and encourages future growth at the Battle Creek plant.

To make the project viable, WK Kellogg requested a $5 million business development program grant and an MSF Designated Renaissance Zone for a period of 15 years from the MEDC.

Their request was ultimately approved Tuesday, and will remain valid for 120 days with approval for an MSF Fund Manager to extend the commitment for another 60 days, officials said.

Kellogg Company split into WK Kellogg and Kellanova Oct. 2.

WK Kellogg is Kellogg's food company for the United States, Canada, and Caribbean, that carries brand-names such as Kellogg's, Frosted Flakes, Froot Loops, Mini-Wheats, Special K, Raisin Bran, Rice Krispies, Corn Flakes, Kashi, and Bear Naked, a press release sent out in March detailed.

It is set to earn $2.7 billion in net sales in 2024.

Kellanova, Kellogg's second company, is described as a "global snacking powerhouse" comprised of brands such as Pringles, Cheez-It, Pop-Tarts, Kellogg's Rice Krispies Treats, MorningStar Farms and more, Kellogg officials said.

With locations in Chicago, Illinois, and Battle Creek, the company is slated to earn $13.5 billion in net sales in 2024, according to Kellogg.
