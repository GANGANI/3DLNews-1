Actor Mark Ruffalo reveals new findings in contaminated Flint water

by Kristen Aguirre, Sinclair Broadcast Group

Mon, March 7th 2016 at 9:20 AM
Updated Wed, March 9th 2016 at 8:53 AM
4
VIEW ALL PHOTOS
Mark Ruffalo and Water Defense scientist Scott Smith speak with NBC25 about the Flint water crisis. (WEYI)

FLINT, Mich. (WEYI) Actor and activist Mark Ruffalo, who most recently starred in the Oscar-winning film "Spotlight" used his celebrity status and his non-profit to shed light on the water crisis in Flint, Michigan.

Ruffalo is the co-founder of the organization Water Defense, which aims to "use technology and public engagement to inform people about what is in their water, hold water polluters accountable, and keep our waterways and drinking water sources free from contamination and industrial degradation." The organization Monday revealed new findings in Flint's contaminated water supply.

"This is going to be a long-term problem that needs to be addressed," Ruffalo said. "The more you understand that and the more information that you have, the longer you can sustain and engage the agencies, the federal government, the state government -- the people who are responsible for this --in repairing it. They're going to probably want to try to do some easy fixes but that's not the nature of this problem. This is a big problem."

Ruffalo was joined by Water Defense's lead scientist Scott Smith who said the organization discovered galvanized-lined pipes were used in "most of the school's" and "a lot of the homes" in Flint, where residents have been forced to resort to using bottled water because of a lead-contaminated water supply.

"Galvanized pipes were used after lead pipes and before copper pipes. And you can see the build-up on here," Smith said brandishing a corroded pipe. "Once the galvanized pipe is compromise by corrosive water, there is no turning back. In the overload in the chlorine in the system, it reacts with this."

At a press conference in Flint Monday, Ruffalo said lead levels were higher in the city's water supply were higher than originally reported. Smith added high levels of chloroform and methylene chloride were detected in the water.

Smith said it would be "incomprehensible" to think the water would be considered safe for bathing. One woman testified that her ears began to bleed after taking a shower.

Ruffalo went on to call what's happening in Flint "an atrocity, a travesty, that shouldn't be happening in America."

Speakers at the "Flint Rising" conference then joined together to ask President Barack Obama to declare the ongoing issue in Flint a federal disaster.

Since news surfaced of Flint's "poisoned" water supply, activists and residents have called for the firing of Gov. Rick Snyder. Democratic candidates for president Sen. Bernie Sanders and Secretary of State Hillary Clinton echoed this calls Sunday during a debate in Flint. Snyder said he wouldn't be stepping down amid the crisis, adding "political candidates will be leaving Flint and Michigan. I am committed to the people of Flint," he said on Twitter.
