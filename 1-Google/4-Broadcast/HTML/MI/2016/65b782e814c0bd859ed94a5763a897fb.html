Pilot believes he spotted missing teens lost at sea

by AL PEFLEY

Sat, April 30th 2016 at 10:51 AM
7
VIEW ALL PHOTOS
Video released by the Florida Fish and Wildlife Conservation Commission shows Austin Stephanos and Perry Cohen, both 14, setting out on July 24.

JUPITER, Fla. (WPEC) There are new developments in the case of two missing teens from Tequesta who vanished last July.

Authorities have just released new video of the teens in their boat heading out on the fateful day they disappeared.

The story of the missing boys has drawn attention around the country.

RELATED | Captain spotted teen boys' wrecked boat during walk with wife

Now, never-before seen video has just been released showing quite possibly the last time the boys were seen alive.

The video released by the Florida Fish and Wildlife Conservation Commission shows Austin Stephanos and Perry Cohen, both 14, setting out on July 24.

Surveillance cameras along the Intracoastal Waterway at a bridge and also at a residential complex got video of the boys as they headed east in their 18 foot boat towards the Jupiter Inlet.

RELATED | Norwegian captain hopes boat leads to closure for Austin and Perry's families

From the video, nothing appears to be wrong.

Just two boys on the water doing what they loved. Little did the boys know that once they hit the open water the weather would change quickly.

They vanished.

Two days later, on July 26, a pilot in a small plane searching 25 miles off the coast of southern Georgia, says he saw something floating in the ocean.

"We went down to take a closer look at it and there was a person laying on this, it appeared to be two large pieces of flat Styrofoam kinda tied together with a life jacket. There's not a shadow of a doubt in my mind that it was one of the boys," said Bobby Smith, a pilot from Reidsville, Georgia.

Smith says he circled in his plane about 300 feet above the water and he says the boy was waving his arms for help.

"We really couldn't believe you know that out on the first attempt you know that we had found somebody floatin' on some debris out there. We were just tickled to death that we know we had spotted somebody," Smith said.

He flew to a higher altitude to radio for help. He says the Coast Guard had a plane out there within 20 minutes, but by then that boy on the Styrofoam pieces that he saw waving his arms was gone.

The pilot, who's from Georgia, says he went back out and searched again for Perry and Austin for several more days. But he did not see anything.

We now know the boys boat was found near Bermuda last month by the crew of a Norwegian freighter.
