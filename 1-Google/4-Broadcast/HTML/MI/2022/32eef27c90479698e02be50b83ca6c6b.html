Former Marquette hospital employee accused of hiding camera in bathroom withdraws plea

By TV6 News Team
Published: Dec. 13, 2022 at 1:09 PM EST|Updated: Jan. 4, 2024 at 2:28 PM EST

MARQUETTE, Mich. (WLUC) - UPDATE: The former Upper Peninsula Health System-Marquette employee accused of hiding a camera in a hospital bathroom has withdrawn his plea.

Jarod Havican appeared in Marquette County Circuit Court Thursday morning for a sentencing hearing that was adjourned from Dec. 27 to allow him time to determine if he would proceed with the sentence the court proposed last week, or if he would withdraw his plea and continue toward a trial.

Havican was originally charged with 14 felony counts, which consisted of 7 counts of using a computer to commit a crime and 7 counts of capturing or distributing an image of an unclothed person. However, the other 13 were dismissed in a Sept. 20 plea agreement, in which he plead guilty to the one computer-related felony.

According to court documents, that agreement proposed that his bond be revoked for 5 months, during which he serve time in jail. Prosecutor Nelson said at the Dec. 27 hearing, however, the court proposed a sentence of six months in jail with 18 months probation. This difference in sentencing led Havican to withdraw the guilty plea.

The withdrawal of his plea means Havican’s case will proceed in circuit court toward trial unless another agreement is reached. Typically, when a plea is withdrawn, all charges before the agreement are also reinstated.

Prosecutor Nelson said Havican will next appear for a scheduling conference. The date for that hearing has not yet been set.

Sentencing for the former Upper Peninsula Health System-Marquette employee accused of hiding a camera in a bathroom was adjourned Wednesday morning so he may decide if he wants to withdraw a previous plea.

Jarod Havican appeared in Marquette County Circuit Court Wednesday morning to be sentenced for one felony count of using computers to commit a crime, which carries a maximum prison sentence of four years. Marquette County Prosecutor Jenna Nelson said that hearing was adjourned, however, after the court’s proposed sentence differed from the terms of a September plea agreement.

Havican was arrested Dec. 13, 2022 after the Marquette Police Department was asked to investigate a hidden camera found in a bathroom at the hospital on Dec. 2 last year.

He was originally charged with 14 felony counts, which consisted of 7 counts of using a computer to commit a crime and 7 counts of capturing or distributing and image of an unclothed person. However, the other 13 were dismissed in a Sept. 20 plea agreement, in which he plead guilty to the one computer-related felony.

According to court documents, that agreement proposed that his bond be revoked for 5 months, during which he serve time in jail. After that, the court would review his sentence. Prosecutor Nelson said at the Wednesday morning hearing, however, the court proposed a sentence of six months in jail with 18 months probation.

Should Havican withdraw his plea, Prosecutor Nelson said the case will continue in circuit court. She said Havican would likely be scheduled for pretrial conferences and could potentially move toward a trial if sentencing is not resolved.

Havican’s next court appearance has not yet been scheduled. We will update this story as it develops.

The former employee of Upper Peninsula Health System-Marquette who was arrested for hiding a camera in a hospital bathroom was bound over to Marquette County Circuit Court.

A warrant for the arrest of Jarod Havican, 26, was issued last month after the Marquette Police Department was asked to investigate a hidden camera found in a bathroom at the hospital on Dec. 2.

Havican was arrested on Dec. 13 on a 14-felony warrant, including 7 counts of using a computer to commit a crime and 7 counts of capturing/distributing and image of an unclothed person.

Havican was released from jail on a $1,000 personal recognizance bond. His next court date has not yet been scheduled. We will continue to update this story as more information becomes available.

Last published: Dec. 13, 2022 at 1:09 PM EST

A 26-year-old former employee of Upper Peninsula Health System-Marquette is in jail for allegedly hiding a camera in a hospital bathroom.

On Dec. 2, the Marquette Police Department was contacted to investigate a hidden camera located in a bathroom at the hospital.

After an investigation, MPD identified Jarod Joseph Havican of Marquette, who was an employee of UPHS-Marquette. The Marquette County Prosecutor’s Office authorized a fourteen-count felony warrant for Havican’s arrest and police arrested Havican Tuesday.

The authorized warrant is for seven counts of using computers to commit a crime and seven counts of capturing/distributing and image of an unclothed person.

Havican is lodged at the Marquette County Jail on a $70,000 bond. He was arraigned in Marquette County District Court Tuesday afternoon and is scheduled for a probable cause conference on Dec. 21 at 10:45 a.m.

In a statement to TV6, UPHS-Marquette said:

“At UP Health System – Marquette, our top priority is the safety of our patients, employees, providers, and visitors, and we take any allegation related to an employee very seriously. We are aware of the arrest of an employee that occurred on Dec. 13, 2022, and are cooperating with the local authorities investigating this matter. This individual is no longer employed by UP Health System. Due to confidentiality, we cannot provide any further information related to this situation or any employee matter. Thank you for understanding.”

The victims of this incident have been identified and notified. The investigation is still ongoing.
