Whitmer kidnapping plot suspects 'excited' by FBI explosives video

by MICHAEL KRAFCIK, WWMT

Tue, March 29th 2022 at 9:46 PM
Updated Tue, March 29th 2022 at 11:52 PM

GRAND RAPIDS, Mich. (WWMT) – The four suspects accused of plotting to kidnap Gov. Gretchen Whitmer lit up with excitement watching videos of explosions, according to testimony from an undercover FBI agent at their trial Monday.

The FBI agent who went by the name "Red" testified the four suspects on trial in the kidnaping plot wanted to buy explosives from him to carry out the plan.

A video played for the jury Monday showed an SUV being blown up, a voice in the heard in the recording said, "You'll have to let your boys know in Michigan,"

FBI special agent Timothy Bates testified he showed the suspects the video which was made by a FBI bomb tech. Bates testified suspect Adam Fox was so excited by the video he asked to see it again.

Bates testified he was brought into the plot to stop the suspects from buying real explosives.

Bates said he wore a wire while undercover and secretly recorded conversations played for jurors Monday.

He testified that FBI informants already brought him into the alleged kidnapping plot in September 2020. Bates testified he was brought into the kidnapping plot by other informants to control the timeline so the suspects couldn't get explosives on their own.

  • Suicide mission:Second man who pleaded to Governor kidnapping plot was on a 'suicide mission'

Prosecutors said the suspects wanted to pull off the plot before the November 2020 presidential election. Adam Fox is heard on a secret wire recording, "If Biden becomes president, that's a great change. We're going to be under attack," according to a chat transcript shown in court.

Bates said he joined the suspects on a nighttime surveillance mission to scope out the governor's Elk Rapids cottage, hours after he met the four suspects at a training session on Ty Garbin's property in Luther, Michigan Sept. 12, 2020.

Prosecutors said Barry Croft was recorded talking about using napalm, a military-grade, highly flammable substance, to burn Whitmer's cottage.

Prosecutors also allege Fox can be heard suggesting they blow up two bridges to slow police response time during a kidnapping. Bates testified Fox asked for an IOU on the $4,000 order of explosives he put in.

Fox agreed to pool money from the other suspects to complete the sale, according to prosecutors.

"That will be for the jury to decide. I didn't see any money change hands" Fox's attorney Christopher Gibbons said outside court Monday.

The FBI arrested three of the four suspects on trial during a sting operation in October 2020.

Prosecutors said the men thought they were meeting Bates to buy explosives when the FBI arrested Fox, Brandon Caserta and Daniel Harris.

Defense attorneys have been arguing more than a dozen FBI informants and agents on the case steered the suspects to the plot.

FBI special agent Adam Harris testified that during his questioning of Daniel Harris the night of his Oct. 7 arrest, Harris said undercover informant Dan Chappel served as "A middle ground to keep everyone's head on straight."

Federal prosecutors said they could finish closing arguments by Tuesday. One unresolved question revolves around whether former FBI informant Steve Robeson will take the stand.

Federal prosecutors referred to Steve Robeson as a "double agent," but defense attorneys claimed his fear of prosecution is unfounded and argued he shouldn't avoid testifying.

"He's made it very clear he doesn't want to be here and the government has made it very clear they don't want him to be here. We're going to see what the judge does with that," Barry Croft's attorney Joshua Blanchard said outside court Monday.
