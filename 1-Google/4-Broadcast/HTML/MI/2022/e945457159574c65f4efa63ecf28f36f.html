New Show, New Stories: Looking Back at Good Day Northern Michigan

In 2022 we were able to bring you a brand new morning lifestyle show! With this new show we were able to bring in new guests with new stories to share to everyone in Northern Michigan for the past few months.

Even though we could never rank the guests, we were able to choose a few of these segments to showcase for the end of the year to show the diverse stories that Good Day Northern Michigan can bring you.

Local cultural advocate, activist, and author Sonya Hernandez, Felipe Perez and Zeke Pena Perez joined us on Good Day Northern Michigan to discuss the difference and the most appropriate identification term of Latin vs. Hispanic vs. Lantinx, colorism discrimination within their own community and debunking myths about Cinco De Mayo and Dia de Muertos.

Sid Simone traveled to Saffron Café in the Lakeland Correctional Facility in Coldwater, Michigan to show off their elevated culinary skills around the stove, the oven and the mocktail beverage station.

And we even had Chef Jimmy Hill stop by the studio to show us how to make some of the dishes they sell there.

Winemaker Kasey Wierzba of Shady Lane Cellars took us to the lab and vineyard to select the right grapes for the upcoming harvest.

We also got to talk to her live about a very rare varietal that you can only find here.

For one of our #WomenDo Wednesdays, Sid Simone made her way to the gun range. While there, she talked to the co-owner Erika Crosby about self defense and teaching other woman these skills.

We sat down and talked to Theresa Smith and Kimberly meek about a rare water filter and how it can change how we drink and utilize water since the diminishing supply of clean water is impacting neighborhoods around the country.

Dr. Martha Walsh, Medical director at Blue Cross Blue Shield of Michigan joined Good Day Northern Michigan to discuss the distinctive disparities in maternal healthcare, between ethnic races.

National Council Executive Director Brenda Roberts and instructor Steven Barrier gave us some poignant tips on how to interact and treat family members with dementia.

We also heard from Barrier, who lives with dementia, who gave us some important insight.

The Girl Scouts joined Good Day Northern Michigan to talk about outdoor activity badges and how earning even one of these badges can make a big difference in a girl’s life.

Emily Page stopped by for a chat and talk about her taxidermy business, Unearthed Remains in Big Rapids.

She even gave a few tips about what you should do with your winter hunting wins!

For one of our Say Less Thursdays we tried improv.

David had no idea what to expect on this segment with actresses Lisa Thauvette and Madison Meter, and it made for a fun way to start the day.
