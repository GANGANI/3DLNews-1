‘It’s just something we all signed up to do’: Officer saves man from fiery wreck

By Karli Barnett and Amanda Alvarado
Published: Feb. 8, 2024 at 1:57 PM CST

ATLANTA (WANF/Gray News) - A Georgia officer is credited with saving a man’s life after a fiery wreck when he broke windows to pull the man to safety before the car became engulfed in flames.

Body camera video shows the moment Coweta County Sergeant Steven Mills approaches the vehicle as the flames build.

“Watch your eyes!” he shouts before breaking the glass.

Mills says he was just at the place at the right time.

“If we were 30 seconds further up the road, or a minute further up the road, unfortunately, we would be having a different story,” he said.

Georgia State Patrol says on Feb. 1 around 3 a.m., the driver of a Ford Edge crashed on SR-154. Mills was first on the scene.

“I noticed an arm reaching out from the driver’s side, so I punched out the front window and was able to see the driver there,” he said.

Mills says it appeared the driver missed a curve, sending him off the road and into a tree. Another Coweta County Deputy Benjamin Whitney arrived a short time after to assist.

“He handed me his pocketknife. I was able to cut the lap belt portion of the seatbelt and then cut the shoulder webbing of the seatbelt, and he made it to the backseat of the car,” Mills said.

Because of how the SUV was positioned, Mills could not gain access through the doors.

“I was able to get his hand and pull them out through the back of the vehicle and probably 10 or 20 feet from the vehicle,” he said. “I looked back for a split second and saw the vehicle was just fully engulfed.”

Mills has been with the Coweta County Sheriff’s office for a decade and says he has to be prepared for this any time he puts on his uniform.

“It’s just something we all signed up to do,” he said. “Moments like these do help to shed some light and some happiness in my life, simply because there was a good outcome. You don’t always get to see the good outcome.”

Coweta County Fire Rescue transported the driver to the burn unit at Grady Memorial Hospital in Atlanta.
