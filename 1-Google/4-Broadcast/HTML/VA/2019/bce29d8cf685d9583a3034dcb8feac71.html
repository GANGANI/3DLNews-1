Experts warn about Bradford pear trees

by Kyle Wilcox

Thu, March 28th 2019 at 11:12 PM
Experts warn that Bradford pear trees can be harmful and advise to not plant them. (WSET)

DANVILLE, Va. (WSET) -- The Bradford pear tree is nice to look at, but it may actually be harmful.

Justin Harris has his hands full with the Bradford pear trees on his land in Cascade.

"Until a bulldozer comes in, there's not much I can do about it," Harris said.

He said the trees showed up years ago and now he cannot keep them away.

"If you just mow them, they just come right back," Harris said

The trees are not just showing up all over Harris' land, they are also in the city of Danville.

"Beautiful tree. People love it because it comes out early in the Spring and it's covered up in white flowers. Unfortunately, we found out it has an invasive tendency," said Stuart Sutphin, an Agriculture Extension Agent with the Virginia Cooperative Extension.

Sutphin, who is based in Danville, said the tree can affect the environment.

"It can displace some of the local vegetation, some of the native vegetation and that can eventually have an impact on local environments like wildlife habitat," Sutphin said.

He said the trees are also hazardous.

"The wood is very brittle, so it tends to break up real bad during wind storms. They can grow three, four, five feet a year, so they get up in the power lines very quickly," Sutphin said.

Eric Wiseman, an associate professor of urban and community forestry at Virginia Tech, said the tree is becoming an issue at the local level.

"In certain areas and certain types of landscapes, it has become a problem and people are starting now to have to deal with the consequences," Wiseman said.

However, both Wiseman and Sutphin said it is not a crisis just yet.

"I would not characterize it as being a state-wide problem," Wiseman said.

"The impact hasn't really been felt at this point, but we're keeping an eye on them," Sutphin said.
