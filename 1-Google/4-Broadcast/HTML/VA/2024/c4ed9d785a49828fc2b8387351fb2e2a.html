GRAPHIC: Fries man charged with animal cruelty after dog found beaten to death in Amazon box

By Isabella Ledonne
Published: Jan. 24, 2024 at 5:16 PM EST

FRIES, Va. (WDBJ) - A man from Fries is facing charges after allegedly beating a dog to death.

Alexander Davis, Jr. is charged with felony larceny of an animal and misdemeanor animal cruelty. The WDBJ7 Investigates team obtained the police report that shows a possible reason behind the dog’s death.

A woman told a Carroll County Sheriff’s deputy her dog, Ghost, disappeared December 21, 2023. She found him the next day in an embankment off the side of the road in an Amazon cardboard box, covered in blood and beaten to death.

Law enforcement traced the box back to the woman’s neighbor, Alexander Davis, Jr.

The police report states Davis, Jr. told his neighbors he killed their dog, “because he found his chickens dead a few days prior and believed they took his dead chickens and ‘hauled them off’. He said when he [saw] their dog, he ‘killed him and hauled him off too’.”

A necropsy report from Virginia Tech Veterinary Teaching Hospital reveals Ghost died from blunt sharp force trauma to the head from a pick axe/hammer or two different weapons. The report states “the dog was alive, and most likely conscious, for all the blows to the head.”

According to the police report, when Davis, Jr. was arrested, he told the deputy he did beat the dog to death, and “he wish it wouldn’t have came down to it. He stated the reason he beat the dog to death was because he believed the dog was getting in his chickens and he placed foot traps around his chicken coops.”

Court records show Davis, Jr. is out on bail.

WDBJ7 has reached out to the Carroll County Commonwealth’s Attorney to clarify why the animal cruelty charge is a misdemeanor and the animal larceny charge is a felony. We have not heard back yet.
