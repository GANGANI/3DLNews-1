Woman brings 7-year-old child with her as she opens fire inside Houston megachurch

by JACKSON SINNENBERG | The National Desk

Sun, February 11th 2024 at 4:56 PM
Updated Mon, February 12th 2024 at 3:01 PM

HOUSTON, Texas (TND) — A seven-year-old child and a 56-year-old were injured in a firefight between a lone gun-woman and two, off-duty law enforcement officers at Joel Osteen's Lakewood Church in Houston Sunday afternoon.

The woman reportedly walked into the megachurch, with the child, just before 2 p.m. local time, as parishioners and staff were preparing for the next service at the prosperity gospel televangelists house of worship, the largest in Texas. According to law enforcement, she brandished a long rifle and began open firing but was met with quick response by an off-duty agent for the Texas Alcoholic Beverage Commission and an off-duty Houston Police Department officer who were working extra employment at the church.

During the firefight, the seven-year-old -- police were unable as of Sunday to provide any known links between the woman and the child -- was struck as well as 56-year-old man, who was shot in the leg. Both were transported to local hospitals were the man is stable and the child remains in critical condition. The woman, the shooter, was killed by the off-duty law enforcement agents.

She also threatened to have a bomb but searches by the Houston Police Department's bomb squad and the Houston Fire Department Decontamination (or Decon) unit of the backpack she had on her and the vehicle she arrived in, as well as a sweep of the area, did not find "anything of concern," according to Fire Chief Samuel Peña.

Police Chief Troy Finner did note that the shooter was observed spraying some unknown substance on the ground ahead of opening fire. Houston Fire's hazmat unit is still investigating that detail in the tragedy.
