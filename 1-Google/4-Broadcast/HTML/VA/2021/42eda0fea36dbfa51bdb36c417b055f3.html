Delegate Terry Kilgore plans to be majority leader of Virginia House of Delegates

by Olivia Bailey

Tue, November 9th 2021 at 9:13 AM
Updated Tue, November 9th 2021 at 9:46 AM

Delegate Terry Kilgore's office is confirming that a deal for the Speaker of the House has been struck internally within Virginia's Republican House Caucus. (WCYB Archive)

RICHMOND, Va. (WCYB) — Delegate Terry Kilgore's office is confirming that a deal for the Speaker of the House has been struck internally within Virginia's Republican House Caucus.

According to his staff, we are told Kilgore (R - 1st District) now plans to assume the role of majority leader of the Virginia House of Delegates.

We are told the deal is a mutual agreement between Kilgore and current minority leader Todd Gilbert (15th District) as the party plans to reorganize as they take on the role as the majority if election results hold.

After the November election, Republicans claimed the majority in the House of Delegates after winning seven additional seats. Two of those seats are expected to undergo recounts in the eastern part of the state.

News 5 has learned Kilgore supports Gilbert for the Speaker of the House, as his office says he is receiving Gilbert's backing for majority leader. The positions are still up for votes within the caucus ahead of session.

The Virginia General Assembly will reconvene in January.


Here is the caucus letter:

Dear Members & Members-elect,

We would like to begin this email by once again congratulating all of you on hard-fought elections. Because of your efforts, not only did Republicans sweep all three state-wide elections, we were able to regain the House Majority. You made that happen.

We are writing to you today to let you know that we have come to an understanding with one another about our intentions with respect to seeking leadership roles. Todd is proud to endorse Terry for Majority Leader, and Terry is proud to endorse Todd for Speaker. Ultimately, any final decision will be left up to you.

Tuesday night was a historic success, and it was due in large part to teamwork. The two of us, along with our entire campaign team, worked very well together and were honored to lead this caucus' efforts to regain our majority. Barring the announcement of others for these two posts, we believe this is the best path forward for a unified caucus to not only advance our shared conservative agenda, but also defend our majority — regardless of whether that happens in 2022 or 2023.

We look forward to meeting with you all on Sunday, Nov. 14th, to congratulate you all in person and nominate our leadership team as we begin planning for the upcoming session.

Respectfully,

Todd and Terry
