Gov. Youngkin defends keeping reports to tip line private

by Mark Spain

Sat, February 5th 2022 at 1:11 PM
Updated Sat, February 5th 2022 at 5:34 PM
Virginia Gov. Glenn Youngkin announced his COVID Action Plan during his first official visit outside of Richmond on Thursday, Jan. 20, 2022. Youngkin visited the medical team at Carilion Health in Roanoke, Virginia. (Credit: WSET)

RICHMOND, Va. (WSET) — Gov. Glenn Youngkin doubled down on his decision to keep correspondence to his education tip line from the public.

The governor set up the tip line for Virginians to report "inherently divisive concepts, including Critical Race Theory."

RELATED: Gov. Youngkin's tip line receives pushback from Central Virginia teachers

However, a number of people and news outlets, including ABC13, have asked for the governor to release the tips coming into his office through Freedom of Information Act requests.

Gov. Youngkin addressed his decision to keep the information private.

SEE ALSO: Universal masking will remain at 7 Va. school districts, Youngkin to appeal judge's ruling

"First of all, all constituent services are confidential. It's a communication between Virginians and the governor. And therefore, it would be, not only inappropriate, but it would in fact be wrong for me to disclose the things that we're hearing in the tip line," Youngkin said.

The Virginia Education Association, Virginia Parent Teacher Association and other education groups have called on the governor to ditch the tip line.
