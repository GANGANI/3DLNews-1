Virginia Democrats flip House and retain Senate control in election sweep

by Robert Locklear

Wed, November 8th 2023 at 7:16 AM
3
VIEW ALL PHOTOS
FILE - House of Delegates members eat boxed lunches on the steps of the Virginia Capitol, April 22, 2020, in Richmond, Va. Virginia lawmakers will reconvene in Richmond on Wednesday, Sept. 6, 2023, to consider a compromise General Assembly negotiators recently reached on the long-delayed state budget. (Bob Brown/Richmond Times-Dispatch via AP, Pool, File)

(WSET) — Democrats seized victory in Virginia on Election Day as voters flipped the House of Delegates out of Republican control and enabled Democrats to hold the number advantage in the State Senate. All 140 legislative seats in the state were up for grabs in the battleground election.

Virginia residents were not alone in watching the results of the election; as one of only four states in the country holding legislative races, eyes across the United States turned to Virginia as abortion rights took the throne as a key running point for Democrats.

RELATED: These are your Virginia House of Delegates winners

The win for Democrats reflected a major loss for Governor Youngkin and the GOP, which had exerted major resources in campaigning against their opposition. The significance of Republicans' loss was not lost on Democrats as results confirmed their own sweeping victory.

"@GovernorVA said his #1 goal was to flip the Senate. Not tonight, Glenn. Not tonight," Virginia Senate Democratic Caucus Chair Mamie Locke wrote in a tweet Tuesday night. In a statement, she again targeted Youngkin and Republicans in a sharp rebuke of their agendas.

“It’s official: there will be absolutely no abortion ban legislation sent to Glenn Youngkin’s desk for the duration of his term in office, period, as we have thwarted MAGA Republicans’ attempt to take total control of our government and our bodies,” Locke said.

RELATED: These are the Virginia State Senate winners

Hours before polls closed, sealing victory for the Democrats, Youngkin predicted that it would be Republicans who would hold the numbers advantage in both the House and the Senate. According to the Associated Press, he was asked how he would work with Democrats if his party failed to meet Youngkin's hopes.

“I think there’s always a place for common sense, and we’ve been able to get things done” during the past two years of divided government," Youngkin said, according to the AP.

As Democrats with control of the House and the Senate look to the future, they will have to find common ground with Youngkin if they hope to accomplish their goals.

Beyond the state, others across the country see Virginia's results as a promise for what could happen in the 2024 elections.

“This is a huge sign of Democrats’ continued momentum heading into 2024. With so much on the line, voters showed up at the ballot box and sent the GOP a stark warning — betting big on the MAGA agenda doesn’t fly with everyday Americans, and it will cost them once again in 2024," said Jaime Harrison, Democratic National Committee Chair, in a statement regarding Virginia's election results.
