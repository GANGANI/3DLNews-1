CNN's Jim Acosta to give 'Real News' lecture at JMU

By WHSV newsroom
Published: Apr. 5, 2018 at 3:07 PM EDT

The senior White House correspondent for CNN, Jim Acosta, will be at James Madison University in Harrisonburg to deliver a lecture on Thursday, April 5.

Acosta, who graduated cum laude from JMU with a bachelor's degree in mass communications, is returning to his alma mater as part of his "Real News" lecture series, in conjunction with the university's Madison Vision Series.

He has been working with CNN since March 2007 after years as a CBS News correspondent, covering the Iraq war from Baghdad, Hurricane Katrina, and the blackout of 2003, among other stories.

Prior to that, he worked as a reporter in Chicago, Dallas, and Knoxville.

Acosta is based in CNN's Washington, D.C. bureau and regularly covers presidential press conferences, visits by heads of states, and any issues affecting the Executive Branch of the U.S. government. He spent much of 2016 following Donald Trump's presidential campaign trail.

During the 2012 presidential campaign, he was CNN's national political correspondent and was embedded with the Romney campaign, traveling to battleground states, as well as the U.K., Israel, and Poland.

He also covered both of former President Barack Obama's inaugurations.

He reported from Havana, Cuba shortly after the Obama administration lifted travel restrictions to the nation, covered the 2008 presidential campaigns, and was there to report on the Virginia Tech shooting.

The lecture on April 5 is scheduled from 3:30 p.m. - 5 p.m. in the Forbes Concert Hall and is free and open to the public.
