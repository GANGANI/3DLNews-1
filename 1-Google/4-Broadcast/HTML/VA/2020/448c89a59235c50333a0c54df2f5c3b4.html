Fallen Virginia police officer remembered as 'full of life' and 'always smiling'

by Brandon Bailey

Fri, January 24th 2020 at 9:58 PM
Updated Fri, January 24th 2020 at 4:44 PM
3
VIEW ALL PHOTOS
Courtesy: Newport News Police Department / CNN Newsource

NEWPORT NEWS, Va. (WCYB) -- A Virginia police officer has passed away after reportedly being dragged by a vehicle during a traffic stop in Newport News.

According to the Newport News Police Department, Officer Katherine "Katie" Thyne stopped a vehicle on 16th Street around 6:40 Thursday evening when the driver accelerated, dragging her. Newport News Police Chief Steve Drew said Thyne was dragged for about a block before the vehicle crashed into a tree, pinning her.

Thyne was taken to a local hospital where she was later pronounced dead.

In a news conference on Friday, Chief Drew held back tears as he addressed the community.

“Officer Thyne is our family. That’s how she will be treated and remembered.”

The 24-year-old was a Navy veteran who joined the department in 2018. She was assigned to the South Precinct Watch II and was active in the Navy Reserves. She leaves behind her two-year-old daughter, mother, stepfather, brother, and partner.

The department described Thyne on Facebook as "an amazing mother, officer and friend." Drew said she was known for always smiling and being full of life.

“As we go forward, the days will be challenging. I encourage the department to lean on each other. Members of the community, it’s okay to cry." Drew said. "We will get through this together as a family.”

The driver suspected of dragging Thyne has been arrested. The Associated Press reports Vernon Green II has been charged with felony homicide, eluding police, and possessing narcotics.
