3 men in critical condition, 1 dead after shooting in Russell neighborhood

By Julia Huffman
Published: Jan. 31, 2024 at 1:32 PM EST|Updated: Jan. 31, 2024 at 1:42 PM EST

LOUISVILLE, Ky. (WAVE) - A shooting in broad daylight has left three people hospitalized and one person dead.

The shooting was reported around 12:30 p.m. at South 12th St & W Muhammad Ali Blvd. That’s not far from Central High School and Coleridge-Taylor Montessori Elementary School.

When officers arrived, they found four men shot. One of the men died at the scene. The other three were taken to the University of Louisville Hospital, all in critical condition.

Col. Steve Healy with LMPD said the men appear to be young adults. He said that they were all inside a car when they were shot.

“This is just so frustrating,” he said. “In broad daylight. You have individuals out here working, you have two schools in close proximity, and you have individuals that feel comfortable enough to try and take someone’s life.”

LMPD said they do not believe there’s a danger to the public and that nearby schools are operating under normal conditions. However, they encourage anyone who saw something or may have information to call the police department’s anonymous tip line at 502-574-LMPD (5673) or use the online LMPD Crime Tip Portal.

“We strongly believe that somebody saw something,” LMPD spokesman Dwight Mitchell said.

Police are canvassing the area, collecting evidence, and looking for witnesses. At this time, there is no information available about a possible suspect.
