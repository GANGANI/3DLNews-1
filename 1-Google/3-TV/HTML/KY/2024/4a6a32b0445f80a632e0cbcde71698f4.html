Dog set to be euthanized finds forever home thanks to pilot, stranger hundreds of miles away

By Greg Brobeck
Published: Feb. 1, 2024 at 6:28 PM EST

NORMAN, Okla. (Gray News) - A dog in Oklahoma that seemed destined to be euthanized is getting a second chance at life thanks to the generosity of a pilot and a woman hundreds of miles away.

Odin’s story began in September when he was brought to the City of Norman Animal Welfare shelter with a badly swollen eye. Shelter officials say the eye was removed and he seemed to be doing fine without it.

Odin, however, did not have any success at being adopted because officials said he had problems getting along with other dogs.

A social media post from the shelter said he doesn’t seem to understand “canine social cues” and would play too roughly with other dogs, not realizing when the other dogs were overwhelmed.

The shelter posted about Odin in January as part of an effort to highlight long-stay dogs that hadn’t received much attention by potential adopters or were showing stress behaviors in their kennels.

On Tuesday, the shelter again posted about Odin, saying no one had come to see him about potential adoption and that if he wasn’t adopted by Friday, he would be euthanized.

People far and wide shared and responded to the post, with offers ranging from free behavioral therapy for Odin and whoever agreed to adopt or foster him, to a private pilot volunteering to fly Odin to an out-of-state adopter if need be.

The latter offer was put to use when Diane Baker, who lives in Atlanta, Georgia, saw the post and said she “had to have” Odin. Parker and Danielle Ferguson agreed to fly Odin to Georgia on Thursday to start his new life.
