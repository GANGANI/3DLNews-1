Search continues for gunman involved in shooting outside McEachern High School

By Atlanta News First staff
Published: Feb. 1, 2024 at 2:33 PM EST|Updated: Feb. 2, 2024 at 7:46 AM EST

ATLANTA, Ga. (Atlanta News First) - Police are still trying to find the gunman involved in a shooting in McEachern High School’s parking lot Thursday afternoon. Meanwhile, a Cobb County police officer at the entrance of student parking told Atlanta News First that there will be classes on Friday.

The “young” victims of Thursday’s shooting were not students at the school, Cobb County Police Chief Stuart VanHoozer said during a news conference. They were taken to Wellstar Kennestone Hospital with what appeared to be non-life-threatening injuries, he said.

The chief said the call of the shooting came in at 1:47 p.m. Multiple law enforcement agencies and EMS soon responded to assist the school district. He said there “was never an active shooter.”

Police are still looking for suspects.

“We’ve got some young people hurt, and potentially some young people involved on the periphery of this event,” VanHoozer said. “We don’t know that we have multiple shooters, we just know that there were multiple people on site when this shooting occurred.”

A video circulating online appears to show a group of young people gathered in the parking lot before scrambling away when gunshots ring out.

Atlanta News First is working to confirm its authenticity, but a McEachern High School student told us that she recognized people in the video and that it appeared to be taken in the school parking lot.

VanHoozer said he’s seen the video and assumes it came from the shooting. A student who saw the video told Atlanta News First she recognized some of the boys shown in it after seeing them on campus earlier Thursday.

>> Watch the full news conference below:

VanHoozer said during the afternoon news conference that all students were safe, but the parking lot was still an active crime scene and the public should avoid the area.

“When we get these calls, we don’t know if it’s an active shooter or not, so we treat it as though it is an active shooter,” he said, noting they train for these situations every year.

A pickup location for parents of students was set up at The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints, at 2595 New MacLand Road.

The campus had been “in code red” as police responded with a shelter-in-place order,.

The school was deemed secure around 4:30 p.m. and loved ones were able to reunite with students.

“I mean, it’s complete shock when your child reaches out and says ‘Code red. This is not a drill’ in all capital letters,” said Melissa Moriarty, who was waiting to pick up her daughter.

“It’s my brother, so of course I had to rush here as soon as I got the news” said Eazy Havin, who left work to pick up his sibling. He was also an alumni of McEachern. “It’s sad. It’s scary,” he added.

Atlanta News First previously reported that Cobb County Schools police responded to McEachern in December after an AirDropped photo of a student with a weapon was circulated at the school.

McEachern High School, about 30 miles northwest of Atlanta, has around 2,300 students.

>> Initial school district statement, sent around 2:40 p.m.:

“This afternoon, and only moments ago, we can verify two individuals who are not current McEachern students have been shot in a parking lot on our campus. Both individuals appear to have suffered non-life-threatening injuries. Cobb Schools Police and Cobb County Police have secured the campus and are actively investigating the identities of both individuals and the associated circumstances. The campus is in code red, police are on campus, and for the safety of both students and staff, parents will not be allowed on campus, and buses will not run until the code red is lifted. We will provide an additional update to the public once one is available.”

This is a developing story. Check back with Atlanta News First for updates.
