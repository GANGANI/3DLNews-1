Cairo temporary homeless shelter shut down; nonprofit leaders looking for solutions

By Lenah Allen
Published: Jan. 31, 2024 at 6:00 PM EST

CAIRO, Ga. (WALB) - Many homeless people in Cairo have been left in the frigid cold after a temporary homeless shelter was shut down this week. The shelter was the only one in the area and had only been open for two weeks.

Just two weeks ago, Grady County Coroner Rusty Powe said a homeless man died after a tree fell on him while he was resting in his normal spot outside -- just a mile outside of Davis Park. Mary Hardwick who is homeless in Cairo said living outside is a scary reality for a lot of people in the city.

“It’s really discouraging. It’s overwhelming. Sometimes you just want to give up,” she said.

She said she’s been homeless for two years-- living under a bridge with her dog Nyla all while battling brain cancer. She added that in Cairo resources for the homeless community are limited.

“We need people to stand up and advocate for the homeless community,” she said.

Some of those advocates are Timothy and Shunterria Henry who have been trying to help the homeless through their nonprofit T and S Foundation. But this week their temporary homeless shelter at a church was shut down after only being open for two weeks. The landlord said no because of insurance concerns.

“The four to six people that we had in our shelter that started out, we had to put them back on the street in the cold,” Timothy said.

The closest homeless shelter to Cairo is 15 miles away in Thomasville.

“You gotta go to Thomasville to get a shower, stay for a while. But see in Cairo we don’t have none of that,” Reuben Booth, another homeless person, said.

Booth has been homeless since September.

“It is sad and you really get emotional,” he said.

He’s been able to get help from the T and S Foundation and said if it weren’t for that support he would still be sleeping on park benches around Cairo. The Henrys said right now they’re working to raise money for a permanent homeless shelter in Cairo.

“Now we’re trying to figure out a situation on how we’ll get our homeless people transported to Thomasville because I know we have upwards of 50 to 100 homeless people that we know about,” Timothy said. “We got shelters for dogs and cats but we don’t have shelters for a human.”

Shunterria said her nonprofit is trying to help homeless people become independent citizens in the community.

“We’re trying to be the ones that push them in the right direction and get them the resources and help that they need,” she said.

For now, T and S Foundation is helping the homeless on a need-by-need basis.
