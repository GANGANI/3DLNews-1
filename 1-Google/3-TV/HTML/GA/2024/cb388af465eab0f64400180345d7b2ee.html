20 in custody, 2 still wanted in human trafficking investigation, South Fulton police say

By Jennifer Lifsey
Published: Feb. 1, 2024 at 9:20 AM EST|Updated: Feb. 1, 2024 at 11:26 PM EST

SOUTH FULTON, Ga. (Atlanta News First) - Numerous people have been taken into police custody in connection to a human trafficking investigation in South Fulton.

Around 8 a.m. Thursday morning, City of South Fulton police said they responded to a 911 call about shots fired at a home on the 5400 block of Orly Terrace in College Park.

When they arrived, officers witnessed the gunshots taking place behind the residence.

“At that point, they were able to establish a perimeter around the residence, and an initial review of the location revealed that we had received quite a few complaints about illicit activity occurring in and around this particular residence,” said City of South Fulton Police Chief Keith Meadows during a news conference Thursday morning. “We’ve had complaints about possibly sex trafficking and so, we have not ruled that out as a possibility for this particular location being involved in that type of activity.”

Neighbors say the rental property has become a notorious party home.

“Everyone has called the police; they have called 911 numerous times of the night. Numerous things have been going on,” said Ryan Gibson.

“You could tell the way the women were dressed something was going on,” said Kathy Shehee.

Chief Meadows added that among some of the complaints they received were illicit parties.

Authorities have 20 people detained, most of them women, and at least two people who are currently wanted.

Police said the individuals in custody range anywhere from “16 to 39 or possibly 40″ in age.

“Many residents have extended their gratitude to the police department, apparently this has been a problem residence for them for quite some time. We are going to do a thorough and methodical investigation in relation to this activity associated with this particular property,” Chief Meadows said.

Gibson praised law enforcement efforts, “I’m just extremely happy to be honest with you, this is a quiet neighborhood very close knit. This definitely had this up in arms shooting, i have young kids, thinking about my kids thinking about them being trafficked or touch gives us a bad feeling.”

A special agent with the Georgia Bureau of Investigation’s Human Exploitation and Trafficking Unit says investigations and arrests in human trafficking cases take a lot of time due to the amount of background work and paperwork needed. From July 1, 2023 to January 26, 2024, the GBI has had 82 human trafficking cases, making 37 arrests, with six indictments. Those numbers are from Agent Anita Ivy.

“Normally it works through undercover investigations. It seems like with this one they seemed to stumble into it,” said Attornery Suri Jimenez.

Senator Jon Ossoff just introduced a bill aimed at making these investigations and arrests less complicated. It’s called the Preventing Child Trafficking Act of 2024.

“We want to empower federal law enforcement and law enforcement at every level to investigate and arrest and prosecute traffickers before they can harm children,” said Senator Ossoff.

Atlanta News First reached out to the property owner and did not hear immediately back.

The number to contact the Georgia Coalition to Combat Human Trafficking is: 1-866-363-4842.
