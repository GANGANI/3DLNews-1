Family remembers man killed in crash on I-85 north in Gwinnett County

By Jennifer Lifsey
Published: Mar. 21, 2022 at 3:22 PM EDT|Updated: Mar. 21, 2022 at 7:32 PM EDT

SUWANEE, Ga. (CBS46) - Suwanee Police responded to a fatal crash Monday afternoon on I-85 northbound at exit 111/Lawrenceville Suwanee Road involving a tractor-trailer and a van.

Suwanee Police Captain Robert Thompson tells us the person killed has been identified as Jorge Zuno, of Buford, Georgia.

Friends and family gathered days after Zuno’s death for a balloon release and memorial service.

Zuno’s fiancé, Alexis Ott, said she and her two children will remember him as a family man with a big heart.

“He always wrapped everyone in love, and only wanted to be loved,” said Ott. “A complete family man. I think all he wanted was to be a dad, and he got that opportunity.”

Zuno was a 32-year-old handyman living in Buford. Loved ones affectionately nicknamed him “Koki,” a playoff on a type of Puerto Rican frog.

Ott said her 8-year-old son and 6-year-old daughter are coming to terms with heartbreak.

“We’re trying to do everything we can to make sure they’re as happy as they can be,” she said. “My daughter’s birthday was yesterday, so we had a big celebration for her. It was beautiful.”

Friends created a GoFundMe to help Alexis Ott and her children.

Daniel Scribbins said he had seen Zuno and Ott’s relationship grow strong over the years.

“They have years of plans in the making, a home they were getting to buy they were getting ready to settle down for the rest of their lives,” said Scribbins.

The crash remains under investigation.
