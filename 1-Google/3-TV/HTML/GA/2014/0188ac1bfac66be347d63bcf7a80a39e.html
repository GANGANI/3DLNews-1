UPDATE | Paquette sentenced to 20 years in prison on assault and child cruelty charges

By Staff
Published: Oct. 23, 2014 at 11:49 AM EDT

Wednesday, Oct. 26, 2016 |

UPDATE:

12:35 p.m.

APPLING, Ga. (WRDW/WAGT) -- A man has been sentenced to 20 years in prison after being found guilty of abusing a woman mentally and physically for a decade.

In court Wednesday, Ryan Paquette pleaded guilty to all charges, which included child cruelty, aggravated assault, and terroristic threats.

According to the District Attorney's office, Paquette will also serve 25 years of probation following his jail term.

Wednesday, Oct. 26, 2016

APPLING, Ga. (WRDW/WAGT) -- A man who allegedly abused a woman mentally and physically for a decade will be sentenced Wednesday at 11 a.m.

Ryan Paquette faces child cruelty, aggravated assault, and terroristic threat charges.

According to an incident report, a woman told deputies that Paquette began yelling obscenities of a sexual nature at her because his sandwich was wrong. He then allegedly dragged her by her hair in front of her children.

At his arraignment in June, Paquette entered a not guilty plea on the felony charges.

Friday, June 3, 2016

APPLING, Ga. (WRDW/WAGT) -- Ryan Paquette entered a not guilty plea on felony charges, including cruelty to children.

The District Attorney said at his arraignment, Paquette pleaded not guilty to aggravated assault, cruelty to children, terroristic threats, and interference with a 911 call.

Paquette was charged back in April after a woman called 911 regarding a domestic violence situation. According to the incident report, the woman said on April 8th, Ryan became very upset when she brought him a sub sandwich that was on white bread instead of wheat. She tells deputies Paquette began yelling obscenities of a sexual nature because his sandwich was wrong.

Monday, May 16, 2016

APPLING, Ga. (WRDW/WAGT) -- A Columbia County man has been indicted on multiple felony charges, including cruelty to children and making terror threats.

Ryan Paquette was indicted for aggravated assault, cruelty to children, terroristic threats, and interference with a 911 call.

Monday, April 11, 2016

APPLING, Ga. (WRDW/WAGT) -- A man accused in a violent case of domestic abuse is behind bars.

Ryan Paquette was arrested for battery and cruelty to children in the 3rd degree Sunday, April 10th.

According to the Columbia County Sheriff's Office, they received a call through dispatch from a woman requesting information about Safe Homes of Augusta. The woman said she knew a friend who needed help.

When deputies took a second call from the woman, they say she confessed that she was the one in need of help. She said Ryan Paquette had been abusing her mentally and physically for the past 10 years.

The woman agreed to meet with deputies to file a police report which details a violent fight between the two.

According to the incident report, the woman said on April 8th, Ryan became very upset when she brought him a sub sandwich that was on white bread instead of wheat. She tells deputies Paquette began yelling obscenities of a sexual nature because his sandwich was wrong.

The report says the fight continued inside a house in a room where three young children were present. The woman claims Paquette grabbed her by the hair and dragged her to a different room. According to the report, one of the children was bruised when the woman fell on them during this process.

The report says the violence went on with Paquette slamming the woman against a refrigerator, choking her.

In the report, the woman says Paquette took away all the cell phones so no one could call for help.

According to the report, the woman told deputies the next attack happened while she was folding clothes. She says Paquette charged at her, head-butted her, and began to punch her multiple times in the back, stomach, arms, and shoulders. The report says the woman revealed she is pregnant and was concerned the alleged beating had hurt her unborn child.

The report says the woman told Paquette, "You're going to kill me!" She says he responded by saying, "I'm trying."

Deputies talked to the children, who told them what they heard and saw.

Ryan was contacted and told that he needed to turn himself in for battery and cruelty to children. Deputies arrested him Sunday.

His current bond is at $4,800.00

Thursday, Jan. 28, 2016

AUGUSTA, Ga. (WRDW) -- Ryan Paquette pleads guilty to simple assault but not terroristic threats against a deputy in court.

Paquette is accused of threatening Richmond County narcotics investigator Joel Danko in 2014. In court, Danko said Paquette has little to no respect for authority, and he is a sick and dangerous man.

A judge sentenced Paquette to 2 years of unsupervised probation, and he will get credit for the time he previously served in jail. Under probation, Paquette cannot have any contact with Danko or his wife, and he will not be allowed to possess a gun under any circumstances.

Wednesday, Oct. 7, 2015

AUGUSTA, Ga. (WRDW) -- A judge grants a modification to the terms of Ryan Paquette's bond. Paquette is accused of threatening a Richmond County narcotics investigator in 2014.

The judge is allowing Paquette to remove his ankle monitor. All other terms, including not having contact with any witnesses or individuals involved in his criminal case, are still in effect.

Paquette was granted bond back in Dec. 2014.

Thursday, Dec. 4, 2014

AUGUSTA, Ga. (WRDW) -- Ryan Paquette, the man who allegedly made threats against a Richmond County narcotics investigator, is out of jail.

On Wednesday Judge Annis granted bond to Paquette. Paquette posted the $20,000 and was released.

Paquette has been indicted on the terroristic threats charges.

Tuesday, Nov. 4, 2014

AUGUSTA, Ga. (WRDW) -- The man who allegedly made threats against a Richmond County narcotics investigator was denied bond, District Attorney Ashley Wright said.

Ryan Paquette is charged with two counts of terroristic threats and acts for allegedly threatening Investigator Joel Danko and his wife, the Sheirff's Office said.

Paquette's bond was denied because Judge Danny Craig felt was a threat to the community and to witnesses, Wright said.

The Richmond County Sheriff's Office said Paquette was threatening Danko because he arrested his brother, Brandon Paquette, during an investigation into the sale of illegal steroids.

An investigation into illegal steroids is ongoing.

Thursday, Oct. 23, 2014

COLUMBIA COUNTY (WRDW) -- Columbia County deputies say a man is in the jail accused of making threats towards a Richmond County narcotics investigator.

Investigators are charging Cameron "Ryan" Paquette with two counts of terroristic threats and acts for allegedly threatening Investigator Joel Danko and his wife.

A witness told Sergeant Chris Langford, with the Richmond County Sheriff's Office, Paquette called him and said he was going to sexually assault Danko's wife. The witness said Paquette also claimed he was going to assault Danko.

Investigators say Paquette made the claims during two separate phone calls. The Sheriff's Office says they were able to record the threats during the second phone call.

The calls were made on Oct. 22, the Sheriff's Office said.

Lt. Allan Rollins with the Richmond County Sheriff's Office said Paquette was threatening Danko, because Danko arrested his brother, Brandon Paquette, during an investigation into the sale of illegal steroids.
