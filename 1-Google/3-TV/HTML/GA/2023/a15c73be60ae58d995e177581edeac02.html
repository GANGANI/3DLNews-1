Top 10 most-clicked News 12 26 stories in 2023

By Staff and Taylor Martin
Published: Dec. 28, 2023 at 5:26 PM EST|Updated: Dec. 29, 2023 at 11:17 PM EST

AUGUSTA, Ga. (WRDW/WAGT) - As 2023 comes to a close, here’s a look back at the 10 local news stories most read by News 12 26 viewers in 2023.

The following stories are based on digital traffic on WRDW.com. Below the 10th story, we have three additional stories that didn’t make the top 10, but we believe highly impacted the community in 2023.

1. As dogs attack, 11-year-old cousin rushes to make unforgettable 911 call

We heard from an 11-year-old boy who made the 911 call during a dog attack on his cousin Justin Gilstrap in Columbia County.

When Mason Aguilar was playing soccer with Justin, he had no idea he would make a call that saved Justin’s life.

911 call:

“Columbia County 911, what’s your emergency?”

“Please, please hurry. My cousin’s getting attacked by a dog.”

2. Local bride dies just before getting to say ‘I do’

A race to their wedding day became a race against time for a local couple.

Calvin Riddick and his partner China saw overwhelming support from people when word got out they were hoping to get married while China was battling stage four cancer.

“We hit it off like, boom, you know,” Riddick said. “But like I say, we never was apart from that day on.”

When he and China met almost five years earlier, it was a done deal for him – love at first sight.

3. Augusta Exchange loses another big store as chain shuts down

Christmas Tree Shops liquidated all of its stores and went out of business unless.

The closure posed another problem for the busy Robert C. Daniel Jr. Parkway area that includes the Augusta Exchange shopping center.

4. Local motorcycle group speaks out against recent violence

After a double-slaying involving a biker group, the FBI and law enforcement agencies up and down the East Coast were on high alert with bike week in Myrtle Beach and another in Atlantic Beach in South Carolina.

The Richmond County Sheriff’s Office was also on high alert because the threat of retaliation was real.

“We got information that the groups may be mobilizing to come to Augusta, and we started coordinating with other law enforcement agencies,” said Sheriff Richard Roundtree.

5. I-TEAM: Closer look at who’s policing the police in Richmond County

An all-new I-TEAM investigation took a closer look at who’s keeping watch over those who protect and serve.

It all started when the I-TEAM obtained a cellphone video of an incident off Glenn Hills Drive in Augusta. We also had the incident report from the Richmond County Sheriff’s Office.

On the cellphone recording, you can hear Richmond County deputies imply they did not have a warrant to enter the home they wanted to search.

6. Mom’s nightmare: ‘They left me in school by myself. It’s dark.’

One Aiken County parent feared the worst when her middle school son didn’t make it home after the first day of classes.

What was supposed to be the start of a new school year at a new school soon became a nightmare for Claytavia Brown’s 13-year-old son.

“My heart stopped, honestly,” said Brown. Panic set in.

7. Kroger breaks ground on new supermarket in Augusta

Kroger broke ground on Eisenhower Crossing, the new Kroger Marketplace expected to open in the spring of 2024.

The new store on Jimmie Dyess Parkway near Gordon Highway represents a $32 million investment and will create 250-300 new jobs.

8. 2 killed, 3 injured in shooting at Sand Bar Ferry, East Boundary

The Richmond County Sheriff’s Office investigated a shooting that left two people dead and three others injured a few blocks from downtown Augusta.

Richmond County Coroner Mark Bowen identified those who died as Keith Coates, 30, of Valdosta, and Germayne Farrell, 51, of Daytona Beach, Fla.

9. Armed suspect kills himself after Aiken County chase on I-20

The Aiken County Coroner’s Office identified a man involved in a police chase who killed himself on Interstate 20 in Aiken County.

According to the Aiken County Sheriff’s Office, deputies pursued a vehicle in a police chase, where the suspect fired shots while driving west on I-20.

The suspect was also throwing drugs out the window, according to deputies.

10. New 1,742-acre golf and spa destination coming to North Augusta

A website was launched announcing a new golf and spa destination that’s coming to North Augusta.

Cypress Shoals will be between West Martintown Road, Interstate 20 and the Savannah River.

The property will include 1,742 acres of land. It’s more than five and a half times the size of Augusta National and the development promises a lot more than golf.
