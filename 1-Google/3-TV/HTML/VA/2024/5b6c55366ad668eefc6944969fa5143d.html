Power lines fall across 288 after crash in Chesterfield

The crash shutdown the highway near Midlothian Turnpike
By 12 On Your Side Digital Team and Desiree Montilla
Published: Feb. 2, 2024 at 12:46 PM EST|Updated: Feb. 2, 2024 at 5:17 PM EST

CHESTERFIELD, Va. (WWBT) - Power lines fell across Route 288 late Friday morning following a crash near Midlothian Turnpike.

Virginia State Police said the crash between a tractor-trailer and SUV happened around 11:30 a.m. and shut down the southbound lanes into the afternoon.

Troopers said a utility pole was hit during the crash, and the active power lines sparked a fire in the grass.

Giovanni Henriques saw the situation unfold from his apartment complex at Westchester Commons.

“The power was out, and I saw the highway starting to slow down,” he told 12 On Your Side. “I went to my balcony, and I couldn’t see around the side of the building, but adjacent to my apartment is the observation deck, so I went there, minute or so after it happened, and you could see everything was on fire, the pole was down.”

Chesterfield County Fire & EMS said the fire burned about 2 acres.

“It was going pretty good. It was a grass fire, and the wind was pushing it towards the parking lot, but you know, we’re in the parking lot. There wasn’t enough fuel for it really to cause any damage,” said Henriques.

One person involved in the crash was tangled in the powerlines but was not injured.

On Friday afternoon, traffic was diverted onto Route 7225 (Watkins Center Parkway) as crews cleaned up and investigated. The southbound lanes reopened around 2:30 p.m.

“I’m just glad nobody was hurt, and they moved it quickly and opened up the highway,” Henriques said.
