Suspect speeding over 150 mph before crash that killed 2 passengers, Tempe police say

By Ben Bradley and Elliott Polakoff
Published: Nov. 27, 2023 at 12:55 PM MST

TEMPE, AZ (3TV/CBS 5) — A man is in custody after police say he was speeding over 150 miles per hour before he crashed and left his two passengers dead in Tempe over the weekend. The suspect has been identified as 32-year-old Derek Glasscock.

Just after 1:30 a.m. on Saturday, Tempe police saw two cars street racing in the area of Rural and Terrace roads. According to court documents, the cars were going over 100 in a 35 mph zone. Officers tried to stop one of the cars involved, a blue Ford Mustang, but it sped away. Police did not attempt to chase the car, but moments later, officers saw a cloud of smoke rising into the air.

Police then found the same Mustang had crashed into a power pole on the east side of Rural Road just south of Broadway. Documents say Glasscock was pinned in the driver’s seat and that his two passengers had been ejected. One passenger, 32-year-old Jesse Ruiz, was pronounced dead at the scene, while the other, 31-year-old Maria Rangel, died later at a hospital.

Per court documents, Glasscock reportedly made statements to officers that he had been drinking and should not have been driving. Police noted that he was disoriented and had trouble remembering details from the evening. After being checked out at the hospital, Glasscock reportedly told investigators he had been at a concert at a downtown Tempe bar and had at least three mixed drinks. He said he didn’t remember leaving the bar or anything else until after the crash.

Based on evidence at the scene, police believe Glasscock was going 153 mph at the time of the crash. Documents indicate that after he lost control, the car went airborne, crashing into a utility pole and two palm trees. Investigators found that the Mustang was over 9 feet in the air when it hit one of the palm trees. Police called it an “extremely violent” crash that resulted in Rangel being thrown from the car with such force that she was found 80 feet away behind a building.

Police say Ruiz and Rangel were in a relationship and lived together. Glasscock was booked into the Maricopa County jail on two felony counts of reckless manslaughter.

“It’s just very tragic,” Claudia Morales said. “It hurts my heart.” Morales, who has lived just down the road for the last 30 years, says she’s never seen the street racing in the area as bad as it is right now. “My boyfriend and I, as we were walking down here he was like ‘Claudia I just don’t know if I want to be walking on the sidewalk’,” she said. “You’re taking your life into your own hands if you’re trying to make a turn at Rural and Southern.”

“Our prayers go out to everybody impacted. May their souls rest in peace,” said Tim States, business manager for Our Lady of Mount Carmel Catholic Church, who added that one of the trees that the car collided with had to be cut down. “I mean the tree was pretty much cracked in half from the trunk,” States said. “So it was a safety issue for our preschool kids because it could fall over. Because it got hit so hard from the impact.”

Moving forward, both States and Morales would like to see more done by the city of Tempe when it comes to cracking down on high-speed driving. “When people drive like that, it’s only a matter of time until an accident happens,” States said. “Constant patrols on Rural Road would be actually nice,” Morales added. “This has to stop. We hear it all the time.”
