‘I thought I was going to die’: Woman clings onto car hood after dog was stolen

By Gray News staff
Published: Jan. 31, 2024 at 6:54 AM MST

(Gray News) - Most dog owners out there will be able to relate.

The woman who was seen clinging to the hood of a car in Los Angeles said she was trying to stop dognappers.

Ali Zacharias said she was eating on a patio with her French bulldog named Onyx earlier this month when a woman picked the dog up and ran away.

She got into a car, and Onyx’s owner jumped on the hood to stop her.

“So, I didn’t know what to do and I didn’t want the car to drive away, so I ran and stood in front of it. And they drove into me and I fell on top of their ... the hood and just started to, like, grab on, like, I’m not letting go. And before I knew it, they were like, backing up and, like, taking off,” Zacharias said.

“And they were going, started to go really fast, and I thought I was going to die. I just was like, ‘This is it. I’m going to die,’” she said. “I was like, on outside of a car going 40 miles an hour, like, it was so scary. And they swerved and like, threw me off the car.”

The man who took the video, Harrison Pessy, told The Los Angeles Times he lives in the neighborhood and was heading home when he heard “the screeching of the car and screaming.”

He saw that the driver of the Kia was trying to throw the woman off the car. Pessy took the video, which has since gone viral, as the car drove off.

The pet owner eventually fell from the vehicle, and the suspects got away. Zacharias was treated for minor injuries.

Los Angeles police are asking anyone with information on the suspects to contact them.

French bulldogs are popular with dog owners, with the American Kennel Club naming them the top breed of 2022.

The expensive dogs are also a target of thieves.

In one of the more high-profile French bulldog thefts, back in February 2021, Lady Gaga’s dog walker was shot, and her two French bulldogs were stolen at gunpoint in Hollywood. The dogs were returned after the star offered a $500,000 reward.

The woman who came forward to claim the reward was among those charged in the theft, Rolling Stone reported, and Lady Gaga ended up not having to pay the reward.
