911 hangs up on pregnant woman

Published: Nov. 7, 2008 at 5:03 PM EST|Updated: Nov. 9, 2008 at 5:01 PM EST

Orangeburg County (WLTX) - An Orangeburg woman delivers a baby with the help of sister-in-law on the side of the road. The operator hangs up to find help but an ambulance never arrives.

"You just love 'em more than anything, you just can't describe it," says Jennifer Melton adoring her daughter.

One day Jennifer Melton will be telling Lillian how she came into the world.

It may sound exciting, being born in a car but for Jennifer Melton and her sister-in-law Olivia Gentol it was one of the scariest moment ever.

When Jennifer contractions started they left their Orangeburg home heading to the nearest hospital.

"We made the quick decision to go to Bamberg and by the time we are a mile down the road the screaming starts," Olivia said.

So Olivia dials 911, first talking to an Orangeburg Operator.

Olivia: "I'm going to the Bamberg Hospital, I have a girl in my car having contractions like super fast."

Orangeburg 911: "Ma'am, ma'am, hold on one moment okay."

Olivia: "She feels like she wants to push. I need to know what to do right now! I am driving this truck. Do I need to stop and deliver a baby?"

Bamberg 911: Ma'am?

Olivia: "I've been transferred, someone tell me what to do. Say Olivia get to 301 as fast as you can."

Bamberg 911: "Just drive towards 301 until we can get you some help."

Olivia: "Oh my gosh what do I do? What should I do?"

Bamberg 911: "Listen are yall at 301 yet?

Olivia: "No, I'm about to pull off the side of the road. I'm on Pinehill Road, I'm on Pinehill Road."

Bamberg 911: "In Orangeburg County?"

Olivia: "Oh my God, yes."

So Olivia keeps driving but over the next few minutes several calls drop on her way to the hospital. By now Orangeburg calls Bamberg: the only problem: Bamberg's dispatcher isn't familiar with surrounding areas.

Orangeburg 911: "A call was given to us by a pregnant female on the road. Did you get any sort of description?"

Bamberg 911: "The caller said she's in a green truck at that cope town limit sign but we don't have the crossing of the road because we don't have an Orangeburg map."

Orangeburg 911: "Okay so did you'll send anybody from yall end to come to the Orangeburg county line?"

Bamberg 911: "No ma'am."

Orangeburg 911: "Okay can you'll do that just in case it is in your county?"

Bamberg 911: "She's actually contacted the caller."

Orangeburg 911: "I'm sorry."

Bamberg 911: "Um, our other dispatcher is in actually in contact with the caller."

What seems like hours is about 20 minutes. Finally Olivia stops, still talking to Bamberg 911. Jennifer is giving birth.

Olivia: "It's coming out oh my God."

Bamberg 911: "Listen, listen, I'm gonna hang up you're gonna have to answer the phone when I call you back okay."

Olivia: "What should I do?"

Bamberg 911: "Listen."

Olivia: "Okay, I'm gonna stop."

Bamberg op: "She is in active labor and she needs some assistance; she's in Orangeburg County."

Olivia: "I'm out of the car, I'm not driving anymore. I'm going around to the passengers side."

Bamberg Op: "Okay, you're at the Cope, town of cope sign?

Olivia: "Yes."

Bamberg 911: "Okay let me get Orangeburg and I'm gonna call you back ok."

Olivia: "Oh my God!"

"There's no mistaking the cope sign is lit and she decides to get off the phone with me," Olivia said.

The baby is delivered in the parking lot of the Mt. Nebo Church of the Redeemer. It's at the intersection of Slab Landing Road in Cope and Highway 301. Technically, that's in Orangeburg County, but it's also five miles from the Bamberg Hospital.

An off-duty officer escorts the women to the Bamberg Hospital after the ordeal.

But why did the operator hang up? Bamberg Sheriff Ed Darnell declined an on camera interview but said over the phone "They followed protocol and communicated with the other county as best they could"

As far as Orangeburg never arriving the Deputy County Administrator John Smith released a statement saying "A new employee was in training and separated from the county the day after the calls."

Meanwhile new mommy Jennifer Melton is taking the focus off of what was almost a complete tragedy, but instead turned into her amazing triumph.

"She's my little miracle baby. It's definitely a story I will never forget. The main thing is that she's okay," Jennifer said.

Thankfully, a healthy mommy and baby are at home safe and sound waiting on any information from the counties on the complaints they've filed.

The Criminal Justice Academy said each dispatcher has one year to complete one week of training with them.
