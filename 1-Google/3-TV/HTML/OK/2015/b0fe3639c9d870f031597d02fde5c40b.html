Body of Durant Man Who Drowned in Lake Texoma Recovered

BRYAN COUNTY, OK -- Search and rescue for a man who disappeared near Lake Texoma about 6:30 pm on Saturday came to an end this morning, after rescue crews found his body.

A recreational activity turned deadly for  26-year old Peter Buckner from Durant Saturday afternoon, when Oklahoma Troopers said he and a group of friends decided to walk off the east end of the Rail Road Bridge at Rock Creek on Lake Texoma to go for a swim. 

"They were actually further along the water parallel to the bridge," said OHP Trooper, Dustin Pruitt.

They say Buckner, who was not wearing a life jacket at the time, was swimming under the bridge towards his friends, when he suddenly yelled for help.

"At such time he had difficulty swimming, went under water, and didn't resurface," said Pruitt.

Rescue crews recovered Buckner’s body a little past 7 am on Sunday, 30 feet from shore in 19 feet of water.

Stickers on Buckner’s car still parked near the railroad tracks leading up to the bridge show he was a young man with a passion for metal music.

His death has caused a stir among residents on Smiser Rd. in Calera, Oklahoma who said the Rail Road Bridge has always been a trouble spot.

"It’s old, dangerous, just be careful,” said longtime resident, Rocky Baker.

Along with being old and dangerous, the bridge can only be reached by hiking a mile up a still active railroad track.

"You have to walk out, and once you get out there and there's a train coming, it's either jump or the train you know," said Baker.

Baker has lived near the bridge for 28 years; he said people usually frequent the remote location for one major reason.

"It’s all about the thrill, to get that rush; it's all about the thrill," said Baker.

Once at the bridge, most people either go swimming or other times do something a bit more risky.

"We just go and jump off; I’ve jumped off many times myself," said Baker.

However, Baker said Buckner's death has prompted him to reanalyze the bridge he and his kids have come to enjoy.

"I don't want my kids down there, and just use precaution," said Baker.

First responders said the exact cause of why Buckner drowned  is not yet known.

They also advise to those planning on swimming near any lakes to wear life vests.
