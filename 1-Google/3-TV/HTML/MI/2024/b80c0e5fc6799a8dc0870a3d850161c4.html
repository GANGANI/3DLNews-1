VIDEO: Florida deputies save overdosing man during traffic stop

by JACK LEMNUS | WPEC

Florida deputies save overdosing man during traffic stop (Flagler County Sheriff's Office)

    TOPICS:

    FCSOFentanylOverdoseNarcanMacNeilDeputiesTraffic stopTassy

PALM COAST, Fla. (WPEC) — Flagler County Sheriff's Office (FCSO) Deputy Caleb Tassy was driving home at the end of his shift when he noticed the driver in front of him erratically weaving through traffic. The car swerved and struck a mailbox.

Tassy flicked on his lights and pulled the driver over. But this wasn't a traditional traffic stop.

The driver, 38-year-old Stephen MacNeil of Palm Coast, was reportedly unresponsive, fading in and out of consciousness. It was clear he was having an overdose.

Tassy checked his pulse as other first responders arrived on the scene. They administered two shots of Narcan, according to FCSO.

"Rescue's here," Tassy said when MacNeil came to, as shown in the Jan. 28 bodycam footage.

As MacNeil clutched his head and adjusted his glasses, strobing emergency vehicles rolled up to relieve the deputies and provide additional medical treatment.

After regaining consciousness, MacNeil refused further medical care and told the deputies he'd purchased heroin in Daytona, and that there was still some in his car hidden inside a cigarette pack. They found the pack of cigarettes inside a "clear plastic package" and found 2.2 grams of what deputies described as a whitish-blue powdery substance. It tested positive for fentanyl, stated FCSO.

FCSO officials arrested him for impaired driving and transported him to the Sheriff Perry Hall Inmate Detention Facility. He was also charged with leaving the scene of property damage and possession of fentanyl. He's currently being held on a $1,500 bond.

"We hope he gets the help he needs," FCSO wrote on its Facebook page.

Sheriff Rick Staly took MacNeil's close call as a chance to discuss the larger issue of rising fentanyl-related overdoses.

“We see time and time again the effects of fentanyl,” said Staly in the Facebook post. “This time our deputies prevented loss of life but driving under the influence of narcotics will land you in the Green Roof Inn every time.”
