2 dead after home explosion in Flint, including 3-year-old girl

by Dave Bondy, Caleb Holloway, Ron Hilliard, Courtney Bennett & Remi Murrey

Tue, November 23rd 2021 at 10:29 PM
Updated Wed, April 13th 2022 at 12:04 PM
15
VIEW ALL PHOTOS
WEYI - House Explosion updated photo.png

FLINT, Mich. — UPDATE as of Tuesday afternoon: According to city of Flint officials, two people have been killed in a home explosion that occurred on Monday night.

The Flint Fire Department says a 3-year-old girl, who was previously reported missing, has been found dead in the home where the explosion originated.


Another person has also been found dead; a 55-year-old woman. The fire department originally thought the body was a 70-year-old neighbor.

The total structures damaged has been raised to 30 from the 17 originally found.


Multiple houses have their utilities turned off during the investigation.

Home Depot is donating wood to help fix homes.

"This is a tragedy in our community. We must pray and wrap our arms around the families impacted. The City of Flint appreciates all of our community partners that have helped expedite our response,” Mayor Sheldon Neeley said. “We ask for sensitivity and compassion during this time. Remember, these are families, someone's loved ones with homes destroyed.”

_ _ _

Police are investigating after a house explosion in Flint on Monday night.

It happened on the 3900 block of Hogarth Avenue.

The city of Flint held updates on the incident on Tuesday.

3:30 p.m. update

11:00 a.m. update

According to the Flint Fire Department, multiple people are injured.

A young girl, that was in the house at time of the explosion, and is 4 years old, is missing at this time.

The parents of the missing girl are in the hospital.

Flint Blight Teams are currently working to clean up damage.

A total of 17 structures received damage.

Flint will be using emergency funds to help the families involved. Call City of Flint at 810.410.2020 to learn more about the accommodations the city will be providing.

The United Way of Genesee County has set up a donation page for the tragedy on their website.

Red Cross has set up a reception and warming center with snacks for those impacted at St. Pius X at 3139 Hogarth Avenue Flint, Michigan. Residents impacted by the explosion may also reach the Red Cross at 1-800-RED-CROSS.

Genesee Health System (GHS) will also be offering mental health services for those affected.

Family Promise of Genesee County (FPGC) is accepting volunteers or donated clothing, food, and household items, please call 810.234.9444 for more information.

Local residents react to home explosion:

Several homes are now completely gone, with many more facing significant damage after a tragic situation that's causing a lot of questions to be asked.

“You know in the bible, it says the world comes to an end when strange things start happening, so it makes you wonder. I mean honestly it makes you wonder whether we're safe,” said Jerry Brackett, a nearby neighbor.

When Brackett got home from work last night, he immediately went to sleep, only to be awakened by something out of wildest dreams

“All you could see was orange and black smoke. People yelling, this was like 475 on the Fourth of July, blocked up,” said Brackett.

The smoke Brackett is talking about came from a massive explosion on Hogarth Avenue in Flint.

It's just one block north of where he lives, and from the looks of his home, the blast left quite an impact, leaving him with busted out windows and doors.

“I didn’t know what to do last night. I took a couple of sheets. Stapled them around the window to keep the air out and turn the heat up on 100 and put on space heaters to make it through the night,” said Brackett.

Then there's Roger Faigley. He didn't experience any damage to his apartment, but says the blast shook him and the entire building to the core

“It was as if a car was going a high speed, a high-speed truck hit the building and blew up,” said Faigley.

A feeling Terry Rush felt miles away from the scene.

“I got up, went and looked out my bedroom window, I didn't see anything but a cloud of smoke,” said Rush.

.Monday night, Rush spent hours trying to contact her brother, who lives on Hogarth.

Tuesday morning, he finally called her back, saying he felt fine, but the explosion leveled his home.

“It's horrible. It's sad. It's real sad,” said Rush.

While there isn't a set timeline for when people can return home, the clean-up process has begun as this investigation continues.

Three homes near the house that exploded were also damaged.

The occupant of the third house is currently safe in a local hotel.

Consumers Energy is on the scene to monitor in case this was a gas explosion, however, a cause has not been determined.

Following the fire, the Mid-Michigan NOW newsroom started getting reports of people feeling the ground shake in Flint, Grand Blanc, Mundy Twp, and Burton.

Flint Council member Dennis Pfeiffer arrived at the scene after feeling the ground shake at his house.

A Flint MTA bus arrived at the scene of the fire to shelter residents who were forced out of their homes.

The manager of the Applebee's on Miller Road called our newsroom telling us the ceiling panels collapsed in the restaurant after the ground shook.

Consumers Energy has a crew on the scene and is working to turn off the gas on the street.

Shelters are being set up at

  • St. Pius X Catholic Church, 3139 Hogarth Ave, Flint
  • Hand of God Ministries, 3408 Miller Rd, Flint
  • End Time Outreach, 1011 Hammond Ave, Flint

Consumers Energy released these details on the explosion:

  • Consumers Energy received a call at 9:37 p.m. Monday about a home explosion in the 3900 block of Hogarth Avenue in Flint. Crews responded and were on site by 9:57 p.m.
  • Due to public safety concerns, crews shut off natural gas and electric service to three homes that were destroyed. Crews shut off service to four other surrounding homes that also were damaged.
  • Consumers Energy surveyed multiple neighboring homes and did not find any evidence of natural gas leaks. Aside from damage caused directly by the explosion, crews have not found any issues on our natural gas system.
  • No cause has been established.
  • Crews are working with local public safety officials to assist in their investigation and will be on site today.
