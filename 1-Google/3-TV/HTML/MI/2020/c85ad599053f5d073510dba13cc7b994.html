Unspeakable pain: Woman devastated after losing husband, son to coronavirus

by BRIA JONES, WEYI

Sat, April 4th 2020 at 11:28 PM
Updated Sat, July 11th 2020 at 11:42 AM
Mid-Michigan woman devastated after losing husband, son to coronavirus (Photo: Sandy Brown)

    TOPICS:

    GRAND BLANC TOWNSHIPGrand Blanc Township,Michigan,United StatesMichigan,United StatesMorganSandy BrownFreddie Brown IIIFreddie Jr.&

GRAND BLANC TOWNSHIP, Mich. (WEYI) -- A Grand Blanc Township woman is dealing with unspeakable pain after losing her husband and son to the coronavirus.

“There is not even a word in the vocabulary that can describe my pain," said Sandy Brown.

Hurt and utter devastation are words that barely touch the surface of what Sandy Brown is feeling.

She lost her loving husband and only child to COVID-19 just days apart.

“I said god my husband is in the hospital and my baby is starting to get progressively sick," said Sandy Brown.

Less than three weeks ago, Brown’s husband 59-year-old Freddie Brown Junior started to feel ill.

“He was so cold he was chilled to the bone and nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, couldn’t even keep sips of water down," said Sandy Brown.

Freddie Jr. was as a kidney transplant recipient, so Sandy took her husband to the emergency room at Ascension Genesys Hospital, but doctors sent him home becuse he didn’t meet the CDC’s testing criteria.

Shortly after, she dropped him off again not knowing it would be the last time she would see her husband of 35 years alive.

Sandy says they talked daily by phone until doctors told her his condition was worsening and they had to put him on a ventilator.

“I just started crying and crying and I said honey honey can you hear me and he said yeah babe it’s going to be fine," said Sandy Brown.

Mr. Brown passed away last Thursday. That same night his son, 20-year-old Freddie Brown III's health took a terrible turn.

“I went into the room and he was panting like a dog that same sound I heard coming from my husband.. I said sonny sonny wake up he said what is it and he was just wet with sweat," said Sandy Brown.

Losing his father and a cousin to the virus.. She says the younger Freddie was scared to go to the hospital.

She would learn another relative rushed her son to the hospital.

“I had a niece in there, Morgan, praise god she is home and I told them both send me pictures of you," said Sandy Brown.

By Saturday she says things were getting better for her son she affectionately called her miracle child, but 24 hours later his health was declining.

“The doctor came and got on the phone and said how long will it take you to get here and when she said that I already knew cause that was the same thing they had said to me three days ago and I said what do you mean...she said we’re losing him,” said Sandy Brown.

His dreams of playing in the NFL would also be lost, but she finds comfort knowing he was well loved by his friends.

The coronavirus striking her family close-knit family. Sandy is now finding the inner strength to carry on.

“Instead of his dad watching over us. The both of them are watching over me," said Sandy Brown.

Brown is preparing to lay her husband and son to rest following a private viewing on Good Friday.
