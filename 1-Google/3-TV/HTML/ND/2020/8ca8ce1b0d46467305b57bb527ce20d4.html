Coronavirus spreads to the US, but officials say North Dakota is at a very low transmission risk

By Morgan Benth
Published: Feb. 4, 2020 at 2:03 PM CST

The coronavirus news is scary for a lot of people.

The first death outside of China was reported over the weekend.

The coronavirus continues a slow spread from mainland China to the U.S.

With 11 confirmed cases of the virus in the U.S., many people are concerned.

Those affected by the virus are being quarantined and the streets of China look quite bare.

Now that the virus has spread to the U.S., North Dakota Department of Health employees have made it their mission to inform the public and prepare physicians.

"Whenever there are new diseases, dependent on what it is and the severity and what's going on, that's what we'll do. We will take priority," says Michelle Dethloff, program manager at the Department of Health’s Division of Disease Control.

The Division is taking coronavirus seriously, but Dethloff says North Dakotans are at low risk of contracting the disease.

Other respiratory diseases, however, are running rampant this winter.

"I think we need to remember that this is cold and flu season. Certainly influenza and RSV and the other respiratory infections out there are going to pose a lot more of a risk right now to the people in our community," said Julie Jacobson, an infection prevention manager at Sanford Health.

Jacobson says that all nurses, doctors, and ill patients need to wear protective masks, gloves, and robes in order avoid the spread of diseases.

She says that coronavirus and common flu symptoms may appear alike at first.

"The symptoms are very similar. It's just that that's the unknown at this point. We don't know how it's going to progress and how it's going to affect people," said Jacobson.

The U.S. has declared a travel ban going to and coming from China.

The World Health Organization has declared the spread of the coronavirus a global health emergency.
