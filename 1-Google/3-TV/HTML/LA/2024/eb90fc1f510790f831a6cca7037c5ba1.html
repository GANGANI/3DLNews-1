Pair of golfers in their 60s finish journey to play courses in all 50 states

By Chanel-Taylar McCarthy and Gray News staff
Published: Feb. 1, 2024 at 11:22 PM CST|Updated: Feb. 1, 2024 at 11:23 PM CST

LINCOLN, Neb. (KOLN/Gray News) - Two friends from Nebraska are celebrating their journey around the country.

A few years ago, Greg Ebsen of Papillion and Terry Forman of Omaha set off to play at a golf course in every state.

It all started as a bold idea from Ebsen, who is originally from Wisconsin. Ebsen said the thought sparked while the two were on a golfing trip in the Ozarks.

“It was October 2017, we were sitting at a bar and I felt like I needed some direction in my life, so I said, ‘Why don’t we golf all 50 states?’” Ebsen said.

Ebsen’s buddy, Terry Forman, said he was immediately on board.

Forman joked saying he wouldn’t have thought to take on such a task as they were both just recreational players. He said he knew that they would have to get the ball rolling immediately.

“Both of us were in our sixties at the time and it was like, OK, we don’t have 30 years to do this,” Forman said. “If we’re going to do it, then we have to jump on it.”

Counting their tour in the Ozarks, the pair visited Florida next.

After that, they would visit multiple states on each trip. They played courses in Alaska after visiting Denali National Park, courses in Oahu, Hawaii, and mountainous courses in Colorado.

The duo said one experience they will never forget is their midnight game in Alaska.

Ebsen and Forman said though most people were asleep, they did get a chance to run into one of the “locals.”

“There were very few people on the course, except for one bear,” Ebsen said.

“We didn’t even see him,” Forman added. “The guy came out in the gold cart to tell us that there was a bear sighting over on the first hole. He said if it was going to cause any trouble, it would have done it by now.”

Forman said he asked what he should do if they came in contact with the bear, but the man shrugged his shoulders unable to offer any advice.

“That wasn’t the answer we were looking for,” Forman said.

During the adventure, the two also kept a tradition of visiting a different diner each morning on their trips.

“We didn’t want to go anywhere we could visit in Omaha,” Ebsen said, “So diners in the morning and nicer places in the afternoon.”

Forman and Ebsen completed their golfing tour in September 2023. They played a game at Lake Presidential in Maryland. And to commemorate their trip, the two got matching license plates that say “GOLFD 50″ and “GLFD 50.”

Ebsen also surprised his friend by getting a tattoo of a golf ball with the number 50 on it.

Terry said once they decided to take on the journey, it was never a matter of “if” they would ever finish, but just a question of when.
