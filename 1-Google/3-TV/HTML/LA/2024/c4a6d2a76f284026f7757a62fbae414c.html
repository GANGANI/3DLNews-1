What’s behind the surge in broken water pipes, boil advisories across Louisiana

Thousands of miles of underground pipes lay vulnerable to bursting
By Zack Fradella and Amber Wheeler
Published: Feb. 1, 2024 at 11:54 AM CST

NEW ORLEANS (WVUE) - There has been a surge in broken water pipes across Louisiana over the past few weeks.

The Fox 8 Weather Authority team says there is more to it than just the cold spell we’ve been through.

It seems like every day a different part of Louisiana is being placed under a new boil water advisory due to water main breaks.

We see what’s going on above ground every day, but it’s what is happening underneath us that’s key to understanding why this has been happening so much lately.

There’s little doubt that Louisiana’s infrastructure, especially underground, is aging. But that’s not the biggest issue recently with all of the busted pipes.

To understand, you have to delve into the science and the impact of cold, warm, wet, and dry.

We have thousands of miles of pipe underground in the New Orleans metro. They’ve been under an immense amount of weather stress over the last six months.

First, we were in a drought which opened space in the ground for pipes to move around throughout much of the summer and fall. Then the ground froze a few weeks back which led to a rapid contraction of the ground. Added on top of that the heavy rains we have seen lately that have flooded the ground and caused stress with heavy mud.

The heavy mud is likely cracking our pipes after the drought moved them.

Typically, when you go from one extreme to another in weather, there are consequences... and until we get away from the consistent heavy rains, broken pipes will likely be our norm.
