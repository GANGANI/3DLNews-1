East Catholic pitcher selected 7th in MLB draft

By Rob Polansky and Courtney Zieller
Updated: Jul. 12, 2021 at 10:29 AM EDT

ELLINGTON, CT (WFSB) - A Connecticut high school baseball standout could be headed to Kansas City.

Frank Mozzicato, an 18-year-old lefty pitcher from East Catholic High School in Manchester, was selected 7th overall in the 2021 Major League Baseball draft by the Kansas City Royals.

The Royals said they are confident they’ll sign the Ellington native, who committed to the University of Connecticut.

Mozzicato told MLB.com that he was speechless when he heard his name called on Sunday.

“This has been my dream since I was little, to play professional baseball, so I couldn’t be more excited to start my career in Kansas City,” he told the website.

The pick was surprising because Mozzicato was ranked 39th on the MLB Pipeline’s top 250 draft prospects.

“When the 6th pick happened last night, they called me and said you’re going to be number 7, congratulations! You’re going to be a Royal! It’s super awesome and super exciting,” Mozzicato said.

His season included four consecutive no-hitters with 135 strikeouts in about 55 innings for his East Catholic High School team.

Just last month, his team won the CIAC championship game where he struck out 17 batters. His fast ball comes in at 93 miles per hour.

Mozzicato’s team won the Class M state championship last month over Northwestern.

“I’m most proud of the guys and winning a state championship game this year. That was our goal from minute one and just awesome that all our hard work paid off and we accomplished our goal,” he stated.

Mozzicato has been playing ball at a young age. His love for the game continued to grow from there. “6 years old, 7 years old, my mom bringing me up, taking me to baseball, soccer, basketball. I loved all three sports but baseball, I think, I had a different feeling out there especially being out on the mound.

He has until August 1 to decide if he wants to sign with the Kansas City Royals or attend UConn, to study business sports management.

Mozzicato decided, “as of right now, I’m going to be a Royal.”

MLB.com said Kansas City will likely be able to sign him for below the allotted value for the slot at $5,432,400.
