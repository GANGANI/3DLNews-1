Connecticut State Police hat seen in Taliban images

By Mike Agogliati and Matthew Campbell
Updated: Sep. 1, 2021 at 3:45 PM EDT

(WFSB) – Images coming out of a former British police training facility in Kabul depict Taliban fighters posing with their weapons.

Also pictured is a British police hat and what appears to be a Connecticut State Police Stetson. The photo was published in The Sun, which is a British newspaper.

The chilling photo comes just hours after the U.S. military left Afghanistan.

The tassels on the Stetson indicates it belonged to a trooper of the rank of lieutenant or higher.

“Hundreds of Connecticut State Police Troopers, active and retired, have and continue to proudly serve in the US armed forces overseas,” Brian Foley, Assistant Commissioner for the Department of Emergency Services and Public Protection said. “The exchanging of a police hat or police patch within law enforcement has traditionally been associated with a sign of trust and respect. While it is impossible to definitively determine; it is plausible and we believe that a retired trooper of the rank of Lieutenant or higher, deployed overseas, exchanged the CSP hat for this honorable purpose. We are proud of the military service of our Troopers both active and retired and have a deep appreciation of the sacrifices they have all made.”

Foley believes this exchange happened during more peaceful times and had a “honorable purpose,” and was not given to the Taliban.

Foley couldn’t guarantee that this was the case, but it seems to be the most likely explanation.

Channel 3 did some digging, and there’s a state law specially for these Stetson hats.

It doesn’t cover gifting the hats, but ‘section 29-6a’ says the hats cannot be worn off duty and there’s a $250 fine attached.
