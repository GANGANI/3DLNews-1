Police are warning iPhone users about the latest update

By Shannon Kane and Eliza Kruczynski
Published: Nov. 26, 2023 at 1:34 PM EST

WATERTOWN, Conn. (WFSB) - Police in Watertown posted a warning on their Facebook page about the latest iPhone update.

A feature called ‘NameDrop’ is enabled by default with the new update. Anyone with this feature can place their phone next to yours or your child’s phone and automatically receive their contact information.

Police say that information passed to another phone could include a picture, phone number, email address and more.

To disable this feature, police say you should go into your iPhone settings, click general, then airdrop and shut off ‘bringing devices together.’

Some iPhone users who commented on the department’s Facebook post said they recently updated their phone and would have never known about this feature.

Security expert Scott Driscoll with Internet Safety Concepts said it is important to remember to turn it off.

“I think what parents need to realize is if it’s on right now and two phones get connected, you still have to give approval for the share, you get a screen that warns you that things are being shared so it’s not an automatic download of information but turning it off gives us a better chance of security,” Driscoll said.

Driscoll said this feature could still be a good thing. If you’re at a conference or just met someone, it’s a quick way to share information like a business card, as long as you understand what information is being sent over.
