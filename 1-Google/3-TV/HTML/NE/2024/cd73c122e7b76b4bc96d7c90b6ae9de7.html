‘He was a survivor’: Veteran’s missing dog found after 38 days, recovering after being hit by car

By Shawna Khalafi and Gray News staff
Published: Feb. 2, 2024 at 12:05 AM CST

LAS VEGAS (KVVU/Gray News) - A veteran’s emotional support dog has finally been found, more than a month after it went missing.

The dog, named Little Bear, is now recovering after being hit by a car and dropped off at the animal hospital by someone who saw the crash.

“He was a survivor, and I missed him,” said Little Bear’s owner, 80-year-old Reuben Nieves.

Thirty-eight days—that’s how long Little Bear was missing, surviving on the streets of Henderson, while Nieves searched tirelessly to bring him home.

KVVU first spoke to Nieves two weeks into the search for his dog on Jan. 6.

“Passed out the flyers to individuals who were walking their dog, and they took it, and they said if they see him around in the area that they would contact me,” Nieves said.

On Dec. 21, Nieves said Little Bear got loose and ran away during drop-off for a veterinarian appointment on Stephanie and Warm Springs. Then, last week, Little Bear was found about four miles away on Sunset Road.

He’d been hit by a car and dropped off at Tropicana Animal Hospital by someone who saw the crash.

The animal hospital then scanned the dog, found a microchip and contacted Nieves.

“I thought that maybe somebody had him in the house and wanted to keep him. I didn’t know,” said Nieves. “The image that he might get into an accident with a car also haunted me.”

Little Bear had a fractured jaw, a tear in his lung and a head injury from the crash. He had also lost a third of his body weight since he has been missing.

“I held him in my arms, and I could feel his ribcage,” said Nieves. “He has a hard time standing, they said. He tries to get up.”

“Thirty-eight days is a long time, and you could tell by the X-rays we took, he’s probably been eating some bones and things that are not regular dog food,” said Dr. Amy Sarcinella at Tropicana Animal Hospital.

Little Bear is still recovering at the animal hospital, and Sarcinella said she’s happy with the progress he’s made.

“He’s not a hundred percent,” said Sarcinella. “We’ve still got some fractures in that jaw that should hopefully be able to heal. But he’s eating and doesn’t seem to be in pain.”

“I think after time goes by, he’ll be reasonably in good shape,” said Nieves. “It’s all I can expect now.”

Nieves said he is grateful to the person who rescued Little Bear after the crash, and he’s ready to bring his best friend back home.

“He normally sleeps with me. I have a little bed on the bed,” said Nieves. “But I’m afraid in his condition, he might hurt himself if he tries to get off, so I may put him on the floor until he’s ready.”
