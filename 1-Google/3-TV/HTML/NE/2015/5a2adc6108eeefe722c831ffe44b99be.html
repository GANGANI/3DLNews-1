Young Boy Born With Half A Heart Overcomes All Odds

By Erin Murray
Published: Aug. 24, 2015 at 10:40 PM CDT

As Brady Luke, 7, pretend battles his brother Adam, 9, in a game of pirates it's hard to see a little line coming out the top of Brady's shirt.

"It's a story line, it's his life, basically down his chest," said his father, Brian Luke. "From top to bottom, every moment of his life is because of that scar."

Brady, after some encouragement will show the seven to eight in scar that runs near the middle of his chest.

"Like when I was born, my heart wasn't working so I had to have three surgeries," Brady said very simply.

For his parents, Lisa and Brian, finding out Brady would be born with half a heart was very difficult. The odds were not in Brady's favor.

"It was hard. Scared to death knowing," as tears fill her eyes and she can't finish the sentence. "We did not know what to expect, what was going to happen, how things were going to turn out," said Brian, quickly picking up where she left off.

When Brady was born, it was not the normal welcome into the world.

"She handed me him, and then they took him away. Literally 30 seconds in the room with us before they took him over into the NICU," said Lisa and Brian. "I said hello and goodbye."

Brady's malady is called hypolastic left heart syndrome and like Brady said, it would take three surgeries to hopefully give his heart a chance to do it's job.

"And so that was the toughest time for me was that first surgery, putting him on that operating table and saying goodbye," said Brian.

Brady was in the care of the doctor's at Children's Hospital and all three surgeries went very well.

When asked what he thinks about having only half a heart, the seven year-old again answers very simply.

"It's kinda like sad, because then you kinda don't feel like everybody else. But that means that you are special," said Brady.

His parents call his scar a badge of honor.

"He tells us he is a champion. I am strong I have to be brave, I am a champion," said Lisa. "Without it he wouldn't be here. With that faint little line going down his chest, it's proof that he is a fighter. That he is a survivor."

And his parent's teach him, while his scar does make him special, it's not something to hide.

"I remember that, I remember every painful moment, every glorious moment, every sad moment, every happy moment for the last eight years. It is because of that that he has been able to experience all those ups and downs. And we as a family have been able to experience all those ups and downs. It is because of that scar," said Brian.

To this day, Brady will still stop by Children's Hospital; even when he doesn't have appointments. He said the medical staff there are his extended family.
