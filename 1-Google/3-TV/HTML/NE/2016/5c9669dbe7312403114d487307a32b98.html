Mike Hutchinson Coming Home

By Travis Robinson
Published: Feb. 22, 2016 at 8:44 AM CST

Deuel County Sheriff Scott DeCoste tells NBC Nebraska Deputy Mike Hutchinson will be coming home Monday

The town of Chappell will welcome him home with a surprise parade between 1:00 and 1:30pm Mountain Time.

The Sheriff will escort Hutchinson and his wife to their home. Residents and those wanting to show support are encouraged to line the street from the intersection of Hwy. 30 and Babcock, heading east.

Deputy Hutchinson was seriously injured in a shoot out while trying to serve an arrest warrant back in December.

Deputy Hutchinson made tremendous strides in his recovery at a Greeley Colorado hospital, after he was shot four times.

He went through multiple surgeries and back in January got off dialysis, antibiotics and started walking again.

UPDATE:

People in the panhandle lined the streets to show support for Deuel county Sheriff's Deputy Mike Hutchinson.

He was shot 4 times while serving a warrant in Big Springs, in December of 2015. He was released from the hospital in Greeley Colorado on Monday.

Deuel County Sheriff Scott DeCoste told Hutchinson while he was on his hospital bed there wasn't going to be a funeral. There was going to be a party.

People from all over Nebraska met up in Chapell to join the party and welcome Hutch home. For the past 3 months, the Deuel County Sheriff's department raised funds and support for Hutchinson.

These supporters lined the streets of Chapell for a parade Hutchinson didn't know about.Seeing everyone who cared for and supported him through the past few months made Hutchinson emotional.

“At the end of the parade, they drove by us and there wasn’t a dry eye on that bus." said Sheriff DeCoste. "I don’t know what he’s feeling or anything like that, I didn’t get a chance to talk to him yet and I’m sure he’ll yell at me later.”

Hutchinson wasn't the only one getting emotional. this was a chance for the entire Deuel County Sheriff's Department to see just how many people in their community care about them and are thankful for what they do.
