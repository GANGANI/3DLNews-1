Teen pleads guilty to murdering Memphis pastor in driveway

By Joyce Peterson and Lydian Coombs
Published: Feb. 1, 2024 at 5:22 PM CST|Updated: Feb. 1, 2024 at 10:36 PM CST

MEMPHIS, Tenn. (WMC) - Sixteen years old with an eighth-grade education, and now he’s headed to prison for murder. The teen gunman who shot and killed a Whitehaven pastor during a carjacking in 2022 copped a plea deal Thursday to a lesser charge.

Prosecutors said Miguel Andrade was just 15 when he shot 52-year-old Pastor Autura Eason-Williams multiple times as she sat in her car talking on the phone. Investigators said Andrade and his accomplices had followed her home, looking to steal her Infiniti Q50.

As the smoke cleared from his gun, Andrade pulled Eason-Williams’ bullet-ridden body out of the driver’s seat and took off with the car, leaving a woman who’d been a vocal advocate for juvenile justice reform to die in her driveway, and her loved ones crying out for tougher, not more lenient sentencing for young criminals.

Now, 17 months later, Andrade is a convicted killer, and Pastor Eason-Williams’ family told the court his punishment does not fit the crime.

“I hate you to my f***ing gut,” her husband Darrell said to Andrade during his emotional victim impact statement. “I have no remorse for you. I wish the worst for you while you’re locked up for 28 years.”

Andrade looked on expressionless as Eason-Williams’ grieving husband continued his testimony.

“You are only out on bond because the juvenile system is messed up. In other countries, they do a life for life,” he said. “But you will be eating three meals a day. Until changes are made with the juvenile system, there will continue to be unjust penalties.”

At the request of the Shelby County District Attorney’s Office, Andrade was charged as an adult in the case. 

On the day he shot Eason-Williams, he was wearing an ankle bracelet after an arrest for a previous crime, and investigators say Andrade carjacked another victim after killing the beloved Memphis pastor. Despite these factors, he was granted bond and was out of jail until Thursday’s sentencing, something that did not sit well with Eason-Williams’ sister, Tricia.

“What you did to Autura was not only selfish, but cruel,” she said. “I hope you get the max sentence for your crime. You are a disgrace. I’ve hated seeing you out, living your life while my sister is dead.”

Andrade was originally indicted for first-degree murder, along with carjacking, especially aggravated robbery, and possession of a firearm during a dangerous felony, charges that carried the possibility of life in prison or the death penalty.

But in Shelby County Criminal Court, on February 1, 2024, the teen killer pleaded guilty to a lesser charge of second-degree murder and carjacking. He’ll serve 20 years for the murder and eight years for the carjacking.

The Shelby County District Attorney’s Office told Action News 5:

“Throughout this case, our office met with the family of Rev. Autura Eason-Williams on multiple occasions. There were split opinions within the family regarding the prospect of a trial and other factors. Considering the nature of the crime, public safety, and the family’s views, we determined that a suitable resolution was to meet in the middle with a sentence of 28 years. In contrast to the legal provision allowing juveniles the chance of release after serving 25 years, Miguel Andrade must serve the entire 28-year sentence without the opportunity for parole.”

Shelby County District Attorney's Office

As for the other two suspects in the murder case, 17-year-old Brayan Carrillo is already serving time in juvenile custody and will be released when he’s 19.

A third suspect, 22-year-old Eduard Tabora Rodriguez, remains locked up in the Shelby County Jail at 201 Poplar on a $1 million bond. His next court date is February 26.
