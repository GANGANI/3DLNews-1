Flight diverted to Amarillo; Passengers arrested after mid-air altercation

by ABC 7 Amarillo

Tue, September 1st 2015 at 12:25 AM
Updated Tue, September 1st 2015 at 5:24 PM
8
VIEW ALL PHOTOS
Passengers detained from diverted Southwest flight grounded in Amarillo. (ABC 7 News/Kayla Conboy)

CONTINUING COVERAGE

AMARILLO, Texas -- A San Diego flight headed to Chicago was diverted to Amarillo after what authorities called a mid-air altercation.

Southwest Airlines flight 1522 landed at Rick Husband Amarillo International Airport at about 10:40 p.m., Monday.

According to Amarillo police, a total of six men were placed in custody. They were identified as Saiman Hermez, Jonathan Khalid Petras, Ghazwan Asaad Shaba, Essa Solaqa, Khalid Yohana and Wisam Imad Shaker. They were being held at the Randall County Jail on federal holds on charges of Interference with a Flight Crew and Aiding and Abetting listed.

READ MORE: Click here to read the criminal complaint.

Police said passengers on board reported feeling "uncomfortable" with the way the men were acting toward crew members while the flight was in the air. Police said a reported verbal altercation then happened on board the plane.

"Obviously several passengers felt like they were out of line very vocal, very obnoxious acting, " said Cpl. Jerry Neufeld, Spokesperson for Amarillo police. "Were they a disruption to the flight? Again that's going to be up to the FBI to determine."

According to the affidavit, the men were accused of initially not putting up their seat backs and tray tables up during the emergency demonstration. The affidavit said the men were not allowed to order alcoholic drinks and the men were acting disorderly.

Police said no weapons were involved and no injuries were reported. Other passengers on board were questioned by law enforcement. FBI was on scene.

Original scanner conversation was recorded from the ABC 7 Scanner.

"463 airport security is advising they had 10 people fighting on an airplane," the dispatcher said. "Therefore the plane is now being diverted to Amarillo they are requesting officers to assist."

Flight 1522 was able to resume its flight several hours later. It landed at Chicago Midway International Airport at 3:22 a.m.

People took to social media explaining the situation.


Southwest Airlines released a statement to ABC 7 News explaining the situation.

"Southwest flight #1522from San Diego to Chicago's Midway International Airport made an unscheduledstop at Rick Husband Amarillo International Airport last night after severalpassengers traveling together became disorderly and, subsequently, refused tofollow instructions from the crew," the statement read. "Out of an abundance ofcaution, the pilots in command of the flight diverted to Amarillo where lawenforcement officials met the aircraft and detained six individuals."

Police along with Southwest Airlines said that in addition to the six men being detained, six others chose to deplane and remain in Amarillo.

This is not the first time in recent years Amarillo has been in the spotlight because of a flight being diverted.

It was March 2012 when a Jet Blue flight made an emergency landed in Amarillo following the captain running down the aisles screaming.
