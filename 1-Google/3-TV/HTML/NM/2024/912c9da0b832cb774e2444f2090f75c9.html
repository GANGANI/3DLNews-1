Convicted killer who escaped Pennsylvania prison appears in court

by JESSICA A. BOTELHO | The National Desk

Fri, February 2nd 2024 at 1:42 PM
Updated Fri, February 2nd 2024 at 1:43 PM
15
VIEW ALL PHOTOS
FILE - Danilo Souza Cavalcante is taken into custody at the Pennsylvania State Police barracks at Avondale Pa., on Wednesday, Sept. 13, 2023. (Pennsylvania State Police via AP)

A convicted killer who escaped a Pennsylvania prison last summer and was on the run for two weeks was in court Friday.

Danilo Cavalcante appeared before a judge for a preliminary hearing on escape charges, according to court records.

Footage from KYW shows authorities escorting Cavalcante to inside Magisterial District Court. He is wearing an orange prison jumpsuit, and his long, curly hair covers his face.

He looks down as he makes his way to the building and doesn't speak when reporters ask him questions.

"Will you escape again?" one reporter shouts at him.

NBC 10 Philadelphia reported that residents who said they saw Cavalcante on their property or in their homes while he was at large took the witness stand.

"One homeowner said he learned weeks after his interaction with Cavalcante that the escapee stole his daughter's sleeping bag from his home," according to the media outlet. "Another homeowner said he fired four shots at Cavalcante after the escapee stole a gun from his garage. A translator was on hand speaking to Cavalcante -- who speaks Portuguese."

Cavalcante made his way out of the Chester County Prison by crab walking between two walls on August 31. Video shows his escape.

Cavalcante eluded police for 14 days, surviving on stolen watermelons and stream water. He told investigators he hid his feces under leaves and brush to evade them.

He was taken into custody on September 13 with help from multiple agencies, including police K-9 Yoda, a 4-year-old Belgian Malinois from the El Paso Border Patrol Tactical Team.

The police dog sniffed Cavalcante out then chased him down as he tried to crawl away from authorities.

Pennsylvania State Police Lt. George Bivens said the police dog bit Cavalcante, with the convict suffering a minor bite wound to his head. An image showed blood covering Cavalcante's face as authorities led him away from the scene.

Since his escape, the Chester County jail board unanimously approved plans to fully enclose the exercise yards at the facility. The project is expected to cost $2.5 million to $3.5 million and could take as long as nine months to complete.

The redesign plan calls for fully enclosed exercise yards, with 18-foot-high masonry walls to replace fencing, and the removal of shed roofs that could inmates could scale. The plan also calls for removing basketball hoops.

Plus, inmate uniforms will soon be brightly colored so they are easier to spot in an escape attempt.

Cavalcante was convicted of killing his ex-girlfriend, Deborah Brandao, in front of her children in 2021. At the time of his escape, he was awaiting transfer to a state prison to serve a life sentence.
