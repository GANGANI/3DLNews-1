Woman accused of dropping her pants, threatening passengers on Frontier flight faces federal charges

By Dorothy Sedovic
Published: Feb. 2, 2024 at 9:09 AM PST

PHILADELPHIA (Gray News) - A woman is facing federal charges for allegedly pulling down her pants, cursing at crew members and threatening passengers on a Frontier flight.

Dulce Huertas, 60, was arrested in connection to her alleged actions on a Frontier Airlines flight traveling from Orlando, Florida,. to Philadelphia, according to the U.S. Attorney’s Office, Eastern District of Pennsylvania.

Several Philadelphia news outlets reported that on Nov. 20, 2023, Flight 4944 was preparing to land when Huertas allegedly got out of her seat and said she needed to go to the bathroom. According to a criminal complaint obtained by CBS Philadelphia, she was served two alcoholic drinks during the flight.

Huertas is accused of yelling and cursing at flight attendants as they told her to remain seated. According to CBS Philadelphia, security was called as flight crew members said Huertas’s behavior became more aggressive after the plane landed and made its way to the gate.

Once it arrived at the gate, Huertas allegedly started to push passengers out of the way as she made her way to the bathroom at the front plane, WPVI reported. She was stopped by flight attendants who were disarming the cabin door.

It was then that Huertas allegedly pulled down her pants as if she was to urinate but then pulled her clothes back up, according to CBS Philadelphia.

Huertas is also accused of using her belly to allegedly bump a crew member who was guarding the exterior door, WPVI reported. When that proved unsuccessful, she allegedly continued to curse and threatened to kill several passengers.

Huertas has been charged with interference with flight crew members and attendants, simple assault and indecent exposure, according to the U.S. Attorney’s Office, Eastern District of Pennsylvania.

The case is being investigated by the FBI and the Federal Air Marshal Service, with assistance from the Philadelphia Police Department.

If convicted, Huertas could face a maximum possible sentence of 21 years and three months in prison and a $355,000 fine.
