Sutton man dies from exposure after house burns, authorities say

By Joey Klecka
Published: Jan. 31, 2024 at 12:12 PM AKST

ANCHORAGE, Alaska (KTUU) - Investigators believe a Sutton man died from exposure amid frigid temperatures after a burning home Tuesday night north of Palmer.

Alaska State Troopers wrote in a dispatch that 59-year-old Kevin Vance was found dead near a residential structure on Prospector Circle in Sutton

Fire crews responded to the blaze shortly after 11 p.m. Tuesday, but troopers wrote that it was hours later — around 4 a.m. — when fire crews discovered Vance’s body near the home.

Troopers said that Vance had died after he escaped the fire. Investigators said they believe Vance died from exposure.

A trooper spokesperson said that includes death from cold temperatures — National Weather Service observations from that area reported temperatures in the single digits below zero at the time — but added his cause of death will be determined by the state medical examiner.

Officials wrote that no foul play is suspected of the fire.
