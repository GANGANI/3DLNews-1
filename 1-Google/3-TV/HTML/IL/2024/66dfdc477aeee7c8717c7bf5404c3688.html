Downtown Rockford Reboots: Businesses Reflect on Challenges, Newcomers Emerge

ROCKFORD, Ill. — With the new year in full swing, Downtown Rockford is welcoming new businesses in 2024. With multiple business owners, current and previous, sharing common trends they've seen for Downtown Businesses. 

STREAMING: 13 WREX is LIVE on your schedule. When you want... Where you want.

    "I feel like with the big builds, especially that the city is investing money into, to me that says they want downtown to be great," said Amie McDonald, the owner of Elora Home Interiors, who's business just reached its one year anniversary in Downtown Rockford. 

    "If they didn't, there would be no money being poured out down here."

    Some business owners, like Justin Carner, owner of Velvet Robot Coffee, have closed their business or relocated for a variety of reasons, including safety, low foot - traffic, or perceived lack of support from the City of Rockford. 

    "Whether it be parking or safety," said Carner. 

    "So we saw this influx of change happening to Rockford... we saw everyone, like you said, moving out."

      News

      Downtown Rockford coffee shop moves to Roscoe

      • Dillon Valencia

      Velvet Robot Coffee has two locations, one on Alpine Rd. and their Downtown Rockford location, which closed at the end of 2023 to move to Downtown Roscoe.

      "It just kind of felt like it was our time to kind of just disconnect from Downtown Rockford and then push out to Roscoe," said Carner. 

       "In my three years of being downtown, I haven't seen Rockford do anything for any small business personally." 

      The City of Rockford has implemented services to help support businesses through the River District.

        "It's certainly always sad to see any business leave," said Rockford Mayor, Tom McNamara. 

        "With that being said, you can name one or two that have left for a neighboring community... we have 16 new businesses in downtown."

        McDonald has seen success with her business in Downtown however, accrediting City Market Days in the summer, or Stroll on State giving her more foot traffic. 

        "I would say the most amount of like general foot traffic that we have received is during City Market... that was amazing being on this main strip," said McDonald. 

        "I do feel like they asked, "What can we do to help you guys down here thrive?"... I appreciate that. I know that there's been a couple of things that have happened to try to promote people coming downtown... to promote more foot traffic where you can park, grab a drink, walk and shop around." 

        Other business owners have had issues with safety. AJ Goff, the owner of Subversive Cycling Company, recently had to change his store hours to appointment only after having multiple issues with violence. Goff shared with 13 WREX that the thought of closing or relocating has crossed his mind because of his issue with theft. 

        "We want to be there to support them. Look at the last couple of years, it's been a really difficult time for local retail and restaurants... we need to think about COVID," said McNamara.  

        "We're not perfect. We always got to improve. We're really fortunate the River District, which is the Business Association for downtown has really come back to life after being dormant during COVID."
