Know Your Candidates 2022: Bridget Barton (R), running for Oregon governor

by KATU Staff

Wed, April 20th 2022 at 4:59 PM
Bridget Barton is a Republican running for Oregon governor.

Throughout the 2022 election, KATU News is helping you “Know Your Candidates” with a series of interviews with people who will appear on your ballot.

We are asking local candidates running for the same office the same series of questions, so you can compare their answers.

Candidates for Oregon governor were given questionnaires to answer.

Read the responses from Bridget Barton, a Republican, below.

IN THREE OR FOUR SENTENCES, TELL US ABOUT YOURSELF.

I'm Bridget Barton, a Republican outsider for governor--I'm not a politician and have never held political office. I'm a small business owner, a conservative writer and publisher, with 30 years of experience in media, publishing, and research, advocating for conservative solutions to big problems in Oregon like school performance and choice, business regulations and natural resources. I started a successful conservative business magazine in the late 1990s. I have been married to my husband Curtis for 40 years and helped put our two (now adult) children through college by breaking horses.

WHY ARE YOU RUNNING?

I’m running to put a hard stop to our state’s 40-year decline, which in the past 10 years has become a near death spiral of wildfires, homelessness, crime, higher prices and more. Our state is unrecognizable after the last 10 years. These problems have reached a crisis level for all Oregonians regardless of their political party. Oregonians are looking for a candidate like me with no interest in building a political career, with nothing to lose, who will fight fearlessly and relentlessly for them, to solve actual problems not grasp at political power.

WHAT MAKES YOU UNIQUELY QUALIFIED TO BE GOVERNOR?

I come to the race as an outsider —with no political baggage. I don't owe anyone anything, so I can do anything necessary to solve our massive "kitchen table" issues, such as our dismal education quality and results, soaring crime and substance abuse rates, constantly rising prices and taxes on working families. With 30 years of experience as a writer, researcher, and advocate on these exact issues, I’m ready to fight for Oregonians.

WHAT IS THE NUMBER ONE ISSUE FACING OREGONIANS AND WHAT WILL YOU DO, AS GOVERNOR, TO ADDRESS IT?

The deeply intertwined issues of crime, substance abuse, and homelessness are most pressing. Despite record spending, the problem is growing and spreading across the state. I've been in recovery from alcoholism for 40 years. What we're doing now is just enabling addiction and homelessness; it's not compassionate. We're allowing people to commit slow suicide on our streets. Worse, it's the root cause of rising crime rates. Oregon desperately needs a leader like me with practical life expertise, experience and backbone to address these crises.

I address solutions to these problems in the questions below.

HOW WOULD YOU BRIDGE THE PARTISAN DIVIDE IN OREGON?

I’ve never been afraid to speak up for rural and blue-collar communities. The conservative business magazine I published consistently exposed the disdain that elitist urban politicians often demonstrate for rural Oregon's hardworking families and businesses. I’m an outsider and that means everything is on the table. I don’t owe anyone anything, and that gives me the freedom to set a new vision for our state that values every Oregonian. To execute this vision, I will more heavily weight state-level economic and business agencies, boards and commissions with strong rural voices -- balance must be restored immediately at the state policy level. But I’m relentlessly optimistic and whether I’m in Crook County or downtown Portland, Oregonians want many of the same things: they want their schools and their businesses back, they want their lives and their freedom back. This 40-year hole that the radical left has created, and the crime and chaos all Oregonians see around them, is actually uniting people across our beautiful state.

AS GOVERNOR WHAT WOULD YOU DO DIFFERENTLY ABOUT THE HOMELESS CRISIS IN THE PORTLAND METRO AREA?

Stop enabling addiction and begin work toward true compassion by getting people off the streets. Solving crime, homelessness and substance abuse is complex, but here are two key pieces:

I would turn the "Housing First" model around completely. Next, I would design a statewide shelter database so we are able to clear the streets legally, create large low-barrier shelters, and reallocate most "housing" funds to substance abuse and mental health treatment.

I’d gather law enforcement, treatment providers, and parent groups across the state to refer Measure 110, which legalized hard drugs, back to voters for repeal. This policy draws cartels, crime, drug users and homeless people to our state, and it's killing our kids through increasingly common fentanyl overdoses.

WHAT IS THE BIGGEST DRIVER OF HOMELESSNESS STATE-WIDE AND HOW WOULD YOU ADDRESS IT?

Our current politicians have utterly failed us by their unwillingness to address root causes. We have a substance abuse problem, not a housing problem. Oregon is #2 in the country in substance abuse per capita, and #50 in the country in treatment beds per capita. After billions of dollars in spending, the problem is worse, not better because it has been approached backwards. Funds must be immediately reallocated toward low-barrier shelters to clear our streets, and to treatment options for substance abuse and mental health. I would also firmly stand with law enforcement to ensure they have the resources they need to keep our streets clean and safe.

AS GOVERNOR, WHAT WOULD YOU DO DIFFERENTLY TO ADDRESS GUN VIOLENCE IN THE PORTLAND METRO AREA?

We don’t just have a gun problem in Portland (and quite frankly, it’s not just Portland but throughout the state in Eugene, Bend, Salem, etc.), we have a criminal behavior problem. To solve this, I would take the same approach to gun violence the way every other kind of crime is addressed: increased attention on the problem through focused policing, ideally by officers who have specialized training.

In Portland, we have increased gang violence combined with street violence in the form of random attacks. As these attacks are allowed to continue, the more people feel they need to carry a weapon, often a gun, to protect themselves, which leads to more shootings. This is happening among the homeless as we allow them to do whatever they want on our streets, while we enable their addiction and make it easy for them to access drugs and weapons. Now that the violence is out of control, the only way to address it is to increase the number of officers in Portland and have a Mayor, Police Chief and District Attorney who are actually tough on crime and hold criminals accountable.

We also need increased funding for police in order to attract more officers to Portland and retain the ones already here. Bonuses and salary would have to at least match what surrounding agencies are offering, and we need to be able to attract and retain law enforcement talent through a good quality of life with safe neighborhoods and good schools. As long as Antifa holds city residents and politicians hostage, running the city through intimidation, the police will never have enough officers to adequately address the crime problems— all of them, including gun violence. A good example to follow would be what Giuliani and Kerik were able to do in NYC in the 1990s when they completely turned that city around.

WHAT DO YOU BELIEVE IS THE BIGGEST FACTOR DRIVING THE RECENT SPIKE IN GUN VIOLENCE IN PORTLAND?

There has been a clear cause and effect with recently adopted leftist policies that have led to this spike in gun violence. The Portland City Council chose to defund the Gun Violence Reduction Team. Then shootings increased. George Soros and the New York-based Drug Policy Alliance tricked Oregon voters into approving Measure 110, which legalized hard drugs in Oregon and created a massive new market for drug users and cartels. Then shootings increased. Multnomah County voters elected a pro-criminal district attorney in 2020. Then shootings increased. Oregonians have unknowingly created a perfect storm for crime and accompanying violence of all forms. It’s easy to commit violent crimes if no one is watching you, no one is holding you accountable for crimes and you can make easy money in a new high-stakes hard drug market.

HOW WOULD YOU, AS GOVERNOR, HELP OREGONIANS DEAL WITH INFLATION?

Financial insecurity is scary. When my husband and I were young parents it was sometimes hard to make ends meet. I’ll work every day to protect you from those nagging fears caused by rising prices in your everyday life. As a political outsider, I’m not afraid to veto any bill that crosses my desk that increases costs for our families for basic things we buy—whether that’s gas, food, medicine, insurance, medical supplies and the list goes on. We can’t control national price increases but we can put a hard stop to new laws that make life more expensive for all of us.

HOW WOULD YOU HELP OREGONIANS WITH HIGHER GAS PRICES?

I’m an outsider and I won’t fold like the career politicians, including some Republicans, who have taken active steps to pass laws here in Oregon to make us more dependent on foreign oil. There are two changes I would enact immediately to provide relief at the pump: Pause the Oregon gas tax until the price of gas drops below $3/gallon and stop the “Green Gasoline” state program that increases the price of every gallon of gas sold in Oregon.

DO YOU SUPPORT MAKING THE HEAD OF OREGON SCHOOLS AN ELECTED POSITION?

I started my career as a conservative writer and advocate as a young mom who wanted school choices for our kids. I championed charter schools and worked to pass a law to allow them to open in Oregon. I have and will always support school choices for parents and families and that also includes who serves as their schools chief.

WHAT IS THE BIGGEST LESSON FROM THE CORONAVIRUS PANDEMIC?

After two years of deafening silence from career politicians who were too weak to challenge Kate Brown on her unconstitutional and endless emergency orders, the biggest lesson the pandemic showed me was that whoever we elect as our next governor must be an outsider who isn’t building a political career and will do what’s right for our people instead of what’s politically popular.

PEOPLE IN SEVERAL OREGON COUNTIES HAVE VOTED TO LEAVE THIS STATE AND JOIN IDAHO. IT’S UNLIKELY TO HAPPEN, BUT WHAT WOULD YOU DO, AS GOVERNOR, TO MAKE THEM WANT TO STAY IN OREGON?

My fellow Oregonians in the rural counties who have voted to secede and join Idaho are rightfully angry at the urban, leftist policies that have hung them out to dry. When I’m governor, I’ll put a hard stop to policies that penalize our rural and blue-collar communities. When I’m governor, rural Oregonians will finally have a Governor who’s in their corner.

WHO IS YOUR HERO, AND WHY?

When Kate Brown first closed Oregon schools during the pandemic, the first thing I thought about was the working parents with young kids in school. Those working parents are my heroes. Oregon families and kids suffered and these parents strung together bringing home a paycheck, caring for their children during a pandemic, home schooling their kids on screens and trying to stay sane at the same time. As the next Governor of Oregon, I won’t forget the unsung sacrifices the parents of young children made during Kate Brown’s needless and ineffective pandemic restrictions and promise them that they will never experience this on my watch.
