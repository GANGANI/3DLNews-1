Know Your Candidates 2022: John Sweeney (D), running for Oregon governor

by KATU Staff

Wed, April 20th 2022 at 4:13 PM
Updated Wed, April 20th 2022 at 4:47 PM
John Sweeney is a Democrat running for Oregon governor.

Throughout the 2022 election, KATU News is helping you “Know Your Candidates” with a series of interviews with people who will appear on your ballot.

We are asking local candidates running for the same office the same series of questions, so you can compare their answers.

Candidates for Oregon governor were given questionnaires to answer.

Read the responses from John Sweeney, a Democrat, below.

(blank)

WHY ARE YOU RUNNING?

Why amI running?

1. To improve our education system; I want to reduce the dropout rate to zero. We must get our students to graduate from high school.

Because, those who do not finish high school, in many cases end up as economic cripples. Failing in life.

2. Clean up our air.. There are many big things in the works. But,most governments and people own newer cars and trucks that are flex-fuel vehicles. Those can be shifted to using E-85 gas, a much cleaner fuel. Thus cleaning the air.

3. Stop the shootings. It may mean calling in the state police &/or the National Guard.

WHAT MAKES YOU UNIQUELY QUALIFIED TO BE GOVERNOR?

(blank)

WHAT IS THE NUMBER ONE ISSUE FACING OREGONIANS AND WHAT WILL YOU DO, AS GOVERNOR, TO ADDRESS IT?

The number one issue is education. Our students are falling behind. I want to build into the students the goal of finishing high school. In the 7th grade they would have 4, 9 week courses; From a life plan; civics; money management (to instill saving & investing); law related education (when I was on the Multnomah ESD the managers said, "it was effective, very effective")

HOW WOULD YOU BRIDGE THE PARTISAN DIVIDE IN OREGON?

(blank)

AS GOVERNOR WHAT WOULD YOU DO DIFFERENTLY ABOUT THE HOMELESS CRISIS IN THE PORTLAND METRO AREA?

Homeless/houseless. This is a local problem; the cities/counties must make their plans. And,the state will aid them in some areas when possible.

WHAT IS THE BIGGEST DRIVER OF HOMELESSNESS STATE-WIDE AND HOW WOULD YOU ADDRESS IT?

The driver of the homeless'houseless problem. The more you do, the more peo[le will come for the benefits.

AS GOVERNOR, WHAT WOULD YOU DO DIFFERENTLY TO ADDRESS GUN VIOLENCE IN THE PORTLAND METRO AREA?

What to do about gun violence? There are 2 types;street shootings and the reaction to bullying.

Street shootings are happening in poor areas & are shoot and scoot affairs. Where people do not have guns to fight back. The bullying needs a listing of the bukkys to shame them. And, if that does not work. Then arrest them.

WHAT DO YOU BELIEVE IS THE BIGGEST FACTOR DRIVING THE RECENT SPIKE IN GUN VIOLENCE IN PORTLAND?

The big factor in the shootings. Lack of police. It is past time to rebuild the police.

HOW WOULD YOU, AS GOVERNOR, HELP OREGONIANS DEAL WITH INFLATION?

Inflation, education. Is the goods & services worth the money, then buy. If not, do not buy. ;

HOW WOULD YOU HELP OREGONIANS WITH HIGHER GAS PRICES?

(blank)

DO YOU SUPPORT MAKING THE HEAD OF OREGON SCHOOLS AN ELECTED POSITION?

The head of the Oregon schools was elected. And, now it is not. It is a toss up.

WHAT IS THE BIGGEST LESSON FROM THE CORONAVIRUS PANDEMIC?

The next pandemic; do not shut down the economy. And, use better health programs for controling it.

PEOPLE IN SEVERAL OREGON COUNTIES HAVE VOTED TO LEAVE THIS STATE AND JOIN IDAHO. IT’S UNLIKELY TO HAPPEN, BUT WHAT WOULD YOU DO, AS GOVERNOR, TO MAKE THEM WANT TO STAY IN OREGON?

Greater Idaho? I think it will die off. As they just said they were going to end some property taxes and raise their sales tax to the highest in the nation.

WHO IS YOUR HERO, AND WHY?

My Hero? My Dd.

Robert Fisher Sweeney

Sgt 1st Class/US Army/Retired

1915-2004

He showed me around the woods of the Oregon Coast. Where he served in the Civilian Conservation Corps before WW2. Which included the fine points of the woods. He showed me how to ride a bike, shoot a gun, fix and drive a car. We talked about the Army, which helped me when I joined the Army National Guard. I retired as a Captain.
