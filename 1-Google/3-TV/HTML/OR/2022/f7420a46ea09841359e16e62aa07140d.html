Know Your Candidates 2022: Nick Hess (R), running for Oregon governor

by KATU Staff

Thu, April 21st 2022 at 5:02 PM
Nick Hess is a Republican running for Oregon governor.

Throughout the 2022 election, KATU News is helping you “Know Your Candidates” with a series of interviews with people who will appear on your ballot.

We are asking local candidates running for the same office the same series of questions, so you can compare their answers.

Candidates for Oregon governor were given questionnaires to answer.

Read the responses from Nick Hess, a Republican, below.

IN THREE OR FOUR SENTENCES, TELL US ABOUT YOURSELF.

I'm Nick Hess and I'm running to be the Republican candidate for Governor. I am a lifelong Oregonian and I am CEO and entrepreneur of two Portland-based high-tech companies that specialize in cybersecurity and telecommunications. I am a cybersecurity expert and I look to bring my 21+ years of experience in business and technology to the role of governor.

WHY ARE YOU RUNNING?

Oregon politicians keep rehashing the same failing ideas. It’s time for a new, fresh perspective. Our state needs a leader that can reach across the aisle, find common ground, and bridges the divisive rhetoric to ultimately get things done. Everywhere I turn, the people of Oregon aren’t winning yet the same politicians keep getting elected. As governor of Oregon, this is my goal: to get results for the people of Oregon and to ensure they have every opportunity to be successful in their own lives, whatever that looks like to them.

WHAT MAKES YOU UNIQUELY QUALIFIED TO BE GOVERNOR?

Building, owning, and operating my own businesses for the last 21+ years has taught me the skills needed to be an effective leader. And Oregon is in desperate need of good leadership.

That is where a business leader can help--a CEO gets things done. We make deals every day, inspire our employees, and keep things moving. The primary job of a CEO is making sure your employees and customers are successful. We live by the motto of: your success is my success. But everywhere I turn, the people of Oregon aren’t winning yet the same politicians keep getting elected. As governor of Oregon, this is my goal: to get results for the people of Oregon and to ensure they have every opportunity to create their own success.

To get things done, the government needs to embrace technology like the private sector. With my experience in technology, I want to improve transparency between the government and its citizens. I want to make state data transparent. That includes election, healthcare, education, and budgets 100% available and information easily accessible by every Oregonian.

WHAT IS THE NUMBER ONE ISSUE FACING OREGONIANS AND WHAT WILL YOU DO, AS GOVERNOR, TO ADDRESS IT?

Homelessness is the biggest and most complex challenge facing Oregon today. There are many factors that contribute to homelessness including drugs and alcohol, the rising cost of housing, and unexpected life events. Each piece must be addressed before we see a dramatic change. Clear metrics for success must be established before money is handed out.

In Oregon, we have amazing non-profits doing critical work across the state but we need more. More state-level grants need to be available for private non-profits with proven models of success. Additionally, we must leverage technology and track available beds in real-time with web-based applications similar to Hotels.com. Currently, in 2022, that information is still being tracked by spreadsheets and phone calls. We can do better. For my full homelessness plan, please visit votehess.com.

HOW WOULD YOU BRIDGE THE PARTISAN DIVIDE IN OREGON?

To bridge the partisan divide we need balanced leadership. Electing a republican governor would help us get there. The reality is that throughout history, one-sided leadership is unsustainable and leads to mistrust among constituents. In Oregon, we have not seen republican leadership in the office of governor for 36 years.

To create that balance of power, I will do away with executive orders. Executive orders are an abuse of power. As governor, I will push for and sign bi-partisan bills and veto anything that gives preference to one party or political power. The sole purpose of the governor is to serve and represent the people of Oregon, not political parties.

AS GOVERNOR, WHAT WOULD YOU DO DIFFERENTLY ABOUT THE HOMELESS CRISIS IN THE PORTLAND METRO AREA?

In the Portland Metro area, we need to identify and support solutions that are showing proven results. Most of those organizations are private non-profits like Rogue Retreat in Medford, Blanchet House and Bybee Lakes in Portland. These programs are getting people off the streets and into long-term housing with jobs. And they are doing so on a fraction of what the city of Portland is paying.

We must also leverage technology to help solve this crisis. Besides tracking available shelter beds using web-based applications, we need to rebuild the Homeless Management Information System (HMIS). HMIS currently collects client-level data and information on housing, shelter, and homeless services. It is outdated, complex, and wasteful. Many organizations have 3 or 4 employees strictly adding data into this system. It is a huge waste of resources.

WHAT IS THE BIGGEST DRIVER OF HOMELESSNESS STATE-WIDE AND HOW WOULD YOU ADDRESS IT?

Substance abuse and access to care are two of the biggest drivers of homelessness in Oregon. We need to add more mental health and drug rehab facilities across the state. To do this, we need to change or abolish Oregon Health Authority's certificate of need which places restrictions on adding new hospital beds and mental health facilities.

AS GOVERNOR, WHAT WOULD YOU DO DIFFERENTLY TO ADDRESS GUN VIOLENCE IN THE PORTLAND METRO AREA?

I support a zero tolerance policy for all violent crimes and advocate for their prosecution. If DAs choose not to prosecute these crimes, I will campaign for their removal and subsequent replacement. We must ensure adequate funding at the state-level for our police force and to make sure new police officers can be trained quickly. Currently it can take up to two years to get police offers through the academy and on to the streets. Each officer in the state must be equipped with high-frame body cameras and we must also increase community engagement.

WHAT DO YOU BELIEVE IS THE BIGGEST FACTOR DRIVING THE RECENT SPIKE IN GUN VIOLENCE IN PORTLAND?

I believe lack of leadership is the biggest contributing factor to the spike in gun violence in Portland. There is a lack of police presence in the city of Portland that has stemmed from poor leadership coming from the mayor and the Portland city council. For the last two years there has been no push to prosecute violent crimes. Portland has become–in every sense of the term–a lawless city.

HOW WOULD YOU, AS GOVERNOR, HELP OREGONIANS DEAL WITH INFLATION?

To combat inflation, we must better utilize our tax dollars and put more money back into Oregonian’s pockets. We can help offset the burden of inflation by attracting more high-paying jobs and bolstering our local economy.

HOW WOULD YOU HELP OREGONIANS WITH HIGHER GAS PRICES?

To minimize the short-term pinch of high gas prices, the state should suspend the gas tax or allow drivers to pump their own gas.

DO YOU SUPPORT MAKING THE HEAD OF OREGON SCHOOLS AN ELECTED POSITION?

Yes. By creating a seperate elected position, you minimize the amount of power the office of governor holds in regards to education.

WHAT IS THE BIGGEST LESSON FROM THE CORONAVIRUS PANDEMIC?

We need more hospital beds. To do this, we must again remove or change Oregon Health Authority’s certificate of need.

PEOPLE IN SEVERAL OREGON COUNTIES HAVE VOTED TO LEAVE THIS STATE AND JOIN IDAHO. IT’S UNLIKELY TO HAPPEN, BUT WHAT WOULD YOU DO, AS GOVERNOR, TO MAKE THEM WANT TO STAY IN OREGON?

Again, it all comes back to leadership. Our rural communities have been ignored for the last 36 years that the democrats have been in power in Oregon. As governor, I will establish a satellite office in Eastern Oregon and visit every county across the state multiple times per year. Oregon leadership needs to listen to the needs of the people. Not dictate their needs to them.

WHO IS YOUR HERO, AND WHY?

Vic Atiyeh. He did what was right for Oregon, not what was right for any one political party.
