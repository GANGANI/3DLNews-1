9 people shot in mass shooting in Rhode Island

by WJAR Staff

PROVIDENCE, R.I. (WJAR) — Providence police said nine people were shot in a mass shooting Thursday.

The shooting happened just before 7 p.m. on Carolina Avenue.

In a press briefing Thursday night, Col. Hugh Clements said an assailant pulled up in a car and began firing toward a home on that street. People at that home returned fire, he added.

The chief said four or five different guns were involved.

Both groups know each other, and this is an ongoing feud, Clements said, adding that there's no threat to the public.

Mayor Jorge Elorza said there will be extra police patrols in the area Thursday night.

Clements said those who were shot at took themselves to the hospital. One of them was taken by ambulance.

Police said three people suffered serious injuries, while the others suffered minor injuries.

The chief said he believes this is the most victims they've ever had in a single shooting.

Police will be releasing more information about the shooting Friday at a press conference.
