Crews prepare for possible thunderstorm, wind as they work to contain S-503 fire

by KATU Staff

Tue, June 22nd 2021 at 11:10 AM
Aerial photos of the S-503 fire. (Courtesy: CTWS - Fire Management)

    TOPICS:

    Oregon,United StatesNull

WARM SPRINGS, Ore. — More than 250 personnel are working on fighting the S-503 fire on the Warm Springs Reservation, officials say.

The wildfire has burned 6,201 acres since the fire broke out on June 18 and is 10% contained.

PAST COVERAGE | S-503 Wildfire on Warm Springs Reservation grows to 6,200 acres, 10% contained

On Tuesday, crews are expected to work on strengthening lines on the fire’s northern and eastern flanks, including securing the heel of the fire in the northwestern corner of the fire perimeter. On the southern and western flanks of the fire, crews are constructing direct fire line where feasible while securing and holding existing containment line.

Crews are preparing for wind in the afternoon and possible thunderstorms in the area.

RELATED | Oregon fire officials warn of potential fire danger for this week, summer

The Wasco County Sherriff’s Office has lowered all homes west of Kelly Springs Rd/Back Walters Rd from Level 3 evacuation to Level 2 - Get Set.

Anything east of Kelly Springs to Reservation Rd, including Walters Corners and the community of Pine Grove, remain at Level 2 - Get Set, deputies say.

Officials say a temporary flight restriction has been placed over the fire area.
