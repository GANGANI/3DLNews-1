McDonald's to roll out burger changes at all U.S. locations

by WKRC

Mon, January 8th 2024 at 8:06 PM
Updated Tue, January 9th 2024 at 12:23 PM
(Photo by Joe Raedle/Getty Images)

UNDATED (WKRC) - One of the world's most recognizable foods might soon look and taste a little different.

The Wall Street Journal reports McDonald's announced in April that it would be making multiple changes to its burgers.

The changes were first introduced at the company's West Coast locations in 2023, prompting strong, mixed reactions online.

Officials recently revealed the company is rolling out the changes to all of its U.S. locations in early 2024.

The fast food giant says the main goal is to make them juicer, and overall create the “best burgers ever.” The changes include:

  • more sauce on Big Macs
  • meltier cheese
  • fresher lettuce and pickles
  • six patties will be grilled at a time
  • brioche buns will be sliced with a thicker bottom
  • onions will be rehydrated after purchase
  • sesame seeds will be further spaced out

"We can do it quick, fast and safe, but it doesn’t necessarily taste great," said Chris Young, McDonald’s senior director of global menu strategy. "So, we want to incorporate quality into where we’re at."
