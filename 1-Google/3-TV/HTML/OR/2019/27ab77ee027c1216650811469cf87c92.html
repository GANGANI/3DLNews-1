Resources

  • CAHOOTS (Crisis Assistance Helping Out On The Streets): Provides mobile crisis intervention 24/7; offers mental health and substance abuse services and more.
  • Catholic Community Services: Provides services to those in need, including help with food, clothing and emergencies as well as housing and more.
  • Community Outreach Response Team: Connects people who frequently end up in the system with services to address their most critical needs before they receive additional charges; run by the Eugene Police Department.
  • Dusk to Dawn: Provides homeless with a safe place to sleep and keep their belongings at night; operates between 4:30 p.m. to 7:30 a.m. at three locations; run by St. Vincent de Paul.
  • Egan Warming Center: Opens cold weather shelters on nights when temperatures drop; operated by St. Vincent de Paul.
  • FOOD for Lane County: Provides emergency and non-emergency food in an effort to alleviate hunger.
  • Homes for Good: Helps low-income residents with affordable housing.
  • Looking Glass Community Services: Provides services to teens in crisis and more.
  • Overnight Parking Program: Provides safe and legal places for unhoused people to sleep in their vehicles or in Conestoga huts; city funded; managed by St. Vincent de Paul.
  • Pearl Buck Center: Supports children and adults with intellectual and developmental disabilities.
  • Relief Nursery: Provides family support services.
  • Rest Stops: Allows up to 20 people, age 18 and older, to sleep overnight in tents or Conestoga huts on designated sites.
  • ShelterCare: Offers housing a support services with a focus on those living with mental illness.
  • Siuslaw Outreach Services: Provides support to people in need, including emergency shelter, housing help, homeless prevention assistance and more.
  • St. Vincent de Paul: Offers assistance with food, housing, employment, clothing and more.
  • Womenspace: Assists victims of domestic violence.
