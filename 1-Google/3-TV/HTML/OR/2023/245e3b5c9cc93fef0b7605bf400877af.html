San Francisco cleans up before crucial meeting between Biden & Xi

by SCOTT THUMAN | The National Desk

Tue, November 14th 2023 at 12:32 PM
5
VIEW ALL PHOTOS
A homeless encampment is seen along Leavenworth Street in the Tenderloin district of San Francisco, Wednesday, Nov. 8, 2023. (AP Photo/Eric Risberg)

    TOPICS:

    San FranciscoBidenXi JinpingCrimeHomelessnessMeetingFentanylClean up

SAN FRANCISCO (TND) — The homelessness and crime that has for years plagued San Francisco’s streets is now back in the spotlight as California’s governor admits it’s being cleaned up ahead of Chinese President Xi Jinping's visit for a high-level meeting with President Joe Biden.

“I know folks say, 'Oh they’re just cleaning up this place because all those fancy leaders are coming into town,' and that's true because it’s true, but it’s also true for months and months and months prior to APEC we’ve been having different conversations and we've raised the bar of expectations between the city the county and the state," Gov. Gavin Newsom, D-Calif., said at a news conference.

Yet the crime is so significant that Target, Walgreens and Old Navy are among businesses to close stores permanently and city employees who crack down on illegal street vendors have been offered bulletproof vests or police escorts.

“Why not clean up the place for the residents, but instead they’re cleaning it up for world leaders, why not just show it off to the world,” complained Jenny Chan, who claims she moved away from the city due to crime. “I used to be able to shop downtown and now most of the downtown stores are shut down.”

But as President Biden heads there Tuesday, he faces a lethal concern that’s on display all across the city: the destructive spread of the deadly drug fentanyl, much of it coming from China. Now, there’s a chance for Biden to confront the communist country’s leader more directly.

“It’s critical. It’s important. This is something we’ve talked about and taken very seriously when it comes to fentanyl and getting that off our streets, certainly, we’re certainly focused on making sure we protect Americans and American families so obviously this is an important agenda item the president wants to discuss," White House Press Secretary Karine Jean-Pierre said.

Progress is expected to be made at the meeting between the two leaders. Many are wondering if it could come with an agreement to resume military-to-military communications, which were suspended last year after Nancy Pelosi visited Taiwan, further angering China’s leaders.

Though the meeting comes during tense times between these superpowers including efforts by China to spy on the U.S. Complicating matters further are China’s ties to Iran, especially as the war in the Middle East threatens to spiral even more out of control.
