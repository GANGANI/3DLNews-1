Tragic end to rescue mission: Good Samaritan's body recovered from water after trying to save woman

by SKYLER SHEPHARD | WPEC Staff

Thu, November 23rd 2023 at 2:47 PM
Updated Fri, November 24th 2023 at 7:14 AM

FILE - The body of a man, who jumped into the water to save a woman, was recovered from the Intracoastal Waterway on Thursday afternoon. (WPEC)

    TOPICS:

    RescueWaterIntracoastalWPBPDWomanSamaritanBodyPolice

WEST PALM BEACH, Fla. (WPEC) — The body of a man who jumped into the water to save a woman was recovered from the Intracoastal Waterway on Thursday afternoon.

The West Palm Beach Police Department said just before 7 p.m. Wednesday, officers were called to Currie Park, at 2400 North Flagler Drive, after a woman reportedly jumped into the Intracoastal.

Police say a good Samaritan jumped into the water to rescue her but went missing.

WPBPD said the woman made it out of the water, but the good Samaritan — a man believed to be a man in his 50-60s — did not. The woman was taken to the hospital for psychiatric evaluation.

Multiple law enforcement agencies were contacted to search for the missing man including WPBPD, fire department divers and crews from the U.S. Coast Guard. A Palm Beach County Sheriff's Office helicopter was involved and searched the waters well into the night, but the man was not found.

At 1:06 p.m. on Thursday, police say a 911 caller reported seeing a body near 2800 North Flagler Drive. WPBPD detectives, Crime Scene Unit investigators and the Palm Beach County Medical Examiner's Office responded to the scene. Police believe the body to be the still-unidentified man who jumped into the water the night before, positive identification is still pending.

On November 14, PBSO units responded to the West Palm Beach downtown marina for a man who jumped into Intracoastal.
