Veterinarian saves woman’s life after shark bite, witness says

By Nine News Australia via CNN Newsource
Published: Jan. 30, 2024 at 5:00 AM CST

SYDNEY (Nine News Australia) - An Australian veterinarian who helped a 29-year-old swimmer attacked by a shark saved her life, according to a witness.

Lauren O’Neill, 29, was bitten by a shark while swimming in Sydney Harbor on Monday. Neighbors rushed to help, with a veterinarian applying a tourniquet to help with the bleeding.

“I heard a soft yell for help. … I looked outside and saw Lauren propped up on the ladder external to the harbor pool, trying to climb in, and behind her was her leg, which was limp and all completely open,” said witness Michael Porter.

O’Neill suffered blood loss and injuries to her leg. She is still in the hospital but remains in stable condition.

Porter said the veterinarian saved O’Neill’s life, and the community really came together in this situation.

“Luckily, Fiona the vet was there because she absolutely saved her life. In my opinion, there is no question about it… The community came together in a really lovely way. It was a total emergency situation and completely surreal,” Porter said.

Based on O’Neill’s injuries, it was determined she was bitten by a bull shark, which are known to frequent the waters in the area, according to a government official.
