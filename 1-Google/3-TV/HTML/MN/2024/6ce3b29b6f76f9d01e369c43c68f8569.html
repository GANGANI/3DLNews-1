2-year-old ejected, killed in car crash on interstate

By WKYT News Staff and Debra Worley
Published: Feb. 2, 2024 at 12:36 PM CST

WHITELY COUNTY, Ky. (WKYT/Gray News) – A toddler was ejected from a compact SUV and killed during a crash on the interstate in Kentucky early Thursday morning.

According to the Whitley County coroner, a Ford Edge hit a GEO Tracker on I-75 around 1 a.m. A 2-year-old was thrown from the GEO Tracker and was pronounced dead at the scene.

Authorities did not clarify if the toddler was in a car seat or booster at the time of the crash.

Kentucky State Police officials said the driver of the GEO Tracker, 25-year-old Alexis Brianna L. Flores, was trapped in the vehicle and had to be freed.

She was flown to a hospital in Tennessee to be treated for serious injuries.

Three other children in her vehicle were also taken to a hospital in the area to be treated for non-life-threatening injuries.

According to police, alcohol was a contributing factor in the crash, and the people who were in the Ford Edge are now facing charges.

Police said it’s not yet clear who was actually driving, but 33-year-old Matthew P. Croley of Williamsburg and 22-year-old Jayna Ann Vorwerk of Chandler, Arizona, are both facing charges of murder, assault, wanton endangerment, and operating a motor vehicle under the influence.
