Iowa teen arrested after making threats of violence against multiple school districts

By KCCI
Published: Jan. 31, 2024 at 7:11 AM CST

CORYDON, Iowa (KCCI) - Officials have arrested a male juvenile after he allegedly made threats against two Iowa school districts.

School leaders at Osage in northern Iowa, and Wayne Community in southern Iowa say both went into lockdown early Tuesday morning.

A 14-year-old former student at Osage allegedly posted online that he was planning shootings at the schools. Charges are pending.

Authorities in Wayne County say they were able to act fast because other students brought the post to their attention.

“They did the right thing,” Wayne County Sheriff Keith Davis said. “See something, say something. So they reported that to the school this morning. Which led to a successful outcome and communication is the big thing that’s what we need to know.”

Parents and students in the district say incidents like this are especially scary, given the deadly shooting at Perry High School earlier this month.
