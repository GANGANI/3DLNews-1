Fort Madison prison remains on lockdown after white supremacist assaults

by KTVO News Desk

Fri, October 20th 2017 at 3:44 PM
The Iowa State Penitentiary in Fort Madison remains on lockdown Friday after two assaults by inmates with ties to white supremacist groups (Photo: KTVO)

    TOPICS:

    Iowa Department of CorrectionsIowa State Penitentiary

FORT MADISON, Iowa — The Iowa State Penitentiary in Fort Madison remains on lockdown with no plans to lift it after a week of violence from white supremacists.

The “lockdown/restricted movement” status is still in place after an assault on Saturday and the stabbing of a corrections officer on Wednesday. The Iowa Department of Corrections said in a press release Friday that the incidents are still under investigation, but do not appear to be related. However, both offenders have ties to group affiliated with white supremacy movements.

Prison officials say they are conducting thorough searches of all areas of the facility, and are drafting plans for modifications to Housing Unit One where Wednesday’s assault occurred. Staffing plans are also being evaluated.

Visits continue to be suspended, and offenders are unable to access the phone system during the lockdown.

The Department of Corrections has no timeline for when the lockdown will be lifted.
