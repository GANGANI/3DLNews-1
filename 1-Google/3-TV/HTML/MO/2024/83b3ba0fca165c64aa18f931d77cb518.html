Former Boston police officer pleads guilty to felony assault in connection to Jan. 6 riots

by JESSICA A. BOTELHO | The National Desk

Fri, February 2nd 2024 at 10:34 AM
7

FILE - Rioters at the U.S. Capitol on Jan. 6, 2021, in Washington. (AP Photo/John Minchillo)

BOSTON (TND) — A former Boston police officer pleaded guilty on multiple charges in connection to the U.S. Capitol riots on Jan. 6, 2021, according to prosecutors.

The United States Attorney’s Office for the District of Columbia said 52-year-old Joseph Fisher, of Plymouth, Mass., was accused of the following:

  • Eight counts of assaulting, resisting, or impeding certain officers
  • Obstruction of law enforcement during a civil disorder
  • Entering and remaining in a restricted building or grounds
  • Disorderly and disruptive conduct in a restricted building or grounds
  • Engaging in physical violence in a restricted building or grounds
  • Disorderly conduct in a Capitol building
  • Acts of physical violence the Capitol grounds or building
  • Parading, demonstrating, or picketing in a Capitol building

Court documents note that Fisher entered the U.S. Capitol Building shortly before 2:30 p.m. About 10 minutes later, a fight broke out between a U.S. Capitol Police Officer and other rioters.

"As a U.S. Capitol Police Officer pursued a rioter who had deployed pepper spray, Fisher pushed a chair into the Capitol Police Officer," prosecutors said in a news release. "Fisher then engaged in a physical altercation with the Capitol Police Officer. Shortly afterwards, Fisher exited the U.S. Capitol Building."

The Federal Bureau of Investigation arrested Fisher in March. His sentencing is set for May.

"In the 36 months since Jan. 6, 2021, more than 1,265 individuals have been charged in nearly all 50 states for crimes related to the breach of the U.S. Capitol, including more than 440 individuals charged with assaulting or impeding law enforcement, a felony," according to prosecutors. "The investigation remains ongoing."
