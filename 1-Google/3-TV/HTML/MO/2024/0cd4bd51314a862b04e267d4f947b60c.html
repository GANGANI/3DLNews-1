Brooklyn building collapse results in worker's death despite stop work order

by ALEXX ALTMAN-DEVILBISS | The National Desk

Fri, February 2nd 2024 at 1:01 PM
Updated Fri, February 2nd 2024 at 1:42 PM
New York Fire Department gives update to major response following a building collapse in Brooklyn, New York on Friday, February 2, 2024. (FDNY)

    TOPICS:

    BrooklynBuilding collapseStop work orderWorker's deathFDNYCellarDepartment of BuildingsInvestigation

BROOKLYN (TND) — One person is dead after a floor collapsed at a residential building in Brooklyn Friday after a stop work order was issued, according to the New York City Fire Department (FDNY).

Officials said they received a call around noon reporting a building under construction with workers trapped at 1266 50th Street between 13th and 12th Avenues in the Borough Park section.

Three workers were working in the cellar area of the building and one of them was pinned, officials added. The first floor had collapsed into the cellar area in a "V-shaped collapse,"

The construction worker was declared dead at the scene.

Their identity has not been released and no other victims were reported.

The Department of Buildings Commissioner James Oddo said a stop work order was issued in January.

"The only work that should have been done at this site is to make the site safe," Oddo added. "As you can, that stop work order was violated. There are absolutely no plans submitted to the Department of Buildings. So this fatality absolutely should not have happened."

The Department of Buildings and the NYPD will continue to investigate.
