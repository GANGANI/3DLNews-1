3 die after small plane crashes into Florida mobile home park, setting several homes ablaze

By WWSB staff and Gray News staff
Published: Feb. 1, 2024 at 8:27 PM CST|Updated: Feb. 2, 2024 at 8:54 AM CST

CLEARWATER, Fla. (WWSB/Gray News) - Three people have been confirmed dead after a small plane crashed into a Florida mobile home park on Thursday, catching several units on fire, according to the Clearwater Police Department.

According to an FAA report, one crew member on the plane, and two people on the ground were killed in the crash.

Crews were dispatched just after 7 p.m. to the Bayside Waters community.

The pilot reported an emergency to St. Pete–Clearwater International Airport shortly before the plane went off radar, about 3 miles (5 kilometers) north of a runway, Clearwater Fire Chief Scott Ehlers said. The airport is about 7 miles (11 kilometers) southeast of Clearwater.

“The tower over there was able to get a radio transmission from the pilot that he was having a Mayday, Mayday, Mayday. And the aircraft went off radar about three miles north of the runway which is in this location here,” said Ehlers.

The plane, reportedly a single-piston Beechcraft 35 Bonanza, departed Vero Beach Regional Airport around 6:08 p.m., according to FlightAware. Flight data shows it was scheduled to land at the small Clearwater Air Park.

A 72-year-old Florida man was drifting off to sleep when he heard an “unbelievable roar” and was thrown to the floor covered in insulation and broken glass, narrowly surviving the deadly crash of a small plane that ripped apart his mobile home and destroyed the trailer next door.
