Marshfield, Mo., family grieves after loss of pet dog; neighbor charged with animal abuse

By Reagan Di Trolio
Published: Jan. 31, 2024 at 4:12 AM CST

MARSHFIELD, Mo. (KY3) - A Webster Co. family is grieving the loss of their pet dog, Teddy, after he was stabbed in the head on January 11 by their neighbor. The neighbor’s attorney said the actions were in ‘self-defense.’

According to the Webster Co. Sheriff’s Office, deputies responded to an animal call. The caller, John Edward Pace, 63, told dispatchers his neighbor’s dog Teddy attacked him while he was getting his mail and broke his skin. Investigators say Pace said Teddy then went after his dog before Pace approached Teddy and stabbed him in the skull with a pocket knife. Pace faces a Class A misdemeanor of animal abuse.

Travis Cummings is Teddy’s owner. He said he saw what happened after he arrived home.

“I thought John walked up and just hit him in the top of the head. I didn’t think that he had stabbed him until after it was over with, and he did,” Cummings said. “And then Teddy fell down in the ditch, and I look up, and I’m like, “What did you do?”

Jenifer Smith, Cumming’s wife, said she found out about Teddy’s death when she wasn’t home.

“I hate to pull in my driveway because I just see that happen right there in my ditch. I don’t even want to pull into my driveway,” Smith said.

Smith later checked her home security camera footage and said she saw Pace stab Teddy. There’s no footage of Teddy attacking Pace since that happened out of the camera’s sight. She said at first, she was concerned that Teddy attacked someone, but after watching the footage, Smith said she couldn’t understand why he would use those actions in ‘self-defense.’

“If you go up and you punch someone and then run away, that does not mean who you punched then can pursue you and stab you and kill you,” Smith said. “So why is it any different with a dog?”

Detectives did take pictures of the alleged dog bite as well as the pocket knife Pace used to stab Teddy.

Brett Shirk is Pace’s attorney. Pace did not want to speak on camera. Shirk said this case is in the early stages, and his client hasn’t even had his first arraignment, so there’s still much to piece through.

“My client will be pleading not guilty to this charge, and I do believe at first glance this is a case of self-defense,” Shirk said. “Regardless of what has been seen on any sort of videotape or something like that. There’s always two sides to the story, and I really do believe my client has a fairly good case for self-defense here.”

Travis Cummings and Jenifer Smith just want to see something done for their beloved family pet.

“I understand someone defending themselves in self-defense, but that was not self-defense in any kind of way,” Smith said. “I just think what my dog must’ve been thinking like, ‘Why did you hurt me?’ When I saw him fall down like, ‘why?’”

Shirk said Pace needs to have his first appearance in court, which will be his arraignment. As of Wednesday, the arraignment has not been scheduled yet.
