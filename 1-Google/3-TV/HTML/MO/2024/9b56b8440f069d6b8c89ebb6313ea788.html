Marshfield High School student finds a block of cocaine during school trip in the Florida Keys

By Chris Bryant
Published: Feb. 2, 2024 at 12:33 PM CST|Updated: Feb. 2, 2024 at 4:27 PM CST

MARSHFIELD, Mo. (KY3) - It sounds like a movie plot. Marshfield High School students recently found two and a half pounds of cocaine on a Florida beach while on a school trip.

“It was a brick of well-wrapped something, and I think I have seen some TV shows and had an idea that’s what it was,” says Wyatt Davis, a Senior at Marshfield High School.

“This is going to be hard to live up to, I must say.” Sometimes, it’s hard to believe what you are being told. “He came over and said, Ms. Fields, I found drugs, and I was like, okay, Wyatt,” says Kim Fields.

Fields teaches ecology. Her students visited a sea turtle hospital and swam with the dolphins.

“This year, I wanted to give back this year,” says Fields.

The students also cleaned up trash along a section of beach in the Florida Keys. On Monday, her class cleaned an eighth-of-a-mile section of Big Pine Key when one of her students made a discovery.

“Yeah, the cocaine thing that was a bit odd,” says Fields.

Autoplay

Senior Wyatt Davis found the cocaine in the grass along the shore. The class had only been at the beach for about 15 minutes.

“I was finding bottles, rope, and just trash, and it was in like a foot of grass,” says Davis.

Fields says at first, she didn’t know what to think when she heard the news from her students.

“Wyatt, he found the cocaine, and he came over and said, Ms. Fields, I found drugs, and I was like, okay, Wyatt, I didn’t know,” said Fields. “So, we finish with our clean up, and we get back to the boat, and they showed us it, and that is what he found,” says Fields.

Davis said he didn’t know what it was at first.

“It was in, like, some grass; it was laying there and kind of hard to get out of the grass. It had been there for a while,” said Davis.

The Monroe County Sheriff’s Office says that the drugs more than likely originated from the Caribbean and floated around before ending up in the Florida Straits before Davis found it.

“It had been there for a while; I pulled it up and looked at it, and kind of thinking, what is this? And I showed Hayden, and we agreed it was drugs,” says Davis.

The students gave it to the captain of the charter boat to notify the Sheriff’s Office, who came out and collected the cocaine before turning it over to the U.S. Border Patrol.

“This is crazy. We didn’t know what a square grubber was. We had never seen a brick of cocaine before all of this is new,” says Fields.

For Fields, the experience also allowed her to have an honest conversation about what just happened.

“We did have that conversation while we were standing at Capitan Brian’s house; it was a little intimidating because if there was a tracker in it, where are they going to come there?” says Fields.

For a group of students from Marshfield, a trip full of memories now includes their legend of the Caribbean Islands.

“I wanted the kids to come down here and experience all the science, and a lot of life comes with that, so this was just another unique opportunity,” says Fields.

U.S. Border Control agents released a statement regarding the discovery.

On January 29, 2024, U.S. Border Patrol agents in the Miami Sector recovered and seized one brick (2.5 pounds) of cocaine. The cocaine was discovered by Good Samaritans in Big Pine Key, Florida.

U.S. Border Patrol – Miami Sector works closely with our law enforcement partners and responds to drug wash-ups along our Florida coastlines.

We urge individuals to immediately contact local law enforcement if they encounter a suspicious package. See something, say something!

U.S. Border Control Assistant Chief Patrol Agent Adam Hoffner
