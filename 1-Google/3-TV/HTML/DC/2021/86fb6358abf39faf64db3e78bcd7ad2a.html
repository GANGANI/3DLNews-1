    Frustrated parents try to unseat school board members in Loudoun and Fairfax counties

    by Anna-Lysa Gayle

    Thu, July 8th 2021 at 11:26 PM
    Updated Fri, July 9th 2021 at 9:27 AM
    6
    VIEW ALL PHOTOS

    Parents in parts of Virginia, including Loudoun and Fairfax counties, are frustrated with some members of their school board. To push back, some are now collecting signatures in order to unseat some of those members, through a recall effort. (7News)

    LOUDOUN COUNTY, Va. (7News) — Parents in parts of Virginia, including Loudoun and Fairfax counties, are frustrated with some members of their school board.

    “We feel that they are overstepping their boundaries,” said Stand-up Virginia President Brenda Tillett.

    To push back, some are now collecting signatures in order to unseat some of those members, through a recall effort.

    “Whatever we believe in our homes, it should be taught in our homes,” Tillett said.

    In Loudoun County, where at least one person was arrested at a recent school board meeting after a public input session was cut short, the organization mainly behind the push is “Fight for Schools."

    They’ve collected over 11,000 signatures, to attempt to recall six school board members.

    “Since taking office, a majority of the Loudoun County School Board has shown utter contempt for state open meetings law,” said Fight for Schools’ Executive Director Ian Prior.

    “We are working all day, every day to remove and replace most of this school board with non-partisan individuals that will give parents a seat at the table,” he said.

    Over in Fairfax County, organizers behind recall efforts to unseat at least three members, are more concerned about the return to normalcy following the pandemic.

    “The recall effort started last October,” said “Open FCPS” spokesperson Saundra Davis. “I’m hoping they will resign.”

    Dr. Todd Belt is watching the patterns associated with the growing trend.

    Belt is the director of the Political Management program at The Graduate School of Political Management, at George Washington University.

    “I think what we’re seeing here is an example of what social scientists call demographic anxiety,” Belt said. “We know in Northern Virginia there has been a big demographic shift in the last 10 years or so.”

    In order to be successful, recall petitions must be signed by residents - equaling ten percent of the votes cast in the last election, a trial will then be held and recall supporters must prove that the board members neglected their duties.

    “That sort of political activity is generally, generated by those certain negative emotions, so it is certainly possible,” Belt said.

    Loudoun County School Board Member Beth Barts announced last month that she hired attorney Charlie King, "to defend her against a removal petition."

    Statement from School Board Member Abrar Omeish:

    "Decisions about how school is conducted must always center what is best for children. Over the past year, it has been our duty to ensure the country’s tenth-largest school system delivers learning safely and effectively.

    Our decisions about how schooling is delivered had to center science, depend on data about the situation as it evolved in real-time on the ground, and take into consideration the realities required to pull off a safe and smooth return to in-person learning for nearly 189,000 students. The decision to teach virtually or in person was never static; it was never about opinions, of which there was every possible type in comparable proportions.

    My job continues to be steadfast advocacy to champion the needs of students, staff, and their families.

    The months of bullying towards our staff by astroturf groups, in similar ways happening to school boards and divisions across the country, have already produced tangible harms to our children. These bad faith efforts to capitalize on the very real fears and frustrations of parents for political opportunity have hurt our kids by drawing attention, resources, and focus from them. For the sake of our kids, the partisan antics must stop.

    We can come together on unifying issues like recovering from a global pandemic. We can collectively recognize the common enemies we all share in a school system: ignorance, division, hate, and this virus. Let us come together in our common goal: to thoughtfully build the best school system in the country for our kids and their futures."
