    MS-13 gang member pleads guilty to 2016 murder of teen in Fairfax Co., US Attorney says

    by 7News Staff

    Fri, November 17th 2023 at 7:57 PM

    Police: 2 sets of human remains found at Va. park after police receive tip. (7News)

    FAIRFAX COUNTY, Va. (7News) — An El Salvadoran man and member of La Mara Salvatrucha (or MS-13) pleaded guilty Wednesday in connection with the kidnapping and murder of a teen boy in 2016, according to the U.S. Attorney’s Office of Eastern Virginia.

    Edwin Orellana Caballero – who was 16 years old and living in Alexandria, Va. at the time – was one of several members of the Park View Locos Salvatrucha clique of MS-13 who kidnapped and killed a 14-year-old, according to court documents.

    According to court documents, the gang lured the teen to Holmes Run Stream Valley Park in Fairfax County on the night of September 26, 2016, and murdered him in a wooded area with knives, machetes and a pickaxe.

    Orellana Caballero hit the teen multiple times with a pickaxe, court documents allege.

    Once the teen was dead, the gang buried him in a shallow grave.

    Orellana Caballero pleaded guilty to one count of maiming in aid of racketeering activity and is facing a minimum sentence of 20 years and a maximum sentence of 25 years in prison.

    He will be sentenced on February 28, 2024, court officials said.

    So far, 17 people have been charged in this case, according to court officials. Of those, five people went to trial and were convicted of all charges. Ten people have pleaded guilty ahead of trials.
