The Kid Who Wanted To Do “Hood Rat Stuff” With His Friends Is All Grown Up Now [VIDEO]

DJ DigitalPublished: June 5, 2015
  • Share on Facebook
  • Share on Twitter

Most people remember Latarian Milton as the 7-year-old viral sensation from West Palm Beach who wanted to do "hood rat stuff" with his friends. Well, he's all grown up now.

But is he still doing hood rat stuff?

Back in 2008, Milton stole his grandmother's car and took it on joyride before wrecking it. When the local news caught up with the boy is when he uttered the now famous line about doing "hood stuff" with his friends.

As if that wasn't enough, Latarian was in the news just two weeks later for hitting his grandmother in a store because she wouldn't buy him chicken wings.

Earlier this week, Milton (now 14) graduated from middle school and the same news station who interviewed him seven years ago caught up with him, and Latarian is definitely a changed young man.

Speaking on how he felt about entering his first year of high school in the fall, Latarian told WPBF,

It's going to be a good when I get into high school because I'll be able to play on the football team, have some good success there and go to college,

Definitely a change of pace from the "hood rat stuff" Milton went viral for. Even though the 14-year-old went viral for saying how fun it was to "do bad things," he is fully committed to doing good things because "doing good," according to Latarian, means he "can be successful."

Good luck on your dreams of the NFL, young man—but you will forever live as the reason why we do "hood rat stuff" with all our "friends."
