  • Self promotions and calls to action
    • Just following up on Ben’s last email on this subject. A few important details about FCC policies got dropped from the code of conduct when it was consolidated about 10 years back. Unless talking about a legitimate non profit organization, or efforts to raise money for WESU, the FCC strictly forbids education stations from mentioning prices for specific products, services, and events. The FCC considers pricing information, including the word “free” to be an incentive to support said product, service, or organization. Using non commercial airwaves to promote a product or service is forbidden. That doesn’t mean that we cannot explore these subjects. DJs need to make sure they present findings in a factual informative way that enables listeners to decide what they think while avoiding hyperbole and calls to action. IF you want to incorporate your personal view, that’s fine too, just remind listeners that it’s your view and not the view of WESU. This all simply means that we should not be telling listeners what to do or think. This includes religious/spiritual calls to action around faith. Shows that deal with spirituality and faith should be sure to understand all of the rules around promotions.
