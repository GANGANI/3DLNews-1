The Next Big Thing: The Flat Stanleys

  • Scout Ehrler
  • Oct 16, 2018
  • Oct 16, 2018

The alt-rock scene has been taken over by a sub-genre of folk-rock/folk-punk/emo-rock. It seems to be the new wave of pop punk in many ways. Pop-punkers flock to bands such as The Front Bottoms, Modern Baseball, Tigers Jaw, Turnover, Pinegrove, et cetera, et cetera. It’s raw, scream-talky rock that you can headbang and cry to, potentially simultaneously. No one has a name for it, but it’s popularity is growing tremendously, as is the number of bands in the scene.

A band by the name of The Flat Stanleys emerged on the scene in 2016. From the get-go, they were buzzworthy. In 2017, the band got the opportunity to work with Jacob Ewald of Modern Baseball and Slaughter Beach, Dog. They are currently working on their first full length album with Joe Reinhart, producer of Holy Ghost by Modern Baseball.

With only a total of 12 songs released into the world, The Flat Stanleys already show potential to be the next big thing. With every additional release, more musical, vocal, and lyrical nuance emerges and over time, this band continues to rapidly evolve. I would argue they have had more evolution in there music between the release of Dancing to Dad Rock in 2017 and their latest release, Too Much and Not the Mood, released earlier this year, than many bands have in their entire careers.

The Flat Stanleys began as what I would affectionately call “The Front Bottoms Jr.” Their sound emulated The Front Bottoms down to specific lyrics and the amount of syllables in their names. They were growing out of a scene with a specific sound and somewhat rigid expectations. So many bands in this genre have tons of parallels, specifically in lyrical and vocal style. The Flat Stanleys fit that mold to a T.

However, upon the release of Too Much and Not the Mood, the band began displaying growth and emulated a sense of self-discovery and self-assurance that their other music did not. I have listened to “Praise Song” more times than I can count and every single time I pick out a new riff or instrument hidden in the crevices. The music is layered and nuanced beyond by own comprehension. Their talent is more than that of just a small band from Baltimore with a few EPs. The Flat Stanleys are going to be huge, and I can’t wait.

Keep your eyes peeled for their first full length album, to be released in 2019. I’m confident it will not disappoint.
