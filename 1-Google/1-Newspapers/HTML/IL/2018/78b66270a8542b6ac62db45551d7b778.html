    Final Celebration featured guests announced

    • Staff Report

    • May 3, 2018

    This year’s featured celebrity guests who will be in attendance at the 40th annual Superman Celebration — June 7-10 — have officially been announced. This year will feature a returning Superman and two newcomers to Metropolis from the new show Krypton.

    Actor Brandon Routh has previously appeared in Metropolis and will make his second appearance this year.

    It has been 12 years since Routh donned the tights and cape for the 2006 movie Superman Returns. Routh is from Iowa but moved to Los Angeles to pursue an acting career.

    He received notoriety as the Man of Steel and following that job, he also appeared on several television shows, including Gilmore Girls.

    Routh had notable supporting roles in Zack and Miri Make a Porno and Scott Pilgrim vs the World.

    In 2014 he started a recurring role on Arrow as Ray Palmer/Atom, which led to a guest role on The Flash and a starring role on Legends of Tomorrow.

    This year two actors from the Syfy Channel hit show, Krypton, will be in town to sign autographs and pose for photos with Superman fans from across the globe.

    Krypton debuted for its first season on March 21. The premise of the show is that it takes place on eponymous planet around 200 years before the birth of Kal-El/Superman and focuses on his grandfather, Seg-El.

    Canadian actor Shaun Sipos portrays Adam Strange on Krypton. His character is an archaeologist that was teleported from a dig site in Peru, on earth into another galaxy, to the planet Rann through a Zeta-Beam. Sipos is also known for his role as Jack on the ABC series Complete Savages, Eric Daniels on Life Unexpected and David Breck on the CW series Melrose Place. Some Smallville fans may even recognize Sippos from the episode Rush, when he portrayed Chloe’s boy thing.

    English born actor and director Blake Ritson, portrays Brainiac on Krypton. Brainiac is an alien known as “the world collector,” seeking to destroy Krypton years before the birth of Kal-El/ Superman.

    Ritson is best known recently for portraying King Edward III in the television miniseries World Without End in 2012, the Duke of Kent in Upstairs Downstairs from 2010-2012 and Mr. Elton in the BBC adaptation of Emma in 2009.

    Routh, Sipos and Ritson will appear on Saturday, June 9. They will join other celebrity guests Jack O’Halloran, Aaron Smolinski, Jeff East, John Haymes Newton and producer Ilya Salkind

    Organizers understand there is some concern over the free ticket offerings this year.

    Last year 100 free tickets were offered for one autograph session. This year, according to organizers, 40 will be offered.

    The two individual sponsorship levels were combined into one this year, however the there are more benefits of this combined sponsorship. VIP tables will be available in a new tent, specifically dedicated for dining seats. There will also be premium VIP seating for events such as the Q&A sessions in the main tent. Individuals will also receive the sponsor T-shirt for the event and so receive VIP only shopping hours in the Chamber of Commerce.

    “This measure was taken not to increase the profits of this fundraiser for the Chamber of Commerce, but as a means to cover the costs of the event itself. For years, we were able to offer many events for free and still do offer many for free, however with the increased cost in production, rentals as well as the much larger premiums required to secure top featured guests, special guests and artists this had to be done to ensure the vitality of the event,” says Karla Ogle, co-organizer of the event.

    Ogle says at this time organizers have not received the specific autograph session details from the featured guests. As part of securing the celebrity guests, the Celebration organizers and Chamber of Commerce must agree to their autograph procedures and costs. Ogle says when they received the guidelines they will be posted on the Superman Celebration Facebook page and on the website www.supermancelebration.net.
