OBITUARIES 7-9-98

ROCK ISLAND

Sylvia Jacks

Sylvia Viola Jacks, of Rock Island, died Tuesday, July 7, 1998.

Services will be 10 a.m. Saturday at the Wheelan Funeral Home, Rock Island, with Rev. Ivan R. Fuller, pastor, Memorial Christian Church, Rock Island, officiating. Burial will be in Greenview Memorial Gardens, East Moline. Visitation will be 4 to 7 p.m. Friday at the funeral home.

Sylvia Gibson was born Aug. 24, 1900, in Rock Island, and was a lifetime resident. She married Fremont L. Jacks Sr. June 16, 1920. He passed away in 1975.

Survivors include her daughter, Kathryn Dierolf, Ridgecrest, Calif.; sons, Fremont Jr., Ft. Lauderdale, Fla., Gerald "Jake", Davenport, and Kenneth, Matherville, Ill. She is also survived by 17 grandchildren, 40 great-grandchildren and 32 great-great-grandchildren. She was preceded in death by her husband, Fremont Sr.; daughter, Geraldine; and sons, Alvin, LaVerne, Arnold and Earl.

Memorials may be made to Memorial Christian Church, Rock Island, where she was a member.

She will be missed.

Laurent Nugent

Services for Laurent J. Nugent, 84, Rock Island, will be 11 a.m. Friday at St. Piux X Catholic Church, Rock Island. Burial will be in Calvary Cemetery, Rock Island.

Visitation will be from 4 to 6 p.m. today at Wheelan Funeral Home, Rock Island, with a rosary to be recited at 5:30. Additional visitation will be after 10 a.m. Friday at the church. Memorials may be made to the church.

Mr. Nugent died Tuesday, July 7, 1998, in Carlsbad, Calif.

He was born Sept. 19, 1913, in West Branch, Iowa, the son of Michael and Margaret McGee Nugent. He married Clara T. Wisor Sept. 13, 1941, in Charlotte, Iowa.

He was an iron worker and supervisor for 42 years, retiring in 1984.

He loved his family and enjoyed dancing and playing golf.

Survivors include his wife, Clara; daughters, Mary Ellen Nest and Carol Krysko, both of Carlsbad, Ann Kallio, Hartland, Wis.; sons Jack Nugent, Dublin, Ohio, and Gregory Nugent, Miami, Fla.; 10 grandchildren; sisters, Sister Mary Laurent Nugent, Congregation of Humility of Mary, Davenport, Beth Abrahamsen, Aurora, Colo., Helen Franck, Moline, Jean Robaidek, Oregon, Wis.; and a brother, Raymond Nugent, Grand Mound, Iowa. He was preceded in death by two sisters.

MOLINE

Richard `Oz' Shoemate

Richard "Oz" Shoemate, passed away at 1:10 p.m. on Tuesday, July 7, 1998, in Illini Hospital in Silvis.

The memorial service will be at 11 a.m. Saturday at Wendt Funeral Home, Moline. Friends are invited to the funeral home at 10:30 a.m. to visit with the family. Burial will be at the LaHarpe City Cemetery at a later date. Friends who wish to remember Oz may make a donation to their favorite charity in his memory.

He was born Feb. 11, 1917, in Durham Township, near LaHarpe, Ill., and has resided in Moline for 45 years. He was the son of John R. Shoemate and Lucy J. Bassett Shoemate. He married Betty D. Dowacter Feb. 9, 1952, in Pocahontas, Ark. He graduated from LaHarpe High School in 1934 and worked in various businesses in LaHarpe, at Leopold Desk Company and the Iowa Ammunition Plant in Burlington, Iowa.

During World War II he served from 1943 to 1945 in the South Pacific earning a Purple Heart in the campaign on Guam. He also served on Iwo Jima. After returning from service, he graduated from barber college in Peoria. After several years in the barber business in LaHarpe, he relocated to Moline and opened Oz's Clip Joint, retiring April 30, 1997, due to ill health after a total of 50 years in the barber business.

He was a member of Moline Elks Lodge 556 and Moline American Legion Post 246.

He was an avid golfer.

He is survived by his wife, Betty, Moline; sons, Richard, Saddle Brook, N.J., and Dennis, Moline; daughters, Marilyn Fox, LaHarpe, Janette Owen, Lees Summit, Mo., and Kathy Strickland, New Port Richey, Fla.; a stepdaughter, Elaine Moore, West Olive, Mich.; 12 grandchildren; four great-grandchildren; and several nieces and nephews.

EAST MOLINE

Alice Light

Services for Alice Wilamena Light, 95, of Life Care Nursing Home, Escondido, Calif., are 1 p.m. Saturday at Sullivan-Phillips Mortuary Ltd., East Moline, Rev. Bill Dayton officiating. Burial is in Hampton Township Cemetery.

Visitation is 6 to 8 p.m. Friday at the funeral home. Memorials may be made to Church of Christ in California.

Mrs. Light died Monday, June 22, 1998, at the nursing home.

She was born Nov. 7, 1902, in Willmington, Vt., the daughter of Charles Rylander and Mary Collins. She married Ralph Light in 1922 in Hampton. He died April 22, 1969.

In earlier years, she worked at Sears & Tri City Food Store in the bakery department. Later, she was a housewife.

She was a member of Church of Christ.

She enjoyed knitting and traveling.

Survivors include a son and daughter-in-law, Duane Frances Light, Escondido; grandchildren, Donald Light, Dallas, Texas, James Light, Hannibal, Mo., and Larry Light, York, Neb.; great-grandchildren, Leesa Bartu, Jimmie Light, Chris Light, Jennie Lape, Kassie Light and Ryan Light. She was preceded in death by a twin sister, Grace, on the same date June 22 of 1992.

AREA

Elden Beerbohm

Elden Wayne Beerbohm, 66, Hoffman Estates, Ill., died Thursday, June 11, 1998, at home.

A private graveside service will be held at 10 a.m. Saturday at Holy Family Cemetery, Davenport.

Halligan-McCabe-DeVries Funeral Home, Davenport, is in charge of arrangements.

Elden was born Aug. 9, 1931, in Watertown, Wis. He married Sharon S. Roemer April 17, 1970, in Chicago. She preceded him in death on Nov. 25, 1995.

He had been employed at Larson Express Inc. in Schaumberg, Ill., where he had worked for many years.

He enjoyed the arts and fine dining.

Survivors include a brother, Kenneth, Escondido, Calif.; a sister, Fae Ann Mekelburg, Watertown, Wis.; an aunt, Julia Kelly, Milwaukee, Wis.; an uncle, Walter Giese, Watertown; and several nieces and nephews.

Caleb Bundschuh

Caleb A. Bundschuh, 17, Elgin, Ill., died Tuesday, July 7, 1998, at Sherman Hospital, Elgin, from injuries sustained in an automobile accident.

Services are 11 a.m. Friday at Our Redeemer Free Methodist Church, Elgin. Burial is in Dundee Township Cemetery, East Dundee, Ill.

Visitation is 5 to 9 p.m. today and 10 a.m. to service time at the church. In lieu of flowers, memorials may be made to Larkin High School Football Program or Anderson Animal Shelter, South Elgin.

Miller Funeral Home, West Dundee, Ill., is charge of arrangements.

He was born June 7, 1981, in Moline. He had completed his junior year at Larkin High School.

Survivors include his parents, Paul and Allena Linn Bundschuh of Elgin, grandparents, Charles Bundschuh of Elgin, and Clarence and Phyllis Linn of New Windsor; and many aunts, uncles, cousins and friends.

IOWA

Jack Burton

Services for Jack L. Burton, 59, Buffalo, are 10 a.m. Saturday at McGinnis, Chambers and Sass, Bettendorf. Burial is in Rose Hill Cemetery, Buffalo, with military honors conducted.

Visitation is from 5 to 8 p.m. Friday. Memorials to the family.

Mr. Burton died Monday, July 6, 1998, at Genesis West.

He was born Feb. 10, 1939, in Davenport. He had served in the Marine Corps. He was a lab technician for Occidental Chemical Co., Buffalo, retiring in 1992.

Survivors include his mother, Helen Johnson, Buffalo; and brothers, Mack Ferrick, Anchorage, Alaska, Tim Ferrick, Tombstone, Ariz., and Rick Ferrick, Hudson, Wis.

Patrick Conway

Patrick E. Conway, 62, Davenport, died Wednesday, July 8, 1998, at Genesis East Medical Center, Davenport.

Arrangements are pending at Halligan-McCabe-DeVries Funeral Home, Davenport.

Among survivors is his wife, Mary.

Waldo Ewoldt

Waldo Ewoldt, 69, Davenport, died Tuesday, July 7, 1998, at Genesis, West.

Services are 10 a.m. Saturday at Berean Baptist, Davenport. Burial in Davenport Memorial Park. Visitation is 4-8 p.m. Friday at Runge Mortuary. Memorials to the church.

He was born in rural Scott County to Otto and Hilda Kluever Ewoldt. He married Elizabeth Hoffman in 1949. She died in 1994. He married Grace Allar Jan. 24, 1998.

Surviving are his wife; daughters, Virginia Ewoldt and Jacqueline Puck, both of Walcott, Kathleen Howard, Tomah, Wis., and Patti Johnson, Davenport; sons, Waldo Jr., Davenport, and Warren, Long Grove; 12 grandchildren; sisters, Alta Badtram, Parkview, and Caroline Backstrom, Davenport; and brothers, Orrie, Davenport, Donald, Greenwood, Wis., Otto Jr., LeClaire, and Kenneth, Delmar. He was preceded by a son, James.

Lorraine Funk

Lorraine M. Funk, 77, Bettendorf, died July 7, 1998, at Riverview Manor Nursing Home, Pleasant Valley.

Services are 10 a.m. Friday at St. James Lutheran Church, Bettendorf. Burial is in Davenport Memorial Park. Visitation is 4 to 8 p.m. today at McGinnis, Chambers & Sass, Bettendorf. Memorials may go to the family.

Lorraine Paustian was born May 10, 1921, in Davenport. She married Mifflin "Meff" Funk Nov. 28, 1946.

Surviving are her husband, Meff; daughters, Nancy Capshaw, Bettendorf, and Holly Jeffry, Richland, Iowa; a son, Jon Funk, Bettendorf; two grandchildren; a great-grandson; and a sister, Barbara Siebel, Stockton.

Luella Gordy

Luella M. Gordy, 75, Davenport, died Tuesday, July 7, 1998, at home.

Services are 10 a.m. Friday at McGinnis, Chambers & Sass Funeral Home, Bettendorf. Burial is in Davenport Memorial Park. Visitation is 3 to 7 p.m. today. Memorials to Scott County Humane Society.

Survivors include her daughter and son-in-law, Jane and Dale Buesing, Davenport; son and daughter-in-law, Dick and Rosemary Gordy, Bettendorf; eight grandchildren; seven stepgrandchildren; 14 great-grandchildren; several stepgreat-grandchildren; sister and brother-in-law, Judy and Tom Knoche, Blue Grass; and a special friend, Jim Kelly, Davenport.

Julia Matelski

Julia S. Matelski, 85, Davenport, died Tuesday, July 7, 1998, at home.

Services are 1 p.m. Friday at Runge Mortuary, Davenport. Cremation will follow the services with the cremains to be buried at Letts, Iowa, Cemetery. Visitation is 4 to 7 p.m. today.

Julia Renner was born Aug. 7, 1912, in Gettysburg, S.D. She married Lewis Matelski July 17, 1956, in Centerville, Iowa.

She had been employed at several local companies including the former Crescent Cookie Factory and Alcoa.

Survivors include her husband; daughters, Marcella Larson, Niles, Ill., and Marlys Johnson, Peoria, Ariz.; six grandchildren; 10 great-grandchildren; sisters, Sylvia Batchelor, Davenport, and Dollie Hites, Albuquerque, N.M.; and brothers, John Renner, Wapello, and Harold Renner, of California.

Dale Pries

Memorial services for Dale E. Pries, 71, of Bettendorf, are 6 p.m. Friday at McGinnis, Chambers and Sass Funeral Home, Bettendorf. Visitation is 4 p.m. to service time.

Memorials to Pathway Hospice or the family.

Mr. Pries died Saturday, July 4, 1998, at the home of his daughter in Bettendorf.

He was born Nov. 18, 1926, in Davenport, to William and Elsie Crosby Pries. He married Jacqueline Young June 26, 1949, in Bettendorf.

Survivors include a daughter, Brenda Blossom, Bettendorf; two grandchildren; a sister, Marion Hintermeister, Davenport; and a brother, Raymond Pries, Rock Falls.
