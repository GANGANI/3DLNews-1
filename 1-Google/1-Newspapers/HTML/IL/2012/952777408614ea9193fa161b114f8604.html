New sheriff, state's attorney ahead for Hardin County

Posted April 02, 2012 11:00 pm
Jon Musgrave

</element><element id="paragraph-1" type="body"><![CDATA[Hardin County will soon get both a new sheriff as well as a new state&#39;s attorney, following the March 6 death of the late Sheriff Lloyd "Smokey" Cullison, and the upcoming resignation of State&#39;s Attorney Roger Ralph.

The county board has 60 days to fill the sheriff&#39;s job currently being filled by the county coroner Roger Little who by law becomes acting sheriff during a vacancy.

The board is expected to take up the issue at their regular meeting tonight which begins at 6 p.m. at the courthouse.

Normally, the political party of the late sheriff would make a recommendation to the county board to fill the vacancy. However, Cullison ran as an independent in the November 2010 election which would seemingly require his appointed successor to be so as well, though that point really isn&#39;t that clear according to Ralph.

"The appointee has to be of the same political party as the person who vacated the position. Since the person who vacated the position was not a member of a party, I think it leaves it open, as to how it should be done," Ralph explained.

"I think if one person is appointed who has been clearly identified with one party or another, then I can see the other party likely to challenge that," predicted Ralph. "In my looking at the law I don&#39;t see anything in black or white that answers that question."

One person that might fit the position is Cullison&#39;s widow, Joyce Cullison who already works for the sheriff&#39;s department and was working there prior to her late husband&#39;s election.

She could not be reached for comment Monday.

County Clerk Mary Ellen Denton said Monday she thought the board would discuss the matter this week and make a selection later. Whoever is appointed would serve just until the beginning of December as a special election would be held in November for the unexpired two years remaining in late sheriff&#39;s term.

Neither the Democratic or Republican parties had candidates for their party&#39;s nomination in the primary election last month. Denton said the parties normally would caucus April 18 to make selections, but since this position came open after the election they would have Aug. 29 to file a candidate.

The Democrats caucused last Thursday and have tentatively backed former Hardin County Deputy Sheriff Brian Reed to be their candidate, according to Hardin County Democratic Party Chairman Dennis Austin of rural Cave-in-Rock.

Meanwhile Denton said there were a number of persons that have expressed interest in running for sheriff as an independent.

Independent candidates will need signatures of 160 registered voters on their petitions to get on the ballot. Those candidates could have started circulating petitions last week. Filing for the office runs June 18-25.

Denton said state law allows voters who signed a petition for a partisan candidate in the primary to also sign one independent candidate&#39;s petition in the general election.

The issue of party won&#39;t be one for Ralph&#39;s successor. Elected as a Democrat in 2008, the county chairman will be required to appoint a Democrat to succeed him.

Ralph confirmed Monday that he would be resigning effective April 15, in order to return to Indianapolis, Ind., where his wife works.

Ralph said his resignation had nothing to do with anything regarding Hardin County or the position.

"[My wife&#39;s] job has her in Indianapolis all the time and we&#39;ve been driving back and forth. This is just a good time to move back," he explained.

Ralph will be 59 next month and plans to work a few more years but doesn&#39;t have anything definite lined up. Both he and his wife are Rosiclare natives.

"I had practiced in Indianapolis for about 20 years," Ralph explained. "I didn&#39;t plan to run for state&#39;s attorney when I came down there. I was just planning to stay part time and spend some time with my family."

"Probably the biggest challenge down here has been the finances of the county and the inability to really fund any of the state or county departments to the level they really need to be. The Hogrock and ICP [Juggalos] events have produced extra work for the office that has presented some challenges. We don&#39;t have the resources to really respond to that the way we would like to," he said.

Ralph didn&#39;t know who the board would name as his successor. Regardless of who is appointed to fill out the last six or seven months of his term, Tara Wallace, the Republican candidate, will face off against Daniel Cockrum, the Democratic candidate for state&#39;s attorney in November.
