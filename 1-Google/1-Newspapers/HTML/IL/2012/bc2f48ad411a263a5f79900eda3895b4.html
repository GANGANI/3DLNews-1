Roxanne Hooper receives Harold Award

Originally Published February 23, 2012

Roxanne Hooper of Essex has been named the Pledge for Life Partnership’s Harold Award recipient for outstanding service to area youth. She received the award at the 16th Annual Family Fun-a-thon held Feb. 12 at Olivet Nazarene University.

Roxanne has worked as a community liaison for the I-KAN Regional Office of Education’s Family and Community Partnership programs since 1998. During that time, she worked closely with the Pledge for Life Partnership to launch the KAN-I HELP Information Network and continues to oversee it to this day.

From 2006-2008, she served as project director for the Emergency Response and Crisis Management School Safety Initiative and played a critical part in the Mental Health in School Improvement Project.

“Roxanne is very important to the Life Education programs and works tirelessly to strengthen parental involvement,” said Brenda Wetzel, director of the Life Education programs. “She understands the issues and complexity of the problems affecting students and their families and knows how to get agencies working together for a common cause – a better life for kids and their families.”

Before joining the ROE, Roxanne served as principal of St. Rose School in Wilmington and of St. Patrick’s Academy in Momence. She also taught at Kennedy Upper Grade Center in Kankakee.

Roxanne and her husband, Ronnie have two grown daughters, four grandchildren and another one on the way in March.
