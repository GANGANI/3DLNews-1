Albino deer is latest victim of deer/vehicle accident near Warsaw

  • By Shannon Campbell, Staff writer

A albino deer jumped into Warsaw's history pages Thursday morning, Nov. 11. The rare deer was hit by a bread truck near Warsaw killing the animal.

Sightings of the albino deer are not uncommon in the area. In fact, Warsaw Mayor Robert Frank said there are possibly two more albino deer near Warsaw.

"I know of one that is a 10-point in my neck of the woods," Frank said. The other albino is said to be a buck as well, according to Franks. "Several people have pictures and tapes (of the albino deer)," Franks said. Some residents have even fed the deer in their back yards.

According to Whitetail Abnormalities on www.kerrlake.com, albinos are identified by their pink eyes. Blood vessels behind the lenses show through the unpigmented irises giving them their pink hue additionally giving then a much better chance of having poor eyesight.

Albinos are considered rare because most do not succeed to maturity because of their inability to camouflage themselves. Both parents must carry the Albinism gene before it occurs in their offspring.

There are at least six states presently who prohibit the intentional death of an albino deer. They are: Wisconsin, Michigan, Illinois, Iowa, Montana and Washington.

Conservation Police Officer, Mike Schell picked up the deer Thursday, and it was taken to Weber Meats in Carthage. The meat was processed and donated to the Western Illinois Regional Council who is going to distribute the meat in the area food pantries. Negotiations are being hammered out to determine what to do with the head and skin of the animal with hopes of having it displayed in one of the buildings in Warsaw.

While this accident is unusual because the deer was an albino, the surrounding scene is not unfamiliar in Hancock County. A leisurely drive down a side road during the evening seems to turn into an animated game testing drivers' dodging skills, sight and senses.

In Hancock County, a number of wrecks have been reported. Approximately 10-15 wrecks are reported each week this time of year according to the Sheriff's Department. Sheriff John Jefferson said this week and next week the deer are extremely active, especially the bucks.

"During this time, the bucks can be out all day because they are in rut," Jefferson said.

Jefferson said the last two years have been extremely heavy with deer versus car accidents. "Usually this doesn't happen until the fall but we have noticed these types of accidents occuring all year long," Jefferson said. He believes it is because of an increased population in deer with some hunters coming to Illinois hunting trophy bucks only and not enough does.

Jefferson advises drivers to pay special attention during the dawn and dusk hours when the deer are most active. "Unfortunately, that is when people are going back and forth to work," Jefferson said.

To alleviate the overpopulation of does, the Illinois Department of Natural Resources has opened a third firearm session between Jan. 14-16 for anterless deer only. A permit can only be obtained by a State of Illinois resident.
