Group Asadullah: New Killer of the Kurdish Nation

Salem-News.com Foreign Affairs

The Asadullah Group are militants tied to Sunni Islamist and nationalist policy with no clear identity of members.


Courtesy: almasdarnews.com

(SALEM, Ore.) - In a Tweet on his personal page, an expert on Turkish affairs revealed the true identity of the Asadullah Group that has recently shown up in the Kurdish regions of Turkey.

Ozcan Turkan is a Turkish analyst and expert on affairs, and is also known as "The Magician" among journalists and regional analysts because of his hidden connections with influential figures and political circles in the region.

He wrote a recent article titled, "The New Killer of the Kurds in Turkey" where he claims the Turkish intelligence service is looking for ways to suppress Kurds. This, according to 7 Sabah website in Turkey.

In the newspaper, he wrote that one of MIT's (Turkish National Intelligence Organization) most brilliant plans in Turkey is creating a new ISIS against the Kurds of the area; if MIT can persuade a new group of Islamic extremists and support them, they can bring Kurds to their knees.

Quoting a senior MIT officer in his article, the Turkish expert writes that the master plan involves scaring away, killing and plundering.

"Every rebellion is wild until they see no wilder rebellion," Turkan wrote.

"The opponent must believe you have more power and if they see you shed more blood they will retreat."

Ozcan Turkan presents the above analysis based on accusations raised by several MIT officers pointing to the official position of Turkish security authorities who believe protesting Kurds are doing wrong, and mean to build their own home in Ottoman Turkey.

The apparent belief is that, "They must be suppressed at all costs, and we'll do it even if it requires the help of ISIS."

The Asadullah group has just recently shown up and the members are apparently militants tied to Sunni Islamist and nationalist policy with no clear identity of members.

According to available evidence and their presence among government forces and the Turkish army, this group is believed to be organized by the Turkish intelligence service recruiting and organizing certain elements of ISIS.
