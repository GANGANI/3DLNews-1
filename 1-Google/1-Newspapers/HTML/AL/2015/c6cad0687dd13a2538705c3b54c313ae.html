‘Miss Red’ celebrates 50 years on the job

  • By JAY BEARD
  • Aug 19, 2015
  • Aug 19, 2015

When Verdeara Sizemore, “Miss Red” to those who might or might not know that she used to have bright red hair, started working at Walker Brother’s Building Materials in Baileyton on Aug. 2, 1965, Lyndon B. Johnson was president, gas was 31 cents a gallon and her boss was 9 years old.

“When I started to work,” Sizemore said. “I made $2.25 per hour. That was $90 a week.

“I asked for a $100 a week,” she continued, “but Cee Buck, owner at that time, told me that he would see how I did after six weeks.”

That was 50 years ago last Monday, and Miss Red is still going strong, says Alan Walker, part owner of Walker Brothers.

“She’s very hardworking and trustworthy,” he says, “but she can turn out more work than three people. She’s missed less than a handful of times since she has been here and those were when she had a fall last year.

“I was in the third grade when she started working here,” he continued. “My brother Terry was in diapers.”

Although Miss Red has done a lot for the company over the years, she is family to the Walkers.

“I guess if you put it into terms,” Walker said, “she is our secretary. That’s what she likes to be called, anyway.

“I’d call her office manger, but she also handles our real estate,” he continued. “She has always handled everything as if it were hers. I’ve never had to worry about her. We treat her like family, and she treats us like family.”

Sizemore’s career at Walker Brother’s began when Marie Walker of Baileyton suggested to, Cee Buck Walker, that she should be hired.

“I wanted to come back to Cullman,” Sizemore said. “I had been driving back and forth to Birmingham to work at Cotton’s Department Store in Ensley.

“I started work on Aug. 2, 1965,” she continued, “and it was on a Monday.”

Around the office, Sizemore is known for her hard work and for being well dressed.

“I used to wear dresses and skirts all the time. That’s where that came from,” she said with a smile on her face. “I don’t as much anymore.

“When I first went to work,” she continued, “I did everything - bookkeeping, accounts receivable. I even wrote all the statements out by hand. I’m only able to work a couple of days a week now, but I’m still here.”

Although Sizemore will be turning 80 on Aug. 20, she still remembers when Walker Brothers bought the bookkeeping machine in 1966, and when the company went to computers in 1988.

“We’ve had our ups and downs,” she said. “The hardest day for me was when Cee Buck passed away in 1996. It was a hard day for us all.”

Although Cee Buck and Ralph Walker, former part owner of Walker Brothers, had been working together for a few years prior, the store wasn’t officially opened until the early 1960s.

Although Sizemore isn’t able to get around like she used to, she still plans on working for Walker Brothers as long as she can.

When Miss Red was asked about possible retirement, she said that she is still going strong and still has a lot of work to do.

Overhearing the conversation, Walker jokingly said that he isn’t going to let her retire until after he does.

“She would like to work as long as she feels like it,” he said. “She knows that we want her here. We don’t anticipate her fully retiring anytime soon.”

According to Miss Red, Cee Buck always joked about her finding a husband and leaving Walker Brothers.

In 1974, she married the late John Hullon Sizemore. He died in 2006. Although she never had any children, she has four stepchildren and four step grandchildren.

When Miss Red isn’t at work, she likes to travel.

“I like to read, and I love my church,” she said. “I travel with the senior group out of Cullman. I’m also a member of the High Hatters.”

Miss Red has traveled to a few different countries and almost every state in the country.

“In June I took a cruise to Alaska,” she said. “I’ve been to Italy, Greece, Jerusalem, England, Denmark, China, Sweden and Australia.

“We went to Sydney and, I think, Melbourne,” she continued. “We could see the Sydney Opera House from our hotel.”
