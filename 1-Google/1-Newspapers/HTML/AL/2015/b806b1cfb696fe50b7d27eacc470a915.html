Town council member arrested

  • By Kevin Taylor
  • May 2, 2015
  • May 2, 2015

Elmore Town Council Member David Foster is facing a misdemeanor DUI charge after being involved in a three-car accident Tuesday night.

Foster, 73, was driving a 2000 Nissan Frontier, which struck a 2005 Ford 500 driven by 56-year-old Coosada woman around 6 p.m. Tuesday on Alabama Highway 14 and the Coosada Parkway, according to a release from the Alabama Law Enforcement Agency.

Her vehicle later collided with a 2015 Hyundai Accent driven by a 54-year-old Millbrook woman.

The Coosada driver was transported to Jackson Hospital in Montgomery for treatment of her nonlife-threatening injuries.

Foster told the Herald on Friday he “was not drunk.”

“I was just going to the store to get some cigarettes and come back home to watch the riots in Baltimore on TV,” he said.

The driver of the Hyundai and her three passengers were taken to Baptist Hospital in Prattville for evaluation.

Foster was booked into the Elmore County Jail early Wednesday morning and was later released on a $700 bond.

Foster told the Herald he “had a drink” after a full day of cutting limbs.

“I’m going to fight that. I’m not going down just like that,” he said.

Foster is in the middle of his second term as a town council member.

No further information was available by ALEA.
