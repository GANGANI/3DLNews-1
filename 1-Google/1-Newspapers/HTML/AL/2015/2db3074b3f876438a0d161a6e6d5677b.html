She's a cowgirl and a rodeo queen

  • Apr 8, 2015
  • Apr 8, 2015
  • 0

  • SMS
  • Email

  • Twitter

Heather Bundy will be centerstage at this weekend’s Morgan County Sheriff’s Rodeo at Celebration Arena in Priceville.

She is the rodeo’s “reigning queen” and will crown this year’s queen. She lives in Huntsville but has a tie to Marshall County. Her grandparents Dairrel and Ethaleen Benefield live in the Mt. Olive community of Sand Mountain. She is the daughter of Mike and Rebecca Bundy of Huntsville.

Rodeo has always been a huge part of Heather’s life. She barrel races and calf ropes. She’s won multiple buckles, saddles and cash prizes.

Being the rodeo queen took her even further. She has participated in lots of rodeos, parades, inaugurations and other events as the Morgan County rodeo queen.

She even represented Morgan County in the Miss Rodeo USA competition in Oklahoma City, Oklahoma.

The weekend rodeo is at 7:00 Saturday night in Celebration Arena.
