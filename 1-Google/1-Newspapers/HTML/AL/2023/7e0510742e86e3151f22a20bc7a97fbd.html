Community rally based on dis-proven claims of police abuse

By Tim Altork | December 20, 2023

Shanetta Catique Winston

A community rally took place Saturday in Roanoke seeking to call attention to alleged police brutality by officers in the Roanoke Police Department. But the accusations on which the rally was based turned out to be completely fabricated.

Shanetta Catique Winston, 49, of Roanoke, was at the center of the controversy and is now being held in the Randolph County jail without bond after making what were shown to be false statements in a complaint against the Roanoke Police Department.

Winston was arrested on August 4 in the parking lot of the Traveler’s Inn on Highway 431 on the south end of Roanoke. She received multiple drug charges from that arrest (those charges are still pending) and was released on bond from the Randolph County jail.

While still out on bond she returned to Roanoke Police headquarters in November to file a complaint against the arresting officer in the case. In that complaint she claimed that she was punched in the head to the point that she lost consciousness, had her leg slammed in a car door and was held at gunpoint by the officer, who she said threatened to kill her.

As a result of that complaint Winston was arrested last week, had her bond revoked and was charged with presenting a false instrument for recording – a law that protects public employees from false accusations against them. That charge is a felony, and because Winston was out on bond when she was charged with that crime she cannot be bonded out again.

An announcement was circulated on Facebook Friday and Saturday and was emailed to the Leader Friday afternoon with the headline “Call to Action!!”
In it there was an announcement for a community rally in support of Winston that was initially scheduled to take place Saturday at First Baptist Church on Riley Street in Roanoke.

According to the announcement, Winston “was violently attacked, physically injured, and, after filing a complaint against the offending officer, she was arrested by Roanoke Police Department for merely exercising her constitutional right to file her complaint against the officer.”

The call to action was put out by the Randolph County branch of the NAACP “and residents of the Historic East Roanoke Community,” according to the announcement. The stated purpose of the rally was to stand up “for local victims subjected to police violence, misconduct and discrimination.”

However, the venue for the rally was changed after members of First Baptist Church – including Roanoke City Council member John Frank Houston – were shown bodycam footage from the arrest of Winston that clearly contradicted her claims.

Roanoke police chief Jonathan Caldwell showed nearly 30 minutes of that same bodycam footage at Monday’s city council meeting as a public way of defending officer Tyler Murphy, who was accused of the wrongdoing. In the videos the only thing that could be construed as confrontational were some brief verbal exchanges between the officers and Winston and another woman who was a passenger in Winston’s vehicle. But Murphy routinely and quickly defused those exchanges and was calm and professional throughout the arrest. There was no trace of physical violence at any point in the footage.

Caldwell also presented the council members with mug shot photos from that night, and the photos show no evidence of physical injuries to Winston.
The video drew a scathing response from council member Kesa Johnston, a practicing attorney in Roanoke. Johnston spoke for just over three minutes and was very direct about the impact that the type of false accusations that Winston is accused of can have.

“These cops every day deal with cussing, fighting, guns, drugs, and then they have to deal with this crap, and so do their families,” Johnston said. “And I also want to commend them because they also have to deal with so-called leaders who don’t know the facts, and perhaps don’t even know how to figure out the facts. You should be ashamed of yourselves for stirring up something you didn’t even know anything about. Now it’s kind of like the woman that’s really raped, and the women who cry rape that weren’t raped. What we have now is every time there’s a real issue we won’t even know because of this all the time.”

And she specifically defended Murphy as well. “So thank you to all of the officers, and especially to Tyler Murphy, because I have seen so many videos of you because we’re always getting complaints because you’re actually fighting crime,” Johnston said. “And in every video you have dealt with things I never could have dealt with. And you’ve dealt with it in a way that nobody sitting here, I would strive to believe, could ever deal with it. So thank you for that. And to all of you. I hope we can all do much better because surely there are smart enough people in this room to do better.”

Speaking after the bodycam footage was shown at the council meeting Caldwell made a point to say that he welcomes complaints.

“We want you to come up and complain if we’ve done something wrong,” he said. But he also said that if those complaints are unfounded, as appears to be the case for Winston, his department will exercise its ability to protect itself and its officers from false accusations.
