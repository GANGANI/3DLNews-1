Brumley

Angie Chapman Brumley, 41, of Rainsville, died Tuesday, Nov. 8, 2005, at her residence.

Services will be Friday, Nov. 11, at 11 a.m., at Rainsville Funeral Home Chapel with the Revs. Howard Bouldin, J. T. Williams, and Max Turner officiating. Visitation will be Thursday, 10 a.m.-9 p.m. and two hours prior to the service. Burial will follow at Rainsville Memorial Park.

Survivors include her husband, Gene Brumley Jr. of Rainsville; daughters and son-in-law, Sommer and Adam Moore of Rainsville, Chasaty Johnson of Rainsville; four grandchildren; step-parents, Jimmy and Barbara Bouldin of Rainsville; brothers, Randy Chapman and Tony Chapman, both of Section; sisters, Lisa White and Tammy Smalley, both of Henagar, and Erica Newsom of Rainsville; father-in-aw, Phillip Bramley Sr.; several nieces and nephews; special friend, Patra Smith.

She was preceded in death by her mother, JoAnn Bouldin; father, David Chapman; and several aunts and uncles.

Boman

Olivia Suzanne Porter Boman, 26, of Clarksville, Tenn., died unexpectedly Saturday, Nov. 5, 2005, while visiting her parents in Albertville.

Services will be today, Thursday, Nov. 10, at 10 a.m., at Christ Episcopal Church in Albertville. The Rev. Van Forman will be officiating. Visitation was Wednesday night, Nov. 9, at Albertville Memorial Funeral Home.

She grew up in Guntersville and was married to Jonathon Boman on Aug. 29, 2003. They lived in Ft. Huachuca, Ariz. while he attended military intelligence school and were transferred to Fort Campbell, Ky. until they moved to Clarksville, Tenn. in 2004. Her husband is currently serving in Iraq. The couple have a six and one-half month old daughter that was also visiting with her mother.

Survivors include her husband, Jonathon Edward Boman; daughter, Haley Grace Boman; mother, Pat Wright Potts (Mel) of Albertville; step-mother, Laurie Armstrong Porter of Raleigh, N. c.; half-sister, Kaitlyn Porter of Raleigh, N. C.; step-sisters and brother-in-law, Meredith and Chris Jackson of Albertville, Kelly Potts of Albertville; grandmother, Ann Hare Porter of Sylcauga.

She was preceded in death by her father, Michael Otto Porter; grandmother, Claudia M. Wright; grandfather, Robert Wilson (Dub) Wright; and grandfather, the Rev. Ralph Benton Porter.

In lieu of flowers, the family requests donations be made in memory of Suzanne to the ALUTMA savings account set up for her daughter, Haley Grace, at BancorpSouth Bank under Patricia Wright Potts as custodian, 7100 U. S. Highway 431, P. O. Box 1488, Albertville, AL 35950 with attention to Leslie Sampler.

Kirtland

J. W. (Pop) Kirtland, 78, of Altoona, died Sunday, Nov. 6, 2005, at his residence.

Services were Wednesday, Nov. 8, at 11 a.m., at Albertville Memorial Chapel. The Revs. Wayne Roden and Jerry Owens officiated. Burial was in Mt. Olive Cemetery in Altoona.

Survivors include his wife, Joan Woodall Kirtland; daughter and son-in-law, Cindy and Eric Brown; son and daughter-in-law, Bart and Dana Kirtland; four grandchildren; chosen daughters, Ann Ehl Alexander and Kay Ehl Jackson; sisters, Veola Kirkland, Maxine Cody, and Margaret Fox; brother, Sylvester Kirtland Jr.; a host of nieces and nephews; and special friends.

Pallbearers were Jeff Scott, Edward Thrasher, Shane Alexander, Greer Davis, David Harp and Chuck Gamblin.

Honorary pallbearers were all friends and neighbors, Dr. K. J. Shah, Dr. Robert Conry, Marshall County Hospice, Dale Johnson and employees of Boaz Discount Drugs.

The family requests donations be made to the Marshall County Hospice.

Sommerville

James L. Sommerville, 93, of Asheville, N.C., died Saturday, Nov. 5, 2005, at The Laurels of Green Tree Nursing Home.

A memorial service will be Saturday, Nov. 12, at 2 p.m., at First Presbyterian Church of Asheville, N.C. Visitation will be at the Sommerville home Friday, Nov. 11, from 3-6 p.m.

A gradate of Pennsylvania State University in 1932 with a degree in ceramic engineering, he was employed with Owens-Illinois Glass Company until his retirement and was a consultant with the Kerr Glass Company for five years after his retirement. He served in the Army Corps of Engineers during World War II and held the rank of major at the time of his discharge. He was a member of the First Presbyterian Church of Asheville, holding several positions over the years, a member of the American Ceramic Society, the National Institute of Ceramic Engineers, the National Railway Historical Society, SCORE and the Rotary Club of Asheville.

Survivors include his wife, Juanita Morgan Sommerville, a native of Albertville; sons, James Laing Sommerville III and his wife Betty of Somerville, N.J., and Morgan Sommerville and his wife Avi of Weaverville, N.C.; sister, Mrs. J. C. Sherrill of Homewood, Ill.; five grandchildren.

Condolences may be sent through the Web site: www.Morrisfamilyfuneralhome.com.

Steed

Iris Dodd Steed, 87, of Boaz, died Thursday, Nov. 3, 2005, at her residence.

Services were Tuesday, Nov. 8, at McRae Chapel with the Revs. Keith Dodd and Terry Sisco officiating. Burial followed in Hillcrest Cemetery. McRae Funeral Home in Boaz directed.

Born May 17, 1918 to Edward and Maude Smith Dodd, she was a retired school teacher.

Survivors include a son and daughter-in-law, Bob and Angelyn Steed of South Carolina; daughter and son-in-law, Ellen and Bill Perryman of Guntersville; two grandchildren; one great-grandson; siblings, Kathleen D. Morgan of Boaz, Audrey D. Bales of North Carolina, Edward and Juanita Dodd of Boaz, Annis and James Nelson of New Mexico, Keith and Iris Dodd of Boaz, Helen Edwards Dodd of Boaz, and Eleanor Green of Falkville; special caregivers, Debbie McWhorter and Dorothy Smart.

She was preceded in death by her husband, R. P. Steed, parents, Edward and Maude Dodd; brothers, Wayne Dodd, J. P. Dodd; granddaughter, Anna Bains.

Pallbearers were David Dodd, Kevin Dodd, Barry Dodd, Jessie Dodd, Michael Dodd, Michael Eidson, Garry Hammett, Morris Beck, David Nelson, Edward Green, and Matthew Green.

Survivors include her sons and daughters-in-law, Keith and Gayle Thomas of Boaz, Donnie and Bobbie Nell Thomas of Mt. Wigley

Mildred Grace Wigley, 77, of Horton, died Sunday, Nov. 6, 2005.

Services were Tuesday, Nov. 8, at New Clear Creek Primitive Baptist Church with Elders Ricky Harcrow, Wendell Robertson and Stoney Dorning officiating. The church choir provided the music. Burial followed in the adjoining cemetery in Horton. Albertville Memorial Chapel directed.

Survivors include her husband, Victor H. F. Wigley; daughter and son-in-law, Carla and Dalmus Wigley Davidson of Arab; sons and daughters-in-law, Sherwin and Lori Wigley of Douglas, Wayne and Gladys Wigley of Jacksonville, and Gary and Jada Lynn Wigley of Douglas; sister, Lynoitte Townson of Evansville, Ind.; brothers, James Ivey of Albertville, and Jerry Iveny of Columbus, Ga.; seven grandchildren; seven great-grandchildren.

Pallbearers were Chris Wigley, Craig Davidson, Chad Wigley, Casey Wigley, Clint Wigley and Kenneth Ivey.

Honorary pallbearers were Tanya Wigley and Wendy Davidson.
