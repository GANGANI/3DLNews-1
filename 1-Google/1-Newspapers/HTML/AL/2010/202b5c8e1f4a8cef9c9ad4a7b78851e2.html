Klibanoff house on Monte Sano gets sizable price cut, now for sale at $6.95 million

  • Updated: Apr. 02, 2010, 1:06 p.m.|
  • Published: Apr. 02, 2010, 12:06 p.m.
By
  • Mike Marshall, The Huntsville Times

HUNTSVILLE, AL --Step inside the $7 million house on Monte Sano. Go ahead. See the sculpture by Anthony Quinn's son, Lorenzo, as you walk in the front door.

Walk onto the floors of heart-sawn maple, the finest maple, the kind that doesn't blemish.

The walls in the den are made of South African pearwood. The floors in the front foyer are made of South African metallic slate.

The media room in the basement has the same digital light projector as actor Tom Cruise and George Lucas, the legendary filmmaker, have.

View full sizeRobin Conn / The Huntsville TimesView of the living room from upstairs of Daniel Klibanoff's mountaintop estate on Monte Sano.

Outside the media room, there is a ticket booth that's equipped to print movie tickets. On the wall by the booth is a first-edition "Gone with the Wind" poster, one of three in the world.

"The house itself is like staying at a five-diamond hotel day after day, night after night, month after month," said Daniel Klibanoff, who owns the home. "You wake up to a great view, and you go to sleep to a great view."

The home is at 3307 Lookout Drive, on the northwest rim of Monte Sano. It has almost 17,000 square feet, including the pool house, and a panoramic view of Huntsville, much of northern Madison County and beyond.

The house has seven bedrooms, nine baths and a three-car garage. The lot is 26 acres, part of it on the northern slope of the mountain.

Julie Lockwood, the Realtor who is showing the house, quipped, "It's a $7 million view - we're just throwing the house in."

The official price is $6.95 million. [See the MLS listing]

On a clear, sunny afternoon this week, with northeast Huntsville and northeastern Madison County unfolding before him, Klibanoff pointed out some of the sites in the distance.

"Fayetteville is just right up there," he said. "We're only 25 miles from the state line. That's what you're looking at from here."

Then, looking to the east, he said, "You can see (the lights from) the Moontown airport at night."

Klibanoff, a Huntsville businessman, bought the land in December 1997. Construction on the house began in January 1999. In July 2003, Klibanoff moved into "my dream," as he called it.

He spent long hours planning the house with Alan Dynerman, an architect in Washington, D.C. The inspiration for the house had been the only house in Alabama designed by Frank Lloyd Wright.

That house, built in 1939, was owned by Stanley and Mildred Rosenbaum of Florence. Mildred Rosenbaum was Klibanoff's aunt, and he spent a considerable portion of his childhood at the home.

For many years, Klibanoff, captivated by the Rosenbaums' house, studied Wright. After he bought the property on Monte Sano, he asked his cousin Alvin Rosenbaum to recommend an architect who could design a house in the mold of Wright.

"It started on a cocktail napkin with an architect," Klibanoff said. "We spent a year and a half discussing what it would be like before we had construction documents."

Klibanoff's philosophy: to build something unique and special, he needed to be methodical. Don't rush it. Let's get it right.

After it was built, the house was cited for an American Institute of Architects Award of Merit.

"A house should be secondary to family," Klibanoff said. "Sometimes, the house almost makes it a priority. My ego (now) is in a different place."

About three years ago, after the birth of his second child, he put the house up for sale.

"Originally, it was difficult," he said. "I had a hard time letting go. At this point, I won't have a hard time letting go."

Last summer, the house was listed for $11.9 million. Lockwood, the listing agent since November, said she has had some recent luck with the reduced price.

"The person who will buy this house loves Monte Sano and loves Huntsville," she said. "There's really nothing comparable to this house at all (in Huntsville)."
