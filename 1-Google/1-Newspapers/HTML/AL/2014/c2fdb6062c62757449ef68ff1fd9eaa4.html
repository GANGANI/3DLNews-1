    Rundhaug would bring experience in finance, construction

    • By Wes Mayberry
    • Nov 19, 2014
    • Nov 19, 2014
    • 0
    • Facebook
    • Twitter
    • WhatsApp
    • SMS
    • Email
    • Facebook
    • Twitter
    • WhatsApp
    • SMS
    • Email
    • Print
    • Save

    The Scottsboro City Board of Education’s superintendent search will continue on Friday, when current Arizona resident Dr. Richard Rundhaug will be in town to make a short visit to the system’s schools before participating in a meet-and-greet from 4 p.m. to 5:15 p.m. at the Heritage Center. This meet-and-greet will give school staff members and the community an opportunity to get to know him prior to his public interview from 6:30 p.m. to 8:30 p.m. in the Bynum Auditorium at the Page Administration Building. This interview is open to the community but questions will only be fielded from the school board.

    Since 2008, Rundhaug has served as the superintendent of Willcox Unified School District in Willcox, Arizona, overseeing an enrollment of 1,300. He has a Ph. D. from Capella University and currently earns an annual salary of $120,000.

    Rundhaug started his career in Globe, Arizona, as an assistant principal in the Globe Unified School District from 2001 to 2003. In 2003, he accepted the principal position in the Maricopa County Regional School District in Phoenix and was later promoted to director of data and technology. In 2005, Rundhaug was named the director of research and development in the Yuma Union High School District in Yuma, Arizona. From 2006 to 2008, he served as the assistant superintendent for the Coolidge Unified School District in Coolidge, Arizona, before moving into his current position in June of 2008.

    In his application, Rundhaug cited his experience leading his district through new legislative requirements, his planning and leadership through periods of transition and his active research into ways of getting parents more involved in the academic lives of their high school students as valuable assets that he would bring as the new superintendent in Scottsboro.

    “I have the ability to interpret complex educational and financial data to staff and the community and to provide leadership in setting priorities to meet legislative challenges in a way that continues to meet community values,” he said. “Not only have I been able to explain complex issues, I have been able to work with my staff to establish clear solutions and have provided leadership in my district, my county and throughout the state.”

    Last Friday, Rundhaug said he applied for this position because he is really attracted to the area, adding that he would bring a good mix of experience to the district.

    “I’m really drawn to that geographic area. My boys and I are outdoorsmen- we like to hunt and fish- so Scottsboro looks to be a great place that would fit our lifestyle,” he said. “And I feel like I bring a lot to the table. I have been in charge of both finance and construction throughout my career, and I have overseen efforts to increase academic standing. I’m looking forward to meeting everyone on my interview date and letting them know that I’m a very down-to-earth person.”

    This is the fourth story in a five-part series introducing the candidates for the superintendent position for Scottsboro City Schools.
