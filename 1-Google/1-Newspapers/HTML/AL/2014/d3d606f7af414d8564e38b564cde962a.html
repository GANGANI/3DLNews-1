Orville Ray Hubble, 90

  • Nov 1, 2014
  • Nov 1, 2014

A well-known Guntersville man who once owned a pie factory and in 1998 was named the Chamber of Commerce Citizen of the Year, Orville Ray Hubble died Tuesday, Oct. 28, 2014, at home. He was 90.

He was born Feb. 27, 1924, in Arkansas to parents Sant Wilson and Frannie Bolding Hubble. His mother was a native of Georgia Mountain, and when he was 11 the family moved to Alabama where Ray grew up in Guntersville.

He was in the Merchant Marines during World War II and enjoyed adventures on the water throughout his life, once traveling 2,000 miles on a small boat with the late Bill Harris from Guntersville to Lake Michigan.

He opened a fried-pie business in Guntersville after the war and sold them throughout north Alabama. In 1958, he became the first independent business owner in the city to give his employees a pension plan.

He sold his pie factory in 1980. A year later, he bought Cub’s Lamps on Highway 431. He sold it 8 years later.

He was active in the Jaycees and helped build what is now County Park No. 1 north of the river bridge, was a co-owner of Val Monte Resort for 20 years, was instrumental in warding off logging at the state park and sponsored boat races on the lake.

He was a long-time member of the board of directors for the Guntersville Museum, where his most recent project was starting a garden outside one of the galleries’ doors. His family asked that memorials be made to the O.R. Hubble Garden.

Mr. Hubble is survived by 3 sons, Gaylor Hubble, Andray Hubble (Martha) and Michel Hubble, all of Guntersville; a daughter, Bonita Hubble of Guntersville; a sister, Martina Kennamer of Tennessee; 3 grandchildren, Heatherly Teal Fason (Chris) of Guntersville, Jason Hubble (B.J.) of Albertville and Troy Teal (Kippi) of Albertville; special friend Naomi “Honey Bee’’ Herbert; 12 great-grandchildren and 4 great-great grandchildren.

He was preceded in death by his parents; 2 brothers, Kenneth and Lavon Hubble; a sister, Patsie McCoy; a daughter, Adonis Hubble Teal; a son, Stephan Hubble; and a grandson, Jonce Hubble.

Funeral services were Friday at Carr Funeral Home with Rev. Chris Herbert officiating. Burial was at Marshall Memorial Gardens. Pallbearers were Larry McCoy, Jerry Rowe, Bruce Underwood, Allen Payne, Chris Fason and Jeff McLaughlin.
