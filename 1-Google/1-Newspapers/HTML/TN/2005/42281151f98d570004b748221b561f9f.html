Askew Continues Leadership in Engineering Field

By Lane Gardner Camp

- Mark West Askew
Name: Mark West Askew
Position: President
Company: Askew Hargraves Harcourt & Associates Inc.
Basics: Askew recently began a two-year term as a national director of the American Council of Engineering Cos. of Tennessee.

Mark West Askew, president of Askew Hargraves Harcourt & Associates Inc. in Memphis, was appointed earlier this summer as a national director of the American Council of Engineering Cos. of Tennessee. His two-year term began in August.

Askew likens his national director position to that of a Congressional senator. He represents Tennessee ACEC members to the national council, which he calls "our main lobbying group."

ACEC promotes the business interests of the engineering industry to Congress, federal agencies and international organizations. It also seeks passage of legislation and policies that create a favorable business climate for engineering companies.

Recruiting new professionals. A member of ACEC for about 20 years with numerous local and state positions under his belt, Askew is well equipped to tackle hot issues at the state level. One item of concern for Askew is the low number of students who are choosing engineering as a career. He thinks it's due to the math and science requirements.

For Askew, a career in engineering was natural because he was always good at math and science and learned to love combining those elements to build things.

"A lot of kids go into computer science now - a field that didn't exist when I was in college," Askew said. "The draw-down on the number of engineering graduates will weaken the strength of our economy if we don't do something soon."

Strong talent pool. Despite his concern about the future pool of engineers, Askew has high praise for the current Memphis engineering community. He is always perplexed by companies that bring in outside consultants when, he said, "We have all the talent right here to design anything."

Askew himself does not limit his engineering work to Memphis. In fact, when a client has multiple projects around the country, it helps that he is licensed in 30 states.

Askew is senior partner at his 45-person firm. His team specializes in civil, structural and mechanical engineering, land surveying and landscape architecture.

Finding his calling. Born and raised in Memphis, Askew is a graduate of the University of Memphis, where he earned a bachelor's degree in civil engineering following some extensive world travel between his junior and senior years.

It was while working on an earthen dam in the Australian Outback in 1969 that Askew found his calling as a civil engineer.

Other travels took him to Thailand to teach English, where he learned from a Peace Corps volunteer how to build and provide sanitation services for impoverished people. From Thailand he traveled across Burma, India, West Pakistan, Afghanistan, Iran and Turkey with a brief stay in Europe. After that, he was ready to return to Memphis and complete his degree in 1976.

Following graduation, he struck out again - this time for Saudi Arabia as a quantity surveyor at a desert construction site for a natural gas liquefaction plant. After that assignment, Askew traveled more across Asia, and then took a job as a structural engineer in Honolulu. He eventually made his way back to Memphis.

Married with three children, Askew started his business in 1986. He gives credit for his success to "a lot of great people like my brother and clients too many to name."
