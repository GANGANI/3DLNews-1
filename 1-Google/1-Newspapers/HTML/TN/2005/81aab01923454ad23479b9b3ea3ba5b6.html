Farnsworth Investment Founder Looks to Future

LANCE ALLAN | The Daily News

NEW ERA: Tommy Farnsworth III, left, and Tommy Farnsworth Jr. are working to move the industrial real estate company built by the latter through a period of transition. -- Photograph By Brad Johnson

Farnsworth Investment Co.'s recent sale of its entire 28-building industrial portfolio signals a new beginning for the longstanding Memphis industrial real estate company.

The sale included just under 2 million square feet of industrial space and 169 acres of undeveloped land. But the company is not all that will experience change as a result of the deal. Company founder Tommy Farnsworth Jr. is scaling back his involvement in the industry after a 40-year real estate career.

Changing roles. Farnsworth, who founded the company 20 years ago, will still serve as chairman of Farnsworth Investment Co., but will no longer be involved in the firm's daily management.

"I've had a great career, enjoyed exactly what I've done and created," he said. "However, it became apparent to me that it was time to not be quite as busy and active as I've been these last 40-odd years."

But that doesn't mean Farnsworth is retiring to the beach. He simply doesn't intend to carry out an active management role with the firm - which, under an agreement with buyers Net Magan Property Owner One LP, will continue to manage the portfolio and serve as a partner in developing the undeveloped land included in the sale. Son Tommy Farnsworth III and partner Michael Mullis will head up the company going forward. Farnsworth Jr. will, however, serve in an advisory role as the firm makes the transition.

"What we'll be focusing on is what's next, and that is the 169 acres of land that we want to see the orderly development of," Farnsworth III said. "You'll see the same kind of real estate that we've been developing for the last 20 years. The demand we've seen over the course of the last four or five months has steadily increased, and I think it's a good time to be providing product type like we are accustomed to providing."

Expert guidance. He said Farnsworth Jr.'s experience is needed in that move.

"In my father's early career, he had partners that he worked with, so his insight on that will be invaluable," Farnsworth III said. "It will certainly be different for me because my only partners in my career have been my father and Michael Mullis. I think in large part, we'll be doing much the same type of stuff we did before. We'll just be doing it with a partner rather than for our own account."

Farnsworth worked with Joseph Mizrachi and David Alcalay and their families, who comprise Net Magan Property Owner One LP, for more than seven months on the deal. Net Magan purchased more than 30 industrial buildings and vacant parcels from Farnsworth for $78 million, according to deeds filed with the Shelby County Register of Deeds office through Aug. 26. The properties include a parcel at 5400 Distriplex Farms Drive, which sold for $10.2 million.

The deal took longer to close than the involved parties expected.

"There were a lot of moving parts to it," Farnsworth III said. "It was a big deal, obviously. In hindsight, I'm not sure that is as big a surprise in light of how big the deal was. We're glad it's finally done."

Forming an exit strategy. Farnsworth Jr. played a major role in the decision to move ahead with the transaction. He wanted to continue as an investor, but no longer to serve as an active operator, Farnsworth III said.

"We wanted to allow an exit strategy for him," Farnsworth III said. "He will continue to be an adviser to Michael Mullis and me, as well as the buyers. And he's not a wallflower. I expect if he sees something he doesn't like, he'll point it out, and of course we want that. He's been the driving force behind our company and is a capable guy. We all value his opinions."

Meanwhile, Farnsworth Jr. hopes to travel more with his wife. He will continue to serve on the board of directors for both SunTrust Bank and the Memphis-Shelby County Airport Authority. And he is now involved in some Florida investments.

New ventures. Farnsworth Jr. is partnering in two projects, an office land development and a health care facility, both in Indian River County, Fla. It seems that even in attempting to pull back from some responsibilities, he can't escape the real estate industry.

"I don't know what the word retire means," he said. "If you're in the real estate business, you just never fully retire. If you're a stockbroker, you just step away from it. You still own your stocks, but you don't have to sell stocks or investment advice. Real estate is something you create and it basically sticks with you as long as you have an interest in it, whatever that interest may be."

Market advances. Farnsworth Jr. is pleased to see how far the Memphis industrial market has come. But there are still some changes he hopes to witness.

"I'm very pleased with the fact that we've become such a large distribution center," he said. "That ties in largely with the benefits of being near the airport. I've seen the rapid growth of Memphis, particularly through the eyes of the airport, having served on the board for 16 years. I've seen the wonderful opportunities that have come for the community through the opportunities at the airport.

"But I think it would be appropriate for us to have a self assessment from time to time keeping the leading edge that we've had for a number of years."
