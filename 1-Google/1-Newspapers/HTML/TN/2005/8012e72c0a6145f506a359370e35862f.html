Sheriff's Captain Demoted, Suspended After Affair With Secretary

David Hamby Demoted, Given 30 Days Off Without Pay

  • Friday, July 1, 2005

The second in command to Sheriff John Cupp has been demoted and suspended after having an affair with his secretary.

Sheriff Cupp said Capt. David Hamby will be reduced in rank by two pay steps from detective captain to sheriff's captain. He said it is a one-step reduction in rank.

Capt. Hamby will be suspended 30 days without pay.

Sheriff Cupp said when he returns on Aug. 12, he will be captain over patrol, the SWAT Team and court officers.

The secretary, Teri Rogers, resigned last week, the sheriff said.

Sheriff Cupp said the affair was an inappropriate relationship between a supervisor and subordinate.

He said none of the affair was conducted on department time.

Sheriff Cupp said last month he received information that the two were having "an inappropriate relationship."

He said his department began "an inquiry," and he said he then went directly to Capt. Hamby on the allegations.

Sheriff Cupp said Capt. Hamby told him "he did wrong. He apologized. He said he was embarrassed by what he had done."

Capt. Hamby's wife, Denise Hamby, is financial officer for the sheriff's department.

Capt. Hamby was in charge of security at the recent Isaac Jones III death penalty case.

Ms. Rogers had been with the sheriff's department for four years. She was secretary to the criminal investigators.

Capt. Hamby was second in command to Sheriff Cupp since chief deputy and assistant chief posts are currently unfilled.
