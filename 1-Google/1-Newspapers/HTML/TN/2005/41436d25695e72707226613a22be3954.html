Mud Island Project Enters New Phase

Demand still high as last homes go up in Island Point

By Rosalind Guy

STILL BUILDING: Island Point LLC has purchased nearly nine acres for a new 73-lot phase of the Island Point neighborhood on Mud Island. Homes were pre-sold in the range of $157,000 to $230,000. -- Photograph By Rosalind Guy

Lenox Homes LLC, Bronze Builders and Chris Christian Homes LLC hit pay dirt when they set their sights 10 years ago on a picturesque stretch of land along the Mississippi River now known as Island Point.

The homes that have gone up in the neighborhood since then have seen huge demand. In the beginning, in fact, demand was so high that more than 70 people camped out to secure their lots of choice on that prime strip of land.

New construction. Island Point LLC now has purchased 8.83 acres for a new phase of the project from Island Park II LLC for $3.2 million, according to The Daily News Online, www.memphisdailynews.com. Island Point LLC financed the property through First Tennessee Bank for $11 million.

The 8.83 acres was approved in December for 73 lots and 10 common, open spaces. Phase one of the project features 86 lots and nine open spaces on 10.67 acres.

Chris Christian, owner of Chris Christian Homes, said the project team is beginning work on the last phase of the Island Point development, with 71 lots left to finish. All of the lots were snapped up in a lottery held in March, and homes were pre-sold in the range of $157,000 to $230,000. Demand was so great that the company went an unconventional route to ensure fairness in meeting that demand.

"People were saying they were going to camp out four or five days ahead of time, and we didn't want to be responsible for something like that," Christian said.

With the lottery system, interested buyers pulled a number and selected lots according to the numbers they had chosen. Construction already has begun on those homes, and Christian expects to begin closing on the sales at the end of August.

"We've been very fortunate to have had such a large presence (on Mud Island) for as long as we have."
- Chris Christian
owner, Chris Christian Homes, and partner in Island Point LLC

Residential draw. The area's prime location has spawned great demand since the first homes were built on Mud Island in Harbor Town, a 130-acre community founded in 1989 by Henry Turley Co. and Belz Enterprises. Location is also the reason that Christian, J. Kevin Hyneman of Lenox Homes and Jeffrey Bronze of Bronze Builders feel pride in having played a part in developing the stretch of land along the Mississippi River.

"We've been very fortunate to have had such a large presence for as long as we have," Christian said.

Location also was the determining factor that led Emily Patterson, 26, and husband Adam to purchase a home in the neighborhood. Patterson, director of grants and business development for the Greater Memphis Arts Council, said she and Adam have enjoyed living in the center of the action, and they were drawn to the diversity of the area.

"With the revitalization of the Downtown area, we wanted to be a part of it all," she said.

Continued resurgence. Downtown's increasing attraction has come not only from new residential development, but also the restaurants, art galleries and other businesses that continue to follow new residents to the area. Growing entertainment options, including the Memphis Grizzlies and Memphis Redbirds, along with entertainment venues such as Peabody Place, add fuel to the flame of Downtown development.

And new housing construction is occurring from the revitalization of Uptown in the north end of Downtown to burgeoning development in the South End of Downtown near the South Main Arts District. The facelift has turned the community into a hotspot for homebuyers.

Areas of Downtown. Over the years, Christian said, his company has seen demand go from OK to strong to phenomenal. He added that competition among Downtown developments hasn't been fierce, in part because the community offers a range of areas and niches for new development. For instance, Christian said the people who choose Island Point want to buy specifically on Mud Island.

Patterson, who has lived in her home for two years, said she continues to enjoy the convenience of the location. And she said driving across the Auction Street bridge almost feels like taking a vacation.

"You drive across the bridge and you enter your own little world - sometimes it looks like a movie set or something," she said, referring to the residents out jogging, rollerblading or walking their dogs along the river.

One more project. Christian said the development team is nearing completion of construction on the island.

"Already, we've built nearly 800 homes," Christian said.

The partners' last project on the island, Riverpoint, is set to begin construction in December. Presales for the 103 planned lots will likely begin in November using another lottery system that will be conducted in phases. The project is slated for completion in February 2007.

Already, there's a waiting list for the homes.

"When Riverpoint is gone, it's gone," Christian said, adding that the only way to get a home on the island at that point will be through resale.

And though they're reluctant to leave the island, Patterson and her husband placed a for-sale sign in their yard a couple of weeks ago.

"We want a bigger home to start the next phase of our life," Patterson said.
