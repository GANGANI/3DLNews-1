Worker Falls Nearly 100 Feet Into Tennessee River

Scratched Up, But OK, After Scary Plunge Into River

  • Friday, February 25, 2005

A worker at the Sculpture Garden at Bluff View fell nearly 100 feet into Tennessee River on Friday afternoon. Officials said he was scratched up, but OK.

At 2:30 p.m., Chattanooga firefighters responded on the fall incident. Upon arrival Chattanooga Fire Department crews confirmed that a construction worker had indeed fallen from the slopes into the river.

The worker, James Harvey, 29, had fallen close to 100 feet into the river, as stated by bystanders.

Mr. Harvey climbed out of the river and firefighters that were staged at the museum lowered a life preserver down to him. Meanwhile, another CFD crew staged at Ross’s Landing where a construction crew with Continental Construction happened by with a small “john-boat.” The firefighters entered the boat with the construction crew and traveled up river to Mr. Harvey.

They found Mr. Harvey clinging to the rocks just above where he had fallen. Firefighters helped Mr. Harvey into the boat and took him back to Ross’s Landing, where Hamilton County EMS was standing by.

Mr. Harvey was cold and a little scratched up, but seemed to be in good condition. EMS transported the Rossville man to Erlanger Medical Center just to make sure he was indeed OK.

He was wearing a harness, but it is unclear at this point whether he had it securely tied off. No one else was injured.

He is a maintenance worker for the Bluff View Art District.
