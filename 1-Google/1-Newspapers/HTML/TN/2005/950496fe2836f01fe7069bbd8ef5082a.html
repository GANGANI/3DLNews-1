McAlister's Plans New Memphis Stores

Downtown, North Mississippi among areas for expansion

LANCE ALLAN | The Daily News

Adding to the Downtown resurgence, a regional brand that has experienced rapid growth nationally now has its sights set on a spot near the Mississippi River.

JME Inc., a franchise company that owns and operates 16 McAlister's Deli restaurants in Tennessee and Mississippi, plans to open its 17th location in Downtown this fall. Slated to open Sept. 15, the restaurant will be located on the plaza level of the SunTrust Bank building just off Main Street.

"Memphis Downtown had a history of being a very difficult place to do business," said Mark Garrett, CEO of JME Inc. "But Since the FedExForum opened, since the new residences Downtown are starting to pop up everywhere, the luxury condominiums, we found that a lot of our population doesn't just work down there anymore, they live there, as well. And of course, during the daytime there are 70,000 people that work down there. So that's 70,000 people who eat lunch, and that's our crowd."

McAlister's Deli operates 170 restaurants in 17 states. About 85 percent of those are franchise stores, including the JME-run Memphis stores.

Unique location. The Downtown McAlister's will only open for the lunchtime crowd on Monday through Friday and will close in the 5 p.m. to 6 p.m. range. That, and the fact that the deli will serve pastries and coffee in the morning, makes it a unique store for the McAlister's chain.

The restaurant eventually might extend its hours into the evening, depending on business in the market.

"We're working right now on some opportunities that would allow us to do some nighttime business, particularly event business," Garrett said. "McAlister's Corp. is looking now at what we need to do with our menu selections to accentuate our evening business. We're about 60 percent lunch, 40 percent dinner. A typical business is the reverse of that."

Market-wide. Growth for McAlister's in the Memphis area is not centered solely on Downtown. Last month, JME opened its 16th restaurant at 3855 Hacks Cross Road. Three others have opened within the last two years in Memphis and Olive Branch.

And the franchisee has plans for more. JME hopes to open two restaurants in and around Wolfchase Galleria and stores in Millington and Hernando over the next two years.

"The reception that the Memphis market has given to the McAlister's concept has been overwhelmingly positive," Garrett said. "After our initial success, the branding we've tried to do, we've tried to connect ourselves with everything Memphis. We have a relationship with the Redbirds, the Grizzlies. We're associated closely with the FedEx St. Jude (Classic).

"By doing those things, we've been able to connect our brand with everything that's Memphis, and we want to continue to do that."

Regional concept. McAlister's quick/casual deli concept was founded in 1989 by dentist Don Newcomb, his two sons and a real estate development assistant in Oxford, Miss.

JME was the first franchisee of McAlister's when Garrett and his partner, Dr. John Fullenwider, decided to give it a go in late 1994. The two, who had built a relationship through a partnership in the health care industry, were lunch regulars at McAlister's in Oxford. After hearing that the company planned to offer franchising rights, the two jumped in, opening the first franchise store in Germantown in 1995.

They soon followed with a second store in Poplar Plaza near the University of Memphis. They haven't looked back since.

"Those two are extremely well attended by our customers," Garrett said. "We've got a great customer base at those two locations, as well as the others in Memphis now."

Although founded in Oxford and headquartered in Ridgeland, Miss., the restaurant has close ties to Memphis, as well. One of its signature sandwiches is even named The Memphian.

Memphis flavor. JME is headquartered in New Albany, Miss., but operates a second office behind its Cordova store off Germantown Parkway.

"The brand, the name - and particularly in Memphis, having been such a long-term name - the brand itself has a great deal of value," Garrett said. "You open a restaurant and people know what they're going to get."

Beyond the Memphis market, JME owns the franchise rights to Panola and Tunica counties in Mississippi. Garrett doesn't expect to open a restaurant inside a casino anytime soon, but a successful franchise does operate inside Harrah's Casino in New Orleans.

National push. With the corporation itself looking to expand into Illinois and Kansas by the end of the year, the local brand with its signature sweet iced tea and Southern-themed sandwiches is making a national push.

"It's a portable concept," Garrett said. "It has legs under it. This is our 10th year and we now know it has great shelf life, the McAlister's concept does."
