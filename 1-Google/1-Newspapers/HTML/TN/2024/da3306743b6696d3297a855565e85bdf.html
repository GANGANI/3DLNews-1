New bleachers delayed due to complications getting steel

  • By CURTIS MARLIN THE PORTLAND LEADER
  • Feb 14, 2024
  • Feb 14, 2024 Updated Mar 27, 2024

The permanent bleachers planned for the home side of Memorial Field-Edgar Johnson Stadium has been put on hold. The Sumner County Board of Education met in a planning session last week and complications with getting steel products was one of the reasons for the delay.

Beech High School and Hendersonville High School are also facing the same situation as Portland High School.

“We couldn’t use the steel company the county had planned to get the bleachers built,” Portland Principal David Woods explained. “There is still a problem with the supply chain since Covid. So, the county has to rebid the job and that takes time.”

The Sumner County Board of Education discussed two proposals at the Tuesday, Feb. 6 meeting.

The first one, or option 1, is to have “staged” bleachers for the 2024 season so Portland High School varsity and junior varsity, along with Portland West and Portland East, can have home games. The staged bleachers would be similar to the ones Beech High School used last season at their home games.

The second proposal, or option 2, is to have all Portland teams, high school, and the two middle schools to travel to all of their games.

Portland has five teams coming to town during the 2024 campaign. Greenbrier, Beech, Hendersonville, and Westmoreland always bring huge crowds as well as the homecoming battle with Clay County.

“If the board votes option 1 in, the temporary bleachers will be ready for the 2024 season,” Woods pointed out. “After the final home game of the year, those will be taken down and the permanent ones will be built by the 2025 season.”

The final vote will be held in March on the two options.

“I feel confident the board will vote for option one,” Woods remarked.

The stadium issue arose last August when the visitor stands at Beech High School collapsed in the middle of the night. A check of all the football stadiums in Sumner County saw Hendersonville, Beech, and Portland needing immediate repairs or to be demolished.

There were newer aluminum bleachers on the home side at Memorial Field-Edgar Johnson. Those were used by the visiting teams while the concrete stands built in 1973 were closed. Portland fans sat on the visitors’ side next to the railroad tracks this past season.
