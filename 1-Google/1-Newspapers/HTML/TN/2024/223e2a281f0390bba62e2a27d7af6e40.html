    Hawkins County Commission names crossing after Vietnam vet and Purple Heart recipient

    • By TESSA WORLEY tworley@sixriversmedia.com

      Tessa Worley

      Reporter

      • Author email
    • Feb 15, 2024
    • Feb 15, 2024

    Harry Barrett, a Vietnam veteran and Purple Heart recipient, stands at a crossing which will be named after him on Carters Valley Road.

    SURGOINSVILLE — The Hawkins County Commission voted at its last meeting to name a portion of Carters Valley Road after a longtime county resident, Vietnam veteran and Purple Heart recipient.

    The commission discussed the resolution at its regularly scheduled meeting on Jan. 22. The Harry S. Barrett Crossing will be a portion of Carters Valley Road near its intersection with Amis Chapel Road, where the road crosses over a creek.

    Harry Barrett, 74, grew up in Hawkins County. He was drafted into the Army and served in Vietnam as part of the 9th Infantry Division for about two years until he was medically discharged in 1971 after being shot in the right arm.

    Barrett received the Purple Heart, which is a high honor given to service members who are wounded or killed while serving in the military.

    District Four Commissioner Josh Gilliam sponsored the resolution.

    “I think vets have given the greatest sacrifice of anyone, and I want to do anything and everything I can for the people who have fought for our country,” Gillam said. “So, when I was approached about this, it was important to me that we honor him, especially since he is a Purple Heart recipient.”

    District Three Commissioner Danny Alvis is a longtime friend of Barrett. He said he wants to honor vets while they are around to appreciate it.

    “It was deserving,” Alvis said. “I respect all veterans. I’m 72 years old, and most of the veterans we’re doing this for are older than I am. So, they’re not gonna be here forever, and I would love to recognize veterans while they’re alive, and they can see this.”

    Barrett said when Alvis told him about the resolution, he thought he was joking.

    “I’m real proud of it,” Barrett said. “That was really nice of them to name it after me.”

    When the resolution went before the commissioner, District Six Commissioner Nancy Barker made a motion to send it to the road committee for approval. Gilliam told the commission that he checked with the E-911 director and that renaming the crossing shouldn’t interfere with emergency services.

    The motion failed with 12 no votes and two yes votes from District Six Commissioner Larry Clonce and District Seven Commissioner Jimmy Riley. The resolution passed with 12 yes votes and two no votes from Barker and District Two Commissioner Tom Kern.

    “It was disappointing that two commissioners didn’t see fit to name that after this Vietnam Purple Heart recipient,” Alvis said. “That bothers me.”

    Gilliam said he plans to continue honoring veterans.

    “I will continue to seek opportunities to honor veterans throughout the county by naming roads and landmarks after them,” Gilliam said. “I think that’s a positive thing.”
