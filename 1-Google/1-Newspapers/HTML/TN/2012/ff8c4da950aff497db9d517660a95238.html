‘Kate Walk’ donates to multi-county cancer network

  • Dec 28, 2012
  • Dec 28, 2012 Updated Jun 15, 2018
  • Facebook
  • Twitter
  • WhatsApp
  • SMS
  • Email
  • Facebook
  • Twitter
  • WhatsApp
  • SMS
  • Email
  • Print
  • Save

The Multi County Cancer Support Network recently received a $2,000 donation from the organizers of the “Kate Walk.”

Friends and family members of the late Kate Bratcher gather each year to participate in the annual 5K walk/run to honor her memory and keep her fight against breast cancer alive and going strong. Bratcher died in 2009 after a long battle with breast cancer.

A runner for over 25 years, Bratcher was diagnosed in 1992 with breast cancer. During her fight with cancer, Bratcher continued to stay active, participating in several “Race for the Cure” events.

By participating in events such as these, the idea for a local annual event came to life.

Since its beginning in 2009, the event has grown to host more than 200 participants in 2012.

The Multi County Cancer Support Network recently received a $2,000 donation from the organizers of the annual ‘Kate Walk.’ From left are Michelle Wilcox, Jimmy Bratcher, Diana Murray, Dottie Darney, MCCSN office manager; Sherry Martin, MCCSN Director; Cile Alexander, MCCSN president; Jessica Bailey, and Diane Darlington. -Staff Photo by Kali Bolle

Event organizer and Bratcher’s daughter Diana Murray said that proceeds from each year’s event have been donated to organizations focused on helping in the fight against cancer.

“So far, we have made $4,000 in donations to the Multi County Cancer Support Network,” said Murray. “And we are also excited about beginning the first Kate Walk scholarships in memory of Kate Bratcher in May 2013. Kate taught in the Tullahoma School System for two dec

ades. Her final year teaching was third grade at Bel-Aire in 2004. Several of this year’s THS graduates were in Kate’s final class eight years ago.”

The walk is held each September at Frazier McEwen Park in Tullahoma.
