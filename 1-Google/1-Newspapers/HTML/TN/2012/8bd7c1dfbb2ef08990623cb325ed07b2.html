Dodd Says Video Shows Billups Displaying Gun At Poker Game, Pointing It At Police; Attorney Says Billups Was Shot In Back

  • Wednesday, April 18, 2012
Photo of Billups bullet wound

Police Chief Bobby Dodd on Wednesday showed members of the news media a video that shows Clifford Billups displaying a gun during last Thursday night's high-stakes poker game, then pointing it at officers who raided the game at a medical office complex on Gunbarrel Road.

And the attorney for Billups, Robin Flores, said his client was shot in the back.

Attorney Flores said, "As of today, we have still not been allowed to review the video. Police seized the video equipment soon after the 'raid,' and they have had it in their sole possession since.

"Dr. Frank King has examined Mr. Billups, and has concluded the gunshot wound was from the rear.

"It seems the CPD is doing all they can to sling mud on Mr. Billups to justify the heavy hand they used against these people playing cards." 

Billups is due to appear in General Sessions Court on June 19 on a number of charges, including attempted murder.

The video shows him playing several hands of poker, then showing off the gun to other players. He is seen unloading the weapon at one point.

The video shows Billups slumping to the ground after he is shot just after officers announced their presence.
