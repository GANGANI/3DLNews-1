Police Say Evans Was Security Guard, Cagle The Dealer At Higdon's High-Stakes Poker Game

  • Monday, April 16, 2012
Kenny Higdon - the operator
John Cagle - the dealer
William Evans - the security guard

Police said William Jeffery Evans was the security guard and John Cagle the dealer for Kenny Higdon's high-stakes poker game that was raided on Gunbarrel Road on Thursday night.

The raid might not have been so notable except for the pedigree of some of the players involved and the fact that a food vendor going out the back door pulled a gun on officers and got shot. 

Evans, 44, of 4000 Signal View St., is charged with aggravated gambling promotion and possession of a controlled substance. Cagle, 58, of 4215 Ealy Road, is charged with aggravated gambling promotion, possession of a gambling device or record and possession of a controlled substance. 

Higdon, 43, of  533 Turtle Crossing, Ooltewah, is charged with gambling promotion and possession of a gambling device or record

All three are due to appear before General Sessions Court Judge Clarence Shattuck on Wednesday morning.

Clifford Jamar Billups, who faces much more serious charges - including attempted first-degree murder - is due to go before General Sessions Court Judge Ron Durby on Thursday morning. Billups was treated for a gunshot wound and then taken to the County Jail. 

The gamblers were cited to court and will get court dates later.

Police said Higdon is the owner of the playing site in a medical office complex at 1618 Gunbarrel Road.

Officers said Higdon "runs the game by providing dealers and security for the game. Higdon also accepts money from this card game."

Police said marijuana was found in the console of Evans'  vehicle.

They said Cagle has been the dealer for about the past year.
