Cleveland Student Attends Leadership Forum

  • Tuesday, June 26, 2001
Jasso is a senior at Bradley Central High.

Hermilo Jasso III, a resident of Cleveland, was among 350 high school students from across the country who attended the annual National Leadership Forum conducted at Harding University in Searcy, Ark., June 10-15.

The annual forum, a part of the university's American Studies Institute, featured a variety of speakers on topics including leadership development, free enterprise, family values, the environment, drug abuse, and AIDS.

Among this year's speakers were Dith Pran, central feigure of the award-winning movie "The Killing Fields," and Lt.

Colonel Dave Grossman of Jonesboro, Ark., who spoke on the topic of youth violence.

This year's forum marked the 45th anniversary for the program, which has continually sought to assist youth in becoming better acquanited with the American scene and to motivate them to a more forceful expression of pride in the country's traditions and ideals.

Hermilo, a senior at Bradley Central High School, was sponsored at the forum by Bradley County Farm Bureau. He is the son of Hermilo Jasso Jr. and the late Lorraine Jasso, of Cleveland.

Harding University is a private, four-year, liberal arts university located in northeast Arkansas. It is the state's largest private university with an enrollment of more than 4,660 students from 49 states and 47 countries.
