Reba Cox Retires From Community Mortgage

  • Thursday, April 12, 2001
  • Irby Park
Reba Cox, right, retiring from Community Mortgage, chats with her successor, Karen Barnett.
photo by Irby Park

Reba Cox, who has been in the banking industry for about 38, is retiring from Community Mortgage where she has served as vice president and loan originator.

She will be succeeded by Branch Manager Karen Barnett.

Mrs. Cox, who joined Community Bank when it opened in March 1991, was honored at the recent Mortgage Bankers Association (MBA) luncheon. She is past president of the local MBA.

She had started her banking career with Hamilton Bank that is now First Tennessee. She left there and went to American National Bank, now Suntrust. After that, she went to Commerce Union, now Bank of America. Then she went to United Bank, now Union Planters, before joining Community Mortgage.

Pat Sandlin, CEO of Community Mortgage, said the Chattanooga branch celebrating its 10th anniversary, “is and always has been my model branch. I wish I could package their expertise and distribute it to all my branches.

Mrs. Cox has been married to Tom Cox for 46 years and they have one son and a grandson and granddaughter.
