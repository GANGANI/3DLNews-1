By JENNIFER MURLEY

U of M is one big construction zone nowadays

By JENNIFER MURLEY

The Daily News

There is hardly a corner of the University of Memphis campus untouched by a construction project of some kind within the past five years.

To the north, work continues on the $15 million Kemmons Wilson School of Hospitality and Resort Management, which is expected to be complete early next year. To the south, sits the $11 million renovated physical education facilities. To the west, construction cranes tower the skies near the $9 million Brister Library/Wilder Tower renovation.

And, to the east, a new $12 million clock tower and attached parking garage can be seen from as far away as Southern Avenue.

The reason for the decade of massive development on the U of M campus is simple: its students.

The overall goal is to make our facilities and our campus student friendly and accommodating, said Tony Poteet, U of M vice president of campus planning and design. There was just an obvious need for better parking, and there has always been a need to consolidate student services into a friendly, one-stop shop like the Wilder Tower is going to give us.

According to the University of Memphis Web site, recent improvements including the $6.5 million Michael D. Rose Theater, total more than $37 million, with still more in the works.

Current projects include the $2.4 million Student Activities Complex Plaza, which will be the focal point of the campus upon its completion next summer. Workers began tearing up the site, formerly an employee and faculty parking lot, in August. It is being replaced with a landscaped plaza that will ultimately stretch from Central Avenue, past the new Ned McWherter Library through the heart of the campus. A large fountain will be centered in the plaza, adjacent to the new clock tower and parking garage, with a nearby seating area. P.C. Mock Construction is the general contractor. Jackson Person is the project designer.

Construction on a new $4.6 million campus bookstore is nearing completion. The facility will complete the walkway from the new garage to the University Center, the existing home of the bookstore. The main floor of the building will house the new bookstore. The second floor will be outfitted for a variety of businesses, which could include a copy center, a bank and a convenience store. Flintco is the general contractor. O.T. Marshall is the project designer.

The old Brister Library building and attached Wilder Tower, former home to the campus library, is currently undergoing massive renovation, which will convert the outdated facility into administrative offices and student services including financial aid, registration and counseling offices. School officials envision the new center providing one-stop shopping for U of M students upon its completion next summer. Jameson-Gibson Construction is the general contractor.

Earlier this fall, Jameson-Gibson secured a $3.8 million capital maintenance contract to make fume hood improvements. The schools existing fume hoods, which are protective devices used during research, date back to 1965, school officials said. The project, expected to be complete by next summer, will update 24 fume hoods to current codes. Allen & Hoshall is the designer.

Flintco recently won the contract for the $5.1 million Athletic Training Center addition and renovation. In addition to building new football dressing and meeting rooms, this project also will renovate the existing dressing and training facilities, as well as replace the entire heating and cooling systems, and domestic hot water systems. The project will be completed in two phases, with the first phase expected to be complete by next fall. Nathan Evans Taylor Coleman Foster is the project designer.

Although slightly fewer projects are slated for next year, the magnitude of the two largest developments, the FedEx Technology Institute and the new Biomedical Engineering Technology Laboratory, is considerable. Both projects will go up for general contractor bids in the spring.

The first of the two is the much-publicized $25 million FedEx technology complex. The project will be near the business college and will be technologically equipped with computer labs, training facilities, hardware and software development labs, as well as a cyber cafe and exhibit area. The Crump Firm and Looney Ricks Kiss are the project designers.

The other major development getting underway next year is the $1.1 million biomedical engineering lab, which will be a more specialized project involving the design and construction of research laboratory space. Ellers Oakley Chester & Rike Consulting Engineers is the design firm handling the project.

The current campus overhaul is the culmination of years of hard work and foresight, Poteet said.

A lot of the vision of some of these projects came to reality in the mid-1990s, and it just takes a long time to develop projects of this magnitude when youve got to secure funding and go through the design and bid process.

Considering the numerous projects scattered across the U of M, Poteet is most encouraged by the potential presented by the Kemmons Wilson School of Hospitality and Resort Management, which in addition to adding a new facility to the campus, will incorporate a new academic program.

There is no other school in the region that provides that type of instruction, and, its a big industry here, and in Tunica, he said, adding that the Wilson school will be marketed as conference center.

I think youre going to see all of these (new developments) kind of feed on themselves. Well have the FedEx Technology Institute right next to the Fogelman Executive Center and the Wilson School, so youll be able to have conferences that involve all three of those facilities that is going to give us flexibility and new synergy on the campus.
