25 years ago

Progressive Baptist Church on Rison Street observed the 29th anniversary of service of its pastor, the Rev. George R. Williams Sr.

A Christian-based pregnancy care center was being planned here. Tomorrow’s Hope Care Center would offer alternatives to abortion.

Jacob Ezra Hostetler, 77, of Bethany Road near Cottage Grove died at his residence. Funeral services were held at the Calvary Mennonite Christian Church in the New Boston community with Jacob Yoder officiating.

The remains of 25 children, mostly just skeletons, were found at the bottom of a ravine and scattered on a weedy lot near Bogota, Colombia. Liquor bottles were found near some bodies and others were tied to trees, where they were said to probably have been tortured. Authorities said there was a possibility it was the result of Satan worship.

Jim “Spider” Dumas, Post-Intelligencer writer and historian, had a book, Tennessee Stories, for sale. 

50 years ago

A fashion show was scheduled at the Henry County High School Little Theater. It was a fundraiser for the HCHS band.

The Route 6 budget of news reported Mr. and Mrs. Glenn Page and daughter visited his parents, Mr. and Mrs. Gean Page, at Henry and Mr. and Mrs. Lou Carter and daughter and Mr. and Mrs. Joe Lankford visited Mr. and Mrs. Neely Lankford.

A feature story by Kevin Weaks, accompanied with pictures, told of Mac McClain’s one-man zoo with a large collection of exotic animals on the outskirts of Paris.

Grand Ole Opry entertainer David Akeman and his wife were brutally murdered at their home in Nashville. Akeman was known as “Stringbean” on the Opry and also on the syndicated program “Hee Haw.” All across the state, the shocking event brought comments about the Tennessee justice system. An editorial in The Post-Intelligencer said perhaps it was time to use the most severe punishment on the books for such heinous crimes.

National commentator Paul Harvey said the death penalty should be reinstated.

Xi Upsilon chapter of Beta Sigma Phi sorority met with Mrs. Jim McKee, with nine members and one guest present. Ouida Cantrell was honored by receiving her life membership certificate.

75 years ago

The “Shovel and Hoe” column by Virginia Scott concerned shrubs good for providing food for birds. She talked of the multiflora rose, a large bush that bears numerous berries and blooms heavily in the spring with white flowers.

More than 50 cities in the United States were banning some comic books. Most of them featured murder and gory depictions.

Grill No. 1 on Blythe Street announced it would be open from 5 a.m. to 2 a.m. every day. Clarence Guthrie was proprietor.

Funeral services for Capt. Warren A. Clendenin, who had been killed in action in Italy as a member of a machine gun squad, were held at Poplar Grove Church in the Springville area. He was the latest Henry County hero to offer the supreme sacrifice for his country. He was serving with Gen. Mark Clark’s Fifth Army and earned several honors.

The US Department of Agriculture said the best food values in the season were cranberries, pecans and fresh carrots.

Richard Dunlap, president of Paris Industries, announced plans and specifications for a new plant here, Holley Carburetor, would be made by a Memphis architectural firm.

100 years ago

The Rev. and Mrs. J.H. Buchanan, the Rev. and Mrs. Martin Ball and O.C. Barton were attending the state Baptist Convention in Martin.

The Wood Street Service Station, Frank Blake prop., ran an ad answering the question “What is a service station?” It was said a real service station featured “service, service and more service.”

The Murray Jewelry Company announced a formal display of holiday jewelry merchandise on a Friday afternoon and evening at its store, featuring music, flowers and refreshments.

All ladies of the Presbyterian Church were invited to a miscellaneous bazaar shower at the church, where plans for an annual rainbow shower would be made for later in the month.

George Luckey returned from St. Louis to Paris, where he would resume his work with Swift and Company.

The Paris Produce Company opened a new feed store on North Market Street, with W.H. Cloys the manager.
