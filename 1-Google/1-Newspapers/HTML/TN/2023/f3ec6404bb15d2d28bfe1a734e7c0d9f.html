Did You Know? Fake News

  • Wednesday, November 8, 2023
  • Jerry Summers
Jerry Summers

The above term has become symbolic with the daily attacks on political parties and candidates that are unending and will continue up to and through the 2024 election (and forever).

While flipping channels during a football game on Sept. 10, a series of 30 minute shows on the National Geographic Channel (N.C.C.) revealed the use of said phrase during the infamous rule of the dictator in Germany from 1933-1945.

In the 1970s as a part of his author research on the life of Adolph Hitler, John Toland (1912-2004) interviewed many survivors of the death camps in Poland and other countries. In the continuous history of Nazi Germany’s past “The Lost Tapes of the Third Reich,” the voices of those victims come alive and add compelling reasons demonstrating how fake news was a major factor in leading the German people down the path of destruction in World War II.

Limited research reveals that false and distorted news has been a part of media history since the invention of the printing press.

“A Brief History of Fake News” (Center for Information Technology and Society- University of California Santa Barbara, 2023) briefly traces the progression in America from the newspaper era of Joseph Pulitzer and William R. Hearst to the present use of the “fake news generation,” who create false stories generally for profit to distribute on the internet.

The instantaneous speed and ability to communicate to a vast audience on the web has transformed the subject into a different form of journalistic nonsense!

(Both young and old readers and viewers should educate themselves on the modern use of fake news and not swallow the first bite of the topic!)
