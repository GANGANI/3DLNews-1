Fentanyl discovered in powdery substance found in WCSO Detention Center

  • Herald Reports
  • Nov 16, 2023
  • Nov 16, 2023 Updated Nov 17, 2023
  • 0

The Williamson County Sheriff’s Office learned Thursday afternoon that a powdery substance discovered at the WCSO Detention Center showed traces of Fentanyl, according to a news release from the department.

There have been no injuries.

“Our initial investigation revealed a powdery substance that field-tested positive as Fentanyl,” the release reads. “The substance has been sent to the Tennessee Bureau of Investigation for confirmation." 

As previously reported, at approximately 2:30 Wednesday afternoon, WCSO detention deputies reported an incident in which inmates, staff members, and first responders were exposed to an unknown substance in one area of the Williamson County Detention Center.”  

At least 10 patients, including inmates, staff and emergency responders, were taken to an area hospital for treatment and further evaluation. Several additional employees and approximately 24 inmates in the immediate area of exposure underwent a decontamination process with the assistance of emergency personnel on-site.

Everyone taken to the Williamson Medical Center for treatment and evaluation were been released.

 “We will continue to investigate the incident and are thankful that everyone involved is OK,” Sheriff Dusty Rhoades said.
