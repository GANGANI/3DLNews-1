Portland teens die tragically in motorcycle wreck

  • By Sonya Thompson, Editor
  • Mar 22, 2010
  • Mar 22, 2010 Updated Dec 22, 2016
  • 0

Two Portland families will lay their children to rest Wednesday afternoon at Halltown Cemetery after a joint funeral at Portland First Baptist Church.

Noah Wilson was two weeks away from his 19th birthday when he and his girlfriend, Kelsie Trobaugh, 17, were headed toward town on College Street while riding Wilson’s motorcycle Saturday night.

It was just after 7 p.m. when a vehicle pulled westbound onto College Street from Fireside Drive. The vehicle turned directly into the path of the Buell motorcycle. It happened so fast and neither Wilson nor the driver of the car, Michael Richards, could have changed the events that immediately followed.

According to Lt. Dewell Scruggs of the Portland Police Department, there was no time for Wilson to brake or avoid the vehicle and, as the motorcycle hit Richards’ Chrysler Sebring, the motorcycle flipped over the hood and roof of the car, sending Wilson and Trobaugh airborne and landing on the pavement. The motorcycle came to rest in a nearby field.

It is presumed that Wilson died immediately; Trobaugh died shortly after EMS arrived on the scene. The Air-Evac helicopter waiting to transport Trobaugh to Vanderbilt Medical Center was used instead to transport Richards. The second helicopter already inbound to Portland was cancelled as the scanner relayed a second 10-73 (fatality).

“Blunt force trauma is what killed them,” said Lt. Scruggs of the injuries sustained by Wilson and Trobaugh. “There was no part of them that – and especially Noah – that … well, it was just bad.”

“I don’t think (Michael Richards) was paying close enough attention,” Lt. Scruggs added of the accident.

Lt. Scruggs added that Richards had a traffic violation history of speeding, an accident with property damage, citations, and a prior suspension of his license (due to unpaid citations). Richards’ license was valid at the time of the wreck.

Richards was released from Vanderbilt Medical Center around midnight Saturday night and had sustained a broken nose, a possible concussion, and cuts and abrasions to his face, according to Lt. Scruggs. He has not returned home to Portland and is staying with his parents.

“He is very upset and we hope to get a report from him soon,” added Lt. Scruggs.

“I’ve worked with every fatality wreck in Portland – well, all but four – since 1988 and this has been the hardest for me,” added Lt. Scruggs sadly. “Noah was friends with my son. He’d come over to my house and the kids would line up two or three televisions in the basement and play video games all night long. He was a good kid. I’ve never seen anybody say anything out of the way about that boy. He was just a super kid.”

Those sentiments and more are reflected on a Facebook page set up by Caleb Goostree entitled “Noah Wilson, will always be remembered.”

Cassidy Green writes: “You were such a blessing to so many people. You taught  everyone to live life to its fullest and just be happy and thankful for what you have and let the people you love know you love em all the time. You truely impacted so many people.”

Megan Hodges writes: “I think you were the most compassionate, hilarious, all around likeable guy I’ve ever met. You lived through Christ and weren’t afraid to let anyone know that. I respected you for putting your heart into everything you did and everyone you loved.”

And Carrie Johnson writes: “I think we should all find the fact that there really isn’t a single bad memory of Noah, is a real blessing. He was always happy, funny, and full of life. We should take the time to reevaluate a lot within ourselves.”

Charges against Michael Richards are pending the outcome of an investigation which will include blood test results.

Full obituaries for Noah Wilson and Kelsie Trobaugh can be found in the "Obituaries" section of this Web site.
