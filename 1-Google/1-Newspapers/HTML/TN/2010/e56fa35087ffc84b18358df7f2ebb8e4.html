Roy Exum: The Post Script

  • Wednesday, June 30, 2010
  • Roy Exum
Roy Exum

I'm bad about telling stories that I shouldn't tell so today allow me to share one that I should.

When I was in church last Sunday, we were singing the closing hymn when I watched Billy Caulkins hug and love an ailing member, Ray Murphy. It touched my soul so deeply I wrote
about it later that afternoon.

Maybe I shouldn't have, especially with Mr. Murphy struggling through the advanced stages of Parkinson's disease. It was a tender, private moment between two old friends. That said, I've gotten many emails from others who adore God's boundless grace and from a heap more from people who love Ray Murphy, so thankful for the indelible way he's touched so many of their lives.

Understand, I don't know Ray Murphy all that well, but for all of my life I've known he is a living legend. The Lord only knows how many marriages he saved with his Sunday School classes for young couples and, as an attorney for the Miller-Martin firm, he was nationally-renowned.

One guy he worked beside at the law office was Dick Crotteau, a fireball of a lawyer himself who you may know best as being the virtual "godfather" to hundreds of adopted kids all over town. Dick was the chairman of Bethany Christian Services for years and has done more for our orphans than "Daddy Warbucks" did in the great musical, "Annie."

Now I'm not fond of writing the same story more than once. My style is more like a bumblebee, buzzing hurriedly from one flower to the next, but today's "post script" is too strong to ignore, in my way of thinking. While this may become a little long, I believe you'll find it is not long enough.

It seems that some years ago, this just after Mr. Murphy had been diagnosed with Parkinson's and was still digesting his nimaginably-cruel fate, some members of the church got up a tour of the Holy Land and the two attorneys - Ray and Dick - were among those who excitedly signed on.

Both have deep and abiding faiths - Dick was actually adopted by a bunch of monks at one point in his hard-scrabble upbringing - and while they were "walking where Jesus walked" there came a day where they went up on some high mountain to look down on what sprawled out below. This is about what happened that day on that mountain. It came in the form of a unique 60th birthday present from Ray to Dick. Actually it is a framed story accompanied by a long-treasured picture of the two, taken from behind, as the two law partners walked back down the mountain in much the same way that Jesus once did.

Remember, both are lawyers so I didn't dare ask permission to print this, certain that they'll instead forgive me for now sharing Ray Murphy's glorious story that is aptly titled, "One Poignant Moment In The Lives Of Two Men." Yes, Ray actually wrote this and months later gave it to Mr. Crotteau:

"We've all heard the comment that a picture is worth 1,000 words. It was never more clearly expressed than in this picture.

"I knew both these men well. One was a trial lawyer who was supposed to be rough and tumble in the courtroom, giving no quarter, completely in charge of his people. He was confident and comfortable on his turf. We'll call him 'TL' for trial lawyer.

"The other was as quick to grasp the facts as anyone you ever knew, and to assimilate them, arrive at a conclusion, and act. He was constantly on the move. His walk was closer to a run than to crawl. His drive was to get the job done right as soon as possible. His energy seemed boundless. We'll call him "DL" for dirt lawyer.

"The story starts on a desolate mountain in Israel. It was scorching hot, barren of any vegetation and good for nothing but rattlesnakes and scorpions. It rises over1,200 feet about the desert floor and was the scene of a long siege and battle between the Jewish people and the Roman army. It seemed to TL that it was 100,000 feet high because, you see, he had a phobia against heights. He had somehow managed to ride up the mountain in a cable car staring at the floor all the way.

"He refused to look up until he had reached the top. At that time he knew he was in deep trouble. It suddenly occurred to him that he would have to somehow get down this mountain. He
would have to inch his way along a narrow open walkway down the mountain to the cable car. He knew at that moment he could not do that, so he when he heard DT and several others say there was another way down this mountain by a walking trail he went for it not realizing what he was able to get into.

"As he turned into the first portion on the path and caught a glimpse of the wasteland below he lost his breath for a minute. He almost lost his balance and fell. He realized he was on a narrow path no more than two to three feet wide. The ground was sun-baked clay and covered with tiny rocks, making t very slippery. On side was straight down into the valley below. The path was ever turning back in a serpentine fashion. For most of the descent down the mountain there was no guardrail.

"TL panicked and froze. In the next hour, he would experience what love really is. "DL saw TL's brokenness and quickly came to the conclusion TL was out of control. DL came alongside him and assured him that he would get him down the mountain safely. For almost an hour DL placed his body between the abyss and TL. He also allowed TL to place his hand on his shoulder for balance.

"Not only did DL give up his freedom to enjoy this mountain trail, but he put himself in harm's way for TL. DL didn't do this in order to get points on someone's scorecard. Rather, this was a natural reaction of a man who walks with Christ and whose heart and mind has been marinated in His word.

"He can rest in the blessing of Jesus when He said, 'I tell you the truth - whatever you did for one of the least of these brothers of mine, you did for Me.'

"No one can really appreciate what was really going on here. TL clearly feared for his life. One slip on the gravel by his friend could have also sent him to his grave. I don't know what was going on in his mind as he felt the loose dirt move under his feet but I bet he was in deep dialog with the Father. But that was nothing new to him for you see he was on a heavenly journey.

"Jesus arranged for that to happen on that mountain that day to make it clear what He meant when He said:

"'Greater love has no one but this, that he lay down his life for his friends.' That is exactly what DL did.

"You have done that for Ray and Kathleen and we will always be in your debt.

"Happy birthday, Kathleen and Ray."
