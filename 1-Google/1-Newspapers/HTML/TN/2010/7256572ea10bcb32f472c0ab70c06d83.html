The High Life

Outlook bright for Downtown’s luxury hotels

MICHAEL WADDELL | Special to The Daily News

Downtown luxury hotels with AAA’s Four Diamond rating are turning the corner following a sluggish 2009, and most expect to see even more improvement in 2011.

Spectators watch the Peabody Duck March in the lobby of the Peabody Hotel. (Photo: Lance Murphey)

The outlook for next year is buoyed by the arrival of the University of Memphis Cecil C. Humphreys School of Law and recent news that Pinnacle Airlines Corp. will relocate its headquarters to One Commerce Square.

Downtown hotel officials are hopeful these developments – especially the Pinnacle move, because the company brings out-of-town employees to Memphis for training – will help increase demand next year.

Some are already seeing recovery take hold.

“We are definitely doing better this year,” said Mohammad Hakimian, managing partner of the Madison Hotel. “The recession of 2008 and 2009 impacted luxury product more than other segments.”

Total revenue at the 110-room Madison increased 9.5 percent during 2010. Revenue per available room rose 6.5 percent over last year, and occupancy rate also rose by 4.5 percent. A 15 percent surge in the food-and-beverage department helped pad the overall revenue totals. Average daily rate dropped slightly by 1.5 percent.

Increased traffic at the hotel’s Grill 83 restaurant, along with increased catering business thanks to a successful “Sunset Atop the Madison” rooftop season, drove the food and beverage totals.

Plans at the Madison for next year include $1 million to $1.5 million in renovations, as well a new affiliation with the World Hotels group, a collection of 500 luxury hotels all over the world.

“We have great expectations for next year and hope to take advantage of the turning economy,” said Hakimian. “We see enormous marketing potential from our alliance with World Hotels. Our revenue grew by double digits month-to-month during the latter part of this year, and we hope to continue that trend.”

The luxury boutique hotel has been open since May 2002. It occupies a building that was built in 1905, making it the oldest structure operating as a hotel in Memphis.

The Peabody hotel also experienced increased numbers this year, and Peabody director of marketing Craig Smith thinks next year will continue to show a positive northward trend for the 464-room hotel.

“We are currently ahead of our group pace year-over-year and are forecasting to be up 3 percent to 4 percent in total revenue for 2011,” he said.

The Peabody’s total revenues for 2010 are up slightly over the previous year due to an 11 percent increase in group bookings. The hotel experienced its largest total in year-for-year group bookings in the past 15 years. Occupancy rate is also up slightly over last year. However, the hotel’s average daily rate has declined substantially.

“Group demand is down nationally, which has resulted in deep discounting coupled with extensive concessions,” said Smith. “Overall, when the industry experiences a significant downturn in demand, as we have seen over the past 18 to 24 months, many first-tier cities such as Chicago, New Orleans, Atlanta and New York will drop group rates and offer exceptional concessions packages to mitigate the decline in other segments.”

The Peabody’s catering revenues are up this year thanks to a significant increase in attendance for the hotel’s rooftop season. Smith expects that trend to continue since next year’s season will feature themed evenings and additional offerings.

The hotel, which has been in its current location since 1925, also is planning to make small renovations to the property next year, mainly to the building’s exterior. A total restoration was completed in 2007 that included guest rooms, public spaces, the lobby, the athletic club and restaurants.

The Westin Memphis Beale Street is another Downtown luxury hotel that is anticipating a solid 2011 following a steady 2010. The 203-room hotel, across from FedExForum, is in its fourth year of operation.

“All things considered, it’s been a pretty good year,” said general manager Patrick Jordan.

The Westin’s occupancy rate during 2010 held even compared to previous years. Its average daily rate dropped slightly due to the downward movement across the luxury segment along with the hotel’s need to stay competitively priced.

The food and beverage segment performed well, thanks to the success of the its restaurant, Sole Restaurant and Raw Bar, which enjoyed a steady lunch crowd and increased dinner business driven from events on Beale Street and at FedExForum.

“We’ve had to find ways to stand out from the crowd because travelers are becoming savvier and more diligent due to the Internet,” said Jordan. “Price has become much more of a deciding factor than in the past.”

Business group occupancy has been down due to the absence of large conventions and citywide functions, Jordan explained.

“We think we’ll see an uptick in group meetings in the second or third quarter of next year,” he said. “People in the business world have been bottled up and are looking to get back out and travel.”
