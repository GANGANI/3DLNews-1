Demetrius Clay Arrested On Charges Of Trapping Girlfriend In Apartment, Beating And Strangling Her

  • Friday, February 19, 2016
Demetrius Dayun Clay

Chattanooga Police arrested Demetrius Dayun Clay, 43, for allegedly trapping his girlfriend in her apartment, hitting and strangling her.

On the evening of Feb. 13, police responded to disorder at 2722 Glass St. They reported hearing a loud struggle inside the apartment where they were dispatched.

Upon entering the residence, the female victim ran towards the police. She advised that Clay, her boyfriend, had trapped her in the apartment over the last two days and assaulted her multiple times by hitting and throwing her around.

She said he stole her cell phone to prevent her from calling 911.

According to the report, before police arrived Clay allegedly snatched the victim by the neck and dragged her “from one room to the next.” When she cried for help, he covered her mouth so no one could hear, the report said.

The victim told police she managed to grab some keys and scratch Clay in the face to break free. That is when she ran to the police inside her apartment, she said.

Police observed the victim’s ripped clothing, pulled-out hair, and red marks around her neck consistent with strangulation. They also noted the bruises on her face.

They ordered Clay to exit the apartment, and he complied. Clay advised, “Nothing happened today other than a verbal disorder.” But police observed the scratch mark under his left eye from where the victim used the keys.

Clay admitted to being a convicted felon due to a previous aggravated domestic assault.

He was arrested on charges of aggravated assault (domestic), false imprisonment, and interference with emergency calls.

Police transported Clay to the Hamilton County Jail, and he was placed on a $10,000 bond.
