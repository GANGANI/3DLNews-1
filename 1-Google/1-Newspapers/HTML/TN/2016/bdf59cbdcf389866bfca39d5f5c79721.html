CVS Buys Bartlett Locale for Pharmacy

A local affiliate of Woonsocket, R.I.-based CVS Caremark Corp., has bought the former Blue Plate Cafe at 6676 Stage Road in Bartlett from Stage Centre GP and Gill Enterprises GP in two transactions for a combined $2.2 million.

CVS 7144 TN LLC paid Gill Enterprises GP nearly $1.4 million and Stage Centre GP $836,000 in a pair of special warranty deeds that closed Oct. 18.

The 5,448-square-foot restaurant sits on 1.7 acres at the northwest corner of Stage and Kirby Whitten roads. Built in 1991, the restaurant has an appraised value of $813,600, according to the Shelby County Assessor of Property.

Gill Enterprises GP, a partnership affiliated with Gill Properties, bought the restaurant in September 2010 from Best Breakfast Inc. for $1 million.

This was the second sale between Gill Properties and CVS, the national pharmacy that entered the Memphis market a couple of years ago.

Gill Properties sold CVS a 1.8-acre parcel of land on Germantown Parkway in Cordova where the Memphis Area Home Builders Association headquarters stood. That building was razed and CVS built a pharmacy on half the property.

The rest of the acreage is slated for the Germantown Station retail center. Gill Properties filed a $1 million building permit last month for the roughly 18,000-square-foot, 11-bay center.

First Horizon's Keen Retires As Chief Accounting Officer

James Keen is retiring as the principal accounting officer of First Horizon National Corp. and its main subsidiary First Tennessee Bank National Association effective Dec. 31.

He plans to retire fully as an employee by June 30.

Jeff Fleming, executive vice president and corporate controller of First Horizon and First Tennessee, has been appointed to succeed Keen effective Jan. 1.

Fleming has been employed by First Horizon since 1984. He’s served as the company’s and the bank’s corporate controller since 2008.

Wharton Names McGowen to Head Innovation Team

The former commanding officer of the Millington Naval Support Activity facility is the new head of the Memphis Innovation Delivery Team.

Memphis Mayor A C Wharton Jr. named Douglas A. McGowen to lead the team funded by the Bloomberg Philanthropies of New York.

The funding was announced earlier this year and is aimed at innovations that will help grow small businesses in inner-city areas as well as come up with strategies for reducing handgun violence.

McGowen served for 26 years as a career Naval officer including commanding a strike fighter squadron.

During his time in Memphis he has served on the board of Leadership Memphis, the LEAD program at the University of Memphis and the Memphis Academy of Science and Engineering.

Civil Rights Museum to Hold Freedom Award Ceremonies

The National Civil Rights Museum will hold its Public Forum and Freedom Award ceremonies Wednesday, Oct. 26.

The ceremonies will honor icons, pioneers and legacy beneficiaries of the U.S civil rights movement.

The Public Forum and Freedom Awards ceremony is part of the Lunch and Learn series and offers participants the opportunity to learn about U.S. civil rights history from museum staff as well as guest speakers as they highlight the role of some of the movement’s leaders. Attendance is free and participants should bring their own lunch. It will be held at the museum from 11:45 a.m. to 1 p.m.

Southwest Tenn. Awarded Dept. of Education Grant

The U.S. Department of Education has awarded Southwest Tennessee Community College more than $3.6 million in funding to implement the Predominantly Black Institutions Competition and Formula Grant programs.

Southwest received a five-year total award of $1.5 million for the PBI Formula Grant, specifying $305,977 for the first year of funding.

The purpose of the PBI Formula Grant Program is to strengthen an eligible institution’s capacity to serve more low- and middle-income African-American students; to expand higher education opportunities for eligible students by encouraging college preparation and student persistence in secondary school and postsecondary education; and to strengthen the financial ability of the institution to serve the academic needs of these students.

Southwest will be eligible to receive $305,977 each subsequent year for an additional four years based on performance outcomes.

Southwest also received a four-year total award of $2.1 million for the PBI Competitive Grant Program, specifying $524,394 for the first year of funding.

The purpose of the PBI Competitive Grant Program is to enable institutions to establish or strengthen programs in the areas of science, technology, engineering, mathematics, health education, internationalization or globalization, teacher preparation and improving educational outcomes of African-American males.

The college will be eligible to receive continuation funding in the amount of $525,441 for fiscal year 2012–2013, $534,245 for fiscal year 2013–2014, and $540,812 for fiscal year 2014–2015 based on performance outcomes.

NIH Awards UTHSC Professor Grant to Study Brain Activity

The University of Tennessee Health Science Center has announced that Dr. Detlef Heck, professor in the Department of Anatomy and Neurobiology, has received an award for $371,723 from the National Institute of Neurological Disorders and Stroke, a subsidiary of the National Institutes of Health, the nation’s primary federal medical research agency.

The grant will be used to develop Heck’s study on brain activity titled “Manipulation and Imaging of Synchronous Population Activity in the Neocortex,” which will be funded over a two-year period.

Heck and his research team are examining how millions of neurons in the brain coordinate their activity.

“We are particularly interested in the neocortex – the part of the brain that allows us to perform higher level cognitive tasks – in terms of planning, judging or speaking, and how this neocortex might malfunction in brain disorders,” Heck said.

“We now know that such complex cognitive processes are performed through the coordinated collaboration of millions of neurons and that these collaborations are highly dynamic.”

Investigating these fast, dynamic processes requires cutting-edge imaging technology.

The grant will enable the research team to purchase a state-of-the-art imaging system that will be used to investigate neuronal brain activity in mice and will aid the research team in gaining new insights into the probable causes of cognitive deficits involved in various brain disorders such as autism.
