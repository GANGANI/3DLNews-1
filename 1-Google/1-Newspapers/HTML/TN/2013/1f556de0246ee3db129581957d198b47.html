Inmate's death under investigation

  • By Michael R. Moser
    Editor
  • May 30, 2013
  • May 30, 2013

The death of an inmate at the Cumberland County jail is under investigation although sheriff's officials do not believe that the death is of a suspicious nature.

Kenneth Charles Stomm, 54, 86 Commerce St., Crab Orchard, was found unresponsive on the bunk in his jail cell around 11 a.m. on May 17, according to Sheriff's Investigator Chad Norris' report.

Stomm was brought in on a parole violation warrant May 14 and had told others he was not feeling well, but he refused to be taken to see the jail nurse or to the emergency room, according to Chief Investigator Casey Cox.

Stomm had a positive drug test at his probation office prior to his arrest, so when he was brought in, he was under the influence of drugs, Cox said. The inmate complained after breakfast on the 17th that he was feeling ill, and he went back into his cell and was alone in the cell when his body was discovered.

After inmates were fed lunch, some noticed that Stomm did not exit his cell to eat, and brought that information to the attention of jailers who went to check on him and found him not breathing.

CPR was started as an ambulance was dispatched to the scene, but Stomm was declared dead on arrival at Cumberland Medical Center.

The body was taken to Nashville for an autopsy even though Cox said there was nothing to indicate the death was anything but an accidental death related to effects of drug use.
