    Oliver Springs sends Kingston packing with pair of losses

    By GOOSE LINDSAY

    Roane Newspapers

    The first game of the new year went well for the Oliver Springs Bobcats and Lady ‘Cats Friday night as they picked up a home sweep over visiting county rival Kingston.

    The girls game was close throughout and Oliver Springs would need a late rally to pick up a 50-46 victory.

    The guys contest was never close as the Bobcats took control from the start in picking up a 66-39 victory over the Yellow Jackets.

    Girls Game

    It was Kayla Christopher to the rescue Friday night for the Lady ‘Cats as the junior star scored six points in the final 1:39 of the contest and that helped Oliver Springs erase a late 46-43 deficit and pick up a 50-46 victory.

    Christopher, who scored a game-high 25 points on the evening, buried back-to-back jumpers within a span of 29 seconds to give the Lady ‘Cats a 47-46 lead with 1:10 left to ply. Christopher then iced the victory with a pair of free throws with 8.1 seconds left to play to give Oliver Springs the four-point victory.

    While the outcome of Friday’s contest was relatively low scoring, the first period of Friday’s contest was a track meet with both teams putting up a lot of points as the Lady Jackets led 22-19 after eight minutes of play.

    The inside duo of Callie Mergen and Tayler Smith led the way for Kingston in the first period as they combined for 15 points while Christopher and Brooke Barger scored 12 of Oliver Springs’ 19 points in the first period.

    Smith drained a three pointer to open the second period as Kingston held a 25-19 lead with 7:35 left to play in the second period, but the rest of the half would see a slower pace as Oliver Springs outscored Kingston 10-6 over the remainder of the period to make the score 31-29 at half-time in favor of the Lady Jackets.

    Things remained tight in the third period as the lead changed hands four times before Kingston took a 41-39 lead into the final period of play.

    Smith led all scorers with eight points in the period while Barger paced the Lady ‘Cats with six points.

    The final period remained close until Oliver Springs forced some turnovers in the final two minutes and that led to Christopher’s points that gave the Lady ‘Cats the victory.

    Besides Christopher, Barger added 13 points and three steals for the Lady ‘Cats while Mariah Ollis pulled down a team-high seven rebounds.

    Smith led Kingston on the night with 23 points and 10 rebounds while Mergen added 10 points, nine rebounds and four blocks. Jennifer Giv-ens had a game-high seven assists.

    Kingston (46) Smith 23, Ray 6, Mergen 10, Je. Givens 2, Ja. Givens 2, Thearp 3.

    Oliver Springs (50) Christopher 25, Hall 1, Barger 13, Ollis 2, Howard 2, Harper 2, Bilyeu 3, Pugh 2.

    Boys Game

    While the girls game was close, the boys game was a blowout from start to finish as Oliver Springs led from wire-to-wire.

    The game opened with the Bobcats jumping out to an 11-2 lead after four minutes of play and that lead would increase to 24-9 after the open-ing period of play.

    The duo of Dan Schultz and Tyler Carter were the big guns for Oliver Springs in the opening period as they combined for 19 of the team’s 24 points.

    James Sams scored seven of Kingston’s nine points in the opening period.

    The second period was more of the same as the Bobcats won the period, 15-4, to make the score 39-13 at halftime.

    Kingston would get no closer than 20 points in the second half as the Bobcats cruised to the 27-point victory.

    Sams led Kingston with 14 points and seven rebounds.
