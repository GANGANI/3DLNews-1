The Daily News, DBIM Join Forces

By Eric Smith

The Daily News and Doing Business in Memphis (DBIM) have entered into a collaborative marketing venture to give their customers a more competitive business advantage.

Their product package pairs The Daily News’ public record research tools and DBIM’s detailed sales leads, giving companies a blend of business information and value at a discounted combination price.

For a limited time, individuals and companies can receive one-year subscriptions to The Daily News print edition and The Daily News Online, as well as DBIM’s eBook on CD, all for $365, or $1 a day (a savings of $158).

For Eric Barnes, publisher of The Daily News Publishing Co., joining forces with DBIM for this offer was a strategic decision that will help customers maximize their marketing and research needs.

“It was a natural fit for us, given their strength in comprehensive business information and ours in broad-based public record research and sales lists,” Barnes said. “Both companies have long emphasized the importance of staying at the forefront of data-based products.”

Barnes said DBIM’s eBook, which includes detailed profiles on 10,000 Memphis-area companies, serves as a perfect complement for businesses that use The Daily News’ extensive public record database.

“We’ve long used their information for our own marketing purposes, so it made sense that our subscribers might want to also,” Barnes said.

Michael Correll, vice president of marketing for DBIM, also lauded the partnership.

“We see a strong synergy between the two organizations, a synergy that is not competitive but indeed complementary,” Correll said. “We think we can bring something to our customers by offering them the resources of The Daily News. And we think it will enhance their sales and marketing and promotional efforts and give them new sources of information.”

The Daily News Publishing Co., at 193 Jefferson Ave., has been the newspaper of record for Shelby County since 1886. And since the Internet’s rise, The Daily News Online, www.memphisdailynews.com, has helped people conveniently and quickly search an exhaustive database of public records and notices.

Doing Business in Memphis (www.memphisbusiness.com) was founded in 1990 by Deborah Camp. The company provides targeted business-to-business information for those in sales and marketing, as well as for nonprofit organizations and others in need of current and accurate data on Memphis companies.
