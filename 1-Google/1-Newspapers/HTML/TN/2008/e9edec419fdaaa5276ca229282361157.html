Couple Charged With Cooking Meth Around 2 Children

  • Wednesday, January 9, 2008
Jennifer Dagnan

Chattanooga Police narcotics investigators on Wednesday night found a 10-month-old infant and a 7-8-year old child in a residence where their mother and father were cooking methamphetamine.

Narcotics investigators earlier Wednesday night received an anonymous tip that meth was being made in the presence of two small children at 510 Central Dr. at Rustic Village Apartments. Investigators verified the information and address and conducted an investigation.

After a search of the residence, investigators found the two children in the residence.

Investigators charged the childrens' mother, Jennifer Dagnan, 34, and the father, Jerry Owens, 33, with reckless endangerment and manufacturing methamphetamine. Dagnan and Owens were also charged with possession of methamphetamine and felony possession of drug paraphernalia.

The Department of Children’s and Family Services were notified and consented for the children to be released to a relative.

Dagnan and Owens were transported to the Hamilton County Jail.

Jerry Owens
