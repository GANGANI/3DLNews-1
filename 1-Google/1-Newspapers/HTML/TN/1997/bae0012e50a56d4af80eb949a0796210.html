lj 10/5 cates Construction begins on J. E. Walker Homes addition By LAURIE JOHNSON The Daily News Construction is ready to begin on a residential development designed for families headed by minimum-wage workers in Southwest Memphis near Whitehaven. Southern Housing Corp. of Memphis has purchased 176 acres of the J. E. Walker Homes subdivision, an affordable housing development originally started in the 1950s, and plans to begin construction on three model homes within the next week. "Weve developed 20 lots so far," said H. B. McAdams, president of Southern Housing Corp. "We have our utilities and the rest of our infrastructure in, and we have filed building permits for three houses. "We are looking forward to getting started because we have had a lot of interest." Eventually, the development will contain 750 lots, McAdams said. A second phase of 162 lots was recently approved by the city planning commission. McAdams said subsequent phases would be developed as sales progressed. For development and home construction purposes, Southern Housing Corp. financed $1 million with Joel A. Montgomery, according to a trust deed filed recently in the Shelby County Registers Office. McAdams development is an extension of the Walker subdivision, which contains about 1,000 homes built primarily in the 1950s. The new addition will go by the same name. The development has been designated as an affordable housing development through the citys infrastructure savings program, which gives developers credit for things like sewer and engineering fees. "Anything that the government can do helps," McAdams said. McAdams, who has been involved in the construction of affordable housing since he began his career in 1950, said the subdivision is designed for working-class families, those headed by couples working full-time at making minimum wage, or bringing home $22,000 per year. We think there is an untapped market for this kind of product," McAdams said. The homes will be about 1,000 square feet in size, and will be priced at about $55,000. They will be built by Southern Housing Corp. "We will offer the houses for as low a price as we possibly can," McAdams said. All of the homes in the development will be one-story. Some will be patio homes, others will have one or two car garages, McAdams said. "We hope that the garages will sell the homes. People living in this area are generally looking for some type of security," he said. McAdams said recent and future development in the area and its proximity to parks, schools, vocational/technical training centers, may be a good selling point with home buyers. Many minimum-wage scale jobs also are located in the south end of the city, he said. Since Mitchell Road is basically an extension of Winchester Road, the development is easily accessible from the citys warehouse district along Brooks Road and Shelby Drive. "We are also going to be seeing a lot more development down there with the Pidgeon Industrial Park and the Birmingham Steel plant," he said. McAdams also said he expected a healthy mix of Tunica County casino workers, as well. "Although there are plenty of apartments down there, they really arent building any homes in Tunica. "We are only a very short distance from the casinos, roughly 20 miles, and we think this will be a very big plus for us." The new section of the Walker subdivision is being built near another affordable housing project under development by the Shelby County Interfaith Association through its Nehemiah Housing Initiative. The Nehemiah development, located at Third and Fairway streets, will contain 157 homes when complete. Homes in this development are priced at $46,500, and range in size from 1,150 square feet to 1,250 square feet.
