Dunavant - Wells

Courtney Elizabeth Wells and James Hannah Dunavant were married in a Saturday evening ceremony, March 7, 2009 at Westminster Presbyterian Church in Oklahoma City. Dr. Randall Spindle officiated joined by the uncle of the groom, The Rev. James Knox Polk Van Zandt of Murfreesboro, Tenn.
The bride is the daughter of Elizabeth and Edward Wells of Oklahoma City. Grandparents are The late Noreen and John Sherman of Kimberling City, Mo., and the late Margaret Collet and George Thomas Wells of Oklahoma City. The groom is the son of Sally and Donald Harris of Cary Mississippi, and of Richard Dunavant of Pulaski, Tenn. Grandparents are Will Mary Simrall Pratt and the late James Edwin Pratt Sr. of Monteagle, Tenn., Louise Colvin and Mark Hannah Dunavant of Campbellsville, Tenne., and step-grandmother Zulene Ryals Harris.
The bride selected a formal gown of ivory, re-embroidered Alencon lace over silk organza with a fitted, princess style lace bodice and a soft hourglass silhouette designed by Christos Yiannakou of New York. Three small tiers of silk organza outlined the strapless, sweetheart neckline echoing the larger tiers at the hemline of the gown that draped gracefully into a train complementing the family heirloom chapel length veil that had also been worn by the bride’s mother. The veil of silk illusion with hand re-embroidered Alencon lace designs flowed from a custom hair piece of pleated antique lace flowers and pearls created by Claire Kennedy Design.
Brenna Collet Wells sister of the bride served as maid of honor, and Whitney Johnson Williams as the matron of honor, both of Oklahoma City. The bride’s other attendants were Katey Bergeron of Baton Rouge; Anna Johnson Hibbard, of Norman; Kelsey Ill, of Boston; Whitney Jones, of Oklahoma City; Jessica Satterfield, of Colorado Springs, Colo.; and Sarah Smith, of New York City.
Richard Dunavant, father of the groom, served as best man. Groomsmen were Thomas Harris, of Water Valley, Miss.; and Ryan Kimball, John Miller, and Walter Rogers, all of Jackson, Miss., Greg Ochs, of Colorado Springs; Kevin Owens, of Campbellsville, Tenn.; and Knox Van Zandt of Jackson Hole, Wyo.
Ushers were Lucius Brock, of Oxford, Miss.; Jody Bullock of Ft. Wayne, Ind.; Charley Crell, Brian Fisher, and Justin Martin, all of Jackson, Miss.; and Billie Drane, of Atlanta, Ga.
Traveling to the wedding were friends and family from states around the country including Alaska, California, Colorado, Connecticut, Georgia, Illinois, Kentucky, Mississippi, New York and Tennessee.
The wedding reception at Oklahoma City Golf and Country Club included a variety of Southern foods and Oklahoma favorites. Music was provided by The Compo-zitions from Jackson. The red velvet groom’s cake featured the Mississippi State University logo, and the chocolate groom’s cake displayed the emblem for Columbia University in New York City where the groom will attend graduate school in business in the fall of 2009.
Following a honeymoon to the island of St. Lucia in the Eastern Caribbean Sea, the couple will reside in Atlanta until they make their new home in New York City.
