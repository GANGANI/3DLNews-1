Power of a wish draws military crowd to local school

  • By Tamara Gregor The Duncan Banner
  • Mar 10, 2020
  • Mar 10, 2020

On Monday, a special visit from a military team granted one wish of one fifth grader.  

Bracen Plummer was given the chance to speak and fellowship with military members on Monday, March 9 at Horace Mann Elementary School when nearly 60 different members of military teams dressed in uniform stopped by the school to join him for lunch.

Bracen’s mom, Tina Henry, said since she adopted Bracen at seven days old he’s been “all about the military.” 

Henry said, “Ever since he could walk, he was all about the military,”  and now, “he is 12 and his whole room is army.”

Plummer has attention-deficit/hyperactivity disorder (ADHD) and adjustment to medications, along with being new to the area, has been a journey for them. He’s had a rough two weeks and “this just shows that people care about him,” Henry said.

While getting used to a new routine, Plummer “has been doing great,” Henry said. He has earned points to help him out in class. With his last set of points he earned, he spent them on his entire class for an extra recess this past Friday.

One wish Plummer asked for included eating with a military person while in a small uniform of his own. Henry had a guy who was going to come and eat with him but something came up the night before and he couldn’t make it. Not wanting to let her son down, she decided to put it out on Facebook the night before, and the next day, a large group of military personnel in full uniform showed up to greet, talk to and have pizza with Plummer.

Henry said, “I thank (the community), this is unbelievable. I just moved here about a year and a half ago and this is awesome.”

“I never expected all this, he’s worked really hard for this in the rough two weeks he had,” she said.

Members of the military team presented Plummer with a flag which came off of their local school flag pole. 

Though worn and tattered, they decided to present it to Plummer so he could put it up on his wall. 

While standing alongside this team, Bracen said, “Thank you.”

Different members addressed Plummer. Brittany Born stepped up to address Plummer with tears in her eyes. 

“When I found out what your mom wanted for you, it spoke to me. This is the greatest thing I’ve ever felt,” Born said. 

Born presented Plummer with a cup which read “Fort Sill, Oklahoma” on it, as well as exchanging a patch she has been wearing since being in Korea in 2016. 

“Challenge coins are always important,” Born said as she presented him with two coins, one for the United States Army and one for the United States Fort Sill Field Artillery. 

In conclusion to her time with Plummer, Born said, “Corporal Bracen, I hope you have a lovely time here and I hope you do whatever it takes to get to where you want to be. Don’t ever let anyone tell you different.  You want to join the army, do whatever it takes to join the army, understand?”

With Plummer in his full uniform members of the military formed a line to him and gave a high-five, a hug and words of affirmation for Plummer to take with him from the special lunch date.
