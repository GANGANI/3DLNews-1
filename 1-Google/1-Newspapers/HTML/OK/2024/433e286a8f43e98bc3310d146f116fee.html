By Kathleen Lourde 

Waynoka school board hears mostly good news about finances, re-hires principals

 
  Share   Tweet 0 Comments

February 14, 2024



The Waynoka Board of Education held its regular monthly meeting Monday, Feb. 12. All members were present: Caleb Zook, Travis George, Garret Gum, John Hanson and Clint Olson. Also present were Superintendent Scott Cline, minutes clerk Lori Adair, and principals Patricia Burrow and Ronnie Nix.

The board listened to a presentation of the 2022-23 school audit report completed by S&B CPA and Associates. The audit had no negative findings, and the board approved it as presented.

Cline gave an overview of the school’s financial situation, pleased that the audit had no findings. “It’s nice to see that again,” he said.

General fund expenditures were up slightly this month, while revenue was down on the month by $121,000 and ad valorem was down $19,000. “Overall, we’re down $199,000,” Cline said.

Building fund expenditures are up due to new lighting at the track, but revenue for the building fund is also up, by $210,000.

Child nutrition expenditures are up on the year by $14,000 and revenue is down $24,000.

In the day care, expenditures are up slightly as is revenue, but the revenue is not enough to cover expenses. “But you know that,” said Cline.

Update on New Track & Field Facilities

The new developments at the track are progressing, Cline said.

“We’ve got the ground stabilized and the long jump poured. We’re hoping to pour the pole vault and shotput this week, then start pouring the curbs for the track.” However, a concrete wall on the north side of the track was poorly done, from an aesthetic point of view. The wall was poured by a different contractor than the one doing the rest of the track. “Structurally, I think the wall is okay, but aesthetically it’s terrible,” Cline said, adding that the contractor will come back, tear out the wall, and start over.

A water leak where the addition is to go has been capped. Cline hopes work on the addition will resume this week.

State Education Department Audits Waynoka Child Nutrition Program: Five Findings

“The State Department (of Education) was onsite two weeks ago for the child nutrition audit,” said Cline. “They found five findings, all minor.” The school is fixing those items, which include using different revenue codes for adult and child meals. Staff members will have to pay more for lunch; the audit found they were underpaying. Students in the afterschool program who get a snack are required to take both a snack and a milk, and the students haven’t always been taking their milk. The school will begin using a snack monitoring worksheet.

FFA Week and Livestock Shows

Dress up days and a petting zoo were enjoyed by students during FFA Week, reported Elementary Principal Burrow. Twenty eighth, ninth and tenth graders competed at the Junior CDE contest, said Nix. FFA members passed out donuts to farmers and teachers, and the officer team decorated the hallways.

Waynoka’s local livestock show was Feb. 3. Some elementary students showed at livestock shows this past month. Thirty-four students competed in the local livestock show.

“I would like to thank the booster club for all their hard work making it a success,” said High School Principal Nix.

The county livestock show was Monday (swine and cattle) and Tuesday (sheep and goats). The district show is scheduled for Feb. 19-27 in Woodward.

January Students of the Week

Middle school and high school students of the week were Aubry Fuller, Colton Fuqua, Chris Solomon, Rhyleigh Curtsinger, Temperance Bouziden, Breya Beck, Chanan Wyatt, and Stetson Nix. In the elementary, January’s Railroaders of the Week were Mackie Litzenberger, Charles Hanson, Anaise Taylor, Adam Santiago, Paisley Inman, Teagan Cline, Caroline Smith and Jorden Fuqua.

Student Activities and Achievements

The elementary celebrated the 100th day of school on Jan. 27 with fun classroom activities, our elementary band rocking us out, and an epic game of rock, paper, scissors.

Pre-K and kindergarten attended a Stephen Fite concert in Fairview on Jan. 30, then finished the afternoon at the Fairview City Park.

Valentine’s Day parties in the elementary were Feb. 13

The fifth and sixth grades will travel to Oklahoma City on Tuesday, Feb 20, to tour the Capitol, enjoy lunch at Charleston’s, then visit the National Monument Museum. Vision screening is Feb. 27, and Mrs. Hill will take some fifth and sixth graders to Honor Band in Alva Feb. 29.

In the high school, Principal Nix reported that the FCCLA competed in the regional star event Feb. 8 in Woodward. Aubry Delano placed first, Temperance Bouziden and Marlie Pitts placed second. Rhiata Bouziden was also second place, and Serenity Green and Keira Durkee will compete in the state FCCLA contest in Stillwater held on March 25.

Mrs. Cline, Mr. Smith and all the 10th grade students toured the Northwest Technology Center in Alva. The students were able to explore different career areas that are available to them.

The junior high academic team had their first meet in Ringwood on Jan. 31. The team members are Alex Delano, Sage Baird, Reagan Wilson and Titan Smith-Dunn. They competed in Waynoka Monday afternoon.

And finally, in sports, the junior high basketball teams wrapped up their season and will be transitioning into baseball, softball and track. The high school basketball teams finished up districts last week, with both teams being district runners up. The Lady Rails and Rails will advance to play in Regionals next Thursday in Leedy.

The fifth- and sixth-grade basketball teams didn’t finish their final games last Thursday due to cancellation, but they did show improvement and growth throughout their season and now track practice has started.

“This week starts our four-day work week,” Burrow also noted.

Other Board Business

In other news, the board approved increasing the price of employee lunches and extra milk prices for the 2023-24 school year, as required by the audit heard at the top of the meeting.

The board renewed the employment of principals Burrow and Nix.

Fundraiser requests from the fifth and sixth grades, esports, girls basketball, cheer/baseball and student council were all approved.

The board approved renewing the Employee Evaluation Systems, Inc., for OKTLE and McRel for the 2024-25 school year.

The board also approved the consent agenda, consisting of minutes of past meetings; general, building, activity, and bond fund reports; encumbrances for general, building, and bond funds; and change orders.
