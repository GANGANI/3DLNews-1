McGuinness one of best of best

Bishop McGuinness’ National Merit Semi Finalists are (front row) Alex Krause, Christina Forest, Matt Brown; (back row) Andrew Douglas, Elizabeth Dowds, Clinton Kubat.
Irish Items by Jennifer Grover
Bishop McGuinness Catholic High School, one of only two Catholic high schools in the metro area, is known for numerous accomplishments around the city. Well, now McGuinness has another accomplishment to add to the list.
Administration has recently been informed that McGuinness is ranked in the Top 50 of all the Catholic high schools in the nation by the Catholic High School Honor Roll. This organization also named McGuinness in the Top 20 in Civic Education.
This is the first year for the organization to announce this award. The Catholic High School Honor Roll wishes to honor Catholic high schools around the nation that stress both the importance of education and the importance of religion.
The organization judged schools on whether they excel in the areas of academics, Catholic identity and civic education.
The Catholic Church has a legacy of being the largest non-governmental school system in the world and this legacy is shown through more than 1,300 Catholic high schools in the United States, all of which were sent a survey to fill out last year in order for the board to learn more about all the high schools. Bishop McGuinness filled one out and sent it back in, hoping for the best.
“We filled out the survey so long ago, we filed it away and forgot about it until we were notified that we were one of the Top 50,” said Michelle McGannon, administrative assistant to principal David Morton. “We were surprised to say the least, but it is a great honor. We are very excited.”
The main goal of the Honor Roll is to encourage schools to educate students as much and as well as possible. The common denominator for all the schools that made the Top 50 is their commitment to academic excellence and religious education.
