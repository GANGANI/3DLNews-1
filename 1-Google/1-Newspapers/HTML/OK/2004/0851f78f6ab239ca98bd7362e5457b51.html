How about they try a white male, whose family came over on the Mayflower, owned a plantation and slaves, gave up 40 acres and a mule after Emancipation and now currently claims to be an upstanding individual who isn't a racist because he "knows a black dude?; Perhaps they will. The only problem I have with this article is the amount of clichs used. Clichs are also a "thing of the past" along with racism. Keep writing because it definitely evokes emotion, and that is what literature needs to do. Thank you, Jaron.-Dubb-I completely agree with the overall message of your article. Black history month needs more respect, Black history needs more recognition in our history books and in our culture. I am a staunch supporter of human rights, and thus of minority rights and womens rights. However, your tone and approach in your article to these issues concerns me. Calling people whom you think are ignorant does nothing except maybe diminish your integrity. Referring to white people as ole boss borders on racism. I am excited to see an African-American writer on SIN bringing social injustices and African-American rights into a condition of visibility. However, if in doing this you create more division between races, pitting one against the other, you will be counter-productive and achieve very little. OU is extremely segregated, just look at our fraternity system. The last thing we need is more division. -Patrick-If people are so quick to jump at the fact that racism is still present in our country, maybe we should look at why. Gay Marriage?By Holly ReasonThis is to all those close-minded bigots out there who hide behind Holy Scripture to justify their hatred. Have you actually read the Bible thoroughly? Have you taken into account that, over the centuries, it has been translated and interpreted by those wishing to portray their already established prejudices? I have done extensive research on this, and if you look at the original Hebrew Scriptures, you will find that nowhere in the Bible does it condemn a monogamous, same-sex relationship between two consenting adults. The direct references to homosexuality refer to general sins such as orgies or rape, not a loving relationship between adults.To the person who claimed that being gay is a choice: are you gay? Do you know what it's like to live in a world where everyone says you are wrong and are going to burn for your sins? Do you know what it's like to fear for your safety and well-being every day because what you believe is different from someone else's views, and therefore wrong? If not, then I would suggest you not use that argument anymore. Why would anyone choose to live a life like that? I doubt someone's intrinsic desire to be different is so great that they would daily threaten their own lives. Whats more, biological and psychological studies have found evidence that homosexuality is a natural human occurrence. Rare, yes, but nonetheless natural. Furthermore, this country can no longer afford to base its laws solely on the morality of the Christian belief. There are too many differing viewpoints in this country to do so. One must encourage the equality and freedom for ALL citizens, even if there are those that would oppose it. Discrimination is discrimination, and should not be tolerated on any level.To the man who stated the article was garbage and should not be on the site: it is her opinion, in the opinion section. She was a right to post it there, just as you do. To the person who argued that marriage is a holy union: gays are not fighting for any religious acceptance, but for recognition from our supposedly impartial government whose job it is to protect equality for ALL. The issue of morality is best left to those involved personally, not to the intolerant masses.-Chris-Thank you."Speaking of Christian morals, it is my understanding that the Christian religion also teaches not to judge other people. Who are Oklahomans and Americans to judge whether or not gay and lesbian people are making moral or immoral decisions?"The author's understanding is wrong and is a fundamental misconception about the Christian beliefs. We are taught not to judge a person but to judge their actions - i.e., I would never say, "You're a bad person and are going to Hell. I would, however, as God tells us to, say that a certain behavior is sinful and that you should turn away from it.Please don't attempt to use generalities to imply something other than what God teaches us. There IS a difference between judging a person and judging that person's behavior.-Ana-Homosexuality is a choice. People do have a tendency towards one sex over the other, but it is comparable to alcoholism through genetics as opposed to the color of one's skin.Science continues to hold onto the paradigm of "the survival of the fittest." Now that homosexuals have been voicing their opinions, science has decided that homosexuality is not a choice. Science concludes that any inferior members of a race are naturally rid of or changed through evolution. I equate homosexuals to inferior members in purely scientific terminology - a member of a species that does not help that species grow and prosper is inferior. Since homosexuals cannot reproduce (at least by themselves), they are inferior to the members of the race that can. Therefore, homosexuality would have been obsolete thousands of years ago. Since it is still around, it is classified as a genetic desire as opposed to a genetic trait.Homosexuality is not a genetic trait like the color of one's skin or gender. Homosexuality is a choice based on a genetic desire similar to alcoholism or a bad temper.-Anonymous-I think the author is well meaning, but confused.
