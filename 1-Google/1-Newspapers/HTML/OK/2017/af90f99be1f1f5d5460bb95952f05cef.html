Erielle Reshef wins an Emmy, now an ABC correspondent

Rose Lane
Erielle Reshef, ABC News correspondent and former KOCO morning anchor, wins a regional Emmy for her work as anchor/producer on the “KOCO Chronicles,” along with editor Jason Anderson. She is flanked by her parents, Dr. Eli Reshef and Edie Roodman of Nichols Hills.
Erielle Reshef, former KOCO morning anchor and now ABC News correspondent, took home a regional Emmy for her work on a local Chronicle segment that focused on how the city handled the Daniel Holtzclaw investigation.
“I am very proud mostly because it was a way to shine a light on OKC and because I worked with my editor (Jason Anderson) start to finish on the project.”
“I wrote, produced and anchored the 30-minute special.”
She called Anderson “immensely talented.”
“Our vision came together through hard work and partnership.”
In April, Erielle took correspondent position with ABC News and now lives in New York City with her husband Daniel Frankenstein and their 2-year-old son Mayr. She covers stories of national and international interest.
“Our family is loving the big city,” she said. “Of course, Oklahoma holds my heart.”
“But, this move was beneficial for both of our careers and our son Mayr is so happy in his new school.”
