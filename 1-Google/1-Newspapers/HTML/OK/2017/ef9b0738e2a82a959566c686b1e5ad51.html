What happened in Friday 2005 - 2014?
2005 - Ann and Jack Taylor adopted the cat “Soccer” after he refused to leave the empty house when Ann finished her job as Show House Chairman. Fridayland’s six high schools have a total of 110 Academic Scholars. North has the most with 36 students recognized. Another six students at all our schools combined made the All-Staters list. Veteran Frank Shidler publishes a book on World War II, “Archangels in Battle.” “Oklahoma Rising” float is in the Macy’s Day Parade in New York City. John Larry Nichols is the Beaux Arts King.
2006 - The Nichols Hills City Council holds a hearing on the building moratorium and new restrictions to limit the so-called “McMansions.” Managers of the Oklahoma Department of Rehabilitation send emails to state employees advising them not to use “Merry Christmas,” and warning them it could be grounds for discharge. Politicians and wives join OKC FRIDAY on an East African safari, including Donna and former Gov. George Nigh, Judy and State Representative Guy Liebmann, and Grace and City Councilman Pat Ryan. Theodore M. Elam is the Beaux Arts King.
2007 - The state kicks off its Centennial year with a float in the Rose Bowl Parade featuring Rocket Man. Lee Allan Smith and Blake Wade led and chaired all the Centennial celebrations. In the fall, Oklahoma commemorates 100 years of statehood in grand style with a parade and the Oklahoma Centennial Spectacular in the Ford Center with its country music stars, Miss Americas, local musicians, and fireworks. The sold-out event is also broadcast in the Bricktown Ballpark. Dr. John Bozalis is the Beaux Arts King.
2008 - The OKC FRIDAY editorial board selects Lee Allan Smith as “Oklahoma’s Man of the Century,” for his 50 years of promoting Oklahoma nationally and internationally and arranging spectacular events for the state. Devon Energy is Fortune Magazine’s “100 Best Companies to work for” list. Martha Santore starts the free prom dress giveaway to any high school girl asking for one. Morton Payne is the Beaux Arts King.
2009 - PC North retires 2008 Heisman Trophy winner Sam Bradford’s No. 16 jersey. The Oklahoma legislature honors Stan Alexander for 32-years of service to the City of The Village and serving in the state House of Representatives. Casady plans to expand its chapel its campus. Next generation’s Ryan Leonard and David Holt announce plans to run for public offices in 2010. Fred Jones Hall III is the Beaux Arts Ball King.
2010 - Energy company executives surge to the top of the OKC FRIDAY Most Powerful Oklahomans list ending the years of the Gaylord family supremacy. Larry Nichols CEO of Devon Energy places first and Aubrey McClendon third. OKC Thunder owner Clay Bennett ranked third. Frank Davis Hill is the Beaux Arts King.
2011 - Oklahoma elects its first woman Governor and Mary Fallin’s inaugural on the state capitol steps makes history, Harding Charter Prep and Harding Fine Arts battle goes to court and involves OKC Pubic School District. Western Village Academy honors Stanley Hupfeld by renaming the school the Stanley Hupfeld Academy. Carl Eugene Edwards Jr. is the Beaux Art King.
2012 - Governor Mary Fallin ranks first in OKC FRIDAY’s Most Powerful Women poll. OKC City Councilwoman Meg Salyer finished in second place. Oklahoma City University is placed as No. 39 in the “Best Values in Private Colleges” list by Kiplinger, a Washington D.C. business forecaster. Sister Cities Oklahoma City presents its Global Vision Award to former Gov. George Nigh for the many relationships he developed abroad for Oklahoma industry and farmers during his terms in office. David E. Rainbolt is the Beaux Arts King.
2013 - YWCA breaks ground on Thelma Gaylord Shelter for women. Governor Mary Fallin selected longtime Oklahoma City attorney Robert H. Gilliland to serve on the Oklahoma Workers’ Compensation Commission. 2013 Nichols Hills Pooch Parade benefits the Animal Rescue Friends of Nichols Hills. Forbes Magazine has crowned Continental Resources Chairman and CEO Harold Hamm the richest Oklahoman, unseating BOK Financial Chairman George Kaiser. OKC FRIDAY founder and publisher J. Leland Gourley dies in October. James Cray Meade is the Beaux Arts King.
2014 - OKC FRIDAY celebrates 40 years of publishing the weekly news in northwest Oklahoma city, “Fridayland.” Heritage Hall football team wins its fourth state championship. Deer Creek Public Schools, Harding Charter Preparatory School, the Harding Fine Arts Academy, Belle Isle Middle School and Classen Middle and High Schools received As as the Oklahoma State Department of Education (OSDE) released the 2014 A-F Report Card for public schools statewide. Sister Cities OKC presents its Global Vision Award to Clayton I. Bennett. Nathan L. Sheldon is appointed as Casady School’s 10th headmaster in 67 years.
