FRIDAY marks 43-years of coverage of you

OKCPS BOARD MEMBER Pat Potts and husband Ray. (OKC Friday 1974)
This week OKC FRIDAY celebrates our 43rd anniversary and the beginning of 44-years of continuous publishing of our community newspaper. We are on our third generation of readers. We want them to grow up with a sense of community and provide the leadership in business, education, the arts, civic organizations, and politics that their parents and grandparents did. Here is a look back at the first 10 years.
1974 – John Marshall PTA president Mrs. H. Marshall Farrier and 200 parents and pupils called on Pat Potts and other members of the OKC Public Schools (OKCPS) board of education to move advanced science classes back to John Marshall from Northeast High School. All courses beyond Biology I had been moved to Northeast and Douglas High Schools. The arrangement began under the “Cluster Plan” for desegregation. Mercy Hospital opens on Memorial Road. Bob Hope, Dionne Warwick, Tennessee Ernie For and Ricky Segall appeared at the Quail Creek Golf and Country
Club Stars and Stripes celebrity dinner dance.
1975 –Tom Legan moves from Ohio to Oklahoma City to be president of The Village Bank. John Kikpatrick Jr was the Beaux Arts King, and David Boren is inaugurated as the 21st Governor of Oklahoma. The prestigious US Suburban Press Association accepted FRIDAY newspaper into its membership.

Ed Gaylord and his 18-pound Norther Pike. (OKC FRIDAY 1976)

1976- Twilight Gardens drive-in theater (12 acres at Britton and May) sold for $700,000 to Bab Moore Cadillac. The Oklahoma Symphony launched its Cabaret Performances. Sidney Upsher was the Beaux Arts King.
1977 – A new intersection at Grand and Western open. Jackie Cooper open a Lincoln-Mercury dealership on May Avenue Robert E. Lee was the Beaux Arts King and Earl Sneed, and F.C. Love is inducted into the Oklahoma Hall of Fame.
1978 – Ed Turner, CBS News Producer, came back to Oklahoma to be the new director of KWTV. Edward L. Gaylord was named the Most Powerful Man in Oklahoma in the FRIDAY newspaper poll. Richard Harrison was Beaux Arts King.
1979 – The top 5 in the FRIDAY Most Powerful poll were Ed Gaylord, Dean McGee, David Boren, Henry Bellmon and George Nigh. The Oklahoma Hall of Fame inducts William G. Paul . Dr. G. Rainey Williams was tapped as Beaux Arts King. B.C. Clark Jewelers opened its first mall store in North Park Mall.
1980 – Patrons fight school board to keep Sunset and Nichols Hill elementary schools open. The Oklahoma Symphony Orchestra kick off its 44th season with Maestro Herrera conduction and Doc Severinsen at the Pops. William Cleary was the Beaux Arts King and Quail Springs Mall opened. Reagan elected President and Republican Don Nichols Oklahoma U.S. Senator
1981 – Ann Taylor is elected to the Nichols Hills City Council. FOP is out as Nichols Hills Police bargainer. Richard Clements was the Beaux Arts King. Should private school tuition be tax deductible was the FRIDAY poll question of the week?
1982 – The Feds close Penn Square Bank. Gov. George Nigh knocks Ed Gaylord out of the #1 spot on the FRIDAY Most Powerful poll. Rex Ball head Chamber’s Goals Panel. J.W. McLean was the Beaux Arts King and James D. Fellers and Lowe Runkle are inducted into the Oklahoma Hall of Fame. Luciano Pavarotti comes to Oklahoma City for a benefit concert for St. Anthony Hospital.
1983 - Pody Poe’s Village Police promote first women to Sgt. F.M Petree was the Beaux Art King and Dr. Tullos Costin, and Jacqueline Carey are inducted into the Oklahoma Hall of Fame.
1984 - Nichols Hills home raided for gambling. South African surgeon Christian Barnard comes to Oklahoma City to consult with Baptist Hospital’s Dr. Nazih Zuhdi on the heart transplant program. Nichols Hills Adopt-A-Park program nets $113,000 its first year. Bob Hope returns to OKC to raise money for Conservative Political Action Committee.
