Beloved plant is blooming in her last days

Joy Richardson
Showing me that his treasured plant is planning its demise after fully flowering, is Kent Phillips. That’s nature! Photos by Joy Richardson | okcFRIDAY
Day did not start well. Third sip of coffee delivered something solid into my mouth. Coffee done right generally is not solid. I took it off the tip of my tongue. It was a housefly. Primal scream and manic spitting while stomping and jumping. For the uninitiated, the taste of fly isn’t like chicken -- closer to salamander.
Then I got a short grocery list at my request from my neighbor, Carol Cooley. Carol is recovering from a broken collar bone, plus a sprained wrist. The iPhone self-corrected all by its silly little self. Milk, bananas and Greek Yogurt asked me to bring her a Greek lawyer. Sprouts didn’t have any.

Proud of his BIG girl is plant guru Kent Phillips.

• Appointment on my calendar: Monday morning, 10 a.m. -- date with plant -- see how exciting my life can be! Janie Deupree let me know about her green nephew/niece in the form of a Century plant, owned by her brother Kent Phillips. I figure it is a girl as it is happily blossoming, feet at a time.
Gracious Kent led me through his totally cool and magazine-worthy contemporary home to a garden backyard full of lush greenery. Couldn’t miss this plant. Kent said he'd had her a few years, but thinks that stuff about Century plants only blooming once every hundred years probably isn’t true.
His girl winters over in a storage facility with windows, and early this spring, when he went in to visit, she was shooting up a spire that would have touched the ceiling soon. He moved her outside about the first of March and she has moved her blossoms up about six feet within three weeks. (Note photo for size contrast) Such plants often send up little knobby shoots which are called “pups.”
The transportation must have been fascinating as this thing had to be strapped into place on a pickup bed, and her spire (I’m guessing some 20+ feet tall) was waving about on the streets as she rode home.
We talked about her like she was a real being, stroking her fat fibrous leaves which were getting a tinge of dark, meaning that after she finished her blooming, she would die. But for now she is bold, blooming and beautiful, and I am so glad I had a chance to meet her.

Honored as Ladies Leaving a Legacy by the Oklahoma Hospitality Club are Kaye Adams and Linda Barnett. This honor was given during the Ladies in the News luncheon.

• Ladies Leaving a Legacy was the theme of Oklahoma Hospitality Club’s luncheon and style show honoring some very special women who are responsible for good things in our city. CK & Co. provided the clothing for the smiling women strutting down the ramp. A busy afternoon for guests as we had a short address and greeting from bombastic Sherri Coale, lunch, brief remarks from the Grant Recipients, (Angels Family Foster Network accepted by Jennifer Abney and Genesis Project, accepted by Garrett Haviland) and Chaplain Adrian Martin serenaded us, accompanied by Charlotte Christopher -- and the fashion show.

Frisky honoree Saundra Naifeh just having stepped off the fashion show ramp during the Ladies in the News event.

You no doubt know the ladies being honored, and can readily see why they were chosen. Honorees were Kaye Adams, Cindy Batt, Linda Barnett, Freda Deskin, Jenny Dunning, Mari Fagin, Tracy Grammer, Nancy Hyde, Eunice Khoury, Saundra Naifeh, Nancy Rogers and Becky Switzer. Becky Ivins co-chaired the event, with Connie Weber. The women rocked the stage with big smiles, waves and impromptu bows -- really cute!

Don and Ellen Lisle chat with Gary Trost during the OKC Golf & CC President’s ball honoring Jim Robertson and wife Claudia.

• Knowing the fun-loving nature of Jim and Claudia Robertson, we knew the Oklahoma City Golf and Country Club President’s Ball would be fun -- and they didn't disappoint. Jim is the new club president. First Lady Claudia looked adorable in a hot pink gown with a graduated hem as she and Jim greeted guests.
Jim had done his homework, having taken a thorough tour of each inch of the very large facility. He came up with, “I just can't believe how many lawn mowers we have!”
Around the ballroom were Helen and Pat Dailey, Ellen and Don Lisle, Amy and Roy Bankhead, Karen and Rob Luke, Brenda and Tom McDaniel, Holly and Clay Healey, Jeary and Mike Seikel, Marilyn and Gene Torbett, Linda Rooker and Joe Fallin, Julee and JW Coyle, and Terri and Andy Hubbard.
Silver candelabrum rose to three feet on alternating tables with hydrangea and streaming ivy, other tables had low bowls of the same lush flowers. Others attending were Nancy and Joe Bradley, Millie and Johnson Hightower, Leslie and Scott Samara, Susan and Mark Robertson, and Wiley and Bill Hugos. If I thought I could get away with it, I would have snatched the red diagonally ruffled dress right off Wiley -- it was gorgeous!
While In10City was ramping up the music, President Jim passed out red ball caps emblazoned with, “Make OKC Golf & CC Great Again!” Gonna’ be a wild ride.
