Garth gave me a 'Go Pokes' squeeze.

Vicki Clark
Carrie Underwood poses for a picture with Barry Switzer, left, and Lee Allan Smith, our main man for the Oklahoma Centennial Spectacular.
OKLAHOMA’S 100TH BIRTHDAY will be a day to remember forever. Just being alive and participating in the celebrations will be something to tell our great grandkids about, however, my day peaked backstage before the Centennial Spectacular.
Garth Brooks grabbed me and gave me a great big squeeze of a hug and shouted, “Go Pokes!”
While I would like to leave you with the impression that he saw me and was so taken with my beauty that he just couldn’t keep his hands off me, it didn’t exactly happen that way.
All the stars were messing around backstage before and after the press conference. Johnny Bench lugged a bag of stuff around asking Garth to sign a poster, Vince Gill to autograph a cowboy hat, etc. I assume Johnny was collecting for a charity auction.
I noticed all the stars, and especially Garth, seemed quite amiable to having their pictures taken with anyone who asked, and more often than not it was the other celebrities wanting someone to take their picture with Garth.
Since he seemed so approachable, I stopped Garth and said, “Thanks for all you do for OSU, I’m a cowboy too.”
He stopped, took my hand, looked in my eyes and said, “What is your name.?”
Under the circumstances, I was extremely proud I could remember the answer to that question.
Then Garth threw his arms around me, gave me a great big squeeze and shouted, “Go Pokes.”
I didn’t have the sense to hand my camera off to someone to take a picture of us. Just breathing and remaining upright was pretty much all I could manage.
Before the hug, the highlight of my evening thus far, was seeing Donna and Clem McSpadden at the Marriott and having a glass of wine (and milk for Clem) before the show and hearing Clem’s Reba stories.
Clem launched Reba on the road to stardom. Reba’s dad and Clem grew up together. Reba wanted to go to the National Finals Rodeo when she was a kid and asked her dad if Clem could get her tickets.
“Call him up and tell him you want a job singing,” her dad advised.
Reba called and Clem hired her for $10 to sing the National Anthem. Al Good and Reba discovered a problem just before show time. Al’s orchestra couldn’t play it in Reba’s key.
“Just get out there and sing it acappela,” Clem told her.
The rest is history, but Reba has never forgotten her original mentor. She sent the McSpaddens tickets to the Centennial Spectacular and her husband Norval Blackstock was picking them up after the show for a private get together.
Reba invites the McSpaddens to all her shows. One time, in Vegas I think Clem said, Reba invited them to her palatial suite after the show.
“We walked in and Reba was standing in front of the fireplace with a $10 bill in her hand,”Clem said.
“Look what that $10 has bought me,” Reba said, waving the bill at him.
The evening just kept building as we took our seats in the Ford Center. The Centennial Spectacular was an absolutely incredible show.
I’ve seen major awards shows on television which didn’t have as many stars.
And these were all from Oklahoma... Garth Brooks, Vince Gill, Toby Keith, Reba McEntire, Carrie Underwood, The All American Rejects, Byron Berline, The Flaming Lips, Argus Hamilton, Kelli O’Hara, Leona Mitchell, Patti Page, Blake Shelton, Jimmy Webb.
Sports stars Johnny Bench, Bobby Murcer, Bart Conner and wife, Nadia Comaneci, Barry Switzer.
Five Miss Americas from Oklahoma, Jane Jayroe Gamble, Susan Powell, Shawntel Smith Wuerch, Jennifer Berry Gooden and the reigning Miss America Lauren Nelson.
Plus Maestro Levine and the Oklahoma City Philharmonic, OCU American Spirit Dance Company and the Canterbury Choral Society.
Truly an awesome evening for a great state.
— OKC FRIDAY Newspaper
