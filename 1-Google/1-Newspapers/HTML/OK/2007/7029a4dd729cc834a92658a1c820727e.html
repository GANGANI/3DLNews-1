Silver shoes are 'in' with Little Joe & me
LOTS of stares at me in downtown OKC last week when I went to the annual Bedlam meeting of our Rotary club. I got at least one vote of approval.
I could read the minds of some of these people -- younger ones and old, uninformed ones. They were thinking:
“Why in the world is Leland going out in public wearing those conspicuous silver shoes.?” (The very same ones you saw in the picture in my column opening on the front page today).
To inform the young and the uninformed old, there was a small in stature, but mighty big in sports headlines coast to coast, OU Sooner halfback named Joe Washington, second highest gainer in OU history.
Per cubic inch, Joe is unquestionably the biggest running back ground gainer in the fabulously successful history of OU football, down through Bud Wilkinson, Barry Switzer and Bob Stoops. (Those last three names, for the benefit of Aggies and other envious football fans, are the 3B winningest coaches in Oklahoma chronicles of grid greatness.)
Well, Little Joe Washington got as much publicity from the silver shoes he always wore, as he did from all those dozens of touchdowns he made.
Joe was our Sooner star on this year’s bedlam Rotary program. Some other nice guy named Paul Blair represented the Aggies and little (well, littler) Bob Barry, was the mediator for the two speakers.
I figured it was the patriotic Sooner thing to do to wear silver shoes. I went to Ace Hardware and got a can of silver spray paint and that’s what happened.
Joe loved it. He got down on his knees and praised me, then insisted on posing with me and my shoes.
Which brings me to a very important point.
You can tell which OU-OSU football fans never set foot in the classroom of either. They’re the ones booing the “other” of the two teams at games. The bona fide Sooners and Aggies cheer each other in all games but one. They never boo each other. Bad taste.
