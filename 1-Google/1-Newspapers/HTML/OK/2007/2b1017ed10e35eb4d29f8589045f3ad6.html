He was a preacher�s kid with a new college degree in Sacred Music and Religious Studies in 1981 when a young church in Oklahoma City hired him as minister for youth and music.
Four years later, during which time he married Kim Townsdin, he became senior pastor of that 26-year-old Belle Isle church which had an average weekly attendance of 143.
Fast forward to 2006 when, in the U.S., the �politically correct� secular movement is sweeping the country.
This senior pastor had led his flock to a growth of 20 full-time associate pastors and 44 full-time other staff.
In 2006 he was preaching to a weekly attendance of 4,200.
He is Marty Grubbs, Senior Pastor of Crossings Community Church (formerly Belle Isle) and he is FRIDAY�s choice as:

Crossings is Oklahoma�s largest in the growing mega church movement now sweeping the country in what many observers believe has slowed the attempt to secularize America.
�Marty,� as he likes to be called, is a happy family man. He and his beautiful wife, Kim, have three children, daughters Kristin (an OSU coed) and Cole, and son Tyler. Marty enjoys time with them.
Marty is a hard-working, dedicated religious leader with a remarkable sense of humor. He believes God is entitled to the same good business practices enjoyed by private enterprise.
He attributes much of the growth of mega churches to their independence. The congregation rules. There is no hierarchy telling them what to do and who will do it. He also points out a church of 5,000 has ten times the resources as do ten churches of 500 each. This offers many extra services and activities.
For instance, Crossings has a $10 million budget and is debt free. This is amazing since the church has no signed pledges on which to build its budget and he seldom mentions money from the pulpit. The weekly offering does it.
Crossings Community Church is a 7-day-a-week beehive of all kinds of activities.
There is a fully-staffed Counseling Center that helps people with problems in marriage, divorce, addictions, abuse and others.
A modern fitness center is available and the church has its own sports teams, even with an Associate Pastor for Athletics.
Plans call for building an additional gym and a new fellowship hall.
A health clinic, including eye and dental care, is on the campus.
The church is supervised by an 11-member Board of Elders, as a separate operation. Crossings also has a debt-free school through the 7th grade. One grade per year is being added to get to a 4-year high school.
