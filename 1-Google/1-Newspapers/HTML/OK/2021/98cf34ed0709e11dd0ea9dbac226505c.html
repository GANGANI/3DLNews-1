Top 10 stories of 2021, Nos. 6-10

By Stephanie Warner on Wednesday, December 29, 2021
Image
Body

The Weatherford Daily News, through staff and reader poll, has selected the Top 10 stories for 2021.

This story features the Top 6-10, which includes Weatherford Public Schools hiring the district’s first female superintendent, SWOSU’s partnership with Yukon Public Schools, building plans for the new Weatherford Police Department building, the demolition of the old hospital and dealing with supply chain shortages due to COVID-19.

No. 6 — WPS hires first female superintendent

The Weatherford Public School Board of Education named Jill Henderson as the district’s next superintendent during its regular meeting March 8.

This makes Henderson the first female superintendent in the district’s history.

“During this transformation time in education, we need a tested leader who has the expertise and experience to engage the community and the vision to propel our school forward for the future,” Dennis Green, president of the board, said. “Mrs. Henderson has deep Western Oklahoma roots, strong leadership and unparalleled knowledge of education needs. The board worked tirelessly to find and hire the perfect leader for our school and Mrs. Henderson is the one.”

Henderson has more than 30 years of experience in public education, from teacher to counselor and various levels of school administration. She began as an elementary teacher before transitioning to a special education teacher and coach for 11 years. Henderson also has been a public school and career technology counselor.

She spent three years as principal of Ripley High School and Altus High School. She also served as the elementary principal for Ponca City Public Schools before becoming the district’s director of special services.

Green said Henderson and her experience stood out from the very beginning of the interview process.

“We are very excited for her to lead our district,” Green said. “She just stood out at the very beginning, and we were excited about her from the very first interview. By the second interview, we were even more excited about what she could bring to the table: her experience and her openness to lead the district and bring it to new heights.”

Henderson said she is thrilled to join the WPS team, which is known for providing exceptional academic and extracurricular opportunities for its students.

“I look forward to serving students, staff, parents and community members as superintendent,” Henderson said. “Weatherford is an outstanding school district with high expectations and strong community support. I believe every student has unlimited capacity to learn, and Weatherford students deserve the very best education possible. Together — with help from the outstanding WPS team — I am excited to build on the district’s foundation of excellence.”

Henderson received her bachelor’s degree in Elementary Education and her master’s degree in School Counseling and Bilingual Education from the University of Central Oklahoma. She also has a master’s degree in Special Education from California State University-Bakersfield and currently is a doctoral candidate in administrative leadership at Southern Nazarene University.

No. 7 — SWOSU expansion — Yukon Campus

Southwestern Oklahoma State University (SWOSU) and Yukon Public Schools (YPS) have announced a major expansion of the partnership formed in fall 2020.

December 6, the YPS Board of Education formalized an agreement with SWOSU which will see the new YPS College & Career Center serve as host to the first SWOSU location in the Oklahoma City Metro.

“The agreement finalized between YPS and SWOSU serves as another reminder of how hard our public school districts work in cooperation with their higher education system to provide a bright future for Oklahoma students,” Oklahoma Superintendent of Public Instruction Joy Hoffmeister said. “The Oklahoma State Department of Education wants to congratulate Yukon Public Schools on forming a first of its kind partnership with SWOSU.”

The center will be the first-ever fouryear university on an Oklahoma high school campus and will be built by YPS. This project was approved by more than 70 percent of voters in the 2021 bond election. SWOSU will provide a capital contribution of $5 million in exchange for a 25-year lease of approximately 12,000 square feet of space within the facility. SWOSU will be designated as YPS’s preferred provider of concurrent programs and associate, bachelor and graduate degrees.

No. 8 — Plans for new police station revealed

Planning and construction continue to move forward for Weatherford’s new state of the art police station and 911 call center.

The 911 call center services all of Blaine County and portions of Custer, Washita and Caddo counties. The updated equipment and expanded work space will be of great help to the department dispatchers and much more efficient.

The new station is twice the size of the current station. The new facility will have 16 jail cells as opposed to the current 32 cells.

The current station has been in use for nearly 50 years and most definitely has outgrown the space in this growing community.

The architects have sent plans to the city which have been reviewed and approved moving the project into the project bidding phase after the first of the year. Bidding on the project will be finalized and construction is estimated to start in March or April.

“We plan to move into our police station close to a year from now which will complete our public safety campus. We will have our ambulance, fire department and police station all in one central location,” Brown said.

No. 9 — Demolition of old hospital

For decades Southwestern Memorial Hospital, now Weatherford Regional Hospital, sat in the heart of Weatherford.

It was the birthplace of many generations of Weatherford families and held an iconic representation for many who call Weatherford home. However, as the community has grown so has the need for broadening the availability of healthcare services and a need for more space.

October 13 crews began demolition of the old hospital building located at 215 N. Kansas St. — in its place will be a stateof-the-art police station and 911 dispatch center.

The project partially is being paid through the YesWeatherford! penny sales tax which was passed in 2020.

Following the demolition of the old hospital, Mayor Mike Brown said the property will be leveled and the city will approve bids for the project early next year.

“After the property is leveled off then the architects will give the final plans to the construction manager which is Joe D. Hall General Contractors, LLC. Then the project will be put out to bid, after bids are approved it will be about 60 days before the project begins. I anticipate the project to begin in March and it will be about a yearlong project,” Brown said.

The “old hospital” demolition for many citizens represents growth, progress, hope and a new era for Weatherford.

No. 10 — Supply chain issues wreak havoc

Throughout 2021, supply chain issues related to COVID -19 have plagued the United States leaving the government and suppliers scrambling to find solutions to shortages on everything from meat to coins.

Shortages on everyday items such as food have resulted in surging prices and increased hardship on families and individuals. Many items have been unable to be found or have been discontinued in production due to shortage of workers, drivers and port operation issues.

In an attempt to find a solution, the government released several COVID-19 relief bills and funding, including funding from the CARES Act.

Since June 15, 2020, the COVID-19 pandemic not only significantly disrupted the supply chain abut also normal circulation patterns for United Sates coin.

As part of that disruption, a plan was adopted to restrict distribution due to the U.S. Mint’s measures put in place to protect its employees. The Federal Reserve lifted the restriction on distributions of coins January 15.

Although these plans and relief funding have helped, America will go into 2022 on a continued trek to finding a sense of normalcy where the supply chain is concerned.
