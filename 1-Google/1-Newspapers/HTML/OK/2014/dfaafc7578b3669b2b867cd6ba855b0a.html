LEED system’s inventor issues words of warning

LEED system’s inventor issues words of warning

By : Kirby Lee Davis//The Journal Record//March 7, 2014//

Listen to this article

TULSA – The founding father of Leadership in Energy and Environmental Design fears its success will not starve off catastrophe.

“I think what’s going to happen is that nature is going to kick our ass as a species in a major way,” said Rob Watson, chief executive and chief scientist of the ECON Group in New York. “We’re going to have to completely rebuild all our systems to fit and then we’ll live happily ever after, but unfortunately it’s going to get real ugly for about 150 years.”

Despite strong inroads by the U.S. Green Building Council’s LEED Green Building Rating System since it was founded in 1994, Watson said, the world economy remains dominated by capitalistic systems geared to destroying our planet. He said that puts LEED in the position of trying to turn around a car that is speeding toward the abyss.

“From the business of the planet standpoint, the only thing that makes sense right now is to let humanity go,” said the 52-year-old Dartmouth College graduate. “We will learn how powerful human law is from natural law. But I fear our kids and grandkids are going to be the ones dealing with the brunt of that.”

ECON’s primary goal is to encourage the adaptation of what Watson called eco-nomics, as opposed to today’s ego-nomics, viewing humanity as the center of the business universe. While he values pursuing LEED, Watson said catastrophic change may be inevitable.

“We’ve set stuff in motion that, no matter what we do, it will happen anyway,” Watson said. “It’s just the nature of nature. Even if we stop emitting everything tomorrow, we’re still going to get our butts kicked.”
