The Ultimate Victory

Pastor Richard Hogue
Jesus defeated Satan and the kingdom of darkness

    Easter is here! Hidden in the midst of the annual traditional trappings of faith, family and friends is the reality of the Easter’s ultimate victory.
    Once again, the vast majority of Oklahomans will make the trek to celebrate this most Holy Day, and even though sermons will be preached and songs will be sung proclaiming Jesus’ sacrificial death, many will miss the good news of this great victory. This great victory is the ultimate purpose Lord’s death: His strategic defeat of Satan and the kingdom of darkness.  
    The consequence of this victory is two-fold. The Lord Jesus Christ is presently seated on his rightful throne as King of kings liberating the whole creation from constant attacks of evil. As a result, those who receive Him as Lord and Savior are saved "from the power of darkness and transferred into the kingdom of the Son of God” (Col. 1:13). This is the glorious good news of the Easter message.  
    But the question must be asked, if Jesus defeated Satan and his kingdom of evil, why does the world suffer the ravages of evil? The answer is that there is still work to be done, and the Church is the only vehicle by which it is to be accomplished. The Church of the Lord Jesus Christ must rise up in the gifts and power of the Holy Spirit and demonstrate the truth that the Kingdom of God has been established and that Satan’s kingdom has been defeated. The Church must preach the gospel of the kingdom, heal the sick, raise the dead, and confront injustice by engaging and overthrowing evil powers, just as Jesus did. When the Church takes her rightful place in the supernatural, then the victory of the Lord Jesus in the cross and resurrection will cover the earth with salvation, righteousness, peace and joy.

Richard Hogue is the senior pastor of Citychurch, a multi-racial, multi-cultural church meeting at 6009 NW Expressway in northwest Oklahoma City. He holds a B.L.S. degree from the University of Oklahoma, a M.T.S. from Harvard Divinity School, and recently completed a Doctor of Ministry degree from Oral Roberts University. With their writings, ministry on television, and pastorates, Hogue and his wife Marilyn have been at the forefront of the move of the Holy Spirit over the past four decades.
