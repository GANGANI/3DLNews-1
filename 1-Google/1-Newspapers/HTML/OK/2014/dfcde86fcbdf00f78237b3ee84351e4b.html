40 Years of Friday: Where are they now?

Rose Lane
Amy Bankhead
Amy and Parker Lee were best friends

In 1997, one of the loves of Amy Bankhead’s life was her buff Cocker Spaniel Parker Lee Richter.
She and Parker Lee were pictured on the front page of a spring 1997 issue of OKC FRIDAY. The newspaper was running a Doggie Look Alike contest.
“The picture was taken in front of my Grandmother’s house that my grandfather built in 1936 on Larchmont Lane,” Amy said. “My mom, Ann Richter took and submitted the picture to Friday for the contest that the newspaper was running. It was in honor of The Nichols Hills Pooch Parade and it was a contest of best look a like dog and owner.”

Unfortunately, they missed the deadline. But, the photo appeared on the front page as the first entry in the 1998 contest.
“My dog, Parker Lee Richter was adorable,” Amy said.”I received him as a college graduation present from my parents.”
“We really did look a lot a like. We both had blonde hair and freckles.”
Amy said she used to dress him up with colorful bandanas and walk him all around Nichols Hills.
“People would stop me and say what a beautiful dog I had,” she said.
That year at the Pooch Parade, Amy said she and Parker Lee won the “Best Tail Wag” contest.
“Parker Lee was the smartest and sweetest dog that I will ever have,” Amy said. “Oh, how I loved him.”
At the time the picture was taken Amy had just moved to Oklahoma City from New Orleans where she graduated from Tulane University. She came to Oklahoma City to The Health Sciences Center to attend The University of Oklahoma College of Public Health.
She graduated in 1996 from The University of Oklahoma College of Public Health with a Master’s degree in Health Promotion Sciences. After working at the Oklahoma State Department of Health, she entered the Oklahoma City University School of Law and graduated in 1999 with a law degree.
Amy currently works part-time at McCaffrey & Associates, P.L.L.C. as an attorney and is a community volunteer. She is on the Board of Lyric Theatre, the Oklahoma County Medical Society Alliance, the University of Oklahoma Breast Institute Advisory Board and is a park chairman for Nichols Hills Parks, Inc. She is the upcoming president of the Medical Alliance beginning in January 2015.
She has chaired Lyric Theatres Broadway Ball and The University of Oklahoma College of Medicine Alumni Association’s Evening of Excellence, co-chaired The Nichols Hills Garden Tour, The Nichols Hills Parks, Inc. “Party in the Park,” The Oklahoma County Medical Society Alliance Kitchen Tour and Pi Beta Phi’s Gathering of Angels luncheon.
“The main loves of my life now are my family members, my husband Roy and two boys, William, age 9 and Christopher, age 6 and my mom, Ann Tankersley Richter, sister, Kim Richter and brother Dan McClain,” she said.
“We are currently in the process of remodeling that old house on Larchmont Lane to continue the tradition of our family living there.”
Parker crossed the Rainbow Bridge at the age of 14.
“I still miss Parker Lee and his wagging tail,” Amy said. “He was one of a kind.”

1997 doggie look alike contest: Amy Richter and Parker missed the deadline, so they are our first 1998 entry.
