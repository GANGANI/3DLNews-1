Judy and Bob turn Catholic Charities campaign kick off into a jump start

Linda Haneborg
All smiles at the announcement of how much has already been raised are Father Rick Stansberry, Chris Cowden, Tom Love, Judy Love and Lexi Skaggs.
Eager attendees gathered recently to hear the exciting news about the future of Catholic Charities and their ambitious capital campaign, Crossbeams.  
    In revealing how all of it came about, Judy Love and Bob Ross humorously recalled how once they decided they did not need to bring in a “professional” fundraiser, through numerous emails, it soon became apparent that Judy and Bob were the perfect pair to lead the pack.

Joan Maguire, Laura Love and Donna Bozalis get their lists together.


    And lead they have.  How exciting it was to hear that through their hard work and that of a phenomenal committee, they are already at 70 percent of their goal! Thus, I am dubbing it a jump start, not necessarily a kick off. Wow oh Wow!!  
    With an ambitious goal of $9.5 million, these folks are showing us how it is done. Who could not pledge to be a part of this new beginning along the Corridor of Hope, as Patrick Raglow so aptly named Classen Boulevard? The new building will be located close to the original location, at 12th and Classen.

From left, Archbishop Paul Coakley shares the good news with Susan and Tucker McHugh.


    The campaign’s name harkens to the organization’s position at the crossroads of church and community. Crossbeams Capital Campaign has an astute and prestigious group of leaders involved beginning with the board of directors chairman Archbishop Paul Coakley, President Charles Wade, Vice President Maria Wallace, Secretary/Treasurer Michael Milligan, Molly Bernard, Hans Butzer, Randall Calvert,Abe Castillo, Deacon Anthony Crispo, Dr James Dixson, Shellie Greiner, Vi T Le, Judy Love, Father Bill Pruett, Father Paul Reyes, Mark Ruffin, Father Rick Stansberry and Jim Everest.

Patrick Raglow, Susie Fuller, Doug Fuller and Linda Mares strategize for the Catholic Charities Crossbeams campaign.


    Joining Chairmen Judy Love and Bob Ross on their committee are Sherry Beasley, Lee Beasley, Tim Berney, Father Tom Boyer, Barbara Brou, Tom Casso, Nick Dell’Osso, Tricia Everest, Beverly Funke, Mark Funke, Deacon Tom Goldsworthy, Bryan Gonterman, Shellie Greiner, Father Joe Jacobi, Mike Milligan, David Morton, Chris Reen, Patrick Rooney, Robert Slater, Father Rick Stansberry, Mike Turpen, Tony Tyler, Hans Butzer, Steven Jacobi and Maria Wallace.
    Catholic Charities staff support includes Patrick Raglow, Lexi Skaggs, Nancy Largent and Sonny Wilkinson.
