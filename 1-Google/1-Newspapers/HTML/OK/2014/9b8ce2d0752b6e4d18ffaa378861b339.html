Our Bill takes on ‘Real Housewives’

Attorney Bill Zuhdi
Rose Lane•
Fridayland attorney Bill Zuhdi has filed a lawsuit in California Federal Court against a cast member of “The Real Housewives of Beverly Hills” and Bravo, the network which televises the reality show.
Zuhdi represents Kay Rozario, a family friend of Housewife Kim Richards. Zuhdi said Rozario was an overnight guest in Richards’ home when she was attacked by Richards’ pit bull Kingsley.
Rozario suffered wounds on her left hand and right arm. Paramedics treated her at Richards’ home.
Kingsley just recently allegedly bit Richards’ niece, requiring the young girl to need surgery on her hand.

Rozario is seeking damages of $75k. Zuhdi said the suit is also against Bravo because the dog is a big part of the show.
“The producers are making money off this vicious dog, while the public is in danger,” Zuhdi said.
“The other Housewives even talk on the show about how ferocious the dog is. They worry about Kim herself being attacked and finding her dead.”
Zuhdi said cases move quickly through Federal Court. In California, he said, laws hold residents to the fact that “if you own a dog and it attacks, you are responsible.”
Zuhdi is considered an authority on constitutional rights and has been interviewed and quoted by The Wall Street Journal and has appeared on “ABC World News Tonight with Peter Jennings.”
