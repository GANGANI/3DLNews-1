Joan Lunden inspires those at Juliette Low lunch

Joy Richardson
Honorary Chairman of the Girl Scout luncheon Karen Luke with Jane Gamble and Kirk Hammons.
You sophisticated folks with knowledge about the ‘70s film and TV production know that a “magazine” is one of those big reels of film. Juliette Low Society luncheon speaker Joan Lunden said she did not know that back then when she got her big news reporting break in New York.
Pulling up excitedly in front of the courthouse in NYC, the film crew bailed out of the truck, pulling out tripods, cameras and boxes of equipment. “How many magazines will you need?” they asked. She was shocked and replied, “This is a big story and I’m pretty sure I won’t have time to read.” She reported it took a considerable amount of time for the crew pounding on the ground in laughter to compose themselves. But that gaffe got them on her side, and they certainly didn’t think she was haughty.
Joan seemed a regular gal, but she has a Superwoman T-shirt under her blouse as she is the mother of seven – two sets of twins – was the host of “Good Morning America” for nearly two decades, and launched a line of healthy non-stick cookware and a skincare line. She’s still going at it as the spokesperson for a leading senior referral service, “A Place for Mom,” hosts RLTV’s “Taking Care,” a series for caregivers across America.
Joan’s admitted positive attitude stood her in good stead jobwise when she had no idea if she could deliver what she was promising. She’s a quick learner, and shared wonderful advice to the Girl Scouts in the audience. The secrets were upbeat and easy such as, “Don’t wait for good things to happen,” “If you want in the game – find a way to get on the playing field,” “Be open to opportunities,” and “find good mentors.” She gave the group some eight pointers and expounded on each with delightful stories.
Oklahoma City Golf and Country Club was filled with Scouts supporters including Lois Salmeron, Sandy Meyers, Sara Branscum, Linda Rodgers, Lil Ross, Becky Roten, Susan Howard, Becky Shaffer, Lissa Blaschke, LaDonna Meinders, Joan Maguire, Nancy Martin, Polly Nichols, Myrla Pierson, Jane Thompson and Linda Whittington. Let me tell you yet another reason why everyone loves Polly Nichols: you can imagine how busy she is as she spends countless hours in work for the good things that go on in our community, state and city. Just out of the blue I got a sweet Polly note last week mentioning a wee little teensy thing I did, that nobody would notice – except Polly. And she found time to send me an unexpected note. You can’t help but love that lady!
Chairmen for the Juliette Low Leadership event were Cindy Batt, Ann Cameron and Katie Price. Because she, like Polly, does things with such depth and commitment, Karen Luke was the honorary chairman for the event. Her husband Rob, daughter Julie, her husband Jeremy and daughter Emily Tubb, son David and wife Becky, and their daughter Hatti Karen Luke (impressive shoes to fill) rounded out Karen’s table. Others enjoying the luncheon were Judy Hatfield, Vicki Gourley, Ellen Jane Wheeler, Tami Murphy-Moore, Barbara Eskridge, Lana Lopez, Janie Dupree, Cheryl Hewett, Anne Gray, Peggy Gandy and Karen Goebel Keith. And, yes, a Girl Scout cookie did top our dessert.
Devon Energy Corporation was the big sponsor of the event, and though there were many others, Chesapeake Energy, The Chickasaw Nation, Ethics & Excellence in Journalism & Inasmuch Foundation, and The Oklahoman – and still many others! Shannon Evers addressed the group and brought tears to our eyes. GO, Girl Scouts!

•How fitting that the American Heart & Stroke Association puts on the Heart Ball as a wildly successful fundraiser, and chooses “Sweethearts” to represent them. These young ladies aren’t only beautiful and talented, but put in a lot of work participating in educational, community service and leadership positions throughout the year.

Yup, twin Sweethearts Lizzie and Katie with mom Meredith Cassidy.


Some 800 people filled the Cowboy & Western Heritage Museum for a great evening with dinner, silent and live auctions, the presentation of the Sweethearts, and dancing. Gazing around the filled room, Chairmen Andrea and Bryan Gonterman looked so pleased that their efforts and those of the vital committees had accomplished all that. The Executive Leadership Team was comprised of Susan Chambers, Mark Grimes, Donna and Bruce Lawrence, Marc Maun, Zavier Neira, Teresa Rose, Kyle Toal and Mike Turpen.

Carl and Anita Milam with Sweetheart daughter Helen.


Volunteer helper Wendy Jordan showed us how the hand-held bidding computer thing-y worked, and was careful not to snicker at our ineptitude. We could bid, we could find out the last bid, we could arrange to always bid higher, we could see what had not been bid upon, we could have it put out the cat and feed the piranha in the moat at home. (That last may be true, but that would require “computer thing-y advanced” lessons.)
Everywhere you turned your head were luscious young ladies in glorious gowns who were really Sweethearts. They joined their tables for dinner before taking the stage with their escorts. Among those I saw were Bill Cameron with his pretty daughter Claire Cameron, and I didn’t see Claire’s mom Laura Love, but come on, even this veteran crowd mingle-er can’t assimilate 800 folks. We saw Sherry and Ike Bennett, Polly and Larry Nichols (I told you they are everywhere), Marnie and Clayton Taylor, Jo Carol Cameron, Carol Smaginski and Bob Allen, Rhonda Corley and Mike O’Meara, Marshall Lucas who was sitting with “Sweetheart” Avery Timberlake and Angela and Jim Timberlake, Sweethearts Lizzie and Katie Cassidy and folks Meredith and Tad Cassidy, and Sweetheart Aubrey Hermen and her parents Tamara and Jeffrey.

Proud of their “Sweetheart” are Chris and Marcy Kenney with daughter Mackenzie, and grandmother B. Ann Cole, little brother Drew Kenney in front.


Desmond Mason can really energize a crowd, as he put one of his paintings up for live auction (it was a heart – so appropriate) and offered to have the winning bidder in a picture with him. It brought, I think, about $9,750 – see how excited people were!
Another Sweetheart was Mackenzie Kenney who is the daughter of Marcy and Chris Kenny, and granddaughter of my childhood friend, B. Ann Cole. Carl and Anita Milam’s Sweetheart daughter is Helen Milam. Sitting at our table was Hanna Northcutt and her parents Jennifer and Barry, and grandparents Donna and David Northcutt.
Nine-year-old Ella was a special story in that she, in losing her baby sister about age three to a “sick heart,” started “Jump Rope for Heart” in her school with a goal of raising a thousand dollars! She has raised over $30,000 in three years and graciously addressed the crowd as a Heart Ambassador. It moved us all. The evening was sponsored by the Chickasaw Nation, Integris Heart Hospital, 7-Eleven, AT&T, and Central Liquor Company, among others. The poised “Sweethearts” made the evening beautiful and the generosity of the supporters, made it meaningful in our world.
