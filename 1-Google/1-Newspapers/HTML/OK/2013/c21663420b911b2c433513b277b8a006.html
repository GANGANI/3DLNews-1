Avis is Orchid honoree

Avis Scaramucci
Avis Scaramucci will be honored with the James R. Tolbert III Crystal Orchid Award during the 12th Annual Orchids in October luncheon set for Thursday, 11:30 a.m. to 1 p.m. at the Myriad Botanical Gardens.
Scaramucci is the proprietor of Painted Door gift boutiques, Nonna’s Bakery and Nonna’s Euro-American Ristorante and Bar.

In selecting her for the award, members of the Myriad Gardens Foundation Board of Directors said that Scaramucci’s commitment to her businesses, customers and employees is matched by a commitment to Oklahoma City.
She serves on numerous boards including the Bricktown Association, Children’s Hospital Foundation Board of Advocates, Greater Oklahoma City Chamber, Regional Food Bank, St. Anthony Foundation, and the Myriad Gardens Foundation. She has been honored by the Oklahoma Hospitality Club Ladies in the News and recognized for her entrepreneurship with the Association for Women in Communications Byliner Award.
She was Panhellenic Woman of the Year and received the Chairman’s Award of Excellence by the South Oklahoma City Chamber of Commerce, named a Living Treasure by the Oklahoma Health Center Foundation, and has received the Neal Horton Award from Downtown Oklahoma City’s Dean A. McGee Awards. She was inducted into the Circle of Excellence for the Journal Record’s Woman of the Year and was also included in the OKC Friday Newspaper as one of the 50 Most Powerful Women in Oklahoma City.
“Her love for and support of the Myriad Botanical Gardens is evidenced by her continuous generous donations of her time and talent,” board members said. “She donates an impressive amount of catering services to the Gardens, most recently, 350 pieces of her famous Chocolate Cake for our 25th anniversary gala Splendor in the Gardens.”
She has also made it possible for the gardens to have a wide variety of children’s books available for use in our Children’s Gardens to encourage literacy in the community.
“The Myriad Gardens Foundation is honored to be a beneficiary of her time, talent and passion,” board members said.

Another facet of the event is the much-loved community orchid sale.
Beginning Thursday morning and running through Sunday, Oct. 21, a stunning variety of orchids will be available for sale. Prices begin at $15. The sale is being curated by Myriad Gardens Conservator Nate Tschaenn, whose keen eye and extensive knowledge will ensure some unusual and beautiful selections.
“We’ll have some Paphiopedilums (the slipper orchids), oncidiums, Zygopetalums, dendrobiums, cymbidium probably cattleyas, miniature orchids like masdevallias, a few vandas and of course the ubiquitous phaleonopsis, though I’ll try to get some of the more unusual styles,” said Tschaenn.
Tschaenn will give two free classes about orchids on Oc. 19:
• Orchid Care 101 -- 11-11:30 a.m. in the second floor conference room at the Crystal Bridge. Learn the ins and outs of how to care for orchids at home. Tschaenn will demonstrate potting and dividing orchids, provide information on watering and proper lighting, and answer questions to help demystify this fascinating flower.
•Deceptive Orchids - 12-12:30 p.m., in the same location. When it comes to ensuring pollination, some orchids go to notorious lengths. Trickery and deceit are essential to this mysterious flower. Join Nate Tschaenn, our conservatory curator, for a fascinating discussion about the deceptive nature of orchids.
