He made me feel at home.

Michael Elder, Anna Williams, Andrew Shaneyfelt, Michelle Tibbs, host Mike Giese, Kevin Bradshaw, Erin Fitzgerald, George Spicier and Sheila Rae show off their costumes during Giese’s Halloween party.
From the time I stepped inside the doors nine years ago to start my job at the FRIDAY Newspaper, I knew I was at home.
Everyone welcomed me with open arms and especially Mr. Gourley whom I had always admired. Being a friend of my grandfather, Friday Fitzgerald, I knew that they were quite alike and I loved that. They were both comedians and loved people.
Mr. Gourley was at work every single day, unless he was traveling of course, and always helpful no matter what. He had a way about him that everyone adored. He was kind, funny, ornery, and always telling jokes. He loved to see people smile.
When Vicki and Leland hired me, they took a chance at someone who had zero advertising knowledge, only retail but eventually found my way and was introduced to a field I love. Mr G loved life, his wife, his family and his newspaper. He was loyal to all those around him. He had an open door policy and enjoyed visitors coming by the office to talk politics, life, or anything on their mind.
We always looked forward to birthdays at the office as he would cleverly write a poem in honor of you and would proudly read it aloud for all to hear. He would just sit back, smile and enjoy the happiness it brought.
The hallways will never be the same but will always bring back the best memories that will last a lifetime. I am honored to say I knew Mr. Gourley and had the pleasure of spending many great years with him. All my love to Vicki, she was a lucky lady.
• Showing he knew how to have a Spooktacular time, Mike Giese worked his tail off to make sure to have a Halloween bash that everyone would remember! From tombstones in the front yard to spiderwebs on the walls, regular lightbulbs were replaced with colored ones to give a glowing effect throughout his home. Every nook and cranny was decorated for the holiday and the hallways glowed with fluorescent lights and ghosts and goblins settled in chairs to scare the guests.
Wonderful hors d’oeuvres flanked the dining room table and the pool table centerpiece was a special cider brewing for guests to enjoy. The costumes were funny, questionable, and dynamic and everyone enjoyed getting a giggle out of each others creativeness.
