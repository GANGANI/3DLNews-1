‘Light from the Darkroom’ comes from Oklahoma

“Light from the Darkroom” actress Lymari Nadal, director Lance McDaniel and actors Gerardo Davila and Louie Arnette during a scene shot in Oklahoma City. Photo by Rose Lane | okcFRIDAY
Fridayland film director Lance McDaniel has wrapped principal photography on his latest movie, “Light from the Darkroom,” a religious thriller set in Panama and China.
McDaniel hired an almost entirely Oklahoma based crew to pull off the foreign set film. He also shot the scenes in Oklahoma City.
Tulsa’s Kathleen Rooney had the idea for the story. She worked with Oscar nominee Al Reinert to develop the script.

Kathleen is married to Manhattan Construction CEO Francis Rooney, who served as the ambassador to the Holy See from 2005-2008 and currently serves on the Panama Canal Advisory Board.
Kathleen’s experiences in the different cultures inspired her to write a story about the courage and strength of Panamanian women, as well as the ongoing challenges facing the Catholic Church, especially in China. The Rooneys also financed the film.
To lead the team as producer, McDaniel tapped McGuinness High School and OCU graduate Nathan Gardocki. Gardocki had served as unit production manager on McDaniel’s previous film, “Crazy Enough,” and had just completed producing The Jogger for writer/directors Casey Twenter and Jeff Robison and Rudderless for director William H. Macy.
“I have known Nathan Gardocki since he was in college at OCU and there is not a more focused, intelligent, professional producer anywhere,” McDaniel said.
After nine months of discussions, McDaniel and Gardocki flew to Washington, D.C. to close the deal. And, the first thing they all agreed on was that they wanted to support the Oklahoma film industry and make the movie in Oklahoma.
“The foreign setting was a challenge,” Gardocki said. “But we knew if we could get all of our Oklahoma crew members on board, we could figure out together how to make something awesome.”
Production Designer Linda Trippe was the first hire and accompanied the team to Panama City to scout locations and buy fabrics, food and artifacts that would later be used in costumes and set dressing. Trippe is a former caterer who has forged an impressive film and commercial career over the last decade, including work on The Killer Inside Me, Bringing Up Bobby, Thunderstruck and August: Osage County.
Gardocki and McDaniel then hired five more Bishop McGuinness graduates to help lead the production. Jon Roman came on board as a co-producer, Walker Milligan as first assistant director, Nathan’s younger brother Jonathan Gardocki as second assistant director and older brother Matthew Gardocki as visual effects supervisor. Kelsey Frederickson, McGuinness graduate and daughter of Oscar winner Gray Frederickson, took on the Art Department coordinator job and helped manage the transformation of local buildings into Panamanian and Chinese sets.
Now that production is finished, the film has started post-production. Beau Leland will lead the editing, cinematographer Alan Novey will now work on the color correction, Dave Hannon at Toy Gun Films will handle the sound mixing and musician Graham Colton will compose and select all of the music for the film.
As the film works its way through festivals and the distribution process next year, most of the attention will focus on the international cast. Former Miss Panama and telenovela star Patricia Deleon, Lymari Nadal (American Gangster), Steven Michael Quezada (Breaking Bad) and Russell Wong (Joy Luck Club) will attract fans and press from their respective markets and hopefully create enough excitement to secure a major release, McDaniel said.
As for the 70+ Oklahoma crewmembers, most will now roll onto the next film production, which is slated to be the first feature from director Brent Green. Green is an award-winning short director from Edmond who is the son of Mardel Books founder Mart Green and grandson of Hobby Lobby founders David and Barbara Green.
Green’s film is one of the last to utilize the state film rebate.
“Hopefully, the legislature will extend and increase the rebate program so the hundreds of Oklahomans working in the film industry can continue working in Oklahoma on high quality, often spiritual films, funded by outstanding Oklahomans like the Rooneys and the Greens,” McDaniel said.
