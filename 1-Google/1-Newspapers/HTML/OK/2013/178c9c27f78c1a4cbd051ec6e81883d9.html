Reception honors young leaders

From left: Christopher Lloyd, Megan Parrish, Ginger Decoteau and Christian Sangree enjoy the evening. Photo by Linda Haneborg | okcFRIDAY
By Linda Haneborg
A star-studded evening recently honored our brightest 30 under and 30 plus young leaders.
Now into its third year, 30_30 NextGen awards program recognizes the YUMIs (Young Upwardly Mobile Influencers) who are taking Oklahoma and the world by storm. Mercedes-Benz hosted the past honorees and this year’s nominees along with many friends, well-wishers and “30 wannabes.” Bountiful buffets tempted the crowd while regal Ruth Meyers models strolled around the showroom.
A short program brought interesting news and opportunities to the audience: Cecil Sims introduced the new Mercedes CLA model (which will be coming to Oklahoma City in October). Cindy Shelby owner of Ruth Meyers described the latest fashion trends and invited everyone to visit her beautiful Nichols Hills Plaza store and Tony Foss, floral designer, provided and gave away four gorgeous arrangements.
Kylie Ethridge and Angie Johnson invited all to join OKYPL and attend the Lt Governor’s Young Professionals Conference Oct. 17 at the Cox Center. Several lucky young leaders went home with gift giveaways from the presenting sponsors Mercedes, Ruth Meyers and Tony Foss.
A special thank you to Tom Davis and Tiffany Haunschild for planning and executing such a lovely evening.
Seen enjoying the fun festivities were Tom Parrish, Susan Coles, Dave Amis, Ginger Decoteau, Ashlie Cornelius, Chan Maddox, Vince Orza, Don Swift, Marsha Swift, Mike Shelby, Brooke Haneborg, Chris Watkins, Evan Vincent, Brian Winslow, Jeremy Gossett, Gray Fredrickson, Katie McDowell, Anna-Marie Costello, Abigail Ogle, Kyle Impson, Brent Skarkey, Bose Akadiri, Christopher Lloyd, Lacey Lett, Michael Turner, Macie Craven-Tims, Blake Jackson, Tina Layman, Kyle Young, Darian Woolbright, Emily Rothrock, Kindt Meyers and Katie McDonnell.
