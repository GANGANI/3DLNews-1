Milligan named McGuinness Alumni of the Year
An accountant, lawyer, father, community volunteer and baseball and softball coach, Michael Milligan wears many hats. But, the newest feather in his cap is the title of the 2005 Alumni of the Year, an honor presented by the Bishop McGuin-ness Catholic High School Alumni Association.
The award will be presented at graduation ceremonies on May 16.
A 1973 graduate of Bishop McGuinness Catholic High School, Mike was active in many aspects of the McGuinness community. He was the class parliamentarian, a Junior Rotarian and was an active member of the baseball and basketball teams, the Spanish Honor Society and the Honor Society.
He and his wife, Patricia, have five children; Beth, Katie, Michael, Bridget and Stephanie. He enjoys golf, swimming and coaching.
Mike earned his Accounting degree from the University of Notre Dame in 1977 and attended law school at the University of Oklahoma. He was admitted to the Oklahoma Bar in 1980. He currently works for Milanco, LLC as a manager.
He has earned numerous awards from organizations such as McGuinness, Notre Dame and associated Catholic Charities. He also won the �Pro Ecclesia de Pontifice� award from Archbishop Beltran. This award is given to men and women in recognition for their service to the Catholic Church.
Mike gives back to the community by volunteering for several different organizations and clubs around the Metro area. He is a member of the St. Francis Parish Knights of Columbus, The Notre Dame Club of Oklahoma City and Catholic Charities. He also serves on the board for Catholic Charities, St. Ann Nursing Home, St. Anthony Hospital Foundation, SSM of Oklahoma and the Archdiocesan Finance Council.
Mike is the freshmen baseball coach for McGuinness and the basketball and softball coach for Rosary School.
