Classen SAS student meets with state leader
Andrew Middleton, a senior at Classen School of Advanced Studies, was able to have lunch with Oklahoma Secretary of State Susan Savage last week.The meeting was part of the YMCA Youth and Government State Conference at the Capitol. Middleton is the Youth Secretary of State for YMCA.As part of the program, students assumed the roles of senator, representative, or press during the conference. The model youth legislature met in a two-day legislative session, presided over by their own student-elected statewide officials, including governor, lieutenant governor, speaker of the house, senate president pro tempore, secretary of state, attorney general, chief justice of the Oklahoma Supreme Court, presiding judge of the state court of appeals and state newspaper editor.“We aim to provide a meaningful experience for young people through hands-on learning in a mock legislature that will hopefully inspire them to become active and concerned participants in government,” said Stan Barton, YMCA Youth and Government Program State Director.

'The Wiz' takes the stage at Classen
The Classen School of Advanced Studies theatre department, in conjunction with the vocal and dance departments, produce a major musical every year as their crowning main stage performance. Past choices have included the love-rock hit Hair and the ever-popular Annie. This year’s pick has been on the calendar since last spring, when auditions were held, and promises to be as popular at Classen as the original was on Broadway. The Wiz, first produced in 1975, features music and lyrics by Charlie Smalls and is an adaptation of L. Frank Baum’s children’s novel The Wonderful Wizard of Oz. The original cast included Stephanie Mills as Dorothy and Dee Dee Bridgewater as Glinda the Good Witch, but the younger generations may know it best by the 1978 film adaptation, which featured Diana Ross (Dorothy) and Michael Jackson (Scarecrow). The stage musical ran for 1,672 performances following its debut on Broadway and has since become known as a Black Pride-era classic. Classen’s production opened on Wednesday and will run through Saturday the 10th. All shows begin at 7 p.m. and will cost $15 for adults, with discounts given to students and children. The musical stars Andrea Coleman (Dorothy), Elvie Ellis (Scarecrow), James Foster (Tinman), Jordan Williamson (Lion), Kaleb M. Bruza (The Wiz), and Claire Deveney (Evillene).

Five given Clara Luper Scholarships
The Clara Luper Scholarship, gifted through a fund at Oklahoma City University, is for freshmen entrants who have shown commitment to education despite socioeconomic hardship. “We are Oklahoma City’s university,” reads the Clara Luper Scholarship Fund website, “and want our student population to reflect the community we serve.” Each year, thirty students are selected for the scholarship, which provides for all tuition, fees, room, and board not covered by other University scholarships, as well as a two-hundred dollar book allowance per semester. The application process includes both an essay and an interviewThis year, five of the thirty annual scholarships have been offered to students in the current senior class at Classen School of Advanced Studies. Alonzo James, Patra Kositchaiwat, Laura Murray, Thanh Nguyen, and Shayla Wright all received responses in the last days of March. “I was crying I was so happy,” says Murray, who plans to pursue a career in visual art, “and my mom and dad cried too.” Nguyen, on the other hand, admits that she’s both “speechless” and “grateful”, and that the scholarship will allow her to study at OCU, a private university, rather than at one of Oklahoma’s state schools. Kositchaiwat, on the other hand, wanted to give advice to other students who may be struggling financially and are concerned with rising college costs: “Stay in school and work hard,” she suggests, “but, at the same time, have fun!” Kositchaiwat, who is invested in community service through organizations at Classen, including Key Club International and National Honor Society, knows how important it is to stay grounded, and says that believing in yourself is crucial: “if you know you can achieve it, go for it!”The highly-sought scholarship was established in honor of Clara Luper, an Oklahoman who championed the cause for equal rights in Oklahoma. It stands for the same principles that Luper advocated—equal opportunity in society and an individual’s commitment to personal values. In her book, Behold the Walls, Luper mentions her private struggle: “I know that I have made mistakes and I have learned from them,” writes Luper, “I have been knocked down and while on my back I knew; if I could look up, I could get up.” This philosophy captures the ideal of an OCU Clara Luper Scholarship winner.
