Longtime KTUL news anchor Carole Lambert to leave

  • RITA SHERROW World Television Editor
  • Jun 11, 2011
  • Jun 11, 2011
  • 0
  • Facebook
  • Twitter
  • WhatsApp
  • SMS
  • Email
  • Print
  • Save
RITA SHERROW World Television Editor

Carole Lambert, longtime news anchor at KTUL, channel 8, confirmed on air Friday she will be leaving the station next month.

Lambert, a primetime anchor with channel 8 since 1982, was emotional as she confirmed co-anchor Mark Bradshaw's announcement Friday she will leave the station in late July.

"Very, very difficult decision to leave," she told Bradshaw and chief meteorologist Frank Mitchell, who were flanking her at the anchor desk. "It's very bittersweet if you want to say that. You all have been my family. ... It's been a wonderful place to be.

"Actually I made the decision to move to Springfield, Mo.," she said tearfully, "(because) as many of you know that is where I am from. I went to high school and college there and my sisters and other relatives are there, and I am already pursuing some new opportunities in broadcasting. So it's not that I am retiring or anything. I'm just getting some new opportunities in a different town."

She told the Tulsa World she is very close to her two sisters and other family members in Springfield and wants the opportunity to "hang out" with them.

"I'm lucky that I live within three hours of Springfield so I have been able to go home a lot, but I'm getting tired of driving up there in a thunderstorm and then at Christmas driving back in a snowstorm. I have to keep up with the weather just to visit."

"My bottom line is if channel 8 could physically move to Springfield, you would have to kick me out of this place. But I can't be in two places at one time."

Lambert's decision, was a "surprise and we hate to see her leave," Pat Baldwin, the station's president and general manager, said Friday in a press release.

"Carole will not only be missed at the station, she will be missed by countless viewers who have grown to love her each night as she delivers Tulsa's news of the day."

A graduate of Southern Methodist University with a masters in mass communications, Lambert anchors the 4 p.m., 6 p.m. and 10 p.m. weekday newscasts for the ABC-affiliated station.

In 2009, she was inducted into the prestigious Oklahoma Association of Broadcaster's Hall of Fame and into the Silver Circle Society by the Heartland Chapter of the National Academy of Television Arts & Sciences. She has also received the Best of Broadcasting award from the American Women in Radio and Television, and the station's 10 p.m. newscast has won two Emmys for best daily newscast.

Lambert also earned a bachelor's degree in music from Drury University and a master's degree from the University of Illinois.

Among her many professional accomplishments is hosting and producing the "Waiting Child" segments for KTUL since 1990. The news segments featured more than 2,500 children waiting for adoption.
