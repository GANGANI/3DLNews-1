McGuinness grad earns spot in U.S. Navy SEALs
ZACK MORTON is congratulated for completing Navy SEALs School.
By GORDON WALKER
OKC FRIDAY
One of America�s biggest, baddest fighting men is one of the nicest guys ever to come out of Bishop McGuinness High School.
Zack Morton, senior class president of McGuinness� Class of 1998, graduated this spring from U.S. Navy SEALs School, the elite and highly trained Naval Commando force.
Morton, son of McGuinness principal David Morton and his wife Nancy, survived the rigorous training process that weeds out 90 percent
of those who qualify. Morton was one of only 11 who walked through graduation with SEALs Class 248 at the Special Warfare Command in San Diego, Calif., on April 30. The class started with 120.
�To make it all the way through and have the tenacity to stick with it is just an incredible story,� said U.S. Navy RADM Greg Slavonic, a Morton family friend who began helping Morton pursue the SEALs when Zack was in seventh grade.
Morton was accepted for the SEALs training in the spring of 2003 and soon began the 30-week training with Class 246. But just four weeks into the session, doctors forced him out due to pneumonia. The Navy invited him to resume training with Class 247, but due to the pneumonia, physicians would not release him.
By the time Class 248 kicked off training later that year, Morton was released to continue with the grind -- 2-mile swims through some of the choppiest waters of the Pacific and other grueling exercise via land or sea.
The graduation ceremonies in San Diego drew more than a dozen friends and family from Oklahoma City, including Greg Slavonic and wife, Molly; high school friend Casey Ashmore and his parents, Arlene and Dr. Glenn Ashmore; as well as David, Nancy and Melissa Morton.
Morton is now completing a three-week session at the SEALs jump school at Ft. Binning, Ga. He will then return to Oklahoma City as part of a 30-day leave where there will be a formal church ceremony to celebrate his marriage. Lisa and Zack Morton were married July 3, 2003, in a private ceremony.
From there, Morton will hit Phase III of the process, heading back to San Diego for four months of Seal Qualification Training. At the conclusion, he will then receive the Trident, the symbol worn on his service uniform that designates his status as a Navy SEAL. The SQT includes training in various climates, from the desert to Alaska.
He has been assigned to SEAL Team 1 in San Diego, where his wife, Lisa, works as a school teacher. Zack and Lisa were childhood friends and then became reacquainted while attending Oklahoma State University. After a year at Virginia Military Institute, Morton completed his bachelors degree at OSU, earning a degree in History. He then enlisted in the Navy to qualify as a SEALs candidate. At McGuinness, Morton was a National Merit Scholar, was 1998 Spring King, and was captain of the football team which finished the regular season undefeated.
