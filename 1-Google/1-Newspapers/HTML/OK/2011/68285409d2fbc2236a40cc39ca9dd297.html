Rodeo a real classic in Pauls Valley

  • Barry Porterfield
    Pauls Valley Democrat
  • Jun 19, 2011
  • Jun 19, 2011

The colorful streamers and banners, the sight of animals kicking up the dirt and the anticipation of that very moment before the gate opens are all again part of an American classic right here in Pauls Valley — next week’s Heritage Days Rodeo.

The 66th annual rodeo is set for 8 p.m. starts nightly Thursday through Saturday, June 23-25 at the Pauls Valley Round-Up Club Arena in Wacker Park.

Yet another classic sight is the rodeo parade, which this year is scheduled to begin at 5 p.m. that Saturday night in downtown PV.

Again leading the way are members of the PV Round-Up Club, who have been hard at  work in recent days and weeks preparing for the International Professional Rodeo Association (IPRA) event this coming weekend.

Dessie Daugherty says all the hard work is worth it because of what the rodeo represents for so many people.

“We strive to keep the Oklahoma heritage going and rodeo is the American spirit,” Daugherty said.

“Skills displayed in the rodeo represent the early west and the settling of America,” she said, referring to things like riding and roping.

“And it’s great family entertainment. Prices are reasonable, and you can bring the whole family. It’s good clean family entertainment.”

Daugherty is quick to credit fellow Round-Up Club members Jerry and Lili Norton for their work and longtime organization of the local rodeo in recent years helping it grow in a big way.

“They have built it to what it is today,” she said about the Nortons.

“They have done an awesome job. I can’t sing their praises enough.”

The rodeo here is also personal to Daugherty since her father, Ralph McGee, was the first president of the local roundup club back in 1945, while her mother, Lois, was once a club queen.

“I’ve been around this my whole life,” she said. “It’s kind of a family thing for me.”

One club board member, Harold Scoggins of Maysville, also gets praise from Dessie for all the work he’s done on the all-important dirt in the rodeo arena itself.

“To hear from the barrel racers they say it’s the best dirt. We usually have it in tip top shape and that’s thanks to Harold,” she said.

Each night of the rodeo gets started with live entertainment at 7:15 p.m.

That’s followed by an action-packed list of events, such as bareback riding, saddle bronc, team and calf roping, bulldogging better known by some as steer wrestling, barrel racing, bull riding, a calf scramble and the mutton bustin’ winners from a buckle series leading up to the rodeo.

There will also be nightly appearances by Miss Rodeo USA Katie Barger.

This year’s candidates for Heritage Days Rodeo queen are Dee O’Connor and Brittany Penner.

The winner will be determined by their ticket sales and a horsemanship and personality contest to see who will take the crown as the next queen.

Tickets for the event are $8 for adults and $4 for children and available at the rodeo gate. There is a senior citizens’ discount on Thursday.
