Green ideas will color Nichols Hills

Hal McKnight, left, and Kathy Walker are part of the new chapter Planet Nichols Hills, which will host an Earthday celebration this month.
By Ryan Piersol
OKC Friday
The community in and around Nichols Hills is about to turn green.
And, if a local organization has its way, it will remain green for a long time to come.
Planet Nichols Hills is the newest chapter of the Oklahoma Sustainability Network. It will hold its first ever meeting as a kickoff to the third annual Earthday Birthday celebration in Nichols Hills on April 22-24.
Planet Nichols Hills is hosting the Earthday Birthday celebration for the first time, in connection with the Nichols Hills Environment, Health & Sustainability Commission, Nichols Hills News, the deadCENTER Film Festival and Chesapeake Energy. More than 20 events are scheduled during the three-day celebration.
“There’s something for everybody,” said Kathy Walker,
president of Planet Nichols Hills. “It’s just a comprehensive look. We’re addressing energy like natural gas and alternative forms, and there are experts from all four major universities to talk about sustainability at our forums.”
The first scheduled event is the season opening of the Chesapeake Farmers Market at 3 p.m. April 21. The celebration will officially begin at noon April 22 with the first members meeting for Planet Nichols Hills.
Anyone interested in joining the group is encouraged to attend that meeting. Founding member fees are $100 and member fees are $25. Aside from Walker as president, John Kennedy already has been appointed vice president and Hal McKnight, Helen Sullivan and Sue Spradling are officers with the organization.
Before a tour of Chesapeake the afternoon of April 23, the day will begin with a presentation called “A Case for Community Gardens” at 9:30 a.m. Walker said tips will be supplied for community gardens that relate to both schools and churches.
“School-related gardens can be used for teaching purposes and for eating purposes. Church-related gardens can be used for feeding the hungry,” she said. “It’s a multi-pronged situation.”
The highlights of the final day on April 24 are a Green Car Parade at noon and the Sustainable Living Eco Expo from 11 a.m. to 6 p.m. The parade will begin at the former First Church of Christ Scientist, 1203 Sherwood Lane.
A series of educational forums will be presented from 1-5:30 p.m. Speakers for the educational forums include Bruce Edwards, Director of Urban Harvest, and Kamala Gamble, advocate for S.L.O.W. Food of Oklahoma City.
There also are a variety of social events scheduled, includes a series of films supplied by the deadCENTER Film Festival and a community picnic on the final evening. Organizers also have been working with local restaurants to offer “Earthday Specials.”
“This is our future, whether we like it or not,” Walker said. “We can make an impact in our community, and by providing these panels, it’s a good way for people to ask questions and find out more. It’s just an educational experience for all of us.”
