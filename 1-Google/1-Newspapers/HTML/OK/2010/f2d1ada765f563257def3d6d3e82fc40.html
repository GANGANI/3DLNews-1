Massive hail storm demolishes windows, cars; heavy damage in NHills, Quail Creek

This street just north of The Village filled quickly with large hail, following a massive storm on May 16. In the corner, a resident holds a handful of golfball-sized hail.
By Ryan Piersol
OKC Friday
Residents of northwest Oklahoma City, Nichols Hills and The Village are still cleaning up the mess left behind by a storm that rained down hail as large as baseballs on Sunday.
In the aftermath, both the city of Nichols Hills and The Village have supplied residents with important reminders in regard to clean-up and repair.
The storm began on the northwest side of Oklahoma City and made a clean path through all of Fridayland, knocking out stop lights, breaking windows, destroying roofs of houses, battering vehicles and leaving behind a messy amount of debris.
Damage sustained to trees and greens forced both the Oklahoma City Golf & Country Club and the Quail Creek Country Club to shut down their courses for several days. Both golf courses were expected to reopen on Thursday.
Officials with Nichols Hills and The Village want to make sure all residents make wise decisions during the repair stage in the upcoming weeks. Nichols Hills sent out a crime alert, warning residents that they could be victims of fraud if they fail to go with a reputable vendor found in the telephone directory for home or vehicle repair. The alert said that it is well known that scam artists and con men have been known to flock to areas affected by natural disasters.
The Village reminded residents in an e-news alert to never pay in advance for work, that a building permit is required for roof replacement and that contractors must have liability insurance to obtain the permit. City crews already have begun picking up storm debris along curbs and will continue to do so in the coming weeks. Residents are encouraged to bag leaves, leave normal trash separate from limbs and place all debris at the curb.
