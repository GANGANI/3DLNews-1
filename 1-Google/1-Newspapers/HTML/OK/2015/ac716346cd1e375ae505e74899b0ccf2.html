Rose Lane is promoted to OKC FRIDAY editor

Rose Lane
Vicki Clark Gourley, Chairman and CEO of Nichols Hills Publishing Company, announced this week that long-time General Manager of OKC FRIDAY, Rose Lane, will become the Editor of the northwest Oklahoma City community newspaper.
Lane began her tenure at OKC FRIDAY, 13 years ago, when she joined the staff as News Editor. She was promoted to General Manager when Gordorn Walker purchased Night Owl Publications and left the position.

Prior to that, she had a 15-year career with Lake Media, based in Camdenton, Mo., where she served in many positions, including Associate Editor of a daily newspaper, Editor of two different weekly newspapers and Sales Executive. Lane’s husband then took a job in Oklahoma.
The Oklahoma Journalism Hall of Fame selected Lane as a 2015 inductee this past April.
Vicki Clark Gourley succeeded her husband, and OKC FRIDAY founder, J. Leland Gourley as Editor after his death in October 2013.
“Rose has been running day-to-day operations of the paper for the past few months,” Gourley said. “She is an award-winning writer/editor and she deserves this promotion as the next step in her career.”
Gourley said she is not retiring, but wants more time to pursue her civic and charitable interests.
“With Leland and I both at the newspaper, we each devoted time to various organizations that we individually had a passion for its work.  Over the years, between the two of us, I believe we served on almost every major civic and arts board. He was especially proud of his presidency of Rotary Club 29 and membership on the OKC Chamber Board. I served as president of the Community Literacy Center’s board and loved being on the Zoo Friends board. I had just become President of Sister Cities OKC’s international board when Leland died,” Gourley said.
“Trying to do both of our jobs meant I had less time to devote to organizations I believe are making a valuable contribution to our community. Rose becoming Editor of OKC FRIDAY means I will have more time for my volunteer work, something I am quite excited about doing,” Gourley said. “Having to put both my business and my life back together after 40 years of working with Leland and 38 years of marriage was tough. This will help me get there.”
Lane has covered city governments, the schools, non-profits and the social scene, since coming to work at OKC FRIDAY.
“Rose knows ‘Fridayland’ like a native, and I have absolute confidence that the newspaper will only get better with her serving as the editor,” Gourley said.
Lane is married to Bob Lane. They have two children. Bobby is married and lives in Lacey, Wash. Her daughter Carrie  is a sophomore at John Brown University, Siloam Springs, Ark.
