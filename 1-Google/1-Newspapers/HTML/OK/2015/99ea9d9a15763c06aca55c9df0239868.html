We need faith, not black-eyed peas

Laurel Hall
Most of us heard very familiar scriptures in church on Christmas Eve. The thing about familiar scriptures is that they can sometimes lose their punch.
The Reluctant Preacher decided to revisit and study one such scripture from Isaiah 7:14 -- “Look! The virgin will conceive a child! She will give birth to a son and will call him ‘Immanuel’ (which means God is with us).”
This prophecy is familiar to us, but the story in which the prophecy takes place is fascinating and gives us a treasure trove of useful application for the coming 2015.
Starting at the beginning of Isaiah chapter 7, the story begins with the king of Judah, Ahaz, fearing an imminent invasion from neighboring Israel and Syria. During this time in history, God’s people had divided into a northern kingdom and a southern kingdom. Ahaz was the king of Judah. He was a descendant of David and Jerusalem was Judah’s capital.
In the story, Isaiah the prophet tells Ahaz flatly that the invasion he fears will not take place. God calls the rulers of Israel and Syria “burned out embers” and says that these nations are only as strong as their leaders…which is not strong at all. Then God says in verse 9, “Unless your faith is firm I cannot make you stand firm.”
Friends, if we want 2015 to be a year during which God is working on our behalf, black-eyed peas and cabbage aren’t going to cut it. God can only work for us in proportion to our faith in Him.
The story continues with the Lord asking Ahaz to test Him: “Ask the Lord your God for a sign of confirmation, Ahaz. Make it as difficult as you want…” and Ahaz refuses. What? Refuses the Lord? This doesn’t make sense to us until we realize that Ahaz is a man who knows his scriptures, and he is following the law of Moses from Deuteronomy 6:16 “You must not test the Lord your God as you did at Massah.” So, following the crumbs, we find that Massah is a place described in Exodus 17 where Moses responds to the grumbling of the thirsty, wandering Israelites by miraculously striking a rock and bringing forth water. In Exodus 17:7 the scriptures say, “Moses named the place Massah (which means ‘test’) and Meribah (which means ‘arguing’) because the people of Israel argued with Moses and tested the Lord by saying, ‘Is the Lord here with us or not?’”
Ouch. Isn’t that the question we all ask ourselves too often? Don’t we doubt He will show up? Ahaz responded as any good man of the scriptures was expected to respond. The problem is that his response is one out of rote and heartless obedience to scripture and not out of discerning love of the Lord, his God. God himself asked for the chance to prove his presence to Ahaz, and Ahaz was so calcified in tradition that he could not discern the Lord’s intentions. How often might we fall into this same category?
Isaiah becomes so frustrated with Ahaz that he proclaims that the sign of God’s presence will be -- you guessed it -- that the virgin will conceive a child ...
Let’s contrast Ahaz’s situation to Jesus’s in the wilderness. They both respond using the Deuteronomy scripture in the face of the temptation to test the Lord…but Ahaz’s response was wrong, and Jesus’s was right. When Jesus, as described in Matthew 4, is “led into the wilderness to be tempted by the devil” he is met by an adversary that is in other places in scripture described as “the ruler of this world” or “the prince of this world” and who also knows his scriptures.
The reason that Ahaz’s response was wrong and Jesus’s response was right was because Ahaz was being courted by the Lord while Jesus was being tempted by the devil. The devil tempts in the same way he always has and always will …with the trappings of this world. We must learn that knowing scriptures is not enough to live an authentic life of faith.
Ahaz knew his scriptures, the devil knows his scriptures and Jesus continually asks his followers, “Don’t you know the scriptures?”
The reason we must know our scriptures is not for the benefit of knowing them so that we can follow the rules -- it’s for the benefit of knowing God. Knowing God is not possible without knowing scriptures, but knowing scriptures can create a false sense of obedience when we fail to know God himself. The good news is that God himself was born of a virgin, lived among us, died, was raised again, and lives forevermore.
May 2015 be a year in which we know Him like we never have before.
