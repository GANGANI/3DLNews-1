Berry - Todd
   Miss Alina Michelle Todd, of Enid, and Lt. Jon Nichols Berry, of Oklahoma City, were married at 7:00 p.m. June 17th in the Chapel of Saint Edward the Confessor at Casady School. Reverend Stuart Malloy and Reverend John Marlin officiated. After the ceremony guests gathered on the chapel lawn as the couple exited, and was honored under the traditional military saber arch. An outdoor reception followed on the lawn by the lake at Casady School.
   The bride is the daughter of Mrs. Karla Patocka, of Enid.
   The groom is the son of Mr. and Mrs. Nichols B. Berry of Oklahoma City. He is the grandson of Mrs. William A. Berry and the late Judge William A. Berry; and the late Mr. and Mrs. Walter Bennett Stallings.
   Her brother, Trey Patocka, gave the bride in marriage. Carrying a bouquet of Calla lilies, the bride wore white satin strapless A-line gown featuring an organza overlay, and a band of seed pearls across the top of the gown, with organza envelope edge trimming. The bridal ensemble was completed with a crystal and seed pearl headband, and a fingertip silk illusion veil with scattered seed pearls.
   Mr. Ryan Kelly of Springfield, Illinois, served as man of honor. The bride’s other attendants were Miss Jessica Carson and Miss Sarah Fletcher.
   Lieutenant Kha Nguyen was the best man. The groom’s other attendants were Mr. W. Bennett Berry and Mr. Morgan Berry. Saber bearers for the formal military saber arch were Lt. Kha Nguyen, Lt. Elizabeth Bare, Lt. Chandler Sims, Col. Russell Reck, Cadet Joshua Grubbs and Lt. Joseph Coleman.
   The bride attended Enid High School and graduated Westminster College in Fulton, Missouri in May with a Bachelors of Arts in Political Science and History.
   The groom attended Casady School, and is a graduate of the United States Military Academy at West Point.  He received a Bachelors of Science degree in Civil Engineering, and was commissioned as a Second Lieutenant on May 28. 
   After a trip to the British Virgin Islands, the couple is at home in West Point, New York where Lt. Berry will be working in the Office of Protocol at the Academy.
