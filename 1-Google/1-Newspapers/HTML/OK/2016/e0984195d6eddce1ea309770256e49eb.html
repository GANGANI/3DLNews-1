Central OK Habitat for Humanity honors volunteers

Carolyn Roberts, Central Oklahoma Habitat Board member and volunteer; Bill Shanahan, Linn Energy Manager of Midstream; Ann Felton Gilliland, Central Oklahoma Habitat CEO and chairman.
Central Oklahoma Habitat for Humanity (COHFH) celebrated its volunteers, donors and partners at its 23rd annual Volunteer Appreciation Dinner at the Embassy Suites Downtown/Medical Center in Oklahoma City.
Nearly 500 people attended the event emceed by News 9’s Robin Marsh.
Central Oklahoma Habitat CEO and Chairman Ann Gilliland said everyone in attendance helps make a difference in the lives of limited-income Oklahoma Families, and the community.
“Central Oklahoma Habitat for Humanity would not be able to move forward with its mission to eliminate poverty housing without the contributions of time, money and talents of our donors and volunteers,” said Gilliland.
Linn Energy Manager of Midstream Bill Shanahan accepted the 2016 Inspiration Award.
“My absolute favorite day is the dedication (of the home) because on that day cycles are broken, lives are changed and it’s just awesome to see God blesses those guys,” Shanahan said. “I’ve been fortunate enough to be involved with 14 home builds with volunteer groups. I just couldn’t find a better place to put my time or my money than with Central Oklahoma Habitat.”
COHFH has completed a total of 867 homes to date. Another 10 homes are set to be dedicated to families by year’s end, which will bring the total home builds in 2016 to 45. In addition, COHFH has helped 42 existing limited-income Oklahoma homeowners, including five veterans, this year through their Critical Home Repair program.
“Since 2008 the Critical Home Repair team has made necessary repairs and improvements to 364 homes that allowed the homeowners to continue their insurance coverage, lower their utility bills, and improve much-needed access and better mobility once inside their homes,” said Gilliland.
For more information on COHFH’s ongoing efforts in the community, or to volunteer or donate, visit: cohfh.org.
