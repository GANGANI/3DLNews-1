At 84, Speligene sleeps at the office

Rose Lane
In the same office: Chris Speligene, general manager with founder and President Fred Jones (1967), left; as president and CEO (1985), center, and in Room 209, 21C Museum Hotel (2016), right.
Chris Speligene, retired president of Fred Jones Mfg. Co., slept in his old office on his 84th birthday.
Speligene celebrated his 84th birthday in his former workplace, the 21C Museum Hotel at 900 W. Main St. The return was particularly meaningful as Speligene was present during the purchase of the building in 1968 from Ford Motor Co. to consolidate remanufacturing operations from nine buildings into one.
Amazingly, founder Fred Jones worked in the building in 1916 when it was a Model-T assembly plant.
Speligene began his career with Ford Motor Co. in 1956 in the corporate customer service department, then joined Fred Jones Mfg. Co. in 1966 as a district manager in New Orleans, La.
In 1967 he became general manager in Oklahoma City, with added responsibilities as President and CEO from 1973-1988. He served on the board for the next 10 years.

Chris Speligene, second from right, with son Jeff, wife Myrna, son Brian and two of three daughters Stephanie and Cristina at the 21C Museum Hotel, formerly Fred Jones Manufacturing Co.

Speligene observed his birthday with extended family at Mary Eddy’s Kitchen in the lobby of the hotel. Then, he and his wife, Myrna, spent the night in Room 209, a king suite which is the site of his old office.
His daughter Cristina Speligene said he could see the seam in the ceiling where his bathroom used to be.
“It was so meaningful because of his history with the building and Mr. Jones,” she said. “It was really fun for him to see the transformation of the building.”
Speligene and his wife, Myrna, residents of Guthrie, have five children, 12 grandchildren and nine great-grandchildren.
