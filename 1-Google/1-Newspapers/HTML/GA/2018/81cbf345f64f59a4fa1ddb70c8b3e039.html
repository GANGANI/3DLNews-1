Behavioral Health open house showcases new programs

By Eve Copeland eve.guevara@galfnews.com
May 13, 2018

TIFTON, Ga. — Behavioral Health Services of South Georgia Tifton clinic held an open house Thursday.

The open house was in recognition of mental health awareness and recovery month and showcased new programs at the center.

Michele Davis, family violence intervention provider, said she was very proud that the center offers her program since it is difficult to get certified to provide this service.

“The Family Violence Intervention Program is a 24 week state-certified, court approved program through the Georgia Commission on Family Violence,” she said. “It offers educational programs for offenders of domestic violence. We currently have classes in Valdosta, Tifton and Adel and are looking to expand into other counties.”

She said they are one of the few certified programs of the kind in the area and that the success rate of these type of programs is high.

Davis said that approximately 85 percent of the offenders who go through the program do not reoffend.

“There is a huge need for programs like this,” she said. “This rehabilitates offenders and cuts down on the amount of family violence.”

Additionally, they offer anger management and shoplifting and theft prevention classes for offenders.

Diane Brunston, one of the therapists at the center, spoke about the Youth and Emerging Adults Succeed (YES) program, which helps teenagers and young adults with suspected or diagnosed severe mental disorders or behavioral and emotional challanges.

“It’s specifically for 16 to 26 year olds to bridge that gap between being a kid and being an adult,” she said. “There is just so much that goes on (for that age group). The program helps wherever the individual needs it.”

The program assists with housing, job hunting, transportation and budgeting, but it also helps those who look at the world differently due to their mental disorder.

“This program is for people who somehow the way they are approaching life is not working,” Brunston said. “They need some direction or some help. They need help learning to manage life with these diagnoses.”

Brunston and Davis agree that a person who is not court mandated to attend a class or program can still utilize the clinic’s services.

“If you feel you need help, we’re here for you,” Brunston said. “We’re here to help.”

The Tifton clinic has been serving Tift, Irwin, Ben Hill and Turner counties for more than 20 years, and is part of Behavioral Services of South Georgia, which also operates clinics in Lowndes and Cook counties.

Behavioral Health Services of South Georgia is a Community Service Board and provides a range of services to children, adults and families with substance use and mental illness disorders and individuals with developmental disabilities. These services are contracted through the Georgia Department of Behavioral Health and Developmental Disabilities.

It’s located at 334 Tifton Eldorado Road and the contact number is 229-391-2300. For immediate assistance (24/7) with behavioral health, developmental disability or an addictive disease issue, contact the Georgia Crisis Access Line at 1-800-715-4225.
