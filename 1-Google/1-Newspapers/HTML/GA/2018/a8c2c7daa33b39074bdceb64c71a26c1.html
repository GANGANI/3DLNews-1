Law enforcement agencies announce joint crackdown on crime

Local, federal agencies conduct joint operation to reduce crime in Albany

  • By Gypsy Crow

    gypsy.crow@albanyherald.com

    Gypsy Crow

  • Apr 20, 2018
  • Apr 20, 2018
  • Facebook
  • Twitter
  • WhatsApp
  • SMS
  • Email
  • Facebook
  • Twitter
  • WhatsApp
  • SMS
  • Email

ALBANY — Representatives of local and federal law enforcement agencies gathered at the downtown Albany Law Enforcement Center to announce their joint operation to reduce violent crime in Albany. The agencies have been meeting throughout the week, resulting in more than 50 arrests of violent drug and gun offenders, as well as offenders with outstanding warrants.

Among the agencies present were the Albany Police Department, the U.S. Marshal’s Service, the Dougherty County Sheriff’s Office, the Dougherty County Police Department, and the Department of Community Supervision and Homeland Security. Charles Peeler, the U.S. Attorney for the Middle District of Georgia, has also been a part of the operation.
