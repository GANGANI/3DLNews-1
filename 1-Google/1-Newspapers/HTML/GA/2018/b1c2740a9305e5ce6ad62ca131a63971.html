5 things: Quick takes on things you might have missed

  • Kaley Lefevre | News Editor
  • Jun 8, 2018
  • Jun 8, 2018 Updated Jun 10, 2018

A lot goes on around Athens, and sometimes it can be hard to keep up. From the Five & Ten hiring a new chef de cuisine, to a sinkhole opening up in the Phi Kappa Tau parking lot, The Red & Black complied five of the top stories not to miss this week. 

 

1. Five & Ten hired new chef de cuisine 

Chef and owner of Five & Ten Hugh Acheson has appointed Telly Mazzio as the new chef de cuisine of Five & Ten. Mazzio will be bringing traditional Italian and French techniques and his open interpretation of Southern cuisine to the table to compliment chef Acheson’s French and Italian influence. Acheson praises Mazzio for blending the old with the new and sees this innovation seeping into their cuisines.

2. Sinkhole formed in Phi Kappa Tau parking lot

A sinkhole opened up in the parking lot of Phi Kappa Tau fraternity last month from unknown causes. Signs and yellow caution tape surround the hole to keep people from walking too close to it, but due to the increase in rain over the past few weeks the sinkhole has grown. A damaged storm drain system is contributing significantly to the problem. The sinkhole is expected to be filled by the end of July.

3. Residents wore orange for gun violence victims

In honor of National Gun Violence Awareness Day on June 2, Athens residents wore orange to honor the victims and survivors of gun violence. An event was hosted at Bishop Park by Athens Moms Demand Action for Gun Sense in America, Everytown for Gun Safety and Students Demand Action to advocate for gun control. Several newly elected officials including mayor-elect Kelly Girtz came to show their support.

4. Nick Chubb signed rookie deal with Cleveland

Former Georgia running back Nick Chubb signed his rookie contract with the Cleveland Browns on June 4. The four-year deal is worth approximately $7.38 million and includes a signing bonus of $3.44 million, according to NFL.com. After leading the Georgia Bulldogs to a birth in the national championship in January, Chubb was selected by the Browns with the 35th overall pick in April’s NFL Draft.  

5. Georgia baseball players drafted to the Major Leagues

Three Georgia baseball players were selected in the Major League Baseball Draft this week, after the team’s season ended on June 4. Junior pitcher Kevin Smith was the first to be taken when he went to the New York Mets in the seventh round on day two, and the Seattle Mariners drafted senior left fielder Keegan McGovern in the ninth round. The San Diego Padres drafted junior Michael Curry in the 16th round.
