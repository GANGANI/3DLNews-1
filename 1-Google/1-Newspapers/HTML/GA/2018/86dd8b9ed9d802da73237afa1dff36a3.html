Northern Ridge District announces new Eagle Scouts

NORTH FULTON, Ga. — The Northern Ridge Boy Scout District, which includes the cities of Roswell, Alpharetta, Johns Creek and Milton, recently announced its newest Eagle Scouts. These are Scouts who passed their Eagle Board of Reviews, held at Alpharetta Presbyterian Church Board of Review on March 29:

Connor Schoenl, of Troop 629, sponsored by Mt. Pisgah United Methodist Church, whose project was the design and construction of three recycle trash bins for the Chattahoochee Nature Center.

Akaash Para, of Troop 3143 and sponsored by Johns Creek United Methodist Church, who designed and constructed a gardening circle for the Sri Hanuman Temple.

Rohan Edara, of Troop 3143, was sponsored by Johns Creek United Methodist Church. For his project, Edara designed and constructed signs for the Autrey Mill Nature Preserve and Heritage Center walking cell phone tour.

Albert Williams, of Troop 10, sponsored by St. Benedict’s Catholic Church, whose project was the collection and creation of 100 personal hygiene kits for the Ronald MacDonald House.

Sankalp Vadlapudi, of Troop 27, was sponsored by the Johns Creek Christian Church. He designed and created four aquarium covers for the displays at Autrey Mill Nature Preserve and Heritage Center.

Jackson Mills, of Troop 27, was sponsored by the Johns Creek Christian Church. He designed and created two ten-foot sections of a boardwalk for Autrey Mill Nature Preserve and Heritage Center.

Rohan Vuppala, of Troop 2000, sponsored by Johns Creek Presbyterian Church, whose project was the design and construction of a fence for the Butterfly Garden at Autrey Mill Nature Preserve and Heritage Center.
