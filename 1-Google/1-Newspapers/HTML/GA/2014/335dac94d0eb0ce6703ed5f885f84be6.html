$51,000 for Boys & Girls Club

  • Sep 17, 2014
  • Sep 17, 2014 Updated Sep 17, 2014
  • 0

  • Email
  • Facebook

  • Save

The community was asked to show its support for the newly-formed Boys & Girls Clubs of Candler County, and local businesses and individuals rose to the occasion as more than $50,000 was raised during the Day for Boys & Girls Clubs Capital Campaign on Wednesday, Sept. 10.

“The community was absolutely fabulous in their support,” said Dennis Allen, president of the Boys & Girls Club executive board.

“I want to express my heartfelt thanks to the community for their support,” Allen said. “The commitment of this community has been remarkable -- just another example of why Everything’s Better in Metter.”

The day prior to the Capital Campaign, the local community was asked to come out and visit the Club, located in the old Metter Intermediate School building, during a Business After Hours event. That event, Allen said, gave the opportunity to see how much work has gone into preparing to welcome the students, who began arriving this week.

Activities at the Boys & Girls Clubs officially kicked off on Monday when the first students arrived after school.

Currently, the local club can accommodate up to 20 students from each grade level, kindergarten through fifth. And while the response from parents has been overwhelming, there are still some spaces available at each grade level, Allen added.

The program includes art and music opportunities as well as tutoring and recreational activities.

“We want to invite the public to come anytime to see our club,” Allen said. “We are proud and we think the public will be proud of what has been done here and how this program can change lives.”

Allen also reminded the community to look forward to other community-wide projects in the near future.

“There will still be ongoing projects in the years to come and we will need the continued support of this community,” Allen said. “Be on the lookout for fun events we have planned in the future.”

Donors to date

Among the Capital Campaign donors giving $51,325 were 38 donors who contributed at the Foundation Partner level ($1,000 or more). Their names will be included on a plaque at the Boys & Girls Clubs.

Not too late to give

“We are still taking donations for anyone who would like to contribute,” Allen said.

Donations can be made by mail to: Boys and Girls Club of Candler County, P O Box 66, Metter, GA  30439, or dropped off at the Boys and Girls Club at 423 W. Vertia Street (old MIS building).
