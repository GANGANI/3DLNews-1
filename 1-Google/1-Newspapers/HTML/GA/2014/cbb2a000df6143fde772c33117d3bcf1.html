Blood drive to honor Conor Dunn

  • By Angie Thompson/senior reporter
  • Aug 15, 2008
  • Aug 15, 2008 Updated Aug 2, 2014

A blood drive to be held from 3 until 7 p.m. Thursday at Omega Baptist Church is in honor of a young man from the community who appreciates, along with his family, the importance of having enough blood available for people who need it. First United Methodist Church in Tifton is also sponsoring a blood drive from 1 until 6 p.m. Tuesday.

Conor Dunn, 14, was diagnosed with congenital heart defects when he was two weeks old. It wasn’t until February when he contracted a life-threatening bacterial infection that he came close to death and in April, he had open heart surgery.

“If people didn’t give blood, I wouldn’t be here today,” Conor said.

Dunn is the son of Patty and Jim Dunn of Omega. Patty said that she was touched by the outpouring of concern and care given to Conor and his family during the time he was critically ill.

“It was an outpouring that was overwhelming,” Patty Dunn said. “We have a big support system in our families, but so many people in Omega and Tifton came through with so much support.”

Conor lives a rather active life with few physical limitations. He is a freshman at Northeast Campus of Tift County High School, an active member of Omega Baptist Church and enjoys 4-H and writing. He’s working on a book of fiction called “Adventures of Don Dallale.”

“It’s actually coming together,” Conor said.

Conor said he had been working on the book for over four years.

In February, Conor was diagnosed as having a staph infection (bacterial endocarditis) in his heart. His temperature spiked to 105 degrees and he lost 12 pounds in 10 days. When Conor was transported to his cardiologist’s office in Columbus, his blood pressure had dropped and his pulse rate was rising dangerously. He was admitted to the hospital’s intensive care unit. Doctors found bacteria on his heart’s tricuspid valve.

Conor was then airlifted to Egleston’s Children’s Hospital where he spent several days in ICU.

“Once he was stabilized, he went into shock,” Patty Dunn said. “It was a scary time.”

Conor’s infection then spread to his lungs and he developed double pneumonia. He survived the critical illness and was able to return home where he received a six-week course of antibiotic treatments.

“He went back to Atlanta in April to repair a valve and some of the birth defects,” Patty Dunn said.

Dunn said Conor will have to have an aortic valve replaced, but that is years down the road.

Connor, a superior honor student, has political aspirations and said Friday that he has volunteered to work on State Rep. Austin Scott’s re-election campaign. He is enrolled in journalism classes and works on the Northeast Campus’s student newspaper. He said one of his assignments in the class was to write his own obituary. It was interesting, he said, because the students who participated in the assignment “got to write their own futures.” The headline on his was, “Former President Dunn dies during speaking engagement.”

Conor’s father is Jim Dunn and his grandparents are Tommy and Betty Hornbuckle of Omega and Larry Dunn and the late Maggie Dunn of Omega. He has a brother, Sam, and two sisters, Sarah and Caroline.

Summer usually means that people don’t take the time to give blood and the need is critical with only a two-day supply available. People who give blood or platelets from July 1 through Sept. 30 are eligible for prizes to be given away. Regional donors are eligible to win one of three $1,000 gas cards. Those in the county who give could receive either a $250 or $100 gas card or one of three $50 gas cards to be given away.
