Sandfly Women Business Owners collaborate to support Buy Local efforts

By Lou Phelps, Savannah Business Journal

December 8, 2014 – A group of Savannah women business owners with companies in the Sandfly area of the city are working to market their charming neighborhood-feel shopping and restaurant district as a ‘daytrip destination, and held a recent organizational meeting.

The group is capitalizing on the synergy of a “creative community of women business owners,” along with the area’s rich history, according to Mary Dugas who publishes the community blog SandflySavannah.com, and who helped to organize the first event of women owners.

Sandfly abuts some of Savannah’s more affluent neighborhoods, including Isle of Hope, Dutch Island and The Landings, and has enjoyed a strong – and growing - retail and dining base over the past 10 years anchored by a grocery store, gas stations and small shopping center.

Retail offerings vary from luxury brand home goods and apparel, local artisans’ wares and several multi-cultural eateries, and is a “unique local magnet for residents and those visiting the Savannah area,” they believe.

A recent gathering of 25 local businesses owned by women successfully showcased the best of Sandfly’s diverse contributions to the area’s tourism draws, explains Dugas. (See photo.) The group’s first event was styled by Sebrell Smith of Designer Events, and featured displays from a variety of local ventures, including designer clothing and high-end décor, jewelry and handcrafted accessories, artwork, and bath and beauty products. Culinary contributions included fresh baked artisanal breads, seared ahi tuna, a gourmet anti-pasta display, wine and cupcakes, all from local restaurants and shops.

Dugas noted, “It was beyond exciting to see all of the gorgeous products and merchandise from these thriving local businesses, all brought together in one place. Sandfly has such a wealth of options for visitors to explore, and these industrious women are a big reason for that.”

Featuring a mix of retail and home-based businesses, the event in all its splendor was captured by Sandfly’s own Katie Joyner Photography at the home of Summer Home owner Summer Marshall.

“Our first meeting started as a welcoming party for Dr. Canfield when she opened Sandfly Family Dental,” said Marjorie Young, CEO of Carriage Trade Public Relations, “Once we all got together, we knew we had to keep meeting as a group. I am glad these businesses collectively worked to encourage Buy Local efforts as Mary kept the momentum going. Our next meeting is Friday, Jan. 16 at Cutters Point Coffee Shop.”

With an eye toward community and philanthropy, women-owned businesses in the Sandfly area are committed to giving back. Donations to local Savannah causes and partnerships between local products and service providers are planned, ensuring that success for one will promote success for all, according to Dugas.

And, to further demonstrate their dedication to community, 26 businesses in Sandfly will join together this week in a Sandfly Holiday Open House and Toy Drive, to be held Thurs. Dec. 11, 2014 from 4:30 p.m.-8:30 p.m. The public is welcome.

Businesses will provide special discounts for shoppers who donate, while everyone attending will enjoy celebrations, live music, and dinner specials. The collection of toys will benefit 21st Century Community Learning Centers.
