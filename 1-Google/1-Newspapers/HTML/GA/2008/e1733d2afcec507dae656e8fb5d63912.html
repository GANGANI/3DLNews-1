Man With Wagon Makes Statement

By: Hannah Pope

Travelers along Hwy. 16 east of Monticello this week may see a wagon with a sign “Man pulling wagon, pass slowly-Thank You,” and a tent near Murder Creek.

The Monticello News received a number of calls about the man early this week, and decided to investigate.
Others did too, as three cars stopped to inquire and chat while this reporter was visiting. Some stop to investigate, others come back to talk more after meeting Roy Gleiter.

The large wagon full of paraphernalia and the tent belong to Mr. Gleiter. He is a Hurricane Katrina victim traveling “Washington to Washington with you in the middle.” He has been traveling for three years and is headed to Washington State; he says either Spokane or Seattle whichever suits him best.

He began in Mississippi and traveled to Washington D.C. He tells a great tale. He is a well spoken middle aged man who is spreading a message about economy and government and what many say cannot be done.

This man is traveling the entire country and is meeting and talking with anyone and everyone who will listen. The most at a time that Mr. Gleiter has spoken with has been more than 1,000 people in a 12 hour period while camped out at a local convenience store in South Carolina.

He himself is pulling the 5,000 plus pound wagon across the country to prove that it is possible. He states that by the world’s standards this can not be done, like a lot of our country’s issues, however he has proven that it can.
Along with him is a woman who he says is like a mom to him. She is a close family friend and has known Mr. Gleiter for about 25 years. She is mentally ill and has been diagnosed as paranoid schizophrenic and has turrets syndrome, he said. Mr. Gleiter sees her as a bit of a challenge but knows that she doesn’t have anyone else, therefore it is he who cares for her.

Along with them is a dog (husky mix) named Sebastian who is training to be a medical dog for his mom.

Mr. Gleiter said that he put in more than 1,000 job applications after Katrina. He was turned down, though, because he had no permanent address. All he was being told was “get a job, get a job,” but when he applied he saw how the scale was weighed.

Man A has a home, a car, a place to bathe, and a telephone. Man B has no address, no shower, no transportation…no job. He has worked odd and end jobs here and there but has gone with his instincts most.

He says that he worked asphalt in the summer and when the job was over the boss took him by a store and asked him to run in and grab a pack of cigarettes. When Mr. Gleiter returned the man was gone. No pay for the man worth nothing.

Chatting for some two hours, Mr. Gleiter espoused many ideas for our economy that he says has become so passive that we are beginning to see those results. He talked of flat tax, and mentioned that the taxes should be on what we spend more than on what we make each year.

He thinks that outsourcing has come to a peak and that America makes little to nothing of its own anymore. He gave the example of his tent. He said that his tent was a product that was made cheaply so that he has bought 42 tents in the last three years. He says that the American made tents are much more expensive, but produced at a higher quality, and the cheaper ones will not hold up long enough for him to save for a new American made one, resulting in you get what you pay for.

He also talked about how our economy has to get back to its roots. We, as Americans, need to get back to the basics of food, water, shelter, health, and energy, he said. He noticed that our infrastructure has suffered and our land is wasting away when it could be productive. He projected that by 2050 farming would shrink disastrously, and no longer produce enough food for the common man. He noted also that we need to stop enriching our enemies. We are the all powerful, but the fact that we provide opportunities for our enemies in bulk will keep America from being the greatest country in the world.

Mr. Gleiter told ways to improve public safety. He has faced some of law enforcement’s finest as he has traveled so many miles. He says that most of the law officers he has come across have been curious, yet kind upstanding men. He also said that there were the occasional hot heads with a badge, and they tried to get him any way they could.

He has more than 11,450 documented sightings, citations, and warnings by law enforcement. He tells them that he means no harm and he is only passing through. He said that he has been told and accused of everything from vagrancy, loitering, and camping, to resisting arrest and refusing to say anything. He gave the example that he couldn’t camp, so he would move, when he moved he was loitering. Instances like that made it impossible to get anywhere.

Mr. Gleiter compares himself to a Johnny Appleseed come again. He says that all most people see are a crazy, homeless man, and he says that the more people he teaches the more productive he is being. He says his mission is for the individual as well as the masses. He says that if a poor homeless guy can get people to listen to him and to respect him then he has done his part. He says that his message will spread because of those people in hopes of some sort of resolution for the country.

And people are listening and his message is spreading. Mr. Gleiter, a homeless man with minimal access to a computer says he has appeared on more than 11,085 web sites and blogs about his trips. He also has a website started for him www.wheresroynow.com, and it has visitors. So as you drive down Highway 16 East between Monticello and Eatonton take time to notice Roy, and his wagon.

If you have enough time stop by and chat with him.
