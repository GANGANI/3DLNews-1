Residents in movie being filmed in Alpharetta

  • Hatcher Hurd

ALPHARETTA - What could be better than having major Hollywood movie stars make a film in your neighborhood? Why, to actually be in the movie, of course.

That is what has happened to several residents of The Manor in Milton, as well as other area homeowners. Working as walk-ons (extras, as they are called in the movie-biz), they are spending screen time with two of the top stars in Hollywood, albeit in the background.

Much of the movie takes place at The Manor, the exclusive golf and country club community in Milton, so it made sense and good local politics for the producers to meet with the residents. Not only to tell them what to expect, but to give them first shot at working on the film as extras.

“They [the producers] met with our residents, and they did put out a sign-up sheet for those who wanted a chance to be in the movie,” said the Manor’s Director of Membership Patti Swan.

The Manor is the setting for the movie, which is about the perfect suburban family – juuust a little too perfect.

But as Manor resident Lisa Cottick said, all the sign-up sheet got folks was first crack at the casting director. Fortunately for Cottick, she had something they were looking for and was in.

Several of them were waiting for the call to the set in the finished basement of the mansion that serves as the sales office at the gated community on Hopewell Road. Cottick and fellow Manor resident Tracy Robbins were part of a group to be in a party scene and still had their hair in curlers awaiting the hairdresser.

“I had to change my dress three times,” said Robbins. “No, you don’t wear your own clothes. They have an idea what they want the cast to look like.”

The casting call went beyond The Manor, and locals from all around the north metro area “auditioned” and got the call.

Duluth resident Wendy Kidder said she heard about the opportunity by “word of mouth,” and came to the casting.

A lot of the work consists of sitting in the background providing “chatter” at a bar, restaurant or a party.

“They don’t record anything we say, that would be dialogue and they would have to pay us more [it’s a union business]. So if they want something particular said, they dub it in later,” Kidder said.

It was the party scene they were all dressed for and expected some 40 extras to participate. Often an extra’s work is just a day for one scene. Since they are playing neighbors who live next to the Joneses, the story calls for them to show up a lot.

“We get a lot of close-ups, and because we’re all friends [in the movie], they use us a lot,” said Kidder.

So far it has been five or six days over the span of six weeks of filming. So that is another consideration for the extras – having some flex time to take off and do this.

“And I just love getting up at 4 in the morning,” said Roswell resident David Feigenbaum.

For Victoria O’Hare, another Milton resident, her participation is matter of having a husband who owns his own business and works from home.

“So he gets to keep the kids,” O’Hare said.

Gerry Robbins (married to Tracy Robbins) and Greg Wright spoke for all of them saying they are just doing it for the experience.

“I just wanted to see how making a movie is done,” said Wright.

Although the actors get a minimum of $95 a day, they all say they would do it for nothing. The pay is well, just extra.
