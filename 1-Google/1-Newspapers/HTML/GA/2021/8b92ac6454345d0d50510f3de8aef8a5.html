Essential Dust Collector Maintenance Tips

Dust collectors play a critical role in keeping manufacturing facilities safe and productive. When a workplace refuses to maintain dust collectors, it can lead to low safety standards, machine downtime, and costly repairs. The essential dust collector maintenance tips below can help facilities keep these machines functional for a long time. Analyze Air Pressure Dust collectors use filters that require regular cleaning. This cleaning comes in the form of compressed air pressure, which sprays the filters and wipes away the dust accumulating there. The purpose of this process is to keep filters efficient while simultaneously extending their lifespan. You must always inspect the air pressure levels and ensure it matches the manufacturer recommendations in the owner’s manual. If you go above or below the recommended air pressure levels, the system will either fail to clean the filters or clean them too thoroughly. Don’t Neglect Hoppers Even the slightest miscalculation can cause issues within a dust collector, some of which can lead to a large accumulation of dust in the hopper. Allowing that dust to fill the hopper is both damaging to the system and a considerable health hazard. To ensure neither of those situations occurs, check your hopper regularly to confirm dust is moving through it swiftly and smoothly. Frequent inspections will help you catch hazardous conditions like "bridging,” which can be present for a considerable length of time before anyone notices. Luckily, there are a few useful methods for preventing bridging. Always Plan Ahead The key to workplace maintenance is to plan well in advance. Not scheduling maintenance until the last minute can make it harder to complete certain procedures within a comfortable timeframe. By planning for maintenance long before the day arrives, you can guarantee that the tools and expert help you need to get the job done are there when they need to be. If you neglect to plan for maintenance early enough, you run the risk of forgetting about it entirely. As you can see from the essential dust collector maintenance tips above, a lack of care can hurt your workflow.

Warrant Taken for Robbery Suspect

An arrest warrant, based on evidence provided by the Monticello Police Department (MPD), was issued Monday, Feb. 24, for Michael Drake Smith of a Hillsboro address, charging him with robbery. According to an incident report from the MPD the victim arrived at the MPD on December 13, 2013, and reported he had been robbed by two men at a trailer park on Hillsboro Street known as “The Dirty.” According to the victim he was approached by two men who knocked him off a ladder, wrestled him to the ground and stole $4,500 in cash from his pocket and his wallet. They also made off with a certified check made out to the victim in the amount of $30,000 and fled on foot. Upon the victim’s reporting of the crime, a subsequent call to the issuing bank stopped payment on the check. Earlier this month the victim identified Mr. Smith as one of the robbers when he was shown a photograph lineup by an investigator with the MPD. Mr. Smith was still at large upon this printing, and his partner in crime remains unknown and uncharged. Anyone with information concerning this incident, or any other criminal activity is encouraged to call the MPD at 706-468-6404.
