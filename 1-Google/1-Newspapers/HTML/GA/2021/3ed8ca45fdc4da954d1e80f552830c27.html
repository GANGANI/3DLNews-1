The Consequences of Not Paying Child Support

Sadly, not every marriage is built to last. By some estimations, as many as 50 percent of all marriages end in divorce. But even after a marriage ends, your responsibilities to your ex-spouse and family may not. Court-ordered child support is a common condition of many divorces, and full and timely payment of child support is not only a moral obligation but often a legal one. In extreme cases, the consequences of not paying child support can even include jail time. Liens One way that parents and courts enforce the payment of child support is the placing of liens. A lien is a public notice that the owner of a property owes a substantial amount of money, with the condition that the creditor can assume ownership of the property if the debt goes unpaid. Most states allow a parent who is owed child support to place a lien on the other parent’s property. This can make it difficult to sell that property because the proceeds from the sale must go toward paying the debt that resulted in the lien. Suspension of Licenses State agencies—once made aware of delinquent child support—have the power to suspend hunting, fishing, boating, and even driver’s licenses. Laws vary from state to state, but all fifty states have some provision that allows for the suspension of licenses. Some states will allow for probationary use of a driver’s license if it is necessary to earn income, but this is not necessarily the case nationwide. Poor Outcomes in Custody Cases Though a family court’s decision is binding, one party still may choose to appeal. However, the appeal process is an uphill battle, and a less-than-exemplary record of following the terms of the divorce will do the appellant no favors. Late or missing child support payments will hurt your case before a judge when trying to renegotiate child custody. Contempt of Court If child support is a court-ordered condition of divorce and that condition goes unmet, a judge can hold a non-compliant parent in contempt of court. As contempt of court can lead to fines and jail time, this is the most serious consequence of not paying child support. More than any other consequence, this is what every divorced parent should seek to avoid.
