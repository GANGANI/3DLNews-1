Fatal fall from West Campus Parking Deck not accidental

  • Amy Leigh Womack
  • Oct 28, 2003
  • Oct 28, 2003

University Police have ruled the fall that led to a University student's death was not accidental.

Christopher Garrett Knutson, a 19-year-old sophomore from Ridgewood, N.J., was pronounced dead Sunday afternoon at Athens Regional Medical Center (ARMC) after falling from the top level of the West Campus Parking Deck early that morning.

Maj. Jimmy Williamson, assistant University Police chief, said the department received a call from a witness who saw Knutson standing on the southwest corner wall of the parking deck's top floor just before 1 a.m.

"When we arrived, he was on the ground," Williamson said.

After conducting a preliminary investigation, Williamson said the police have no reason to believe foul play was involved or that the incident was accidental.

He said witnesses who saw Knutson standing on the wall -- which is located opposite Brumby Hall at the highest part of the deck -- told the police no one else was near him, and "he wasn't yelling or acting like he was playing around."

University Police filed a search warrant Sunday morning for Knutson's Lipscomb Hall dorm room and a room at the Days Inn located at 230 N. Finley St.

A Days Inn card key was found on Knutson's person, according to the search warrant's listing of probable cause.

The warrant was issued to determine if foul play was involved in the incident, according to the warrant.

The signed warrant permitted University Police officers to seize "items and articles to support the manner or plan of serious injury and/or death," according to the warrant issued just after 3 a.m. Sunday.

After searching the Days Inn hotel room, police seized a Holy Bible, a torn page from the Bible, a set of three Chicago Cutlery knives, knife packaging, a black leather wallet and its contents, and a half full bottle of vodka among other personal items, according to documents accompanying the warrant.

Police also seized a notebook containing Knutson's personal diary and poetry from his dorm room, according to the documents.

Williamson said the police are waiting for the return of a standard toxicology test -- which was sent to the Georgia Bureau of Investigation crime lab -- to complete the investigation.

He said he expects the results won't be ready for another six to eight weeks.

Williamson said Knutson received treatment from medical personnel after the fall Sunday morning and was transported to ARMC.

Athens-Clarke County Coroner Randy Garrett said Knutson was pronounced dead by his attending physician at 2:30 p.m. He said the cause of death was a lack of brain activity.

"From my understanding he was like that almost after his arrival at the hospital," Garrett said.

Information regarding Knutson's funeral arrangements was not available Monday night.

Grant Lor, a sophomore from Duluth who lived across the hall from Knutson, said he and Knutson often watched television and kidded around.

"He was open to everyone -- a really nice guy," Lor said.

Lor's roommate, Lindsey Smith, a sophomore from Canton, said the two were gone for the weekend and didn't hear about Knutson's death until they returned.

"It was really sudden," he said. "He was an all-around good guy."
