Spinach Balls

Spinach Balls...or Spinach Casserole 2 boxes frozen chopped spinach, cooked and drained 2 cups herb bread stuffing mix 2 onions chopped 3 eggs beaten 3/4 cup melted butter (can use less) 1/2 cup parmesan cheese 1 T. garlic salt or powder 1/2 tsp. thyme 1/2 T black pepper or to taste Mix into drained spinach all of the above. Bake either in a casserole or shape into balls for appetizers (yields about 70). Bake about 20 minutes (for the casserole) or 12-14 minutes (for the balls). Can be frozen before baking. Allow one hour thawing time baking. The spinach balls are very popular. But my family thinks they are too good only to be eaten when you want a hot appetizer. Thus, the casserole.

APPLE FRENCH TOAST

APPLE FRENCH TOAST 1 cup brown sugar 1/2 cup butter2 Tablespoon Corn Syrup 2 tart apples, peeled and sliced10 eggs*** 1 1/2 cup milk1 teaspoon Vanilla 1 loaf french bread, broken into piecesCook sugar, butter, & syrup until smooth. Pour into 9x13 baking dish. Spread apples over this mixture. Put bread pieces on top of apples. Blend together remaining ingredients and pour over bread. Cover and refrigerate overnight. Bake uncovered 40 mins. - 350. Serve with spicy apple syrup.Spicy Apple Syrup1 cup applesauce 1 (10 oz) jar of apple jelly1/2 teaspoon cinnamon 1/8 teaspoon ground clovesdash saltCombine all and cook over medium heat. Stir until melted. Serve with Apple French Toast.***CAN USE EGG BEATERS

Vegetable Sandwiches

I saw an old friend recently, and she asked me if I could locate the recipe for vegetable sandwiches. These were a staple for years for the finger sandwiches we made—great for bridal and baby showers. They’re very pretty, and really delicious. I thought I had it, but could not find it when I looked for it. However, my baby sister, Jenny Murphy, came through. Hope you enjoy. Kathy MuddVegetable Sandwiches3 loaves bread 1 cup celery2 medium cucumbers 1 small onion1 medium carrot 1/2 medium bell pepper2 cups mayonnaise 1 teaspoon salt1 envelope unflavored gelatinChop vegetables very small. For best results use food processor. Dissolve gelatin in 1/2 cup vegetable juice (drained from chopped vegetables). Combine mayo, salt, and melted gelatin with vegetables. Mix well. Refrigerate several hours or overnight. Freeze bread for easier cutting; no need to butter bread.Speaking of Jenny, she makes the best little smokies for parties and get-togethers. Her measurements aren’t real exact...just season to taste!In saucepan, heat half jar of grape (or other flavor) jelly. Add little smokies. Add your favorite barbecue sauce, and a dash of worcestershire. Stir, simmer at least 30 minutes. The longer it simmers the better it tastes. Can keep hot in crock pot or chafing dish.

Squash Casserole

Squash Casserole 6 fresh squash 1 onion 1 cup shredded sharp cheddar 1 cup sour cream 1 can cream of mushroom soup 2 eggs 2 cups Ritz Crackers (ground) 1/2 stick butter salt & pepper to taste Boil squash and onion until tender. Drain and mix with butter and salt & pepper to taste. In a separate bowl beat the sour cream, soup and eggs together and combine with squash and onions. Add crackers and cheese. Add more crackers if mixture is too soupy. Pour into a casserole dish, top with more cheese and cracker crumbs, dot with butter, and bake at 350 degrees for 30 minutes.
