Walter Joseph Kramer

Walter Joseph Kramer, 77, of Dandridge, Tenn. died at home on Friday, Nov. 23.

Prior to moving to Dandridge in early 2007, Mr. Kramer lived in Monmouth County N.J. for more than 75 years in Matawan, Little Silver and Wall Township.

He was born on August 5, 1930 in Matawan, N.J., the son of the late Harold and Helen (Felber) Kramer and was preceded in death by his wife, Dorothy (Orr) Kramer in 1993.

He was a graduate of Matawan Regional High School and Rider University. He was a veteran of the U.S. Air Force and served during the Korean War. He retired in the early 1990s from RCA where he was an International Audit Manager.

Survivors include his children, Lynn Kramer of Long Branch, N.J.; Pamela Laughlin and her husband Hal of Bridgewater, N.J.; Scott Kramer and his wife Susan of Jackson; and Craig Kramer and his wife Jessica of Denver, Col.; three grandchildren Sam, Jake and Luke Laughlin; sisters Iris Smith of Keyport N.J., Janis Jacques of Belford, N.J., and Phillis Spears of Toms River, N.J.

Mr. Kramer’s beloved dogs Mike Dundee, Maddie and Lucky are all being placed in new homes.

A private memorial will be held at a later date by the family in New Jersey and ashes will be interred at the Holmdel Cemetery and Mausoleum in Holmdel, N.J.
In memory of Walter Kramer, donations may be sent to the Australian Terrier Rescue Group c/o Carole Larsen, Treasurer, ATR, Inc., 3714 Brighton Place, McHenry, IL 60050-6444.

Friends may sign the guest register and send condolences to the family of Walter J. Kramer at www.farrarfuneralhome.com.

Arrangements by Farrar Funeral Home, 162 East Meeting St. Dandridge, Tenn.
