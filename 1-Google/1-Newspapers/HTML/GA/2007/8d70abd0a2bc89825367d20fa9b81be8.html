Drug Arrests Are Made

By Ted Dunagan•
For the second week in a row deputies with the Jasper County Sheriff’s Office have made drug arrests inside the city of Monticello.

Last Friday evening, August 17, investigators with the JCSO executed a search warrant on Webb Street. The search warrant came after an ongoing investigation into the location, which resulted in several arrests for drug charges and other offenses, according to the annouoncement from the sheriff’s office.

Arrested at the location were Lonnie Beasley, Emily Beasley, Clifford Lynch, Lee Hyman, Jr., Summer Carrey, Tiffany Mattison, Russell Vowell, and Jacob Holland, all of Monticello. Marijuana, Xanax, drug related items, and glass smoking devices were found inside the residence. All of the above individuals have been released on bond.

Also, on the same date, a traffic stop was made by a deputy with the JCSO on a white Jeep Grand Cherokee at Liberty Church Road and Wild Road. The traffic stop resulted in the arrest of Deborah Celeste Livers of Monticello. Ms. Livers was charged with felony possession of marijuana with intent to distribute.

Approximately four ounces of marijuana were found in Ms. Livers’ vehicle in the rear seat area. Because of the amount of marijuana found in the vehicle it is subject to forfeiture under state law, the sheriff’s office announcement said.

Ms. Livers has been released on a $25,000 bond.
