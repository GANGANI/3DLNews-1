The Church of The Bible

The word “church” does not appear in the Old Testament but it appears about 120 times in the gospels and the New Testament. The original word translated “church” from original manuscripts is “ekklesia.” This word is the Greek word kaleo (to call), with the prefix ek (out). In English the word is translated from the original "called out ones" to “church.”

In the Bible the word “church” does not mean a building. There is nothing the slightest bit holy about a building. Christians are the church, the assembly. Jesus said wherever two or more are gathered in His name, or His authority, He will be there also, Matthew 18:20.

The first time the word church appears in the Bible is Matthew 16:18. There Jesus tells Peter he will build “His church” at some point in the future upon the fact that He is the Christ, the Son of the Living God.

Somewhere between Matthew 16:18 and Acts 2:47 we read His church has become a reality. 47 praising God and having favor with all the people. And the Lord added to the church daily those who were being saved (Acts 2:47)

Which church is the Lord adding the saved too? The obvious answer is the church He promised to build. There is no other church mentioned in scripture before or after that church. Peter, in the first gospel sermon ever preached, said, “For the promise is to you and to your children, and to all who are afar off, as many as the Lord our God will call,” Acts 2:39. The Lord adds the “called out” ones who save themselves by obedience to His church. Christ's church consists of the obedient saved.

Going back to Matthew 16, Jesus says the gates of Hades, death itself, will not prevail against His church. Nothing will prevail over His church or change it—not time, not size, not doctrine, not additions or subtractions. That means we should be able to find that same exact Bible church today by comparing a church today to the first century Bible church.

If a church, like the first century original church, has many members, the death of some of the members or the addition of new members may add or detract from the church's total membership but it does not effect the existence of the church. The Bible church's existence is fueled by the constant preaching of Jesus' teaching.

We can look around us today and see hundreds and hundreds of different churches. Many of these churches teach the opposite of each other or teach opposite of what the Bible teaches. They cannot all be Biblical. Does it really matter what they teach as long as they believe in God, Jesus and the Holy Spirit? The Bible says it matters. The Bible church is the pillar and ground of the truth, I Timothy 3:15.

We can determine whether or not a church of today is that same Bible church by comparing a church today with the first century Bible church. If a church does not meet the Bible church's identifiers it is not the Bible church.

John 4:24 says those who worship God MUST, not can, not may, worship Him in spirit and truth. John 17:17 says God's Word is sanctifying truth. 2 John 9 says whoever, whether an individual or a group, who goes outside the doctrine, the teaching, of Christ does NOT have God. There is no universal church, there is only the Bible church. Churches who teach anything other than Jesus' salvational teaching are merely man-made copies.

Any church that teaches the opposite of a core salvational Bible teaching such as Mark 16:15-16, Acts 2:38, I Corinthians 15:1-4, II Thessalonians 1:7-8, Romans 6:3-7, I Peter 3:21, is not the Bible church, II John 9.

The absolute easiest and quickest way to identify non-Biblical churches is their starting date. Any church started after the first century, or based on the teachings of men and women rather than Jesus is not the Bible church.

The plea of the first century church is a plea for the existence of the church fashioned according to the doctrine of Christ found in the New Testament.

The heralds of God's message today must obey the Spirit's injunction to Timothy in his day, when Paul admonished the young man, “charge men 'that they teach no other doctrine'" (1 Timothy 1:3); and again, "hold the pattern of sound words" (2 Timothy 1:13).
