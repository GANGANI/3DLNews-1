Debra Killings’ gospel opens South Fulton ‘Good Things Road Show’

Jazz-gospel recording artist Debra Killings plays the guitar at Fairburn’s Duncan Park on opening night of the South Fulton “Good Things Road Show,” sponsored by District 7 Commissioner William “Bill” Edwards.

Also on the bill were gospel choirs from Cascade United Methodist Church and Riverdale’s Lakewin Christian Center. The performances kicked off a free variety music series held Saturdays in Summer 2004 at venues throughout South Fulton County. The next show in the series will be at Wilkerson Mill Park, 8095 Wilkerson Mill Road, Palmetto, July 10, featuring rock music by the Mike Rogers Band, and folk music by the Kitchen Band from the Palmetto Neighborhood Senior Center.

Showtime is 6 p.m. For the full schedule, call 404-703-5786 or go to www.fultonarts.org or to www.myfultoncountyga.us and look under “Events.
