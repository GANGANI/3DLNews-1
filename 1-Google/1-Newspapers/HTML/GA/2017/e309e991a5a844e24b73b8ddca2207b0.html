Dearing fifth graders graduate from C.H.A.M.P.S. program

  • Caitlin Boland
  • Dec 27, 2017
  • Dec 27, 2017
  • Facebook
  • Twitter
  • WhatsApp
  • SMS
  • Email

Dearing Elementary School fifth graders celebrated the end of their C.H.A.M.P.S. program on Dec. 8.

C.H.A.M.P.S. (Choosing Healthy Activities and Methods Promoting Safety) is taught to all fifth grade students in McDuffie County by McDuffie County Sheriff’s Office Deputy Barry Whitfield.

“We want our students to participate in activities that are healthy and drug free,” Whitfield said.

Whitfield said in 2003 the Georgia Sheriff’s Association adopted C.H.A.M.P.S. and solicited deputies to teach the program in local schools throughout Georgia. The program is taught in more than 86 counties in Georgia.

Students learn lessons in areas such alcohol, ATV safety, hunting safety, peer pressure, water safety, bullying, choices and consequences, gangs, child abduction safety and Internet safety as well as lessons on drugs and stress.

“The first letter in C.H.A.M.P.S. stands for choosing,” said McDuffie County Sheriff’s Office Maj. Ronnie Williamson. “Your life is all about choices. When you go to middle school, you’re going to be faced with a lot of choices. When you go to high school, the choices you make will be even more important. You’ve got to have an education, and you’ve got to keep your body clean. If you don’t try to strive when you’re younger, you won’t be successful.”

After Maj. Williamson spoke, guest speaker Chief Matthew Ellis, of the Georgia Department of Community Supervision, spoke to the fifth graders about choices as well.

“Absolutely anything is possible,” Ellis said. “But it’s not possible with drugs. It takes away a lot of your dreams. It takes away your life.” 

Ellis said the students have to make the right choices and find someone to be their mentor.

“Find someone you can listen to, someone you can talk to, someone you can look up to,” Ellis said.

Ellis closed by imploring the students to be kind to one another no matter what.

“I challenge every single one of you to be kind to one another,” Ellis said. “If you’re being kind to yourself and one another, you’re not chasing negative things.”
