Hearing on Turner suspension is June 3

Hearing on Turner suspension is June 3

By Walter Geiger on May 30, 2024

Suspended Barnesville city manager Tim Turner has requested a public hearing on his preliminary suspension and removal by the city council. That action was taken May 6.

The hearing is set for June 3 at 5 p.m. in the council chambers. The regular council meeting will follow.

According to the city’s public notice of the hearing, which may be found in the 5.28.24 print edition of The Herald Gazette, both the council and Turner will have the opportunity to provide testimony prior to the council’s consideration of a final resolution for Turner’s removal.

Comments will also be taken from the public but those comments will be limited to two minutes.

For more information, call city hall at 770.358.0181.

Warming up for the cap toss

Warming up for the cap toss

By Walter Geiger on May 23, 2024

Photo by Donna Flournoy

Lamar County Class of 2024 graduates Anniston Tvrdeich (left) and Jake Hutto used the Trojan statue at the high school to practice for the cap toss which will close out commencement exercises Saturday morning at Trojan Field.

The festivities will begin promptly at 9 a.m. Approximately 181 seniors will walk across the stage and get their diplomas. The total number of grads is 191, the largest in school history.

For much more, see our annual graduation section published in the 5.21.24 print edition of The Herald Gazette.

LC’s Everett Horne wins state golf title

LC’s Everett Horne wins state golf title

By Walter Geiger on May 21, 2024

Lamar County’s Everett Horne came from five strokes back after round one to win the GHSA Class A, Div. 1 golf championship Tuesday at the Georgia Southern University Course in Statesboro. Horne, a junior, fired a 74-70: 144 to finish two strokes ahead of freshman Colton Lewis of Swainsboro.

As a team, the Trojans finished sixth.
