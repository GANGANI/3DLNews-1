Jimmy Buffett's Margaritaville New Orleans will relocate after closing current location

  • Updated: Jun. 04, 2015, 7:25 p.m.|
  • Published: Jun. 04, 2015, 6:25 p.m.
By
  • Curtis Rockwell

11053252_10153301042271425_6053346734168615208_n.jpg

It was announced Thursday that Jimmy Buffett's Margaritaville New Orleans will be relocating after the current location closes later this month.

A day after it was revealed that Jimmy Buffett's Margaritaville New Orleans would close in its current location after over two decades, it was announced on Thursday that the popular destination is merely changing latitude.
In a press release, Margaritaville New Orleans officials said that the restaurant, bar and live music venue will move from its current address at 1104 Decatur Street to a new location on upper Decatur. The new venue is currently in the design stage, with construction scheduled to begin later this year.

"We will always have a presence in the city that gave Jimmy his start. Margaritaville has had an awesome 22 year run on Decatur Street and we are not done yet," said Bret Brown, Margaritaville's Director of Domestic Operations.  "We have made the decision to vacate our current location on lower Decatur before completion of our new property in the interest of making way for another namesake live music venue, BB Kings, to become the next tenant at the historic Storyville Music Hall location."

Margaritaville's last day of operation at its current location will be on June 30th, 2015.  Employees were told of the news Wednesday morning.

Tommy Peters, president of the B.B. King's Blues Club chain -- which has locations in Memphis, Nashville and Orlando, Fla. -- told the website NOLA Defender that he had spent 15 years looking for a New Orleans location for the restaurant and music venue.

The new B.B. King's outpost will, like Margaritaville, employ local musicians, Peters told the site, but the menu will reflect the cuisine in its mothership's hometown of Memphis: barbecue.

Margaritaville featured many performances over the years by Buffett, especially during the annual Jazz Fest. The relocation of the New Orleans venue comes on the heels of the closing of the Margaritaville gift shop last year which sat adjacent to the club, a move that was attributed to a rent increase. It also comes eight months after  Jimmy Buffett's Margaritaville Casino and Resort in Biloxi shuttered its doors.

Prior to becoming Jimmy Buffett's Margaritaville New Orleans, the property was the site of the legendary Storyville Jazz Hall.

No opening date for the new location of Jimmy Buffett's Margaritaville New Orleans was announced.
