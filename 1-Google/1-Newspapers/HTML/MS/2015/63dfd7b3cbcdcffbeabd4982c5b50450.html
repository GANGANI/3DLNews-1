The Garcinia Weight Loss Evidence..Does It Exist?

There hasn’t been lot of research on Garcinia Cambogia – but there are a handful of highly cited and credible studies of noteworthy discussion.  Therefore, the research is limited. This should be a warning sign for people who care about the evidence. Limited evidence will only tell you so much. Here’s an example of limited evidence in action.

You are in a dark room. You can’t see anything but a slimmer of light. You feel a hard round object on what looks to be a basketball. This is limited evidence. The limited evidence being the lack of visibility. When you actually get some more light on, you’ll see that what you thought was a basketball is actually a coconut. More evidence needs to be gathered for anything to be definitive.

The American Medical Association conducted a study on HCA in 1998. The conclusion? Garcinia Cambogia did not produce significant weight loss for the subjects. This study showed that Garcinia Cambogia did not provide any weight loss benefits.

What’s interesting to note is that future studies have conflicted this result. That is the nature of science. The study got criticized by a researcher named Harry Preuss. Preuss said that the study used the wrong HCA. This is a red flag. If the wrong HCA was used then the study is invalid. You put in wrong data and you’ll get wrong results. That’s how it works.

Another, criticism is that too much fiber was used in the study. Scientists believe that fiber can mess up the results, as it interferes with HCA. A combination of these factors ends up making the study invalid.

The Second Study

Scientists in Japan conducted a study in the year 2003. They did not experiment on humans but on mice. It’s remember to know that mice are genetically altered to have identical genes to humans. What is tested on mice will have a high chance of yielding the same effect on a human.

The conclusion of the study was that Garcinia Cambogia had no connection with weight loss. However, other effects were discovered. It decreased insulin and leptin levels. Insulin controls your blood sugar levels while leptin is responsible for controlling your appetite.

This study indicates that the claims of Garcinia Cambogia of being an appetite suppressant is true. However, this is not the case when talking about diabetes. Generally speaking, more insulin is a good thing for the human body. Insulin is used as a stabilizing force for your blood sugar levels. This is why people who do not have diabetes do not experience high/low blood pressure. Insulin provides balance. Garcinia Cambogia is counterproductive if you want to cut the risk of diabetes.

The Third Study

The researcher Preuss conducted his own study after he saw the inaccurate study conducted before. His study is interesting. The study concluded that Garcinia Cambogia provides rapid weight loss among overweight people and is an adequate way of suppressing appetite. It appears that Garcinia Cambogia is especially effective if you are overweight. Bonus points for appetite suppression as that’s a common problem overweight people experience.

The Fourth Study

Preuss re-did his study over to determine the validity of his previous experiments. He got identical results. Overweight subjects experienced substantial weight loss and suppressed appetite.

The Fifth Study

Preuss conducted another study with other doctors. The study’s quite complicated. If you ate the HCA in its natural form you would be getting less results. Within its natural form, HCA is connected to calcium. If you extracted the substance and combined it with salt – you got better results. Eating the actual fruit isn’t as effective. It needs to be artificially altered. The HCA + salt, is called HCA-SX.

It actually confirmed most of the claims that people make about Garcinia Cambogia.

  • Increased happiness. (Could be used to treat depression.)

  • Reduces the risk of obesity

If you combined HCA with salt you got all the powerful results that people claimed it have. Perhaps, Dr.Oz is onto something here…
