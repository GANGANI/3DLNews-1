Troopers, co-workers remember Triplett

  • By Cheryl Owens cowens@themeridianstar.com
  • Sep 8, 2015
  • Sep 8, 2015

    Fellow Mississippi Highway patrolmen and other co-workers are fondly remembering Willie Marcus Triplett, who died unexpectedly Sunday.

    "He was an outstanding officer," said Mississippi Highway Patrol Sgt. Andy West, "and the highway patrol mourns his loss."

     Triplett, 44, of Philadelphia, died Sunday morning of an apparent heart attack, Lauderdale County Coroner Clayton Cobler said. The incident occurred around 9:45 a.m., while Triplett worked at his part-time job at Piggly Wiggly in Collinsville.

    "He was found in the cooler of the store by his daughter," Cobler said. "She started CPR and called EMS. He was transported to a local hospital, where he was pronounced dead."

    Piggly Wiggly Manager Joe Williams said Triplett, who had worked at the store 11 years, will be greatly missed.

    "Willie was a very dependable, fun guy to work with," Williams said. "He kept us all laughing; he was just part of our family."

    Triplett graduated from the Highway Patrol Academy in 2007, and was assigned to Troop H in Neshoba County.

    "We have lost a brother and a good friend, and the state of Mississippi has lost a good police officer," West said. "Our thoughts and prayers are with the family at this time."

    Triplett is survived by his wife and two children.
