Narcotics sting nets 18 arrests

  • By Jim Clark
  • May 6, 2015
  • May 6, 2015
  • 0

  • Twitter
  • WhatsApp
  • SMS
  • Email
  • Facebook
  • Twitter
  • WhatsApp

  • Save

On May 4, the Lee County Sheriff’s Department and the North Mississippi Narcotics Unit (NMNU) conducted a secret indictment saturation in south Lee County.

“This was after about eight months of undercover work, where narcotic officers were actively making drug buys from these individuals,” said Lee County Sheriff Jim Johnson. “There were 38 indictments in all, mainly in Verona, and south of the Verona area.”

There were 18 total arrests and two search warrants executed where quantities of meth and crack cocaine were confiscated.

The following individuals were charges; Brenda M. Syles, of Verona, 42, for sale of crack cocaine times five. Her bond was set at $50,000;

Calvin B. Clark, of Verona, 53, for sale of crack cocaine times three and possession of crack cocaine with intent to sell. His bond was set at $30,000;

Drewtil Gillespie, of Shannon, 21, for sale of marijuana times two. His bond was set at $20,000;

Brandon L. Smith, of Tupelo, 33, for possession of a controlled substance. No bond was set;

Derreck D. Watkins, Verona, 29, for sale of crack times five, $60,000; he was also charged with child endangerment times two and possession of crack cocaine. There was no bond set on those two charges;

Crystale G. Dean, Verona, 32, for sale of crack cocaine times two. Her bond was set at $20,000;

Daniel L. Ross, of Plantersville, 51, for sale of methamphetamine times three. His bond was set at $30,000;

Danny L. Long, of Tupelo, 40, for sale of crack cocaine. His bond was set at $10,000;

Anthony Williams, of Tupelo, 41, for sale of crack cocaine times two. No bond was set;

Barbara A. Dillard, of Verona, 48, for sale of crack cocaine times two. Her bond was set at $20,000;

Charles L. Carter, of Verona, 35, for possession of marijuana times three. His bond was set at $15,000;

Michelle R. Bullox, of Verona, 40, for sale of crack cocaine times five. Her bond was set at $50,000;

James O. Smith, of Verona, 37, for sale of crack cocaine. His bond was set at $10,000;

“You see in one of these cases the individual was charged with child endangerment. They had a minor child with narcotics in the residence,” Johnson said. “These people have no regard for your life, or even their own children. Their main concern is drug distribution.”

All of the indictments were returned from the March term of a Lee County Grand Jury. At that Grand Jury probable cause was substantiated to issue capias.

“You hear statistics about the prison population and about rehabilitation,” Johnson said. “None of these people, who we arrested, are first timers. They all have prior criminal history.”

There will be several more indictments served in the near future.

“These individuals, instead of being locked up, are out here living and carrying on their corrupt lifestyle in our neighborhoods. It is time for it to stop,” Johnson said.

Anyone with additional information on these cases is asked to call the LCSD at 841-9040.
