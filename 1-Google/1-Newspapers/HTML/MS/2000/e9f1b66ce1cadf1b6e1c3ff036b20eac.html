Published 5:00 am Wednesday, October 11, 2000

By

Fermon Cephus Brown

Services for Fermon Cephus Brown of Marrero, La., were Oct. 9,2000, at Mothe Funeral Home with burial in West Lawn Cemetery inGretna.

Mr. Brown, 73, died Oct. 6, 2000. He was born on Oct. 25, 1926,to Mildred and Elvyn Brown.

Justin Dallis Hickman

Services for Justin Dallis Hickman of Brookhaven are 1 p.m.,Thursday, Oct. 12, at Brookhaven Funeral Home Chapel with burial inWoodlawn Cemetery in Columbia.

Visitation is Wednesday, Oct. 11, from 5-9 p.m. at BrookhavenFuneral Home.

Mr. Hickman, 19, died Oct. 9, 2000, at University Medical Centerin Jackson. He was born in Hinds County on June 17, 1981.

He was a clerk in an attorney’s office. He was a member of FirstBaptist Church.

Preceding him in death was his grandfather, Houston Hickman.

Survivors are his father, James Neel Hickman Sr. of McComb; hismother, Jullia F. Hickman Ivey of Brookhaven; his stepfather, ChuckIvey of Brookhaven; his brother, James Neel Hickman Jr. ofPensacola, Fla.; his grandparents, Maurice and Jeanette Ingram ofColumbia and Evelyn Hickman Golay of Brookhaven; his aunts anduncle, Teresa and Mike Kinsey of Gautier and Joy Harlene Smith ofBrookhaven; and his first cousins, Joe and Consuela Dambrino andJoy Smith Ladd.

Memorials may be made in Justin’s name to the Division of PublicAffairs, UMC Surgical Intensive Care Unit, 2500 North State St.,Jackson, MS 39216.

Camie Elizabeth McCaffrey

Services for Camie Elizabeth McCaffrey are 11 a.m., Thursday,Oct. 12, at Calvary Baptist Church with burial in the churchcemetery.

Visitation is Wednesday, Oct. 11, from 6-8 p.m. at BrookhavenFuneral Home.

The infant was born and died Oct. 8, 2000, at King’s DaughtersMedical Center. Her mother, Casey Elizabeth Brownlee McCaffrey,died Oct. 9, 2000, of injuries sustained in an automobileaccident.

Preceding her in death were her great-grandparents, Mr. and Mrs.George Brownlee and J. P. McAlexander.

Survivors are her father, Richard Tate McCaffrey of BogueChitto; her sister, Carlie Paige Patrick of Bogue Chitto; hergrandparents, Paula and Hank Smith of Wesson, David and KayBrownlee of Potts Camp, and Mr. and Mrs. Richard McCaffrey andMelinda Bales, all of Bogue Chitto; her great-grandparents, Mr. andMrs. Robert King of Potts Camp, and Mr. and Mrs. James McCaffreyand Mrs. Clifton Bales, all of Bogue Chitto; and hergreat-great-grandmother, Mrs. J. G. McCaffrey of Bogue Chitto.

Memorials may be made to Calvary Baptist Church LibraryFund.

Casey Elizabeth McCaffrey

Services for Casey Elizabeth McCaffrey of Bogue Chitto are 11a.m., Thursday, Oct. 12, at Calvary Baptist Church with burial inthe church cemetery.

Visitation is Wednesday, Oct. 11, from 6-8 p.m. at BrookhavenFuneral Home.

Mrs. McCaffrey, 21, died Oct. 9, 2000, at University MedicalCenter in Jackson of injuries sustained in an automobile accident.She was born in New Albany on Nov. 12, 1978, to Carl David Brownleeand Paula Jean McAlexander Brownlee Smith.

She was a teller at State Bank. She was a member of CalvaryBaptist Church.

Preceding her in death were her infant daughter, Camie ElizabethMcCaffrey; and her grandparents, Mr. and Mrs. George Brownlee andJ. P. McAlexander.

Survivors are her husband, Richard Tate McCaffrey of BogueChitto; her daughter, Carlie Paige Patrick of Bogue Chitto; herparents, Paula and Hank Smith of Wesson and David and Kay Brownleeof Potts Camp; her brothers, Scott Brownlee and Kyle Brownlee, bothof Brookhaven, Chad Brownlee of Potts Camp, and Christopher Smithof Weir; her sisters, Jennifer Cooper, Jennifer Meilstrup, andEmily Springfield, all of Brookhaven, and Lisa Bynum and KathyTaylor, both of Potts Camp; her grandparents, Mr. and Mrs. RobertKing of Potts Camp; and five nieces, two nephews and a host ofaunts and uncles.

Memorials may be made to Calvary Baptist Church LibraryFund.

Verlie Marie Wroten Watts

Services for Verlie Marie Wroten Watts of Brookhaven were 10a.m., Wednesday, Oct. 11, at Brookhaven Funeral Home Chapel withburial in New Prospect Baptist Church Cemetery.

Mrs. Watts, 88, died Oct. 9, 2000, at King’s Daughters MedicalCenter. She was born in Lincoln County on Jan. 22, 1912, to JamesMonroe Wroten Sr. and Olive Strait Wroten.

She was a homemaker. She was a member of New Prospect BaptistChurch, where she was a member of the WMU.

Preceding her in death were her parents, and her husband, BurtonWatts.

Survivors are her daughters, Olive Watts Jones of Brookhaven andCynthia Watts Briggs of Jackson; and seven grandchildren and fourgreat-grandchildren.
