Haunted hallway?

Believers say DeSoto County full of spooky places

  • By Amanda Compton-Ortiz
    Times-Tribune News Staff
  • Oct 25, 2008
  • Oct 25, 2008 Updated Apr 14, 2015
  • 0

Whether you believe in the paranormal or not, accounts told by some DeSoto County residents may make you think twice.

Footsteps down the hall, strange voices echoing from an empty room and shadows on the wall are all images of a great ghost story. But what if those stories were true. What if ghosts are real? What if spirits do get trapped in time?

Employees at the BankPlus bank branch in Hernando’s town square believe they have a ghost haunting their building.

One employee, who asked to remain anonymous, has been witness to strange activity at the bank. She said one Saturday morning while she sat alone at a desk making a Gingerbread house for Christmas, she suddenly heard tape rolling on one of the business’ adding machines in another part of the building.

“I got up to look and called the office manager and told him what was happening with the machine,” she said. “When he asked me to just turn it off, I looked down and it was off. I looked closer and noticed that the clear button on the machine was pressed down as if someone was there in the room holding it down.”

The woman, and some of her other co-workers, have also heard faint footsteps down a narrow, wooden floor hallway in the bank. Employees have also come to work to crooked picture frames hanging on the wall. The volume of their office radio has also been turned up and down in an empty room.

“One time we heard sounds coming from our conference room,” the woman said. “It sounded like someone was throwing rocks on the floor. It was loud. When we got to the room, there was no one there. We only saw pieces of brick from the conference room wall laying on the floor.”

An Olive Branch city employee said he believes he once saw a ghost at what used to be the old Eli’s Cotton Gin Restaurant in Old Towne.

After moving his business into the old facility in 1993, he said one day he was working the counter and looked up and saw a figure of a man walk into a room down the hall.

“I remember seeing the man but I never heard anyone come into the shop,” he said. “Then I waited about 30 minutes to wait on whoever went into the room to come out. Well, no one ever came out so I got up enough courage to walk down the hall and into the room. There was no one there.”

Other neighboring shop owners in the Old Towne area have also claimed to have seen the ghost, which everyone referred to as “Eli.”

The Hernando BankPlus branch claimed to be haunted is located on Losher Street across from the DeSoto County Courthouse, which is also believed to be haunted.

Employees in the courthouse’s circuit court office said muffled voices can be heard in the hallways of the two-story building that dates back to the 1800s.

“It doesn’t matter if it’s day or night, we hear the voices,” one clerk said. “We have also heard slamming doors at all hours.”

The clerk said one evening some years ago when the courthouse was empty, two workers hired to restore the courthouse’s murals heard the voices and other strange noises.

“They were so scared they left without finishing their work,” she said.

The current courthouse was built in 1941 after being burned down three times prior since the early 1850s. Many people who work at the courthouse today believe the ghost of a man who burned down the first structure still lurks through the hallways today.

“I know a woman who has said she saw him once sitting in a chair right in front of her,” a clerk said. “She claims the ghost laughed at her and then asked her ‘what are you doing sitting at my desk?’”

The clerk said a tape recording of strange, paranormal activity in the courthouse’s basement does exist. The tape was recorded overnight by a former county administrator. Two cleaning ladies of the courthouse have heard the recording.

“On the tape you can hear doors slamming, chains rattling and someone hollering out loudly for help,” one of the cleaning ladies said. “You can hear all kinds of strange noises.

“It definitely made me a believer.”
