Local Youtubers in the limelight

  • By Lyndy Berryhill
  • Mar 21, 2019
  • Mar 21, 2019 Updated Mar 21, 2019
  • Comments

Danny and Wanda King never set out to be famous on YouTube.

Working on their farm in Perkinston, the couple would occasionally watch YouTube for entertainment, but when Danny saw some, “farming tutorials,” he knew he could publish better content.

Shooting video of their everyday lifestyle and posting to social media, they have more than 8 million views and more than 60,000 subscribers to their channel, Deep South Homestead.

“Homesteading is just a phrase that came out here three or four years ago; I’ve lived this life my whole life,” Danny said.

Together, the Kings raise pigs, goats, turkeys, chickens and cows.

They grow a large variety of vegetables, fruits and herbs using their two greenhouses and less than 10 acres. What they don’t cook fresh, they can and put away in their cellar.

“It’s easier to tell you what we don't grow,” Wanda said with a quick laugh. “We just grow a huge variety of everything.”

Along with everything they plant, they are both working on establishing a permaculture or edible landscape to simplify some of their work.

Danny has foraged some native plants like Huckleberry bushes from the woods and replanted them in their front yard. Wanda makes a tart jelly from the small berries.

Their first video published in December, 2015. It was only Danny in the videos at first. With a background in preaching, running his own business and his 4-H public-speaking experience, Danny transitioned to social media seamlessly.

Wanda filmed and edited until she felt more comfortable on the other side of the camera.

Now, in addition to the original channel, they both have their own YouTube channel for more personal video diaries and they run an Etsy account, where they sell Danny’s homemade spoons, bowls and tortilla presses when he has the time to make them.

Wanda says they have to be careful about what they post for sale because orders pile up many times quicker than Danny can keep up.

Along with their daily chores, they struggle to find time for hobbies.

They both love to read and, when they first were married, they would watch “Dancing with the Stars” or “The Voice.”

Lately, they usually will watch a movie every few months.

The couple has traveled by invite across the nation to speak and serve as panelists for homesteading conferences.

Several years ago, due to their notoriety on YouTube, the Kings began hosting The Gathering near their home. Other homesteaders from Mississippi and around the country travel to Stone County to meet and learn from one another.

This year they had more than 300 attendees. They rent out the Stone County fairgrounds since it has grown so much.

The hotels in Wiggins filled up and the overflow of attendees had to find rooms in Hattiesburg or closer to Gulfport.

Despite their full days and workloads, neither Danny nor Wanda complain. They have grown to love their little piece of the world.

Danny has lived on his current property for about 30 years while Wanda grew up in Columbia. Wanda was living in Hattiesburg where she was attending classes for medical coding at Pearl River Community College when they met in 2013 through Christian Mingle, an online dating website.

Both were skeptical of finding a partner online, but within a few weeks they were talking five to six hours every night. The couple married in 2013.

Danny and Wanda balk at the mention of cheesy reality shows like “Doomsday Preppers.”

Danny’s demeanor is not that of someone who lives in fear of anything. He is a man of faith, a former minister, and is comfortable with slow days on his farm.

They are not preparing for the end of the world, but, Danny says, there are countless situations that might require having a hefty dose of self-reliance.

If government trade relations deteriorated tomorrow, the Kings’ diet would not change; they grow or raise nearly everything they eat.

If the banks failed, the Kings homestead would be undisturbed because they live happily below their means and buy only what they need.

Even if the situation was more dire, like if the government became unstable, the Kings are prepared.

While the possibility of a life-changing event could seem far away for many, Danny has come face-to-face with several.

When his wife passed away from cancer in 2011, he was spent financially from paying medical and funeral costs. Right after, he seriously injured his neck and was not able to work.

His family also needed him.

Danny said there was quite a while that he was not making a sufficient income or any money at all to support himself. Fortunately for him, he and his late wife had made a habit of storing food and stockpiling necessities.

Thanks to their frugal habits, his hardships did not break him.

“By not going out on vacations, and not going out to eat every weekend, and blowing a lot of money, I put it [back into my farm],” Danny said. “It didn’t matter that I didn't have an income; everything I had was paid for.”

Slowly, Danny’s health improved and he began working again.

The couple still run a tight ship. When their internet and phone bill doubled in within a year, they were determined to make it pay for itself.

“I’m the type of person where, If I spend a dime, I want to see 15 cents back,” Danny said.

On advice from a neighbor, they began filming video tutorials on farming know-how.

Now, Danny and Wanda live off of the revenue from YouTube for their dispensable income. Tool and gardening companies send them products for free for them to give an honest review on their channel.

They have also written several e-books on growing different crops.

At the most recent Gathering the second weekend in March, Danny and Wanda were greeted by friends they have made through YouTube and several fans.

Some of their subscribers even got star struck to meet them, which Danny and Wanda both find a little surreal. When Danny mentioned on a YouTube video how much he liked pecans, his viewers would send him sacks full.

Danny cracks them and Wanda makes him a special, gluten-free cookie.

Wanda ate a snack of candy corn and mentioned how much she liked it. Now, she has an entire cabinet, which Danny built by hand, to hold all the different flavors of candy corn she has received as gifts from viewers.

“I didn’t realize there was more than one flavor,” Wanda said.
