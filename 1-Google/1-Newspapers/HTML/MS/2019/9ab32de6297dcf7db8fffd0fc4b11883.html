Dixon was an inspiration, ‘sweet’

Dorothy King Dixon
Posted Tuesday, October 22, 2019 11:00 pm

Dorothy King Dixon, a businesswoman and fourth generation farmer, died on Saturday. She was 94.

Services will be held on Thursday at 2 p.m. at The First United Methodist Church. Burial will follow in Cedarlawn Cemetery. Visitation will be held on Thursday at the church from 12 noon until the service.

Born on December 19, 1924, to Hub and Gladys DeWees King, she was a respected member of the community, described as sweet and kind. "An inspiring woman, ahead of her time," said Valeria Hardy of Philadelphia.

She liked to say she was born in The Citizens Bank parking lot where the family's home was located before the bank was built.

She graduated from Philadelphia High School in 1940 and four years later from what was then Mississippi State College for Women or Mississippi University for Women today.

From MUW she went to Vanderbilt University and after graduating from the dietetic program she worked at the hospital there. She would later work at Kennedy Veterans Hospital in Memphis.

She returned to Philadelphia in 1949 and worked as a bookkeeper in the family Ford dealership until she and her brother, Allan King, a now late longtime mayor of Philadelphia, sold the dealership.

In 1955, she married Ernest A. "Dick" Dixon, who had the Pontiac and later the Chevrolet dealership. They all sold their dealerships in the 1960s.

She and Allan opened an auto parts store and Dick worked at the Bank of Philadelphia until his death in 1972. In the meantime, she managed the family farm in the Shady Grove community for 40 years as part of the fourth generation.

She was always interested in farming and served on various farm committees. She grew up in the First United Methodist Church and joined the church in 1936 and served on many committees.

Most of all she loved Philadelphia and knew much of its early history, her family said.

She is survived by nieces and nephews, Carolyn King Briscoe (Jimmy) of Jackson; Hub King of Starkville; and Andy King (Susan) of Philadelphia.
