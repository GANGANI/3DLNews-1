Locals Help film Movie

  • By: Kaylee Hall
  • Mar 23, 2022
  • Mar 23, 2022
  • Comments

  • WhatsApp
  • SMS
  • Email
  • Facebook

Some locals got a behind-the-scenes look at the Hollywood thriller film set during February.

Cast and crew from California traveled to Flint Creek Water Park to film scenes for “Hunt Club,” a movie centered around the idea of a hunting club where women, rather than game animals, are the targets.

Among the crew, three Stone Countians worked directly alongside actors and producers.

“I have enjoyed it,” said Wade Beasley, a Wiggins Fire Department employee who served as an on-set medic for the film. “I like the atmosphere and being able to stand off-site…to see how it’s being directed.”

As a medic, Beasley treated any cuts, scrapes, or minor injuries the crew sustained.

“Everything went really well, “Beasley said.

Though he is not on film sets often, this is not the first time Beasley has been a crew member. In the past, he has worked as a medic on a home makeover show filmed in Gulfport.

Beasley said he enjoyed the job so much he always volunteers to work on more productions.

He thinks Flint Creek is a great location for producers to shoot movies.

“Flint Creek is a good attraction for everybody,” Beasley said. “It’s a remote area that offers boating and swimming. Overall, it has a lot to offer.”

The film crew shot boating scenes on a secluded area of Flint Creek Water Park, indoor scenes in a cabin made to look like a barn, and other scenes shot at night.

Wiggins Police Chief Jeff Thomas said being a medic for the film was eye-opening.

“To someone not familiar, it’s very chaotic,” Thomas said.

Since it was his first film to work on, he did not know what to expect day-to-day.

“It was a completely new experience for me,” he said.

He was impressed with the set designers and the details put into each scene.

“The work that goes into the sets by the craft, art, and costume departments is phenomenal,” Thomas said. “It was really interesting to be behind the scenes and see how movies are made.”

But Thomas said those who know Flint Creek Water Park will still be able to recognize the locations.

“Local viewers will see the movie and recognize local areas,” he said.

While Thomas and Beasley both enjoyed their roles on set, neither plan to quit their day jobs for the entertainment industry anytime soon. Stone County resident Mason Runnels can’t say the same.

He wants to continue learning more about the film industry, and one day he hopes to start an entertainment business in Mississippi.

“I like having that experience and the environment that comes with working on set,” Runnels said.

Runnels worked as a production assistant on “Hunt Club.”

As a PA, Runnels made sure everything was ready for the film crew and actors to start each day.

“From setting up pop-up tents, passing out waters, using fog machines, or moving furniture around set,” he said. “Some days, I may be picking up actors from the airport. Others, I may be on set all day helping out where I can with the other departments.”

Since college, Runnels has worked on other film sets in Biloxi, Natchez, Laurel, Starkville, and many others.

He originally wanted to be an architect.

“After a semester, I found it just wasn’t for me,” Runnels said.

Two years ago, Runnels enrolled in the film, video, and technology courses at Pearl River Community College and was hooked.

“A month later, I got my first feature film job in Jackson through some connections I made,” he said. “Ever since then, I have been nonstop working all over the state.”

Typically, Runnels works behind the camera and can shoot film, but he is familiar with the entire process.

“Hunt Club” kept Runnels busy, but he was happy to film near home.

“Flint Creek is just a beautiful place,” he said. “The lake is nice, and the cabins they have are also amazing.”

Runnels said he has more work lined up in the future and said many more films are coming to Mississippi for filming. He and his wife, Rebecca, live near Wiggins.

“I am excited to say that more films and TV shows are moving into Mississippi,” he said. “The industry is coming here because we have an open and welcoming environment. I am to be working in and during this upbringing of more features in Mississippi, especially in my own neck of the woods.”
