Albert James 'A.J.' Charlton

Albert James “A.J.” Charlton, 71, died Aug. 29, 2017, in Ketchikan

He was born Sept. 9, 1945, in Seattle. He grew up in Wrangell and other areas of Southeast Alaska. He attended Wrangell High School and Ketchikan High School, and received his GED, and later went on to the University of Alaska Fairbanks to obtain 3 masters degrees in psychology, teaching and human service.

In his youth, he was the state’s youngest licensed mortician when he worked in the family mortuary business in Wrangell.

He joined the U.S. Army in 1965. He served in Vietnam in the Special Forces, and was honorably dischargd in 1969.

From there, Mr. Charlton traveled around the West Coast, returning to Alaska to attend college.

He worked briefly as a bartender on the Alaska Marine Highway System, and as a sound technician for NBC News in Los Angeles.

Once he graduated, he joined the State of Alaska Department of Corrections in Fairbanks and Sitka, from 1974 to 1978.

He then joined the Alaska State Troopers, which took him to Nome, Iliamna and the Aleutian Chain, Ketchikan, and Kodiak posts. His first period of employment with the troopers continued through 2002, and he worked again with the troopers from 2002 through 2004.

“He enjoyed working the (Alaska) State Fair in Palmer and traveling to remote locations all over the state,” his family writes.

Mr. Charlton was married in July 1984 in Canyon Lake, California. He resided in Ketchikan from 1988 to 1990, and then again beginning in 1994.

After his second retirement from the troopers, Mr. Charlton worked as a tour/bear guide for local flight operations, including Taquan Air and Island Wings.

“Just weeks before his death, he finished his last tour working security on the North Slope for Purcell Security,” his family writes. “He really enjoyed staying busy and taking his wife with him whenever possible.”

He volunteered as a law enforcement officer at the 2002 Winter Olympics in Salt Lake City. He also volunteered as a law enforcement officer in Anchorage during the winter Special Olympics 2001 World Games.

Mr. Charlton was a member of the Alaska Peace Officers Association, Fraternal Order of Alaska State Troopers, Masons, Fraternal Order of the Elks, VFW, Ketchikan Historical Society and the American Association of State Troopers. He also participated with the Boy Scouts and Eagle Scouts.

He enjoyed helping other people, reciting Robert Service poems, fishing, hunting, riding his Gold Wing motorcycle, cutting down trees, spending time tinkering, wood and soap stone carving, collecting wood scraps, toothpicks, and bungee cords. He also enjoyed travel and spending time with his family.  

Mr. Charlton was preceded in death by his parents; brothers, Gerald Hall of Wrangell and Mel Charlton of Bellingham, Washington; and sister, Hazel Charlton of Bellingham.

His is survived by his wife, Laura Charlton of Ketchikan; son-in-law and daughter, Bobby and Shannon Hickman of Ketchikan; son, James Anderson of Walla Walla, Washington; grandchildren, Wyatt and Stella Hickman of Ketchikan, and Andrea, Jessie, Brittany and Ashley Anderson of Walla Walla; brother, Glenn Hall of San Diego; sister, Lorraine Charlton of Bellingham; and many nieces and nephews.  

A celebration of life will be from 3 p.m. to 6 p.m. Monday, Sept. 4, 2017, at the Sunny Point Conference Center.

Mr. Charlton’s remains will be cremated, with his ashes to be spread near Wrangell. A plaque will be placed at Bayview Cemetery.
