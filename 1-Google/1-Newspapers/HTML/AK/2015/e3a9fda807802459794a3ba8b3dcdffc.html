By Dani Palmer 

Local wins Petersburg Salmon Derby with 50 pound fish

 
  Share   Tweet 0 Comments

May 28, 2015

Glo Wollen / Submitted Photo

Scott Stafford's 50.9 pound king salmon earned him the top spot in this year's 34th Annual Salmon Derby. See pages 15 and 16 for list of derby results.

He was alone on his boat, and it was a bit of a battle. Scott Stafford, of Petersburg, caught his Salmon Derby winning 50.9 pound fish on Saturday.

Fishing from his 22-foot-long Alice Marie, just outside of the North Arm with fishermen from a dozen vessels watching nearby, Stafford said it took about 30 to 40 minutes to reel in the fish.

The fisherman brought the king salmon up to his boat about seven times. It went under the boat, around the boat. Finally he got it to where the head was pointing at him and he could get it in the net.

"He was pretty tuckered out by then and it was over," Stafford said.

He went fishing with friends Phil and Sandy Meeks the next morning, when Phil caught his ninth place salmon in about 10 minutes. The rest of the day was slow.

"The first day I got skunked," Stafford said. "The last day I figured I couldn't do any better."

The 34th Annual Petersburg Chamber of Commerce Salmon Derby began Friday morning and continued until Monday evening. The fish caught weighed anywhere from 6.7 pounds to Stafford's 50.9.

Stafford's participated in the derby since 2006 and done it "pretty much every year" since. Three years ago, he placed third.

Stafford plans to take the $7,000 he won this year and apply it to a boat fund. He's been saving up for a new one.

Madison Whitethorn, of Petersburg, placed second with a 45.8 pound salmon, making her one of the derby's youngest top finishers at age 13.

She got it on the line around 9:50 a.m. Friday morning, and on the boat at about 10:10. She noted all was quiet until "all of a sudden, Grandpa was yelling for me, telling me to take the pole."

The fish was a struggler.

"It was kind of hard because I didn't know how to get it out, so I was just wrestling it," Whitethorn said.

Her arm was cramping by the end of it, but when she saw how close the fish was after the line had been 150 feet out, she "started crying tears of joy."

Whitehorn has participated for "quite a few years because I love fishing. I go with my Grandpa when I get the chance." She was actually fishing from her grandpa's boat, the Maddy D, in a "top secret" location when she caught the salmon.

Whitehorn thought it was "pretty cool" to come out in the top three, especially at her age. She added that she couldn't have done it without her grandpa and dad. They all stayed out fishing until Sunday.

She's putting most of her $3,500 toward a college savings fund, but is also buying some new clothes and using it to help remodel her room.

Third place with a prize amount of $2,500 went to Stephanie Sell, who caught a 45.4 pound salmon. Kent Wegener won the cumulative weight prize, as his total catch weighed in at 112.3 pounds.

Chamber of Commerce Manager Cindi Lagoudakis said the 364 participants from this year's derby came from at least 18 states, including Arizona, Illinois, Texas and Connecticut, covering all regions of the U.S.

"Not unexpectedly, the majority of out-of-staters were from Washington," she said, adding there were some from British Columbia, too. "Last year we had no recorded Canadian participants, but had several from Australia."

Alaskan towns represented besides Petersburg were Fairbanks, Juneau, Sitka, Talkeetna, Wrangell, Craig, Haines and Valdez.
