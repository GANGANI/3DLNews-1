More gas exploration

Hilcorp plans wells at Kenai Peninsula's Cottonfield, Whiskey Gulch prospects

Kristen Nelson

Petroleum News

Hilcorp Alaska is continuing natural gas exploration at its southern Kenai Peninsula Cottonfield and Whiskey Gulch prospects, with one well planned at each for early 2024.

In both cases, if the wells are successful, Hilcorp said it will apply to the Alaska Department of Natural Resources' Division of Oil and Gas to form units and participating areas, prior to bringing the wells online.

The company has drilled stratigraphic test wells at both prospects, 12 at Whiskey Gulch in 2019 and 2020 and 10 at Cottonfield in 2022. In 2022 Hilcorp drilled two exploration wells at Whiskey Gulch.

Hilcorp applied to the Alaska Oil and Gas Conservation Commission on Nov. 7 for a spacing exception to drill the Cottonfield 6, which will be the first exploration well at that prospect, and on Nov. 14 for a spacing exception to drill the Whiskey Gulch 15.

Whiskey Gulch

Hilcorp said Whiskey Gulch 15 is a delineation well. Results from the two previous Whiskey Gulch explorations wells are confidential. Whiskey Gulch 1 was completed in 2021 and Whiskey Gulch 14 in 2022. The Whiskey Gulch field is directly north of Hilcorp's Seaview unit, some 2.5 miles north of Anchor Point on the southern Kenai Peninsula.

Hilcorp told the commission both of the earlier wells targeted "unproved oil and/or gas reserves in the Sterling, Beluga, and Tyonek Formations within the Whiskey Gulch Undefined Oil and Gas Pools. There was some gas found in each well but neither are commercially viable at this time."

The company said Whiskey Gulch 15 will be the third well it has drilled at the field and will target "unproven gas reserves in the Sterling, Beluga, and Tyonek Formations within the Whiskey Gulch Undefined Gas Pool."

A spud date of Jan. 15 is planned for the well, which will be drilled from Whiskey Gulch Pad No. 1. The surface location is on ADL 392666, in which Hilcorp has 100% working interest, in section 23, township 4 south, range 15 west, Seward Meridian, 1,496 feet from the south line and 1,605 feet from the east line. The bottomhole will be on a fee lease in which Hilcorp has 100% working interest, in 23-T4S-R15W, SM, 2,361 feet from the north line and 372 feet from the west line.

The well will be drilled to a total depth of 8,370 feet measured depth with the top of the productive horizon from 1,500 feet measured depth to total depth.

Hilcorp said that upon successful completion of WG 15, and prior to putting the well online, it will apply to DNR's Division of Oil and Gas to form the Whiskey Gulch unit and an initial participating area.

"Hilcorp understands there are complex ownerships involved in the Whiskey Gulch Prospect Area, and AOGCC is tasked with protecting all parties' correlative rights. In order to protect the correlative rights of all owners and landowners, Hilcorp will allocate production in accordance with DNR's instructions following successful completion and testing of the WG 15 well," the company said, with royalties allocated to leased owners/landowners based on tract allocation percentages, mineral ownership and lease royalty in accordance with DNR approvals.

Cottonfield

Cottonfield is the newer and more northerly of the two prospects. The well will be drilled from the Cottonfield pad in Happy Valley, Hilcorp told AOGCC, with drilling operations expected to begin Jan. 18. Hilcorp said Cottonfield 6 is some 7.25 miles north of Anchor Point. The surface location is on tract 001, in which Hilcorp has 100% working interest, in section 36, T3S, R15W, SM, 431 feet FNL and 2,454 feet FEL, with the bottomhole in the same section, township and range, also on a tract in which Hilcorp has 100% working interest, but at 493 feet FNL and 218 feet FEL. Cottonfield is on private fee land, not on state oil and gas leases.

Hilcorp said the well will be drilled to a total depth of 7,838 feet, with the measured depth and the top of the productive horizon from 1,500 feet measured depth to total depth, targeting unproven gas reserves in the Sterling, Beluga and Tyonek formations.

The company is applying for a spacing exception for the well because the target "cannot be reached by conforming to applicable statewide spacing restrictions," which require that testing and production not be closer than 1,500 feet from a property line where ownership changes.

Hilcorp said that following successful completion of Cottonfield 6, and prior to bringing the well online, it will apply to DNR's Division of Oil and Gas to form the Cottonfield unit and initial participating area.

The company said that to protect correlative rights of all owners and landowners, it "will allocate production in accordance with DNR's instructions following successful completion and testing of the Cottonfield 6 well," with royalties allocated to all leased owners/landowners based on tract allocation percentage, mineral ownership and lease royalty.
