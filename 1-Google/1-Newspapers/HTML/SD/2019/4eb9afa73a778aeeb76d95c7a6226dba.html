Sutton retires after a career spanning five decades

Pat Sutton, as seen in this family-provided photo, will retire today after 52 years working with the family business in Flandreau.
Posted Sunday, December 29, 2019 11:00 pm
Brenda Wade Schmidt

When Pat Sutton was growing up, she thought about becoming a hairdresser.
But her parents had other plans.
Sutton, the daughter of Anton “Tony” and Dorothy Kuhle, would join her husband Duane “Bud” Sutton in the Kuhle family’s auction business. It’s a choice that brought her 52 years of business experience, saw the expansion into insurance and real estate and included decades working with her sons, Chuck and Dan.
As of today, she is retired from the Kuhle-Sutton Agency. That’s not to say she might not come down and answer phones once in a while or fill in for the new office manager, Wendy Schutte. But it does give her permission at 83 to stay home and enjoy her time with family more.
“It’s just time. The biggest thing I’m going to miss is the people. I enjoyed my work because no day was the same,” she said. “I always found it exciting. It wasn’t anything you could get bored with.”
The agency and her family will honor her with a retirement reception from 3 to 6 p.m. Friday at the office at 127 W. Second Ave.
“She’s been a real cheerleader,” her son, Chuck, said. “As far as she was concerned, family was paramount and everything would fall into place.”
Sutton was a working mom before the title was even coined, raising Chuck of Flandreau, Teri Jaspers of Madison, Anita Ackerman who is deceased, Bill of Sioux Falls and Pam and Dan, who both live in Flandreau. She has 15 grandchildren and 31 great grandchildren.

She and Bud married in 1954 after she graduated from high school. While he was several years older than she, they had gotten to know each other hanging out at the Sugar Bowl, dancing at the VFW and riding horses.
As a young wife, she had stayed at home a few years raising some of the older children, but when her husband joined the business and it moved from her dad’s home to the current downtown location, she started working there half days in 1967. Shortly thereafter, she went fulltime when her husband became licensed and stared selling insurance, along with real estate.
Her husband died in 1985 when she was 49 years old. Her oldest son, Chuck, took over the auction and real estate part of the business and owns the Kuhle-Sutton Agency and Chuck Sutton Auctioneer and Land Broker businesses in Sioux Falls, along with recently-purchased Pipestone Realty. Her youngest son, Dan, sells real estate at the Kuhle-Sutton Agency and owns an insurance business there.
During her career, Pat Sutton has clerked auctions, helped with the farm and property management duties and assisted her husband in the property and casualty insurance business. She handled the bookkeeping and daily operations at the office.
Son Chuck worked with his dad and grandpa, grooming to lead the multi-generational business. “I told her you don’t have to worry. You’ve got a spot here as long as you want. I didn’t know she would take me up on it as long as she did, but she did,” he said. “I’ll still be surprised if she doesn’t show up once in a while …. just to be there.”
Her work was good for everyone – the business, the family, the clients and Pat herself. She has an historical knowledge of real estate in Flandreau, was able to keep client information private, helped balance the personalities of family members and was dependable, he said.
“It was a good release valve for her because she could be out,” Chuck said. “It was just a good mechanism for her to have to get up and get going in the morning and really to have a feeling that you had self-worth and you had responsibility.”
The job also was flexible, allowing her to attend her grandchildren’s events whenever she wanted.
While her children have grown and raised families of their own, the families still get together in for holidays and still love to sing, something their father also was good at, she said. “Music has always been a part of our life.”
At the office, his mom was really the boss, Dan Sutton said. She and his Grandma Kuhle kept things running while their husbands were out meeting with customers. “They were women ahead of their time,” he said. He has worked with his mom since 1998, after moving back to his hometown.
“She might like people to believe she was the assistant. She kept the show going and had the doors open,” he said. “She’s the silent hero behind the success of our business. If you ask anybody that, they would agree.”
His mother’s interaction with people is her biggest skill, Dan Sutton said.
“She just knew how important it was, about community and taking care of your clients. Anybody can sell insurance, and anybody can sell real estate, but it’s building those relationships,” he said. “Our clients became like friends and family. They were more than just a person. That was passed down from her dad.”
Pat Sutton did that all without turning on a computer, he said. “Strictly typewriter.”
She looks back fondly on what her career has meant.
“I just am so appreciative of the years I had, things that I enjoy, the people I met and the experiences. It was more than I could have imagined,” she said.
