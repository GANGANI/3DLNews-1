Fun For All At 23rd Harvest Days

Wed, 08/10/2022 - 12:00am

Britton’s 23rd Annual Harvest Days was a success again this year. Friday and Saturday morning’s rain put a little damper on a few events. The dive-in movie and Road Race were ultimately canceled, but most everything else went off well and partakers seemed to appreciate the mild and pleasant conditions most of the day on Saturday. Hillarey Holland, the Britton Chamber President, said they were pleased with how the weekend went off. Numbers were comparable to previous years she thought, or in some cases, better when it came to foot traffic at downtown retailers.

The weekend started off with the parade Friday evening. The theme was ‘Decades’ and folks decked out vehicles, floats, tractors and even horses to make for a festive affair.

The cornhole tournament moved to the fire hall that night because of the rain but was still a popular event. 47 teams competed. First place winners were Levi Christianson and Thomas Lee (Aberdeen). Second place was Kenny Johnson and Johnny German (Sisseton) and third place was Todd Shirley and Robb Krokel (Aberdeen).

On Saturday morning, the Lions Pancake Feed served 279 people and raised $2,074 for Lions projects. The volleyball tournament that morning was won by the team of Megan and Dustin Ochsner and Emily and MJ Hrabik. The Good Jones team won the jigsaw puzzle contest out of the nine teams that competed. The team made up of Marcey Jones, Madeline Carlson, Pam Burchill and Tracy Jones put together their puzzle in only 42 minutes and 13 seconds. Proceeds from the competition were donated to the Marshall County Food Pantry.

The7thAnnualRibCookoff was again a big hit. The event which also functions as fundraiser for Marshall County 4H Archery sold out of over 775 pounds of ribs in less than 40 minutes. Mama Tot’s team headed by Janelle Koster of Yankton and helped by her two kids Elaina and Boe took first place. This was her first year competing. Sweet Caroline’s BBQ who has been competing at Harvest Days for four years took second place as well as the People’s Choice Award. The team is a big family affair, led by Matthew Christopherson, Michael Christopherson, Nicole Biever, and Tyler Melroe. Three Guys Gettin’ Sauced, including Chad Wilkie and Kyle Hoeltzner took third place.

The talent showcase in the afternoon brought out some fun performances. The Danger Zone Band with members Jessica Moore, Travis Moore, Jake Zuehlke and Luke Zuehlke played. Singer Hailey Hoffman performed and there was a special guest appearance by Raina Stabnow, Kelly Burley and Ty Fagerland.

The brand new corn shoveling contest sponsored by Hurley & Associates and Full Circle Ag was quite popular. Over fifteen teams of two competed in kids and adults divisions. In the kids division, Trey and Colton Biel took first place. For the adults, Ryan Carlson and Erik Mundt won, shoveling a whopping 1,850 pounds of corn.

Dozens of vehicles lined the street later in the day for the car show. Winners included: Ed Jerde- Best Modern; Don Slotto- Best Ford; Mark Ellingson- Best Tractor; Mike Werner- Best Dodge; David Wade- Best Chevy; Mike Momb- Best Truck; Mike Forester-Best Hotrod; Terry Nelson- Best Car 1900-1962; Clyde Fredrickson- Best Car 1963-Present; Bob Ingvalson- Best Motorcycle; Mayors Pick- Jeff Long; Fire Fighters Pick- Jacob Vandervorst; Bartenders Pick- Mike Werner. All donations for the event went to the local food bank.

The streets of Britton were also rocking all day. The Weston Frank Band played during the late afternoon. Then the Tripwire Band performed as the main act for the street dance that night. Almost 800 people attended the event.

Other weekend activities included the kiddie train, inflatables, face painting and a craft show at the library, and the vendor and craft fair,

Warner said, “We were able to have such a fun schedule because of businesses, individuals, and organizations who hosted fun events!” She added that the volunteers throughout the weekend were crucial to Harvest Days success as well. See elsewhere in the paper for the full Chamber thank you.

Harvest Days in 2023 is scheduled for August 4-6.
