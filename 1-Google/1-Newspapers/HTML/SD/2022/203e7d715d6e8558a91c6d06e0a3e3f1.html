Binkley files for school board

Courtesy photo: Teresa Binkley
Posted Wednesday, February 2, 2022 7:18 am
Addison DeHaven, The Brookings Register

BROOKINGS – Former school board member and retired South Dakota State University assistant professor Teresa Binkley has filed her petitions for a seat on Brookings School District’s School Board.

Binkley has lived with her husband, Mark, in Brookings for 38 years. She graduated from South Dakota State University in 1988 with a degree in physics with teaching. She then earned her master’s degree from SDSU in 1997, in science teaching/chemistry, as well as Ph.D. in biology from SDSU in 2007.

As a South Dakota public school science teacher, Binkley taught for 10 years, including five at Brookings Middle School (1993-1998). She was then an SDSU assistant professor for 22 years, in human research studies for the Ethel Austin Martin Program in human nutrition. 

Binkley said she wants to serve on the school board because “I believe in public service and served on the Brookings School Board from 2000-2003. Now that I am retired, I have time to serve again.”

She said the greatest challenge in education today is finding qualified teachers to fill open positions. 

“There are just not enough teachers. Veteran teachers are retiring, and younger teachers are leaving the profession. We need to keep qualified teachers in our schools by allowing them to have a voice in decisions that affect their classroom. Teachers need to be recognized as professionals and supported by the administration,” Binkley said in an email to the Register.

Her goal as a board member is to understand the issues, ask pertinent questions, and justify the decisions made as a board. 

Binkley said there are a variety of issues she believes the district needs to address in its academic programs and offerings.

“Our district dropped some of the AP courses offered at the high school. I would like to see those reinstated. I am supportive of an alternative program for high school students, continuing the middle school team approach, and growing the MCL programs at the elementary level,” Binkley wrote.

“Board members need to justify their decisions with facts and data and be accountable to the community. My background in education and work in research are qualities that will benefit our school board,” Binkley added.

The Brookings School Board election is scheduled for April 12. The two open seats are currently held by Van Fishback and Mellissa Heermann, both with three-year terms. Heermann has previously announced she plans to seek a District 7 seat in the state House of Representatives.

School board candidates’ nominating petitions are due by 5 p.m. Feb. 25 at the Brookings School District business office in the administration building. Petitions may also be sent by registered mail by the 5 p.m. Feb. 25 deadline.
