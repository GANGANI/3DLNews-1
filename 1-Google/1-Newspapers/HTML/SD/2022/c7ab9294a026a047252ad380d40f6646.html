B-H Gymnasts Claim Title

Wed, 12/28/2022 - 12:00am

The Britton-Hecla varsity, junior varsity, and junior high gymnastics teams all had a chance to swing into action in meet in Aberdeen last week Tuesday.

Coach Angela Grupue’s varsity squad picked up its first win of the season, scoring 125.85 points to top Aberdeen and Sisseton. The JV team placed second and the junior high squad third. The local gymnasts are back in action on Thursday in the B-H Invitational set to start at 5p.m. That meet was rescheduled due to weather.

“This was a little bit of a practice meet after not having practice for six days due to weather, and the JV and junior high were finally able to get in their first competition,” noted Grupe.

Sterling Mertens was able to post her second vault statequalifying score, while Abby Lee hit the state-qualifying mark for the first time in vault. Gymnasts need three statequalifying efforts to earn a spot in the state meet.

Britton-Hecla swept the top four spots in the vault competition with Mertens taking the top spot. Mertens also won the uneven parallel bars.

“We are still in the transition stage of adding new skills, but this has been slowed down due to lack of practice,” said Grupe. “I would say the meet was okay and the varsity still pulled out a win, but I believe we have quite a bit of an upside left for this season.”
