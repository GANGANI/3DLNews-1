Flier girls storm past Cossacks

Posted Tuesday, February 8, 2022 12:40 pm

FLANDREAU – After falling for just the second time this season in the Big East Conference championship game a week and a half ago, the Flandreau girls’ basketball team bounced back with a 68-54 win over Sioux Valley on Friday night.
“The Big East Tournament…we tried to have a short term memory, but it stung,” said Flandreau head coach Megan Severtson. “It really did, and knowing that you were going to come back and play against again one of the top teams in our conference, against quite honestly a scary team with the amount of girls that can score, I knew it was important to put pieces back together, especially offensively, to get a little bit into a flow here as we end the year.”
Flandreau (10-2) jumped out a 11-4 lead and led 15-7 at the end of the first quarter. Sioux Valley (11-4) cut the lead to 22-19 midway through the second, but the Fliers closed the half on a 10-5 run to make it 32-24 at halftime.
The Fliers extended their lead to 44-30 in the third quarter and led 49-35 heading into the fourth. The Cossacks cut the lead down to 55-47 in the fourth, but Flandreau immediately pushed it back to double digits. The Fliers knocked down free throws down the stretch to put the game on ice.
Sioux Valley head coach Jamie Granum said even though his team lost, he liked the way they competed.
“We competed extremely hard. It might have been the hardest we’ve played all year. We were competing against college athletes on the floor. All five of those [starters] will play college athletics if they choose in one sport or another. So they put those athletes on the floor and we had to kind of jump the game up a little bit to keep it competitive. They’re really, really good. This is one of the best teams in Class A and we played them for about 28 minutes,” Granum said.

Flandreau shot 38.6% from the field and was 4-18 from three. The Fliers were 20-24 at the free throw line. Severtson said she liked the way her team played on offense.
“I thought we did a pretty good job [offensively]. I think that we practiced this week on being a little bit more patient offensively and waiting for a good shot. Moving without the basketball was really important for us tonight with them being in a zone. So I thought my girls did a really good job of trusting one another and knocking down open shots,” Severtson said.
Lizzie Pavlis had 21 points to lead the Fliers. Claire Sheppard had 15 points and nine rebounds. Maria Parsley had 13 points and eight rebounds.
Sioux Valley shot 39.6% from the field and were 10-29 from three. The Cossacks were led by Isabelle Bloker who had 15 points. Ashley Bjerke, Reagan Johnson and Julia Schneider each had 11 points.
Despite getting outrebounded 34-20, Granum said he liked the way his team fought on the glass.
“[My team] surprised me with how well we rebounded. We’ve been really going to work, rebounding out of a zone is that much harder, but we knew we had Sheppard played man-to-man, so we thought we could keep her off the glass. … Overall I thought we rebounded the ball well,” Granum said.
“We just proved and we just showed, against one of the best teams in Class A, we’re not the team of four or five years ago where we were going to come in and someone was just going to roll us over. Our kids compete, and our kids are a hard guard,” Granum said.
Flandreau is to play four games in the this week starting with a matchup at home against Madison on Monday, Feb. 7. The Fliers will then play three road games against De Smet, Castlewood and Clark/Willow Lake on Tuesday, Friday and Saturday. Severtson said this stretch will be a good test for her team.
“This upcoming week is going to be a pretty big test for our team. We have four games in six days, three of them are pretty far road games against nice teams too. So it’ll be a great test as the season winds down here, playing against a couple really good schools,” she said.
In the JV game, Flandreau defeated Sioux Valley, 49-33. Scoring leaders were Faith Wiese 18, Grace Krull 17.
