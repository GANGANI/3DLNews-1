Memories: made for a moment, not to dwell

Posted Friday, July 15, 2022 11:00 pm
Benjamin Chase of the Plainsman

“Toast to the ones here today
Toast to the ones that we lost on the way
‘Cause the drinks bring back all the memories
And the memories bring back, memories bring back you.”
“Memories” — Maroon 5

On Tuesday, Huron native Cheryl Ladd turned 71 years old. The former “Charlie’s Angels” actress certainly has stories around town if you bring her name up at one of the local watering holes.

Seeing as I’m younger than Ladd’s daughter, I don’t have any recollection of Cheryl as a Huronite, but I do have one tie to her and Huron - The Barn Restaurant.

Cheryl had worked at the restaurant, the story goes, and therefore, there was a display of memorabilia from her acting career that sat in a prominent place within the restaurant.

When Ladd’s name is mentioned, I almost immediately flash back to visiting with Grandpa and Grandma Chase near the organ after church before heading out to eat at The Barn Restaurant with my family for the Sunday buffet.

Memories of fried chicken and mashed potatoes quickly fill the nostrils and taste buds, and I can see that pink jacket clearly in my mind that used to seemingly tower over patrons as we each gathered our next plate from the buffet line.

The experience always ended with a Dum-Dum sucker (my favorite was green apple).

Often the comments on social media groups lament losing The Barn.

Discussion typically leads to a lack of quality family establishments to eat. Of course, there are nearly 20 sit-down restaurants in town, and that doesn’t count the dozen fast food places, sandwich shops, and coffee shops where someone can get a meal as well.

Many of those same groups lament a lack of available recreation for children in the community.

Yet, nearly every night of the week throughout the summer, one could find a baseball game to watch either on the Roger Kasa Little League Complex fields or at one or both of Memorial Stadium and Klock Field. Splash Central is one of the better waterparks one can find, and the Huron park system has multiple options available.

Often we get too hung up on what was and memories of those “good old days” and struggle to understand that while the world continued spinning and dramatic changes happened in science, technology, and so many other aspects of life, we were still living in the past.

Heck, I’m a huge baseball fan.

Baseball, probably as much or more than any other sport, is known to be a stickler to the traditions of the game. People can recite numbers from childhood idols without delay decades after that childhood is passed.

Baseball took 30 years to recognize Roger Maris as the single-season home run record holder after Maris broke Babe Ruth’s record in 1961. Ruth was an icon of the game, and people weren’t ready to have some good, but not iconic, player come along and “steal” his record, so until Commissioner Fay Vincent created a committee for statistical accuracy and pushed that Maris’ record be recognized as the true record in 1991, there were two records listed in record books.

Even today, fans who don’t want to consider the game “tainted” will deny that Barry Bonds is the current single-season and career home run record holder.

A coach in college told me, “you can lead a team forward by doing things no one else is doing yet and setting the standard, or you can lead the team backward by digging in your heels and refusing to change.”

That same coach challenged the team during a video session.

“How many of you were all-city, all-county, all-conference, all-state, even all-American in high school?” he asked the room after watching video of morning practice.

Nearly every hand in the room was up at some point during that question. Murmuring began to capture the room as stories of high school achievements came out from every corner.

“Now, how many of you have ever won a Big Ten championship?”

All hands quickly went down.

“That’s why we’re here,” he stated, plainly. “Leave the old moments behind, and let’s go create some new ones!”
