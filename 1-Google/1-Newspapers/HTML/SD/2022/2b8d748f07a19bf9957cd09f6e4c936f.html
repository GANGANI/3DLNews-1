Appel announces his run for Spink County Sheriff

Above, Isaac and Courtney Appel and their children Jadie, Tucker, Caleb and AlleRae Nelson. Appel is running for Spink County Sheriff.
Posted Thursday, April 7, 2022 10:17 am

Hello! My name is Isaac Appel and I am running for Spink County Sheriff.

My family has been in the Spink County area for over 125 years, and I appreciate the heritage and strength of this great region. I have been married to my wife Courtney Nelson-Appel for four years and I have four children: Jadie, Tucker, Caleb, and Alle Rae Nelson. My parents are Mark and Gini Appel. My grandparents are Roger Appel and the late Barb Appel.

I am running for Sheriff because of my desire to SERVE and PROTECT the people of Spink County. I was born in Redfield. I love our town. I have a strong desire to see it grow and flourish with positive law enforcement interaction. I have been out talking to individuals in the community, and it seems clear we need to have better county-wide and city-wide community interaction. We need improved communications and contact with all towns throughout Spink County. We need to set a better example of public service.

We have spent thousands of dollars of Spink County funds training Sheriff office staff who have wanted to serve our community, but were “forced” to find friendlier environments in other towns where they can flourish! Law Enforcement low retention and poor morale is a serious issue that is hurting us all.

We need to be working with and training Emergency Responders, EMS, Fire Departments, Emergency Management– the patriotic citizens who serve our population.

We need better collaboration with City and County officials to make Spink County a growing and attractive community to residents and businesses.

I’m offering a kinder, gentler approach to Law Enforcement. Law Enforcement that listens and cares about your safety and protection.

For instance, we need more timely investigations. Several individuals have approached me with concerns about calls made to the Sheriff's Office. These individuals feel they are not taken seriously. I want Spink County to be confident their concerns will be treated with the utmost respect!

I believe we need to start publishing the entire Sheriff’s Log (Magistrate Court report) in the newspaper again.

I believe in commitment to the people of Spink County, positive community interaction with law enforcement, and common sense policing.

I believe we must hold those responsible for harming our most vulnerable people in our community. We need a renewed focus on family violence and the drug trade that is hurting so many of our citizens.

My experience:

-6 years Certified Law Enforcement Officer

-4 years Spink County Deputy

-5 years Family Crisis Center Board Member

-Police Handgun Instructor

-2 stripe white belt Brazilian Jiu Jitsu/Intermediate No-Gi BJJ

Growing up my parents instilled a strong work ethic in my siblings and me. They taught us to serve God, respect our elders, and serve generously and with humility. One of my passions is to serve others.

I served as Spink County Deputy Sheriff a short three years ago. I loved my “job” as a Deputy as it allowed me to meet people from all walks of life. I had the opportunity to work with others not just from a law enforcement standpoint, but also a moral standpoint, as I aimed to make a difference in people’s lives. To me, law enforcement is more than just enforcing the laws of this great country. It’s about making a lasting, positive impact on our community.

1 John 3: 16-18 says,

“This is how we know what love is: Jesus Christ laid down his life for us. And we ought to lay down our lives for our brothers and sisters. If anyone has material possessions and sees a brother or sister in need but has no pity on them, how can the love of God be in that person? Dear, children, let us not love with WORDS or SPEECH but with ACTIONS and in TRUTH.”

Friends, as a candidate campaigning to be your next Spink County Sheriff, it is my desire to serve Spink County not just with words, but with the actions I take, and to lead as an example in the department.

Please allow me the honor of serving you and our community. Vote for me, Isaac Appel, in the Primary Election on June 7th!
