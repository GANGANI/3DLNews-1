‘It’s important to give’

Local philanthropists:

Lani and Scott Ramsdell of Flandreau were named the Outstanding Philanthropists for South Dakota by the state chapter of the Association of Fundraising Professionals.
Posted Tuesday, November 24, 2020 10:24 am
Brenda Wade Schmidt

When Scott Ramsdell was young, he remembers his family’s generosity, even when they didn’t have much themselves.
His father was part of the Flandreau booster club and gave of his time and financial support, although he struggled himself, especially after his business burned. Ramsdell’s grandmother was always giving, too, he said.
That provided the examples that he worked to follow.
“To support your community is very important,” he said. “No matter who you are in our community, it’s important to give.”
Ramsdell, 68, and his wife, Lani, 66, were named this month as the Outstanding Philanthropists for South Dakota by the state chapter of the Association of Fundraising Professionals.
The couple has been supporters of projects in the community and beyond, both large and small. They support the Boys and Girls Club of Moody County, the Moody County Museum, Helping Kids Round First, Feeding South Dakota and other projects.

Scott Ramsdell serves on the board of directors at the corporate level for the Boys and Girls Club of Brookings, Moody County & Yankton and has helped with local hands-on projects, including paying for milk for kids to have after school with their snack.
Bekah Bambas, executive director at the Moody County facility, said he also heads up the pancake feed fundraiser each year.
“We appreciate all the support that we get from Scott. He truly believes in our mission and is a great advocate for the Moody County club, while on the corporate board and out in the community,” she said.
The Ramsdells, through Dakota Layers, also have donated millions of dozens of eggs to Feeding South Dakota, a state-wide program to provide nutrition to hungry people through food banks and on-site distributions.
The Ramsdells grew up in Flandreau and supported the community when they were starting out in business, mostly with their time dedicated to organizations and projects.
“Giving of your time is probably more important than giving of your money,” he said. “There are so many needs.”
In the early years, they were active in the local Jaycees, for example.
“We’re committed to our community – our whole lives – both of us,” he said. “We give of our time and effort, and we’ve taught our children to do the same thing.”
All four of the couple’s children live in the Flandreau area, and are either owners or investors in businesses, including Ramsdells F & M, J.C. Ramsdell Enviro Services and Dakota Layers, a large egg production agriculture business northeast of Flandreau.
Ramsdell said he was surprised by the honor of recognizing their gifts to the community, something that is just a part of who they are.
“We just don’t make it a big deal,” he said.
Other awards from the Association of Fundraising Professionals went to Brookings for the outstanding philanthropic community, Black Hills Energy for the outstanding philanthropic corporation, Kathy Hammond of Sioux Falls as outstanding volunteer fundraiser, Pi Kappa Alpha fraternity at the University of South Dakota for outstanding youth in philanthropy and Connie Olson of Rapid City for outstanding fundraising professional.
