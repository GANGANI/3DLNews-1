Two complete cadet program

By Dan Bechtold

Editor

Alyssa Williams and Nicole Farley are two recent Winner High School graduates who have completed the Tripp County Ambulance cadet program.

Both of them learned so much from this program and it will be a great help as they pursue a career in nursing.

In fact, Williams has received her EMT certification through this program and Farley is in the process of filling out the application to take the EMT training.

Williams says she has learned a lot of how the emergency system works and how all work as a team to communicate and help people.

When she was living in North Dakota, Williams did  an EMT ridealong in Bismarck, and that is where she saw how much she enjoyed it.

When the family moved to Winner this year, Williams talked to WHS guidance counselor Val Ewing who told her about the cadet program. Williams also talked to Paul Schueth and Kathie Cole who work with the cadets.

Willams, whose father is the CEO of Winner Regional Health, says she loves medicine. “I love how you are able to help people when they are terrified and they do not know what to do and you can be that person who shows up for them.”

Williams plans to attend Brigham Young University in Provo, Utah and major in nursing and then go on and  be a  nurse practitioner.

Farley said she has always wanted to go into nursing. She felt being in the cadets and being on call would help her get a foot in the door to a nursing program.

She plans to move to Rapid City and attend Western Dakota to get a nursing degree. She hopes to move back and be an EMT.

The cadet program is open to juniors and seniors at Winner High School and Colome High School. To learn more about the program students can talk to their guidance counselor or

Paul Schueth, Sissy Orel or Kathie Cole.

Both Williams and Farley said they would recommend the cadet program to other students. “Especially if you are going into nursing I would recommend it because you learn a lot,” said Williams.

The future looks bright for two talented young women who want to go out into the world and help people.
