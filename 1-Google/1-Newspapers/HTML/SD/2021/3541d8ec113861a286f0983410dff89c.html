Huron teams compete in ESD meet

PHOTO BY JENNA ORTIZ/AMERICAN NEWS Huron sprinter Max Kranzler hands off to teammate Tyson Lien in the 4x200 meter relay at the ESD Championships at Swisher Activities Complex.
Posted Sunday, May 16, 2021 11:00 pm
Plainsman staff

ABERDEEN — The Huron boys’ and girls’ track teams both finished ninth at the Eastern South Dakota Conference Meet on Saturday.

Aberdeen Central claimed the boys’ title with 147.5 points, while Brandon Valley was second at 136. Harrisburg was third at 103.5, followed by Watertown at 95.5 and Brookings at 77.

Brandon Valley amassed 217.5 points to win the girls’ title. Brookings was second at 107, while Aberdeen Central was third at 97.5. Yankton and Watertown shared the fourth spot with scores of 76,

The Huron boys finished at 17.5, while the Huron girls had 15.

Lexy Gilbert earned All-ESD honors for the Tigers with a third-place finish in the 100-meter dash at 12.78 seconds.

Brynn Gose was fifth for the Huron girls in the long jump at 15 feet, 5.75 inches.

Jayden Beck was fifth for the Huron boys in the 400 at 52.39, while Quinston Luellman was eighth in the 100 at 11.61.

Isaiah Decker and Tanner Marone claimed spots No. 3 and 7, respectively, in the boys’ javelin with throws of 127-4 and 122-3.

The Huron boys were fifth in the 800 relay and 1,600 relay events, while finishing sixth in the medley relay and seventh in the 3,200 relay.

The Huron girls were seventh in the 400 and 3,200 relays, while taking eighth in the medley relay.

The Tigers will close the regular season with the Mark Wendelgass Relays on Thursday at Tiger Stadium.
