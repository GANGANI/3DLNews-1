Update on Commissioner Brian Heinecke’s court case

Published by admin on Fri, 04/12/2024 - 9:03am

Roberts County Commissioner Brian Heinecke appeared in court at the Roberts County Courthouse in Sisseton on Thursday, Apr. 4, at 10:00 a.m. before the Hon. Marshall Lovrien, Circuit Court Judge. The result was that all charges were dismissed except for charge 3, which was for Contributing to Abuse, Neglect or Delinquency. Heinecke pled No Contest to that charge, which means he doesn’t admit to guilt but does not want to fight the charge.

The value of a county jail in Roberts County

Published by admin on Fri, 03/29/2024 - 8:58am

by Mimi Larsen, Staff Writer When Roberts County built its jail in 2006, it avoided the plight of several counties around the state that were forced to close their jails. They now house their prisoners in counties with modern jails like Roberts County’s, spending many thousands of dollars in transportation costs, and forcing county residents to travel far to see family or loved ones.

The $7 million 20-year bond for the jail will be paid off in 2025. The project began with the ground breaking in October of 2005, and was completed in 2007.
