Did transfer hit Target? Brookings land near Interstate 29 finally heading toward development

Big economic news is expected in the months ahead following the transfer of city land east of Interstate 29 and north of Sixth Street to the Brookings Economic Development Corp. (Josh Linehan/Brookings Register)
Posted Sunday, March 5, 2023 10:00 am
Mondell Keck, The Brookings Register

BROOKINGS — A land transfer approved by the Brookings City Council on Tuesday night could herald big economic news for the city in the months ahead.

“I for one am really glad this finally happened. We’ve had this property for quite a few years — pretty much a decade. We’re finally going to get something to happen,” Mayor Oepke “Ope” Niemeyer said. “I’m really happy that we held onto it and I think the public is going to be very extremely happy with what we got coming. We can’t say anything, so don’t try to get it out of us — I’m just telling you that right now.”

The 18.53 acres involves land north of Sixth Street and just east of Interstate 29 and is commonly known as the Marketplace. It’s been in the city’s possession for a number of years but, with a 7-0 vote from the City Council, it will be transferred to the Brookings Economic Development Corp. for approximately $1,130,000.

“The city of Brookings was approached by Ryan Companies of Minnesota to purchase 18.53 acres for retail and commercial development,” City Manager Paul Briseno said. “Ryan Companies design, develop, finance, manage and construct projects with their many national retail partners.”

According to a City Council memo, the city has worked with Ryan Companies, which is based out of Minneapolis, over the past year to solidify commercial development, including retail and other businesses, located east of I-29 and north of Sixth Street. To help with this development, the BEDC sought the purchase of the land.

The developer has an aggressive development strategy “(and) … will work to bring a desired anchor tenant and commercial which complement the needs of the City of Brookings.”

Longstanding and informed speculation has held that a Target store would be built on the parcel, which the city has held since 2013 with an eye toward retail development. Target was established in 1962 in Minneapolis, and its headquarters remain there.

The Register reached out to Target on Wednesday for comment. “We are continually evaluating potential store locations to serve new guests, but (we) don’t have any new-store news to share at this time,” a spokesperson for the company wrote in a statement.

In other business on Tuesday night, the City Council:

• Gave the OK, on a 7-0 vote, to transfer 11.35 acres of land to the BEDC for $247,203. The development corporation has, per a City Council memo, “worked with a developer over the past 10 months to solidify a business to be located in the Freeland Addition, which is located east of 32nd Avenue and north of (the U.S.) Highway 14 Bypass.”

Briseno wrote in an email that, “It is an innovative ag business that works to expand existing businesses and partner with efforts from the university.”

• Said “yes” to “No Mow May” on a 7-0 vote. The program — whose goal is to help raise awareness for pollinators, such as bees, in urban landscapes by encouraging temporarily ceasing mowing lawns in May — is entirely optional for residents in Brookings to participate in.

“Spring is a crucial time of year for pollinators as they emerge from the soil and look for habitat and food,” according to a City Council memo. “By not mowing in … May, people can protect and provide these beneficial resources for emerging pollinators.”

It’s a national campaign advocated by Bee City USA. Yard signs will be available for free to program participants at the Brookings City & County Center in Suite 230. Once May wraps up, people will have until June 7 to get their lawns back into compliance, which is a height limitation of 15 inches.

• Gave emergency approval, via a 7-0 vote, for the purchase of an Altec boom truck for an estimated $204,756. Such boom trucks are most commonly used in forestry operations — specifically, in clearing, trimming and maintaining city trees.

The current boom truck, a 2009 model, was out of service most of last year due to maintenance and safety issues, including failing a biannual safety inspection.

Deputy City Manager Jacob Meshke noted that the trucks generally last between 10 and 20 years, and are essential pieces of equipment, especially during severe weather.

• Voted 7-0 to give a green light to amending the city’s parking ordinances with regard to minimum required parking spaces throughout Brookings. The essential details, which affect a broad swath of uses, can be found in a PDF at https://bit.ly/3ENAZ5f.

• Heard a report from a representative of the SDSU Student Association that touched on electric scooters on campus and House Bill 1079, which would allocate $2 million for mental health and suicide prevention resources for youth and young adults.

The bill is currently in the hands of the South Dakota Senate after passing the state House on a 56-13 vote.

• Approved an agreement, on a 7-0 vote, with NorthWestern Energy to furnish and sell natural gas to the city and its residents.

The next City Council meeting is slated for 6 p.m. March 14 at the Brookings City & County Center.
