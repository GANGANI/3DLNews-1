Harkless checks off senior goals

A proud Harkless family poses together following the awards ceremony at last week’s State Class “B” Championships in Rapid City. The youngest son Marcus is not pictured, but despite missing the photo opportunity, he did however show up shortly afterwards with ice cream in hand and in “all smiles,” much like the rest of his proud family.

By Brett Nachtigall

Closes high school career with 160-pound state title

HOT SPRINGS – Entering this year’s high school wrestling season, Hot Springs senior Brady Harkless set three goals for himself.

No. 1 – Extend his matches long enough to score at least six points in each one … Check.

No. 2 – Win a state championship at 160 pounds … Check.

And, No. 3 – “Go out with a bang” … Big check.

Prior to last month’s State Class “B” Wrestling Tournament in Rapid City, Harkless said his intentions to “go out with a bang,” simply meant “having fun and just throwing everything out there that I can.”

Little did he know at that time, however, with just how much of “bang” he would exit his final high school match, when, in the second period of double-overtime, he earned a fast five points with a reversal and near-fall in the final seconds to break a 1-1 tie and secure a 6-1 decision over Tyce Meyer of Winner.

Once the referee blew the whistle to signal the end of the match – and his first-ever state high school title – Harkless exploded to his feet and began bounding around the mat in a moment of pure joy and excitement.

Returning to the center of the mat, he raised both arms in victory and then bent over and put his face in his hands to express his near disbelief of what just happened.

He did it. He’s a state champion … Bang!

Harkless finished his championship season with a 28-2 record overall, and while he was considered the top-ranked 160-pound Class “B” wrestler in the state throughout the entire season, the year did not go without its challenges.

Undefeated through the first seven weeks of the season, Harkless was on his way to another tournament title at the Chadron Invitational when he sustained both a dislocated shoulder and a dislocated collar-bone. The injury forced him to default from the tournament and suffer his first loss of the season. It also sidelined him for the remaining three weeks of the regular season.

He said the injury caused him to lose some interest in the season and his goals of attaining a state championship.

“I wasn’t sure if I would be able to come back and be as capable as I was before,” he said. “I thought it might give others an advantage over me.”

He earned a win in his first match back from the injury, which came in the first round of the district tournament. But the pain was still there and presented enough of a distraction that it forced him into another injury default versus rival Clayton Wahlstrom of Custer in the district championship.

Once at the Region 4B tournament, Harkless said he felt more himself and returned to his dominating style of earlier in the season to win the region title, including an 11-5 decision in the finals over Wahlstrom – a wrestler who Harkless said presented him his biggest challenges of the regular season.

At the state tournament, Harkless had little trouble in his first three matches, first winning by a 9-2 decision, then a 1:39 pin, followed by an 8-2 decision over last year’s 145-pound state champion Derek Dickerson of Wagner – a wrestler who Harkless had defeated this past summer by a score of 1-0 during wrestling camp at South Dakota State University.

In the state championship match against Meyer, Harkless said with a smile, “he was like wrestling Derrik!” – referring to the size and strength of his friend and former Hot Springs wrestler Derrik Walton, who won the 285-pound state title last season.

Feeling slightly outmatched in strength, Harkless said he knew he had to rely more on his quickness and technique to earn the victory. “I had to just keep shooting and hoping to catch him off guard,” he said.

Still tied 1-1 at the end of the first period of the second overtime, Harkless needed only to earn a one-point escape for the victory. With just over 10 seconds left, he made his move.

“I knew how much time was left and it was now or never,” Harkless said. Using an “inside roll” that he had been working on in situational practice sessions throughout the season, he not only got the escape but also a reversal and a near-fall.

“I was just trying to get out and try to get an easy escape, but instead he ended up on his back,” Harkless said. “I think it surprised him. I couldn’t believe it at first either.”

Despite winning only one match his first season of AAU wrestling at the age of six, success on the mat did come early and often for Harkless.

During his time on the Hot Springs AAU wrestling team, Harkless won a pair of state titles, including his first one at the age of 9 in 2003 and then again at age 11 in 2005. He was also a gold medalist in the 2008 Middle School Duals.

After joinging the high school team as a seventh-grader, during the 2006-07 season, he became the youngest Hot Springs wrestler to win a match at the State Class “A” Tournament while competing at 119 pounds. He followed up that season with a fourth-place finish in the state at 125 pounds as just an eighth-grader.

Harkless recalls that season as a difficult one, cutting weight from 140 pounds and being forced to lose six pounds the night before the finals. “I had to run for six hours the night before and lost one pound per hour to make weight.” It would be the last season however that he would need to cut weight.

His freshman year became a turning point for Harkless when made it all the way to the 145-pound state championship finals, only to lose by decision to James Wilson of Rapid City Central.

“That was the first year that I got that feeling in my stomach that I could do it,” he said. “I finished fourth at region and wasn’t predicted to place above the top-six that year, so I had to hustle to get there.”

That season was also the year teammate Cole Romey won his second state title at 135 pounds and was the school’s final year in Class “A”.

Earning his own state title however continued to elude him throughout his sophomore and junior years, as he finished fifth at 145 pounds in 2010 and fourth at 152 pounds in 2011. Team success however was his focus during those two seasons as the Bison won the State “B” Dual Tournament his sophomore year and placed second in the State Class “B” Tournament his junior year.

Harkless said he credits his hard work in the off-season for his success on the mat, as well as all his coaches who have helped him along the way, including former coaches Mark Rude and Jonas Weiss, as well as current coach Ray Ringstmeyer.

He said his favorite thing about the sport of wrestling is its “family atmosphere.” And when it comes to wrestling, there is likely no other family that is going to leave its mark on Hot Springs wrestling in the years to come than the Harkless family.

In addition to his older brother Matt who also wrestled and graduated in 2010, Brady this year competed on the mat with younger brothers Justin (sophomore), Jared (freshman) and Brice (seventh-grader). And behind them, awaits two more brothers including Morgan (fifth-grade) and Marcus (third-grade). All five younger brothers wrestle, and among them, total seven individual state AAU titles of their own, including one for Justin (2006), one for Jared (2005), three for Brice (2006, 07, 08) and two for Marcus (2009, 11).
