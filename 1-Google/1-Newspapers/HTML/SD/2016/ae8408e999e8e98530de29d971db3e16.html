Bills' Rex Ryan backpedals on Trump after controversy

Leo Roth   | @leoroth

ORCHARD PARK – Rex Ryan’s support of Republican presidential nominee Donald Trump has the Buffalo Bills coach backpedaling better than his defensive backs these days.

Quizzed for the second time in three days about a 2005 recording in which Trump describes how his celebrity allows him to lewdly treat women, Ryan once again punted.

“I still haven’t seen the video and I don’t care to,’’ Ryan said Wednesday during his news conference at New Era Field. “I’m watching plenty of videos on the (San Francisco) 49ers.’’

Trump dismissed his comments made to then-host Billy Bush of Access Hollywood as "locker room talk." When Ryan was asked if he has ever heard players or coaches talk about women the way Trump did in NFL locker rooms he was hesitant to respond.

“I don’t know. I guess I wasn’t in that locker room so I don’t know,’’ Ryan said.

Earlier in the week, Ryan said he hadn’t seen any of the presidential debates or followed any news regarding the race between Trump and Hillary Clinton.

“My thing is, I’m under a rock,’’ he said. “I’m focused on whoever we’re playing and that’s it. So I don’t … know a whole lot about politics or anything else. Right now, I’m strictly a football coach.’’

Back in April, however, Ryan was very much in the political arena as a Trump super fan.

He agreed to introduce the New York billionaire to the crowd during a rally at Buffalo’s First Niagara (now Key Bank) Center. That day he said there were many things he admired about Trump, especially that he has the courage to speak his mind.

Some of Trump’s remarks on the campaign trail have also been racially, ethnically and religiously charged.

According to the Bleacher Report, seeing their coach's public support of Trump didn’t sit well with some of his African-American players. Two were quoted anonymously.

“Rex is such an open-minded guy, a really good person,” one said. “But the fact he could back someone as closed-minded as Trump genuinely shocked me.”

Two African-American players that spoke on the record with the Democrat and Chronicle said they supported Ryan’s right to vote for whomever he wished.

“I mean, that’s Rex’s prerogative,’’ linebacker Lorenzo Alexander said. “There are other guys in here who support Trump as well. Whether or not you agree really doesn’t have an effect on my working with you as a teammate and trying to get a ‘W’ each and every week. If that’s who Rex wants to endorse, that’s fine. I know Rex as a person, people hitting me up on Twitter, ‘How can your coach support a racist?’ But I know Rex.  He’s very down to earth and very acceptable of people, so I have no issue.’’

Defensive end Corbin Bryant also said he respects his coach’s right to choose a candidate.

“He can vote for who he wants, this is America,’’ Bryant said. “For me to say, ‘You can’t vote for Trump because he’s this, he’s that,’ that makes me look like a bad person. We can vote for who we want and leave it at that. At the end of the day, we are still going to be here playing football and living our lives and that’s the end of that.’’

Guard Richie Incognito, a white player notorious for a bullying scandal with racial overtones while playing for the Miami Dolphins, is also an outspoken Trump supporter. He has worn a “Make America Great Again’’ hat.

“I still support him, yes,’’ Incognito said tersely. “He’s my pick, he’s my candidate, and that’s it.’’

Ryan and Incognito actually did a lot better handling questions about Trump than did New England Patriots quarterback Tom Brady, another member of the NFL who has made his political leaning well known.

“If you have kids of your own, how would you respond if your kids heard Donald Trump's version of 'locker room talk'?” a reporter asked Brady on Wednesday.

Brady smiled, said “Thank you guys, have a good day,’’ then walked out of his news conference.
