Ubly mourns death of student


By ohtadmin
on October 02, 2014

By John Bonke
Staff Writer • 810-452-2668 • jbonke@mihomepaper.com

UBLY — “He always had a smile for you,” Ubly Superintendent Rocky Aldrich said of a 16-year-old student who died Friday in an ATV accident.

Brandon Dickert, 16, of Harbor Beach, was a passenger in on an ATV that rolled and pinned him underneath. His friend, Bailey Bischer, 16, of Ubly, who was driving the vehicle, escaped serious injury.

Ubly Community Schools were closed Wednesday so students and faculty could attend Dickert’s funeral Mass, which took place at Our Lady of Lake Huron Catholic Church in Harbor Beach.

“Brandon will be greatly, greatly missed,” Aldrich said.

Soon after the news of Dickert’s death spread throughout the Ubly community, friends shared their grief on Facebook.

“When I got the news today, I didn’t’ know what to say. So I just hung up the phone. I took a walk to clear my head, and this is where the walkin’ led. I can’t believe you’re really gone, don’t feel like goin’ home. Brandon Dickert wish I coulda hung out with ya more, we shared some good times,” Justin Messing wrote.

William Halifax shared about Dickert’s love of trucks by posting a video that featured a revving engine and flying dirt.

“This one goes up to you Brandon. You loved a good time in our trucks. I can picture you in my passenger seat right now with a smile on your face as I do this. We talked everyday about when we’re gonna get your truck straight piped, we can still get your truck straight piped in heaven when I meet you there,” Halifax wrote.

Macanna Briolat reflected about Dickert’s smile and sense of humor.

“I feel lucky to have even known you and appreciate all your jokes and laughter. We love you and will always remember you. Keep ‘em laughing upstairs bud, love you and rest in peace,” she wrote.

According to a news release from the Village of Ubly Police Department, on Friday, Sept. 26, at approximately 4:45 p.m., the Ubly Police Department was dispatched to an ATV roll-over accident on private property located south of Munford Road and east of M-19.

An Ubly resident, Bischer, 16, was driving the ATV and lost control causing the ATV to flip over. Bischer was able to crawl out of the ATV, but Dickert, of Harbor Beach, was pinned underneath. Bischer was unable to upright the ATV and immediately called 9-1-1.

Ubly Police and Bingham Township Fire Department arrived on scene and were able to upright the ATV. Bingham Township Ambulance arrived on scene and began treating Dickert for his injuries. Dickert was transported to Huron Medical Center where he passed away from his injuries. Bischer was transported to Huron Medical Center and treated for his injuries. He was later released.

Huron County Sheriff’s Department’s Accident Investigation Team were dispatched to assist with the investigation. Russ’s Towing was called and removed the ATV from the scene.

Neither Bischer nor Dickert were wearing seatbelts or helmets. Alcohol and or drugs were not involved.

Dickert attended Ubly Community Schools where teachers met before the start of classes Monday to learn the information, according to Superintendent Rocky Aldrich. They told the other students at the beginning of the first class what had happened and said that counselors were available. Students also were allowed to walk in the hallways or gather informally and some did so in small groups.

Aldrich said that these counselors “know the kids” and they were available throughout the day. Although the week’s athletic activities were left as scheduled, Homecoming activities, planned for Sept. 29 through Oct. 3 were postponed until the week of Oct. 20-24.

Huron County View Editor Kelly Krager contributed to this report.
