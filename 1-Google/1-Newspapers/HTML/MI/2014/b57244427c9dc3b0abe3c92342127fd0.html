Softball players have Friday night league of their own

  • By David Johnson
    News Editor
  • Oct 1, 2014
  • Oct 1, 2014 Updated Dec 9, 2016

THREE OAKS — Friday nights from late summer through September weren’t just for football anymore.

An adult co-ed softball league played its games that evening at the Post 204 Legion Field in Three Oaks.

“We all just come down here and have four games a night,” said Kelly McCarty, one of the league’s organizers and a member of the Art FX squad. “It’s fun, something to do.”

Other teams competing on Friday nights through Sept. 26 were the Featherbone group, the J&J Construction team and the Light Harbor Rentals/Sawyer Hardware and Lumber squad.

“It’s a beautiful night for softball in Three Oaks!” exclaimed center fielder David Sokolowski prior to a Sept. 19 game.

The league started in late July.

McCarty said players range in age from just out of high school (or in the case of substitute player RyLee Haughey, still in high school), to well past their school days (pitcher Brad Ponegalek was pointed to as an example).
