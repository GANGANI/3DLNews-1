A new chapter; Laingsburg's Greg Mitchell named men's basketball coach at Hope College

  • By JEROME MURPHY Argus-Press Sports Writer
  • Jul 5, 2014
  • Jul 5, 2014
  • 1
  • Facebook
  • Twitter
  • WhatsApp
  • SMS
  • Email
  • Facebook
  • Twitter

  • Save

LAINGSBURG — Greg Mitchell, who has coached varsity basketball at Laingsburg the past 25 years with a record of 362-201, became the new men’s head coach at his alma mater, Hope College in Holland, on Thursday.

Mitchell, 48, who led Laingsburg to a state runner-up finish in 2013, played shooting guard for the Flying Dutchmen for four years before graduating in 1989. He is the 11th head coach at Hope and will succeed former Flying Dutchmen coach Matt Neil, who stepped down in May after leading Hope to a four-year record of 88-26.

“I signed the contract today at about noon,” Mitchell said by telephone Thursday. “I played there and I always think it’s special for a  coach to be able to come back and coach at his alma mater. Anybody who knows anything about Hope College knows what kind of place it is — the academic excellence and the athletic tradition is incredible. I’m very blessed and I’m very excited.”

Mitchell played on two MIAA championship teams at Hope and the team went 74-27 during his four seasons. Mitchell holds the school record for career 3-point shooting (46.4 percent). He averaged 10.3 points per game as a senior.

In almost the same breath, Mitchell said Thursday it was hard even applying for the position since it would mean leaving Laingsburg, which he has been a part of for a quarter of a century.

“It’s a great opportunity but with that being said, I’m going to miss everything that has been Laingsburg for us,” Mitchell said. “Great loyal players, family, staff members, all the way up and down the administration. I was very torn just to apply. This has been a great place for me.”

Mitchell, the dean of area boys’ high school basketball coaches, has taught at Laingsburg for 23 years — 11 years of English and 12 years of physical education.

He and his wife Dina, a who also graduated from Okemos in 1984, have four children. They include three daughters  — Aleah, 21; Quincie, a 2013 graduate of Laingsburg; and Jalynn, a senior at Laingsburg.

They also have a son, Bryson, who will be a high school sophomore.

Mitchell led Laingsburg to the Class C state title game in 2013. The Wolfpack ended up being denied on its final possession in the final seconds while falling 40-39 to No. 1-ranked Flint Beecher. Laingsburg crafted a 24-3 record. Mitchell was named Class C State Coach of the Year.

Under Mitchell, Laingsburg has won three regional titles (1997, 2002, 2013), six districts (1997, 2001, 2002, 2004, 2011, 2013) and six league titles (1997, 2000, 2001, 2004, 2006 and 2013). The Wolfpack produced winning seasons in 14 of its last 15 campaigns under Mitchell.

Jake Zielinski, an All-State guard as a senior on that Wolfpack state runner-up team, said Mitchell was due for a shot at the college level.

“I feel really, really fortunate to have had him for four years and I think he’s about due that he gets his college exposure,” Zielinski said. “What stands out the most about coach is his relationships and connections with the players. You always felt that what you were doing was really important, your role on the team. He’s just a wonderful dude and he makes you feel good about yourself in different aspects of life. It definitely goes beyond even basketball. He’s taught me so much and I could come to him for anything. He always had the right answer.”

Zielinski, who is enrolled at Olivet College, is hoping he will get a chance to play against Mitchell at the collegiate level along with two other former Laingsburg players.

“I’m going to Olivet — so I’ll actually play him,” Zielinski said. “So that will be interesting. Shaun (McKinney), Zach (Walker) and myself are over there.”

Luke Pohl, the head boys basketball coach at Pewamo-Westphalia, has been a Central Michigan Athletic Conference archrival and close friend of Mitchell’s for several years. Pohl said it was an emotional time for him after learning of the news that Mitchell would be leaving Laingsburg.

“That’s the thing, I’ve gone against him for years — in a way, it’s kind of sad,” Pohl said. “It’s the end of an era also. And I’ve just got so much respect for him. He’s a poster child for what a high school basketball coach should be. Because he handles himself so well, he’s such a professional and he’s a tremendous competitive coach and just a great friend also. I’m kind of a little bit emotional almost.”

Ironically, Mitchell’s last game as Laingsburg’s head coach came March 7 during the Class C district championship game vs. P-W at Laingsburg. The No. 10-ranked Pirates won 31-12, a typical low-scoring game between the two rivals. Laingsburg finished 12-9 last winter while returning just one starter from the state runner-up squad.

Mitchell was a sophomore and freshman member of two state title teams at Okemos High School (in 1981 and 1982) under head coach Stan Stolz, who later told Mitchell of a physical education job opening at Laingsburg.

Mitchell’s first year as the Laingsburg boys varsity basketball coach was 1989-1990. Now, he’ll get a chance to return to Holland and coach at the same college he played at — a chance he just couldn’t pass up.

“There’s no question I was passionate about coaching at Laingsburg and everything there is here — but with the roots that I have at Hope and having a chance to go back there and coach and live is very, very exciting for me and for my family,” Mitchell said. “We’re going to give our kids a chance to go to school there, which is a tremendous perk — to give them a chance to experience the whole Hope experience, which I had.”
