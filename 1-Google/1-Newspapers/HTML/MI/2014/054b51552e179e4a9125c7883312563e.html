Judge Audrey Van Alst sworn into 84th District Court

  • By Jeff Broddle Cadillac News
  • Nov 2, 2013
  • Nov 2, 2013 Updated Oct 12, 2017
  • Facebook
  • Twitter
  • WhatsApp
  • SMS
  • Email
  • Facebook
  • Twitter
  • WhatsApp
  • SMS
  • Email
  • Print
  • Save

CADILLAC — Standing in front of an elbow-to-elbow crowd in Wexford County’s 84th District courtroom, Judge Audrey Van Alst recalled how early on in her legal career she learned the importance of mentoring. As she advanced, Van Alst said she made a point to mentor other attorneys with whom she came in contact.

Van Alst’s experience served her well, given she was appointed by Gov. Rick Snyder as 84th District Court Judge in Wexford County. An investiture ceremony, attended by many of her own mentors, and those she mentored herself, was held Friday at the Wexford County Courthouse.

Probate Judge Kenneth Tacoma presided over the swearing in ceremony, joined by 84th District Court and Missaukee County Probate Court Judge Charles Parsons and 28th Circuit Court Judge William Fagerman.

Van Alst recalled that Tacoma was an able teacher when she had worked in front of him as a public defender.

“He was an excellent role model for me,” Van Alst said.

Van Alst replaces Judge David Hogg, who was unable to attend the ceremony. Tacoma relayed a statement from Hogg stating he felt Snyder had served the people of the community well with Van Alst’s appointment.

Van Alst had praise for Prosecuting Attorney Tony Badovinac, whom she worked with even before finishing school at Thomas M. Cooley Law School.

Prior to the judicial appointment, Van Alst had served as director of the Friend of the Court for Wexford and Missaukee counties. She also had worked in private practice specializing in family law, divorce, paternity, child custody and adoption, and served as Wexford County attorney magistrate.

In addition to thanking members of her family, Van Alst also acknowledged the efforts of local legislators who worked to keep the judge’s seat from being eliminated, including Rep. Phil Potvin, R-Cadillac, Sen. Darwin Booher, R-Evart, and Rep. Bruce Rendon, R-Lake City. The seat had been faced possible elimination at the recommendation of the State Court Administrative Office.

Van Alst must seek election in November 2014 to continue to hold the judge’s seat.
