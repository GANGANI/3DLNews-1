2014: A look back at the local events, people that made headlines this past year

R.D. Hohenfeldt  |  The Rolla Daily News

As we looked back over the 300-plus issues of The Rolla Daily News in 2014, some of the news seemed like it happened in the distant past, while some seemed like it happened only a few days ago.

Maybe it was the nature of the news. Say what you will, though, whether the news was boring or riveting, there was a lot of it.

For a still-small town out in the middle of, uh, everywhere, Rolla generates a lot of noteworthy activity. Some of it is worthy of pride; some of it we’d just as soon forget.

Take a look at this review of the top news stories month-by-month.

Yes, this retrospective is long. Heck, it seems like it was a long year.

Don’t read it all at one sitting. Save these pages of The Year 2014 Reviewed and refer to them from time to time over the next few days.

On New Year’s Day, while you are having coffee, skim over it again and you’ll think, as we did, “Did all this really happen in one year in this small town?”

January

--Kole Gearhart, son of Kyle and Kara Gearhart, of Steelville, was the first baby born at Phelps County Regional Medical Center. Time of birth: 2:05 a.m. Wednesday, Jan. 1.

--A fire gutted a mobile home northeast of Doolittle in the afternoon of New Year’s Day, displacing the Ross family, a family of three. The Missouri State Fire Marshal’s Office found it to be caused by an electrical malfunction behind the clothes dryer.

--The Missouri State Highway Patrol reported no traffic fatalities during the New Year’s holiday.

--A light snow covered the Rolla area on the morning of Jan. 2, the first snow of the new year. The National Weather Service reported 0.7 inches fell overnight.

--“Idol insider” was the headline over a Jan. 2 story about Rolla resident Sheryl Camp who had been chosen by the American Idol producers, along with other successful women who had a presence in the social media, to visit the show during its taping in Hollywood in December.

--Pam Grow, a rural Rolla resident, and nine other people filed a lawsuit on Jan. 2, charging Gov. Jay Nixon with shirking his duties for his failure to set special elections in districts without representation, including the 120th Legislative District that covers parts of Phelps and Crawford counties.

--A fire gutted a house on Woodland Drive off Salem Avenue Jan. 4, the same day the new tenant planned to move in.

--Three Rolla High School robotics teams advanced to the state contest in the qualifying tournament held at the RHS gymnasium.

--Several inches of snow blew into Rolla Jan. 5. On Jan. 6 area residents woke up to a foot of snow in some rural areas and record-breaking cold. Ten degrees below zero was the new record set at the Rolla National Airport weather observation station right after midnight on Jan. 6, but before the day ended, that record was broken when the temperature dropped three more degrees.

--The Rolla City Council Jan. 6 began discussing what to do about a new Rolla Animal Shelter.

--Nicole Suzanne Licklider, 25, was found dead in an upstairs bedroom of a residence in the 700 block of Avon Court Jan. 6; Phelps County Deputy Coroner Lenox Jones said no foul play was suspected.

--No one was hurt in a flue fire that damaged a house on County Road 7240, southwest of Rolla. Resident James Lackner was able to extinguish the fire before Rolla rural firefighters arrived Jan. 7.

--Trevor P. Turnbull, 21, was charged with first-degree murder in the death of Kyle G. Holz-hausen, 24, Rolla, who was found dead on the night of Jan. 9 at a home on Elmwood Drive.

--A man identified as James Howell, 34, Rolla, was found dead at his home on East 14th Street on Jan. 9.

--Rolla students went back to school Jan. 10, after snow and cold weather canceled school Jan. 6-9.

--A 45-year-old Rolla woman, Sheila Quigley, was found dead in her home in the 10 block of Windsor Street Jan. 12.

--Award-winning photographer Noppadol Paothong was the guest speaker at the Greater Ozark Rivers Audubon Society’s annual banquet Jan. 9.

--U.S. Army Chief of Staff Gen Ray Odierno visited Fort Leonard Wood for the first time Jan. 13.

--A fire that destroyed a log cabin and wood frame structure on County Road 7100 Jan. 20 near Doolittle was declared “suspicious” by the state fire marshal’s office. The property was owned by Tom Aaron and had been in the Aaron family’s possession for many generations.

--Cassandra Roberts, 4, who was reported missing in Phelps County Jan. 22 was found the next day in Las Cruces, New Mexico. Her mother, Nikie Robertson, 39, was arrested on a Phelps County warrant for parental kidnapping.

--A log home destroyed Jan. 23 was investigated. Lishia Stewart, 53, and her son, Brian Sands, 30, lived in the house on County Road 7150 south of Rolla.

--The Pryor’s Pizza sign, up since 1962, was destroyed by 35 mph winds  Jan. 27.

--The Phelps County Commission approved a $15 million budget Jan. 28. Two jobs were cut.

--A rural Rolla family was displaced from their home Jan. 31 by a fire that appeared to have started in the garage. Four adults and three children lived at the home on Spencer Road, off Bridge School Road.

February

--Feb. 1 the Daily News reported Gov. Nixon had announced Aug. 5 as the special election date for the 120th Legislative District. Lead plaintiff Pam Grow said she wanted the election before the General Assembly ends its session May 16.

---Sleet and freezing rain, along with black ice Feb. 1-2 caused many accidents, some serious, in Rolla and Phelps County.

--Fire destroyed an abandoned mobile home Feb. 3 in the Woodcrest Mobile Home Park, located off Vichy Road. There were no injuries; the cause was unknown.

--Feb. 4, 25th Circuit Judge David Gregory Warren announced he would not run to retain the seat he had been appointed to in December 2012 by Gov. Nixon to fill the term of Mary Sheffield who had been appointed to the Court of Appeals.

--More snow, another 1-2 inches, dumped on Rolla Feb. 4, causing even more accidents on Interstate 44 and elsewhere.

--Also Feb. 4, Phelps County Prosecuting Attorney John Beger announced he would file as a candidate for the office of circuit judge, the position held by Judge Warren.

--Two people were hurt Feb. 5 when a Jeep Cherokee driving on the North Outer Road between Rolla and St. James rolled over and off the left side of the road. It came to a rest on its wheels.

--Phelps County Clerk Carol Bennett announced Feb. 6 she would not seek another term. She was appointed to the office in April 1997 by then-Gov. Mel Carnahan and ran for her first full four-year term in 1998.

--The Phelps County Commission Feb. 6 authorized the issuance of $24 million in bonds by the Phelps County Regional Medical Center for construction of a clinic at Waynesville and other capital projects to the buildings and grounds of PCRMC in Rolla.

--Feb. 8, the 29th annual Cancer Gala far surpassed its $91,000 goal.

--On the weekend of Feb. 8-9, the Missouri University of Science and Technology Fencing Club hosted a regional fencing tournament on campus. Fencing teams from campuses throughout the state showed up for a little swordplay.

--Two people were hurt in a fire that destroyed a Doolittle rural home Feb. 12. The mobile home was a total loss.

--Brendon Fox, the city of Rolla prosecuting attorney and an assistant county prosecutor, announced Feb. 12 he would file for the office of Phelps County prosecuting attorney.

--Feb. 13, visiting Judge Kerry Rowden revoked the probation of Harland Frohmader, 27, Newburg, and reinstated his previous sentences totalling 35 years in connection with a string of burglaries in August 2013.

--At the Feb. 13 meeting of the Phelps County Emergency Services Board, Chairman Paul Rueff announced Edgar Springs was finally a member of the Missouri Statewide Wireless Interoperable System (MOSWIN), allowing municipalities to be connected to a central dispatch via the same radio frequency.

--The Daily News reported Feb. 15 that Amy Beechner-McCarthy had been named the CEO of Your Community Health Center, a new federally qualified health center that was slated to open the first week of March.

--The first Rolla Polar Plunge was held Feb. 15 at The Fugitive Beach, southeast of Rolla on Highway 72, to raise money for Special Olympics. More than $20,000 was raised.

--Shawn Sisco on Feb. 17 was named the Republican nominee for a special election in the 120th Legislative District.

--Missouri S&T announced Feb. 18 that enrollment was up 7.7 percent. The institution had 7,700 students enrolled, the highest ever in school history.

--On Feb. 19, the Army announced Fort Leonard Wood could lose 1,000 soldiers by October 2015 due to base realignment and force reduction.

--Strong winds Feb. 20 knocked over large trees in and around Rolla, causing damage to some buildings.

--Rolla police said a 12-year-old boy was stuck with a “dirty needle” he and another boy found Feb. 21. The children found several syringes near the road in the 1000 block of Laguille Court. While playing with the syringes one boy was stuck in the arm.

--Jeremy Light, who started fighting fires when he was 15 and a cadet with the Rolla Rural Fire Department, was honored for his service Feb. 21. After 23 years, he retired from firefighting back in January.

--A fire was believed to have spread from an outdoor furnace to a trailer near Highway CC and County Road 7210 Feb. 23.

--The Missouri S&T campus was evacuated Wednesday, Feb. 26, because of a gas-line break near Straumanis-James Hall. No injuries were reported.

March

--A severe winter storm that was predicted to dump 6-8 inches of snow left only an inch or two on the Rolla area Saturday, March 2.

--At the First Tech Challenge Missouri State Championship on the S&T campus, two robotics teams from RHS advanced to the North Super Regional in Iowa City, Iowa, April 3-5.

--March 3, the Rolla City Council passed a resolution to explain to voters what to expect if they were to pass a quarter-cent parks sales tax in April. Half of the $950,000 expected to be raised would be used for operating the parks, The Centre and SplashZone while the other half would be used for capital expenditures in the parks, such as playground equipment, trails, restrooms, parking lots and facilities.

--A home southeast of Rolla was destroyed March 3 by what authorities believed to be arson. Cody W. Yardley, 22, Rolla, was arrested and charged with arson, burglary and tampering with a vehicle in connection with the fire.

--David Earl Lundin was caught March 6 after a three-hour manhunt; he was charged with first-degree murder  in connection with the shooting of Richard R. Finnell, 57, at a home about a mile south of the Rolla city limits.

--Three Rolla Board of Education incumbents--Tony Froehlich, Jane Haskell and Keith Strassner--and their lone challenger, Rodney Southard, engaged in spirited debate March 10 in advance of the April school election.

--Meramec Regional Planning Commission this month was out holding public forums and gathering ideas about transportation needs for the region.

--Thomas Johnson, 30, Rolla, who had already pleaded guilty to his ninth charge of driving while intoxicated, was sentenced to 15 years in prison March 13.

--March 15, the St. Pat’s Parade was held downtown in the sun and with temperatures in the high 60s. It even reached into the 70s at the Rolla National Airport weather station. But in less than 24 hours, 3 inches of snow fell east of Rolla and 2 inches of snow fell northwest of Rolla. Rolla’s snowfall was somewhere in between, but Pine Street, where the parade had been held, was covered with snow. Pictures on Page One proved it.

--More dirty syringes were found, this time  March 12 in the grass outside East Central College and the Rolla Public Schools Administration Building.

--The Dollar General Store on Forum Drive was robbed March 18 by a suspect who grabbed money from the cash register and fled. No threats were made; no weapon was displayed. Freddie Patterson, 46, of St. Louis, was arrested for the robbery March 21 at a residence in the 2000 block of Forum Drive.

--A Rolla city policeman on patrol discovered a fire at a home on Rolla Street in the early morning of March 23. The occupants got out with no injuries.

-- Tear gas, a canine officer and the SWAT team were all used to capture Chance Aaron Melton, 35, March 21 on charges of false impersonation of a police officer, first-degree burglary and second-degree assault.

--Trevor Turnbull pleaded not guilty March 19 to the charge of murdering Kyle G. Holzhausen Jan. 9.

--At a traffic stop March 25 on Interstate 44, lawmen found 300 pounds of marijuana in a vehicle. They arrested the driver, Brian F. Ackerman, 30, of Lake Park, Georgia, on first-degree drug trafficking.

--Filing for the August primary ended March 25 with races for county clerk and associate circuit judge.

-A Rolla man, Caleb A. English, 29, faced a charge of first-degree domestic assault and several other charges after police were told he held his wife against her will for about 12 hours March 26.

--A statewide report published in the Daily News March 29 showed meth incidents were down in the state in 2013, and in Phelps County.

--A report published March 31 showed that Phelps County’s population has declined slightly since 2010.

April

--Proposition A, the quarter-cent parks and recreation tax, took over Page 1 of the first day of the month with the spring municipal election just a week away. In one report, Parks and Recreation Director Scott Caron made the case that the line between the parks and The Centre was “fuzzy.”

--Gov. Nixon visited RHS and applauded the excellent education available in Rolla Public Schools.

--Tara Peters, a state employee, withdrew from the race for the Republican nomination for Phelps County clerk, leaving Pamela K. Grow, Roger A. Harris and Rosalind Ann Breske as the three remaining candidates. Paul Long remained the only Democratic candidate, for long-time deputy clerk Mary Ann Rasmussen had withdrawn just days after filing.

--April 2, some 2.7 inches of rain fell within an hour, causing flooding on city streets and county roads. It also slickened the interstate so much that at least one car ran off the road and overturned.

--A shooting on Fort Hood, Texas, April 2 rippled back to south central Missouri. Spc. Ivan Lopez, the gunman who killed three other soldiers before killing himself, had trained at Fort Leonard Wood to be a truck driver, transferring to Fort Hood in February. Even worse, Sgt. Timothy Owens, 37, was one of the three murder victims. Owens attended RHS before joining the military; he married a RHS graduate, Billy Humphrey, at her grandmother’s house in Rolla just months before the shooting.

--The Daily News reported April 4 that Brian Pohl, 23, Rolla, had been arrested after lawmen served a search warrant in the 1600 block of North Pine Street and seized a “butane hash oil lab.” That location was within 2,000 feet of a school.

--Police reported April 6 that a girl was “stuck in her left palm with a dirty needle” while playing in her front yard on Fourth Street.

--The Rolla City Council April 7 hired a consultant, Shelter Planners of America from Arlington, Texas, to do a study on what the city needs for an animal shelter and the best place to put it.

--Proposition A, the parks and recreation tax, failed Tuesday, April 8. Louis Magdits was elected mayor. The three Rolla Board of Education incumbents were returned to office. Voter turnout was 16.7 percent countywide.

--The funeral for Sgt. Timothy W. Owens, was conducted at 11 a.m. Saturday, April 12, at First Baptist Church; burial with full military honors was at Lake Springs Cemetery. The Patriot Guard motorcycle riders escorted the sergeant’s remains from Lambert International Airport to Long-Kloeppel Funeral Home in Dixon on Friday afternoon, April 11. Gov. Nixon ordered the flags flown at half-staff at state buildings in all 114 counties and the city of St. Louis in honor of Sgt. Owens.

--S&T went even greener with an electric shuttle bus that began operating on campus April 14.

--Robert L. Walker, pleaded guilty April 15 to robbery and assault charges that resulted from his participation in a Sept. 1, 2012, robbery of the Delano convenience store on Highway 72. He was sentenced to 17 years in the Missouri Department of Corrections.

--Mercy announced April 15 it would close the We Care Clinic, which offered services two evenings per month, in early May. The closure was said to be in support of the new Your Community Health Center.

--Gov. Nixon came to Rolla April 17 and called on the Missouri General Assembly to expand Medicaid. He spoke at Phelps County Regional Medical Center

--The Rolla Board of Education April 17 honored Sgt. Vince Giacalone, director of the Rolla Police Department’s Volunteers in Police Service (V.I.P.S.) program, for his work with the school.

--On April 20, Christian Life Center, a Rolla church, celebrated Easter by dropping 30,000 candy-filled plastic eggs from a helicopter to thousands of people at Ber Juan Park.

--At the Rolla City Council meeting April 21, plans for a big shopping center called Westside Marketplace were outlined by the St. Louis company, UTW Rolla Development LLC, that wants to develop it. The council held a workshop the next night and appeared to be eager to work with the firm on a public financing of the shopping center.

--Dr. Shannon Fogg, associate professor of history and political science, was named 2014 Woman of the year by Missouri S&T April 23.

--Jessica R. Taylor, 21, Rolla, was arrested for stealing, filing a false report and tampering with evidence in connection with a report of an armed robbery at the Subway on Forum Drive earlier in the month. Also arrested were Michael J. Wallenfang, 19, Rolla, and Dustin W. Taylor, 19, Rolla, on a charge of stealing. Jessica R. Taylor was an employee of the sandwich shop at the time of the report.

--Herman Ilges, 56, was shot and killed at a Rosati area home. Dean C. Groh, 53, was also shot and injured April 23. Authorities later announced that Oryon Dain Guinn, 29, was charged with first-degree murder, armed criminal action, second-degree burglary, first-degree assault, unlawful possession of a firearm. Also, Chelsea Lynn Maxfield was charged with first-degree murder, first-degree burglary and first-degree assault. Groh died June 29 at his home.

--Dennis  Elms, of Rolla, was sentenced to 15 years in prison April 28 for possession of heroin. Police say he hid 38 capsules of the substance in his underwear back in April 2013.

--Pam Grow, of Rolla, testified April 30 at the conclusion of a Republican-led Missouri House committee hearing on three measures seeking to impeach Gov. Nixon.

May

--At the May 1 meeting, the Phelps County Commission began discussing health insurance by hearing a proposal from a local insurance company for the commission to offer insurance to employees only, allowing their dependents to buy insurance through the Affordable Care Act health exchange. No action was taken.

--Rolla area residents joined in the National Day of Prayer observance with a service held at First Baptist Church on the evening of May 1.

--A ribbon was cut May 5 to mark the opening of the new Brewer Science facility at the Rolla National Airport. Dr. Terry Brewer predicted the construction of the new facility will lead to more companies building at the airport land.

--Preliminary funding agreements for the Westside Marketplace and the Forum Plaza redevelopment were approved by the Rolla City Council May 5.

--On May 8, the Daily News profiled Jeff Sandquist, vocal music director and music department chair at Rolla Public Schools, who would retire at the end of the school year.

--The Rolla Optimist Club turned 50 this year and celebrated that fact May 10.

--Republican Roger Harris withdrew from the race for the nomination for Phelps County clerk, it was announced May 10.

--Denny LaBantschnig took over as executive director of The Community Partnership, succeeding Amy Beechner-McCarthy who left to be the CEO of Your Community Health Center, according to a May 13 announcement.

--On May 13, the Missouri House approved a constitutional amendment for a transportation sales tax to be put before voters. It would be placed on the August ballot.

--The Rolla Board of Education May 15 approved the new Comprehensive School Improvement Plan with five goals.

--Groundbreaking was held May 16 for the eighth Habitat for Humanity house in Phelps County.

--Rolla City Councilman Tony Bahr apologized May 19 for his outburst at a previous meeting against Fat Cats Entertainment Complex owner Josh Noe. Noe had been in front of the council numerous times the previous months, responding to complaints from neighbors about noise. Other neighbors defended Noe. Much time was devoted to dealing with Noe and Fat Cats, and Bahr had said he would like to see the business shut down.

--Community Development Director John Petersen told the council May 19 that neighbors oppose the rezoning of land on McCutchen Drive, land owned by Robert L. Davis, for the construction of an assisted living center. The council heard first reading of an ordnance to make the change but declined to waive its rules and pass the final reading of the ordinance was placed on the June 2 council agenda.

--Missouri S&T shut down its campus power plant May 21 as the campus switched to a geothermal energy system.

--Farrell Wayne Cross on May 22 was found guilty of first-degree murder in the Feb. 3, 1991, death of his wife, Tammy, 32. Her body was found next to her car on Three Mile Road off Highway P, dead of a gunshot wound to the head. Cross was arrested, but at his preliminary hearing, then-Associate Circuit Judge Mary Sheffield ruled there was insufficient evidence to require a trial. Additional evidence was gathered over the years; a murder charge was filed against Farrell Cross on Sept. 22, 2010. His sentencing hearing was set for Aug. 8, 2014, in St. Francois County. Federal prosectors with the U.S. Attorney’s Office prosecuted the case. Cross was represented by Missouri public defenders.

--The Mars Rover Design Team at Missouri S&T took second place in a international contest held May 29-31 at the Mars Desert Research Station in Hanksville, Utah.

June

--Capt. Lee Ann Kenley retired June 1 as the first woman in Missouri history to serve as a troop commander for the Missouri State Highway Patrol. She spent nearly 30 years with the patrol, becoming commander of Troop I in Rolla in 2008. Lt. James W. Remillard was promoted to the rank of captain and appointed to be the new commander of Troop I.

--Dr.  Kevin W. Brewer, 62. Rolla, was killed June 1 after being thrown from the motorcycle he was driving west on Edgar Springs on County Road 6380. He failed to negotiate a hard-right curve at the intersection with Route M, according to the MSHP.

National Cancer Survivors Day was celebrated in Rolla with a picnic June 1 at the Rolla Lions Club Park.

--At the June 2 meeting, the Rolla City Council approved rezoning property along McCutchen Drive, owned by Robert L. Davis, for a nursing home company to operate a facility there.

--Steve Bowles was sworn in as the Fourth Ward councilman to complete the term of Louis Magdits, who was elected mayor.

--Ashlee Smith, 27, of Rolla, was sentenced to 15 years in prison June 3 for possession of heroin with the intent to distribute it.

--After June 5 high-speed car chase on Highway 72, law men found a meth lab in the car. They arrested Daniel L. Benton, 36, of Union, and Mitchel L. Peck, 37, of Kansas City.

--A bear was struck by a vehicle and killed June 9 near Meta.

--Members of CORE, the Champions of Rolla Education, filed an appeal with the state after it lost the bid to continue operating the license fee office, which over the years had allowed the group to raise money used for enrichment activities in the Rolla Public Schools. The state granted an extension through July.

--Four Phelps County projects were listed among the transportation priorities by MoDOT for the constitutional amendment to allow a sales tax that would be voted on in August.

--John Jamison ended his 48-year career with Sears June 14 when turned over the ownership of the Sears Hometown Store, a business he had operated since moving to Rolla in 1981.

--A load of cheese spilled onto westbound I-44 June 14 near Jerome when two tractor-trailers collided.

--Walmart in June filed for a rezoning of property owned by Robert L. Davis between Forum Drive and McCutchen Drive, next to the 18th Street roundabout. City Administrator John Butz made the announcement at the June 17 city council meeting. That announcement came right when the council heard first reading of an ordinance to begin the formation of a redevelopment plan as authorized by Chapter 353 of the Missouri state statutes, a section of the state law that would allow a tax abatement for the Forum Plaza.

--A seven-legged frog was found southeast of Newburg and was pictured on Page One of the Daily News June 19.

-Ten people, including  Editor Paul Hackbarth of the Rolla Daily News, graduated form Leadership Phelps County June 19.

--An open house on the transportation sales tax that would be voted on in August was held June 26 at Rolla City Hall.

--A drug roundup held June 24 resulted in 29 arrests. The area drug task force had been investigating for more than 30 months.

--On June 30, several area legislators toured the new Your Community Health Clinic.

July

--Aaron Hadlow was named the new editor of The St. James Leader-Journal July 1.

--Two Rolla men, Richard Sanders, 40, and Lazaro Molina Jr., 29, were arrested July 3 in connection with a June residental burglary that occurred while the victim was asleep.

--The Moving Wall, a smaller replica of the Vietnam Veterans Memorial in Washington, D.C., was in Licking July 4-6.

--Federal authorities this month opened an investigation of Missouri University of Science and Technology, one of a dozen institutions studied for the handling of sexual assaults.

--The Rolla Lions Club Carnival wrapped up another successful four-day event July 5.

--Wayne E. Feeler Sr., 66, Rolla, drowned July 5 in Maries County while trying to retrieve a small boat on a farm pond.

--The new Rolla Historic Preservation Commission began its research of the history of local buildings.

--The Missouri Department of Transportation (MoDOT) responded to requests and added Highway 63 widening through Rolla to its list of priorities for the transportation sales tax, removing from the list the work planned on the section of the highway from Rolla to Vienna.

--Joe Polizzi, vice president and general counsel for Country Mart, told the Rolla City Council July 7 that rezoning of property to allow construction of a Walmart Neighborhood Market would have dire consequences: “We fear Forum Plaza is going to go dark if there’s another supermarket less than a mile down the road,” he said.

--At its July 8 meeting, the Rolla Planning and Zoning Commission declined to recommend a rezoning request from Walmart for a Neighborhood Market at 18th Street and Forum Drive.

--Mike Woessner, president of Investment Realty, told the Phelps County Commission July 10 to beef up courthouse security before it is too late.

--Phelps County Democratic Club held its Stump Picnic at Lions Club Park July 12. Democratic candidates in the August primary introduced themselves.

--The Phelps County Historical Society held its annual birthday party to honor Edmund Ward Bishop, called the founder of Phelps County, at the Phelps County Courthouse July 14. Historian Dr. Mark Stauter showed “then and now” photos of buildings. Flint Holler performed old-timey music.

--Phi Kappa Theta at 707 State St. tore down the old frat house to build a new one at the same site. Completion of the new house is scheduled for July 2015.

--At a workshop July 17, the Rolla City Council considered asking for a parks-only sales tax that would not include funding for The Centre.

--The Public House Brewing Company of Rolla and the St. James Winery announced July 18 a plan to partner for a new drinking establishment in St. James.

--Phelps County Regional Medical Center  (PCRMC) hosted a Topping Out Ceremony July 21 at the Waynesville clinic construction site to mark the installation of the last beam that was signed by guests at the ceremony.

--The Rolla City Council July 21 granted a delay to Walmart for its Neighborhood Market rezoning request and also set a public hearing at its Aug. 18 meeting for comments about the Forum Plaza redevelopment plan that would include a tax abatement and a taxing district that would impose a 1 percent sales tax.

--The Phelps County Fair opened July 23 and continued through July 26.

--Cassandra J. Lewis-Robbins, 24, Rolla, was found guilty of six counts of animal abuse July 25. She abandoned dogs in a trailer in 90-degree temperatures with no air conditioning and no open windows. A sheriff’s deputy and a Rolla animal control officer found the six dogs in a state of near starvation. The animals could not be saved and had to be euthanized after being diagnosed with Parvo disease. Sentencing was set for Sept. 4.

--A 6-year-old boy drowned July 25 at the Fugitive Beach, a popular swimming spot in Phelps County and the home of the Polar Plunge. He was later identified as Noah Cook, of Fenton.

August

--A Page One story on Aug. 1 quoted Army spokesmen as saying the number of cuts in personnel had grown and that 5,400 positions were expected to be lost at Fort Leonard Wood.

--William Earl Harrison, 49, was arrested on a charge of first-degree murder in connection with the killing of Jerry Wayne King, whose body was found in early July 1992 near the entrance of Gourd Creek Cave. King, of Cuba, Missouri, was 26, and he died of an apparent gunshot wound to the head. Harrison, who was 28 at the time, was arrested on a charge of murder in 1992 but was later released.

--Diana Watkins took over as Rolla Public Library interim director after Cathy Smith left to take another position as a teacher at a local university.

--David E. Lundin, 61, Rolla, was sentenced to life in prison without parole Aug. 4 in the murder of Richard R. Finnell in early March of this year.

--At its meeting Aug.4, the Rolla City Council heard the first reading for the parks-only tax. It would be called Proposition P.

--Walmart withdrew its Neighborhood Market rezoning request temporarily, according to a letter read by Mayor Lou Magdits at the Aug. 4 council meeting.

--In the primary election held Aug. 5, Brendon Fox won the Republican nomination for county prosecutor, defeating Brant L. Shockley. Fox would not face a Democrat opponent in November, for none filed.

--Republican Pam Grow won the nomination for Phelps County clerk, defeating Rosalind Breske. She would face Paul I. Long, the uncontested Democratic candidate in November.

--At the Aug. 5 meeting, Phelps County Commissioners heard an update on the work being done to tighten security in the courthouse.

--Also at the Aug. 5 meeting, the county commission also voted to become self-insured, approving a contract with Group Benefit Services, of Springfield, Missouri, to serve as the county’s third-party administrator.

--The Rolla License Fee office temporarily closed Aug. 11-12, and when it reopened it was in a new location with a new manager. CORE (Champions of Rolla Education) was out as the manager.

--A late fire Aug. 11 damaged a home on Jimmy Street. It was ruled accidental.

--The Daily News reported Aug. 14 that Rolla Police Chief Mark Kearse had been put on administrative leave. He was also planning a Sept. 30 retirement. Kearse responded to the allegations at length Aug. 16. Lt. Rick Williams was named interim chief.

--Edgar Prairie Days was held with a parade Aug. 16.

--The Rolla City Council decided Aug. 18 to place Proposition P, the parks tax on the Nov. 4 ballot. It would impose a sales tax of one-quarter of a cent for eight years, then reduce it to three-16ths of a cent starting April 1, 2023.  “These funds are prohibited for support of The Centre,” the ballot language stated.

--Rolla Municipal Utilities asked customers to cut use of electricity Aug. 21-22 to keep peak demand from climbing

--Public hearings were held at the Rolla City Council meeting Aug. 18 on the Forum Plaza tax abatement and sales tax district.

--The county’s transition to self-insurance will not start on Sept. 1 after all, the Phelps County Commission decided Aug. 19.

--PCRMC this month, with the help of the Meramec Regional Planning Commission, applied for $1.4 million in Economic Development Administration funds to be used for parking lots and waterworks for the Delbert Day Cancer Institute that is in the planning stages.

--Groundbreaking for the Delbert Day Cancer Institute was held Tuesday, Aug. 26.

--A bomb threat was made by phone to CVS Pharmacy at Kingshighway and Highway 63 on Aug. 27. The caller demanded money. No explosive device was found after a search of the building.

--The PCRMC trustees at their Aug. 27 meeting honored and thanked Dr. Mary Graham, who do not attend, for her service to the Phelps County area for the past 16 years as the radiation oncologist.

-- On Aug. 28, the new Route D bridge over the Burlington Northern Santa Fe Railroad tracks west of Jerome opened.

--Keith Kilmer, retired San Bernardino, Calif., police chief, now living in Maries County was hired by the city to look into the allegations against Rolla Police Chief Mark Kearse, it was announced Aug. 30.

September

--At the Sept. 2 meeting, the Rolla City Council heard first reading of the fiscal year 2015 budget that contained many cuts in parks and recreation services.

--The Rolla Public Schools continued the investigation into the need for an early childhood center by encouraging voters to watch a video on the school website and then taking a survey.

--A fire damaged a home on County Road 8150 Sept. 5. The Doolittle and Rolla rural fire departments responded.

--The family of Robert and Wanda Davis was honored at the Phelps County University of Missouri Extension picnic Sept. 8 for being the county Farm Family of the year.

--Rolla students scored slightly lower on statewide standardized tests this past year, the school board learned Sept. 4, but that was the statewide trend.

--RMU said more than 4,200 customers lost electric power during or after a strong storm that swept through the area Sept. 9.

--Republican Shawn Sisco was sworn into office in the Missouri House of Representatives Sept. 10. He was elected Aug. 4 in a special election to serve out the open term in the 120th Legislative District. Sisco was not, however, the Republican nominee chosen for that office in the August primary, so his term will end in January with the swearing in of the November general election winner.

--The right-to-farm state constitutional amendment, which narrowly won in the August, survived a recount in September; results were certified Sept. 15 in Jefferson City.

--The Rolla City Council Sept. 15 approved the fiscal year 2015 budget and raised sewer use fees by 25 cents per 1,000 gallons of water used.

--The Boeing Co. and Missouri S&T entered into a master research agreement Sept. 16.

--St. James school administrators and the NAACP Sept. 19 addressed allegations of racial discrimination among school employees. The NAACP had filed a complaint with the U.S. Department of Education and U.S. Department of Justice.

--The Daily News reported Sept. 20 that Rolla Municipal Judge James T. Crump, 42, Rolla, had been arrested earlier in the month on a suspicion of driving while intoxicated.

--Missouri Sen. Scott Sifton, D-St. Louis County, told the Phelps County Democratic Club Sept. 20 that he was thinking about running for state attorney general. Frank Barnitz, a Democrat, a former state representative and former state senator, told the local Democratic Club that he was considering re-entering politics.

--Fall enrollment at S&T was 8,642, a new record, it was reported Sept. 24.

--Drill Sgt. Angel M. Sanchez was found guilty in military court Sept. 24 of sexually assaulting and harassing multiple female soldiers. He was sentenced to 20 years in prison and given a dishonorable discharge.

--Amy Beechner-McCarthy, CEO of the Your Community Health Center, spoke at the Sept. 24 meeting of the PCRMC trustees. She encouraged the passage of Medicaid expansion.

--Gov. Jay Nixon announced Sept. 26 that he had appointed Rolla attorney and former state legislator David Steelman to serve on the University of Missouri Board of Curators.

--The Celebration of Nations celebrated the diversity of Rolla life Sept. 27.

--State Sen. Dan Brown complained at the Phelps County Republican Central Committee’s picnic Sept. 27 that the Rolla Daily News had given Page One coverage to his former political rival, Frank Barnitz, but would not print his own weekly column.

--The St. James library tax passed, 287-255, on Sept. 30. It was a 30-cent property tax. The Sept. 30 election was held after a ballot miscue occurred in the original election held Aug. 5 for the library tax.

October

--Rolla Police Chief Mark Kearse began his retirement on Oct. 1.

--The Rolla Daily News staff won 14 awards in the Missouri Press Association’s 2014 Better Newspaper Contest.

--PCRMC announced it had received a $1.4 million grant from the state EDA for parking, water and sewer work at the Delbert Day Cancer Institute.

--Rolla Municipal Utilities announced it won an award for water fluoridation from the Centers for Disease Control.

--A St. James school district employee involved in the alleged racial discrimination event was placed on unpaid leave Oct. 1.

--Charges against Rolla Municipal Judge James T. Crump were formally filed Oct. 3. The charges listed were driving while intoxicated and failure to register a motor vehicle.

--Hundreds of people walked in the Relay for Life Oct. 4 at Rolla Lions Club Park and raised $6,000 to fight cancer.

--Brooklyn A. Jackson, 8, of Rolla, was seriously hurt after trying to jump onto a moving SUV and then falling off outside of Rolla Oct. 5.

--Parks and Recreation Director Scott Caron presented the list of capital improvement priorities that would be completed if the Nov. 4 Proposition P passes. He released the list at the Oct. 6 Rolla City Council meeting.

--A forum on Proposition P was held Oct. 9. Not even two dozen people showed up to ask questions and make comments.

--Philip H. Allen, 42, Rolla, and his wife, Carin Allen, 44, were killed in a head-on crash in Morgan County Oct. 10  when another motorist attempted to pass a tractor-trailer. Carin Allen was the Rolla High School Latin teacher. Their two children were seriously injured.

--Walmart’s request for a rezoning to allow construction of a Neighborhood Market was filed again, and on Oct. 14, the Rolla Planning and Zoning Commission recommended the Rolla City Council pass the rezoning ordinance.

--A suspicious package found in a mailbox at the Rolla Post Office Oct. 16 resulted in evacuation and re-routing of traffic. It turned out not to be a bomb but rather a homemade tool to steal mail from the box.

--A public hearing Oct. 21 regarding the Neighborhood Market request for rezoning from R-1 to C-2 with a couple of conditional use permits was long and filled with comments from the neighbors who almost were unanimously opposed to the change.

--The St. James school worker allegedly involved in an alleged racial incident returned to work after two weeks of unpaid leave. The worker’s supervisor resigned Oct. 1 to retire; that supervisor had not reported the alleged incidents. On Oct. 23, Johnnie A. Landis, 44, St. James, was indicted on a felony harassment charge related to the hanging of a makeshift noose as well as use of racial epithets.

--Missouri S&T dedicated a new $22.3-million building at 11th and State streets as Bertelsmeyer Hall Oct. 17. It will be used for the new chemical and biochemical department. The building is named in honor of James E. Bertelsmeyer, Tulsa, Oklahoma, who earned a bachelor’s degree in chemical engineering in 1966; he donated $5 million toward construction.

--Interim Rolla library director Diana Watkins was hired to the job permanently on Oct. 17.

--Billy Joe Godfrey, 51, was arrested Oct. 24 on a charge of first-degree statutory sodomy stemming from alleged incidents when his stepson, now an adult, was a minor.

--Whoops! Rolla city officials Oct. 24 asked county officials and a judge not to count Proposition P ballots in the Nov. 4 general election. Turns out that  Councilman Matthew Miller, an attorney, had been tipped off by a constituent about a state statute that prohibits governing bodies of cities and counties from going back to ask voters to approve certain sales tax initiatives that failed within the previous year.  He brought that to the attention of City Counselor Lance Thurman. The city voters had turned down a similar tax measure in April.

--A Missouri State Highway Patrol helicopter Oct. 24 helped find a missing hunter by using a thermal imaging device on Mark Twain National Forest property in southwest Phelps County.

--Mark Christeson, who killed Adrian Brouk, Kyle Brouk and their mother Susan Brouk in Maries County in 1998, was scheduled to be executed at midnight Oct. 29. The U.S. Supreme Court issued a stay just two hours before the sentencing was to be carried out.

November

--An expensive oversight: In a Nov. 1 report, Phelps County Clerk Carol Bennett said Proposition P ballots (the city parks tax) would not be counted. She also said the city’s cost for the election, even though the ballots would not be counted, would be $16,000.

--Alex’s Pizza Palace on Eighth Street celebrated its 50th anniversary Nov. 3.

--Phelps County Assistant Prosecuting Attorney Brad Neckermann was appointed by Mayor Lou Magdits with the consent of the council to succeed Brendon Fox as city attorney. Fox was elected with no opponent in the Nov. 4 general election as county prosecutor.

-- On Nov. 3, the hour of reckoning: The Rolla City Council voted AGAINST rezoning for the Walmart Neighborhood Market, 10-1.

--The Rolla City Council approved a plan to find a new Rolla police chief.

--Voter turnout for the November general election was a 37.56 percent.

--A ceremony Nov. 6 at Missouri S&T officially marked the end of the geothermal energy system installation.

--Pamela Grow was elected Phelps County clerk on Nov. 4. Republican Mark Calvert unseated Phelps County Associate Circuit Judge Ken Clayton, but voters retained Associate Circuit Judge Ron White who was challenged by Brian Woolley, Republican.

--In the 120th Legislative District in eastern Phelps County and Crawford County, Jason Chipman, Republican, defeated Zech Hockersmith for the seat held by Shawn Sisco, who was elected in a special election in August during the primary.

--Ozarks Coca-Cola/Dr. Pepper Bottling Co. announced plans in November to close its Lebanon facility and move all operations to the Rolla plant at Hy-Point Industrial Park.

--On Nov. 10, charges against Rolla Municipal Judge James T. Crump were amended from driving while intoxicated to careless and imprudent driving. His next scheduled court appearance is Jan. 13.

--Jenks-Long Insurance Co. marked its 80th anniversary on Nov. 12.

--In a Nov. 14 report, Rolla City Administrator John Butz said the investigation of former Police Chief Mark Kearse found he had committed only minor workplace violations. Allegations of nepotism and bribery were “not sustained.” The investigation cost $16,000.

--The Rolla City Council at its Nov. 17 meeting took no action on the Forum Plaza redevelopment plan that would subsidize construction of a new supermarket and renovation of other stores with $4 million in tax money for a $13 million project. The council chose to wait two more weeks.

--A suspect in a burglary of the Pawn Center on Highway 72 surrendered to police Nov. 19, after threatening to harm himself.

--Trevor Turnbull pleaded guilty Nov. 17 to a charge of second-degree murder in the death of Kyle G. Holzhausen, 24, Rolla, on Jan. 9, 2014. He had been originally been charged with first-degree murder.

--Newburg Superintendent of Schools Dr. John Westerman announced his resignation, effective this summer.

--The Fill-a-Ford campaign began its 24th year on Nov. 21.

--More than 400 contributors helped reach the $7 million goal for the Delbert Day Cancer Institute, it was announced Nov. 24.

--Maj. Gen.Leslie Smith, commanding general at Fort Leonard Wood, said at a town hall meeting Nov. 19 that most of the job cuts at Fort Leonard Wood had been “absorbed.” That means the 1,000 military positions and more than 100 civilian jobs had mostly been moved to other positions.

--Lawrence Collins, 67, Newburg, was killed Nov. 28 in a one-vehicle crash on Highway FF, 1 mile east of Edgar Springs.

--Public House Brewing Co. opened its St. James venture in conjuction with St. James Winery Nov. 29.

December

--At its Dec. 1 meeting, the Rolla City Council approved a strategy for raising money, designing and building a new animal shelter. Three committees will be formed.

--The council also voted to Dec. 1 support a solar farm proposal from MCPower Companies.

--The council appointed a police chief search committee.

--Taylor Louderman, 23, who acted in Ozark Actors Theatre productions include the title role of “Annie” when she was 10 years old, starred as Wendy Darling in the NBC production of “Peter Pan Live” on Dec. 4.

--Lt. Jason Smith, formerly a captain and the officer who filed the original complaints against Police Chief Mark Kearse, appealed his demotion to lieutenant, which occurred on Nov. 13, according to a report in the Dec. 6-7 Daily News.

--Christmas parades were held in Rolla and Doolittle-Newburg Dec. 6. Doolittle had a community tree-lighting evening with music and food.

--S&T students staged a “die-in” demonstration on Dec. 8 to protest police brutality in Ferguson/St. Louis and other major metropolitan areas. There was no mention of brutality on the campus or in the community of Rolla.

--Circuit Judge David Gregory Warren on Dec. 4 sentenced Jason William Hannan, 35, of Siloam Springs, Arkansas, to 25 years in prison for first-degree assault of a law enforcement officer and five years each for resisting arrest and possession of a controlled substance with the intent to distribute, all charges that stem from a high-speed chase on Feb. 16, 2012.

--The county sales tax was up 6.32 percent for the year, the Phelps County commission learned on Dec. 9.

--The Phelps County Commission gave Melrose Quarry owner Dave Weinbaum until Jan. 15 to remove berms at its Arlington site or come up with an alternate plan to alleviate flooding concerns from neighbors across the Gasconade River. Weinbaum said he doesn’t believe he is legally obligated to remove them, but he hopes the issue is resolved in a good way for everyone.

--Phelps County Clerk Carol Bennett was honored Dec. 10 at a retirement reception.

--MSHP Sgt. Danny Crain, retiring after 34 years, gave an interview to the Daily News on Dec. 12, saying “It has been a great job.”

--Courthouse security changes will take place in January, according to a report in the Dec. 16 Daily News.

--Circuit Judge William E. Hickle sentenced Trevor P. Turnbull to 23 years in prison on Dec. 15 for the Jan. 9, 2014, murder of Kyle G. Holzhausen.

--St. James Mayor Dennis Wilson announced he would not run for another term, the Daily News reported Dec. 16.

--At the Dec. 15 meeting, the Rolla City Council waived its rules and heard both first and second readings to approve a community improvement taxing district to raise $4 million in sales tax money to help build a new Country Mart and renovate the rest of the shopping center. Super Market Developers LLC dropped its request for a property tax abatement.

--The council on Dec. 15 also heard first reading of an ordinance calling for an April election for the parks sales tax. Final reading to get it on the ballot will be required at the first meeting in January.

--PCMRC trustees on Dec. 17 approved a 5.2 percent price increase in the 2015 budget.

--Missouri S&T officers were equipped with body-worn cameras on Dec. 18.

--Doolittle Alderman Ron Bramlett Dec. 20 at the age of 72. He had been an alderman  for more than 15 years.

--On Christmas Eve, most Rolla fuel stations were selling regular gasoline for $1.99 per gallon.
