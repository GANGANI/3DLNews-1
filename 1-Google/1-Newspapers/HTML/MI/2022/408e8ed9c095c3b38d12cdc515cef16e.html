City of Benton Harbor Dedicates Street to Shamika Jordan
Benton-Michiana Spirit News•
A Benton Harbor woman who perished in an apartment fire last May now has a street named after her -- to honor the life she lived, and the city she loved.

Shamika Jordan passed away on May 3, in a fire at 1111 McAlister St., Benton Harbor. Officers from the Benton Harbor Department of Public Safety (BHDPS) observed heavy smoke coming from a downstairs apartment, and heavy flames coming from the rear of the residence. Bystanders advised firefighters that there was at least one person in the downstairs apartment.

The St. Joseph Department of Public Safety (SJPDS) also responded, to provide mutual aid BHDPS and SJDPS officers immediately entered the lower apartment and located a woman inside, identified as Shamika Jordan, removed her from the apartment, and rushed her to an awaiting ambulance. She was then taken to Lakeland Hospital.

Unfortunately, she succumbed to her injuries, and was declared dead at the hospital. In honor of her life, the City of Benton Harbor decided to rename the street as Shamika Jordan Way. Family members, friends and city officials gathered recently to share memories of Shamika.

First cousin Samantha Jordan remembered Shamika as a person who took pride in where she lived, and always celebrated her community.

“Our goal as a family is to improve, our hometown of Benton Harbor, because Shamika loved her community,” Samantha said. She was born and bred in this city, I couldn’t even get her to move. Every place I moved, I tried to get her to come. But she was Benton Harbor all the way, and she was the true essence of a Benton Harbor resident.”

The Jordan family will continue pushing for basic safety improvements, “so we don’t want this to happen to anyone else again,” Samantha said. “Our fight in this is to make sure that public safety is adequate and that fire hydrants are working for every citizen in Benton Harbor. We want her life to be
remembered.”

Benton Harbor City Commissioner-at-Large Mary Alice Adams said, “I think today is a great day to honor a young lady whose family has been long-standing residents of the City of Benton Harbor, and as tragic as she has left this earth. I think that this day shows how we can come together, and love on one another, and support one another and uplift one another in times of tragedy, and taking that pain and turning it into a passion that we can thank Shamika Jordan for.”
— Benton Spirit Community Newspaper
