Gov. Gretchen Whitmer Declares November Veterans and Military Families Month
Benton-Michiana Spirit News•
Governor Gretchen Whitmer has proclaimed November 2022 Veterans and Military Families Month, to celebrate their successes, and honor their service and sacrifices statewide.

“This month, we thank Michigan’s brave service members and veterans for their service and sacrifices to keep us safe and secure our freedoms,” Whitmer said. “We also recognize the families of service members for supporting their moms or dads, aunts and uncles, brothers or sisters, sons or daughters while on deployment. We owe those who serve and their families our gratitude and we pledge ourselves to work together to help them thrive.”

U.S. Army Maj. Gen. Paul D. Rogers, adjutant general and director of the Michigan Department of Military and Veterans Affairs, voiced a similar feeling in his own statement.

“The dedication and strength of military families during rotational deployments and long separations is an inspiration to us all. Michigan understands that families also serve and is honored to pay tribute to them," he said.

There are many ways to celebrate Veterans and Military Families Month. One of the best ways to show support for the military is to donate to a charity that supports them.

The Michigan Department of Military and Veterans Affairs is accepting donations to support military families with grants funding financial assistance. For more information, click: https://www.michigan.gov/dmva/military-family-relief-fund-donate.

The Michigan Veterans Affairs Agency (MVAA) is also accepting donations to help connect veterans get the services and benefits they've earned. For more details, click https://www.thepayplace.com/mi/mva/donations2/billpreview.aspx. You can also consider supporting the Michigan Veteran Homes (MVH), which is accessible at https://www.michigan.gov/dmva/military-family-relief-fund-donate.

“Michigan is home to more than 550,000 veterans and their families,” said MVAA Director Zaneta Adams. “Michigan’s veterans are brave and selfless individuals who, when duty called, willingly put themselves in harm's way to defend the lives and liberty of others.”

To learn more about employment, healthcare, education and quality-of-life benefits, veterans can contact the Michigan Veteran Resource Service Center at 1-800-MICH-VET, or visit www.michigan.gov/MVAA.

For more information on upcoming ceremonies, and activities to honor veterans and military families, please visit the MVAA events calendar at www.michigan.gov/mvaa/events.
— Benton Spirit Community Newspaper
