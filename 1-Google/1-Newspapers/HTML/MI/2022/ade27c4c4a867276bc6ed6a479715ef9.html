The Four Best Berlin Farmers Market

Written by: TBG Staff

Published on: February 8, 2022

Berlin is a beautiful city that offers many things to do. From the food culture to the history, and the arts, there is something for everyone in this city.

One thing that many people fail to realize is that Berlin is home to some amazing farmer’s markets that offer fresh produce for very reasonable prices. 

In this article, we will introduce you to four great Berlin farmers’ markets where you can find everything from organic veggies to freshly baked bread.

Table of Contents

  1. Markthalle Neun
  2. Great Market Hall Berlin on Maybachufer
  3. Friedrichshain Farmers’ Market in Boxhagener Platz
  4. Hermsdorf Markthalle in Volkspark Pankow
  5. Takeaway

Markthalle Neun

The Markthalle Neun is an indoor marketplace where you can find lots of small, independent businesses in one building.

This market has been around for more than a hundred years and it’s the perfect place to enjoy a nice lunch or dinner with friends or family because there are so many vendors offering delicious food from all over the world. 

There are tons of fresh fruit and veggies at the Markthalle Neun. You can find high quality food and drink here that won’t leave a dent in your wallet.

Plus, there’s also a ton of delicious food stalls where you can grab something to eat or drink to take home. The best part about this farmers’ market is that everything here costs less than a couple of euros. 

Great Market Hall Berlin on Maybachufer

Another great option if you’re looking for fresh produce at affordable prices is Great Market Hall Berlin on Maybachufer – also called “Wochenmarkt am Maybachufer”. There are some stalls outside under the open sky but most of them have their own kitchens where they make their dishes. 

At this farmers’ market, you can find a great selection of organic produce and lots of delicious food as well as flowers for your home or garden. It’s the perfect place to grab some fresh fruit after work on your way back from work because it’s open during weekdays until early afternoon. 

You can get everything here – from fruits, veggies, cheese, and bread to tea sets, spices, and freshly brewed coffee, plus so much more. If you’re planning a garden party, you can definitely find a great selection of plants and flowers. 

Friedrichshain Farmers’ Market in Boxhagener Platz

Another farmers’ market worth checking out for those looking for fresh produce at affordable prices is the Friedrichshain Farmers’ Market located in Boxhagener Platz – which takes place every Saturday from early until late afternoon. At this farmers’ market, you will be able to find a wide selection of fresh fruits and veggies, plus lots of delicious food. 

For the best experience here, arrive early to make sure you get everything that’s available. You can also pick up some souvenirs from Berlin for your friends or family while you’re there because many vendors offer handmade items including stationery sets and small art pieces. 

You’re going to be able to find anything at this market, whether it’s cheese from Greece or freshly baked bread from Germany – just name what you want. There are even vegan options if that’s your thing. 

Plus, all products offered here have been carefully checked by farmers who sell their produce directly so they know where every single item comes from which is great since more people demand healthier food. 

Hermsdorf Markthalle in Volkspark Pankow

The last farmers’ market worth mentioning here is the Hermsdorf Market Hall – which you can find every Sunday from early until late afternoon at a beautiful location called Volkspark Pankow. 

At this exciting place, you will be able to find lots of great products including fresh organic fruit and veggies as well as delicious meals cooked by several independent vendors from all over the world. 

This church-like building looks amazing with its old, unique architecture so it’s definitely something worth seeing when visiting Berlin. It’s also very easy to get there because it has good connections if you’re taking public transport (bus number 200 or E). 

However, you might have to wait in line for some time because this market is very popular.

The best part about coming here is the affordable prices of products so it’s definitely worth checking out if you’re looking for fresh fruit and veggies at a cheap price. 

Takeaway

Markthalle Neun, Maybachufer Market Hall, Great Market Berlin on Boxhagener Platz, Friedrichshain Farmers’ Market in Mauerpark, Volkspark Pankow Hermsdorf Markthalle – all these farmers markets are amazing places where you can find lots of different plants and flowers as well as good food at great prices. They’re perfect when visiting during weekends or after work since they’re open every day except Monday.
