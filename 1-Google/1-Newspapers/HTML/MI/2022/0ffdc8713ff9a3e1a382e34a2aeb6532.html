Gerhard M. Williams, Jr. and LeeAnn Williams

By abowman@floridaweekly.com
on October 06, 2022

Gerhard Mennin Williams, Jr. and LeeAnn Monroe Williams

Gerhard Mennen Williams, Jr. passed away September 18, 2022, less than five weeks after the death of his wife, LeeAnn Monroe Williams, August 15, 2022. Both were 81 years of age.

Mr. Williams was the only son of former Michigan Governor G. Mennen “Soapy” Williams and Nancy Williams. He was born May 30, 1941, in Lansing. Mrs. Williams was also born in Lansing January 19, 1941, to Forrest Kahl and Zelma Monroe.

Mr. and Mrs. Williams both attended Sexton High School, where they became friends at the age of 14 and started dating at 15. Mrs. Williams was graduated from Michigan State University and Mr. Williams was graduated from Princeton University. They were married June 30, 1962, and celebrated their 60th anniversary this year.

The couple lived and started a family in Ann Arbor for several years while Mr. Williams studied for his Ph.D. in urban planning from the University of Michigan. To this union were born a son, Gery, and daughter, LeeAnn.

The Williams family moved to Austin, Texas, in 1973 where Mr. Williams taught public policy at the LBJ School, University of Texas. He was recruited three years later by Conrail and the family moved to McLean, Virginia, and then to Conrail headquarters at Philadelphia where he served as a vice president of sales and marketing.

He completed his career as a consultant for CSX Railroad.

In the mid-1990s, Mr. and Mrs. Williams retired to Savannah, Georgia and their children and their families lived nearby. They filled their time with golf, tennis, and traveling in their motorhome and they shared their lives with dogs, birds, and a tortoise.

In early 2020, they moved to the Dallas, Texas area to live close to their daughter and her husband, Bruce. They enjoyed two years in Dallas, with frequent visits from their son and his wife, Deborah, and their children, Samantha and Will (G. Mennen IV).

Mackinac Island was a fundamental part of the lives of Mr. and Mrs. Williams. Much of their courtship played out on the Island as the Williams family lived first in the Governor’s Summer Residence and then in the Pines cottage on the

West Bluff. As a teenager, Mr.

Williams worked summers at the Chippewa Hotel, and Mrs.

Williams was a frequent visitor. A football player and wrestler, Mr. Williams could often be seen running up the

Fort Street hill to the Governor’s mansion.

When the family lived in

Ann Arbor, they spent each summer on Mackinac Island with their young children and

Mr. Williams’ parents, Soapy and Nancy Williams.

Mr. Williams was a lifelong friend of Charlie Puttkammer and their families became close friends. Mr. Williams and Mr. Puttkammer were both graduated from Princeton University, as were their daughters, LeeAnn and Nancy, in the same year. Mr. and Mrs. Williams were also longtime friends with the Musser and McIntire families on Mackinac Island.

The couple are preceded in death by their parents, and his sister, Wendy.

Surviving are their children and families, G. Mennen Williams III “Gery” and Deborah, of Washington, D.C., and LeeAnn and Bruce Stewart of Dallas; grandchildren Samantha Kay Williams of Chicago and G. Mennen Williams IV “Will” of Washington, D.C.

The couple are to be buried in a private ceremony for family and close friends in the Williams family plot in the Protestant Cemetery on Mackinac Island in the summer of 2023, accompanied by an Episcopalian service.
