Members of Bridgman’s Royal Court begin 2023 reigns

BRIDGMAN — The community of Bridgman has “Grape Expectations” for members of the 2023 royal court crowned on Saturday, Dec. 3.

Beginning their reigns on the Bridgman High School Performing Arts Center stage were: Miss Bridgman 2023 Javen Graves; Mr. Bridgman Aiden Conley; Miss Teen Bridgman Abby Stevens; Miss Junior Teen Brooklynn Wheeler; Mr. Junior Teen Elijah Sanders; Little Miss Bridgman Hailey Germain; and Little Mr. Bridgman Finley Boersma.

Court members are: Miss Bridgman First Runner-up Alivia Abram; Mr. Nice Guy Brad Salmon; Miss Teen First Runner-up Tierney Thomas; Miss Teen Second Runner-up Ashley Ramirez; Miss Teen Congeniality Emma Sanders; Miss Junior Teen First Runner-up Emma, Siegmund; Miss Junior Teen Second Runner-up Kinsley Lambert; Miss Junior Teen Court members Katie Lambert and Lily Munson; and Little Miss Court members Sophie Rick and Layla Rick.

Also participating in the pageant were Miss Teen contestants Jazlyn Culbertson, Emily Lambert, Katie Buist and Sarah Siegmund.

“I’m so happy, I cannot even describe it,” Javen Graves said moments after being crowned.

Graves later said she can’t wait to help build the community float.

“I’m looking forward to all the community events, and I’m really excited to represent Bridgman,” noted Abby Stevens.

Elijah Sanders said he can’t wait for the parades, while Finley Boersma (Bridgman’s first ever Little Mr.) said he is fired up about going to more pageants.

Hailey Germain said she is looking forward to building the float as well as going to different pageants.

Aiden Conley said being crowned Mr. Bridgman “is just a great feeling – I’m excited!”

Brooklyn Wheeler (who served as Little Miss Bridgman during COVID) said she is looking forward to doing everything she couldn’t do during her previous reign with her big brother (Aiden).

Hayley Germain, 9, enjoys cheerleading, soccer and swim class and want to be a veterinarian when she grows up.

Finley Boersma, 7, likes basketball, football. Legos, floor hockey, and someday wants to be a police officer.

Elijah Sanders, a fourth-grader, enjoys football, baseball, hockey, music and his “crazy dogs.” He wants to be a baseball player when he grows up.

Brooklyn Wheeler is in the sixth grade and her pastimes include band, horses and 4-H and like to eat tacos.

Abby Stevens is an eighth-grader who participates in basketball, track, volleyball and dance and enjoys drawing and camping.

Javen Graves is a senior at Bridgman High School and a former Little Miss Bridgman. Her activities include marching band, concert band, sideline cheer, volleyball, track and Key Club. She volunteers at her church and helps with Mini Bee cheerleading. Her career goal is to become an accountant.

Aiden Conley, a Bridgman High School junior, is involved in track, band, Student Senate and Key Club. He plans to become a journalist.

Judges for the first of two pageants held on the afternoon of Dec. 4 (Little Miss and Mr., Junior Teen and Teen) were: Virginia Goodenough; Megan Hildebrandt; and McKenna Smith.

Judges for the Miss and Mr. Bridgman Pageant were: Megan Roth; Dylan Catalano; and Karen Garlanger.

Emcees for the Little Miss and Mr., Junior Teen and Teen Pageant were Krystin Buist and Stephanie DeMeulenaere. Co-hosting the Miss and Mr. Bridgman Pageant were “Wild Bill” Lewis and Sarah Spoonholtz.

Ads sales awards went to: Haley Rick; Emma, Siegmund; Katie Buist; and Aiden Conley.

People’s Choice honors went to: Hailey Germain; Lily Munson; Jazlyn Culbertson; and Alivia Abram.

Saying (and dancing) their good-byes were 2022 court members Miss Bridgman Stephanie DeMeulenaere; co-Mr. Bridgmans Andreas Wilder and Mason Williamson, First Runner-up Adrianna Nimtz, and co-Second Runner-up Madi Rochefort (Ava Ward was the other 2022 Second Runner-up); Little Miss Bridgman Ravin Burkhard (Hailey Germain was first runner-up in 2022); Miss Junior Teen Bridgman Zoey Mallette and First Runner-up Kendall Steinbrook; Mr. Junior Teen David Shonk: Miss Teen Bridgman Lily Barker and First Runner-up Anne Scott.

DeMeulenaere said her year of being Miss Bridgman was filled with new experiences and relationships while representing a community that has unconditionally supported her.
