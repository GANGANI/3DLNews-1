Protesters again shut down Grand Rapids city meeting demanding justice for Patrick Lyoya

  • Updated: May. 11, 2022, 10:10 a.m.|
  • Published: May. 10, 2022, 10:05 p.m.
By
  • Michael Kransz | mkransz@mlive.com

GRAND RAPIDS, MI – Another turbulent Grand Rapids City Commission meeting was adjourned Tuesday evening after protesters, enraged by the death of Patrick Lyoya, disrupted the meeting.

Like the April 26 meeting that was also adjourned, the city commission had completed its business and was hearing general public comment when Mayor Rosalynn Bliss around 8 p.m. Tuesday, May 10, first recessed and then adjourned the meeting.

Related: Prostesters shut down Grand Rapids city meeting, demand justice for Patrick Lyoya

At the start of the meeting, Bliss had warned people that disruptions of the meeting would constitute a misdemeanor breach of peace charge under state law. The statement drew a brief outcry.

No one was arrested Tuesday because of the disruptions. However, Grand Rapids police said one person who attended the meeting was arrested outside of City Hall after the meeting after they got into a physical altercation with another meeting attendee.

In her opening remarks, Bliss said she would first ask disrupters to leave. If the disruptors didn’t leave, Bliss said she would recess the meeting, clear the Commission Chambers and then reconvene the city commission meeting.

If the disruption continued, she said, she would adjourn the meeting.

Like the last meeting that was shut down on April 26, Bliss adjourned the meeting midway through the final public comment period. This time, one person swore and Bliss turned off his microphone, reminding him of the rules against swearing.

Others began to shout and eventually Bliss recessed the meeting, saying those who were disrupting the meeting would be asked to leave.

As she recessed the meeting, people again went toward where the commissioners were seated and eventually began chanting, calling for justice in the killing of Lyoya. Bliss soon adjourned the meeting.

Lyoya, a 26-year-old Black man, was fatally shot once in the back of the head April 4 during a struggle with Grand Rapids police officer Christopher Schurr.

Kent County Prosecutor Chris Becker has yet to announce whether he will file any criminal charges against Schurr.

Protesters again demanded that Becker remove himself from making the determination on whether to charge Schurr, that Grand Rapids police officers get their own liability insurance and that Schurr be arrested.

Lyoya was pulled over the morning of April 4 because his license plate didn’t match his vehicle.

Schurr initiated the traffic stop and then tried to subdue Lyoya after Lyoya attempted to run and then struggled with the officer. The pair fought over control of Schurr’s Taser.

Eventually, Schurr was on top of Lyoya and shot him once in the back of the head.
