The Future Is Now: Mr. and Miss Benton Harbor Contestants Take BHHS Stage This Sunday, Feb. 6.

Don't miss the 2022 Miss and Mr. Benton Harbor Pageant this Sunday, Feb 6, in the Benton Harbor High School's Performing Arts Center.
Benton-Michiana Spirit News•
The Miss and Mr. Benton Harbor Pageants are back with a bang this weekend – with a theme that puts pride and progress at the center of attention. For longtime pageant chairman Rodger Reinaldo Tripplett, this year's theme, “New Beginnings...All Things Are Possible,” marks a fitting return from a pandemic-clouded 2021, when the ongoing struggle against COVID-19 forced the cancellation of both pageants.

“We chose it, because we believe our children are the future, and the future is now,” Tripplett said. “We chose that, so we could start putting their voice at the table – as it relates to their community – and what their thoughts are, as it relates to Benton Harbor. We want to note the progress. Those things happen for a reason, and it's important that we understand, as a result of this crisis, that we have come together. Things are happening in our city.”

The pageants will take place at 5:00 p.m. Sunday, February 6, at Benton Harbor High School's Performing Arts Center, 870 Colfax Ave., Benton Harbor (doors open at 3:30 p.m.). Tickets are $20, and available from any contestant; at the high school, and at the door.
Contestants will compete for the respective Miss and Mr. Benton Harbor honors, with all the major trademarks remaining in place – including a formal presentation, as well as the interview question rounds, and platform speeches from the five Miss Benton Harbor finalists, who will also treat the crowd to a special dance routine.

Certain safety procedures remain in place, due to the pandemic, Tripplett said. All those attending must wear face masks, and only those who pass temperature checks will be allowed into the building. Those who feel sick or show symptoms are asked to stay home. “We're just trying to use every precaution that we can, for our kids,” he said.

In addition, the PAC will be using ActivePure Technology AirSurface Pro, donated by the City of Benton Harbor.

There will also be a chance to meet area Blossomtime royalty, with special attention being paid to Miss Benton Harbor 2020, Jada Willis, and Mr. Benton Harbor, Jeremiah Sterling II. Willis was also named Miss Blossomtime's Second Runner-Up, while Sterling is the current Mr. Blossomtime.

“We'll be honoring and thanking them for all that they've done throughout the pandemic – the queen, in particular,” Tripplett said. “She never stopped doing her work in the community, and we're very proud of it. She really worked – when she could have, after her year was over, said that she was done. But she stayed.”

The benefits of competing go well beyond the individual titles, for those who end up winning – which is why Tripplett's stuck with both of them so long, in his familiar roles of chairman, and emcee. Asked what Benton Harbor's young men and women get from competing, Tripplett responded: “That all things are possible. Sure, everybody wants to win, but it's not about winning. It's about a process, that they're going to gain so much, from their participation.

“People stepped out onstage and got out of their comfort zone. I've seen a lot of growth, from beginning to now – one thing, I think, this process has taught this group of kids is that there are more similarities, than differences. New friendships are being garnered.”
