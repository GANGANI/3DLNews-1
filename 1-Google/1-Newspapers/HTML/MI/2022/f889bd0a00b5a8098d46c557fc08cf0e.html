New Buffalo edges Eau Claire to defend district crown

  • By David Johnson News Editor

EAU CLAIRE — The New Buffalo High School boys basketball team worked hard to earn a second straight Division 4 district title Friday evening, March 11, besting Eau Claire 66-63.

The host Beavers, staged a furious second-half rally that yielded a 50-49 lead with about six minutes left in regulation.

The lead would change hands five times down the stretch, with Ryan Vinson’s offensive rebound and put-back at the 3:17 mark gave New Buffalo a 55-54 advantage.

Three clutch scores (a Kaden Parmley scoop shot and drives to the hoop by Jeremiah Mitchell and Mark Shaw) helped the Bison maintain a slim lead (61-58) with less than a minute to go.

Eau Claire kept the pressure on to the end thanks to Karmelo Taylor’s three-pointer and a regulation bucket by Damarion Travis.

But time was running out and the Beavers needed to foul. New Buffalo made sure that ball was in Parmley’s possession in the late-going, and he came through by sinking 5 of 6 free throw opportunities.

Eau Claire had a last-second chance to to force overtime, but a half-court heave did not find its mark.

New Buffalo was in control for most of the fist half, leading 17-8 after one period and 33-20 by half-time.

“We knew it was going to be tough and they were going to make a run,” Parmley said.

The Beavers out-scored NB 24-15 in the third quarter to get back in the game.

Shaw finished with 25 points for the Bison, most coming on drives the basket. Zack Forker finished with 10 points while Parmley, Mitchell and Vinson each contributed 9 points.

Shaw said the middle was open for much of the night, so he kept attacking the basket.

Forker said both he and Vinson also took advantage of a lot of open looks in the paint.

Travis tallied a game-high 29 points for the Beavers (15-6).

New Buffalo head coach Nate Tripp credited his veteran group of players for not getting rattled when the pressure was on from a “phenomenal” Eau Claire squad.

The March 11 district championship was originally scheduled to be played at River Valley, where earlier contests had taken place.

The venue change appeared to be related to the following, posted on the afternoon of March 11 on the Chikaming Township Police Department Facebook page: “At 7:50 am Officer Holtz responded to the River Valley High School to investigate a possible threatening situation involving a student. After conducting a thorough investigation with the Principal and Vice Principal, it has been deemed that there was no credible threat to the lives of students and staff or the overall safety of the school district.

We are thankful to the students that stepped up and reported what they believed to be questionable statements and behavior and encourage them to continue to speak out when they feel a situation or circumstance doesn’t feel right. This shows genuine leadership and integrity. If you see something, say something.

In moving forward it is crucial to speak up but it is also important to allow the powers that be the time to conduct a thorough and complete investigation before jumping to conclusions.

We would also like to thank the district for their cooperation in the investigation.

Our main priority is keeping the community safe and we are able to do so through community partnership and cooperation.”

New Buffalo (22-1) advanced past the semifinal round of regionals on Monday, March 14, with a 85-46 rout of Gobles.

Parmley led New Buffalo against the Tigers with 25 points while Lijewski added 21. Shaw had a triple-double with 13 points, 10 rebounds and 10 assists.

Tristan Harbaugh scored 13 for Gobles (6-18).
