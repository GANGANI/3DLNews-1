Putin making decisions in a ‘bubble,’ Fiona Hill says of Russian invasion of Ukraine

  • Published: Mar. 01, 2022, 11:44 a.m.
By
  • Cory Morse | cmorse1@mlive.com

GRAND RAPIDS, MI – A former U.S. national security advisor believes Russian President Vladimir Putin is making decisions in a “bubble.”

There has been change in Putin’s demeanor in recent weeks, she said while in Grand Rapids to speak at an event just days after Russia invaded Ukraine.

“I think a lot of people are noticing that something seems to have flipped somewhat with Putin,” Fiona Hill told MLive in a video interview on Monday.

“Almost as if he’s made a rather emotional and, on the surface, a somewhat unexpected decision. He’s usually pretty cynical and calculating and very calm. Always very sarcastic and kind of harsh in the way that he talks about things. But the announcement that he was basically going to invade Ukraine, he was viscerally emotional, and I think that sort of drew my attention as did other people who’ve been watching him for a long time.”

Fiona Hill, former senior director for European and Russian affairs on the National Security Council under then-president Donald Trump, gave her analysis about Putin’s decision to invade Ukraine during a presentation titled “Russia and the U.S.” for the World Affairs Council of Western Michigan at Grand Rapids Community College’s Spectrum Theatre on Monday, Feb. 28.

After being in power for 22 years, Putin has created a Russian political environment with little to rein him in and is now making decisions in a “bubble,” she said.

Hill, who captured national attention when she testified at Trump’s first impeachment hearing relating to U.S. foreign aid money to Ukraine, believes people around the world should continue to protest and speak out against Putin’s leadership.

Related: ‘My heart dropped’: Grand Rapids’ Ukrainian parishioners recount the moment Ukraine came under attack

“(Putin is) the guy in the driving seat, he’s made the decision,” Hill said. “So if this turns out badly (for Russia), it’s very much on him. It’d be very hard for him to blame anyone else.”

Today, Hill is a senior fellow in the Center on the United States and Europe in the Foreign Policy program at Brookings, a research-based nonprofit public policy organization. She is also a member of the Council on Foreign Relations.
