Dr. Beverly Wright Sounds The Alarm Against Environmental Racism

Dr. Wright helped reestablish the National Black Environmental Justice Network in summer 2020 and is now helping advise President Joe Biden on his emergency environmental plan.
Benton-Michiana Spirit News•
The links between environmental pollution and racism are more than just another science project for Dr. Beverly Wright, who’s been documenting them – and sounding the alarm about them – since 1992, when she founded the Deep South South Center for Environmental Justice, at Dillard University, in New Orleans, LA.

The organization, a community-university partnership, does what its name suggests – investigating the impact of environmental racism, with a special focus on an 85-mile stretch of the Mississippi River between Baton Rouge and New Orleans, where cancer rates are disproportionately high.

Wright feels a close connection to these issues, as a child who grew up in a pollution-ravaged area, as she stated in a 2020 interview. “All the birds had disappeared except for the crows. Screens on their windows were rusting and falling off in like three months. The air was so corrosive that the paint on their cars was being melted off,” she said.

Through her work at the center, as well as numerous scholarly articles, Wright has established herself as a leading advocate for equity in environmental policy, such as using carbon pricing – essentially, a tax on pollution – to fight climate change. “When it comes to carbon trading and pricing, the bottom line is if it does not include a reduction in co-pollutants, then it’s not just and it’s not dealing with the racial inequity that exists in this country,” she said.

The center helped New Orleans residents in the aftermath of Hurricane Katrina in 2005, offering training programs and helping displaced residents. Her group was also involved in the response to the Deep Water Horizon oil spill disaster in 2010. Wright’s organization has also developed curriculums, ranging from elementary students in New Orleans, to guidelines on hazardous materials suits for the workers that use them.

Dr. Wright helped reestablish the National Black Environmental Justice Network in summer 2020 and is now helping advise President Joe Biden on his emergency environmental plan. For more information about the center, and Wright’s work there, visit https://www.dscej.org.

(Sources: www.reckonsouth.com, and https://yaleclimateconnections.org/)
