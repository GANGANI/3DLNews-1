New Year New Owners – January 12, 2022

MANISTIQUE – After just over a month on the market, the formerly student-run Grind Coffee House has new owners. The establishment was placed for sale in November after Limestone Federal Credit Union, the entity which helped the coffee shop come to life, said it had “outgrown the business model of operating as student run.”

Those new owners happen to be Hali and Daren Holt, Manistique residents who officially closed on the business Friday.

Hali, who was born and raised in Manistique, left the area to attend college, but soon returned to the area after realizing that her “heart was still in the small town I left behind.”

Daren, originally from Ohio, relocated to the area before retiring from the military.

The couple currently has three children, with one more expected in February.

Their interest in starting a business? Hali said it was as simple as, “We love coffee and we loved what The Grind Coffee House stood for.”

Hali added that, overall, she and Daren are excited to embark on this new adventure and are appreciative of all those who made it possible.

Above, Daren and Hali Holt pose in front of their new business venture – the former student run Grind Coffee House. Courtesy photo

“My husband and I are thankful for this opportunity,” she said. “We want to express our sincerest gratitude to all that had a hand in making The Grind into what it is. A thank you to Limestone Federal Credit Union, employees of The Grind, the community, students and staff of Manistique Area Schools. A heartfelt thank you to Nicolet Bank in Manistique for allowing us to make this dream come to fruition. Lastly, to Grover Real Estate and Transnation Title Agency for the professional, easy, and smooth transition, we applaud you.”

‘Wish of a Lifetime’ granted for Garden resident – October 26, 2022

WAUWATOSA, Wisc. – Allen Groll, 67, is a hardworking and humble man that has volunteered much of his life to helping those in need. Born and raised in rural Michigan, Allen worked as a logger in his family business and served as a volunteer

EMT and fireman for 30 years.

He says that he is in “awe” of these brave men and women. While he was in Froedtert’s hospital receiving treatment for a rare form of cancer, he watched the Flight for Life team daily through the window from his hospital bed and thought, “It would just be neat to meet them and hear some of their experiences…it would be fascinating for a rural person who came from humble beginnings to see what big city folk do.”

In September, Wish of a Lifetime made his wish a reality. With transportation provided by Kathy’s House, a hospital guest house providing lodging and caring support for families traveling to Milwaukee to access medical care, Allen and his wife, Dora traveled to Waukesha, Wis., to tour the Flight for Life air base and thank the dedicated staff.

The Flight for Life team welcomed Allen and Dora to their air base. This was an impactful experience for him as well as the EMT helicopter team who ranged in ages from their early 20’s to 40’s.

“This opportunity gave me a chance to reflect on what I used to do and pride in knowing there are folks out there still doing this and making sacrifices. I was glad to see the dedication that these young folks have hasn’t gone away.”

 

A new era: Hoholik’s Ace Hardware changes hands – Sept 7, 2022

MANISTIQUE – In a recent and somewhat quiet exchange, one of Manistique’s longest family-owned business has changed hands.

Mike Lynn recently purchased the Hoholik’s Ace Hardware. Last year, Lynn also acquired the NAPA Auto Parts business located next door. That business was originally established by his father in 1989.

Bill Hoholik and Lynn are both third generation owners of their respective businesses.

For Hoholik, his ownership in the hardware business dates back to 2003. At that time, the store was still located on the west side of Manistique at the corner of Deer and Houghton streets.

After nearly 63 years at that location, they purchased the former Do It Best Hardware building on River Street in 2009 and quickly settled in their new, larger location.

Starting in the auto-parts industry, Lynn has diversified into hardware. He operates two True Value stores and three Ace Hardware locations. His most recent purchase was the 50- year family-owned business, Parkers Hardware, in Sault Ste. Marie.

According to both Hoholik and Lynn, the transition should be seamless to the public. The business name will remain the same and Hoholik will continue to assist customers with hardware needs. He will be working on a limited three-day a-week schedule — Tuesday, Wednesday and Thursday.

One of Manistique’s longest running businesses officially closed its doors for the last time on Nov. 30. After servicing the areas electronics for nearly 80 years, Manistique Radio Center has closed. “We have been at this a long time and I am going to miss this place,” he said. “I have been blessed with a great store, great employees and a great customer base. Manistique Radio Center closes after nearly 80 years of business – December 7, 2022

MANISTIQUE – One of Manistique’s longest running businesses has officially closed its doors after nearly 80 years in business.

Manistique Radio Center first opened in the early 1940s on Deer Street in Manistique, and was owned and operated by Henry (Hank) and Dorothy Brolin. The Brolin’s operated the business for over 40 years while raising their family before finally selling the business to Gerry and Mary Messenger in 1982.

As time progressed, the building the Manistique Radio Center was originally located in became too small.

In 1988, the Messengers purchased the larger building located on Cedar Street. At the new location they continued to offer a variety of products and services.

Over the last few decades products included video rentals, sales of electronics including televisions, digital cameras and VCR’s, and of course, the servicing of the said electronics.

 

Disruptive competitors and changing technology trends left smaller businesses finding themselves unable to compete, and Manistique Radio Center was no exception.

“Technology is moving so fast right now, that a business like ours can’t keep up with it,” explained Gerry. “You have to buy the inventory to have the product that’s moving at that time, and then all of a sudden it becomes not wanted, and something new is out there taking its place, so you have to go out there and buy the new inventory. Unless you are a big box store, a business like this cannot survive.”

While the Messengers had hoped to run their business for a bit longer, they warmly recall memories of their time they were able to serve the community, and expressed gratitude to have had a loyal base standing by them.

Looking to the future, the Messengers say they are looking forward to taking it easy and enjoying time with their family.

“We are going to enjoy being able to take life easy, visit with the grandkids, take a week or two in the winter and maybe go south. Other than that, we are planning on staying here,” said Gerry.

 

Now Showing: New Owners – April 20, 2022

MANISTIQUE – Manistique’s iconic theater has officially changed hands. The sale and new management team were announced last week.

The cinema’s newest owners are Brian and Diana Taylor, with local Don Erickson stepping in as cinema manager.

Erickson will lead the cinema until the Taylors can “make the move permanently to the area.”

“Just like thousands of vacationing people, we love the U.P. and for the last 50 years, we have enjoyed Manistique,” said Brian. “It is the perfect hub for visiting all the other beautiful areas. My life has always been full of adventures, but one dream that I always had was to own a movie theater. After we purchased the theater, I told my wife that now my life is complete – I’ve fulfilled all my goals and now I can relax and eat all the popcorn every night I want.

 

What a blessing it is, to have a business that can serve a large area of the U.P. with entertaining movies and refreshments,” he continued. “I don’t know about everyone else, but I need an escape, a place I can go and relax, and get away from the world’s chaos. To be able to treat the kids to loads of popcorn and snacks, while making them laugh at a silly cartoon. There is nothing better than being with like-minded people screaming, laughing, or crying together while watching a great film. It’s all about that theater experience.

I want everyone to be able to have that special experience that I love.”

The cinema last sold in 2015 to the mother/daughter team of Debbie and Toni St. Pierre. The duo, along with Paul Gibbs, spent a significant amount of time renovating the building, which had multiple issues, including water damage. The renovation also included an upgrade to a digital projector. By the time they were finished, everything in the theater except the seats, the two screens, and the fabric on the walls in screen one had been updated. In fact, the theater was also re-branded as Cedar Street Cinemas.

On April 12, Toni announced via the Cedar Street Cinemas Facebook page that the cinema had found new owners after initially being listed in 2019. At that time, St. Pierre stated that she was ready to “move on to a new adventure”.

“We just wanted to let you all know as of Friday the 15th, the cinema will be under new ownership,” St. Pierre stated last week. “We cannot thank all of our customers enough for the last six-plus years for the continued support, good laughs, and great times. Thank you to our amazing family and staff for sticking by our side and all the help making the cinema what it is today. We couldn’t have done it without you.”

 

Wouldn’t it be Nice – February 2, 2022

MANISTIQUE – The westside of Manistique is now home to a brand-new outdoor ice rink. The rink, free and open to the public, was created and will be maintained with volunteer labor and donations.

According to Jason McEvers, vice-chairperson of the Manistique Recreation board, the idea for the outdoor ice rink first arose in the “midst of lockdowns and closures in the winter of 2020-21”, when typical winter events are hosted by the city.

“The Recreation Board was striving to find some sort of activity for the city residents as Little Bear West Arena was closed to the public and the city could not operate the Frosty 5K, or the Little Jiggers Kids’ Ice Fishing Derby,” he explained. “Between cabin fever and press coverage of the Houghton ‘ribbon’ (a public outdoor ice-skating ribbon in the city’s Ray Kestner Waterfront Park created in 2021) some residents discussed the concept of an outdoor rink with our parks and recreation board members.”

Since the idea for an outdoor skating area came a “bit late” in the season in 2021, McEvers said plans were put into motion to get started on the project for the 2021-22 winter.

“Planning for the 2022 rink started in the fall,” he explained. “There were some details that needed to be sorted out prior to allowing use of city property and Blanchard Park was identified as the best candidate for the outdoor rink. Blanchard Park is situated between the Habitat for Humanity – Hiawathaland building and the railroad on the northside of Highway M-94. The park had been donated to the city in 2009 by the Blanchard family.”

During the planning process, McEvers noted that it was determined that some city resources would be needed for construction and start-up of the rink, and that volunteers would be vital in creating and maintaining the ice.

“The rec board approved the use of the property for the outdoor rink for the 2022 ice season,” he said. “The volunteers came together quickly and established the rink. A shout out to all the volunteers who helped with this project, especially those who stood outside in the arctic blast to get the water down.”

 

What’s brewing at LaTulip Brewing Co. – Septeber 28, 2022

COOKS – A new family owned brewery, LaTulip Brewing Co., recently opened in Cooks, Mich. on Aug. 22. Jeff LaTulip, in partnership with his cousin Jason LaTulip, purchased the building two years earlier, previously known as the Garden Bay Winery in August of 2020.

Jeff grew up in the area and after graduating high school and furthering his education at Michigan Tech University where he obtained a degree in mechanical engineering, he discovered craft beer for the first time.

After college, Jeff and his family moved downstate for six years before ultimately deciding to move back to the Upper Peninsula. During this time, Jeff learned the process of home-brewing craft beers and decided to take the next step.

Currently, they offer seven craft beers on tap and are working diligently to implement their own sodas, wines, ciders, seltzers, and add brick fired pizza to their menu in the future.

Additionally, you can find hats, TagABrew tags; a copper memory tag you collect as you visit breweries across the country, and apparel which is made inhouse by Jeff’s wife, Alecia.

Looking to the future, Jeff says, “We hope to keep LaTulip Brewing Co. a family friendly environment and be something we love coming to do every day.”

Under (the weather) Boardwalk – June 15, 2022

MANISTIQUE – The Schoolcraft County Board of Commissioners has pledged to use a portion of American Rescue Plan Act funds to assist in the repair of the Manistique Boardwalk. The decision was made during a meeting of the board last week.

ARPA was signed into law March 2021 to provide additional relief and address the continued impact of COVID-19 on the economy, public health, state and local governments, individuals, and businesses. The county received the first of two $800,000 payments in June of 2021 – the second of the $1.6 million will be dispersed this year. A special ARPA committee, formed by the board, stipulates how and when the funds will be spent.

The board then voted unanimously to approve a $65,000 disbursement from the county’s ARPA funds to the city for boardwalk repairs.
