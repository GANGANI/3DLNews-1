    Roseville police say special needs student found after running away from school

    Student to receive mental health counseling

    Share this:

    • Click to share on Twitter (Opens in new window)
    • Click to share on Facebook (Opens in new window)
    • Click to print (Opens in new window)
    By Mitch Hotts | mhotts@medianewsgroup.com | Macomb Daily
    PUBLISHED: December 13, 2022 at 1:58 p.m. | UPDATED: December 13, 2022 at 8:27 p.m.

    An Eastland Middle School special needs student was reunited with her family Tuesday afternoon after she apparently bolted from the school while experiencing a mental issue, according to police.

    Officers from the Roseville Police Department, Michigan State Police and MSP’s helicopter unit all participated in the search for the student in the area of Common Road, east of Utica Road. She was found about 1 p.m., Roseville police said.

    Authorities were concerned not only about the student’s well being, but the fact she was out on foot in 33-degree weather.
