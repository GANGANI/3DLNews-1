Oakland County drain commissioner attends meeting

Holly Twp. - Oakland County Drain Commissioner John McCulloch came to Holly Township on Tuesday to discuss major initiatives that…

  • By Cheryl Dennison
  • May 25, 2004

Holly Twp. - Oakland County Drain Commissioner John McCulloch came to Holly Township on Tuesday to discuss major initiatives that have been undertaken by his office, but residents were more interested in what's happening in their own back yard. Two topics residents brought to the attention of the drain commissioner included the lake level of Bush Lake and problems with the county's maintenance of the Holly Patterson Drain.

Resident Larry Lilly asked McCulloch if there was anything the Drain Commission could do to help them get a legal lake level established for Bush Lake. He also questioned him about the lack of county maintenance on the Holly Patterson Drain, which is an issue that concerns many residents.

McCulloch said residents must formally petition the Drain Commission about establishing a legal lake level.

The water level of Bush Lake has been under fire as the level of the lake, which is controlled by a weir in the village of Holly, has been lowered. Conflict arose regarding the flooding of wetlands on property owned by a developer. Most of the surrounding property owners believe the lake level is too low.

In answer to the questions regarding the drain, McCulloch explained the Holly Patterson Drain is a Chapter 4 Drain, which was originally used only for agricultural purposes but it is now a much more residential area. He also said the Drain Commission is only authorized tax dollars in the amount of $2,500 for maintenance of the drain and it isn't enough.

In order for the Drain Commission to clean the drain adequately, he said the amount needs to be changed, which would require a special assessment to residents. Lilly said residents formed a task force and cleaned the drain themselves a couple of years ago. He also said the maintenance of the drain should be turned over to the residents.

McCulloch also discussed county initiatives. He said a comprehensive Master Plan project has been undertaken by the county with the help of a federal grant. Those working on the plan will be visiting communities in Oakland County to determine needs in the area and will seek community input, he added.

McCulloch also said the Oakland County Drain Commission is working with Jeffrey Wright, the drain commissioner in Genesee County. He said Wright is looking at alternative water sources from Lake Huron for Genesee County and Oakland County is keeping their eye on the results of a feasibility study done by Genesee County.

McCulloch said Oakland County is interested in watching what happens in the study because of problems which have occurred, such as last year's blackout that resulted in the loss of water for many communities. He said communities who operate on community wells are also looking at alternatives due to federally mandated requirements of stricter arsenic standards in water supplies.

He urged residents to contact him with any concerns at (248) 858-0958.
