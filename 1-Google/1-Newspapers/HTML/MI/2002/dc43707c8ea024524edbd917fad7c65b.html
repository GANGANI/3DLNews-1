Leota M. Briggs

Leota M. Briggs, 77, of Shelby, passed away Sunday, Nov. 3, 2002 at the Oceana County Medical Care Facility.

She was born July 10, 1925 in Hawkins, Wis. She came to Three Oaks, Mich. at the age of 3 and lived there until 1958. Mrs. Briggs lived in Galien from 1958 until 1970 when she came to Shelby. She was preceded in death by her husband, Richard. Mrs. Briggs had been employed as a nurses aid at Unity Hospital in Buchanan. She gave 25 years of volunteer service to Muskegon-Oceana C.A.A.P.

She is survived by her daughter, Faye (Curt) Trott of Shelby; grandchildren, Aaron Trott, Jennifer Trott, Carrie (Tim) Nania, Tony (Heather) Nania, Carl (Carol) West, Lisa West, and Don (Missy) West; and nine great-grandchildren. She was also preceded in death by her sons, George and Floyd Briggs; daughter, Debbie Mollett; brother, Nelson Teeter; and sister, Julia Marrow.

Funeral service Tuesday, Nov. 5, 11 a.m. at Cooper-Harris Funeral Home, Shelby with Rev. Peggy Boltz officiating. Interment in Galien Cemetery, in Galien, Mich. Memorials to the Oceana County Medical Care Activities Room are appreciated.      *

Charles H. Kiefer

Charles H. Kiefer, 82, of Pentwater, passed away Friday morning, Nov. 1, 2002 at Ludington Woods AFC Home.

He was born April 25, 1920 in Chicago, Ill., the son of Charles and Hattie (Tunsman) Kiefer. He married Elizabeth (Betty) J. Candlin Sept. 18, 1943 in Chicago. He was in the U.S. Army during WW II. Charles retired from Mutual Trust Life Insurance Company after 40 years. He was a member of Palm City Presbyterian Church in Florida and was affiliated with Centenary United Methodist Church in Pentwater. He was a member of the Union League Club in Chicago, and Pentwater Yacht Club.

Survivors include his wife, Betty Kiefer of Pentwater; sons, Charles C. and Nancy Kiefer of Scottville, and Donald C. and Susan Kiefer of Glendale, Wis.; grandchildren, Donna and Doug Miller of Pentwater, Charlotte Kiefer and Carolyn and Jason Shimel both of Ludington, Steven Kiefer and Courtney Kiefer both of Glendale, Wis.; three great-grandchildren; and sister, Loraine and Beryl Sprinkel of Illinois.

Funeral service Monday, Nov. 4, 11 a.m. at Tate Funeral Chapel, Pentwater with Rev. Mike Tupper officiating. Burial in Pentwater Cemetery. It is suggested that memorials may be given to Centenary United Methodist Church or Palm City Presbyterian Church.      *

Warren Luther Stanton

Warren Luther Stanton, 86, died Wednesday, Oct. 30, 2002 at the Oceana County Medical Care Facility.

He was born in Trempealeau, Wis., to Carl and Estella (Courtois) Stanton Sept. 9, 1916. The family moved to Muskegon where he graduated from Muskegon High School in 1934. He worked in the Muskegon area for many years, having many jobs during difficult times including the Norge Co. He married June Dibble in 1937. They had a daughter, Janice. Warren became Marshall for the Village of Hesperia. He was then hired as undersheriff of Oceana County. He served as sheriff of Oceana County from Jan. 1, 1965 to Dec. 31, 1976. His wife June died in 1968. He married Marguerite North Tate who was a Deputy County Clerk. Warren was a member of the Fraternal Order of Police, Masonic Lodge and the Hart Area Fire Department. He enjoyed hunting, traveling and most of all fishing. He loved helping his family, friends and neighbors.

He survived by his wife, Marguerite; daughter, Janice (Charles) Hunt of Hesperia; step-children, Rex (Elaine) Tate of Hudsonville, and Jean (Michael) Powers of Hart; grandchildren, Todd Rose of Newaygo, and Jeffrey (Pearl) Rose of Mesa, Ariz.; step-grandchildren, Brent (Colleen) Tate of Hudsonville, Brenda (Jon) Zwyghuizen of Middleville, Todd (Amy) Powers of Rockford, and Dr. Timothy Powers (and fiance´ Ann Kik) of Grand Rapids; one great-grandchild; six step-great-grandchildren; sister-in-law, Evelyn (Floyd) Fox of Shelby; brothers-in-law, Charles Ferguson North (and wife Evelyn) of Dexter, Mich., and Clifford Smith of Plymouth; and several nieces and nephews. He was preceded in death by his parents; and his sister, Lorraine Smith of Gladwin.

Funeral service Saturday, Nov. 2, 11 a.m. at Hart Congregational United Church of Christ with Rev. Lorraine Boucon and Rev. Earnest Cummings officiating. Interment in West Hesperia Cemetery. Memorials to Oceana Cancer Relay for Life or the Masonic Lodge of Michigan are appreciated. Arrangements by Cooper-Harris Funeral Home, Shelby.      *

Richard Tatro

Tatro, Richard, 55, of Ferry Township, Hesperia, Sunday, Nov. 3, 2002 at his home. Arrangements by Cooper-Harris Funeral Home, Shelby.      *
