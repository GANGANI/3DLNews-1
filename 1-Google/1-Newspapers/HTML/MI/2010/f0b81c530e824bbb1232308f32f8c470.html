New Buffalo, Bee soccer teams roll into second season

  • By David Johnson
    News Editor
  • Oct 20, 2010
  • Oct 20, 2010

NEW BUFFALO — The New Buffalo High School soccer team picked the right time to get hot.

The Bison knocked off Red Arrow Conference rivals Lake Michigan Catholic and Covert during the last week of the regular season to even their league mark at 4-4.

“We’ve won three out of the last four games,” said New Buffalo coach Randy Veldman. “The guys are finally gelling at the right time — going into districts.”

The Oct. 12 victory over the Lakers, by a 4-3 margin, was especially sweet.

Alex Dixon tallied two goals, Madison Mashburn had a goal and two assists, and Cosmo Briatta scored the other goal.

Kendall Artz made 10 saves in goal to help seal the big win.

“Everybody played really hard. Our defense stepped it up,” said Artz. “We had three starters out, and we had to play a bunch of guys off the bench — they played their hearts out.”

New Buffalo then bested Covert 5-3 on Oct. 14.

Mashburn scored twice and assisted on another point while Briatta, Chuck Dabbs and Tom McCullom also contributed goals.

Sam Bartelmay was credited with two assists.

Artz stopped 18 Bulldog shots in goal.

The Bison also walloped Saugatuck 10-2 on Oct. 5.

New Buffalo finished the season on Oct. 16 with a 3-1 setback against an 11-4 La Lumiere squad.

McCullom booted in the lone NB goal (with an assist from Ben Brown) while the Lakers goals were scored by Matt Jelinek, Will Kesling and Nick Struyf.

The Bridgman High School soccer team reached the title match of the Lakeland Conference tourney before falling 1-0 to Edwardsburg on Oct. 13 as Luke Rohrman scored the game’s only goal.

Since the Bees came in alone in first place, they ended up sharing the league title with the Eddies.

Bridgman opened defense of its Division 4 district crown on Monday, Oct. 18, by topping Howardsville Christian 2-0. Michael Kamp and Mat Englund scored goals, while Ryan Randall and Kyle Rotondo had assists.

Jackson Kamp came up with five saves for Bridgman.

The Bees (13-7) were set to face Michigan Lutheran at 7:15 p.m. Wednesday, Oct. 20, while New Buffalo gets to see Lake Michigan Catholic again in the 5:30 p.m. semifinal. Both games will be played at Marcellus, with the winners advancing to a title match at 6:45 p.m. Saturday, Oct. 23.
