Max Meadows residents face meth charges

  • 0

Larry Stoots

From staff reports

Two Max Meadows residents face drug charges after a search warrant was executed at their 135 Ashley Lane residence on Friday. Donna C. Smith and Larry W. Stoots were taken into custody by the Wythe County Sheriff’s Office, the Claytor Lake Drug Task Force and the Virginia State Police.

According to Sheriff Keith Dunagan, the warrant was a result of an investigation involving the manufacture of methamphetamine. The search revealed a meth lab at the residence where Smith and Stoots were.

Smith, 28, was charged with possessing two or more essential ingredients with the intent to manufacture meth, conspiracy to possess two or more essential ingredients with intent to manufacture meth, manufacturing meth, conspiracy to manufacture meth, allowing a mentally incapacitated adult to be present during the manufacture of meth, conspiracy to allow a mentally incapacitated adult to be present during the manufacture of meth, two counts of allowing a child to be present during the manufacture of meth and two counts of conspiracy to allow a child to be present during the manufacture of meth.

She was remanded to the New River Valley Regional Jail in Dublin. Smith, who has 2010 Wythe County convictions for manufacturing/etc. a controlled substance and child abuse or neglect, is being held on a $10,000 secure bond.

Stoots, 27, faces the same charges.

According to court records, Smith has two small children and Stoots’ mentally incapacitated brother lived at the residence.

Wythe County Department of Social Services was called to the scene on Friday, Dunagan said. The department was also working to place Stoot’s brother with relatives, according to the sheriff.

A preliminary hearing for Smith and Stoots will be held April 16 in Wythe County General District Court.
