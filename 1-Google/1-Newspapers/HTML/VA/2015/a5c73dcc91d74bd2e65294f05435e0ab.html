Channel 7 News anchor and ministry celebrate local women

  • By Zann Nelson

  • Oct 31, 2015
  • 0

By Zann Nelson

Several local women were honored at the recent 2015 Excel Awards luncheon held at the Culpeper Center. The Women in Worship Ministries hosted the event Oct. 17, with journalist Maureen Bunyan as the guest speaker.   

According to Nan Butler Roberts, co-founder and CEO of Women in Worship Ministries, the organization was founded in 2009 and is the vision of three women who combined their God-given talents, skills and abilities to produce a "Women's Conference" in 2006, 2007 and 2008.    

These conferences were so successful and in such high demand, that they provided the blueprint for other area churches and ministries to follow their lead, Roberts said.   

In time, God revealed the vision to move the Women's Conference into a "ministry that would provide an opportunity for more women and girls to draw closer to God and learn what He has in store for their lives,” Roberts said.

In 2014, Women In Worship Ministries, Inc. (WIW), seized an opportunity to recognize women in a variety of categories for their achievements and celebrated its First "EXCEL" Honoree Awards.   

At the recent luncheon, 10 women received the 2015 EXCEL Awards, including Marie Ferris, Susan Keller and Kym Majors, all of Culpeper; Aline Johnson, of Rappahannock County, Karen White, of Fauquier County, and Marceline Catlett, of Fredericksburg.  

Excel 2015 Awards

 ‘Generation Next’

This year, Women in Worship Ministries also honored 10 younger women who have proven to be standouts in their communities. These women and teens set themselves apart as leaders and received the “Generation Next” Award.   

“It is appropriate that their values and commitment to shape their future in a positive way be supported and recognized,” stated the WIW organizers.

“These young women are exemplary role models of what the future can be and deserve all the accolades available.”

The guest speaker   

A 2014 Excel honoree, Maureen Bunyan celebrated the accomplishments of the honorees and encouraged the women to continue to excel.   

Born in Aruba, the eldest of three daughters, Bunyan’s family immigrated to America and settled in Wisconsin when she was 11 years old. The product of a family deeply devoted to education, Bunyan received her undergraduate degree in English and education from the University of Wisconsin-Milwaukee. She then attended the Columbia Graduate School of Journalism and holds a master’s degree from the Harvard University Graduate School of Education.   

Broadcast journalism has been Bunyan’s love for decades, and she currently anchors the 6 p.m. weeknight newscasts for ABC7/WJLA-TV. Equal to her commitment to journalism is a dedication to encouraging women to be true to their natural talents, establish goals and follow their dreams.  

She is a founder of the International Women’s Media Foundation which serves women in the media in 100 countries. She is also a founder of the National Association of Black Journalists, where she was inducted into the NABJ Hall of Fame in January of 2014.   

Bunyan has an extensive record of service to the community. She is a board member of the International Women’s Media Foundation. She also serves on the National Advisory Board of the Casey Journalism Center on Children & Families; the Girl Scout Council of the Nation’s Capital Women’s Advisory Board; the Advisory Committee of Women in Film & Video and is a board member of Women of Washington   

Full biographies of all of the 2014 and 2015 EXCEL Award recipients and detailed information about the Women in Worship Ministries can be found at www.womeninworshipministires.org .
