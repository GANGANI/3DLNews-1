As group seeks abolition of death penalty, here's a look at the 113 people executed in Virginia since 1982

  • Richmond Times-Dispatch
  • Jan 7, 2020
  • Jan 7, 2020
  • 0

Virginia has executed 113 people since the U.S. Supreme Court allowed capital punishment to resume in 1976; the second most in the country behind only Texas (539).

Virginians for Alternatives to the Death Penalty announced on Nov. 18 that 13 Virginians who have lost a family member to homicide are asking the General Assembly to make Virginia the 22nd state to abolish the death penalty.
