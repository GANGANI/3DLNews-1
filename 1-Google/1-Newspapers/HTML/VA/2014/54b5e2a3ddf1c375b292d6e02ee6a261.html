William Monroe swimmers take part in Central Virginia Ben Hair Invitational

  • Jan 16, 2014
  • Jan 16, 2014 Updated Aug 5, 2020
  • 0
  • Facebook
  • Twitter
  • WhatsApp
  • SMS
  • Email
  • Print
  • Save

The William Monroe boys and girls swim teams competed last week’s Central Virginia Ben Hair Invitational.

The meet of 12 Central Virginia High Schools was to raise funds and awareness for the Ben Hair Foundation.

The Invitational is named after a young swimmer who died in an accident while in college. His desire was to teach more youth, especially under privileged, how to swim. His parents have carried out that dream through a foundation in his name.  

Of the 12 teams, William Monroe finished in ninth place. However, many of the teams swimming were Division 4, Division 5 or prep schools.

William Monroe had three individuals place in the top eight in their respective specialty events: Samantha Hammer, eighth in 500-meter freestyle; Wyatt Morin, seventh in 200-meter individual medley; and Kara Taylor, seventh in 100-meter breaststroke.

Monroe had one relay place in the top eight: Girls 400-meter relay, fifth place, Samantha Hammer, Skylar Valentin, Kara Taylor and Zoe Williams.

To this point in the season, the boys team district record is 2-3.  The girls team record is 4-1.

The boys have wins over Manassas Park and Madison, but losses to Central, George Mason and Clarke.

The women have wins over Manassas Park, Central, Madison and Clarke. Their only loss is to George Mason.

Event winners so far this year include Wyatt Morin, Tyler Bunyea, Alec Yost and Lucas Ward for the boys; and Kara Taylor, Samantha Hammer, Zoe Williams and Skylar Valentin for the women.

“After graduating six seniors last year, we have replenished with plenty of new swimmers,” said Monroe coach Chris Taylor. “The kids are working really hard and improving at a rate which should have us very competitive in the district.”
