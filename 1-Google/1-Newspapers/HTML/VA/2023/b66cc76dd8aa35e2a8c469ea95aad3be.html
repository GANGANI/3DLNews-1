1. Friends collaborate on new thriller set in small-town Virginia

Two friends, under the pen name Katherine Greene, wrote a thriller titled “The Woods Are Waiting.”

Set in a rural Appalachian town in Virginia, the novel tells the story of two childhood friends who set out to learn the truth about a local legend of an evil entity that haunts the woods and if it's connected to the disappearance of children. When another child goes missing, they seek to discover the town's secrets rooted in its terrifying past.

The book was a collaboration between British authors Abbi Walters Horne and Claire Riley, who she met in England. Horne, who grew up in Shenandoah County, saw Woodstock and Edinburg in her mind while creating the fictional town of Blue Cliff, Virginia.

2. Police chase across three counties ends at Walmart in Warren County

A three-county car chase ended at the Warren County Walmart on Aug. 7. The pursuit started near the 240-mile marker in Rockingham County and traveled through Shenandoah and Warren Counties on Interstate 81.

The suspect vehicle crashed into a median and three people fled toward Walmart on U.S. 340-522 while one person remained in the vehicle. Law enforcement officers pursued the suspects on foot and other units went to the Walmart area. A suspect tried to carjack a vehicle and injured the driver.

The three suspects went into Walmart, prompting the store to activate an alarm and evacuate. Law enforcement officers set up a perimeter around the Walmart and started to search the store. Officers found and detained all three suspects.

State police recovered a variety of drugs estimated to be worth $54,000. 

3. Shenandoah County deputy fired after being charged with DWI

Shenandoah County Deputy Sheriff Yolisma Uribe-Campos, 30, was fired after state police charged her with a DWI on Sept. 2. 

A preliminary breath test showed she had a blood-alcohol content of 0.33% — more than four times the legal limit of 0.08%. She was charged after a Virginia State Police trooper responded to a crash on Shenandoah Avenue involving a gray Audi sedan and two parked vehicles, according to a criminal complaint.

Sheriff Timothy Carter said that he spoke to Uribe-Campos and fired her after his office conducted an internal investigation.

4. Gas line explosion rocks Strasburg area

A gas line exploded in Shenandoah County, shaking nearby residents who heard the blast around 8:40 a.m. July 25.

The explosion, which caused huge flames, occurred in the area of Battlefield and Copp roads near Strasburg. The pipeline, owned by TC Energy, was shut off shortly after the blast, preventing flames from spreading. No injuries were reported. Interstate 81 was shut down for a short time at the 294-mile marker.

A federal agency later reported that the explosion was likely caused by cracking in the pipeline. 

5. New Front Royal eatery offers fresh, homemade quick bites

Warren County builder and entrepreneur Shelly Cook opened Poppy's Place in mid-August at 205 S. Royal Ave. in Front Royal.

Poppy’s leans into bountiful crops, using whatever is in season to serve fresh, homemade soups like tomato basil, squash chowder, and pumpkin, Cook said, adding that she will be making bread fresh daily, as well as sweet treats like cookies, cinnamon rolls, and other desserts.

“We’re really looking forward to serving our little hometown and having an additional place that you can come and eat and have another choice,” Cook said. “We have a lot of amazing restaurants in Front Royal and this is just another option.”

6. Builder revitalizing downtown Front Royal with motel, bookstore remodels

You might find Shelly Cook in the kitchen or behind the counter at Poppy’s Place, her new restaurant in downtown Front Royal. Or maybe you’ll find her next door at Royal Oak Bookstore, which she purchased this summer. Still no luck? Try the motel under renovation down the block where she’s bringing back its mid-century charm.

Cook, an entrepreneur and builder who operates Servants Hands Construction, is one busy lady on a mission to bring function and beauty to the town she loves.

“I’m trying to re-do the town one project at a time,” Cook said. “I want people to fall in love with our little town. No place is perfect, but I think we have the most wonderful community. I love the people here. I think they’re kind, generous, loving.”

7. Gourmet bakery opening in Front Royal

Melania’s Gourmet, which opened in September at the corner of Jackson Street and South Royal Avenue, serves up pastries, Costa Rican coffee and hand-painted chocolates in an atmosphere that welcomes relaxation both indoors and out.

“I want everyone to feel welcome,” pastry chef Melania Castegnaro said. “I want everyone to feel that they can come here and get the cookie, the cheesecake or a pop tart and it takes them back to when they were kids.”

Castegnaro, who has an extensive background and education in the culinary arts, uses family recipes as well as those developed from her years of creating. Ingredients like fruits and vegetables are sourced as locally as possible and are mostly organic. 

8. Hop into downtown Strasburg's newest restaurant, Blue Wing Frog

Blue Wing Frog, which moved from downtown Front Royal to King Street in Strasburg, had a soft opening in late September. It was a big hit with residents, said co-owner Robert Hall. 

The response to the new restaurant was overwhelming with tables frequently full and residents placing regular to-go orders.

“We love this town, man,” Hall said. “Strasburg is a quaint little town, about half the size of Front Royal, with a little bit more down-home flavor, as opposed to the hustle and bustle going on there. We’re excited about the new audience and getting more locals involved.”

9. Two dead, two injured in Mt. Jackson shooting

Two people died and two were wounded in Mount Jackson Shooting at approximately 2:47 a.m. Aug. 27 in the 200 block of Orkney Drive.

Police found a 63-year-old man and a 51-year-old woman deceased. Emergency medical services workers took the remaining victims, a 67-year-old woman and a 48-year-old man, to hospitals with critical injuries. Police found a handgun at the scene and spent bullet casings in the area. Police found one of the victims, who they believed was the suspect, with a self-inflicted gunshot wound.

Mount Jackson Police later revealed Frederic S. Conrad approached Jane L. Moomaw and others outside the residence at 2:45 a.m. An argument ensued and Conrad shot Moomaw and the other two people before shooting himself.

10. Mount Jackson nonprofit has big plans for old schoolhouse

A nonprofit wants to bring new life to the old Triplett School in Mount Jackson as a community center and performing arts venue. Community Resources for Education and Wellness, or CREW, was established earlier by David Ferguson and Randy Doyle with the goal of partnering "with public and private organizations to a to advance education.”

W. Todd Holtzman, who purchased the 6044 Main St. property after the fire department relocated, leased the facility to CREW for 10 years for $1 per year with the option to buy at any time for a low fixed price. 

Along with upgrades to the classrooms, planned renovations include accessibility upgrades, a new HVAC system and converting the cafeteria’s commercial kitchen to a more conventional kitchen the public can use. The big-ticket item is the large gymnasium and stage — which Ferguson envisions as a space for Parks and Recreation programming and a public performing arts facility.
