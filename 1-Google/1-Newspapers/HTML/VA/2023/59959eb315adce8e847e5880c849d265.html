IN THE NEWS 

D.C. teams would move to Va. in tentative agreement

Gov. Glenn Youngkin said a tentative deal is in place with the owner of the Washington Wizards and Washington Capitals to move the teams to northern Virginia. Youngkin on Wednesday announced a $2 billion entertainment and sports complex in Alexandria. The teams currently play at Capital One Arena in Washington. 

The proposal requires approval from the General Assembly. The site is in the Potomac Yard area of Alexandria, near Virginia Tech’s ambitious Innovation Campus being developed.

RULING: A judge on Tuesday ruled that a database used for the state's voting rights restoration process is exempt from public records law. The Virginia NAACP filed a Freedom of Information Act request seeking records on how the state reinstates voting rights of those with felony convictions who have served their sentences. Gov. Glenn Youngkin's administration provided the documents, but the group sued, saying some records were withheld.

ODDS AND ENDS 

CLOSED: A report into the quality of Virginia's state-run psychiatric hospitals said the Commonwealth Center for Children and Adolescents in Staunton is an "immediate threat to the health and safety of the patients" and should be shut down. The report was compiled by the Joint Legislative Audit and Review Commission, the auditing body of the General Assembly. The report called the facility the worst of the nine state psychiatric hospitals, with the highest rates of violence, self-harm and substantiated human rights complaints. Officials said they are working to improve conditions and have removed leadership. It is Virginia's only state-run psychiatric hospital for children.

APPROVED: County supervisors in northern Virginia last week approved one of the world’s largest data center projects after a public hearing that ran through the night and lasted 27 hours. The Prince William County Board of Supervisors voted 4-3, with one abstention, in favor of the Digital Gateway project. It will bring as many as 37 data centers over approximately 2,000 acres in the western part of the county, not far from Manassas National Battlefield. The project drew opposition from residents concerned about noise, the need for electricity and high-voltage transmission lines, and the possibility that it would damage views of the battlefield.

IN THE NEWS 

Roanoke center to honor veterans

The Virginia Veterans Care Center in Roanoke was renamed Tuesday to recognize two decorated Vietnam War veterans. Retired Army Col. Paris Davis received the Medal of Honor for rescuing three U.S. soldiers during hand-to-hand combat in 1965. Retired Navy Capt. Eugene “Red” McDaniel spent six years being tortured as a prisoner of war after ejecting from his aircraft above above North Vietnam in 1967. Both are from northern Virginia. The newly named Davis & McDaniel Veterans Care Center has 224 beds. 

“What a profound honor it is to name this facility after two heroes who stood for freedom,” Gov. Glenn Youngkin said at the dedication ceremony.  

CRASH: A Tesla was speeding and operating on its autopilot system before it crashed with a crossing tractor-trailer last July, killing the car's driver, the Fauquier County Sheriff's Office said Tuesday. The Tesla was traveling 70 mph on four-lane U.S. 29 near Opal, 25 mph over the speed limit.

The death of Pablo Teodoro III, 57, is the third since 2016 in which a Tesla using autopilot ran underneath a crossing tractor-trailer. The National Highway Traffic Safety Administration is investigating 35 Tesla crashes since mid-2016. The agency suspects that cars were operating on a partially automated driving system in all of the crashes. At least 17 people have died.

The Virginian-Pilot and Associated Press contributed to this report.
