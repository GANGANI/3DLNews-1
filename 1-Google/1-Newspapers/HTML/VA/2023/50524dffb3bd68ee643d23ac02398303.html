    Top 10: The Winchester Star's most clicked on stories of 2023

    These were the 10 most viewed stories on winchesterstar.com in 2023:

    1) 2 popular downtown businesses abruptly cease operations

    One of the most surprising things that happened this year occurred on Sept. 19 when two popular Old Town Winchester establishments — The Monument and Ellie's Irish Pub — closed for business permanently.

    What made the closures even more shocking was that 18th Rebellion, a 1920s-themed speakeasy bar that was just days away from opening in the basement of The Monument, was also part of the shutdown even though everything was ready for its grand opening.

    All the businesses were part of Monument Entertainment, a venture established by investor Ron McGehee of Winchester after he purchased the former Virginia National Bank Building at 186 N. Loudoun St. for $1.2 million in 2019. The bank building was repurposed to house The Monument and 18th Rebellion.

    In 2021, McGehee also bought the building next door at 168 N. Loudoun St. for $940,000. The former home of Brewbaker's Restaurant was renovated to become Ellie's Irish Pub later that year, and its kitchen prepared dishes for event attendees at The Monument.

    When asked on Sept. 19 why he chose to shut down Monument Entertainment, McGehee said he didn’t want to go into detail but attributed it to a family situation that required his immediate, full-time attention.

    "All of this stuff was done with love," a visibly distraught McGehee said of his Monument Entertainment holdings. "I wanted to make this the coolest place in Winchester."

    He was well on his way to doing that, especially with The Monument. Since opening in the fall of last year, the entertainment venue hosted numerous concerts, late-night dance parties, weddings, special events and fundraisers.

    When The Monument's closing was announced, there were still three weddings and a Blue Ridge Hospice fundraiser that had already been booked. McGehee brought in staff specifically for those events before shuttering the building for good on Oct. 6.

    Nearly three months later, The Monument building is vacant and unused, but the former Ellie's Irish Pub next door is showing signs of life. The owners of Manolete's Taqueria Gourmet restaurant at 2240 Valley Ave. in Winchester, who will soon lose the lease because their building is slated to be demolished as part of an upcoming redevelopment of the Ward Plaza shopping center, announced in late November they were moving to the former Ellie's in downtown Winchester. A date for the move has not been announced, and it is not known if they plan on buying or renting the building.

    As for The Monument building at the northern end of the Loudoun Street Mall, Winchester Economic Development Director Jeff Buettner said recently that potential buyers have surfaced.

    "We have had conversations with people who are actively engaged in looking at The Monument," Buettner said. "We feel The Monument is very vital to downtown as a music venue to complement the Bright Box Theater [at 15 N. Loudoun St.]."

    Buettner said the city government is willing to work with buyers and provide them with tax breaks and special incentives that are available to businesses in Winchester's Historic District, but he ruled out any possibility of Rouss City Hall purchasing the building.

    — Compiled by Brian Brehm

    2) The Tuleyries estate in Clarke County is slated for auction

    In September, The Tuleyries, a 168-acre estate with a 10,000-square-foot, circa 1833 home in Clarke County, was auctioned to the highest bidder by Torrence, Read and Forehand Auctions, a firm hired by the U.S. Bankruptcy Court.

    According to the listing on the National Register of Historic Places, the estate was formed by Joseph Tuley Jr. who inherited a fortune amassed by his father in the leather tanning business. The name “Tuleyries” alludes to the French royal palace — the “Tuileries” — and a play on his own name, Tuley. The brick mansion features a Corinthian portico, an interior domed entrance, a sweeping curved stairway and is topped by a copper-domed, windowed cupola that allows for spectacular views of surrounding farms and the Blue Ridge Mountains.

    The high bid for the Tuleyries was $4.1 million. Also at the auction, there was a high bid of $6 million for the neighboring Westfield Farm, a 237-acre equestrian estate with an events center.

    3) James Wood High School unveils phase one of renovation project with ribbon cutting

    On Aug. 9, James Wood High School in Frederick County debuted the first phase of its $72.8 million renovation project — which aims to update and modernize the building as well as expand it to accommodate the school's growing population.

    Built in 1980, this is the school's first major renovation.

    Phase one included a 38,000-square-foot addition, with new guidance, career services and administration offices, as well as an extensive school clinic, refurbished gym locker rooms and 12 new classrooms.

    Phase two, which is slated for completion in July of 2024, will include more classrooms, a new media area and a new cafeteria and kitchen.

    The renovation is expected to be finished before the start of the 2025-26 school year.

    Phase three will turn the old kitchen and cafeteria space into departments like Special Education and Family and Consumer Sciences, among other improvements.

    The project is expected to be finished before the 2025-2026 school year.

    4) New restaurant is something to Savour

    There’s a new restaurant in Winchester that hopes you will step in and step away from worries. When you enter Savour by CRUSH in Creekside Station, you will be transported to a place where both elegance and your comfort are paramount.

    The menu features five courses, says Savour Executive Chef Mia Curbow. To kick things off, you’ll enjoy an amuse-bouche, which is “one bite to open your palette and get you ready for what’s to come.” The team then brings a course of seasonal homemade breads to your table with house compound butters like organic apple blossom honey with banana mint, Pernod lemon and caper and harissa.

    Next, Curbow offers a seasonal salad followed by an intermezzo (palette cleanser) to prep you for one of the three main courses from which you can choose. “You can come three times a month and have something different each time,” owner Branden Goldizen says. Curbow is committed to making sure one of the three main courses is vegetarian. For the fifth course, you choose a decadent dessert.

    Branden Goldizen and his wife, Tracy, opened Savour as a sister business to CRUSH Wine Bar, which is a stone’s throw from the new restaurant.

    5) Out of the Past

    Out of the Past, which is published every Monday and compiled by Priscilla Lehman, features news stories that were in The Winchester Star 100, 75, 50 and 25 years ago. The weekly column  is a reader favorite.

    6) Police: Frederick County man killed in crash; N.Y. driver charged

    Joseph Shane Stephens, 24, was less than a mile from his family's house in The Overlook housing development when his car was struck by a vehicle that ran a red light on Berryville Pike (Va.7) at 9:47 p.m. July 20.

    Stephens, who worked at Red Lobster in Winchester, had taken the Woods Mill Road exit off eastbound Berryville Pike en route home. The exit goes up a short hill to a traffic light at the intersection of Woods Mill Road and westbound Berryville Pike. Stephens, who had the green light, was crossing westbound Berryville Pike to enter his neighborhood when the 2015 Honda Accord he was driving was hit on the passenger side by a 2008 Toyota Corolla driven by Frances A. Rotondo, 42, of Brooklyn, New York, according to police.

    Stephens was pronounced dead that night. Rotondo was charged with voluntary manslaughter and reckless driving by speed.

    “He was the kindest most gentle soul anyone could ask for,” said his mother, Fawn Stephens. “We are heartbroken and also very angry and we want something [done] at that intersection” to improve safety for motorists.

    His family has since asked officials to improve safety at the intersection.

    The Virginia Department of Transportation is conducting a speed study looking at possible safety improvements at the intersection. And the Frederick County Transportation Committee has recommended that the county proceed with red light cameras at certain intersections.

    7) Stephens City officials: Operator of shuttered restaurant owes $32K in meals taxes

    The operator of the now-defunct High Point Diner at 5106 Main St. in Stephens City owed the town roughly $32,000 in meals taxes in October, according to Town Manager Mike Majher.

    “The Town’s position is that these taxes are not dischargeable under bankruptcy laws and we will continue to pursue collection of these tax dollars paid by our citizens to the (operator),” Majher told The Star.

    8) Winchester man gets 15 years for embezzling from Harrisonburg-Rockingham Community Services

    Andrew T. Hahn, of Winchester, was sentenced to 15 years in prison in October for his role in an embezzlement and money laundering scheme from the Harrisonburg-Rockingham Community Services Board (HRCSB). He also was ordered to pay $189,410 in restitution.

    From mid-2019 until his arrest in October 2021, Hahn served as the board’s information technology manager. He was accused of embezzling over $890,000 from the organization, which is a public provider of mental health, substance abuse and developmental services. He did so by creating false invoices for computer purchases from Anchored Technology LLC, which was solely owned by Hahn, according to a news release from Virginia Attorney General Jason Miyares’ office.

    8) Winchester police investigating report of another missing woman

    As local law enforcement continued its search for 45-year-old Nanci Britner, a Winchester woman who had not been seen since June 28, another woman was reported missing.

    Twenty-seven-year-old Carly Harris vanished on Aug. 2 after calling for a ride and getting into a vehicle on Westside Station Drive.

    Harris, a resident of Farmville, was located Aug. 13 and there were no indications she had been taken against her will, according to Winchester Police Chief Amanda Behan.

    Britner remains missing. She was last seen on the night of June 28 at the Relax Inn at 2645 Valley Ave. in Winchester.

    If you have information that could help find Britner, call the Winchester Police Department at 540-662-4131 or the Winchester-Frederick-Clarke Crime Solvers at 540-665-TIPS (8477). Tips can also be submitted via Crime Solvers’ P3 app, which is available as a free download for Apple and Android smartphones.

    9) From a blacksmith's daughter to a country music legend

    Before rising to stardom as a country music singer, Patsy Cline was born Virginia Patterson "Ginny" Hensley on Sept. 8, 1932, in Winchester. After her father abandoned the family, they moved into a two-story house at 608 S. Kent St. "Ginny" Hensley lived there from 1948 to 1953, the period when she was launching a singing career by performing in and around the Winchester area.

    Authors Tracie Dillon and Tim Poole are compiling a book, "Patsified," that will contain Cline-related stories and photos. If everything goes as planned, the book will be published on March 5, 2025, the 62nd anniversary of Cline's death in a plane crash.

    10) Winchester man sentenced for fatal fentanyl dose

    Kenneth James Hughes Jr., of Winchester, was sentenced on Aug. 8 in U.S. District Court for the Western District of Virginia to seven years in a federal penitentiary for selling a deadly dose of fentanyl to a friend who overdosed and died.

    His charges related to the death of a 19-year-old Winchester man identified in court documents only as Z.S., as well as an incident in Berryville where he sold fentanyl to a police informant.

    Hughes pleaded guilty on April 11 to distribution of fentanyl resulting in serious bodily injury and a separate count of fentanyl distribution. In exchange for his plea, a second count of fentanyl distribution stemming from an alleged incident on Dec. 2, 2020, was dismissed.

    — Compiled by Josh Gully
