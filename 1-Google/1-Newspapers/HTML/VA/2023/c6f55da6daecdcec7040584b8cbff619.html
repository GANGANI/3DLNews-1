Homecoming plus Halloweekend historically makes for likely crime uptick

Story Highlights

  • During 2013, Halloweekend and Homecoming occurred at the same time, which led to a significant uptick in crime rates.
  • Among 101 respondents to an Instagram survey, 91% said they think there will be a difference in safety levels during Homecoming-Halloween weekend. 
  • Harrisonburg Director of Communications Mike Parks said the city is aware of the threats a Hoco-Halloweekend poses and are preparing accordingly.

This year, two JMU traditions, Halloweekend and Homecoming, will take place at the same time — something that has historically led to an uptick in Harrisonburg’s crime rate.

In an Oct. 13 email, Harrisonburg Director of Communications Mike Parks said the city is aware of the threats that a Halloween-Homecoming weekend poses and is planning ahead. 

“We do not publish information about security plans that may render those plans ineffective,” Parks said. “We do work closely with our partners such as JMU police and the Rockingham County Sheriff’s Office to prepare for and respond during events and busy weekends.”

The last time JMU celebrated both Halloween and Homecoming during one weekend was in 2019. On the football field that Saturday, the Dukes beat Towson 27-10. 

The majority of JMU students have not experienced both Halloweekend and Homecoming simultaneously but feel the coinciding events will cause chaos.  

According to a Breeze Instagram poll posted on Oct. 15, 91% of 101 respondents said they think there will be a difference in safety levels during Hoco-Hallo weekend when compared to other weekends. 

Senior Sophie Stimpson added the potential for increases in criminal activity could be worsened by the fact that the kickoff is at 8 p.m. Saturday and emphasized it’s “too much,” and said she worries police won’t be able to regulate the situation. 

“So many people come in for Halloweekend anyway, so there's going to be double the amount of people [this year],” Stimpson said. “The police presence will be insane.”

Senior Lizz Cook shares Stimpson’s thoughts about the weekend and said she is nervous for the increase in crowds the weekend will produce. 

“I feel very safe with the JMU students, but I don't know the other people that are coming in,” Cook said. “I feel like other people coming might not have the same intentions as the students here.”

In 2013, The Breeze published an article examining a noticeable increase in crime when Homecoming and Halloween fell on the same weekend. 

In the article, then-Harrisonburg Police Department (HPD) Lt. Roger Knott said the weekend resulted in about 591 calls from Thursday to Saturday night, which was far above Knott’s estimate of roughly 80-100 calls during a typical weekend.

During that 2013 weekend, Knott said the department saw 13 cases of either rape, robbery or aggravated assault and 38 cases of property crimes, including vandalism and theft.

In the same article, then-Chief of JMU PD Lee Shifflett urged students to trust themselves and “if your gut’s telling you something’s wrong, it’s likely that it is.” He explained students should be aware of their surroundings and be “mindful of who they’re letting into their parties."

Where to have outdoor fun on and near campus over Homecoming Weekend

JMU's Homecoming has events from Monday through the weekend and offers a number of ways for people to celebrate, both indoors and out. The Breeze has compiled a list of outdoor events for those who want to celebrate Homecoming while still enjoying the fall scenery.

Headed to the pumpkin festival this weekend? Here’s what to expect.

Back Home on the Farm will be hosting its second weekend of the Virginia Pumpkin Festival on Saturday and Sunday. Included at the event is pumpkin-themed treats, a petting zoo and a Reba McEntire-themed corn maze.

Former Duke Dogs unleash stories from college days behind the mask

Adam Wermus ('13) and Matt Hoffman ('18) are both alumni who played the role of JMU's mascot, Duke Dog. Both shared stories of football games, ESPN College GameDay and walking across the stage at graduating while dressed in Duke Dog's feet and paws. 

SGA approves over $8,000 in funding for three student organizations

The Student Government Association (SGA) Senate approved funding totalling over $8,000 for JMU student organizations: the Industrial Designers Society of America, the Kappa Delta Pi Alpha Chi chapter and Student Ambassadors.

City tourism spending still sees growth with outdoor recreation focus

The City of Harrisonburg is continuing to see its tourism industry profit after focusing its efforts primarily on businesses that specialize in outdoor recreation during the pandemic. The tourism center previously invited local business owners to provide feedback to the office so it could consider the community when strategizing how to boost tourism.

The Breeze's voter resource guide for in- and out-of-state students

With local election season upon us, Dukes can take advantage of various resources that aim to help people exercise their right to vote. This guide includes resources for voter registration absentee voting and voting in-person ahead of Election Day, which is Tuesday, Nov. 7.
