Modular home company to shut doors

North American Housing closing facility on Va. 55, citing valley’s lack of demand

By James Heffernan — jheffernan@nvdaily.com

STRASBURG — North American Housing Corp. will close its modular home manufacturing facility on Strasburg Road later this month, affecting about 60 workers.

Roger Scholten, senior vice president of parent company Champion Home Builders, said Friday demand for modular homes in the region has steadily declined to the point that it is no longer feasible to keep the facility open.

“It’s unfortunate,” Scholten said. “The workers at that plant made a really good product. … We just felt [closing] was something we had to do.”

Modular homes are prefabricated structures that consist of multiple sections that are manufactured at a plant and then delivered by truck to the site for assembly. The cost is generally lower than site-built homes of comparable size. Like traditional homes, they come in a variety of styles and floor plans with customized options.

North American Housing builds modular homes at 14 plants through a network of approved builders in Virginia, West Virginia, Maryland, North Carolina, New Jersey, Pennsylvania, Delaware and the District of Columbia.

The local plant is the company’s only facility that will be closing, Scholten said. The work will be transferred to its Strattanville, Pa., location, he said.

Employees were notified of the closure about two weeks ago, Scholten said. The workers were offered severance packages, and some accepted transfers to other company locations, he said.

Reached for comment on Thursday, plant manager Allen “A.J.” Judy referred questions to the company’s corporate office in Troy, Mich.

The facility will continue to fill existing purchase orders, but production should cease by Thanksgiving, Scholten said.

North American Housing dates to 1876, and is a former division of Barber & Ross Co., which introduced roof trusses to the Washington area in the 1950s. Barber & Ross, whose products also included windows, pre-hung interior and exterior doors, moldings and custom millwork products, relocated from Leesburg to the Fort Collier Industrial Park in Frederick County in 2003, but was forced to shut down in 2007 due to financial difficulties. The company filed for bankruptcy, and its assets were sold at auction in January 2008.
