Chester UMC

CHESTER - Chester United Methodist Church, 12132 Percival St., will spend this morning making a difference in the community. Instead of holding its regular contemporary worship service at 8:30 a.m., members will put their attitudes into action by volunteering with diverse mission projects in the Chester area.

"If your family is seeking a meaningful way to share a Sunday morning with people in our community who could use a hand, please join us for this special event," said Pastor Marg Kutz.

Volunteers are asked to meet at the church at 8:30 a.m. Then, small groups will visit a nearby nursing home, provide landscaping at Pathways in Petersburg, feed the homeless in coordination with the Cornerstone Assembly of God, gather professional clothing, and collect groceries for its food pantry and for children who don't have enough to eat in the summer.

Afterwards, volunteers will be treated to a luncheon at noon in the church's fellowship hall. Free nursery care with professional staff will be available during the Mission Sunday activities. Also, the church will hold its regular traditional worship service at 11 a.m. and Southern gospel service at 6 p.m. For more information, call the church office at 748-6006.
