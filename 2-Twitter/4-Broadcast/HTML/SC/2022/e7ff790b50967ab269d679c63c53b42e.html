CCU golf course prepares turf for cold Christmas weather

by Courtney Rowles

Thu, December 22nd 2022 at 3:41 PM
Updated Thu, December 22nd 2022 at 5:49 PM

CONWAY, S.C. (WPDE) — Many golf courses along the Grand Strand are making preparations for the winter weather.

The team at Hackler Golf Course at Coastal Carolina University is working to protect their turf from the frigid temperatures predicted for Christmas weekend.

The head golf superintendent for the course, Robert Bell, was with his crew Thursday to lay tarps on their 24-hole facility.

Bell said the tarps will hold heat and protect the turf from cold weather.

When asked about golfer turnout due to the weather, Bell said the lull has been to be expected for this time of year.

"It's about like this every year. Kind of getting ready for the holidays, people traveling. This time of year is kind of slow," Bell said.
