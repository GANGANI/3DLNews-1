Football 9/7/2018 12:41:00 PM MSU Sports Information

Game Notes: St. Scholastica

Story Links

Watch Live
Listen Live

The Comet Football team finally comes home to Jerome Berg Field for a battle against the College of St. Scholastica Saints on Saturday afternoon, kickoff is set for 1 p.m. Saturday's home opener will kick off a new era for the Comet Football team as Mayville State fans will see the Comets play on brand new field turf on Jerome Berg Field, donors and contributors to the project will be recognized in an opening ceremony before the game and again at halftime on Saturday. 

This will be the first meeting between Mayville State and St. Scholastica. The Saints come into this weekend without having played a game, St. Scholastica had a bye in the first week of the season. Last season, the Saints finished 7-3 overall and 7-2 in the Upper Midwest Athletic Conference, and were picked to finish second in the conference in preseason polls this year. This is the first season for St. Scholastica's Interim Head Coach George Penree, he will oversee special teams units and the defensive backs. Making it the first time a Saints Head Coach will not be calling the offense. On the other side of the ball, St. Scholastica was stingy last season. The Saints only allowed 143 YPG through the air in 2017, ranking them 13th in the NCAA Division III passing defense. But, the Saints lost four defensive starters from that team last year, including three in the secondary. The offense lost six starters, including four of the five starting linemen from a year ago.

St. Scholastica's junior quarterback Zach Edwards finished second in NCAA Division III in passing yards per completion (18.2) in 2017. He led the conference in passing yards (2,608) and touchdowns (27). The 2,608 matched a program record for
passing yards in a season. One of this favorite targets was senior wide receiver Aaron Olson. Olson finished second on the team with 742 receiving yards and 10 touchdowns despite missing two games in 2017, and he is the only Saint receiver in program history to record back-to-back seasons with at least 10 receiving touchdowns and over 700 receiving yards.

On defense, the Saints return their top two leading tacklers from 2017 in senior linebacker Jonathan Senger and junior linebacker Cal Germain. Senger led the team with 78 tackles last season and finished tied for third in the UMAC with four interceptions, Germain was second on the team with 76 tackles and 7.5 tackles for a loss. He also tied for second on the team with three sacks.
 
The Comets are looking to put the past two weeks behind them and start fresh in front of their home crowd. Saturday's fans should see plenty of offense through the air, as both teams come into Saturday's game with passing high on their list of play calling. Mayville State in 2017 was 24th in NAIA Division I passing offense, this year they currently rank sixth after two contests. In 2017, the Saints liked to go to the air as well ranking 43rd is passing offense among all NCAA Division III teams.

Kickoff is set for 1 p.m. tomorrow on Jerome Berg Field. There will be plenty of other activities surrounding the football game with Saturday being the annual Farmers Bowl game, there will a 5K Run/Walk to start things in the morning, followed by a parade and hot dog and corn on the cob feed. There will also be inflatable games, a farmers market, face painting, and other fun activities before the game kicks off. After the game, the Comet Athletic Club will host a post-game social at the Top Hat in downtown Mayville. It is scheduled to begin at 5 p.m. and everyone is welcome to attend. The social is being sponsored by Citizens Insurance Agency of Finley, Cooperstown, and Northwood.
