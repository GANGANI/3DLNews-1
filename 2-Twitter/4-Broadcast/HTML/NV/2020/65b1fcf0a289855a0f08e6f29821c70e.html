Due to record human-caused fires, campfires in Nevada banned indefinitely

by News 4 and FOX 11 Digital Team

Fri, August 7th 2020 at 2:01 PM
Updated Fri, August 7th 2020 at 3:44 PM
Due to record human-caused fires, campfires in Nevada banned starting Friday

    TOPICS:

    Nevada,United StatesenvironmentDisaster AccidentArticlesCampfireCampingFirefighterFireplaces

RENO, Nev. (News 4 & Fox 11) — Due to the increase in human-caused fires, local fire officials are banning campfires indefinitely starting Friday.

The new heightened restrictions will prohibit all open flame campfires on state and federal lands including within designated campgrounds.

Officials say due to the current fuel conditions, there is a high likelihood of a fire exhibiting extreme behavior and posing containment problems for firefighters. To date, there have been more human-caused fires across the state than what is normally averaged in an entire year.

Affected areas include the following:

  • State of Nevada (all state parks)
  • Bureau of Land Management
  • Humboldt-Toyiabe National Forest

The restrictions will be in place until further notice and will be jointly mandated and enforced by each agency. You can be fined up to $5,000 and/or six months in jail for illegal campfires.
