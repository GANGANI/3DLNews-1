New housing project limits parking, cuts down trees in historic Reno

by Kimberly Burrows

Fri, March 26th 2021 at 1:56 PM
Updated Mon, March 29th 2021 at 1:08 PM
3
VIEW ALL PHOTOS
New mixed use housing project at Riverside Dr. and Washington St. near downtown Reno (KRNV)

    TOPICS:

    Nevada,United StatesReno CityRon BrownNaomi DuerrBrian KuykendallenvironmentCities In The United StatesGeography Of California

RENO, Nev. (News 4 & Fox 11) — Residents are buzzing over a new construction project that started on a small piece of land near downtown Reno. It's at the corner of Riverside Drive and Washington Street.

Crews recently put up a temporary fence around the small parking lot next to the Hub Coffee Roasters that sits along the Truckee River.

The project is expected to be mixed-use and includes 34 housing units.

"I'm curious how it's going to work out and I'm afraid there's not going to be enough parking and really impact the businesses here," said Ron Brown, a Reno resident.

Crews cut down an Elm tree on Washington Street that is about 100 years old. More trees are expected to come down or be moved according to Reno City Councilwoman Naomi Duerr. She is on the Reno Urban Forestry Commission. She said the developer proposed to take nine trees down and three at Lunsford Park next to the development but on a sliver of land the developer owns. Duerr said the latest proposal said more trees will be saved by being relocated. She suspected the developer would take down at least three or four of the old trees.

Housing is badly needed in the Reno area. The median rent for a one bedroom apartment is about $1,000 and nearly $1,300 for a two bedroom, according to Apartmentlist.com. The price to buy a condo or a townhome in Reno is more than $308,582. A 21% increase from last year, according to the Reno/Sparks Association of Realtors.

News 4 reached out to the developers and left several messages to ask about the price of the new housing and did not hear back.

"There's a housing shortage right now in Northern Nevada. We have a lot of new companies moving in... So all and all I think it's good. Obviously, parking is a struggle whenever you do stuff like this, but for the most part, I look forward to it," said Brian Kuykendall, a Reno resident and real estate agent.
