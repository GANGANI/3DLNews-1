UPDATE | Grovetown City Council appoints new City Clerk

By Alma McCarty, Lindsay Tuman, Staff
Published: May. 25, 2016 at 11:23 AM EDT

News 12 NBC 26 at 11 / Nov. 14, 2016

GROVETOWN, Ga. (WRDW/WAGT) -- City Council changes its tone, and makes a move to calm a controversy.

Monday, all council members unanimously approved the appointment of a new Planning Director, and a new City Clerk.

Former Clerk Vicky Capetillo was put on administrative leave, then stepped down during a federal investigation into City Hall that began earlier this year. But as agents continue to look into reports of missing money, the council comes together to approve Roxanne Brown as her replacement.

"There were no confrontations, there were no controversies," said councilman Eric Blair, "So this meeting, I hope all future meetings go like this because this was very, very good. This is what we need to do for the people of Grovetown."

"I just felt like that might have been an issue, but it wasn't. I'm glad to see that," said Mayor Gary Jones.

Monday, all hands went up, to approve the appointments of John Waller as Planning Director, and Brown as the new Clerk. Both were in interim positions for a couple of months.

Brown retired after 25 years of working as the County Clerk for Lincoln County.

"I left for 2 weeks, and I was bored to death," she explained, "So, I needed a change of scenery and I started here as an administrative assistant."

It was not long before she took over Capetillo's position.

While Monday's meeting showed a change in tone for the mayor and council, some wonder if the good feeling will last.

"I think that we're going to move forward as a council now and try to get things done in a positive fashion," reassured Jones.

City leaders say the forensic audit is on hold for now, while they get the right paperwork from the bank.

News 12 NBC 26 @ 11 o'clock / Thursday, Aug. 11, 2016

GROVETOWN, Ga. (WRDW/WAGT) -- Two closed door meetings, and a lot of discussion ends with Grovetown's City Clerk resigning. Vicky Capetillo's resignation takes effect on the 30th of next month. It comes as Mayor Gary Jones and council members ask questions about missing money in the city.

A split room ends with a unified decision. "I move that we accept Vicky Capetillo's resignation," City Council member Sylvia Martin said.

It was not a quick decision. It took nearly two hours of discussion behind closed doors. Something this full house was not expecting.

It's not the way Mayor Jones thought it would go either. He expected the city accountant and lawyer to present their case to shift Capetillo from paid to unpaid leave. "It entails that there's been some embezzlement and he plans to lay that out and show the public by way of some paper work that he's going to present," Mayor Jones told News 12 NBC 26 before the meeting.

Those papers, stayed in the box. Instead, only one came out, a resignation letter, read by councilman and Capetillo's step-father, Dennis Trudeau. "By no means am I admitting responsibility of wrongdoing. I maintain my innocence under the Constitution of the State of Georgia and the United States of America," he read.

Her resignation takes effect September 30th. City attorney Brendan Fleming says it's in the best interest of the city. "This will provide an end to any payments to Vicky after September 30th," he said.

It left a divided council united once again. "I think the outcome was perfect for everyone involved, the city and Ms. Vicky Capetillo," city council member Eric Blair said.

Her attorney agrees. "It was sort of her idea and I'm glad the mayor and the council agreed as well as Mr. Fleming," her attorney, Danny Durham said.

And now they say the next step, is moving on. "We hope this begins the healing process for the city," Blair said.

Capetilllo will get paid until the end of September. If she was on paid or unpaid leave she would still get medical insurance. So this decision ends all payments after that, although her letter states she still plans on collecting her retirement and all benefits earned.

UPDATE: Thursday, Aug. 11, 2016

GROVETOWN, Ga. (WRDW/WAGT) -- A City Clerk working for the city of Grovetown will resign in September.

The decision being made during a city council's special called meeting Thursday night.

It took about an hour in executive session for council members and Mayor Gary Jones to reach this decision.

News 12 NBC 26's Lindsay Tuman was at the meeting and will share more details about their decision at 11 o'clock.

Wednesday, Aug. 10, 2016

GROVETOWN, Ga. (WRDW/WAGT) -- Grovetown City Attorney, Brendan Fleming, on behalf of the City of Grovetown filed a civil suit against City Clerk Vicky Capetillo.

The complaint for breach of fiduciary duty was filed back on June 1, 2016. According to the suit, the City is "seeking to have any tangible or intangible properties found or known to be in possession of the Defendant to be returned or reimbursed." The City believes while Capetillo was employed as City Clerk, funds have become unaccounted for. The same department is under federal investigation.

On June 8, Capetillo and her lawyers filed a response to the allegations, stating "the Defendant has insufficient knowledge to admit or deny allegations" pertaining to the missing funds, and states since she is unaware if funds are missing or not, it is "by no fault of the Defendant."

Thursday, Grovetown City Council will hold another special called meeting to discuss Capetillo's status as city clerk at 6:30 p.m. at City Hall.

Beginning Wednesday, August 10, the Georgia Municipal Association is conducting an investigation at City Hall, concerning the water billing process. Jones expects they will be here for a several days gathering information. Once the investigation is complete, they will present the findings and recommendations to the Mayor and Council.

UPDATE: Tuesday, Aug. 9, 2016

GROVETOWN, Ga. (WRDW/WAGT) -- Grovetown City Council has decided the date and time for a special called meeting to discuss Vicky Capetillo's status as city clerk.

According to Mayor Gary Jones, the meeting will be Thursday, Aug. 11, 2016 at 6:30 p.m. It will be held at City Hall.

According to a court filing, a suit was filed against Capetillo in Columbia County Superior Court on June 1 by Attorney Brendan Fleming, after allegations that city funds were mismanaged at Grovetown City Hall.

Right now, Capetillo is on administrative leave with pay, but there is talk of putting her on leave without pay.

Council put off a vote on that change during their meeting Monday night.

News 12 NBC 26 @ 11 o'clock / Monday, Aug. 8, 2016

GROVETOWN, Ga. (WRDW/WAGT) -- A crowd fills Grovetown's City Hall, looking for answers about missing money. Some took part in Monday night's meeting, shouting as the debate heated up.

"It's been a great circus, Barnum and Bailey couldn't have done a better job," former Grovetown Mayor and Coincilman Dennis Trudeau said.

Monday night's Grovetown City Council meeting did prove to be a spectacle. City attorney Brendan Fleming repeatedly asked to hear from city clerk Vicky Capetillo. "I would like to hear from Vicky where the money is," Fleming said.

Much of the controversy surrounds Capetillo. Monday night the council was supposed to decide whether to put her on unpaid leave. She has been on paid leave since June. Her lawyer was at the meeting, he said she was advised not to comment on the matter.

Councilman Dennis Trudeau, Capetillo's step-father, recused himself on the advice of the city attorney. Councilmember Sylvia Martin was also not in attendance, which meant there was not a quorum. Questions came up between the city attorney and Capetillo's attorney on whether Mayor Gary Jones could step in and vote. In the end, Mayor Jones decided to push the vote back to a later date.

It was not the outcome some came to hear. "I don't think we got any results, I think we got the proverbial delay again. No results, just delays," Grovetown citizen Allen Transou said.

One matter that was not delayed, choosing an accounting firm to audit the city for 2015. The city accountant recommended the city choose to pay an outside accounting firm to audit the city instead of immediately using insurance to fund the audit. Twelve companies reportedly were interested in doing the audit, but five got back to the city in time. Ultimately the city accountant said they were all qualified, so they decided to go with the cheapest option, which is Dabbs, Hickman, Hill, and Cannon LLP. It will cost about fourteen thousand dollars.

The council also decided to only audit 2015 for right now. They say that if the results of this audit show they will need to go further back, then they can then go through insurance fund it. The hope is that if they pay less up front now, they will wind up paying less in the long run.

Councilman Dennis Trudeau believes that audit will clear things up. "There's been rumors and stories that there's been millions of dollars embezzled," he said, "We'll find out the truth, we're going to vote for the forensic audit tonight."

Mayor Gary Jones, a former investigator himself, says there is evidence, and he's seen it himself. He says although Capetillo's attorney says there is not evidence, there is enough evidence from a law enforcement perspective. "I think once it comes out, those who have doubts will change their minds," he said.

In the mean time, it's a split room, with a split decision, and emotions are running high.

Monday, Aug. 8, 2016

Grovetown, Ga. (WRDW/WAGT) -- Grovetown City Council is set to meet Monday at 6 p.m.

On the agenda is a discussion to change City Clerk Vicky Capetillo's leave status to administrative leave without pay. Documents show Mayor Gary Jones placed Capetillo on leave with pay starting May 31.

The council will also talk plans to move forward with a forensic audit after allegations of misuse of finances.

Wednesday, July 13, 2016

GROVETOWN, Ga. (WRDW/WAGT) – The City of Grovetown is moving forward with a Forensic Audit, after allegations of misuse of finances.

Shirley Beasley, Grovetown City Administrator, tells News 12 NBC 26 that the city accountant Richard Matthews submit a request for proposal for a forensic audit, which was approved by the council.

This means that the city accountant can start looking for and getting bids from companies to conduct the audit. There is currently no idea on how much this will cost, and the bidding should take at least a couple of weeks.

The audit will go through all finances in all departments, dating back to at least 2013.

Matthews is currently sending out requests to local firms and those in Columbia, Greenville, and Atlanta with a deadline of August 3.

The next step will be for the City Council to evaluate and pick a firm to do the Audit at the Council meeting on August 8.

Friday, June 3, 2016

GROVETOWN, Ga. (WRDW/WAGT) -- Grovetown Mayor Gary Jones has placed the city clerk on Administrative Leave with pay.

Through an open records request, News 12 NBC 26 received documents showing Mayor Jones placed Vicky Capetillo on leave starting May 31.

According to the notice of administrative leave, Capetillo has to turn in any city ID cards, keys, and property. She also is not allowed on the city property unless she is ordered to be there.

The FBI and Department of Treasury have been investigating possible mismanaged money at Grovetown City Hall. Mayor Jones says this has also turned into a criminal investigation, and a lot of money is missing.

News 12 First at Five / June 2, 2016

GROVETOWN, Ga. (WRDW/WAGT) -- It's been a week since the investigation began, by the FBI and the Department of Treasury, over the possibility of mismanaged money at Grovetown City Hall. Now, Mayor Gary Jones says this has turned into a criminal investigation, and although he cannot speculate, he says there's quite a bit of money missing.

The week of questions has not been met with many answers. Last week, federal investigators showed up with a warrant, looking for illegal activity involving taxpayer money. Today, Jones gave News 12 NBC 26 an update.

"We have identified a suspect. And that person will be placed on administrative leave as soon as we can make contact with them," he said.

Jones says he has tried to reach that person for the past two days to give them notice of the suspension. And for those two day, the employee has not been to work.

"I can tell you that this person has not reported back to work this week since the execution of the search warrant," said Jones.

Workers at City Hall say the City Clerk - Vicky Capetillo - has been out for two days, and was not in today.

"Vicky wouldn't steal a dime from the city. I know that," said Dennis Trudeau in an interview last week.

Trudeau is a council member, a former Mayor of the city, and Capetillo's step-father.

News 12 NBC 26 stopped by her home for a comment. Capetillo did not come to the door.

Due to the ongoing nature of the investigation, Jones says he can't name names, but says they'll mail the administrative leave notice Friday.

"Hopefully this individual will come forward and speak to the agents in regard to it and cooperate," he said, "If not there will be some more interviews that will take place with city employees to kind of recap some of the things. Also, probably can look forward to going before the grand jury to obtain an indictment."

Longtime members of the community say they're ready for the investigation to be over, and to move forward with no matter the outcome. Many still assert that Jones has not been communicating any of his requests and actions with the council members since he took office this year.

News 12 First at Five / May 25, 2016

GROVETOWN, Ga. (WRDW/WAGT) -- Agents with the FBI and Department of Treasury executed a search warrant for Grovetown City Hall, after Mayor asks them to investigate possible illegal activity involving city money.

"This investigation continues," said Mayor Gary Jones, "I have no doubt that some criminal activity has transpired, so we'll leave it at that at this point."

Investigators looked through computers and documents, conducted interviews and took pictures at City Hall today.

Last night's election results, posted to Council Chamber doors, and mark the finish of the first election since Jones became the mayor. Since then, and even before, Jones said he had suspicions of illegal activity, dealing with the collection of taxpayer money.

"I began to certainly develop what I felt like was a good case of ...embezzlement, so to speak," he said.

Today, federal agents went to work on the investigation.

"They are learning the working processes of each particular employee, so that they can track a trail of money how it comes in," said Jones.

He says taxpayers have a right to know what's going on here.

"A lot of, I guess, ways in Grovetown have been that way for so long," he said, "People have been doing things a certain way."

"He's learning but he's taking the wrong approach," said former mayor and current council member Dennis Trudeau, "I personally feel he's taking the wrong approach."

Trudeau, once a longtime mayor of the city, says with the new mayor, things are changing.

"This is a nice town with good people in it," he said, "Just right now, we're having trouble with the administration. It's just been torn apart."

But he says, the rocky relationship between the city and its mayor does not point to criminal activity.

"I'm sure everything will turn out fine. I have all the confidence in our city clerk even though she is a stepdaughter," he said, "The books are there. They're open. They can check all they want and they might find sloppy correspondence or something like that."

Mayor Jones says he still wants to get a forensic audit approved by council, to make sure everything is looked over carefully because the search warrant can only do so much.

Wednesday, May 25, 2016

GROVETOWN, Ga. (WRDW/WAGT) -- We have just learned that the Federal Bureau of Investigation and the U.S. Treasury Department are executing a search warrant of Grovetown City Hall.

Mayor Gary Jones confirms he requested the US Department of Treasury to come investigate the finances at Grovetown City Hall. He says this is a sad day for Grovetown, but will help moving forward into the future.

Both the Treasury Department and the FBI are investigating the scene now. Stay tuned to News 12 NBC 26 for continuing coverage.
