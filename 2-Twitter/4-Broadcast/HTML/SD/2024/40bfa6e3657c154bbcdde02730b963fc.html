Abundant cloud cover for weekend: Storm Center AM Update – Saturday, February 3

Jay Trobec
4 months ago

Widespread dense fog enveloped KELOLAND overnight and this morning, and is stubbornly warding off attempts to burn off. But even as the fog things, we expect mostly cloudy skies for the rest of Saturday, with an easterly breeze. Sprinkles or very light showers will be possible East River. More substantial rainfall, in the range of a half inch, will be possible in Rapid City and western South Dakota.

Tonight it will remain cloudy, and we may be in for another round of fog as moisture remains trapped near the ground. Overnight lows should again be on the mild side.

More clouds on Sunday, but we don’t anticipate any more rainfall East River. In the west, additional rainfall could result in a few more tenths of an inch. And in the coldest hours, like Sunday morning, Rapid City and the west could have a few flakes of snow mix in with the – predominantly – rainfall.

Next week will start very warm again, with highs in the upper 40s to low 50s East River. We might even have a shot at some records. Western South Dakota will be well into the 50s if not around 60.

A cold front will come through for the Wednesday and Thursday time period, dropping temperatures back into the 40s with rainfall (or a light mix of snow with the rain). After that, we’ll finish the week with slightly cooler air. That will still be above normal, and we expect warmer than normal temperatures through Valentine’s Day with the upper 30s to low 40s for highs the rest of next week.
