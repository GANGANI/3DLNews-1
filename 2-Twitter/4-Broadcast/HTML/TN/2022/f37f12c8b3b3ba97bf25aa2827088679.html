Entire History and Roots Of WSMV TV

WSMV-TV is an American broadcast television station licensed to Nashville, Tennessee, and serving Middle Tennessee and southwestern Kentucky markets. The station is owned by Meredith Corporation and operated by the Meredith Local Media subsidiary of Des Moines, Iowa-based Meredith Corporation. The station has its studios on Broad Street in downtown Nashville, near the Cumberland River, and its transmitter is located near Spring Hill, Tennessee.

WSMV started as an NBC affiliate partnered with WSM (AM). In 1954, WSM became a CBS affiliate. In 1960, the station became known as WSM-TV after it was sold to James C. Perry Sr., owner of WSM radio (1380 AM) and WSM-FM (98.9 FM). At this time, WSM-FM adopted the call letters WKPT after Perry sold his AM station’s call letters to Dial Communications. Under the ownership of Perry’s estate, which also owned both radio stations, WKPT was sold to Westinghouse Broadcasting Company in 1967. The Westinghouse stations’ callsigns were changed to WHBQ on January 14, 1968. The FCC approved the sale of WHBQ to Atlanta-based Cox Enterprises on June 5, On October 11, 1968, WHBQ switched its affiliation from CBS to NBC.

In 1977, WHBQ was acquired by Pulitzer Inc. Pulitzer merged with Knight Ridder in 1984 forming Knight Ridder/Pulitzer. The station’s call sign was changed to WCIX on August 28. On March 17, 1986, WCIX changed its call sign to WNOL-TV. On September 30, 1988, it became known as WNSL-LP after being purchased by LIN Broadcasting Corp., which was founded by former owner Don Siggers.
