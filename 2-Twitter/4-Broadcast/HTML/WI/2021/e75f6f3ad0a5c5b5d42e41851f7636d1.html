Oneida Nation kicks off Wisconsin's first legal sports wagers at main casino

by Gabriella Premus, FOX 11 News

Tue, November 30th 2021 at 8:08 AM
Updated Wed, December 1st 2021 at 8:43 AM
3

(WLUK) -- The Oneida Nation has officially started sports betting on Tuesday.

It's the first time such wagering has been allowed in Wisconsin.

Sports betting is the result of a new gaming compact amendment with the state that allows "event wagering." Than can include betting on sports and events such as nationally televised award shows, professional sports league drafts and professional games, including football, basketball and baseball.

Alma Webster and Sandra Brehmer are known as the Oneida Bingo Queens. They were the first to make legal sports bets in Wisconsin on Tuesday at the Oneida Nation Casino.

The bingo parlor they started more than 50 years ago helped give Oneida a start in gambling operations. They did it all to pay a few simple bills.

Now, they placed their bets on something new.

“We are out there paving the way for other places to do these new businesses," said Brehmer.

Oneida says its targeted audience, sports fanatics. For the first time in Wisconsin, fans of any sport can come and legally place bets on their favorite teams.

“We do anticipate that we have a large sports customer base and we have a lot for gaming customers so we are hoping to merge those so our gaming customers and our sports customers are more engaged in the state of Wisconsin with professional teams," said Chad Fuss, Oneida Casino Assistant Chief Financial Officer.


Since Oneida is the first sanctioned market in Wisconsin, Fuss says it's hard to know how much profit sports betting will bring in.

“It's another amenity, as some of our regular customers come in we hope they bring some of their family members who enjoy sports to make a sports bet book," said Fuss.

Rose Blozinski is the executive director of the Wisconsin Council on Problem Gambling. She’s nervous that bringing legal sports betting closer to home will increase unhealthy gaming habits.

“This is a new type of gambling legally so we know some people have never tried it that maybe haven’t gambled in the past so there’s always that risk that problem gamblers are going to increase,” said Blozinski.

Blozinski suggests keeping in mind how much time and money you’re spending while sports betting, and to seek help if any type of gambling is becoming a problem.

The casino has 20 betting kiosks around the casino floor and a betting counter. The event wagering area will eventually move into the casino’s former Vince Lombardi’s Bar and Grill.

You have to be at the casino to place your bet and you can't bet on Wisconsin-based college teams or teams involving children.

Betting was originally scheduled to begin in September, but supply chain issues led to a delay in some of the equipment arriving at the casino.
