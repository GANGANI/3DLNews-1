Thompson's Point adding rooftop solar panels to historic Depot Pavilion

by Reilly Musgrave, WGME

Wed, November 30th 2022 at 8:54 PM
Updated Wed, November 30th 2022 at 10:15 PM
Thompson's Point to install solar panels early 2023, helping Portland meet its ambitious climate and energy goals. (WGME)

PORTLAND (WGME)-- Thompson's Point is partnering with local solar professionals to install solar panels in time for next summer's concert season.

The Depot Pavilion is one of the last pieces of the former St. John's Street Union Station that was demolished in 1961; it originally functioned as a train canopy, but after Union Station was demolished, the pavilion was moved and partially reconstructed at its current location.

  • Also read: University of New England unveils plans to move medical school to Portland

And now this historic structure is set to help the city of Portland meet its ambitious climate and clean energy goals.

The proposed solar panels, installed by employee-owned ReVision Energy, are expected to generate more than 141,000 kilowatt-hours of clean solar electricity every year.

  • Also read: Options available to help with high heating costs, more assistance could be on the way

In addition to reducing energy costs for the venue, this initiative will also decrease their carbon footprint, offsetting 138,400 pounds of carbon annually, equivalent to planting 1,010 trees.

Installation of the panels is expected to take place in the first half of 2023, before the summer concerts begin.
