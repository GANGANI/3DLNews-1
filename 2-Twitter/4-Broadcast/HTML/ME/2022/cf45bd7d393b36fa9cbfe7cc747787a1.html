Traffic stop leads to drug bust in Waterboro

by Ariana St Pierre, WGME

Thu, December 29th 2022 at 10:49 AM
4

Brandon Corliss and Danielle Lemay were both charged with aggravated trafficking of scheduled drugs. Corliss was also charged with OUI. (York County Sheriff's Office)

WATERBORO (WGME) – The York County Sheriff's Office says they pulled over a suspected drunk driver in Waterboro on Wednesday and ended up arresting two people for drug trafficking after allegedly finding meth and fentanyl in the car.

Police say a deputy spotted a car driving erratically on Rt. 202 around 11:30 p.m. The deputy pulled the car over and saw a passenger making suspicious movements inside the vehicle.

Another deputy and his K-9 named Rebel responded to the scene and conducted an exterior drug "sniff" of the vehicle and alerted to the odor of an illegal narcotic, according to police.

  • Also read: Man accused of trying to steal Auburn fire truck

Police say deputies then searched the car and found 25 grams of methamphetamine and 22 grams of fentanyl.

The driver, identified as 32-year-old Brandon Corliss of New Hampshire, and the passenger, identified as 39-year-old Danielle Lemay of New Hampshire, were both arrested.

Corliss was charged with OUI-liquor and aggravated trafficking of scheduled drugs. Lemay was charged with aggravated trafficking of scheduled drugs.

The suspects were taken to the York County Jail where they are being held on $1,000 bail each.
