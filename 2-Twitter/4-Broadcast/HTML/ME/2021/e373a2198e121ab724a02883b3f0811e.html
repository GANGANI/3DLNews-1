'It doesn't taste right': Some Wiscasset residents concerned to drink tap water

by WGME

Updated Wed, December 22nd 2021 at 11:15 AM

WISCASSET (WGME) -- Orange water stains and bad-tasting water are forcing some people in Wiscasset to stop drinking the tap water.

Clean, good-tasting water is something we’ve come to expect, but for some people in Wiscasset, it’s hard to come by, forcing them to buy their own.

  • Also read: Maine's sparrows are being wiped out by mercury and climate change

Samantha Main lives in downtown Wiscasset. While her water is clear out of the faucet, the taste prevents her from drinking it.

“It has this like tangy taste to it that doesn’t taste right. My parents don’t drink the tap water. I don’t drink the tap water,” she said.

They only use it for coffee and cleaning.

“It doesn’t even make coffee taste right in my opinion,” Main said.

Kate Steed who lives near town hall cleaned her sink on Tuesday.

“The water is orange, or it leaves an orange residue. It’s a funny orange. It’s very kind of bright,” Steed said.

  • Also read: 'Pack our patience': Holiday travel expected to be near pre-pandemic levels in New England

She says this started about three months ago when she noticed orange water in her cat’s bowl.

“I immediately started buying water so I’m too old for this but I’m lugging gallons of water into the house,” Steed said.

She’s spent hundreds of dollars on bottled water.

“It’s a little bit like it’s the edge of something not comfortable and possibly dangerous,” Steed said.

Others in town posted about issues with the water on a community Facebook page. CBS13 took these concerns to the local water district.

“Is it concerning to you knowing that people are out there buying their own water and don’t feel safe drinking the water from their faucet?” CBS13 reporter Blair Best asked.
“It’s an education process. All the water quality concerns we take very highly. We are routinely testing our water for safety,” said Wiscasset Water District Superintendent Chris Cossette.

The water district says they just learned about the issue.

“We’re out there right now flushing and pulling water samples to check for the water quality,” Cossette said.

They’ll have the results by the end of the week.

“Have you ever had an issue like this with the water in the area in the past?” CBS13 reporter Blair Best asked.
“Not like this,” Cossette replied.

if you're experiencing issues with your water, those at the water district say the best thing you can do is to let them know.
