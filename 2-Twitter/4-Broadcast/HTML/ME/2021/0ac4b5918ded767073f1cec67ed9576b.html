International Food Festival draws hundreds of people

  • By Susan Farley
  • May 23, 2021

PITTSFIELD — Maine Central Institute was the place to be for genuine international cuisine.

Sunday was the annual International Food Festival put on by students and faculty of the Maine Central Institute.

Roughly 200 people came to try food prepared by international students of the school representing 20 different countries

“I think it’s so wonderful that people are so willing to try different foods and try different things. It’s so great that people can enjoy food from other people. I tried all the food out here, Argentina, Brazil, Japan everything. There were so many different cultures here and so nice to be able to experience stuff like that and have people enjoy what you make and prepare for them,” said Hector Blight, a student at the school from the United Kingdom.

A teacher from the school who organized the event said the students put in a lot of hours of work to make this happen.

“Our students worked really hard from shopping going to portland going to Bangor, preparing, planning, lots of work,” said Artur Fass, the event organizer.

He added the festival is the perfect way to not only celebrate the rich cultural diversity of the students but also connect them with the surrounding community.

“There’s no better way to share our culture than through food. Of course, our international students who live here in the dorms love meeting our people in the community and they get to have those connections. Of course, it’s through food, which is tasty and delightful,” Fass said.
