Wheel of Fortune fans fuming after contestant guesses right, but technically loses big win

by ZACHARY ROGERS | The National Desk

Wed, December 22nd 2021 at 5:24 PM
5

CULVER CITY, Calif. (TND) — She said the right words and guessed correctly, but she was just a bit too slow.

That's what officials on the iconic American game show "Wheel of Fortune" said concerning a controversial decision involving contestant Charlene Rubush, who was competing on Tuesday night in the bonus round to win a big prize, an Audi Q3.

Rubush was attempting to solve a puzzle by guessing the mystery phrase. The phrase was in the category of "what are you doing", was four words, and only four out of twenty letters were missing.

"_H_ _SING THE RIGHT _ _RD" was displayed on the board. Rubush began to answer.

"Choosing the right... word" Rubush said just as the buzzer sounded. She had gotten the phrase out, and incredibly enough she had guessed correctly, but her pause between "right" and "word" was, according to the game show's rules, too long.

Rubush lost the big prize of a brand new SUV due to the technicality.

You know, this one’s tough, because you said all the right words, including the word ‘word’ but, as you know, it’s gotta be more or less continuous,” host of the show Pat Sajak said to the contestant. “We’ll allow for a little pause, but not four or five seconds. I’m sorry. You did a good job in getting it, but we can’t give you the prize, and it was the Audi.

Some fans of Wheel of Fortune were not happy with the show's decision, with some even saying they were planning on boycotting and not watching the show anymore.

"Wheel of Fortune, you just lost a viewer. 'I am choosing the right word' to never watch this show again," said Twitter user @TalinOrfaliGhaz in a tweet. "I'm done! I'm with that lady. She deserves that Audi! That rule is the most dumbest thing ever! She had it right before the time was up!"

Others on Twitter mostly agreed the show had "cheated" Rubush out of her win. Some Twitter users pushed back, saying rules are rules. Twitter user @jamiebeth1 tagged carmaker Audi, hoping the company would right the game show's perceived wrongdoing.

"‘wheel of NO fortune’ you owe that woman a car! She got screwed! @Audi please step up - give Charlene a damn car for #Christmas," said @jamiebeth1 in her tweet.

Rubush's loss also gained the attention of former champion professional poker player and 'Jeopardy!' All-Star Alex Jacob, who took her side, saying "Come on @WheelofFortune, the woman literally chose the right word. Give her the car" in his tweet.

Jacob went so far as to contact Wheel of Fortune about Rubush through a direct message on Twitter.

Hello there, Alex. we appreciate your feedback. At this time we are reviewing this situation internally. Thank you for reaching out," the Wheel of Fortune Twitter account said in response to Jacob, according to a screenshot Jacob shared.

This isn't the first time a contestant on Wheel of Fortune lost due to a technicality with the rules. In April 2021, contestant David Petersen reportedly lost because he added the word "and" to his answer, a slip-up which had happened to another contestant in 2019.

"I asked Pat [Sajak] to solve it. And as I went to solve the puzzle, of course, he says every time, ‘Do not add anything.’ Well, he says this, you hear it, but the number one thing I'm focused on is pronouncing the words correctly," Petersen told Fox News in an interview.

Fox News then reached out to the show, which defended itself and the rules of the game by saying contestants are made aware of all the rules when they come to the show.

Contestants are thoroughly briefed prior to the show that to keep the game fair and objective, precision is necessary when solving the puzzles. Also, in this particular category, Pat often volunteers a reminder of this rule when contestants solve a puzzle," a representative of Wheel of Fortune reportedly said to Fox News.

Even if they are upset by the technicality costing her the SUV, fans should know Rubush did not leave the show empty-handed. She still won $16,500 in prize money and also took home a nice vacation package.
