Franklin County Dog Shelter faces overcrowding

by Bri Buckley

Sun, December 26th 2021 at 9:34 PM
Updated Sun, December 26th 2021 at 9:02 AM
You can help clear out the Franklin County Dog Shelter and Adoption Center this holiday season! (Photo: Franklin County Dog Shelter)

    TOPICS:

    Maxsocial issuesKaye PersingerHuman InterestHospitality RecreationAnimalsBoDogs

COLUMBUS, Ohio (WSYX) — After a pandemic adoption puppy boom last year, shelters across the country and here in Central Ohio are faced with overcrowding issues.

"It’s been a problem across the nation right now just overwhelming overcrowding and we’re not special," said Franklin County Dog Shelter and Adoption Center director Kaye Persinger.

Persinger said the shelter is coming close to capacity levels with an influx of dogs coming in. She said a staffing shortage combined with a mild fall and winter has led to more dogs getting out and multiplying.

"There’s a shortage nationwide for veterinarians, vet assistants and vet techs so it’s nearly impossible to get in to see a vet right away, so some of them are unfortunately out there procreating because they haven't been spayed or neutered," said Persinger.

Max capacity at the shelter is 300 and Persinger said they have had over 200 dogs in house over the last few weeks.

If you've been thinking about adding a four legged friend to your family, now may be the time.

She suggested fostering to adopt to make sure it's the right fit for their fur-ever home.

"We give you everything you need to start off with that pet, we give you the bedding, the food any medications they’re on, they come fully vaccinated, spayed and neutered, microchipped licensed and this way if its not a good fit you can just give us some information about the dog so we can find a good fit for the dog," Persinger said.

If you already have a furry friend at home Kaye said to double check their registration if they are microchipped.

"If you have a dog and he’s microchipped or she, make sure that microchip is registered, we have gotten in the last two weeks three or four pure bred, very expensive breed dogs in with microchips but they’re not registered to anyone and they’re not licensed so we struggle getting them returned."

The shelter is always accepting donations, dry dog food and soft or chewy treats are currently at the top of the list.
