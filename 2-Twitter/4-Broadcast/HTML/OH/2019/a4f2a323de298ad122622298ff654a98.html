Alum Creek Lake will accept donated Christmas trees to turn into fish habitats

by Kalea Gunderson

Fri, December 27th 2019 at 5:58 PM

ALUM CREEK STATE PARK, Ohio (WSYX/WTTE) — If you opted for a real Christmas tree this year, it will soon have to be thrown out, but the U.S. Army Corps of Engineers is partnering with the Ohio Department of Natural Resources to offer an environmentally sustainable option.

Trees can be taken to Alum Creek Lake between from January 2-10 to have your tree turned into a fish habitat.

Before Alum Creek Lake existed, Resource Manager for the U.S. Army Corps of Engineers, Sylvia Chelf described the vast space as farmland.

"Around 3,000 acres of water surface, so it's a decent size lake," Chelf said.

Underneath the surface, there's not a lot of habitat for fish to find protection. That's why Christmas trees can be put to use.

"Small fish can hide from larger fish. The needles will fall off pretty quickly, but the branches will be left, and they'll be able to hide in there to protect themselves," Chelf said.

Any type of tree could be used, but Christmas trees are already cut down.

"Instead of us having to go out and cut down trees to put into the lake, we're trying to use what's already available," Chelf said. "We'll use cables to tie, usually 3-6 trees together, and then attach them to a few concrete blocks and put them in the lake."

Chelf said it's important to let professionals do it.

"If you just throw it in, it's just going to wash away. It's not going to provide the habitat you're hoping for. If it was alum creek, it could cause problems with the gates that we use," Chelf said.

So, to give your tree a new job to do and provide fish with a new home, it's simple.

"For anyone who's interested in helping out the environment, it's a great way to use your tree," Chelf said. "Just bring it here, make sure there's no decorations on it, and that you haven't sprayed any flocking on it or anything on it. Bring it up, and we'll have an area to put it at, and it's as easy as dropping it off."
