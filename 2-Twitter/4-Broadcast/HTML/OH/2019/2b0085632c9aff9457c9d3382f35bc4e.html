The most-viewed local stories on Local12.com in 2019

by WKRC

Thu, December 26th 2019 at 1:34 PM
Updated Thu, December 26th 2019 at 1:45 PM
The most-viewed local stories on Local12.com in 2019

    TOPICS:

    American footballAndy DaltonBataviaCincinnatiDisappearance Of Timmothy PitzenMarvin LewisSports in the United StatesUnderground Railroad

CINCINNATI (WKRC) - It was an eventful year filled with triumph and tragedy. Local 12 looks back at some of the most-viewed local stories on the website in 2019, in no particular order.

Please keep in mind these are not the most important nor the most impactful-- it's based strictly on pageviews since Jan. 1, 2019.

Thank you for making Local 12 your trusted source for breaking news, the latest information on top stories, weather and sports.


  • Woman found dead in Batavia Township business has been identified

The Clermont County Sheriff's Office identified the woman found dead inside a vehicle at a Batavia Township business in July as Miranda Hesler, 27.


  • Here's why Coney Island is getting rid of all of its rides

Coney Island announced in September that it is planning an "enhanced water park experience" that it will complete in phases over the timeframe of the next few years. These plans include removing all of the amusement park rides, game areas and closing shops.


  • Cincinnati Christian University to close its doors

Cincinnati Christian University students heard rumors about their school shutting down but were hopeful they could at least finish out this academic year. In October they learned via an email letter that when they packed up for winter break, they would not be coming back to campus.


  • Marvin Lewis, Hue Jackson each land head coaching jobs

Former Bengals head coach Marvin Lewis and former Cleveland Browns head coach Hue Jackson each landed a head coaching position - at least temporarily.


  • Coroner identifies adult, child killed in Butler County crash

Three people were killed, including a child, after a crash in Butler County in October. The Butler County Coroner has identified two of the victims as Alexa Perry, 25, and Daxton Perry, 2.


  • Three people, including gunman dead in crime spree in Adams, Pike counties

A crime spree that included murder, robbery and arson shook a small town in rural Ohio in September. Three people died, including the suspect of a self-inflicted gunshot wound.


  • Four members of a family shot and killed inside West Chester apartment

Police investigated the death of four members of the same West Chester family in April after another family member found them dead inside their apartment.


  • Cincinnati rapper, wine entrepreneur accused of running 'significant' drug operation

A federal grand jury indicted a Cincinnati rapper and wine company owner along with 11 others on charges related to a fentanyl and marijuana trafficking operation.


  • Norwood couple sentenced for rape of daughters will likely die in prison

In March, a judge sentenced a Norwood man who abused his three daughters and his wife who covered up the abuse for 14 years. Neither Herman See nor Angela Stites will ever get out of prison.


  • Funeral service for Clermont County Det. Bill Brewer

Funeral services were held in February for Clermont County Detective Bill Brewer, who was killed in the line of duty.


  • Mother of late teen who would have graduated Friday told to stay away from ceremony

Graduation is supposed to be a happy time, but for one local mother, it's double the heartbreak. Courtney Thompson died when she was 14. Her class graduated in May -- a ceremony her mother thought might include remembrance for her daughter.


  • 1 feared dead, 4 injured after downtown building partially collapses

A building under construction partially collapsed in downtown Cincinnati, according to Cincinnati Police. Four construction workers were hurt and another was feared dead at the time. After roughly 31 hours since a partial building collapse downtown Monday, firefighters located the body of the missing worker inside the Fourth Street structure. He was identified as Preston Todd Delph.


  • Local 12 anchor Rob Braun says goodbye after 35 years at WKRC

Local 12 celebrated Rob Braun's career on June 28


  • Local Steak 'n Shake shut down due to health code violations

Inspectors said it wasn't safe to eat at a local restaurant and ordered it closed in April.


  • Police trying to determine if teen found in Newport is boy abducted in 2011

Newport Police investigated after a teen told police he was abducted seven years ago and that his name is Timmothy Pitzen.


  • Former Bengals head coach Marvin Lewis sells Indian Hill home

Marvin Lewis sold his Indian Hill home on Alberly Lane for $2.3 million. Lewis put the 7,000-square-foot home on the market about six months prior, listed at $2.79 million.


  • Cincinnati man murdered, dumped in Madison County

A man found dead along I-71 north in Madison County has been identified as Rodney Watkins from Cincinnati.


  • Tracie Hunter dragged from court as protests erupt during her hearing

In dramatic fashion, former juvenile court judge Tracie Hunter passively resisted deputies who took her into custody and was dragged out of the courtroom by a female deputy to serve her six-month jail sentence.


  • Grant County High School teacher arrested for alleged sexual relationship with student

A Kentucky teacher was charged with raping her 15-year-old student.


  • Procession and public memorial service for Officer Dale Woods

Officer Dale Woods was a cop's cop. That sentiment was expressed more than once at his public memorial service at Xavier University's Cintas Center on Jan. 14.


  • Police: Woman found dead in her home was attacked by her Great Danes

An incident report filed by police says that a woman who was found dead in her home in Waynesville, Ohio died after being attacked by her two dogs.


  • Bengals QB Dalton angry, hurt about decision to bench him

Bengals quarterback Andy Dalton appeared to have to fight back tears at times in an eight-minute interview with Cincinnati-area media, one day after he was benched by head coach Zac Taylor in favor of rookie Ryan Finley in a move Dalton said took him by surprise.


  • Possible skeletal remains found as vehicles pulled from the Ohio River in Aberdeen

Possible human remains were found inside a car that was pulled from the Ohio River in June. Police say large skeletal bones were seen once the car was brought on land.


  • Cammy Dierking ends her 31-year career with Local 12

Cammy Dierking signed off on Dec. 20.
