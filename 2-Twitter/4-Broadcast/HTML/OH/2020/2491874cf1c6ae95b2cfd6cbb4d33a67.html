Community leaders, members outraged over officer-involved shooting in north Columbus

by Mary Smith

Wed, December 23rd 2020 at 10:43 PM
Updated Wed, December 23rd 2020 at 12:58 PM
4
VIEW ALL PHOTOS
A Columbus Police officer shot and killed a man along Oberlin Drive near Upper Arlington early Tuesday morning Dec. 22, 2020 following a report of a suspicious car in the area, police said. (WSYX/WTTE)

COLUMBUS, Ohio (WSYX/WTTE) — Mayor Andrew Ginther said the officer accused of shooting and killing an unarmed man early Tuesday did not have his body camera on when he fired his gun.

Sources confirm the officer to be Adam Coy, a more than fifteen year veteran of the force.

Investigators have not released the name of the man killed.

Tuesday night, a small protest was held downtown outside of the statehouse.

"I was outraged. Like, are we still doing this? You know, we just got done marching for Casey and we've been doing this all summer. It's unbelievable," said Joshua Williams, a concerned community member.

Dejuan Sharp, another protestor, is asking for accountability.

"I understand it's a hard job to be a police officer. But we have trust and faith in police officers because they should be held accountable to a higher standard than normal citizens," Sharp said.

The city confirms the officer has been relieved of duty, following the shooting near Upper Arlington. The Ohio Bureau of Criminal Investigation has taken over the case.

"I am deeply saddened, frustrated, angry, demanding answers of what happened in our community earlier this morning," said Ginther at a press conference Tuesday afternoon.

Police said they wereinitially called to the area for a noise complaint.

WSYX/WTTE spoke with the neighbor who called 9111.

"There was a car parked out all night long running. And I was kinda concerned about that. You don’t have things like that in this neighborhood," Bob Ronker said.

"While it is very early in the investigation, there is one fact that disturbs me greatly. The officer involved did not turn on their body worn camera until after the shooting. The function of the technology provides a 60-second look back, recording 60 seconds prior to the camera being turned on," said Ginther, who said the recording did not capture audio. "Let me be clear; if you're not going to turn on your body-worn camera, you cannot serve and protect the people of Columbus."

Ginther has asked US Attorney David DeVillers's Office to investigate the shooting over a potential civil rights violation.

DeVillers's Office sent us the following statement:

After speaking with Mayor Ginther and Ohio Attorney General Yost, I was informed that BCI was immediately notified of the fatal officer-involved shooting that occurred early today. BCI is investigating this matter, as is policy with all shootings involving Columbus Division of Police. The mayor requested that the U.S. Attorney’s Office review the investigation for possible federal civil rights violations, and after consulting with Ohio Attorney General Yost, I agreed that my office will review the case as requested once BCI’s investigation is complete. This office will then consult with the Franklin County Prosecutor’s Office on how to proceed at the conclusion of our review.

We requested an interview with Columbus Police Chief, Thomas Quinlan. Our request was declined.

Congresswoman Joyce Beatty sent out the following statement on the shooting:

There are not enough words to express the pain and anger I feel that another Black man has been killed in our community at the hands of law enforcement in less than a month. While we do not know all of the facts, what I do know is that this is unacceptable. I am greatly concerned and call into question police procedures as well as the timing and inconsistent use of body cameras. I stand with the community in seeking the truth and demanding justice. My condolences go out to the family and friends of the victim.

The Fraternal Order of Police Lodge 9 tells us it has no comment at this time as it waits for more information on the incident.

Columbus City Council said in part:

The compounding heartbreak of learning an unarmed Black man was killed last night by a Columbus police officer is beyond description. Thoughts and prayers cannot soothe this pain, and the members of Columbus City Council are beyond frustrated at this senseless death. Too many families in our community are mourning at a time when we should be seeking peace and hope. We appreciate the swift, clear action taken by Mayor Ginther, calling for a Federal investigation into the violation of the victim’s civil rights, relieving the Police Officer of duty, and working with BCI to immediately start an independent investigation. We are impatient for answers but insist on a thorough, professional, and complete investigation that ensures justice is done. Our prayers are with the family affected by the shooting.
