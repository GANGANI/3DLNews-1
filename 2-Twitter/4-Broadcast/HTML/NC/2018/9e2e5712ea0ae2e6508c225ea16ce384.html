Local wildlife sanctuary wants your old Christmas tree

by Christina Thompson and Katie Caviness

Thu, December 27th 2018 at 2:13 PM
Updated Thu, December 27th 2018 at 4:15 PM

HUBERT, Onslow County — Places all across Eastern North Carolina are willing to take your old Christmas tree off your hands.

One of those places is Possumwood Acres Wildlife Sanctuary in Hubert, North Carolina.

The sanctuary uses the old trees to naturally insulate cages and for animal enrichment. There are some rules if you're interested in donating your tree.

You have to clean it off first. No decorations, tinsel or strings can left on because those are potentially harmful. You can drop your donated tree off any day between 11 a.m. to 5 p.m.

The sanctuary is located at 119 Doe Drive Hubert, NC 28539. If you have any questions, their phone number is (910) 326-6432. The group has rescued and rehabilitated nearly 1,500 birds, mammals and reptiles this year.

Their mission statement includes the following key points:

  • To provide a wild animal facility for the rescue, treatment, and release of sick, injured, and orphaned native wildlife
  • To educate the public about and to promote understanding of the local wildlife and their habitats
  • To increase awareness of and appreciation for the value of wildlife and the environment, and to protect these natural resources
  • To promote the highest standards in all activities of the wildlife rehabilitator profession
  • To promote networking, communication and cooperation among rehabilitators, wildlife organizations and facilities on local, statewide, and national levels

The sanctuary specializes in rehabilitation care and treatment of small mammals, songbirds, waterfowl, raptors and shorebirds. If you're interested in visiting, tickets are $7 for adults and $6 for children 4 years old and above.

If you can't make it to Possumwood Acres to donate your tree, the Town of North Topsail Beach and the City of Greenville are also accepting tree donations.
