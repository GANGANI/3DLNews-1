Owner of Cruso campground demolished by 2021 floods has high hopes for new year

by Rex Hodge

Thu, December 29th 2022 at 6:26 PM
Updated Fri, December 30th 2022 at 8:44 PM
7
VIEW ALL PHOTOS

DEC. 29, 2022 - Laurel Bank Campground owner Sherrie McArthur said cleanup from flooding in 2021 is ongoing and could take several years to complete. (Photo credit: WLOS staff)

CRUSO, N.C. (WLOS) — In August 2021, the remnants of Tropical Storm Fred devastated parts of Haywood County. A campground in Cruso was hit especially hard and with fatal consequences.

Campground owner Sherrie McArthur is still recovering, but she has hopes for a better 2023.

For McArthur, the impacts of the tragedy remain. A lot of destruction is still present, though many of the debris piles have been picked up. But for her, she must figure out what her next moves are with the property and how to handle things emotionally as we head into a new year.

“It's just been hard on so many people,” McArthur said.

Almost a year and a half have passed since the remnants of the storm swamped her Laurel Bank Campground, taking four lives onsite and two others in Cruso. The upheaval lingers.

“All up and down the highway, people's lives are still disrupted,” McArthur said.

For her, it has been months and months of navigating what she said can be complicated assistance.

“I guess it’s county and state that are supposed to come and clean up all the riverbank, and there's piles that they made on the riverbank with an orange X,” McArthur said.

Many of the mangled campers have been removed. However, some remain, as do some debris piles. The cleanup is going to take time.

“They've told me three years, maybe, on some things,” McArthur said.

Then, there’s what to do with the land.

“I really need to get with, I guess, the county to see if there is something I can do with the property and how much of a flood plain zone, ordinance thing is on this property,” McArthur said. “I’ve got to start finding out what I can do or can’t do. I'm still thinking I may raise hog."

But it's all a sorting out process for McArthur.

“It will work out the way it’s supposed to, I do believe. I just don't know. I'm just lost, still,” she said.

Knowing just what move to make is difficult.

“So, maybe, it's just time for me to go,” McArthur said.

That would be hard for McArthur. Cruso is home and the campground her father's legacy.

Still, there are many pieces to figure out -- even after all this time. She is sure and appreciative of the community's help along the way and sympathizes with everyone still struggling to recover after the flood.

The recent sunlight lifts her spirits, and she keeps her faith in the future.

“I just keep plugging along. I think we all kind of go into the new year with hope of it being wonderful, something good, and I have that hope.”
