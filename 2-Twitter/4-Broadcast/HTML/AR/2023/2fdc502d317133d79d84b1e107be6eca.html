Watch the Red Wolves Live Camellia Bowl Special

By Logan Whaley
Published: Dec. 19, 2023 at 11:08 PM CST|Updated: Dec. 23, 2023 at 12:17 AM CST

MONTGOMERY, Ala. (KAIT) - Arkansas State football will play in its first Bowl game since 2019 when the Red Wolves take on Northern Illinois in the Camellia Bowl on Saturday, December 23 on ESPN.

Logan Whaley arrived in Montgomery on Tuesday, you’ll find detailed reports of each day below.

For a full breakdown of the Camellia Bowl matchup, check out Logan’s preview here.

You can get ready for gameday with the Red Wolves Live Camellia Bowl Special. Hear from Arkansas State players, head coach Butch Jones, and much more.

Watch the Red Wolves Live Camellia Bowl Special tonight at 10:15pm on KAIT, kait8.com, and on the K8 News app.

THURSDAY - Preparation Continues

The Red Wolves had another day of practice, this one was closed off to the media as the team continues to prepare for the Camellia Bowl.

I got to see firsthand how the team unwinds after a busy day of preparation. It was at Wednesday’s practice where I learned how Arkansas State is preparing for its upcoming Camellia Bowl matchup against Northern Illinois, but after practice, I learned probably the biggest news of the weekend. Well, big for me as a video game nerd. The team has a players lounge, one safety Justin Parks described to me as like an arcade.

“It’s the best I’ve seen so far,” Parks said. “It has everything you could ever need.

Like a true investigative journalist, my interest was piqued. I had to learn more.

I signed off from my live shot on K8 News at 6 by telling Chris Carter that I thought I was going to sneak in there. So I did. And I was right, it was everything I hoped for and more.

“[I] Watch guys play 2K, Madden, see guys play pool, shoot basketball, skee ball, even race,” senior wide receiver Jeff Foreman said. “Same thing with pool, we have a tournament with pool and all that stuff. My team, we got beat in the second round, which wasn’t my fault.”

For the bulk of this young team, Saturday will mark their first Bowl game in their college careers, so of course they’re soaking in all the fun they can have, but at the same time, the team is focused on one thing: Beating Northern Illinois on Saturday. The general mood I’ve seen out of the time is one of happiness and excitement, but also focus.

“I told a lot of people to enjoy this moment, soak in everything, but remember at the end of the day we still came here to win,” Foreman, who was one of a few players to play in A-State’s last Bowl game, the 2019 Camellia Bowl, said.

“It’s good to have fun but at the end of the day, what’s all that fun if we lose the game,” Parks added. “The plan is to win the game, so just staying locked in is the biggest thing.”

“That’s just basically showing the maturity of the team,” sophomore defensive lineman Tim Hardiman said. “You have these events out here, you’re going to have all this stuff where you going to, you have free time to go out but at the same time you’ve got to still got to be locked in and have a game. Be locked into team preparation, watching film and do all that stuff. You enjoy the moment and enjoy being around your team but at the same time you still got a job to accomplish.”

Friday will feature a packed day, with a team luncheon in the hotel, a pep rally in the evening and an A-State Bowl Bash at Wintzell’s in Montgomery.

WEDNESDAY - First Day of Practices

Arkansas State wrapped up what will more than likely be the busiest day leading up to the bowl game.

It all started in the morning with National Signing Day. Butch Jones started his shift, so to speak, at 6 in the morning, waiting on the first batch of signees to roll in.

“It’s been non-stop, chaotic, chaos but in a good way,” Jones said. “We started off with a staff meeting and we continued evaluations of where we’re at in the recruiting process, meetings, individual meetings and even recruiting on the field. Recruiting never stops.”

Jones was leading today’s hour-and-a-half-long practice, while also taking some recruiting phone calls. He says he expects a few more signings over the next couple of weeks.

As for the practice itself, the Red Wolves continue to put the final touches on the game plan, one that was built in Jonesboro.

“Our players are very comfortable with the gameplan, obviously they’re the ones that make it come to life,” Jones said. “To be able to have all of our gameplans installed, and then come here and really it’s fine-tuning and then making some last-minute adjustments, but everything is through repetition.”

The team wrapped up their day unwinding a bit, they’re playing in the Camellia Bowl in 3 days, but they spent Wednesday night bowling, literally, at Bama Lanes. You can tell the team is having fun with the majority of the team experiencing a bowl game for the first time.

Even with the fun, the team is staying focused on their upcoming opponent.

“We still got a game to win,” junior safety Justin Parks said. “It’s good to have fun but at the end of the day, what’s all that fun if we lose the game. The plan is to win the game, so just staying locked in is the biggest thing.”

TUESDAY - The Arrival

Arkansas State football arrived at the team hotel in the afternoon. It was a quick turnaround for Butch Jones, holding a team meeting as soon as the group arrived. Then, a half-hour later, he talked for around 10 minutes at the introductory press conference with him and Northern Illinois head coach Thomas Hammock.

When the Red Wolves stepped off the bus, it was a business-like demeanor, yet you could see glimpses of joy. For the bulk of these players, it will be their first Bowl game they’ll play in.

“I think when you go a few years without a bowl game, it kind of puts things in perspective so I’m just proud of all the hard work behind the scenes,” Jones said. “When we had our team meeting when we arrived and just seeing the excitement of our players, that was a great gratifying moment for me.”

For Jones, and really for the A-State program in general this season, it’s an all-too-familiar place. When the Red Wolves take the field Saturday, it will have marked the third time the team has played in Alabama this season, let alone this being the seventh time in school history the team is playing a Bowl in Alabama.

Beyond that, Jones spent three seasons as an offensive analyst and a special assistant to Nick Saban at Alabama.

“[I’ve] been here many times and I’ve already been reminded that I’m staying in the Nick Saban suite so I had to send them all a video of it,” Jones said with a laugh. “It’s exciting to be able to come back here and then my three years at the University of Alabama was probably one of the three most gratifying years I’ve been a part of.

As for the Red Wolves opponent, NIU, much like A-State, won 3 games last season and improved to 6 wins this year. The Huskies are searching for their first Bowl win since beating A-State in the GoDaddy.com Bowl in 2012. The team has dropped each of their past seven Bowls.

“We’ve had an opportunity to watch [Arkansas State’s] whole season, watch them progress, watch guys take steps and get better,” NIU head coach Thomas Hammock said. “Their quarterback is certainly a guy that we have to make sure we have 11 hands on deck. They got two really good running backs. They’ve got receivers that can make plays. And they’re ginormous on the offensive line... We’ve got a tremendous challenge. And I know the history of these games, hopefully, it’s a great game in the fourth quarter fighting like heck to win the game.”

Stay tuned to this page for updates. Wednesday will feature both teams hitting the practice field for the first time, as worlds collide with it being National Signing Day. A-State will hold a press conference over Zoom, you’ll hear updates on K8 News at 5:00, 6:00, and 10:00pm.
