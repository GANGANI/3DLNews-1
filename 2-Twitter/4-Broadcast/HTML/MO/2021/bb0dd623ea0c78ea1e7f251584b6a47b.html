Southeast Iowa dog breeder fined additional $40,000 over puppy mill

by Tasha Turner

Sun, November 14th 2021 at 9:20 PM
Updated Mon, November 15th 2021 at 3:52 AM
7

Two dogs found dead at the facility run by Daniel Gingerich of Seymour. (Photo from U.S. District Court exhibits)

SEYMOUR, Iowa — Daniel Gingerich of Seymour, Iowa is now facing an additional $40,000 in fines.

Back in October, The U.S. Department of Agriculture (USDA) sued Gingerich $20,000 due to over 120 documented violations.

From September to November, four court hearings have taken place. During the fourth hearing, Gingerich surrendered over 500 dogs to the Animal Rescue League of Iowa. He also agreed to no longer make a profit off selling dogs.

The Iowa Department of Agriculture and Land Stewardship (IDALS) said that Gingerich has yet to pay the $20,000 in fines he owes. He also passed the time in which he could have filed an appeal.

IDALS officials have now added an additional $40,000 in fines and revoked his license due to the finding inspectors discovered while walking through his facility.

Gingerich has until Nov. 19 to either appeal the order or pay the fine.

The Animal Rescue League of Iowa and the ASPCA are still currently working to remove the remaining dogs in his facilities.

Over 200 dogs have been rescued already.
