FIRST WARN FORECAST: Super Bowl Sunday is set to see highs but cooler than past temps

By Warren Sears
Published: Feb. 10, 2024 at 7:49 PM CST

All eyes on Super Bowl Sunday! We are trending towards a cooler afternoon in the lower to mid 40s with a good deal of cloud coverage during the day. Some later day sunshine is possible. At least our breeze looks light. The actual game looks cool but dry. The backyard tailgates look just fine! The later we go into Sunday night, the closer we need to watch a passing storm system just off to our south. There is a rain snow mix that tries to inch closer into Monday morning, but I do think most of us stay dry. Those of you down in Anderson, Linn, Bates and Henry counties would have the better chance to see a bit of moisture early Monday. The best snow chance is in far southern Missouri.

The main story over the next week is a warming trend that peaks again Tuesday, Wednesday and Thursday as highs push back into the 50s. There is a small opportunity for a wintry mix late next week, but only one of our data models is hinting at this. Just keeping a 20% chance as of now. We are seeing signs of a shot of colder air for next weekend as highs could dip back into the 30s.
