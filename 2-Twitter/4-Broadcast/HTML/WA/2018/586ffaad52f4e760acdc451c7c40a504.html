Concerned Sultan woman's phone call saves German hiker's life

by KOMO Staff

Tue, October 30th 2018 at 2:02 PM
Updated Wed, October 31st 2018 at 5:47 PM
4

Katharina is seen after her rescue. (Photo credit: Snohomish County Helicopter Rescue Team)

GLACIER PEAK, Wash. - A phone call from a concerned Snohomish County resident ended up saving the life of a German woman who was attempting to hike through the Cascades to the Canadian border without the proper gear as snow and cold hit the region on Monday.

Nancy Abel of Sultan originally met the German woman, Katharina Groene, about a week ago as she was hiking near Stevens Pass. Katharine told Nancy that she had started off on a solo hike along the Pacific Crest Trail at the Mexican border in May and was determined to keep going to the Canadian border.

Nancy, who is an avid hiker, joined Katharina for the first couple miles of her trek from Stevens Pass and tried to talk her out of hiking so far north in the mountains so late in the year without the proper equipment and experience, but Katharina believed she was well prepared for the weather and kept heading north.

Nancy returned home to Sultan. But over the weekend, as snow and cold weather moved in to the mountains, Nancy became concerned and called 911 to report her concerns and see if rescuers could check on Katharina, Snohomish County sheriff's officials said.

The Snohomish County Helicopter Rescue Team launched SnoHawk 1 on Monday and were barely able to fly the helicopter due to the blowing snow and sleet.

Once in the area they began searching for Katharina and found footprints in the snow, then followed them until they located Katharina at the 5,000-foot level near Glacier Peak.

It took several attempts to find a suitable landing area and then several more attempts to get the helicopter safely on the ground. There were no level areas to set the helicopter down.

Chief pilot Quistorf finally was able to find a place where he could place one skid on the ground while pilot Espeland jumped from the helicopter and began stacking logs under the other skid to make a somewhat level platform to set the helicopter on.

Once they were on the ground they reached Katharina and found she was woefully unprepared for winter mountain weather. Her shelter had blown away, she had lost two sets of gloves, she was soaking wet and running low on food. She said she had attempted to call for help the day before, but had no cell service.

Finally, she became so desperate that she left messages on her phone apologizing to her friends and family for dying on the Pacific Crest Trail.

"Katharina did not need any encouragement to climb onboard SnoHawk 1 and fly off the mountain," the rescue team said in a Facebook post. "A huge thank you to Nancy .... who made the phone call that saved this young lady's life!"

The rescuers flew Katharina back to Taylors Landing. Nancy met her there and invited Katharina to stay with her until she is ready to fly back to Germany.

The Snohomish County Sheriff's Office said Nancy's phone call undoubtedly saved Katharina's life.

"If Nancy had not taken action Katharina would have most likely died in the mountains," the sheriff's office said in a Facebook post.
