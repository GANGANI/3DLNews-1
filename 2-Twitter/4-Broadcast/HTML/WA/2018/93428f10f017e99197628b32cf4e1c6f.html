Tips to prevent plumbing problems

by Herbert Weisbaum

Mon, December 10th 2018 at 6:30 AM
Updated Thu, December 13th 2018 at 3:46 PM

    TOPICS:

    BathroomsEuthenicsFlush ToiletGarbage Disposal UnitHygienePublic HealthSanitationToilet

Having a plumber come to the house isn't cheap. So, you want to do everything you can to reduce your chances of having plumbing problems.

You probably know that grease doesn’t go down the drain. Let it congeal and then throw it in the trash.

Kevin Brasler, executive editor atCheckbook.org, says the same goes for uncooked rice – it's compost.

“Uncooked rice creates major problems for both garbage disposers and regular drains. It gets in there and the water gets in the rice and the rice expands and really tears up the pipes,” Brasler said.

Do yourself a favor, don't flush stuff down the toilet that should go in the trash can.

“Only flush toilet paper down the toilet. Don't throw feminine products down there or cat litter or anything else really, it will clog up the works,” Brasler said. (Note: This includes wipes, they do not go in the toilet.)

You never know when a pipe will burst, especially when the temperature drops below freezing. That leak can cause serious damage. Everyone in your family should know where the main water shutoff is located and how to use it.

“That way, if you have a problem, you can turn the water off before it floods out your entire house,” Brasler said.

More Info:Tips for Maintaining Your Home's Plumbing

Correction: This story originally stated that grease could go in the compost bin. That was incorrect. It should go in the trash.
