Reward increased to $15,500 in Portsmouth dog abandonment case

by JESSICA A. BOTELHO, NBC 10 NEWS

Thu, December 2nd 2021 at 8:58 PM
Updated Thu, December 2nd 2021 at 9:07 PM
7
VIEW ALL PHOTOS
Police say Gravy is one of two dogs abandoned in Portsmouth Thanksgiving morning. (Photo courtesy of The Potter League for Animals)

PORTSMOUTH, R.I. (WJAR) — A reward for information that leads to the arrest and conviction of whoever is responsible for abandoning two dogs in Portsmouth Thanksgiving morning has more than tripled.

The Potter League for Animals said the reward was originally $5,000 but was raised to $15,500 Wednesday, thanks to anonymous donors.

Kara Montalbano, who is the director of marketing and community relations at the Potter League, previously told NBC 10 News she is grateful that people are stepping up to help.

“Bad things happened but a whole lot of people are doing the right thing," Montalbano said. "We just know how awesome people can be, and hopefully, we can turn a bad situation in to a good one.”

Police said they received a call about two female dogs Thursday morning at 7.

The dogs, affectionately named Turkey and Gravy, were found lying on the side of the road in the Gardner Seveney Sports Complex.

Police said the animals appeared to have been abandoned, describing them as lethargic and emaciated.

  • READ MORE: Two 'emaciated' dogs found abandoned on Thanksgiving Day in Portsmouth

“They were both severely underweight,” Brad Shear, who is the CEO of the Potter League, told NBC 10. “You can see every bone every rib on this dog’s body. They’ve been malnourished for a very long time. It takes weeks for dogs to lose this much weight.”

Shear said a veterinarian hospital initially treated the dogs, but quickly realized they needed to go to an emergency hospital immediately.

Turkey’s health declined suddenly, and she had to be euthanized.

“Unfortunately, one of the dogs did not survive because her condition was so bad,” Shear said. “She was having issues with her liver that she was not going to recover from.”

Despite all she has endured, Shear and Montalbano said Gravy is recovering.

Montalbano said the dog was initially shy but has gotten more comfortable.

“Seeing her peppier kind of made everything better,” Montalbano said.

Shear agreed. He said Gravy is a trooper.

“Through all she’s been through, her personality still really comes through,” he said. “She’s just so sweet and loving.”

  • READ MORE: $5K reward offered in abandoned Portsmouth dog case

Her breed is unknown, and it has not been confirmed if Turkey and Gravy were from the same litter.

As to how the dogs were abandoned, an investigation is ongoing.

“This shouldn’t happen just because of a lack of resources for someone,” Shear said. “Last year, we gave away 40,000 pounds of pet food for people who needed help.”

In the meantime, Shear said he and his staff are working to restore Gravy’s health. Then, he said, she will be available for adoption.

Shear and Montalbano are optimistic the reward will help uncover whoever is responsible.

“We’re hoping that it leads to something,” Montalbano said.

Police asked anyone who has information about the case are to contact the Detective Division at 401-683-0994 or 401-683-0300.

(NBC 10's Christina Vitale and Anthony Vega contributed to this report.)
