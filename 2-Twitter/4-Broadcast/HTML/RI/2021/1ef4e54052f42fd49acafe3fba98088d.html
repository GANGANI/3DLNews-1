Misquamicut Fall Festival makes a return after two long years

by MOLLY LEVINE, NBC 10 NEWS

Sat, September 18th 2021 at 10:46 PM
Updated Sat, September 18th 2021 at 11:19 PM
4
VIEW ALL PHOTOS
The Misquamicut Fall Festival returned after two long years. (WJAR)

WESTERLY, R.I. (WJAR) — After two long years, Misquamicut Fall Festival is back!

It's taking place at the town beach all weekend long and tickets are available online. There is no mask or vaccine requirement as this is an outdoor event.

Many of the vendors at events, like Fall Fest, survive off these crowds.

"I got laid off from my job, I had never gotten laid off before, you know work was slow for all of us," said Joe Dourado, owner of Joe's Snack Shack.

Opening Joe's Snack Shack was a dream of his, and he's happy to see peoples smiling faces while they enjoy his food. Joe is not alone in building a new career path during the COVID-19 pandemic.

"I was one of the first people put out of work because I was a waitress. And so I pulled out the starter kit, went to town, and built this up," said Chrystal Bariteau who sells jewelry at festivals.

Organizers of the festival said the vendors really make up a fair.

"During COVID a lot of the vendors went out of business. Some vendors we had for years are just gone, so it took a little more effort, but we were able to do it. We got 60 vendors for this; we've had 80 before, so we definitely lost some because of COVID," said Caswell Cooke Jr., the executive director of the Misquamicut Business Association.

The money raised at the Fall Festival goes towards beautifying the small town and making free events possible.

The festival will go through the end of the weekend.
