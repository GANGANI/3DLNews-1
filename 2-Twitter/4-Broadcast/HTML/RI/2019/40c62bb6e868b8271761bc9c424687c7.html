Brown gets $53.4 million grant to aid dementia patients and caregivers

by BARBARA MORSE SILVA, NBC 10 NEWS

Tue, September 10th 2019 at 6:38 PM
Updated Tue, September 10th 2019 at 7:43 PM
Brown University was awarded a major grant that will aid dementia patients and caregivers. (WJAR)

PROVIDENCE, R.I., (WJAR) — A major announcement out of Brown University promises to make improve care for people living with dementia, and their caregivers.

They've been awarded $53.4 million dollars from the National Institute on Aging to help fund non-drug, care-based interventions.

"It's a really big deal," said Vincent Mor, a professor of health services, policy and practice at Brown’s School of Public Health, who's also playing a lead role in this massive collaborative research, trying to come up with better ways to care for those with memory loss and their caregivers.

"Most of the things that are currently working are done in small pilots or is done, you know, there's a really good study published in the New England Journal but can a health system; could RI Hospital, could Mass General, can they actually do something with their population of people that would work in the real world," said Mor.

Brown University, along with Hebrew SeniorLife, will act as the research incubator, soliciting ideas and applications for clinical trials. Ideas like a current Brown University study that's looking at individualized music to reduce anxiety in those with advanced dementia.

"The idea is that the music will stimulate memories that will quell that anxiety," said Mor.

"We hope to fund 30 to 40 different pilots. We're going to be very carefully supporting the pilots and monitor what their experiences are so the real question that this is all about is basically can you go -- instead of the bench to beds -- can you go trial to health system and is it workable? Is there a business case, is there a way to implement these things in a structured manner."

These pilot programs will take place in real-world health care systems, and they are now accepting applications.
