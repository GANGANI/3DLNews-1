Hundreds of Oklahomans lose power following Sunday night storms

by Megan Butler, KTUL Staff

Mon, October 11th 2021 at 8:01 AM
Updated Mon, October 11th 2021 at 3:12 PM
5
VIEW ALL PHOTOS
Damage is seen in Coweta, Okla., on Oct. 11, 2021, following storms. (KTUL)

This severe weather story is accurate as of 3 p.m. Monday, Oct. 11.

UPDATE: OG&E says all customers in northeast Oklahoma should have power restored.

PSO is still reporting nearly 700 customers without power.

---

TULSA, Okla. (KTUL) -- PSO and OG&E are reporting hundreds of outages in northeastern Oklahoma this morning.

Storms ripped through Green Country Sunday night, leaving damage behind.

  • RELATED | Coweta damaged as strong, tornadic storms push through Oklahoma

PSO is reporting roughly 1,500 customers without power, most of which are in or around Coweta.

OG&E is reporting 4,750 customers without power, but only about 700 of those are in northeast Oklahoma.
