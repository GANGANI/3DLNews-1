Top Sports Moments of 2022 (Part 2): Rivalries, softball, summer baseball and track

by Hayden Herrera

Mon, December 26th 2022 at 8:18 PM
Updated Mon, December 26th 2022 at 9:38 PM

EUGENE, Ore. — Stunning upsets, local dynasties, the mighty underdog, and the biggest competition on earth.

It's time to find out which stories rose above the rest in our "Top Sports Moments of 2022."

Moments 6 through 10 can be found here.

Here are the Top 5:

#5 – Oregon tight end Spencer Webb passes away

At No. 5 on our countdown, a moment from the summer for Oregon football that bonded them through tragedy.

On July 13, Ducks tight end Spencer Webb died in a cliff diving accident near Triangle Lake.

His death was a shocking one, and one that helped bring the football team together.

This season at Autzen Stadium, the 4- and 18-yard lines were marked yellow in honor of Webb; and many times this season, opposing schools would lay flowers at those marks to show their respect.

Spencer Webb was 22 years old.

#4 - Oregon State softball reaches Women’s College World Series

At No. 4, how about a team that no one gave a chance to - the Oregon State softball team?

The Beavers finished 6th in the Pac-12 standings but not only advanced to a Super Regional; the Beavs swept the Stanford Cardinal at home to advance to the Women's College World Series for the first time in 16 years.

While the trip to OKC did not end with a national championship, Laura Berg's team proved to everyone that something special is brewing at Kelly Field in Corvallis.

#3 – Eugene Emeralds win Northwest League title

To No. 3, it’s the boys of summer – and winning a title is hard enough. But winning back-to-back titles is nearly impossible.

That's what the Eugene Emeralds did in 2022.

On September 17 in Vancouver, British Columbia, in front of nearly 6,000 Canucks, the Ems swept the Vancouver Canadians to give Eugene their fourth championship in the last six seasons - a dynasty in the Willamette Valley - with Carlos Valderrama also winning Manager of the Year for the Giants high-A club.

#2 – Oregon State football upsets Oregon with dramatic second half comeback

Only two spots left! At No. 2, we revisit to November 26, a day in Corvallis that started with half of the city not having electricity and ended in one of the craziest finishes in the history of the Ducks and Beavers illustrious football rivalry.

8-3 Oregon State hosted 9-2 Oregon, and it's not often that this game is a Top 25 matchup; but that’s what we got last month at Reser stadium.

The game started in the Ducks’ favor. Near the end of the third quarter, Oregon State was down 21 before the unthinkable happened.

Everything that could go wrong for Oregon did - and the Beavers mounted their comeback.

Freshman running back Isaiah Newell wrote his name into Beavers lore with two touchdowns to give OSU the lead. A last-chance Oregon rally never materialized, and the half-packed Reser stormed the field after the Beavers’ victory.

It’s a game and rivalry that should give us some fantastic clashes for years to come.

#1 – Eugene hosts World Athletics Championships Oregon22

And at No. 1, the top sports story of 2022 starts with four words: “Hello world, meet Oregon.”

For the first time ever, the World Athletics Championships were held in North America at the newly redesigned and upgraded Hayward Field in Eugene.

A ten-day extravaganza with athletes from every corner of the world making their way to Eugene in hopes of leaving a world champion.

Oregon22 was an event unlike any other for our area, with tourism at a level that isn't even seen during football season.

Over the course of ten days in July, nearly 150,000 ticketed persons saw more than 1,700 athletes from 179 countries go for the gold.

Team USA could not be topped: 33 total medals, including 13 gold - more than any other country, without question.

We also saw three world records broken: Sydney McLaughlin in the women's 400 hurdles; Tobi Amusan in the women's 100 hurdles; and Mondo Duplantis in the men's pole vault.

Oregon22 also marked Allyson Felix's final time competing on the track.

An event that Western Oregon will never forget.
