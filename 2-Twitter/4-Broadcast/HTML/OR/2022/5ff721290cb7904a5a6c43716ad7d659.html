Community Access Center at Valley River closes with plans to move to mobile care

by Gold Meadows and NBC16.com Staff

Wed, December 21st 2022 at 11:24 AM
Updated Thu, December 22nd 2022 at 5:43 PM
(Gold Meadows/SBG)

EUGENE, Ore. — The end of the year means changes for one of Lane County's newest health access centers.


Lane County Public Health announced this week that the Community Access Center at Valley River Center will close December 30.

The Valley River Center's Community Access Center began offering vaccinations to the public in June, but Lane County Public Health says they're shifting focus to mobile services.

Jason Davis with LCPH says the pandemic struck in ways that highlighted disparities in accessibility to health services like flu and COVID vaccines.

"It underscored the need for us to look at the gaps in the systems that we were and the services that we were delivering. One of the strategies for that was having a dedicated storefront at a place where people already go like Valley River Center, where people are already shopping."

Vaccinations, COVID testing, blood pressure screenings, and other preventative care was available, but the data showed LCPH they weren't reaching people in underserved areas who also needed these services.

"A lot of the folks that we were serving were folks that already had established primary care provider relationships, people who would traditionally go somewhere else for their health care, and typically do get vaccinated on a regular basis. So while it's convenient, it wasn't necessarily hitting that target of us expanding into areas like rural areas. We have places like Oakridge, Florence, Veneta where you know there are limited health care services."

LCPH says mobile services will allow them to focus available resources on those areas including economically challenged areas.

They purchased the mobile unit in June, and it cuts down staffing needs from four or five workers to two.

"Valley River Center and the mobile health unit are costly. Staffing is a challenge for us and everybody else around the county. Just looking at what can we do to become as fiscally responsible as possible."

Davis says that means going where public health is needed.

He says it will mean serving roughly 20 people a day rather than 75.

Davis expects LCPH to have four to five mobile clinics each week beginning in the new year.

"We've done clinics all over the place, and I think that diversity of locations is really important to meet people where they're at to make it easy and accessible to them and to really try to make the healthy choice the easy choice."

The center will operate during normal business hours through December 30.

It's open Wednesday-Saturday, 1:00 p.m. to 7:00 p.m., with the exception of December 24 (open from 1:00 p.m. to 6:00 p.m.), December 25 (closed) and December 31 (open 1:00 p.m. to 6:00 p.m.).

LCPH has its Communicable Disease Clinic at 151 W 7th Ave in Eugene open from open Monday through Friday from 8:00 a.m. to 5:00 p.m.
