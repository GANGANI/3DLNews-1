Harris' approach to immigration is a 'failed strategy,' says ex-border official

by ELISSA SALAMY, The National Desk

Tue, December 28th 2021 at 5:45 AM

FILE - In this March 2, 2019, file photo, a Customs and Border Control agent patrols on the U.S. side of a razor-wire-covered border wall along the Mexico east of Nogales, Ariz. (AP Photo/Charlie Riedel,File)

WASHINGTON (TND) — Several hundred migrants that were making the trek to the United States on foot began boarding buses to northern Mexico Wednesday, bringing them closer to the nation's southern border.

Vice President Kamala Harris has been tasked with addressing the border crisis and explained in a recent interview her approach to immigration.

“My approach to the issue in Guatemala and its neighboring countries, which has been formally called the Northern Triangle, is to do what we have, I think, a responsibility to do as a member of the Western Hemisphere; to assist in dealing with the root causes of migration out of those countries and that is my primary focus,” said Harris. “I think it's important to focus on the details of what we collectively want. And I think everyone, regardless of where they are on the political spectrum, appreciates the point that people don't want to leave home.”

Earlier this month, the Biden administration announced the investment of more than $500 million in the Northern Triangle, by companies like Pepsi and Microsoft, in order to create opportunities and to keep people from wanting to migrate to the U.S.

But former acting commissioner of Customs and Border Protection Mark Morgan says Harris is “out of touch.”

“I think what all Americans want, regardless of their political affiliation, is to feel safe and secure in their neighborhoods and their communities, and that starts with border security," said Morgan to The National Desk’s Eugene Ramirez.

According to Morgan, Harris’ strategy to focus on “root causes” is historically a “failed strategy.”

“We can continue to work to improve their economic prosperity, their safety and security, while simultaneously securing our borders right now against the catastrophic numbers that we’re experiencing under this administration,” said Morgan.

Rep. Henry Cuellar, D-Texas, has expressed his disappointment in Harris’ handling of immigration.

“She was tasked with that job; it doesn’t look like she’s very interested in this, so we are going to move on to other folks that work on this issue,” said Cuellar to the New York Times.

“Part of any vice president's job is to actually work with members of Congress to further the strategy of the current administration, as well as secure the safety of this nation,” said Morgan. “She's not only not doing that, but she's not doing that with a member of her own party and a member on Capitol Hill that probably knows more about border security and illegal immigration than any member of Congress. I think it shows where her head is.”
