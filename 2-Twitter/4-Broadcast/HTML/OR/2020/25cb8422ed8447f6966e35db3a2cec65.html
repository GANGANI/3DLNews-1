Talent Spotlight

Marc Acton

San Jose

Marc was born in the Bay Area when “My Sharona” was Number 1 on the Billboard Charts. He began his radio journey when “Fergalicious” was Number 1 and joined Mix 106.5 when Adele’s “Rolling In the Deep” topped the charts. His favorite thing about radio is getting to connect with listeners. Community building and being of service is what Marc sees as the most important aspect of being on Mix 106.5. Marc was the first radio personality in the Bay Area to become an official fundraiser for the Susan G. Komen Breast Cancer Foundation. He is also the emcee for the Bay Area Brain Tumor Walk and the Out of the Darkness Suicide Prevention Walk.

Outside of radio Marc is living the “Dad Life” dream. Walking the kids to school and driving them to soccer, gymnastics, swimming, and baseball. He’s an animal lover and has a great dane named Mama, a cat named Whiskey, two bunnies (Cuddles and Milkshake), and a gecko named Gex. He loves playing and watching soccer, Go Quakes and Barcelona! One of his favorite things to do is see live music with his wife. He also enjoys making delicious cocktails and BBQing in the backyard. When he’s not at home in the Bay Area you’ll find him in Lake Tahoe – snowboarding in the winter and hanging at the beach in the summer.

Zac Grantham

North Dallas

Zac was born just a few miles away from the Katy Country studios and has been fortunate enough to spend his entire 15 year radio career in Texoma. Zac BARELY graduated from Callisburg, Texas High School, so Texoma is truly home.

On the Katy Country AM Show, you will routinely hear the most random, wild, and craziest things to get your day started. Zac has a beautiful wife and two handsome boys. He loves to serve and play drums at church, has an addiction to chips & salsa, cheers for all the Dallas professional sports teams and enjoys going out to Lake Texoma.

He is also President of The Texoma Back The Badge Foundation which has raised almost \$400,000 in 5 years to support local law enforcement families in Southern Oklahoma and North Texas. Zac is also heavily involved with the National Organization for Disorders of the Corpus Callosum and Special Olympics Texas.

Faith Daniels

Dayton

An alumnus of Tennessee State University, Faith majored in Communications and Marketing and minored in Speech and Dramatic Theatre. Although Faith began her radio career at just 14 years old, after college Faith furthered her knowledge while working all over the country. With retirement on the horizon, in 2011 Faith asked God to enlarge her territory and joined Dayton’s R&B Leader 92.1 WROU, Dayton, Ohio as Program Director/Music Director/ Community Affairs Liason and Afternoon Drive Personality 3-7pm. She also has appeared on Dayton’s Fox 45 as an entertainment consultant for Fox’s hit shows “American Idol,” “Empire!” and “STAR!” Faith is also a Professional Voiceover Artist, Concert Hostess/Mistress of Ceremonies, Event Producer and Licensed Minister. Faith’s accomplishments also include hosting The Cincinnati Music Festival for the last 20 years, being nominated for a “Gracie Award”, “Marconi Award” and a TEDX Dayton speaker. Mrs. Daniels was also the only civilian to receive a commendation from the State Of Ohio for her work in the recovery efforts in the city of Trotwood following the Memorial Day tornados. Organizing a donation and distribution operation for victims while living in The Trotwood Firehouse #72 for Three days while broadcasting live. Faith’s passion is motivating others and helping communities through her “Go, Live and Give” initiative. Through this program, “The Annual Holiday Help Out Food Drive” secures enough food to provide 110 families holiday meals AND fills the food pantry of The Good Neighbor House as well as 3 selected churches in the greater Dayton area each year. Faith and her husband Kent have been married 23 years and have 3 beautiful children, Trinity 17, Matthew 19, and Brandon 31.
