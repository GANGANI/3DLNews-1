Communities for Healthy Forests names Cribbins Executive Director

by Gold Meadows and KCBY.com Staff

Fri, February 24th 2023 at 2:50 PM
Updated Fri, February 24th 2023 at 3:35 PM
3
VIEW ALL PHOTOS
Former Coos County Commissioner Melissa Cribbins has been named Executive Director of Communities for Healthy Forests, the organization announced on Feb. 16, 2023. (photo via Communities for Healthy Forests/BP Media)

COOS BAY, Ore. — In recent months, following the end of her term as Coos County commissioner, Melissa Cribbins has continued to take up causes she supported during her time in office.

One of them is protecting state forests.

Last week, Cribbins was named the new executive director of Communities for Healthy Forests.

CHF is a non-profit based in Roseburg that brings awareness to government officials about the impacts of wildfires.

This comes after a decade of work in forest management, including her role as a member of the Elliott State Forest Advisory Committee.

"People across the spectrum that are concerned about the impacts these wildfires have on their communities. And they do public education, especially for youth, and education for lawmakers on the impacts of the wildfires," said Cribbins. "I'm really the first generation that didn't make a living in the woods, of my family. So I have strong feelings about how we manage our forests."

Cribbins will replace former Douglas County Commissioner Doug Robertson as executive director.

Last month, Cribbins was also named legal counsel for the Coos County Airport District.
