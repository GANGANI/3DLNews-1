95-year-old retired teacher returns to classroom in Grand Blanc

GRAND BLANC, Mich. (WJRT) - A 95-year-old Grand Blanc woman is celebrating her birthday a little differently this year by going back to work.

Lorene Wilson was a teacher for 30 years in Mid-Michigan. She worked at schools in Flint, Fenton and Grand Blanc before retiring in 1989. Tuesday, she was back in the classroom and said it was like she never left.

"It's just natural," said Wilson. "It's just wonderful to be back and it's quite an experience."

Wilson subbed in a fifth grade classroom in Grand Blanc. She spoke with Perry Innovation Center students about what school was like when she was teaching and they shared with her what it's like now.

"It's a lot different," said Wilson. "Twenty-five students. My first year in Grand Blanc in 1966 I had 43 first graders. So this would be a pleasure to work with just 25."

Returning to the classroom was a bucket list item for Wilson. Her daughter, Jan, accompanied her Tuesday and helped make it all possible.

"Earlier this year we went to another classroom and she said you know I'd really like to come and teach," said Jan Wilson. "She said, 'I think I should do that for my birthday' and so I said, 'OK, we'll make it happen.' I called Dr. Alward's office and they said that would be great."

When asked about her favorite part of today, Lorene Wilson couldn't pick just one.

"I just can't pinpoint one thing because I'm enjoying every minute of it," said Wilson. "They're just, the children are all so great and so attentive and it's just been a pleasure."

After going through the process to become a substitute teacher, Wilson said Tuesday was not just a one-and-done experience. She is already planning on returning to sub again soon.
