At least 25 people dead in Buffalo snowstorm, 8-12" of snow expected through Tuesday

by CNY Central

Mon, December 26th 2022 at 10:23 AM
Updated Tue, December 27th 2022 at 6:16 AM
At least 25 people are confirmed dead in snow-related incidents in Buffalo Christmas storm (Courtesy: WUTV)

    TOPICS:

    BuffaloDisaster AccidentMark PoloncarzNiagara County,New York,United StatesPennsylvania,United StatesWatch BelowNATIONAL GRID PLCBuffalo, New York

Buffalo, NY — Snow is still falling in Buffalo as emergency responders continue to react to the Christmas weekend deadly blizzard.

In a press conference Monday morning, Erie County Executive Mark Poloncarz reported 25 storm-related fatalities confirmed by the Medical Examiner's office. He issued warnings against shoveling snow and traveling outside, as a travel ban is still in effect for many Buffalo communities.

Below is the latest travel ban map as of 10 AM Monday:

The Thruway also remains closed from the Henrietta exit all the way to the Pennsylvania border.

WATCH BELOW: The Governor delivers a press briefing on storm cleanup efforts.


Poloncarz reports that the city of Buffalo remains impassable. He writes, "Even if some of the mains have a single lane open, these are still only for emergency responders." A state of emergency remains in place for all of Erie County. Law enforcement are issuing tickets in travel ban areas, with Poloncarz saying vehicles are getting stuck and blocking emergency responders and ambulances.

Power outages are also still a problem. There are 12,000 National Grid customers without power. Nearly all NYSEG customers have had power restored as of 10 AM.

Poloncarz is warning people to check their furnace vents. At least one person died from carbon monoxide in Niagara County because of a blocked vent.



He asked people to check on their neighbors if it is safe to do so, saying the Erie County Sheriff's Office is sending deputies for medical runs for those stuck in homes.

"We are actively coordinating food efforts for shelters, emergency responders who have been stuck at stations/shops, and those who may be running out of food who have been stuck inside," Poloncarz wrote. He said a key reason to stay off roads is to make sure food and fuel can reach hospitals and emergency crews.


"I know this has been an incredibly taxing, difficult, scary storm and we need to be able to continue life-saving operations and road clearings." Poloncarz also tweeted.
