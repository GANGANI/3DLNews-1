3 WNY towns lift travel advisories after lake-effect snow

Justin McMullen
6 months ago

BUFFALO, N.Y. (WIVB) — Three municipalities in Western New York asked drivers to stay off the roads Monday night.

Police in Orchard Park, the Town of Evans and the Village of Angola issued travel advisories as Buffalo’s Southtowns were hit with rapidly accumulating lake-effect snow. In Orchard Park, accumulations of more than a foot of snow were reported by the National Weather Service.

As of 6:30 a.m. Tuesday, the Town of Evans and the Village of Angola lifted their travel advisories.

The travel advisory in Orchard Park was lifted at approximately 9:20 a.m. Tuesday.

Drivers are still urged to use caution due to slippery road conditions.

Facebook posts by the police departments in both Orchard Park and Evans reference “multiple accidents” and vehicles off the road Monday night.

The NWS issued a Lake-Effect Snow Warning for Chautauqua, Wyoming, Cattaraugus, and southern Erie County. This alert remains in effect until 7 a.m. Wednesday.
