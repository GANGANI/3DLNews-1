Stefanik vs. Cobb: Congressional candidates weigh in on New York's budget crisis

by Anne McCloy

Tue, October 6th 2020 at 7:37 PM
Updated Tue, November 3rd 2020 at 11:37 PM
Stefanik vs. Cobb: Congressional candidates weigh in on New York’s budget crisis

It's a heated race in New York's 21st Congressional district!

Incumbent Congresswoman Elise Stefanik R- Schuylerville is in a rematch with challenger, former St. Lawrence County Legislator and Democrat Tedra Cobb.

In this segment, CBS 6’s Anne McCloy asked the candidates about New York’s fiscal crisis. Right now NYS has a 14.5 billion dollar deficit, about six billion of which existed before COVID-19 hit the state.

Tedra Cobb leads off the discussion, accusing her opponent of not doing enough to help New York State in this crisis.

Cobb: She sits in a special position and should be working day and night to make sure New York gets the funding we need, she should be working with the governor and making sure we are getting the funding that so that we need to get through this crisis. I’m going to fight for federal funding I’m going to make sure we have the funding to keep our hospitals open.

Anne: Do you think the governor’s responsible for the mismanagement of our budget and allowing our state for getting into that hole before the pandemic? Because now we are in a much worse situation.

Cobb: We can look at the whole federal government and say, why did we give a tax break to the richest among us, but not help working families?

Anne: But is the answer always a bailout? At some point shouldn’t the state be responsible for managing its budget?

Cobb: This president downplayed a virus and now the economy is in shambles, people cannot go to work. Schools are struggling to stay open. That is where we are right now.

Incumbent Stefanik responded.

Stefanik: First of all, this state has received 9 billion dollars in federal funds to date. Governor Cuomo never wants to talk about, but those are the facts. That on top of all of the assistance with the Javits Center, U.S. Navy Comfort and direct aid to hospitals. This is not an excuse for the governor to clean up his fiscal mismanagement.

Anne: What’s your response to that notion that the decision not to bail New York out with have this huge impact?

Stefanik: My response is if you look at the facts New York has gotten more aid than any other state. I don’t think we should be bailing out the fiscal challenges pre-COVID. I think it needs to be very targeted federal funding, but we don’t need to be sending direct aid to take care of Governor Cuomo's mismanagement of the state.

Yesterday, we addressed the issues of New York's bail reform policy, the state of policing, and COVID-19 response.
