Healthcare workers receive COVID-19 vaccine at Shasta Regional Medical Center

by Mike Mangas, Miranda Angel

Fri, December 18th 2020 at 8:29 PM
Healthcare workers lined up to be vaccinated on Thursday at Shasta Regional Medical Center in Redding.

    TOPICS:

    FRONTLINE LTD.Health Medical PharmaPFIZER INC.PfizerAmit BawaRyan WellerCasey FatchCOVID-19 Vaccine

REDDING, Calif. — It was a production line of COVID-19 vaccinations at Shasta Regional Medical Center in Redding on Thursday.

Frontline healthcare workers lined up in the hallway outside of the room where several nurses were administering the vaccines practically non-stop.

CEO Casey Fatch was among those who rolled up their sleeves, believing the Pfizer vaccine is the light at the end of the tunnel.

Medical Director and Chief of Medicine, Dr. Amit Bawa, was one of the first to get vaccinated. He says that even though it's being produced in record time, he is completely confident in the vaccine.

"This is a very important thing. Nothing has been rushed in this. There have been no cutting corners about this vaccine," commented Dr. Bawa. "This technology has been there for more than 10 years. With COVID-19 it accelerated everything, catalyzed everything. With the government chipping in, with a lot of funding it took the risk away from all these companies. And here we go, it's nothing less than a medical miracle that in less than nine months we were able to get a vaccine."

Disaster Prevention Coordinator, Ryan Weller, is thankful something is being done proactively, rather than just treating the disease. He was also vaccinated today.

“It went smoothly, it felt just like any other vaccination. So far, I think we’ve done about 100 and everyone’s doing fine. There’s smiles on everyone’s faces underneath their masks, so that’s been going well," Weller said.

Shasta Regional Medical Center anticipated 350 workers would be vaccinated by the end of the day.

Dr. Bawa says there's minimal risk, using a seatbelt as an analogy.

“There is a small chance if you have a seatbelt in a car accident it can kill you. But, the majority of the time the seatbelt is there to protect you and save your life," he explained.

Dr. Bawa says there are three very rare side effects: an allergic reaction, an immune system overload response, and Bell's Palsy, which is a usually temporary paralysis of the face. Bell's Palsy is reversible most of the time but is a very rare side effect.
