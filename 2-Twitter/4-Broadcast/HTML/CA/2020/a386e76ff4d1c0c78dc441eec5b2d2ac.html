Heavy, wet snow impacting travel on Christmas

by Darren Leeds

Wed, December 23rd 2020 at 5:26 PM
Updated Wed, December 23rd 2020 at 8:51 AM
KRCR Wx 12-22-20

    TOPICS:

    weatherBlizzardsGlobal Storm ActivityIce stormsMeteorological PhenomenaMeteorologyNatureSnow

It will remain dry through the next few days, but we're tracking rain and snow returning to the Northstate on Christmas Day.

It will be dry and mild at least through Thursday (Christmas Eve) with highs in the upper 50's to around 60 degrees.

Our next Weather Maker looks to arrive Christmas Day, but the wet weather will hold off until the second half of the day.

Once the rain does arrive, snow levels will be as low as 4,000 feet and will have an impact on mountain travel.

A Winter Storm Watch has been issued from Friday morning through Saturday morning due to the heavy, wet snow this next storm system is looking to bring in.

Saturday also looks wet with snow levels around 4,000 feet. Once all is said and done Friday and Saturday, the valley could pick up between .50 and 1.00 inch of rain.

Although we could see a break from the wet weather on Sunday, more rain is in the forecast to start next week with snow levels between 3,000 and 4,000 feet.
