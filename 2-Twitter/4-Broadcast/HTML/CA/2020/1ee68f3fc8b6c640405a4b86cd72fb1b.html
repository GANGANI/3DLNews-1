Healthcare workers say COVID vaccine is next step in a long process

by Alexandria Williams

Tue, December 22nd 2020 at 6:22 PM
Updated Tue, December 22nd 2020 at 4:22 AM
Dignity Health Siskiyou County. (KRCR)

    TOPICS:

    California,United StatesHealth Medical PharmaPFIZER INC.PfizerSiskiyou County,California,United Statesdignity healthsocial issuesJeanne Yalon-Owens

SISKIYOU COUNTY, Calif. — The COVID-19 vaccine is being distributed across the State of California.

Siskiyou County Public Health said the county will be receiving its first shipment of the vaccine this week.

KRCR spoke to Jeanne Yalon-Owens, a nurse practitioner and the chief medical officer for Shasta Cascade Health Center.

Owens said she and her staff have been waiting what feels like a tremendous amount of time for the vaccine, but they realize it's just the next step in a long process.

Public Health said Siskiyou County will be receiving 385 doses of the Pfizer vaccine.

The vaccine will be housed in both Dignity Health and Fairchild Medical Center.

According to Siskiyou County Public Health, the vaccine will first be given to frontline healthcare workers who are most likely to be exposed to COVID-19.

Owns said they know there is still a long road ahead, but are doing what they can to be there for their patients and give them hope.

"I think the vaccine is definitely going to interrupt the transmission of the virus, while we are slowing down any transmission through all of our protective measures of social distancing and wearing masks and hand washing and all that," she said. "This is just a part of the package, but it's the first step that we've had towards hope instead of having this endless date of isolation."

Public Health said the county could be receiving the first doses of the vaccine as early as Monday, December 21.

There is still no timeline for when the vaccine will be distributed to the general public.
