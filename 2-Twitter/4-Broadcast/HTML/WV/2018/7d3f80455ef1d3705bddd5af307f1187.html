Ohio bill would extend time new drivers must use learner's permits

By WSAZ News Staff
Published: Oct. 22, 2018 at 11:14 AM EDT
UPDATE 10/22/18 @ 6:55 p.m.

Getting behind the wheel for the first time can be a scary thing for both teens and their parents, but Ohio House Bill 293 is hoping to change negative teen driving statistics.

The bill aims to increase the length of time that young drivers will hold their temporary instruction permits from six months to a year.

If passed, it could also change the curfew from beginning at 12 a.m. and ending at 6 a.m. to instead starting at 10 p.m. and ending at 6 a.m. for those in their first six months of holding a probationary driver's license.

Just a few days ago, Dorlese Green’s daughter, who is 15 and a half years old, brought up learning how to drive.

"Another driver on the road that I’m responsible for,” she said.

As a parent, she worries about letting her kids get behind the wheel.

"Who else is on the road and are they safe drivers? Can you wrap this car in bubble wrap and wear a crash helmet,” said Green.

With two teen drivers on the roads soon, she believes Ohio House Bill 293 would be a step in the right direction. "Make sure they not only go out, but come back."

However, Cathy Stapleton is concerned that the changes could make family schedules more difficult.

"Especially when they're doing opposite things in opposite directions and you can't be in both places at one time,” she said.

She said it could even affect teens' shifts at work.

"But a lot of times, especially on the weekends, they have to work till midnight,” said Stapleton.

Regardless of point of view, a lot of parents are invested in the issue because when it comes to safety.

"From a parental point of view, you can't have enough,” said Green.

The bill must pass the state House and Senate by the end of the year to become law.

ORIGINAL STORY 10/22/18

A bill in the Ohio legislature aims to make the roads more safe by extending the amount of time new drivers have to wait before getting their licenses.

House Bill 293

would increase the amount of time a driver must use a learner's permit (or probationary driver's license) from six to 12 months.

It would also establish an earlier curfew for new drivers under the age of 18. Currently, new drivers in Ohio are not allowed to be on the road between midnight and 6 a.m. That includes highways, public property, and even private property. HB 293 would change the curfew to 10 p.m. instead of midnight.

However, a teenager is allowed to drive after curfew if a parent, guardian, or other person with a valid driver's license is in the passenger seat.

State Rep. Gary Scherer (R-Circleville) and Rep. Michael Sheehy (D-Oregon) jointly-introduced the legislation.
