'Travel strongly discouraged’ as ice storm hits parts of Western, Central Massachusetts

  • Updated: Dec. 31, 2019, 7:59 a.m.|
  • Published: Dec. 30, 2019, 6:59 a.m.
By
  • Jackson Cote | jcote@masslive.com

Forecasters are warning against travel as an ice storm hits parts of Western and Central Massachusetts on Monday, impacting the morning and afternoon commutes.

An ice storm warning will remain in effect until Tuesday morning in Northern Worcester, Northern Middlesex Counties and much of Western Massachusetts, including western Franklin, western Hampshire and western Hampden Counties as well as all of Berkshire County, the National Weather Service said.

The strong low-pressure storm will bring freezing rain and ice to the state, mainly impacting the Berkshires and Worcester Hills. A chilly rain is expected in the rest of Massachusetts.

A winter weather advisory for Eastern Massachusetts has been canceled as temperatures there will be higher and are not likely to result in much freezing rain.

But for Western Massachusetts and Northern Worcester County, slick roads are expected to develop ahead of the morning commute and will linger into the afternoon. Power outages and tree damage are likely due to the ice.

“Travel is strongly discouraged," the National Weather Service said. "If you must travel, keep an extra flashlight, food and water in your vehicle in case of an emergency.”

Up to a tenth of an inch of ice is expected to accumulate in communities across Southern Berkshire, Hampden, Hampshire and central Worcester Counties, including Great Barrington, Amherst, Greenfield and Worcester.

Northern Worcester and northern Berkshire Counties may receive a quarter of an inch to a half of an inch of ice, while central Berkshire County may get a tenth to a quarter inch of ice.

Snow accumulations in the affected areas may reach one inch, and winds gusts will reach as high as 35 miles per hour in Berkshire County.

Temperatures were hovering around the low 30s Monday morning. Boston, Worcester and Springfield will see temperatures stay at around 35 degrees for much of the day then rise to the low 40s overnight Tuesday.

A few intermittent snow showers are expected Tuesday night in the Pioneer Valley, but drier weather is likely for the rest of the week.
