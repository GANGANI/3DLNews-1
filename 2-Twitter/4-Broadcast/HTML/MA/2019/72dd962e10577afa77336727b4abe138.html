Ice storm warning for Western, Central Massachusetts to remain until Tuesday morning; More downed trees, power lines expected

  • Published: Dec. 30, 2019, 2:51 p.m.
By
  • Jackson Cote | jcote@masslive.com

A winter storm that caused thousands of power outages and brought more than a quarter of an inch of ice to Western and Central Massachusetts will continue through Tuesday.

An ice storm warning will remain in effect until 7 a.m. Tuesday for northern Worcester and northern Middlesex Counties as well as northern and southern Berkshire, western Franklin, western Hampden and western Hampshire Counties.

Travel is strongly discouraged as slippery roads, downed trees and fallen power lines are expected, according to the National Weather Service.

Significant icing is expected in the Berkshires and Worcester Hills, with up to a tenth of an inch of additional ice accumulating across higher elevations. A chilly rain will remain in the rest of the state, and intermittent snow showers will hit the Pioneer Valley.

“The accumulating ice coupled with 20 to 30 mph wind gusts will continue to cause tree damage and down power lines,” the National Weather Service said.

Around an inch of snow was reported in Lunenburg in Worcester County on Monday, and two inches dropped in Rowe in Franklin County.

As much as half an inch of ice accumulated in Hampden and Hampshire Counties. Franklin and Worcester Counties saw less ice accumulations, with as much as 0.40 inches reported in Rowe and 0.25 inches in Holden.

Nearly 2,500 customers were without power around 11:45 a.m., according to the Massachusetts Emergency Management Agency. More than 2,000 still had no electricity around 2:30 p.m.

Several trees and electrical wires were downed across Hampden, Hampshire, Huntington, Middlesex and Worcester counties.

Warmer weather is expected Friday and Saturday, with “above-normal temperatures” continuing through the upcoming weekend, the National Weather Service said.
