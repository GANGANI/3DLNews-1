Boston Celtics come back from late 17-point deficit, Jayson Tatum scores 27 in 116-111 win over Pacers

  • Updated: Dec. 29, 2020, 10:37 p.m.|
  • Published: Dec. 29, 2020, 9:29 p.m.
By
  • Chris Cotillo | ccotillo@MassLive.com

A furious fourth quarter comeback saved the Celtics after another sleepy third quarter as Boston beat the Pacers, 116-111, to salvage a split of a two-game series in Indianapolis on Tuesday night.

The Pacers outscored the Celtics, 37-25, in the third and even led by 17 points late in the quarter before Boston came back with a burst at the beginning of the fourth. Boston improved to 2-2 on the season.

Jayson Tatum led the Celtics with 27 points, including 14 in the fourth quarter. Three other Celtics -- Jaylen Brown (20), Marcus Smart (17), Tristan Thompson (14) and Payton Pritchard (10) -- were in double figures, with Thompson and Tatum both recording double-doubles.

Boston came out with energy on the defensive end and took an early lead behind eight points and seven rebounds from Thompson in the first quarter. After leading, 58-57, at halftime, the C’s were outscored by double digits in the third quarter for the third straight game and entered the fourth trailing by 11.

Led by Pritchard and Tatum, the Celtics went on an 11-0 run to tie the game before regaining the lead with 7:29 to go. The Pacers cut the deficit to two twice in the final 1:01 but Tatum sealed the win with two free throws with 14.8 seconds left.

All five Pacers starters finished in double figures, with Victor Oladipo leading the team with 24 points.

The Celtics will return home Wednesday for a matchup against the Grizzlies before heading back on the road for a four-game trip to Detroit, Tampa (where the Raptors are playing) and Miami. Tip-off against Memphis is scheduled for 7:30 p.m.

STEVENS’ TAKE:

Celtics coach Brad Stevens on the fourth-quarter comeback: “The Pacers were playing exceptionally hard heading into that quarter. That was as physical as a quarter in the third as I remember seeing in a long time. I thought our guys kept at it and we had a couple really good contributions from the bench, getting to some balls and some tips from behind. Rob (Williams) ran back in and got that rebound. There were several instances of really good effort. I don’t know what the lead was for them but it sure felt like it dissipated pretty quickly and then we were able to keep guarding. I think our bigs did a good job late being up on the screens on (Victor) Oladipo, helping us at the rim in a couple of cases.

“It’s a good team win. That’s a good basketball team. We’re not what we want to be but sometimes you’ve got to find a way to win.”
