Community engagement soars 298% in Champaign Police Department, impacting crime rates

by David Pierce

Sat, December 23rd 2023 at 8:13 PM
Community engagement soars 298% in Champaign Police Department, impacting crime rates (WICS)

CHAMPAIGN Ill. (WICS) — The Champaign Police Department reported it had a 298% uptick in community engagement this year with certain factors allowing officers to meet with residents.

It includes hosting open houses, assigning officers to certain districts, and being present at community meetings.

News Channel 20/Fox Illinois spoke with Public Information Officer, Joe Lamberson, and he said the events helped officers build relationships with residents.

They're engaged and engaging," Lamberson said. "The community gets the opportunity to have a conversation

Lamberson added that officers creating those connections have helped investigations.

We have seen an increase in witnesses coming forward, [and] we've seen an increase in people willing to tell us what was going on,

Lamberson said.

And this can be seen in shooting data with incidents dropping from 259 in 2021, and 129 in 2022 to 63 as of November 30th of this year.

CPD said it's currently down 18 officers.

But it says the 7 new officers hired this year have allowed officers to be more available.

And believe it or not, when you don't have every officer on shift holding a crime scene for an extended period of time, Lamberson said. "They have more time to connect and engage and work with the community in those regards

But improvements were not just made with violent crime.

Resident, Pattsi Petri, says she and other neighbors hosted meetings to address people speeding down Green Street and McKinley Avenue which has led to multiple crashes this summer.

It caused the city to place more stop signs at the intersection.

But Petri said it started with CPD attending their meetings and then putting down speed trackers

With the data and prove the concerns. or backup, I should say, the concerns of neighbors,

She said.

She also added working with police has led to Champaign's Public Works department working to reduce the speed limit in the neighborhood from 30 to 25 miles per hour.

CPD also shared how it will be hosting its community police academy next fall to give more insight into what officers do and hopefully gain more connections.
