Schools reporting closures after Tornado Watch in Central Va.

by Elizabeth Thomas

Fri, May 27th 2022 at 5:55 AM
Updated Fri, May 27th 2022 at 4:40 PM
46
VIEW ALL PHOTOS
Damage left behind in Bedford County, Virginia, after a possible tornado early Friday morning, May 17, 2022. County leaders said several residential neighborhoods in the Crockett Road area suffered catastrophic damage. (Credit:Kara Forbes via Chime In)

FRANKLIN COUNTY, Va. (WSET) — A morning full of tornado warnings has left behind damage in Central Virginia.

The latest tornado warning for our area expired at 8:15, though severe weather persists. We are continuing to track the severe thunderstorms as they move across Central Virginia.

RELATED: 1 injury reported after tornadic activity Friday morning in Bedford County

A Tornado Watch is in effect until 2 p.m. for the counties of Amherst, Appomattox Bedford, Campbell, Charlotte, City of Danville, City of Lynchburg, City of Martinsville, Franklin, Halifax, Henry, Mecklenburg, Nelson and Pittsylvania.

View the interactive radar here.

The weather Friday morning is impacting schools:

  • Henry County Public Schools will be closed on Friday due to the severe weather. Graduation will still take place at Magna Vista High School Friday evening as scheduled.
  • Martinsville Schools is operating on a one-hour delay.
  • Lynchburg City Schools says high school students, already in school, are safe. Buses were sheltered in place at the high school. Elementary and Middle Schools will operate under a one-hour delay.
  • Amherst County Public Schools says elementary schools are closed; secondary schools are letting out early at 11 a.m.; graduation practice moved to 1 p.m.

Several thousand power outages are reported in Central Virginia as a result of the storm (Current as of 10 a.m.):

  • Bedford - 1715
  • Campbell - 79
  • Franklin - 1063
  • Pittsylvania - 1591
  • Roanoke County - 804
  • Roanoke City - 457
  • Montgomery - 1019
  • Botetourt - 129

We have received reports of buildings destroyed in Bedford County as a result of Friday morning's storms. We have crews in the area.

A Tornado Watch has been issued for parts of the District of Columbia, Maryland, North Carolina, Virginia and West Virginia until 2 p.m. EDT.

The first tornado warning was issued at 4:25 a.m. Friday, for Martinsville, Collinsville and Stanleytown.
