Methane gas explosion at Knox County barn nearly misses delivery of 2,500 hogs

by Nik Todorovich

Tue, January 2nd 2024 at 3:47 PM
Updated Wed, January 3rd 2024 at 7:14 AM
3
VIEW ALL PHOTOS
Baring Fire Chief Merlin Oberholtzer told KTVO at the scene that there were two people inside the barn when it exploded, but they made it out safely. (Nik Todorovich/KTVO)

NEAR BARING, Mo. — A methane gas explosion damaged a Knox County, Mo., hog barn Tuesday afternoon.

Firefighters from the Baring and Hurdland volunteer fire departments were called to 49094 Highway J, five miles west of Baring, around 1:30 p.m.

Baring Fire Chief Merlin Oberholtzer told KTVO at the scene that there were two people inside the barn when the explosion happened, but they made it out safely.

There were no animals inside the barn at the time.

Tractor-trailers containing about 2,500 hogs were about to drop them off, but due to the explosion, they were unable to.

When the explosion happened, there were no visible flames, just an abundance of smoke.

Oberholtzer also told KTVO that he believed it was a methane gas explosion because methane rises, and most of the damage was on the ceiling of the barn.

He described the damage as moderate and said the feed and water systems received the most.

Oberholtzer also said he doesn't suspect any foul play.
