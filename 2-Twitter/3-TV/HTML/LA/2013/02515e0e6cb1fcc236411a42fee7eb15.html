Photo shoot for the masses, Unscene holds monthly art happening

  • Craig Sims
  • Dec 14, 2013
  • Dec 14, 2013
  • Comments
  • Facebook
  • Twitter
  • WhatsApp
  • SMS
  • Email
  • Facebook
  • Twitter
  • WhatsApp
  • SMS
  • Email

  • Save

 

Unscene, the monthly art happening that's quickly becoming a Shreveport tradition, held a photo shoot for the masses Saturday.

Unscene hosted the Inside Out Project. It's a world famous idea that encourages people to take a picture that shows their true selves. Those pictures are then plastered for all to see in their community. Today's pictures were plastered around Shreveport Common. Dozens lined up to have their picture taken in a mobile photo booth. The event also featured musical performances and more. Unscene organizers say such happenings bring awareness to the Shreveport Common, which they hope to revitalize.

"It's a historical area," Casey Jones said. "I mean, this is where people like Cap Calloway, Louis Armstrong and Billy Holiday used to play. It's just as important as the Hay Ride over at the Municipal. We really want to bring this culture back."

If you'd like to know what Unscene's next art happening will be, follow the link to their web site: http://unsceneshreveport.com/.
