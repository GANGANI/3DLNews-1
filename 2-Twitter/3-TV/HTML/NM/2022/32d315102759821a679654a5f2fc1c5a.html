Man killed by Iranian security for celebrating US Soccer's win, report says

by ZACHARY ROGERS | The National Desk

Wed, November 30th 2022 at 12:09 PM
4

BANDAR ANZALI, Iran (TND) — A man was reportedly killed by Iranian security forces while celebrating the United States Men's National Soccer Team's (USMNT) win over Iran in the 2022 World Cup.

Mehran Samak, 27, was shot in the head after he joined anti-government protesters in publicly celebrating Iran's elimination from the World Cup, according to sources cited by the BBC.

Samak was reportedly killed after honking his horn in celebration of Iran's defeat at the World Cup, but not much detail is given on the entire altercation between him and Iranian security forces.

A Persian news channel journalist who is based in London, Maryam Moqaddam, also reported on Samak's death, asking why the man was killed and answering her own question in a Twitter thread.

Iranian security forces shot and killed 27-year-old #MehranSammak in the northern city of Anzali just because he was honking his car horn following the US-Iran football match," Moqaddam said in her tweet.

The Iranian national football team was defeated on Tuesday by USMNT in a match that was politically charged due to ongoing protests in Iran and a social media controversy that put America's team in disfavor with Iran's Islamic government.

Protests in Iran have been escalating ever since the death of 22-year-old Mahsa Amini, who was arrested for allegedly violating the country's strict dress code for women which involves wearing a headdress called a "hijab." Amini died in police custody.

The United States Soccer Federation (USSF) upset Iranian authorities when it changed Iran's flag on several of its social media platforms. The temporary change was in an effort to show solidarity with protestors in the country, CNN reported, with the USSF saying it altered Iran's flag to show "support for the women in Iran fighting for basic human rights."

Iran even called for the U.S. to be removed from the World Cup following the social media controversy.

The U.S.'s Christian Pulisic scored the only goal of the game, giving USMNT a 1-0 win. Pulisic was injured in the process of scoring that goal, but expects to play in the next game against The Netherlands on Saturday.

Iran was eliminated from the World Cup following the loss.
