Students walk at U of M - Flint commencement ceremony

by MID-MICHIGAN NOW Newsroom

FLINT, Mich. - Over 500 local students put on their caps and gowns today as they graduated from The University of Michigan - Flint.


A commencement ceremony was held today at the Dort Federal Credit Union Center where 555 total degrees were handed out to graduates.


The University released these highlight "news-maker" facts about some of the graduates:

12 Maize and Blue Distinguished Scholar Award recipients, the highest academic award bestowed upon the graduates of the University of Michigan-Flint

Nicholas Wills will be the first student to graduate with a degree in Green Chemistry from UM-Flint. UM-Flint introduced the first Bachelor of Science in Green Chemistry in the fall of 2018. As the only undergraduate degree of its kind in the United States, the program provides the same preparation that traditional chemistry programs offer with additional areas of emphasis in fields such as life-cycle engineering, toxicology, and public policy.

UM-Flint’s Bachelor of Science in Nursing for Veterans is dedicated to building healthy communities by strengthening the healthcare workforce through recruitment, successful nursing program matriculation, licensure, and employment of U.S. veterans. Since the program’s inception in 2014, UM-Flint has graduated 20 such veterans. This ceremony will see an additional 8 veterans graduate with such a degree.

UM-Flint is home to many first-generation college students, such as Esthefanie Paredes. Paredes says “I graduated from Genesee Early College, the 5-year high school located on UM-Flint's campus, in 2017. I decided to continue my education at UM-Flint because it became my second home. I love the atmosphere on campus and throughout the downtown area.”
