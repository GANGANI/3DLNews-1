DON'T DRINK & DRIVE: Bars around the area are anticipating crowds for "Thanksgiving Eve"

by Remi Murrey and Caleb Holloway

Wed, November 24th 2021 at 8:53 AM
Updated Wed, November 24th 2021 at 11:17 AM

GENESEE COUNTY, Mich. - This will be a big night for bars and nightclubs.

“Thanksgiving eve” is typically the busiest bar night each year, except last year due to the pandemic.

But this has all changed, and now local owners are getting ready for big crowds.

RELATED LINK: 'One of the busiest days of the year': Michiganders prepare to travel for Thanksgiving

The owners at Kicker's Sports Bar and Grill in Grand Blanc have already been buying extra food, beer and liquor weeks in advance to get ready for the big night, and to avoid shipping delays.

Like most, they too are feeling the worker shortage; but, they assure they are ready for tonight, and are as fully staffed as they can be.

“I'm happy that everybody is just out and about and getting together, and another thing that I'm thankful for is that the customers are patient because they know we're short staffed. They get it. They understand," said Owner Mitra Pagonis.

Pagonis is also feeling extremely thankful because last year, things looked much different.

“If I remember correctly, Mid-November is when the state of Michigan got their second shut-down so all we did was take-out,” said Pagonis.

It costed their restaurant thousands of dollars, which affected her business and workers’ livelihoods.

But this year, Kicker's Sports Bar and Grill is fully open, and it's something Pagonis can't stop smiling about, as she counts her blessings.

“Because of last year, I know I'm a little bit more thankful for everything that I have. I hope everyone else is as well. I mean we've been blessed with being here for 15 years with our friends and family, our staff, our customers, I'm so thankful. I can't even start to say how thankful I am. I wouldn't know where to start,” said Pagonis.

Michigan State Police urge Michiganders not to drink and drive and to be safe on the roads.

AAA Tow to Go is available Wednesday through Monday throughout the state of Michigan. When called, a tow truck transports the would-be impaired driver and their vehicle to a safe place within a 10 mile radius. The service is free, yet should be treated as a backup plan.

“AAA is proud to bring back this life-saving program this Thanksgiving,” said Adrienne Woodland, spokeswoman, AAA-The Auto Club Group. “Now as millions of Americans get behind the wheel this weekend, none of them will have an excuse for driving impaired. However, we still strongly encourage people to identify a safe ride home and treat Tow to Go as their last resort.”

Call (855) 2-TOW-2-GO or (855) 286-9246.

Michigan Liquor Control Commission Consumer Tips for a Safe Night Out:

  • Plan a safe way home before you leave the house. Once you start drinking you likely won’t make good choices.
  • Designate a sober driver plan to use public transportation or ride sharing services to get home safely.
  • If you see a drunk driver on the road, call 911 to reach law enforcement.
  • Have a friend who is about to drink and drive? Take the keys away and make arrangements to get your friend home safely.

“It’s never OK to drink and drive as it’s not only illegal, it’s deadly, and no one should ever take that risk,” said MLCC Chair Pat Gagliardi. “Even if you’ve only had one drink, make it your priority to ensure the safety of yourself and others. Give the car keys to a sober friend, or use public transportation or a rideshare service to avoid impaired driving and get home safely.”
