Stabilization starting this month on the Sanford dam

by Courtney Bennett

Sat, October 23rd 2021 at 9:59 PM
4

Stabilization starting this month on the Sanford dam (Credit: Courtney Bennett)

SANFORD, Mich. - Work to stabilize the Sanford dam will begin this month according to the Four Lakes Task Force.

In their latest meeting the group said work will continue through June of 2022, this is to get the Tittabawassee River back to its natural path.

In Sanford, it has been 17-months since the dams failed in the communities in May of 2020.

The community is coming back but it has taken some time and money to get to this point.

Flood waters destroyed businesses and homes after two dam failed.

The Four Lakes Task Force says it will cost approximately $250 million over the course of a few years to do repairs.

They say the money will come from special assessments from homeowners, grants and the federal government.

Four Lakes Task Force still says they plan to have the water back in Sanford Lake by 2025.

But there is still progress made in the community.

“I think the project we are all looking forward to is the village park to return the village park to get the ballfields up and running to see children on the playground to have the horseshoe pits to have basketball, we sponsored a lot of five-k runs that started from that park our homecoming parade started from that park, so we are looking forward to having that park back up and running," said Dolores Porte who is the President of the Village of Sanford.

Their goal, to have the park back by spring and now replacing the downtown lights is currently being done and will continue into the spring.

“We have enough to make it safe down there but we want to put the whole effect of street lights back in place," said Porte.

This year, some businesses were rebuilt and returned to the downtown area.

Porte says she thinks this made them stronger.

“We love Sanford more and more everyday in fact I think we are transforming it into a closer knit community. With comradery and even the other communities have become closer to us because they keep coming to help us," said Porte.

The four lakes task force has said repairing all 4 dams on this system in Midland and Gladwin counties, could cost up to 400-million dollars, when they’re complete.

As of March, the four lakes task force said it had 26 and a half million dollars secured through donations and grants.

Another 20-30 million available through matching grants.

Funds are being raised through special assessments on homes, that were on the Wixom and Sanford lakes.

Due to the dam failures, those lakes are now empty.
