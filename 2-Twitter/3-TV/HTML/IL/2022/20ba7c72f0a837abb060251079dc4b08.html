Springfield man claims he was under direction of Trump to participate in Jan. 6 riot

by WICS/WLDS

Wed, December 21st 2022 at 3:56 PM
Updated Thu, December 22nd 2022 at 10:06 AM
This mugshot of Thomas B. Adams Jr. is from a Sangamon County arrest in 2016. (Sangamon County Sheriff's Office)

SPRINGFIELD, Ill. (WICS) — A Springfield man says he stormed the U.S. Capitol on January 6th under direct orders from former President Donald Trump, according to our media partners at WLDS.

Thomas Adams Jr., 39, was arrested in April of 2021.

RELATED: What we know about Springfield man accused of storming Capitol

Adams was accused of knowingly entering or remaining on restricted buildings on grounds without lawful authority, violent entry and disorderly conduct on Capitol grounds, and obstruction of an official proceeding.

According to a document filed on Monday by Federal Public Defender A.J. Kramer, Adams believed he was directed and authorized to engage in the conduct set forth in the indictment by Donald J. Trump and his agents and representatives.

RELATED: Springfield man accused of storming Capitol during Jan. 6 riot

Adams’ counsel also filed motions to dismiss charges of tampering with a witness, disorderly and disruptive conduct in a restricted building, and violent entry and disorderly conduct on the Capitol grounds.

Adams is next due in the Washington D.C. Federal Court on March 3rd.
