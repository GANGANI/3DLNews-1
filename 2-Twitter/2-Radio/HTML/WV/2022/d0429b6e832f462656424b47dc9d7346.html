“THE LIVES OF BRIAN”: If fate had a plan for Brian Johnson, fate didn’t stand a chance. 

After spending over a decade trying to make his mark with a succession of bands and then taking a job where he ran his own business, Brian was invited to London out of the blue for an audition with one of the world’s best up and coming acts. AC/DC was a band in crisis following the tragic death of their lead singer, Bon Scott, but with Brian on board they would record their masterpiece: “Back in Black”. It became the biggest-selling rock album of all time. 

The tour that followed played to packed-out arenas and Brian was embraced by their fans. But there was to be a twist in the tale. In 2016, Brian was forced to quit the band after being diagnosed with hearing loss, only to make a triumphant return to the band he loved with the release of 2020ʼs smash hit album “Power Up”. Brian has lived many lives within his lifetime, but somehow he has kept his feet on the ground, never losing touch with his roots. 

Touching, personal, indomitable, but always laugh-out-loud funny, “The Lives of Brian” is the story of one of the world’s best-loved performers, told in his own inimitable and unique voice. 


BRIAN JOHNSON & AC/DC:

Nothing More is an American rock band from San Antonio, Texas. The band self-funded and recorded their fourth studio album, Nothing More, over the course of three years and used it to gain the attention of Eleven Seven Music record label, who signed the band to a five album record contract upon hearing it. Dave Davies Co-Founder, Lead Guitarist, Backing Vocalist for The Kinks & Rock Hall Inductee on New Memoir & Band’s Boxed Set

PROMOTING: NEW 50TH ANNIVERSARY BOXED SET OF “MUSWELL HILLBILLIES” & “EVERYBODY’S IN SHOWBIZ”, AVAILABLE NOW AT AMAZON.COM & ALL MAJOR DIGITIAL RETAILERS 

PROMOTING: NEW MEMOIR “LIVING ON A THIN LINE”, AVAILABLE NOW ON KINDLE AND AUDIBLE AT AMAZON.COM. HARDCOVER AVAILABLE IN JANUARY, 2023 

PROMOTING: SINGLE “21ST CENTURY” WRITTEN WITH GERRY GOFFIN, AVAILABLE ON VINYL ON RECORD STORE DAY, NOVEMBER 25TH “MUSWELL HILLBILLIES/EVERYBODY’S IN SHOWBIZ”: This 50th anniversary boxed set from The Kinks includes the band’s pivotal 70s releases – Muswell Hillbillies, Everybody’s In Show-Biz and the single, 1975’s Everybody’s A Star. 

The beginning of the 70s marked a shift in the Kinks’ career. Having signed to RCA Records in 1971, the British band started a new era and gradually formed a new identity. With their growing American fanbase, the Kinks revised the phenomenon of Englishness and looked back on their London roots. This revision started with the 1971’s album Muswell Hillbillies which combines tales about working-class families from North London and whimsical country sound, associated with American music. Following this trajectory, Celluloid Heroes, the final track from Everybody’s In Show-Biz, reflects on Hollywood as a factory, mass-producing the bittersweet American dream. 

The anniversary edition contains a previously unseen, digitally restored 1971’s home movie, narrated by Ray Davies. The songs are remastered from newly discovered Davies’s original master tapes. “LIVING ON A THIN LINE”: In his autobiography, Dave Davies revisits the glory days of the band that spawned so much extraordinary music, and which had such a profound influence on bands from The Clash and Van Halen to Oasis and Blur. 

Full of tales of the tumultuous times and the ups-and-downs of his relationship with his brother Ray, along with encounters with the likes of John Lennon and Jimi Hendrix, this will be a glorious read for Kinks fans and anyone who wants to read about the heyday of rock 'n' roll. “21ST CENTURY”: This never-before-heard song was written by Dave Davies and Gerry Goffin on napkins in 1992 while the two were together at a coffee shop in LA. 

The song was originally written about the then-upcoming new millennium; however, lyrically the song could have been written today. The B-side "Web of Time" comes from the 2018 album “Decade”. 

DAVE DAVIES & THE KINKS: *Co-founder, lead guitarist, & backing vocalist for The Kinks, fronted by his brother, Ray Davies. 

*The Kinks were regarded as one of the most influential rock bands of the 1960s & part of the first British Invasion. 

*The Kinks have sold over fifty million records. 

*The Kinks’ songs include “You Really Got Me”, “All Day and All of the Night”, “Tired of Waiting for You”, “Lola”, “A Rock ‘n Roll Fantasy”, “Come Dancing”, & “Don’t Forget to Dance”. 

*Davies got inducted into the Rock ‘n Roll Hall of Fame in 1990 as a member of The Kinks. 

*Davies is prolific with his innovative nature on the electric guitar, specifically in being one of the first to utilize electric guitar distortion and exposing the effect to the mainstream. 

*Davies’ signature distorted power chord riffs on songs would heavily influence future heavy metal and punk rock acts. 

*UPCOMING: Two Kinks films are currently in the works.
