    All Things Considered

    Monday-Friday, 3-6:30pm; Saturday-Sunday 4-5pm

    All Things Considered hosts Audie Cornish, Mary Louise Kelly and Ari Shapiro present the program’s trademark mix of news, interviews, commentaries, reviews and offbeat features.

    Visit program website »

  • +

    BBC The Arts Hour

    Saturday, 10-11pm

    Curated, written and presented by Nikki Bedi, The Arts Hour brings you the best in global arts in a weekly showcase of rich arts, culture and entertainment stories from across the BBC and broadcasters around the world. This program reaches the world’s culture centers and taps into stories, personalities and big global debates.

    Visit program website »

  • +

    BBC Newshour

    Monday-Friday, 9-10am; Saturday-Sunday, 3-4pm

    With the world’s 24-hour news cycle now more intense and unrelenting than ever, BBC Newshour cuts through the background noise and provides you a definitive take on the big stories of the day.

    Visit program website »

  • +

    BBC World Service

    Overnight

    International news, analysis and information from the BBC’s global network of correspondents. The World Service provides news and reports on location, as well as insightful analysis of current events.

    Visit program website »

  • +

    Code Switch/Life Kit

    Sunday 8pm

    What’s Code Switch/Life Kit? It’s the fearless conversations about race that you’ve been waiting for. Hosted by journalists of color, our podcast tackles the subject of race with empathy and humor. We explore how race affects every part of society — from politics and pop culture to history, food and everything in between. This podcast makes all of us part of the conversation — because we’re all part of the story. Code Switch/Life Kit was named Apple Podcasts’ first-ever Show of the Year in 2020.

    Visit program website »

  • +

    Freakonomics Radio

    Saturday, 1-2pm and Sunday, 11am-noon

    Host Stephen Dubner has surprising conversations that explore the riddles of everyday life and the weird wrinkles of human nature — from cheating and crime to parenting and sports. Dubner talks with Nobel laureates and provocateurs, social scientists and entrepreneurs — and his Freakonomics co-author Steve Levitt.

    Visit program website »

  • +

    Fresh Air

    Monday-Friday, 7-8pm

    This Peabody Award-winning weekday magazine of contemporary arts and issues hosted by Terry Gross gives interviews as much time as needed, and complements them with comments from well-known critics, commentators and more.

    Visit program website »

  • +

    Here and Now

    Monday-Friday, 11am-Noon and 1-3pm

    The news you need to know today — and the stories that will stick with you tomorrow. Plus, special series and behind-the-scenes extras from Here & Now hosts Robin Young, Scott Tong and Deepa Fernandes with help from Producer Chris Bentley and the team at NPR and WBUR.

    Visit program website »

  • +

    Hidden Brain

    Sunday, 6-7pm

    Shankar Vedantam uses science and storytelling to reveal the unconscious patterns that drive human behavior, shape our choices and direct our relationships.

    Visit program website »

  • +

    It's Been a Minute

    Saturday, 5-6pm

    Each week, interviews with people in the culture who deserve your attention. Plus weekly wraps of the news with other journalists. Join Sam as he makes sense of the world through conversation.

    Visit program website »

  • +

    KCRW's Left, Right & Center

    Sunday, 2-3pm

    Left, Right & Center is KCRW’s weekly civilized yet provocative confrontation over politics, policy and pop culture.

    Visit program website »

  • +

    Latino USA

    Saturday, 6-7pm

    Maria Hinojosa hosts this radio journal of news and culture — the only national, English-language radio program produced from a Latino perspective.

    Visit program website »

  • +

    Marketplace

    Monday-Friday, 6:30-7pm

    Hosted by Kai Ryssdal, Marketplace presents business news that’s in-depth, understandable and interesting. It’s news on business, economics and money for the rest of us.

    Visit program website »

  • +

    Morning Edition

    Monday-Friday, 4-9am

    NPR’s Morning Edition prepares listeners for the day ahead with two hours of up-to-the-minute news, background analysis, commentary and coverage of arts and sports. Hosted by David Greene, Steve Inskeep, Noel King, and Rachel Martin.

    Visit program website »

  • +

    The Moth Radio Hour

    Saturday, 7-9pm

    The Moth Radio Hour features true stories told live on stage without scripts, notes, props or accompaniment. Moth storytellers stand alone, under a spotlight, with only a microphone and a roomful of strangers. Each hour mixes humorous, heartbreaking and poignant tales that captivate, surprise and delight with their honesty, bravery and humor.

    Visit program website »

  • +

    Notes from America with Kai Wright

    Sunday, 5-6pm

    Notes from America with Kai Wright is a show about the unfinished business of our history, and its grip on our future.

    Visit program website »

  • +

    On Point

    Monday-Friday, 9-10pm

    On Point is a rare public space where you hear nuanced explorations of complex topics live and in real time. It’s urgency and timeliness matched with depth. Podcasts, too, are rising as powerful tools for long-format journalism. But by definition, on-demand content is decoupled from the immediacy of the day’s or week’s news.

    Visit program website »

  • +

    The People's Pharmacy

    Saturday, 6-7am

    Everything from home remedies to the latest breakthrough drugs are discussed on The People’s Pharmacy. Pharmacologist Joe Graedon and medical anthropologist Terry Graedon talk to leading experts to discuss issues relating to drugs, herbs, home remedies, vitamins and related health topics.

    Visit program website »

  • +

    Planet Money / How I Built This

    Saturday, 11am-Noon

    Two exciting shows in one hour of programming. Planet Money explains the economy with playful storytelling and Peabody award-winning deep dive, roll up your sleeves journalism. How I Built This weaves a narrative journey about innovators, entrepreneurs and idealists—and the movements they built.

    Visit program websites: Planet Money and How I Built This »

  • +

    Radiolab

    Sunday, Noon-1pm

    Radiolab, with Jad Abumrad and Robert Krulwich, is an award-winning radio show and podcast weaving stories and science into compelling sound and music-rich documentaries.

    Visit program website »

  • +

    Reveal

    Sunday, 7-8pm

    We’ve heard the news, but we want to know what really happened. That’s what Reveal is all about: finding out what’s going on behind the scenes, hidden from public view. A project of The Center for Investigative Reporting and co-produced with PRX.

    Visit program website »

  • +

    Snap Judgment

    Saturday, 9-10pm

    Dramatic tales, killer beats and the edgiest new talent in storytelling come together for a weekly radio hour hosted by Glynn Washington.

    Visit program website »

  • +

    TED Radio Hour

    Saturday, 2-3pm and Sunday, 1-2pm

    A journey through fascinating ideas, astonishing inventions, and new ways to think and create. Based on riveting TEDTalks from the world’s most remarkable minds.

    Visit program website »

  • +

    Texas Standard

    Monday-Friday, 10-11am and 10-11pm

    Crisp, up-to-the-moment coverage of politics, lifestyle, the environment, technology, innovation and money from a uniquely Texas perspective. The show is based at KUT in Austin, and KERA is a proud co-producer with other public radio stations across Texas.

    Visit program website »

  • +

    Think

    Monday-Friday, Noon-1pm and Monday-Friday, 8pm-9pm

    KERA’s original, award-winning call-in show hosted by Krys Boyd explores current affairs, culture, science and more.

    Visit program website »

  • +

    This American Life

    Saturday, noon-1pm and Sunday, 9-10pm

    This American Life, with host Ira Glass, documents and describes contemporary America with stories that are alternately engaging, intimate, surprising, funny, disturbing and bittersweet — an entertaining kind of journalism that’s built around plot.

    Visit program website »

  • +

    Travel With Rick Steves

    Sunday, 10-11pm

    Travel with Rick Steves is a fun, hour-long weekly talk show with guest experts, practical advice and calls and questions from travelers.

    Visit program website »

  • +

    Wait Wait... Don't Tell Me!

    Saturday and Sunday, 10-11am

    Wait Wait… Don’t Tell Me! is NPR’s weekly hour-long quiz program, hosted by Peter Sagal, with judge-scorekeeper Carl Kasell. Each week, test your knowledge against some of the best and the brightest in the news and entertainment worlds.

    Visit program website »

  • +

    A Way With Words

    Sunday, 6-7am

    Public radio’s show about words and language and how we use them, with Martha Barnette and Grant Barrett.

    Visit program website »

  • +

    Weekend Edition Saturday

    Saturday, 7-10am

    From civil wars to hospital rooms, police stations to America’s backyards, Weekend Edition Saturday, hosted by NPR’s Scott Simon, covers hard news, a wide variety of newsmakers, and cultural stories with care, accuracy and a wink of humor.

    Visit program website »

  • +

    Weekend Edition Sunday

    Sunday, 7-10am


    NPR’s Weekend Edition Sunday, hosted by Lulu Garcia-Navarro, combines the news with colorful arts and human-interest features, appealing to the curious and eclectic with a visit from Puzzlemaster, Will Shortz, puzzle editor of The New York Times.
    Visit program website »
