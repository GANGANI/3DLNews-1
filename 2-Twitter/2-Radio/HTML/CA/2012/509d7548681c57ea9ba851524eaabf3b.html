Springsteen fans frustrated by online Ticketmaster errors

  • Published: Jan. 27, 2012, 6:30 p.m.
By
  • Peggy McGlone | NJ Advance Media for NJ.com
Dan Bracaglia/For The Star-LedgerBruce Springsteen performs in Asbury Park on Jan. 14. The Boss is playing shows in N.J., but Ticketmaster's website had problems due to high levels of traffic.

Ticketmaster called it "highly abnormal traffic patterns." Fans called it frustrating.

Tickets for three New Jersey concerts by Bruce Springsteen and the E Street Band went on sale this morning and many fans who tried to buy online reported frozen screens and error messages that left them without tickets.

A Ticketmaster spokeswoman said the company is investigating. The shows are part of the first leg of the band's "Wrecking Ball" tour, which kicks off March 18 in Atlanta.

"We have been experiencing highly abnormal traffic patterns on our site this morning that have impacted the fan buying experience for some customers. We are investigating the source of the problem and are working to resolve it as quickly as possible, but tickets are selling so please stay patient. We will update fans as we know more," read the official statement from the company, the largest ticket service in the world.

The statement was also posted on Bruce Springteen’s website, which acknowledged that The Boss had heard that many of his fans "have been having trouble getting tickets on this morning’s on-sales."

Tickets for the April 3 and 4 Izod Center shows went on sale at 10 a.m., followed by the May 2 at Prudential Center an hour later. At noon, the April 6 and 9 Madison Square Garden shows went on sale.

Jesse Regis, 23, of Long Valley spent 45 minutes fighting with the website this morning.

"You wait for 15 minutes. and you get an error message and you have to start all over again," Regis said. "It happens all the time with Springsteen (shows)."
