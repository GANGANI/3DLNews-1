There is a Verdict – Don’t Fall for It

Thu, May 30, 2024  |  By John Schroeder

Announced the jury has reached a verdict.

Number One – expect a guilty verdict and do not get upset.  That’s the first “don’t fall for it.”  The verdict will end up reversed when all the appeals are in.

Number Two – Do not fall for the protest and riot bait.  When the verdict is announced, let it go.  Don’t take to the streets, don’t shout and scream, don’t carry on.  All you’ll do is make us look bad and give them satisfaction.

Hatred Can Only Destroy

Thu, May 30, 2024  |  By John Schroeder

The host is quite animated on the show this morning due to the farce of the Trump NYC trial.  He carried on about it at great length at Fox.  His case is simple – the entire episode is driven by TDS – Trump Derangement Syndrome – hatred.  I presume the host’s uncommon level of energy on this specific story is due to his love of the rule of law.  And so we see hatred destroying the rule of law.  So deep is this hatred of Trump that the haters feel they must protect “science” from Trump.  In the first place, no president can threaten “science” – they can only threaten the science bureaucracy in the federal government, two very different things. Which means what they are doing is undermining the will of the people.  We elect a president to execute the policy that he or she campaigned on, but here we see a prior administration acting to prevent the next administration from doing what we, the voting public, elected it to do.  And thus hatred is destroying the very foundations of our nation.  But it gets even deeper.

The Key To Wealth…

Wed, May 29, 2024  |  By John Schroeder

…is becoming increasingly difficult to obtain.  The past couple of weeks have seen story after story about the rising barriers to home ownership.  Since its founding, owning some land or a home has been the key to wealth accumulation for pretty much anybody.  But thanks to Joe Biden and his policies home ownership is increasingly out-of-reach.  Likewise, inflationary pressure causes those who have done their accumulation to seek more real property to protect that which they have accumulated which further raises the barriers.  People are looking to drastic solutions – like “tech.”  But they fail to realize that the job displacement such would create would simply worsen the situation.  It’s an ugly picture.

Trampling On Merit’s Grave

Tue, May 28, 2024  |  By John Schroeder

This from Salena Zito last week:

The Civil Rights Act prohibits workplace discrimination on the basis of race, sex, religion and national origin. EEOC attorney Debra M. Lawrence wrote that criminal background checks “cause a disparate impact because of race or other protected classifications.”

Lawrence went on to say the employment hiring practices must be shown by the employer to be necessary to ensure the safe and efficient performance of the particular jobs at issue.

“Even when such necessity is proven, the practice remains unlawful if there is an alternative practice available that is comparably effective in achieving the employer’s goals but causes less discriminatory effect,” Lawrence added.

If your jaw has not hit the floor, it should.

Memorial Day, Hope and Wonder

Mon, May 27, 2024  |  By John Schroeder

Yesterday, I noted that to die for one’s country was an act of love.  Way too many people these days view it as an act of futility, or worse a symbol of oppression.  How did we get here?  I think because of the death of hope.  Hope transforms how we look at so much.  Those that have died in service to the nation know it is not perfect, but they love it because our nation provides great hope for a better future, and so they die for it.  But without that hope, all we see are the nation’s imperfections.

This Weekend

Sun, May 26, 2024  |  By John Schroeder

Jesus said:

Greater love has no one than this, that a person will lay down his life for his friends.

That’s what we remember this weekend – people that loved this nation, loved us, so much that they died in our defense.  We live in an age when the military is ill-considered by many, where war presumes barbarity and people would just as soon ignore its reality.  That’s not this nation, that’s not the United States.  We do not start wars, but we do finish them – and when we do, we do so out of love of country and our fellow Americans.  Not out of malice or evil, but out of love.  This weekend is about loss and love.  Let’s put the nonsense aside this weekend and focus on that.
