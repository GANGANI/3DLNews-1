Dallas, Central swim teams growing with each stride

  • Jennifer Biberston
  • Dec 19, 2018
  • Dec 19, 2018

WEST ALBANY — It’s the second swim meet of the season, and for some swimmers on the Dallas and Central high school swim teams, it’s one of the few swim meets they have ever participated in.

Kyle Reed, a junior, has less than a month of his first swim season under his belt with the Dallas Dragons, and he said he’s beginning to realize the intensity of the sport.

“(This meet) is more swimming, and it’s harder than I was expecting it was; when we were at Silverton, there was more time to relax. And I ate too much during lunch.”

He participated in the 200-yard freestyle, the 100 freestyle, and the 200 and 400 freestyle relays.

As the season continues, Kyle’s goal is to improve.

“I just want to do my best,” he said.

On the Panther side, Sean Stephenson, a junior, started swimming last year and is already seeing huge improvements.

“(The season) is going pretty good,” he said. “I got three personal records. … Last year I just really got down the basics,” but he said this year, he’s focusing more on technique.

Stephenson’s events were the 200-yard individual medley, the 100 butterfly, the 200 relay and the 400 relay.

Now that he’s a few weeks into the season, Stephenson said he’s formulating some goals he hopes to achieve.

“I’m trying to go 57 (seconds) on my 100 freestyle, and 1:05 on my butterfly.”

There are some swimmers on Central’s 25-athlete roster who have multiple years of competitive experience, and one of them is Lynzy Bradley.

Bradley, a junior on the Panthers girls team, has been swimming competitively since 2009. She said she’s starting to find her groove again.

“Well it’s only the second meet, and it’s a little stressful at first, doing events you haven’t done since last year, so. It’s going better than I thought. I PR’d in everything so far.”

Her events for the three-way meet were the 200 freestyle, the 100 backstroke, the 200 freestyle relay and the 400 freestyle relay.

“I think I’m getting better, because obviously I’m PRing and stuff,” she said. “I just need to start trying harder in practice to make the progress a lot better and faster.”

Her biggest goal is to improve the time on her back stroke.

“I want to try beating the back stroke school record, which I have to get seven seconds faster, which is a big jump, but if I try hard enough I’ll get there.”

Central’s school record for the back stroke is 1:11, and she sits at 1:18.

CHS head coach Jesse Genualdi said the team is in a period of growth, even with some of his swimmers having competitive experience.

“We are in a rebuilding season, as many of our athletes have never swam competitively before,” Genualdi said. “We are still learning how to compete at meets and learning what it means to be a team that works together. Our goal this season is to give the best effort we have in everything we do; this includes but is not limited to what we do at practice (and) meets, but also academically. We have had a few PR’s since our first meet and I think we are moving forward in the right direction, but we have a lot of work to prepare for the district meet in February.”

On the Dragons boys team, junior Gabriel Applegate holds seven years of competitive experience.

“Everyone is doing a good job,” he said. “I like that it’s team motivation, and when you’re in the water, you know your team is cheering for you.”

Applegate swam in the 50 freestyle and the 100 butterfly.

Even with having multiple years of experience, Applegate recognizes that he can still get better, and that’s what he’s looking forward to.

“Just getting better, and trying to get better every day in practice from the last day, and knowing that you’ll always have something to improve on,” he said.

Although Dallas lacks in numbers, with five boys on the team, head coach Mark Maxwell said they work hard, and that’s what counts.

“I’m pleased overall,” he said. “They’re working, they’re coming to practice; those are things that you need to take care of. You may have a bad swim today or next week, but it’s all gonna work out in the end.”
