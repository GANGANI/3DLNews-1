Mary Frederickson: Resource betrayal

  • Updated 2 hrs ago

Editor: If our federal and state governments cannot protect us from foreign bad actors extracting our resources and leaving behind a scarred landscape, polluted water systems, degraded environments, unpaid fines/penalties/fees, and ruined roads, what good is our government? Protecting our land and resources from foreign actors seems to be a basic function of a sovereign nation or state. It’s sad (or cynically ironic, if your mind goes that way) how people who rail against western nations for their long-ago abuse of colonies’ resources through extraction industries are now turning a blind eye to the fact that we have become the target of other nations for damaging extraction of our natural resources. Editor: Monday, May 27, is the date we celebrate Memorial Day. Memorial Day was started to remember our war dead on both sides of the Civil War in the late 19th century. In an effort to nationally remember those killed in action,May 30th was made Memorial Day . But more importantly it was to unify this country as the United States.
