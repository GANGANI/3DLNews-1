Suspect in custody, charged with Bisbee Rd. murder

Laura Swan

The Bisbee Observer

Two days after a man was found dead on Bisbee Rd., a man wanted in connection to his suspicious death has been taken into custody and is being charged with murder.

Bisbee Police took 45-year-old Nathan Banbury into custody shortly after 5 p.m. Tuesday in Old Bisbee. An arrest warrant had been issued for Banbury after police received tips and tracked down evidence that he is allegedly connected to the death of Lee Vance, 73, who resided at the Bisbee Rd. home where he was found Sunday just before 1 p.m.

According to a press release form Bisbee Police Chief Tim Cox, “On May 26, 2024, at approximately 12:51 p.m. the Bisbee Police Department was dispatched to 409 Bisbee Road for a check welfare. The caller stated they had observed a male laying on the floor who was not responsive. Shipping container ban put on hold

Laura Swan

The Bisbee Observer

After expressing reservations of moving forward with an ordinance that would ban unmodified shipping containers on private property, Mayor Ken Budge suggested the council table the item while further information is gathered.

Mayor Budge said, “I have some reservations, and several concerns have come before us.” Adding that feedback received included the definition of unmodified.

Welcome To The Bisbee Observer Newspaper Website

The Bisbee Observer serves an area of more than 10,000 residents in the communities of Bisbee, Douglas, Naco, McNeal, Double Adobe, and Palominas / Hereford, Sierra Vista, in Southern Arizona.

The Bisbee Observer is the community newspaper and has been published since 1985.

The Bisbee Observer is the Official Newspaper of Record for the City of Bisbee.

The Bisbee Observer is published every Thursday, by Laura Swan and is available at many locations in Bisbee and the surrounding area. The Bisbee Observer is sent to subscribers second class paid postage at Bisbee, AZ.
