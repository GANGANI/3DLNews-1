Sunrise Mountain exerts dominance with 9-0 win over No. 3-ranked Kellis

  • By Darrell Jackson, Special to Peoria Times
  • Apr 22, 2013
  • Apr 22, 2013 Updated Apr 22, 2013

In a battle featuring two of the top three ranked teams in Division II and two of the top 25 teams in the state of Arizona, it turned out to be a show of domination by one over the other.

The Sunrise Mountain softball team used a five-run fifth inning and Taylor Nowlin struck out 10 as the Mustangs cruised to a 9-0 victory over Raymond S. Kellis Thursday at Kellis High School.

“I am very surprised at the final score,” Sunrise Mountain head coach Jody Pruitt said. “(Kellis) is a very fundamental softball team and they made a couple mistakes that we were able to execute in our favor.”

Sunrise Mountain entered the game ranked No. 2 in Division II, No. 7 in Arizona and No. 18 nationally. Kellis entered ranked No. 3 in Division II and No. 23 in Arizona.

The win extended Sunrise Mountain's winning streak to 22 straight, as Kellis dropped their sixth in a row.

“I am surprised, but we just didn't show up to play today,” Kellis head coach Shawn Sullivan said. “Hopefully this loss will tick some of my girls off and they will practice better and work a little harder.”

Leading 1-0 in the top of the fifth, Sunrise Mountain (28-1-0 overall, 14-1-0 in Division II, Section II) sent nine batters to the plate to blow the game open.

Danae King led off with a double to right center field and moved to third on a bloop single to center by Michala Erickson. After Morgan Leonard drove a run in with a groundout, Nowlin singled to right, scoring Erickson. Kirsten Hostetler then came up and crushed a 1-2 pitch over the left field fence for a two-run home run.

“My first two at-bats I was barely missing the ball, so I just thought I would try and get my hands inside the ball and crank something,” Hostetler said. “I really wanted to change the game and she threw me a pitch inside and low, which is where I like it, and I got a good swing on the ball.”

The Mustangs added one more in the fifth when, with two outs and a runner on first, Brooke Taylor hit a triple off the left field wall, scoring Whitney Chamberain, who had walked.

Nowlin, who started on the mound, retired 11 of the first 12 batters she faced en route to a complete game, one-hit shutout She struck out 10 and walked four for the win.

“I was having a hard time, walking some batters in the beginning,” Nowlin said. “This was a statement game for us because the last couple of games for us have been close and we haven't played our best, even though we won them.”

In the seventh, with runners first and second and nobody out, Tatume Birt hit a slow roller to Kellis (20-6-1, 13-2-0) pitcher Jessica Rojo, who fired the ball over the first baseman's head, scoring two. After a strikeout, Molly Stern grounded out to second, scoring Birt to put the Mustangs up 9-0.

Nowlin, in between pitches to batters, was laughing and joking with her teammates in a pressure-filled game between two of the top teams in Arizona.

“If Taylor is not laughing, we have a problem,” Pruitt said. “I'm sure to other people that may not seem nice, but that is just her personality.”

While Pruitt is confident in her team and knows what they can do, she knows this victory is a strong win for her team.

“I think everyone takes us seriously and always plays hard against us,” she said. “But beating a team like Kellis, we feel it is great momentum heading into the last couple games and into the playoffs.”
