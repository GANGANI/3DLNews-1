    12 essential planning steps for brides-to-be

    • Linda Schnitzer | Special to the Jewish News
    • Sep 20, 2017
    • Sep 20, 2017

    The beautiful fall weather in Arizona is right around the corner, which means we’ll start seeing an uptick in weddings around the Valley.

    But for a dream wedding to become a reality, a lot of planning needs to be done, and that means many niggling details. Advance planning is definitely worth it: If a bride-to-be is organized during the planning process, she’ll be able to relax and truly enjoy her special day.

    Here, we offer some planning musts.

    To-do list: A good plan starts with a good checklist, which is a key component in making sure your day goes off without a hitch. There are many templates and apps available to make sure you don’t forget any of the details, such as the venue location, flowers, band and photographer.

    Timelines: Set due dates for specific tasks to stay on schedule. Keep in mind that some decisions take time, such as trying on different wedding dresses and interviewing different vendors.

    Wedding date: Pick your wedding date and start the planning process right away. You’ll need as much time as possible to go into your wedding day feeling prepared and relaxed.

    Budget: It’s important to know how much you and your fiancé want to spend and which items on the list are a priority. This will help in choosing vendors that fit your specific needs.

    Guest list: This should be one of the first tasks to accomplish. This will determine which venues have the capacity to accommodate the size of your wedding party.

    Venue location: Now that you have your guest list, it’s time to find your wedding venue. Wedding venues book up very quickly, especially for popular locations. This is another priority to check off your list.

    Wedding planner: Determine whether you want or feel you need an event planner. If you feel you need help in managing the details and finding the right vendors, a wedding planner is a good option. Take into consideration the cost of your time in managing all the details and whether hiring an event planner works within your budget. Some venues include all or some planning services, so be sure to ask what is offered.

    Officiant: Meet with your rabbi or wedding officiant and make sure they are available on your date. They can help with special items needed for the ceremony, such as the chuppah, kiddush cup and ketubah (Jewish marriage contract).

    Break in your shoes: This sounds minor, although you’ll thank me later. You’ll most likely be on your feet most of the day, so being comfortable is very important. Be sure to bring a back-up pair of comfortable shoes that you can slip into if needed. There are even some very cute wedding tennis shoes with plenty of bling!

    Food to go: Have your venue pack food to go at the end of the wedding. With so much going on, it’s easy to forget to eat during the event. It will be nice to know you have a wonderful meal you and your spouse can share at the end of the night … and don’t forget the cake.

    Have fun: Let the little things go and simply enjoy your day. No one will know or care if something minor didn’t go as planned. Your guests just want to be part of your special day and see you happy and having a great time. Sometimes those little things are what make up those funny and memorable stories that you will tell your grandchildren one day.

    L’chaim to life: This is the beginning of your new life, so remember to cherish and enjoy your special day! JN

    Linda Schnitzer is the events planner at Pomelo at the Orchard in Phoenix. For more information, visit pomelophx.com.
