Havasu prepares for July 4 festivities

  • By BRANDON MESSICK Today’s News-Herald
  • Jun 28, 2017
  • Jun 28, 2017 Updated Jun 29, 2017

There’s a secret to Havasu’s unique architecture that may further residents’ appreciation for the July 4 holiday next week.

When C.V. Wood designed what would become Lake Havasu City, it was a design the like of which had rarely been seen. Streets parallel to State Route 95 would curve upward against Havasu’s foothills, before gradually intersecting the highway once more. The unique layout of Havasu’s major thoroughfares resembles a form of “amphitheater,” and has a similar purpose – no matter where residents are in Havasu, they can often find a view of the lake. This July 4 weekend, that same city architecture will provide Havasu residents with another form of spectacle.

Strokes of dazzling dazzling light and technicolor blooms are expected to paint the skies over Spectator Point this weekend, for the city’s annual July Celebration. The annual event will present a twenty-minute fireworks display in celebration of America’s independence, unleashing thousands of pounds of patriotic ordinance above the Havasu coastline in a spectacle that routinely draws hundreds of boaters and thousands of spectators every year.

“People are looking forward to it,” said Lake Havasu City Convention and Visitors Bureau Interim-President Chip Shilosky. “We’re expecting a big holiday this year. The fireworks will shoot directly over Spectator Point, near the Nautical Beachfront Resort…people are sure to see a good show.”

According to Shilosky, visitors have in recent years enjoyed a three-day July 4 weekend in Havasu, taking in the sights of the city before enjoying the fireworks displays. This year, Shilosky expects the gap between this weekend and Tuesday’s celebration to potentially mitigate traffic – weekend vacationers and July 4 spectators will not have to compete for parking space, or against other drivers on Havasu’s roads.

Shilosky expects specialists from Utah-based Lantis Fireworks to begin prepping for the event next Tuesday.

According to Mayor Mark Nexsen, Havasu’s July 4 festivities are a reflection of the city’s patriotism. As with Memorial Day, Independence Day brings more families to Havasu than weekend revelers, who he has little doubt will enjoy Tuesday’s display.

“July 4 is a huge holiday for us,” Nexsen said. “We’re celebrating the birth of our nation, and all of the rights and privileges our veterans fought to give us.”

According to Nexsen, the city’s traditional July 4 celebration left an impression on him long before he moved to Havasu. “There are clear skies, warm weather, friendly people and you can get out on the lake and cool off…it’s an ideal spot,” Nexsen said.

According to CVB officials, the best spots in Havasu to see the fireworks will include Rotary Park, the Nautical Beachfront Resort, London Bridge Beach and at Lookout point. For those who venture onto the Lake, the CVB recommends Thompson Bay as an ideal location to see the display.

The Lake Havasu City Fire Department will maintain two engines on each side of Bridgewater Channel, according to Battalion Chief Jasen Stello, and a fire boat will remain on Lake Havasu throughout the show.

The City’s festivities aside, July 4 is also the one day of the year when firework usage will be legal within Lake Havasu City limits. Residents are advised to use such fireworks away from dry brush or flammable materials. The Fire Department reminds residents that explosive fireworks are illegal in Mohave County, and only permitted fireworks such as novelties, sparklers and smokers will be permitted for use by Havasu residents.

Lake Havasu City’s July 4 Celebration begins at 9 p.m. Tuesday. Residents are reminded to keep their pets inside for the duration of Havasu’s fireworks display.
