    Thank you, council for ‘no’ on light rail

    • Letter to the Editor
    • Oct 26, 2017
    • Oct 26, 2017
    • 1 min to read
    • Facebook
    • Twitter
    • WhatsApp
    • SMS
    • Email
    • Facebook
    • Twitter
    • WhatsApp
    • SMS
    • Email
    • Print
    • Save

    Last week our council stopped light rail from coming into downtown Glendale. Thank you.

    Thank you for showing the common sense needed to stop another financial fiasco from affecting the city’s comeback continuing by using better business sense than previous councils.

    I’m not an engineer, nor am I a quasi civil servant. I can’t say with 100-percent certainty that light rail would have been a boom, or doom to our downtown. The gentlemen from Valley Metro couldn’t say, either. I am a betting man and I would bet a large sum of money that the cost of this project would have been much higher than predicted, the income derived and benefit from it would be much less, and the time to build it would be much longer than estimated.

    The fact that many of our council are business people who, like myself, have to play with their own money, probably had something to do with their wise decision to pass on this project. Most politicians and civil servants have no skin in the game. The notion that we are giving up “free” federal funds is a fallacy. The federal government has only the money it takes from us.

    The cost per mile is outrageous. The maintenance and operating costs are just as bad. It would be cheaper for our city to send Uber drivers to 43rd and Glendale avenues to bring those wanting to come downtown than it would to participate in light rail.

    We continue looking for that special thing that will make our downtown grow with more activity, and we will find it eventually. The council’s decision to opt out of light rail will help insure this. If there is any doubt in your mind about this, spend some time on the light rail at various times. Go up to 19th Avenue and Olive (Dunlap Road). The neighborhood has changed and not for the better.  

    At a time when light rail cities are having to spend more on security to make it safer for the few who do ride and more (on) hosing the urine out of the cars each night, thank the majority of our council for having the guts and brains to say, “Thanks, but no thanks.”

    Ron Kolb
