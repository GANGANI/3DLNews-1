Why this petition matters

Started by Eddie Garcia

On June 15, 2016, Vermont's alt-newspaper of record, Seven Days, published an article by reporter Paul Heintz titled "The Gun: How I Bought an AR-15 in a Five Guys Parking Lot" describing his purchase of an AR-15 rifle from a private party.

The article was replete with fearmongering and exploitative references to the Orlando nightclub tragedy in which 49 innocent people died.

Exploiting the deaths of innocents to advance a pre-existing agenda is unacceptable, and nothing remotely resembling respectable journalism.

The article gives the impression that Heintz was trying to portray a legal practice as something that borders on crime, and given the positions of the three Democrat Party candidates for Vermont Governor on gun control, represents naked partisanship, election-season propaganda, and shameless, agenda driven agitprop.

It is worth noting that Heintz' article was one of many published simultaneously and nationwide by leftist media in the wake of the Orlando shooting, which further speaks to an inapproriate collusive agenda on the part of Heintz and Seven Days. 

It is also worth noting that the publisher of Seven Days, Paula Routly, has as her significant other a Vermont State Senator of the Progressive and Democrat Parties, Tim Ashe of Chittenden County, who has earned a rating of "Extremely Anti-Gun" from Gun Owners of Vermont.  

We, the undersigned, condemn Seven Days' transparent and despicable attempt to write the Vermont Democrat Party's anti-liberty agenda in the blood of innocents.

We, the undersigned, demand that Paul Heintz's employment with Seven Days be terminated forthwith, and pledge to boycott all businesses advertising with them absent this.   

Self defense is a human right.
