Climate Strike, Security Cams, Bigg-A-Bed: Here's what's happening on campus.
View this email in your browser
Thursday, September 26, 2019
Vol. CXVIII, No. 3
Happy Thursday! 

This week, students and faculty settled into their routines while leaves started turning (we just discovered this foliage tracker for official coverage). Here are the stories this week that people are (or should be) talking about on campus. 

The community flooded College Park on Friday as Middlebury joined the global Climate Strike movement, the Title IX office was reshuffled (it now falls within the purview of the Office of Institutional Diversity, Equity and Inclusion) and the field hockey team won its 25th straight home game.

Also, check out our Photo of the Week, featuring the grand opening of the dog mushing Cobble Hill Kennel, which will offer inaugural sled rides this season. 

'Til next week,
Amelia & Benjy

This Week's Briefing:

1. Community joins global Climate Strike
BENJY RENTON/THE MIDDLEBURY CAMPUS
Local
What went down. Over 500 college students, faculty, residents, high schoolers and middle schoolers rallied last Friday at College Park, joining a surge of activists around the globe.

Best quote. "There's nothing I can think of that's more liberal arts than professors and students and community members getting hauled off to jail together," said Sociology Professor Jamie McCallum of the potential consequences of practicing civil disobedience.

2. Administrative updates:
Public Safety cams & building renovations
BENJY RENTON/THE MIDDLEBURY CAMPUS
News
Big Brother? Just kidding. But in an all-school e-mail on Tuesday, the college announced that new security cameras will be installed across campus. Body cameras are also in the works, but are in a nascent planning stage. 

Provost's take on building renovations. “Do we have funding? No. Do we have a timeline? No. Do we have a specific plan? No. But are we talking about it? Yes,” the college's treasurer David Provost said regarding potential plans for a new student center.
 

3. Student voting triples for midterms
ISHA KOTHARI/THE MIDDLEBURY CAMPUS
News
How we compare. National voting rates doubled, from 19% to 40%, while ours more than tripled, in a 15% to 51% jump.

Voting rates by major. Highest: Environmental Studies, English and Studio Art. Lowest: Computer Science, Psychology and Mathematics. 

4. Who's behind Bigg-A-Bed?
PAUL FLORES-CLAVEL/COURTESY PHOTO
News
The inspiration. Founder Adam Wisco ’22 hadn’t given much thought to the inconveniences of sleeping in a twin XL prior to founding Bigg-A-Bed. But there was one thing that was for sure: “It definitely wasn’t nice." And then? "I got a girlfriend," he added.

How it works. The Bigg-A-Bed package includes metal hooks, wooden support beams, a foam mattress, frame attachments and zip ties. The equipment adds on to the current standard Twin XL frame to create the ideal bed for you (and your girlfriend). 

5. Funny Femme Fest:
Rewriting the comedy narrative
MAX PADILLA/THE MIDDLEBURY CAMPUS
Arts & Academics

Femmes unite. Female-identifying comedians from Middlebury Discount Comedy, various improv comedy groups and the satirical newspaper The Local Noodle led a series of workshops on comedic writing and performance. 

The ice breaker. Rather than asking for a fun fact or favorite dining hall, the MDC workshop began with an unusual question: "What's your most serial killer-like trait?" 

Photo of the Week
ERICA BISAILLON/THE MIDDLEBURY CAMPUS
Mushing season. Cobble Hill Dog Kennel had its grand opening last week, promising dry-land dogsledding this fall and snow dogsledding this winter. And psst, Midd Kids: students get 50% off.
