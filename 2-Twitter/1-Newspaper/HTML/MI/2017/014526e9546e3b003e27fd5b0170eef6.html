Dow's Booth earns 100th wrestling win, Chemics go 2-3 at own invitational

By The Midland Daily NewsJan 21, 2017

FENTON — Brennan Doyle and Brent Booth highlighted Dow High’s efforts at the 10-team Fenton Wrestling Tournament on Saturday, where the Chargers took sixth place at 2-3.

Dow won 52-18 over Warren Lincoln and 47-32 over Farmington, and lost 45-25 to Clinton, 43-33 to Caro, and 40-27 to Fenton.

Both Doyle (152 pounds) and Booth (215) were 5-0, with Doyle beating Division 3 second-ranked D.J. Daniels of Caro, and Booth, a junior, earning his 100th varsity win. Booth knocked off a pair of top-10 opponents from Lincoln and Clinton to put an exclamation point on his performance.

Chemics finish fourth at own meet

Midland High placed fourth out of six teams with a 2-3 record at its own wrestling invitational on Saturday.

The Chemics beat Warren Mott 39-35 and beat Freeland 50-30, and lost 40-23 to Anchor Bay, 54-18 to Gaylord, and 42-26 to Cedar Springs.

Anchor Bay is ranked in the top 10 in Division 1, and Gaylord is in the top 10 in Division 2. Anchor Bay beat Gaylord for first place on Saturday.

“We want to see (top) teams like that,” Midland coach Mike Donovan said. “We’re getting prepared for the (Saginaw) Valley (League) tournament and districts. ... I was extremely impressed with how we wrestled.”

Brothers Jake Hine (160) and Sam Hine (171) both went 5-0 to lead the Chemics, while Jessy Hays at 152 and Matt Waier at 119 each earned four wins, and Jacob Gomola at 135 and Mateo Diaz at 112 each earned three wins.

Midland is 13-6, and its next scheduled match is Wednesday, Feb. 1 at home vs. Gaylord and Traverse City West.

The Chemics will host the Division 1 team district on Thursday, Feb. 9, taking on the Flint combined team while Dow wrestles Bay City Western in the other semifinal.

Meanwhile, Freeland went winless as a team at the Midland invitational, losing 69-5 to Gaylord, 57-15 to Cedar Springs, 63-18 to Anchor Bay, and 40-39 to Warren Mott in addition to the match vs. Midland.

Tobias Niedzwiecki (215) went 5-0 to lead the Falcons, and Zach Anderson (103) and Chase Crook (285) both went 3-2.

“Everybody (at the tournament) was from Division 1 and 2 except us,” said Freeland coach Jacob Ott, whose team is in Division 3. “My kids were matching up with pretty high level competition, and they struggled, but it was a good experience for them.”

Moore leads Western to fourth place at Holt

HOLT — Coby Moore went 5-0 on Saturday to lead Bay City Western’s wrestling team to a fourth-place finish at the Holt Invitational. The Warriors finished behind Walled Lake Central, Temperance Bedford, and Holt.

Moore finished first at 152 pounds, while Vic Schoenherr and Noah Schoenherr both finished at 4-1 to take second at 135 and 140 pounds, respectively. Finishing third for the Warriors were Austin Hale (119) and Drake LaFramboise (171), both with 4-1 records.

Alex Simmons (125) was fifth with a 4-1 record, while Caleb Gosselin (112), Gabe Brucewski (160), and Zeke VanOchten (215) all placed sixth and finished at 3-2. Jared Gosselin (145) was 3-2 but did not place, while Jordaan Lofaro (189) had two wins, and Scott Neumeyer won one match.

“I thought we wrestled really well. It was a good tournament with a lot of good teams and good individuals,” said Warriors’ coach Isaiah Thomas. “It was a great opportunity for us. We’ve been pushing to fix some of our problem spots lately, and I think we’ve done that.”

Western (14-4) heads to Mount Pleasant on Wednesday to compete in a Saginaw Valley League quad against Flint and Saginaw Heritage.

Wetherell, Solano both medal; Mustangs take seventh

ITHACA — Tyler Wetherell and Brady Solano earned their first tournament medals of the season to contribute to Meridian’s seventh-place score of 97.5 at Saturday’s 14-team Ithaca Yellowjacket Wrestling Tournament.

Chesaning won the meet with a 216, while Hemlock was sixth at 114 and Breckenridge tied for 13th with 11 points.

Both Wetherell (125 pounds) and Solano (160) went 3-1 to finish fifth, with Solano earning his medal in his first taste of varsity competition.

“I don’t want it to overshadow some of the other guys, but it’s good to get some variety with your lineup,” Meridian co-coach Corey Robinson said of the Mustangs looking to their depth while missing a couple starters due to injury.

Brandon Avery (140) went 2-1 to take runner-up honors at his weight class, while Travis McDonald (119) went 3-1 to finish third, Paul Streeter (171) went 2-1 to finish third, Cam Houtman (112) went 2-1 to place fifth, Greg Cowlings (152) contributed a pair of wins, and Jacob LaFevor (130) and Brandon Drouin (215) had one win each.

“We got six medals from the 12 guys we sent,” said Robinson. “Medaling half your guys on any given day is a good day in my book.”

The Mustangs travel to Farwell to wrestle the host Eagles and Houghton Lake on Wednesday.
