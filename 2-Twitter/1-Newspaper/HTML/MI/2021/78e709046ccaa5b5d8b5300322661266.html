Wilber Township wildfire caused by target shooting

WILBER Twp. – The East Tawas Fire Department (ETFD), Tawas City Fire Department (TCFD) and U.S. Forest Service (USFS) were called to the scene of a wildfire on Kobs Road, south of Davison Road, in Section 34 of Wilber Township.

ETFD Chief Bill Deckett says the incident occurred on Sunday, at 3:09 p.m. “Witnesses on scene stated that they were target shooting and a small fire started under a metal target.”

They attempted to extinguish the fire with sand, but were not successful. So 9-1-1 was notified, who then dispatched the fire departments and Iosco County EMS.

The arriving fire units flanked the fire with hand lines and hand tools, until a dozer from the USFS completed a fire line around the burning area, Deckett stated.

The total area burned was less than two acres.

The ETFD and TCFD cleared the scene at about 4:30 p.m., with USFS units remaining on scene to complete final containment, mop-up and extinguishment.

Deckett says that there was no damage to any structures, and there were no injuries.

In addition to EMS and the aforementioned fire crews, the Oscoda Township Fire Department also manned ETFD’s station, to cover both Tawas City and East Tawas, while their members were dealing with the wildfire.
