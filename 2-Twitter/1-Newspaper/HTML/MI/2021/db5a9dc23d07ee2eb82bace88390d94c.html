NOW earns ‘Freshwater Heroes’ award

  • Jenny Haglund
  • May 11, 2021
  • May 11, 2021 Updated May 11, 2021

OSCODA – The efforts of the Oscoda-based Need Our Water (NOW) community action group have been recognized by the Freshwater Future organization.

During an online ceremony – fittingly held on Earth Day, April 22 – NOW was among the several other clean water activists to receive a 2020 Freshwater Heroes Award.

Along with pressing the local, state and federal government, NOW has consistently pushed the Air Force (AF) and others for faster and more comprehensive cleanup of sites on and around the former Wurtsmith Air Force Base (WAFB) in Oscoda.

As reported, the AF utilized aqueous film forming foam – containing per- and polyfluoroalkyl substances (PFAS) – at WAFB, for training activities and to extinguish fires.

The former base was established in 1923 and, although it has officially been closed since 1993, PFAS have continued to seep from WAFB, decades later.

“As you know, working to protect and restore our precious waters – drinking water, lakes, rivers, wetlands, and shorelines is hard work. But it is also very rewarding,” reads the latest issue of Freshwater Future’s newsletter, “Freshwater Voices.”

It outlines the 2020 award winners, while also highlighting and celebrating the work of these everyday people who jump into action to solve some of the most pressing water issues across the Great Lakes region.

“These individuals and organizations represent a dedicated group of water protectors,” the information continues. “Many groups and individuals receiving our Hero Awards had significant victories in their work to protect water resources in 2020.

“Others are being applauded for the many years of ongoing efforts. Freshwater Heroes never give up on doing what’s right for our waters and communities.

“In every community around the Great Lakes, you’ll find thoughtful, committed residents taking action to protect our lakes, rivers, streams, wetlands, shorelines, and drinking water. Small, individual actions can make a big difference, and at Freshwater Future, we’re inspired by those who devote so much of their time to making things better for their communities and all of us.

“These heroes provide seemingly unlimited energy and passion for their causes without asking for recognition, because it is in their hearts and it is the right thing to do. We salute these Freshwater Future Heroes and all of the thousands of citizens taking time from their busy lives to make the Great Lakes and our communities better places for each of us.”

Accepting the award on behalf of NOW was one its members, Anthony Spaniola, an attorney and Oscoda property owner.

Freshwater Future states in its newsletter that back in 2010, the “forever chemical” PFAS was detected at WAFB, contaminating residential drinking water wells, area surface water and shorelines. 

While numerous studies and investigations had been conducted, those from Freshwater Future say that it took the help of the community-driven group NOW, organized in 2017, to push for action on a long-term cleanup plan.

“NOW continues to advocate and hold government agencies accountable. By becoming a voice for the Oscoda community and organizing with other groups who share their concerns, this group has created a strong movement that has captured the attention of decision-makers and is working with Michigan’s congressional delegation to press for quicker action and funding,” the summary goes on. “We commend NOW’s continued diligent efforts to bring a spotlight to PFAS issues at the local, state, and national levels. This group ensures that the community knows what’s happening and clean-up actions move forward.”

While the 2020 awards celebration was held live in a virtual format, the ceremony was recorded and can be viewed at https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8VJXBkFqxHA.

“Now more than ever, we need to take the time to recognize some of the positive things happening to benefit our communities,” Freshwater Future stated, in reference to the ceremony. “This short event will inspire you and give you hope as we share stories of a few people, organizations, and communities all around the Great Lakes region that are making a difference.”

Along with NOW, awards were also presented to several individuals who have made significant contributions in the fight for clean water, including those who have stepped up during the Flint water crisis. Also receiving awards were such groups as the Michigan Citizens for Water Conservation, Toledo Water Council and Friends of Kingston Inner Harbour, Ontario.

The mission of Freshwater Future – which has helped thousands of community-based groups and individuals since 1996 – is to ensure the healthy future of the waters in the Great Lakes region.

According to its website, Freshwater Future is the only watershed-wide organization dedicated solely to supporting the needs of community-based groups and actions working to protect and restore Great Lakes land and water resources.

“The groups we help work at the local community level where large environmental organizations cannot,” the site reads. “It is at the local community level where thousands of decisions are made every day that impact the health of our waters and our communities.”

For more information, visit www.freshwaterfuture.org.
