'Baby, it's cold outside' float quite appropriate at Hurley Christmas Parade

  • Updated: Dec. 07, 2013, 5:30 p.m.|
  • Published: Dec. 07, 2013, 4:30 p.m.
By
  • Gareth Clary | GulfLive.com

HURLEY, Mississippi -- Snowmen, penguins and a large polar bear meandered through the streets of Hurley Saturday morning on a perfect day for snowmen, penguins and polar bears.

The Jackson County Horsemen's Association float managed to capture what was on the mind of the all of the bundled-up folks who attended the annual Hurley Christmas Parade.

The float was led by an inflatable snowman -- pumped with air in a way to simulate shivering -- with a fitting sign that read, "Baby, it's cold outside."

Many people remained in their warm vehicles until the parade started moving and others sought warmth at Creations and More Coffee Shop, where business was quite brisk.

The main parade route along Miss. 614 and Miss. 613 was lined with families and friends, as children stocked up on candy and got to see Santa Claus.

The theme of the parade was "Christmas Past and Present."
