ALPINE, Miss. (WTVA)  Union County Sheriff Jimmy Edwards says autopsies will be performed Sunday on two bodies found in the backyard of a man wanted in connection with the abduction of a mother and her three daughters.

According to Edwards, those bodies were discovered late Friday or early Saturday behind the home of 35-year-old Adam Mayes. 

Mayes is accused of abducting 31-year-old Jo Ann Bain of Hardeman County, Tenn., and her daughters: 14-year-old Adrienne, 12-year-old Alexandria and 8-year-old Kyliyah. 

The four were reported missing on April 27.  Law enforcement says Mayes was last seen in Guntown on May 1.  His home is located in the Alpine area, which is between Baldwyn and New Albany.

The sheriff adds that law enforcement knows the identities on the bodies but cannot release that information yet.

Edwards says a command post is being set up at the Alpine Volunteer Fire Department as the investigation proceeds.

ALPINE, Miss. (WTVA) -- From the very beginning, officials have stressed that members of a Tennessee family abducted last month may be in "extreme danger."

Now in the wake of the discovery of two bodies in Union County, the search continues for Mayes and any of the family members who may still be with him.

The Union County Sheriff's Department confirmed Sunday afternoon that a manhunt is underway in the Union County area and other parts of north Mississippi for Adam Mayes.
