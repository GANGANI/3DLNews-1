Laurel

  • LAUREL MEMORIAL DAY- Memorial Day services at Laurel American Legion Post 19, on Monday morning, concluded with the “Placing of the Wreath” by Post 19 Commander Richard Elliott (center), Ladies Auxiliary President Trudy Parker (right) and Post 19 Commander of Sons of American Legion Jeff Shockley. Photo by Tony Windsor
  • TAPS- Inclement weather late Monday morning, May 27 resulted in the American Legion Post 19 holding its traditional “Playing of the Taps,” under cover of the Post porch. The Taps are held at the close of the annual Memorial Day services to honor those military members who lost their lives in service to the country. Post 19 member Donald Gootee, was chosen to lead this year’s playing of the Taps. Photo by Tony Windsor
  • Representatives of the Laurel Women’s Civic Club attended the Spring FFA Banquet and were presented a certificate of appreciation for the club’s support of the Laurel Chapter. Pictured are Laurel Women’s Civic Club members Maggie Proctor and Laina Betts-Dunn, accepting a certificate from Laurel FFA President Briana Spradlin. Submitted photo
  • Pictured are Nanticoke Rotary members Rob Hemmen and Rob Harman after finishing the set up at Phillips Signs. Submitted photo
  • GRADUATES - Community leaders throughout the state have had the opportunity to participate in the Mid-Atlantic Community Development Institute’s intensive training, which began in July of 2022. Pictured are the nine members of the inaugural cohort who have completed all three years and graduated on Thursday, May 2. Sussex County graduates are Daniel Wilson, Lynne Betts and Brentdy Chavez. Following a rigorous application process, Betts has also been approved to sit for the National Community Development Council’s PCED exam in June.
