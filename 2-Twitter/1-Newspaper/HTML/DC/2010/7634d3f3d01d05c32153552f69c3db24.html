The central theme of the Dec. 25 front-page story "Obama launches a charm offensive" appears to be that President Obama should strive to be more like President Clinton so that Democratic funders and Republican legislators do not have bruised egos.

Let's see. Clinton is thoroughly outgoing, and he gave us "don't ask, don't tell," failed to deliver on health-care reform and got impeached by the Republicans he wooed. Obama is more insular by nature, but has managed to accomplish a massive expansion of health care and eliminate "don't ask, don't tell," and is wringing tough compromises from the Republicans whose feelings are purportedly being hurt.

I'll take the introvert who is evidently sustained by his ties to close friends and family, disdains trivial social interaction and manages to get important things done.

Eric R. Glitzenstein, Washington
