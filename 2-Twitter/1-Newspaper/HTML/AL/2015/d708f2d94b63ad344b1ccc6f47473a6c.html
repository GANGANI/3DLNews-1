HISTORIC NIGHT: Beauregard secures perfect regular season with win

  • Oct 29, 2015 Updated Jul 15, 2020

SEALE – Beauregard head football coach Rob Carter thought before the season that the Hornets would be pretty strong this year. The players believed it, too.

But perfect? Undefeated? Historic?

"Never in a million years did we think all of that," linebacker A.J. Tate said.

It's all a reality after the Hornets' 82-36 win against host Russell County on Thursday night.

Beauregard (9-0, 6-0) clinched its first undefeated regular season in program history — in style, too. The 82 points set a school record for most points in a single game. The previous record was 70, which was set in 2013 and tied earlier this season.

"It all hasn't sunk in yet," Carter said. "It won't really hit us until we have the time to reflect on what we accomplished."

Added Tate, "It's something special. It really is."

The best part?

"Just being part of the team," Tate said, "that can say it made history."

The game followed the same script of all the Hornets' other games this season: score early, score a lot, play good defense in the second half and win.

Beauregard scored a touchdown on its first 11 possessions.

There were the usual offensive stars. Quarterback Quay Wright was 6-for-6 passing for 144 yards and four touchdowns. La'Damian Webb ran for 154 yards and four touchdowns on 14 carries. Jacob Callaway added 125 yards and a pair of scores on the ground on 12 carries. Malik Gibson caught two touchdown passes and ran for another score.

Despite being winless, Russell County (0-10, 0-7) put up a fight early.

Beauregard led 54-30 at halftime. While the Hornets maintained a double-figure advantage since early in the first quarter, it wasn't exactly the kind of start Beauregard's defense had in mind.

Maybe it was a case of underestimating the opponent. Maybe it was a case of thinking the game was won before it started. Whatever it was, Tate vowed it won't be happening again.

"Never," Tate said. "We chose the wrong game to get a big head. It was a good wake-up call."

Indeed. After all, there is much still to be accomplished. Beauregard will host a very athletic Williamson (4-5, 3-3) team next week in a first-round playoff game.

So what does a group that just accomplished something none of the school’s football teams from previous years do next?

Focus on achieving something else that hasn’t been accomplished, of course.

"This," Tate said, "isn't the only thing we are setting this year."
