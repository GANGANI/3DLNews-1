Where to ring in the New Year in Richmond

  • Colleen Curran

Colleen Curran

The countdown to New Year’s Eve has officially begun.  3 … 2 … 1 … happy new year!

But first, you’ve got to plan it. We’ve got some ideas for you.

The big news is that RVA NYE, the huge party that was held on Brown’s Island last year, is moving indoors to the Siegel Center.

Last year’s event was a huge success, drawing 14,000 people for music, dancing, fire shows and a giant countdown to the new year. But it was a bit chilly at 28 degrees.

This year, you can expect the same kind of festive atmosphere with DJs, dancing, an impressive light show and a countdown on the Siegel Center’s multimillion-dollar video screens.

The biggest difference is that it’s ticketed and indoors. Also, 21 and older only.

R. Anthony Harris, founder of RVA Magazine, threw the first Carytown New Year’s Eve event in 2006. He’s partnering with Jimmy Budd and Hayden Fisher of Fisher+Budd Ventures to host this year’s event.

Harris sees a citywide New Year’s Eve celebration as “a valuable, cultural event. When it’s successful, there’s nothing else like it.”

Organizers will turn the video screens into a massive New Year’s Eve ball and tower. They’ll also be building a 360-degree stage underneath the screens for the performers.

“The idea is that people can walk around the stage, dance and have fun,” Harris said.

Beer, spirits and food will be available for sale, and there will be a VIP area with express bar lines, a complimentary champagne toast and other goodies.

“There is something about New Year’s (Eve) that can set the vibe for the whole year,” Harris said. “I feel like it reverberates and carries over into the new year.”

But a word to the wise: The Siegel Center has a capacity of 6,000 people. If you want to go to this shindig, we suggest you get your tickets in advance.

***

Want a little fire in your New Year’s Eve celebration?

The Party Liberation Front, a group of visual, fire and performing artists, will host a New Year’s Eve party at Diversity Richmond, and it’s calling the event Decemberween.

You might know the PLF as the fire performers at the popular Gallery 5 events, where they juggle and spin fire in the streets. They also manned the secondary stage at last year’s RVA NYE event on Brown’s Island.

Organizers have held Decemberween as a smaller, more underground New Year’s Eve party over the years. But this year, they’re going big and public.

“Decemberween will fuse a dance party, circus show, art and community gathering into one night of new year’s revelry,” said Conway Jennings, PLF founder and Decemberween organizer. “(W)e hope to share this unique experience with a broader contingent of RVA’s music and art lovers.”

The event also is a fundraiser for Art on Wheels and PLF.

It will be held at Diversity Richmond, which offers 10,000 square feet of space, including an outdoor area for the PLF fire show and a sprawling indoor space for DJs, belly dancers and circus performers.

Josephine the Colossal Skeleton Marionette, a 20-foot-tall metal skeleton that appeared at the base of Burning Man 2015, will make an appearance at the event. This is her first East Coast appearance, and it’s up to partygoers to make her dance.

There will be a bar serving beer from Hardywood, wine, mixed drinks and champagne.

Consider taking a nap earlier in the day because the event is scheduled to run until 4 a.m.

Decemberween will max out at 450 people.

***

GWAR is playing a New Year’s Eve show at The National called Midnight in the GWARden of New Year’s Evil.

It’s an old-school GWAR show, reminiscent of the shows the heavy-metal band used to play around town at Christmas and New Year’s.

“We will ring in the new year by killing the old year and bringing in the baby new year in the most excruciating way possible, ensuring that it will be a miserable year for everyone,” said Blothar, lead singer for GWAR. “Actually, it will be a blast.”

GWAR will headline the event along with performances from Kepone, Occultist and Guns and Roses cover band Rocket Queen. VIP tickets will include dinner for two at GWAR bar and a meet-and-greet with GWAR band members.

If blood and gore aren’t your bag, there are other music choices around town.

Richmond’s favorite yacht rock band, Three Sheets to the Wind, will host a prom party for New Year’s Eve at The Broadberry. Dress accordingly (as if you’re about to board a yacht), and you could be crowned king or queen of the prom.

The Camel will have bluegrass from Jackass Flats.

And Siné Irish Pub will have a double New Year’s Eve celebration with a heated tent in the street and two toasts: one for Ireland and one for the U.S.

***

You don’t have to party till the break of dawn to welcome the new year.

If you’ve got little ones, you can “roll in the new year” at the New Year’s Eve Family Frolic at Lewis Ginter Botanical Garden. It’s a New Year’s Eve tradition to “roll in” the new year by tumbling down the big hill next to the conservatory.

There also will be a concert by the Ululating Mummies; craft activities, such as make-your-own New Year’s party hat; and photo opportunities with the GardenKeeper. Visitors to Family Frolic are welcome to stick around to enjoy GardenFest of Lights.

The Children’s Museum of Richmond will have its annual juice-box toast at noon at all locations, as well as other activities for youngsters.

You can even “skate in” the new year at outdoor ice rinks.

RVA On Ice, the downtown outdoor ice rink, will be open on New Year’s Eve and New Year’s Day, as well as The Rink at West Broad Village in Short Pump, the rink at Stony Point Fashion Park and the Illuminate Light Show at Meadow Event Park.

Happy new year, everybody!
