Decatur police: Man beat girlfriend unconscious with shovel, boards, fists

  • Jaylyn Cook
  • Dec 21, 2018
  • Dec 21, 2018
  • 0
  • Facebook
  • Twitter
  • WhatsApp
  • SMS
  • Email
  • Print
  • Save

DECATUR — A 29-year-old man was arrested Thursday after police said he beat his girlfriend unconscious using a shovel, boards and his fists.

The man faces preliminary charges of aggravated domestic battery, domestic battery with priors and unlawful restraint, which are subject to review by the Macon County State's Attorney's Office. 

In a sworn police affidavit, the 34-year-old woman told officers that the incident happened Dec. 16, in a house they share. In addition to hitting her with the objects, the affidavit said, the man also dragged her around the house by her hair and threw her against the wall. 

The woman said the man dragged her into the basement at one point and left her tied to a chair for 10 to 15 minutes. He later put a sheet around her neck, the affidavit said, and choked her using it and his hands, causing her to black out multiple times.

The affidavit said the man threatened to kill the woman multiple times and aimed a saw at her. In the days after incident, the woman told police the man struck her in the jaw several times a day and punched her in the nose, causing it to bleed. 

Photographs taken of the woman's injuries showed that she had large bruises on her arms, legs, lower back and other areas of her body, the affidavit said, and a bruise near the middle of her back was shaped like a shovel.

The man was being held in the Macon County Jail on Friday on $200,000 bond. He is scheduled to appear in court on Jan. 22.
