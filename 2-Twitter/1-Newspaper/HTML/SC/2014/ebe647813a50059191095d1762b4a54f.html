Gullah elders are 'singing the old songs' at Spoleto Festival USA

  • By Chelsea Grinstead cgrinstead@postandcourier.com
  • May 28, 2024

A once-in-a-lifetime compilation of Gullah songs and stories collected on St. Helena Island becomes a lavish concert of modern jazz and chamber music for Spoleto Festival USA 2024. “Lowcountry: South Carolina Music in the Gullah Tradition” premieres June 2 at the College of Charleston Cistern Yard, a culmination of years of archival documentation and the first orchestral iteration of the 2023 album “Lowcountry.”

Black faces painted white portray American West stereotypes in ‘Dark Noon’ at Spoleto

  • By Chelsea Grinstead cgrinstead@postandcourier.com
  • May 16, 2024

With the staging of “Dark Noon” during this year’s Spoleto Festival USA, theater lovers can experience another plane where cultural identities fold in on themselves to evoke a story that is as true as it is strange. “Dark Noon” takes place at Festival Hall in downtown Charleston on May 31, June 1 and June 2.
