Bell, Newby produce for Stallions, Riley’s Commanders win in AAF week four

  • Landon Wirt

    Landon Wirt

    Senior Sports Editor

    • Author email
  • March 5, 2019
  • March 5, 2019

Several former Huskers continued to produce solid numbers in week four of the Alliance of American Football.

Terrell Newby posted the strongest performance of his Stallions career on a snowy Saturday night in Salt Lake, while Josh Banderas failed to make an impact on the Stallions’ defense.

In other week-four action, the Memphis Express picked up its first win of the season against the San Diego Fleet. Memphis’ defense forced four turnovers, and former LSU quarterback Zach Mettenberger threw for 174 yards and one touchdown to guide the Express offense.

Mettenberger’s lone touchdown pass came in the fourth quarter with the Express down 23-15 and he proceeded to run in the two-point conversion to tie the game at 23.

The game’s biggest story, however, was the play of Memphis’ kicker. After Memphis released kicker Josh Jasper earlier in the week, they signed former Kentucky Wildcat Austin MacGinnis—who was working as a financial advisor in Lexington, Kentucky when the Express offered him the starting job.

MacGinnis didn’t let the Express down, drilling four field goals including the go-ahead 45-yard kick with 2:46 remaining to give the Express a 26-23 victory.

While defensive reigned supreme in week four, all of the week’s action featured close, exciting games. Most importantly, former Huskers continued to have big impacts in three of the four contests.

Kieron Williams, Arizona Hotshots

After recording two tackles in week three, Williams failed to make an impact at safety for the Hotshots in week four.

Arizona, the No. 1 team in the West Division, fell 14-11 to the formerly winless Atlanta Legends. Former Georgia Bulldog quarterback Aaron Murray threw for 254 yards for the Legends, while former Michigan standout Denard Robinson ran in Atlanta’s only touchdown of the game.

Atlanta kicker Younghoe Koo, best remembered for missing crucial kicks for the Los Angeles Chargers in weeks one and two of the 2017 NFL season, kicked a 33-yard field goal with 1:07 remaining in the fourth quarter to seal Atlanta’s first AAF victory. Koo is 8-8 on field goals this season.

NEXT UP: The Hotshots will look to break their two-game losing streak when they host the San Antonio Commanders on Sunday, March 10, at 7 p.m. on NFL Network.

Terrell Newby, Salt Lake Stallions

Newby’s role in Salt Lake’s offense is expanding on a weekly basis, and his week-four performance against the undefeated Orlando Apollos was the strongest of his young AAF career.

While Newby is Salt Lake’s third option out of the backfield, he amassed 15 yards on two carries. He also totaled two receptions for 17 yards.

The most important play of Newby's AAF career came in the third quarter with the Stallions trailing Orlando 14-9. After a Joel Bouagnon rushing touchdown, Newby found the end zone on the two-point conversion with a reception from quarterback Josh Woodrum to cut Orlando’s lead to 14-11.

The Stallions failed to complete the comeback, as their offense stalled in the fourth quarter and Orlando scored another touchdown to seal a 20-11 victory.

NEXT UP: The Stallions hope to rebound from a disappointing home defeat when they travel to San Diego to battle the Fleet on Saturday, March 9, at 7 p.m. on NFL Network.

De’Mornay Pierson-El, Salt Lake Stallions

Pierson-El’s production fell in week four compared to a team-high 90 yards receiving in week three. Pierson-El only managed 24 yards on two receptions, but the snowy conditions in Salt Lake City Saturday night were less than ideal for receivers.

Salt Lake’s offense struggled with the poor conditions against the Apollos, as the Stallions stalled on key drives. Orlando quarterback Garrett Gilbert wasn’t hindered by the snow, as his 244 passing yards and one touchdown helped the Apollos remain undefeated.

Kenny Bell, Salt Lake Stallions

Bell built on a strong week three with another productive receiving performance in week four, totaling 25 yards on three receptions. Bell will look for his role in the Stallion offense to continue growing as the season progresses.

Josh Banderas, Salt Lake Stallions

For the third-consecutive week, Banderas saw action on special teams and defense but did not record any tackles.

Alonzo Moore, San Antonio Commanders

San Antonio quarterback Logan Woodside barely cracked 100 yards passing against the Birmingham Iron in week four, and Moore’s receiving production suffered for the second-straight week.

Moore only caught one pass for 18 yards and rushed one time for six yards in the Commanders’ 12-11 victory against the undefeated Birmingham Iron.

NEXT UP: The Commanders will look to build on the momentum from Sunday’s victory against the Iron when they travel to face the Arizona Hotshots on Sunday, March 10, in a crucial West Division showdown. The game starts at 7 p.m. and will be shown on NFL Network.

Mike Riley, San Antonio Commanders

Riley turned in the best coaching performance of his AAF career against the Iron, limiting one of the best teams in the league to just 11 points.

San Antonio’s defense was dominant against the Iron, forcing four turnovers and limiting Birmingham running back Trent Richardson to 15 yards on eight carries. After giving up 31 points to the Fleet in week three, the Commanders rallied to turn in their best defensive performance of the season.

Despite San Antonio’s strong defense, the Commander offense struggled throughout the game. Woodside threw for a mere 106 yards, so San Antonio turned to the ground game to boost its offense. Running back Kenneth Farrow led the Commanders with 142 yards rushing, and fellow backfield member Trey Williams ran for San Antonio’s lone touchdown in the third quarter.

After a Nick Rose field goal extended San Antonio’s lead to 12-3 in the fourth quarter, Iron quarterback Luis Perez quickly marched Birmingham down the field. Richardson capped the drive with a one-yard touchdown but even after Perez found wide receiver DeVozea Felton for the two-point conversion, the Iron remained down 12-11.

However, Birmingham was unable to complete the onside conversion, and the Commanders held on to win 12-11, snapping San Antonio’s two-game losing streak.
