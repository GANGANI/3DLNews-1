Red-hot Frenchman leads Winnebago over N-O

  • By TOM BEHMER

  • Dec 28, 2016

Highlights from Winnebago's 94-67 win over Neligh-Oakdale.

    • Facebook
    • Twitter
    • WhatsApp
    • SMS
    • Email
    • Print
    • Save

    WISNER — Winnebago’s shooting ability wasn’t a surprise to Neligh-Oakdale in their first-round matchup at the Shootout on the Elkhorn here Wednesday.

    What the Class C No. 4 Warriors weren’t expecting, however, was the No. 2 Indians’ main source of offense while turning a 53-44 lead into a 94-67 win.

    “I kept turning around to my assistant coach, Ethan Larsen, and I was like, ‘Who’s No. 11?’ " Neligh-Oakdale coach Frank Gade said. “We were worried about their other guys because they can shoot the lights out of the ball. But give No. 11 credit, he stepped up.”

    No. 11 was sophomore T.J. Frenchman, who scored 19 of his game-high 24 points after the Warriors had trimmed a 17-point first-half deficit to nine on back-to-back 3-pointers by Alex Kerkman and Grant White three minutes into the third quarter.

    Frenchman finished a perfect 7 of 7 from the field, including a 5 of 5 effort from beyond the 3-point line, and was 5 of 5 from the free-throw line, too. He answered the Neligh-Oakdale 3s with a three-point play on an offensive rebound to push the lead back to double digits. He then knocked down four 3s — including one while being fouled — to ignite a 19-4 run that had Winnebago ahead 77-52 with 6:05 left in the game.

    The closest the Warriors got the rest of the way was 23.

    “That’s all he (Frenchman) does is shoot. He was a ball boy as a little-bitty guy all the way up. He lives right down the street from the court and just walks down and shoots,” Winnebago coach Jeff Berridge said. “So I know he can shoot. It’s just building his confidence.”

    In the first half, Winnebago’s damage came via the ability of junior point guard D’Von LaPointe to turn defense into offense.

    LaPointe scored 19 of his 20 points in the first half, turning three steals into layups as the Indians jumped out to a 17-2 lead with 4:12 left in the first quarter. And after Neligh-Oakdale had pulled within 23-13 at the start of the second quarter, LaPointe scored 11 more first-half points as Winnebago extended its lead with 6-7 all-state senior David Wingett on the bench in foul trouble.

    “We always try to start fast, and tonight we did that,” Berridge said. “D’Von stepped up. He’s a proven player, and he can do that any given night.”

    Wingett finished with 17 points and 13 rebounds while also sitting out the opening three minutes of the fourth quarter with four fouls. The Indians, coming off a 97-86 loss to Class A Omaha Central, improved to 8-1.

    “Teams are focused on stopping him (David) at all costs, and it’s hard for him to get free,” Berridge said. “It’s good to have all the weapons we have, especially with young guys who can shoot the ball.”

    Neligh-Oakdale’s third-quarter run was sparked by the play of senior point guard Tyson Belitz, who had four assists in the third quarter — kicking out to both White and Kerkman for 3s to pull the Warriors within single digits. Belitz finished with 20 points, 12 rebounds and seven assists.

    “Tyson makes us go. He can take anybody off the dribble,” Gade said.

    White followed with 19 points and three assists, and Kerkman added 17 points as Neligh-Oakdale drops to 6-1 with its first loss of the year.

    “It was obvious to everyone in the gym at the start, it was like we were playing all-stars. We were tripping there for a couple minutes, and I told them that was at least 10 points right there,” Gade said. “This will help us going forward. We had spurts where we played really well. We just didn’t do it for four quarters.”
