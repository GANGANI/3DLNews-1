    By Hartford Courant
    PUBLISHED: December 21, 2016 at 6:00 a.m. | UPDATED: December 6, 2018 at 12:11 a.m.

    For good or bad, there’s no denying 2016 was a year we won’t soon forget. The roller coaster nature of the year was evident when we looked back at what stories you, the readers, found most interesting. Donald Trump’s polarizing first visit to Connecticut? It’s there. An Easter egg hunt disrupted by unruly parents? You bet. A heartwarming reunion? Of course.

    Take a look back at the year that was, and see what our readers found to be the most compelling stories of 2016.

    25. West Hartford Dentist Charged With Selling Oxycodone

    This story may have originally garnered attention thanks to Dr. Peter G. Delaney’s interesting sartorial choice, but the details of the case are just as gripping. Delaney was accused of writing prescriptions for pain killers to people who did not obtain treatment at his office. Read more.

    24. Police Identify Simsbury Woman Killed In Avon Mountain Crash

    Whenever the words “Avon Mountain crash” are heard together, thoughts immediately turn to the tragic 2005 crash that left four dead and injured 19. This crash, in May, killed a 33-year-old woman and shut down the main artery during the busy evening commute. Read more.

    23. Canton Couple Found Dead Tuesday Remembered For Lifetime Of Advocacy


    Few couples were more well-known throughout the Farmington River Valley than Louis and Judith Friedman. The pair, known for their tireless environmental advocacy, were found dead in their Canton home in late July. Louis was 81, Judith was 80. Read more.

    22. Meriden Mosque Shooter Seeks Forgiveness From Muslim Community

    A man who fired several shots into a Meriden mosque in November 2015 reached out and apologized to his Muslim neighbors in 2016. Read more.

    21. UConn Student From West Hartford Fatally Struck By Campus Fire Truck


    The UConn community was rocked in October, when student Jeffny Pally was struck and killed by an emergency vehicle. Pally, 19, was seated with her back against the bay door when it opened. The SUV hit her on its way out. Read more.

    20. Malloy Proposes Deep Cuts in School Funding, With 28 Districts Losing All Money

    Gov. Dannel P. Malloy spent much of 2016 trying to find ways to close the now-$1.45 billion deficit. One suggestion in April did not go over well – deep cuts to cost-sharing grants for 28 of the state’s wealthiest school districts. Read more.

    19. Aetna To Cut Workforce, Reduce Work-At-Home Policy


    Aetna has long been viewed as one of the most flexible employers in the country — but that’s about to change. Starting next year, employees who live within 50 miles of an office are expected to make the commute, a stark change from Aetna’s policy that allowed employees to work from home. Read more.

    18. Political Leaders Blast Ad That Links William Petit To Trump And ‘Attack On Women And Families’

    The caustic nature of the national election spilled over to local politics in October. An ad paid for by Labor United for Connecticut linked William Petit to Donald Trump’s “attack on women and families.” Petit, who won election to the state House, is the lone survivor of the 2007 Cheshire home invasion that killed his wife and two daughters. Read more.

    17. PEZ Easter Egg Hunt Canceled After Parents Cause Chaos

    An Easter tradition in Orange had to be abruptly canceled in March due to unruly parents. Staff said that parents rushed the field after ignoring staff instructions to wait. Reports from parents told tales of bloody noses and broken baskets. Read more.

    16. Transgender Teacher Steps Out To Students

    When the North Carolina “bathroom bill” was signed into law in March, Avon High School teacher Dusty Rader decided to come out to his students as transgender. Rader, from Glastonbury, said he felt it was important to let people, especially young people, know that it’s OK to be transgender. Read more.

    15. Op-Ed: The Day Bernie Got Me Off The Hook

    In February, as Bernie Sanders continued his unlikely challenge to Hillary Clinton in the primaries, readers got a taste of what Bernie did as mayor of Burlington, Vermont. The writer, a West Hartford resident, told a story about how Sanders, the newly elected mayor, helped him avoid a felony for a noise violation when he was in college. Read more.

    14. Reputed Mobster’s Associate Adds New Mystery To Gardner Museum Art Heist


    The 1990 robbery of Boston’s Isabella Stewart Gardner Museum remains one of the greatest unsolved mysteries in history, but Hartford gangster Robert Gentile could hold the key, an associate told the Courant in an interview in January of this year. Read more.

    13. Layoffs Continue As Malloy Prepares to Release New Budget

    In April, as Gov. Dannel P. Malloy prepared to announce his revised budget, layoffs began to hit the ranks of state workers. Malloy said as many as 2,000 workers could be laid off. Read more.

    12. Glastonbury Police Chief To Retire After ‘Lapse In Judgment’ On Nude Emails

    A four-decade career in law enforcement came to an abrupt end in December after Glastonbury police Chief David A. Caron announced his retirement when the Courant discovered he used his official town email address to send and receive nude photos. One, dated Nov. 14, contained a headline and caption that could be considered racially offensive. Read more.

    11. Missing Coltsville Plaque Found Chopped Into Nearly Two Dozen Pieces


    An iconic piece of Hartford history was destroyed late this summer. The bronze crest on the Samuel Colt monument at the entrance of Colt Park was discovered missing on Sept. 11. It was found several days later chopped in nearly two dozen pieces. Read more.

    10. Trump Brings Fiery Message To Thousands In Hartford

    The Trump Train made its first stop in Connecticut April 15 in Hartford. The rally was ahead of the Republican primary, and not only did Donald Trump bring his legions of supporters, but he brought throngs of protesters outside the Connecticut Convention Center. Read more.

    9. Nearly Killed In 1998 Fire, ECSU Graduate Reunites With Hartford Cop Who Saved Her

    It’s one of the most iconic photos in the Courant archives. ln 1998, Al Chaniewski snapped a picture of 5-year-old Josibelk Aponte cradled in now-retired Hartford police detective Peter Getz’s arms after a fire. This year, 18 years after that picture was taken, the two reunited as Aponte, now 23, graduated magna cum laude from Eastern Connecticut State University. Read more.

    8. After Marathon Filibuster, Murphy Says Senate Will Hold Vote On Gun Legislation

    Compelled to act by the Orlando nightclub shooting, U.S. Sen. Chris Murphy delivered an impassioned, 15-hour filibuster with the goal of enacting common-sense gun legislation that would hinder future tragedies. Murphy’s stand fueled round-the-clock social media discussion, right up until he finally yielded the Senate floor with an emotional coda: With a photo of Sandy Hook victim Dylan Hockley as his backdrop, Murphy recounted the first-grader’s death in the arms of his beloved aide, Anne Marie Murphy. Read more.

    7. From Jail To Yale: Ex-Offender Graduates With Law Degree 10 Years After Release

    At 16, Reginald Dwayne Betts went to jail for carjacking. At 35, he graduated from Yale Law School. The intervening years won’t define him, he says. Instead, he hopes he can lean on the experience of his incarceration to help pull others out of those same depths. “We think of people in the system, but we don’t think of how to get them out from under the thumb of the system,” Betts said. Read more.

    6. Warrant: ‘This Is How Joseph Silva Does It,’ Killer Boasts In Hartford Double Homicide

    In a chilling piece of testimony, a witness to a gruesome double homicide in Hartford said they heard the killer proclaim, “This is how Joseph Silva does it” as he unloaded as many as five rounds into one of his victims on that May night. He proceeded to fatally shoot the victim’s girlfriend, who sat waiting in a nearby car. Hartford police called the incident the boiling point for what was an ongoing feud. Read more.

    5. Nathan Carman: Grandfather Was ‘Like A Father To Me’

    The Courant drew the ire of one of its subjects in September when Nathan Carman — who was lost at sea for eight days in a boat wreck that presumably claimed the life of his mother, Linda — took issue with a report that said he was a suspect in the 2013 shooting death of his grandfather. Windsor police had issued an arrest warrant for Carman on a murder charge that went unsigned by the prosecutor, but it adds a layer of intrigue to one of 2016’s enduring mysteries. Read more.

    4. General Electric Moving Headquarters To Boston

    In a blow to the state’s already struggling economy, General Electric Co. announced its intention to move to Boston in January, abandoning its 42-year-old Fairfield headquarters. More lucrative offers from New York were spurned in favor of what GE’s leadership viewed as an innovation and technology hub in Boston, which is also home to top-tier academic institutions. “This hurts,” said Gov. Dannel P. Malloy. Read more.

    3. Malloy Bypassed Airport Security Checkpoint With Son’s Backpack

    Controversy swirled in July when surveillance footage of Gov. Dannel P. Malloy bypassing a federal security checkpoint at Bradley International Airport while carrying a backpack belonging to his son surfaced. The footage, shot in November 2015, showed Malloy carrying the red backpack on his shoulder through a side door within the Bradley terminal to which airport security personnel admitted him. Malloy apologized, saying the family was merely in a hurry, but airport personnel objected to their perceived special treatment. Read more.

    2. Sources: Student Pilot Was Distraught, Fought With Instructor; Road Reopened

    A dramatic mid-air struggle led to a fiery crash on a busy East Hartford street in October, comprising one of 2016’s most intriguing stories and the second-most popular one among readers. According to a law enforcement source, Feras M. Freitekh, the plane’s student pilot, began to fly the plane erratically, which prompted his instructor, Arian Prevalla, to fight for control of the plane. The ensuing crash killed Freitekh and left Prevalla burned. Read more.

    1. Video Shows State Police Discussing How To Charge Protester

    Amid simmering tension between police and the public, it’s no surprise that a story like this was our most-read in 2016. A protester stood legally armed at a DUI checkpoint, warning oncoming drivers of the police presence ahead. After a lively exchange the protester captured on video, troopers can be heard deliberating about what, if any, charges they could bring against the man, playing right into his stunt. One trooper’s ill-advised plea to his colleagues — “we gotta cover [ourselves] — became the focal point of the internal review that ensued. Read more.
