Back by Popular Demand: Deep Sleep Meditation Instruction

<p>Joy Hoffman, a certified Yoga Nidra and Yoga Therapist, is once again bringing her Deep Sleep Meditation instruction to St. Stephen’s North Hall on Sunday, June 9, 7 p.m.-8:15 p.m. The public is welcome, no experience is necessary, and it is designed for all ages.Deep Sleep Meditation is composed of a series of breath, body and awareness techniques that progressively relax participants into deeper brain wave patterns.&nbsp;&nbsp;Because of its restorative effects on the body, mind and spirit, one hour of this practice is comparable to 3-4 hours of deep sleep.&nbsp;It is especially beneficial to those who feel overwhelmed and always on the go or those practicing intense or competitive sports. &nbsp;There are no yoga poses, only reclining on one’s back.&nbsp;</p><p>Participants will need: a throw pillow, 1-2 blankets, a yoga mat (let us know if you need one, and it will be supplied), an eye pillow or washcloth and an optional bolster for under your knees. “These props are meant to assure comfort while you are lying on your back for an hour,” according to Rich Stein, organizer of the event. &nbsp;Registration is required, and a $20 donation is requested.&nbsp;Please register <a href="https://onrealm.org/StStephensChurc/PublicRegistrations/Event?linkString=MzMxMWIxZjgtZWIxYy00NTg4LTk0NWYtYjE3NjAwZDhlMDZi" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">here. </a></p>

Celebrate and Honor Dan O’Brien with Ridgefield Rotary on June 12!

<p>Join us on June 12th at 6:00 PM at Silver Spring Country Club to honor Dan O’Brien, a distinguished Ridgefield resident, and to express our heartfelt "Thank You" for his unwavering dedication to our community.</p><p>Tickets for this special event are $125, with a cash bar available. Tickets can be purchased on the Rotary's website at <a href="https://ridgefieldrotary.org/citizen-of-the-year" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">https://ridgefieldrotary.org/citizen-of-the-year/</a></p><p>Proceeds from the event will support Ridgefield Rotary's Scholarship Program and grants to Ridgefield non-profit organizations.</p><p>When you think of the Rotary’s motto “Service Above Self,” you can’t help but think of Dan O’Brien. His commitment to serving the people of Ridgefield makes him the perfect recipient of this award.</p><p>Dan was born in Manhattan. After completing his early education, he attended Fordham University, where he met his wife Annette. Both graduated with a B.S. from the university in June 1968. Following their graduation, they lived in Princeton, NJ for 20 years before moving to Ridgefield in 1994. Two of their four daughters graduated from Ridgefield High School.</p><p>Dan has successfully balanced family, career, and community service, always willing to go the extra mile quietly and without seeking accolades. His extensive community involvement includes:</p><ul><li>Chair of the Ridgefield Historic District Commission</li><li>Past President and current Board Member of the Ridgefield Chamber of Commerce</li><li>Board Member of RVNAhealth, serving on the Finance, Investment, and Audit Committees</li><li>Longtime Treasurer and current Board President of the Ridgefield Symphony Orchestra Endowment Fund</li><li>Former Advisory Board Member of The Ridgefield Playhouse</li><li>Former Board Member of The Lounsbury House</li><li>Former Board Member of the Keeler Tavern Museum &amp; History Center Endowment Fund</li><li>General Partner with J. H. Whitney &amp; Co., an investment firm in New Canaan</li></ul><p>Dan has been quoted as saying, “I’ve been very lucky in life.” However, we at The Rotary Club and the residents of Ridgefield can confidently say, we are the lucky ones! Congratulations, Dan!</p><p><br></p>

National Charity League (NCL) Ridgefield Chapter Honors Exceptional Achievements

<p><span style="color: rgb(13, 13, 13); background-color: transparent;">The National Charity League (NCL) Ridgefield Chapter convened on May 22, 2024, for its highly anticipated yearly award ceremony and closing meeting marking the culmination of the 2023-2024 year. Brimming with joy and gratitude, Patronesses and Ticktockers united to celebrate the profound impact of NCL and its unwavering commitment to nurturing the special bond between mothers and daughters while imparting values of philanthropy, culture, and leadership.</span></p><p><span style="color: rgb(13, 13, 13); background-color: transparent;">Yenny Reed, Chapter President, extended heartfelt recognition to numerous individuals for their steadfast support and unwavering dedication to NCL. This included acknowledging both current board members and those stepping down after serving commendable two-year terms. </span><span style="color: rgb(13, 13, 13);">Kathryn Mastandrea, the distinguished guest speaker of the evening and Senior Director of Strategic Giving at Connecticut FoodShare, deeply engaged the audience with her profound reflections on the personal significance of NCL to both herself and her mother, delivering her message with heartfelt sincerity.</span><span style="color: rgb(13, 13, 13); background-color: transparent;"> Mrs. Mastandrea, a proud alumna of the NCL Ridgefield Chapter's Class of 2005, shared a legacy with her mother, who once held the position of chapter president.</span></p><p><span style="color: rgb(13, 13, 13); background-color: transparent;">A highlight of the evening was the spotlight on the chapter's 195 members, whose collective efforts resulted in an impressive tally of over 3,600 leadership hours. From evening planning sessions to fundraising endeavors, training initiatives, and researching new philanthropic ventures, the dedication of NCL members shone brightly throughout the 2023-2024 year. Notably, the chapter welcomed two new philanthropy partners, SOAR Together and CT FoodShare, bringing the total number of philanthropic endeavors to forty-two. A testament to their commitment to giving back, the chapter logged nearly 6,400 philanthropy hours.</span></p><p><span style="color: rgb(13, 13, 13); background-color: transparent;">The ceremony also featured the presentation of awards to the top Ticktockers with the highest philanthropic hours, with thirty-one Ticktockers receiving the esteemed Presidential Service Award, established by the President’s Council on Service and Civic Participation in 2003.</span></p><p><span style="color: rgb(13, 13, 13); background-color: transparent;">No celebration would be complete without honoring and congratulating the Class of 2024, whose collective achievements were showcased in a heartwarming slideshow presentation, capturing the essence of their years of participation in NCL.</span></p><p><br></p>

State Rep. Aimee Berger-Girvalo Nominated for 3rd Term

<p>Registered Democrats in Ridgefield’s 111<sup>th</sup> State House District, meeting in caucus at Town Hall, unanimously nominated State Representative Aimee Berger-Girvalo for reelection in&nbsp;November.</p><p>Berger-Girvalo’s name was placed in nomination by Ridgefield First Selectman Rudy Marconi, who heralded her tireless and successful efforts on behalf of the Town of Ridgefield.</p><p>Local education advocate and former teacher, Colleen Broderick, seconded the nomination, citing Berger-Girvalo’s deep community involvement and commending her “ability to listen, understand, and act on the concerns of her constituents, ensuring their voices are heard and their needs addressed.”</p><p>At the close of nominations, other Ridgefield residents offered their endorsement.&nbsp;Pastor Deborah Rundlett of Ridgebury Congregational Church and Director of the Meetinghouse, Berger-Girvalo’s daughter Gracie Girvalo, and community organization activist and director Amanda Cordano lauded Berger-Girvalo’s dedication to Ridgefield, effective representation of the town’s interests, strong moral compass, and unwavering commitment to justice and human rights.</p><p>In her acceptance remarks, Berger-Girvalo thanked the caucus participants, First Selectman Marconi, Ms. Broderick, the endorsers/speakers, Ridgefield Democratic Town Committee Chair (and caucus chair) Arny DiLaura, and residents throughout Ridgefield.</p><p>Berger-Girvalo spoke passionately about doing the heavy but satisfying work of cultivating trust and relationships across the aisle, citing dozens of bills she led to passage with bi-partisan support; she punctuated the point by relating how she is found on the GOP side of the chamber during crucial votes, answering questions about the legislation.</p><p>Berger-Girvalo favorably contrasted bi-partisanship that arises from the pragmatic, good-faith pursuit of common interests and needs, against a faux bi-partisanship that pursues substance-free compromise as its own end, without meaningful positive impact or worse, with deliberately harmful ramifications. She assured constituents across Ridgefield that she will continue to both deliver for the needs of our town and state, and to represent the moral values of compassion, justice, tolerance, inclusivity, equity, and humanity that make Ridgefield so special.</p><p>Underscoring the family focus of the evening, caucus participants were entertained by the acoustic musical stylings of State Rep. Berger-Girvalo’s son, Ridgefield High School graduating senior Austin Girvalo, before the caucus was gaveled into session.</p>

Annual Father’s Day 5K Hosted by Boy Scout Troop 431 at Tiger Hollow!

This event is stroller-friendly and perfect for parent-child teams.</p><p>All participants, including Fun Run entrants, will receive a commemorative race shirt. Additionally, there will be a food spread and raffle for all runners, and medals will be awarded to the top finishers.</p><p>The race schedule starts with the Fun Run at 8:00 AM, followed by the 5K Run/Walk at 8:30 AM.
