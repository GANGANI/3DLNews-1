FC Buzz Weekend May 31 to June 2: Rock, Honeybees, Local History, and More!

<p class="ql-align-center"><br></p><p>HamletHub is proud to partner with the Cultural Alliance of Fairfield County and FCBuzz to Elevate Arts and Culture in Fairfield County! Each week, look for a listing of weekend events and activities. The Cultural Alliance of Fairfield County celebrates the vibrancy and diversity of the arts and culture across 15 towns in coastal Fairfield County. Sign up to receive a free eBuzz newsletter with top picks and spotlight events from the hundreds of events posted on FCBuzz.</p><p><a href="https://events.culturalalliancefc.org/events/suzanne-chamlin-studies-in-color-1bdaa" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank" style="color: rgb(17, 85, 204);"><strong>SUZANNE CHAMLIN: STUDIES IN COLOR</strong></a></p><p><strong>Bellarmine Hall Galleries</strong></p><p>Friday May 31, 11:00 am - 4:00 pm</p><p>In this exhibition of recent work, Chamlin explores ideas about color theory and light through a series of landscape and interior stills. Since 2012, the artist has carefully charted her paints using the Munsell color system, which analyzes colors in terms of hue (the color itself), value (relative light and dark) and chroma (level of saturation or brilliance). For each of her paintings, Chamlin sets a highly specific palette; experimentation within this limited range then guides her decisions about process and pictorial space.</p><p><a href="https://events.culturalalliancefc.org/events/we-will-rock-you-546be" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank" style="color: rgb(17, 85, 204);"><strong>WE WILL ROCK YOU</strong></a></p><p><strong>Downtown Cabaret Theatre</strong></p><p>Friday May 31, 12:00 am - 12:00 am</p><p>Based on the music of Queen, We Will Rock You is set 300 years in the future. A handful of rock rebels, the Bohemians, fight for freedom, individuality and the rebirth of the age of rock. Two young outsiders, Scaramouche and Galileo, cannot come to terms with the bleak conformist reality. They join the Bohemians and embark on the search to find the unlimited power of freedom, love and rock! The songs of Queen with a book by Ben Elton including We Are The Champions, Killer Queen, Don’t Stop Me Now,Bohemian Rhapsody, Another One Bites The Dust, of course, We Will Rock You!</p><p><a href="https://events.culturalalliancefc.org/events/performing-arts-at-moca-herlin-riley---jazz-at-lincoln-center" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank" style="color: rgb(17, 85, 204);"><strong>PERFORMING ARTISTS: HERLIN RILEY for JAZZ AT LINCOLN CENTER</strong></a></p><p><strong>MoCA Westport</strong></p><p>Friday May 31, 7:00 pm - 12:00 am</p><p>Herlin Riley is a native of New Orleans. He’s a member of a New Orleans musical family (The Lastie Family). Today he’s considered to be one of the most premier and prolific drummers in the world today. The range of his musical experiences makes the demand for his drumming skills a first call for performers, recording artist and teaching engagements. In the early 80’s Herlin switched to playing the drums exclusively and was called to London, Australia and Europe to play in a 1920’s style musical called "One Mo' Time."</p><p><a href="https://events.culturalalliancefc.org/events/all-about-honeybees" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank" style="color: rgb(17, 85, 204);"><strong>ALL ABOUT HONEYBEES</strong></a></p><p><strong>Connecticut Audubon Society Fairfield Nature Center</strong></p><p>Saturday Jun 1, 10:00 am - 11:30 am</p><p>Panagiota Raguskus of the Back Yard Beekeepers Association leads this program about honeybee anatomy, the hive, and how important these remarkable insects are in our ecosystem. She will show you how to care for bees and what protective equipment beekeepers use to keep safe from stings. Discussion will include parasites of the hive, the harms of pesticides, and other threats to honeybees. Participants will get to taste local honey, hold and smell honeycomb, and learn how candles and other wax products are made. </p><p><a href="https://events.culturalalliancefc.org/events/shannon-mcmahon-live-on-the-patio" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank" style="color: rgb(17, 85, 204);"><strong>SHANNON MCMAHON LIVE ON THE PATIO</strong></a></p><p><strong>Easton Public Library</strong></p><p>Sunday Jun 2, 12:00 pm - 2:00 pm</p><p>Shannon McMahon, singer/songwriter and Library Assistant will be performing an acoustic set on the Library patio. Ridge Crest Unveils New Therapy Gym, Advanced Therapy Technology

<p>Ridge Crest, the award-winning sub-acute rehabilitation and skilled nursing facility at <a href="https://www.meadowridge.com/" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">Meadow Ridge</a>, is excited to unveil a renovated therapy gym and expanded outpatient facilities. The new area, which is twice as large as the former gym, features state-of-the-art equipment, large and comfortable outpatient treatment rooms, and spaces specifically designed for occupational therapy. Ridge Crest provides physical, occupational, and speech therapy to patients from the community, short- and long-term skilled nursing residents, and residents of Meadow Ridge.&nbsp;</p><p><br></p><p>As part of the renovations, Ridge Crest has added virtual reality therapy with the <a href="https://www.realsystem.com/yseries/?gclid=Cj0KCQjw3ZayBhDRARIsAPWzx8o0C6xUXfRzizJ93AzIMpB5kN0G8kfsjILgFe8njiHumlPMyF00heQaAh4qEALw_wcB" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">REAL y-series by Penumbra</a>. Featuring a fully immersive headset, the system helps target functional movement, balance and stability, and cognitive improvement by placing users in a virtual world of games and challenges. Sensors placed on the body provide real-time data to measure and monitor the patient’s progress. This allows therapists to better target treatment and helps patients, in many cases, recover more quickly. The equipment is the world’s only full body, hands free, tether-less system. </p><p><br></p><p>“Physical and occupational therapy help not only those recovering from major illness or surgery, but also those overcoming pain or mobility limitations in order to age in place,” says Meadow Ridge Campus Executive Director Chris Barstein. “With added space for outpatient treatment, we’re able to deliver this high-quality care and enhance quality of life for more members of the community as well as residents.” </p><p><br></p><p>“We feel fortunate to offer technology and facilities that match the level of skill our therapy staff brings to Ridge Crest,” says Director of Rehabilitation Services for Meadow Ridge, Brian Gallo. “And the new VR therapy is a tremendous tool that helps our patients get more out of their therapy experience and actually have a little fun along the way.” </p><p><br></p><p>The renovations also added a doctors suite, which will be able to accommodate on-site primary and specialty care appointments, lab draws, and consultations for Meadow Ridge and Ridge Crest residents. Further work to renovate Ridge Crest’s patient rooms and common areas, and add a new cafe, is planned for later this year.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>

Services planned for Redding resident Thomas MacDonald, 54

<p>Thomas Marks MacDonald, of Redding, Conn., unexpectedly passed away on May 21, 2024.&nbsp;He is survived by his beloved wife, Lisa (Amabile) MacDonald, and cherished daughter, Hadley. He was the beloved son of Richard and Martha (Cook) MacDonald of Wayland, Mass. and loved by his brother, Rick MacDonald of Needham, Mass. and sister, Mary Ellen MacDonald of Wayland, Mass. He was especially devoted to his Samoyeds, Abner and Sawyer. He loved his dogs.</p><p>Tom was born Dec. 3, 1969 in Waltham, Mass. and raised in Wayland, Mass. He earned a B.A. from Merrimack College, where he also played varsity lacrosse, and an M.S. from Simmons University. He was employed by Altrinsic Global Advisors, in Stamford, Conn.</p><p>Most important to Tom were his family and friends. He always looked forward to the holidays, when his family would get together for good food, festivities and lots of laughter, love and conversation. Each Christmas Eve, Tom would make meatballs with his daughter, Hadley, using a recipe handed down from his great grandmother. Seasons and weekends would be filled with biking, lacrosse, and watching Hadley compete on her swim, lacrosse and basketball teams, as well as keeping a close watch on his favorite teams, the Boston Red Sox and New England Patriots.&nbsp;</p><p>Tom enjoyed growing up in Wayland, and spent many summers as a lifeguard and swimming instructor at the Wayland Town Beach. Later, he and his daughter cherished their annual bike rides to the town beach.
