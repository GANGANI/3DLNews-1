In Connecticut, approximately 99.70% of the state has internet service with speeds of 15 Mbps available for purchase through internet service providers; however, approximately 23.80% of the state’s residents have little to no internet connection readily available to them. Some of those with limited internet access have dial-up or legacy copper DSL internet service, but their internet is so slow that streaming, video conferencing, online gaming and other activities requiring high-speed internet are out of reach.

Wireless providers reach 100% of the state of Connecticut, but other forms of internet technology are less widely available in Connecticut than in other states. Only 97.40% of the population of Connecticut has access to DSL internet service. Cable internet is available to 98.40%, while only 44.40% have access to fiber internet. Less than 68.30% of Connecticut is served by three or more wired internet providers (DSL, cable and fiber internet).
