Harper Joy Theater begins season with comedy

  • Oct 18, 2018
  • Oct 18, 2018 Updated Apr 26, 2022
  • 0
  • Facebook
  • Twitter
  • WhatsApp
  • SMS
  • Email
  • Facebook
  • Twitter
  • WhatsApp
  • SMS
  • Email
  • Print
  • Save

Charles L. Mee’s “Big Love” retells Aeschylus’s “The Suppliants,” setting the play in a modern-day villa  in Italy, where 50 brides flee to avoid marrying their 50 cousins. 

While the brides and grooms await their wedding day, the play explores issues of gender politics, love and domestic violence. 

“I wanted to go back to what some people thought was one of the earliest plays of the Western World and see how that would look today, see if it still spoke to the moment, and of course it does. It’s all about refugees and gender wars and men and women trying to find what will get them through the rubble of dysfunctional relationships, and anger and rage and heartache,” Mee said.

The New York Times describes the play as “comedic, gymnastic, musical, sensual, shocking and redemptive.” It is recommended for teens and older. 

Performances will be in Freimann Studio Theatre at Harper Joy Theater, 41 S. Park St., on the Whitman College campus, at 8 p.m. tonight-Saturday and Oct. 26-27 and at 2 p.m. Saturday- and Sunday and Oct. 28. 

Tickets are $8-$12. Online see ubne.ws/2GiDAmP or call the box office at  527-5180.
