State Soccer: Nolensville makes a splash in state tournament debut

  • By Charles Pulliam • Multimedia Reporter

    Charles Pulliam

    Sports Editor

MURFREESBORO – Missing a few starters and making their debut in the State AA girls’ soccer tournament didn’t affect the Nolensville Lady Knights Wednesday in Murfreesboro.

Nolensville overwhelmed Covington 6-0 to reach the state semifinals for the first time in the young school’s history. The Lady Knights will battle Seymour, which topped McMinn Central 7-0, at 1:30 p.m. Thursday at the Richard Siegel Soccer Complex for a spot in Saturday’s state title match.

State Soccer Photo Gallery – Nolensville vs. Covington

The Nolensville girls' soccer team advanced past Covington 5-0 in the first round of the State AA Tournament in Murfreesboro on Oct. 24, 2018. 

“This is incredible,” junior Kaitlyn Sneed said. “It’s just crazy to watch our team get here. It’s kind of embarrassing, but last year we barely won two games and now we are in the state semifinals.”

The Lady Knights, who improved to 17-1-2 overall, advanced to the state tournament with a 4-0 win over White House in a Class AA sectional over the weekend, but lost some starters to injury in the contest, including Makenna Gebhard and Parker Leftwhich. Sophomore Melanie Gebhard also didn’t play Wednesday, but coach Eric Shelton is hoping she can return.

“We went in not knowing what to expect,” Shelton said. “We needed some players to step up. I think Amelia Logiudice came in and was incredibly creative and was good on the ball.

"We have had freshman like that have to contribute all season long so it’s nothing new for us.”

Freshmen Millie Smith, Elise Dobson and Logiudice all scored as Nolensville controlled the ball from the get-go and racked up 27 shots on goal in the contest. Sneed, Smith and Dobson all had eight shots and combined for 15 shots on goal. 

Nolensville outshot Covington 26-1 in the first half but managed just one goal on Smith’s tally in the 32nd. The freshman worked around a defender, fired a hard shot that banged off the left post right back to Smith for a rebound that whistled past Covington keeper Destiny Ballard. 

Ballard was busy all match and came up with 13 saves in the first half and eight more in the second 40 minutes.

Logiudice scored off a pass from Smith from the middle of the box in the 53rd to make it 2-0. Smith raced down the sideline and worked around two defenders for her second tally four minutes later. Dobson stretched the lead with back-to-back goals in the 63rd and 70th, while Sydney Hummel provided the exclamation point on a set shot from outside the box in the final seconds of the match.

“We knew if we played on a big pitch like this, it fits our style of play,” Shelton said. “We like to possess the ball and we do a good job of moving the ball around and finding feet. I think that’s what happened as we eventually broke through.”

Ciampa Ava stopped two shots for the shutout.

Defensively, the Lady Knights held Covington top scorer Madison Luttrell without a shot. The Lady Chargers had combined for 27 goals in their last three matches, but had just two looks on goal Wednesday. 

Sneed gave credit to the Nolensville fans for helping the team have confidence from the opening whistle.

“Our student section is insane and gets us hyped up right away,” she said. “The whole school is on our side. I think, that’s just incredible.”
